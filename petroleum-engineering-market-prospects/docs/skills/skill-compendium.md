## Skill 01 - Well Control

Why this skill: [Well control](01-well-control.md) is the highest safety-critical competence for drilling and completions roles. Mastery reduces risk and makes you immediately valuable offshore.

Roadmap
- Beginner (0–3 months): Pressure basics, mud weight, terminology, basic kill-sheet math.
- Intermediate (3–12 months): Choke & kill procedures, BOP systems, surface and subsurface barriers, simulator practice.
- Advanced (12–36 months): HPHT [well control](01-well-control.md), well intervention planning, advanced kill sequencing, team leadership in incidents.

12‑month step-by-step plan
1. Month 0–1: Read the [IWCF](../glossary.md) syllabus and basic well control primers. Learn hydrostatic vs. formation pressure math.
2. Month 2–3: Take an introductory [well-control](01-well-control.md) workshop (IADC/[IWCF](../glossary.md) Level 1 prep). Practice kill-sheet exercises and shut-in pressure scenarios.
3. Month 4–6: Attend [IWCF](../glossary.md) Level 2 (practical) / IADC WellCAP course. Log simulator hours (BOP stack, choke control).
4. Month 7–12: Run weekly drill-team tabletop exercises, shadow a rig-based [well control](01-well-control.md) rep, and lead a dry-run on kill procedures.

Courses & certifications
- IWCF Well Control (Levels 1–4) — required progression for drilling roles.
- IADC WellCAP — complementary classroom workshop for causal understanding.
- Vendor BOP familiarisation courses (manufacturer/operator-specific).

Books & references
- IWCF & IADC course materials and well control manuals.
- API and industry guidance on blowout prevention and BOP (check latest API documents).
- SPE technical papers and OnePetro resources on kicks and kills.

Hands-on projects
- Build a set of kill-sheet calculators (spreadsheet + worked examples).
- Run BOP and choke control scenarios in a simulator or classroom kit and record a short report for each scenario.

Tools & software
- Well-pressure calculators and spreadsheet templates.
- BOP / choke simulator (vendor or training-provider software).

Edge-building courses
- HPHT well control workshops, pressure transient analysis, and basic reservoir-pressure modelling to anticipate kicks.
- Learn Python for automation of [well-control](01-well-control.md) check calculations and reproducible kill-sheet generation.

## Skill 02 - Drilling Fundamentals

Why this skill: It is the language of the rig — without it you cannot function on a drill floor or in a rig planning team.

Roadmap
- Beginner (0–3 months): Rig components, drill string, basic drilling parameters (ROP, torque, WOB).
- Intermediate (3–12 months): Casing design, hydraulics, tripping operations, drillstring mechanics.
- Advanced (12–36 months): Drilling optimization, rig hydraulics modeling, casing-program design, contract & cost drivers.

12‑month plan
1. Month 0–2: Study rig types and read daily drilling reports; complete a '[drilling fundamentals](02-drilling-fundamentals.md)' online course or university module.
2. Month 3–6: Learn drilling fluid interaction with drilling mechanics; practice pressure, ECD, and torque/drag calculations.
3. Month 7–12: Join ride-along/field placement or simulated planning exercises; produce a simple well plan (TD, casing points, mud program).

Courses & certifications
- IADC Drilling Engineer short courses; operator/vendor training for rig systems.
- University modules (BSc/MSc drilling electives), Coursera/edX petroleum basics.

Books & references
- Industry drilling handbooks and operator well-program templates.

Hands-on projects
- Create a full 7-step simplified well program for a target reservoir (depths, casing, mud weights, cement intervals).

Tools & software
- Drilling hydraulics calculators, torque-and-drag tools, basic well-planning spreadsheets.

Edge-building courses
- Learn borehole stability, real-time drilling optimization, and basic directional-well planning with Petrel/WellPlan.

## Skill 03 - Drilling Fluids (Mud Engineering)

Why this skill: [Drilling fluids](03-drilling-fluids.md) act as the primary pressure-control barrier and are central to wellbore stability and cost control.

Roadmap
- Beginner (0–3 months): Fluid types, basic lab tests (mud weight, viscosity, filtrate).
- Intermediate (3–12 months): Formulation, solids control, lab interpretation, lost-circulation mitigation.
- Advanced (12–36 months): HPHT fluids, synthetic-based fluids, specialty additives and lab design for challenging wells.

12‑month plan
1. Month 0–2: Learn mud property basics and perform/observe basic lab tests.
2. Month 3–6: Shadow a mud engineer on rig or in lab; run mud weight and rheology adjustments under supervision.
3. Month 7–12: Lead a small mud-design case (e.g., choose a formulation for an extended-reach section) and report learned trade-offs.

Courses & certifications
- Vendor mud-school courses (Newpark, [Halliburton](../../data/company-directory/halliburton.md), [Baker Hughes](../../data/company-directory/bakerhughes.md)/Baroid, SLB).
- API RP references for [drilling fluids](03-drilling-fluids.md) testing.

Books & references
- Industry mud manuals and vendor technical bulletins.

Hands-on projects
- Design and document a mud program for a hypothetical deepwater well and prepare contingency plans.

Tools & software
- Mud-logging and lab software, rheometer use, and mud-design spreadsheets.

Edge-building courses
- Polymer chemistry basics, non-Newtonian fluid mechanics, and environmental regulations for fluid disposal and compliance.

## Skill 04 - Formation Evaluation & Log Interpretation

Why this skill: Determines whether you hit pay and influences completion and reservoir decisions.

Roadmap
- Beginner: Basic gamma/neutron/density/resistivity log shapes and lithology indicators.
- Intermediate: Porosity, water saturation calculations, basic petrophysical interpretation.
- Advanced: Integrated log+core interpretation, advanced logs (NMR, SR), [formation evaluation](04-formation-evaluation.md) studies.

12‑month plan
1. Month 0–3: Study well log fundamentals and complete basic log-interpretation exercises.
2. Month 4–8: Take a Petrophysics short course (SPE/vendor) and practice on open datasets.
3. Month 9–12: Complete an interpreted log-across-section study and write a short reservoir recommendation.

Courses & certifications
- University petrophysics modules, [Schlumberger](../../data/company-directory/slb.md)/other vendor log-interpretation courses, SPE short courses.

Books & references
- Standard petrophysics and log-interpretation texts (look for titles used in university courses and SPE recommended reading).

Hands-on projects
- Interpret logs from an open well dataset, produce net pay and saturation estimates, and validate against synthetic core data.

Tools & software
- Petrel, Techlog, basic log-interpretation tools (open-source alternatives exist for practice).

Edge-building courses
- NMR interpretation, rock physics, and quantitative log-driven reservoir characterization; Python for log analytics.

## Skill 05 - Directional Drilling

Why this skill: [Directional drilling](05-directional-drilling.md) enables economic development of deepwater fields via ERD and multilateral wells.

Roadmap
- Beginner: Azimuth/inclination basics, survey maths, dogleg/closure concepts.
- Intermediate: [BHA](../glossary.md) design for directional control, downhole motor vs rotary steerable systems.
- Advanced: Geosteering, wellbore placement in thin reservoirs, 3D trajectory design.

12‑month plan
1. Month 0–3: Learn survey math and practice basic trajectory calculations.
2. Month 4–8: Explore [BHA](../glossary.md) design and steering tools; use software to model hole trajectories.
3. Month 9–12: Participate in a geosteering workshop or simulator and produce an as-drilled vs plan reconciliation.

Courses & certifications
- Vendor [directional-drilling](05-directional-drilling.md) workshops, university short courses, and geosteering bootcamps.

Hands-on projects
- Plan and model a directional well from surface to target using available well-planning tools.

Edge-building courses
- Advanced geomechanics, real-time drilling data analytics, and ML-driven steering optimization.

## Skill 06 - MWD/LWD Tools & Operations

Why this skill: Real-time formation and directional data are crucial for safe and efficient drilling; [MWD](../glossary.md)/LWD engineers are the bridge between downhole sensors and surface decisions.

Roadmap
- Beginner: Telemetry basics, tool string components, surface acquisition systems.
- Intermediate: Tool maintenance, data QC, basic downhole sensor theory.
- Advanced: Real-time interpretation, tool failure diagnosis, sensor fusion with [LWD](../glossary.md) measurements.

12‑month plan
1. Month 0–2: Study mud-pulse telemetry and downhole power systems.
2. Month 3–6: Learn surface data acquisition, QC procedures, and run synthetic logs.
3. Month 7–12: Practice troubleshooting with vendor tool simulators and participate in field rotations.

Courses & certifications
- Vendor [MWD](../glossary.md)/LWD product training ([SLB](../../data/company-directory/slb.md), [Halliburton](../../data/company-directory/halliburton.md), [Baker Hughes](../../data/company-directory/bakerhughes.md)), basic electronics and telemetry workshops.

Hands-on projects
- Build a data QC checklist, analyze sample telemetry logs, and document solutions to common tool malfunctions.

Edge-building courses
- Embedded systems basics, signal processing, and Python for time-series data analysis.

## Skill 07 - Mudlogging & Cuttings Analysis

Why this skill: Mudloggers are the eyes on lithology and gas shows during drilling and are central to real-time decision support.

Roadmap
- Beginner: Cuttings descriptions, basic gas detection, sample handling.
- Intermediate: Geochemical screening, lithology logs, correlation with [LWD](../glossary.md).
- Advanced: Quantitative source-rock evaluation, integration with petrophysics and geosteering.

12‑month plan
1. Month 0–3: Learn lab sample workflows and gas-detection instrumentation.
2. Month 4–8: Practice cuttings description and create a lithology log from a drill run.
3. Month 9–12: Lead a short real-time reporting exercise and produce a concise technical summary.

Courses & certifications
- [Mudlogging](07-mudlogging.md) vendor training, basic geology/petrology courses, and geochemical intro modules.

Hands-on projects
- Prepare a complete [mudlogging](07-mudlogging.md) report from raw sample data and write recommended actions for casing points.

Edge-building courses
- Geochemistry, basin analysis, and sedimentology for a more accurate lithology and reservoir prediction.

## Skill 08 - FPSO Process Operations

Why this skill: FPSOs are the production hubs in Angola — process operators keep the plant producing safely and reliably.

Roadmap
- Beginner: Process flow diagrams, basic separation, safety systems, and rotating equipment awareness.
- Intermediate: Hydrocarbon handling, control loops, alarms and shutdown logic, and process troubleshooting.
- Advanced: Production optimization, [artificial lift](09-artificial-lift.md) integration, and topside modification design.

12‑month plan
1. Month 0–3: Learn P&ID reading, process fundamentals (separation, dehydration, gas handling).
2. Month 4–8: Gain hands-on experience with simulation runs and process safety case study reviews.
3. Month 9–12: Run a plant-safety walkthrough, prepare standard operating procedure drafts, and study rotating-equipment maintenance basics.

Courses & certifications
- Operator process training, API/ISO process-safety short courses, vendor-specific rotating-equipment courses.

Hands-on projects
- Create a process troubleshooting guide for a common [FPSO](../glossary.md) upset (e.g., slugging, gas carry-over).

Edge-building courses
- Dynamic process simulation, process-control tuning, and hydrocarbon accounting.

## Skill 09 - Artificial Lift Systems

Why this skill: [Artificial lift](09-artificial-lift.md) extends production life and is a key differentiator for production engineers.

Roadmap
- Beginner: Gas lift vs ESP basics, selection criteria, surface intefaces.
- Intermediate: ESP design, gas-lift system sizing, well performance analysis.
- Advanced: Lifetime optimization, reliability engineering, and integration with reservoir management.

12‑month plan
1. Month 0–3: Study lift mechanism basics and perform case-study calculations (ESP curves, gas-lift rates).
2. Month 4–8: Take vendor ESP/gas-lift workshops and model a lift solution for an aging well.
3. Month 9–12: Plan an ESP workover or gas-lift conversion and quantify uplift vs capex.

Courses & certifications
- Vendor ESP and gas-lift training, reservoir performance short courses.

Hands-on projects
- Design and justify an [artificial-lift](09-artificial-lift.md) program for a sample well and present ROI analysis.

Edge-building courses
- Reliability engineering, well-intervention planning, and production-data analytics.

## Skill 10 - Flow Assurance

Why this skill: Preventing hydrates, wax, and asphaltene issues is mission-critical for long subsea tiebacks.

Roadmap
- Beginner: Thermodynamics basics, hydrate and wax formation drivers.
- Intermediate: Insulation/heating design, chemical inhibitor selection, and transient flow modelling.
- Advanced: Long-term multiphase flow simulation and system-level integrity planning.

12‑month plan
1. Month 0–3: Learn hydrate fundamentals and PVT basics.
2. Month 4–8: Use a transient multiphase-flow tool (training or interface) to model an example tieback.
3. Month 9–12: Propose a combined mitigation plan (insulation, trace heating, chemicals) and cost analysis.

Courses & certifications
- [Flow-assurance](10-flow-assurance.md) vendor courses (OLGA/[Schlumberger](../../data/company-directory/slb.md)/Cambridge Energy), PVT workshops.

Hands-on projects
- Model a hydrate-formation case and design an inhibitor/thermal mitigation plan.

Edge-building courses
- CFD for multiphase flows, advanced thermodynamics, and chemistry of inhibitors.

## Skill 11 - Subsea Systems

Why this skill: Angola is a subsea-first basin — subsea engineers and [ROV](../glossary.md) technicians are central to operations and intervention.

Roadmap
- Beginner: Subsea vocabulary, tree and manifold components, and umbilicals.
- Intermediate: [SURF](../glossary.md) installation awareness, ROV operations basics, and integrity management.
- Advanced: Subsea architecture design, intervention planning, and deepwater flowline engineering.

12‑month plan
1. Month 0–3: Learn subsea equipment names, functions, and key drawings.
2. Month 4–8: Attend [SURF](../glossary.md)/ROV familiarisation courses and study subsea installation case studies.
3. Month 9–12: Participate in an intervention-planning exercise and draft an intervention scope with [ROV](../glossary.md) support requirements.

Courses & certifications
- [ROV](../glossary.md) training (IMCA/industry providers), [SURF](../glossary.md) and subsea engineering short courses, DNV/ABS subsea integrity workshops.

Hands-on projects
- Prepare a subsea intervention plan and ROV-task list for a hypothetical leaking spool.

Edge-building courses
- Umbilical design, subsea controls (SCADA/telemetry), and reliability-centered maintenance for subsea assets.

## Skill 12 - Offshore HSE & Safety Management

Why this skill: HSE competence is a minimum-hire requirement for any offshore role and essential for leadership positions.

Roadmap
- Beginner: [BOSIET](../glossary.md)/HUET, basic permit-to-work and toolbox talk skills.
- Intermediate: Incident investigation, risk assessment, and behavior-based safety programs.
- Advanced: Safety leadership, process safety management, and regulatory compliance for ANPG and operators.

12‑month plan
1. Month 0–2: Obtain [BOSIET](../glossary.md)/[HUET](../glossary.md) and offshore medical.
2. Month 3–6: Take NEBOSH or equivalent process-safety short course and assist with incident-report reviews.
3. Month 7–12: Lead a small HSE initiative (e.g., risk review) and produce a management-level summary.

Courses & certifications
- OPITO [BOSIET](../glossary.md)/[HUET](../glossary.md), NEBOSH, [H2S](../glossary.md) training, Firefighting & First Aid, ANPG compliance workshops.

Hands-on projects
- Run a mock incident investigation and produce corrective action tracking.

Edge-building courses
- Process-safety leadership, human factors in operations, and safety analytics using simple data tools.

## Skill 13 - Cementing & Completions

Why this skill: The completion and cementing program determines long-term well productivity and zonal isolation.

Roadmap
- Beginner: Cement slurries, primary cementing steps, perforating basics.
- Intermediate: Squeeze jobs, stimulation basics, sand control strategies.
- Advanced: Completion design for ESPs, multilateral completions, and long-term integrity planning.

12‑month plan
1. Month 0–3: Learn cement design basics and common failure modes.
2. Month 4–8: Study completion case studies and participate in a completion-design workshop.
3. Month 9–12: Draft a full completion design for a sample well including selection of sand-control and packer systems.

Courses & certifications
- Vendor cementing and completion workshops ([Halliburton](../../data/company-directory/halliburton.md), [Baker Hughes](../../data/company-directory/bakerhughes.md), Schlumberger), SPE completion seminars.

Hands-on projects
- Run slurry-design calculations and prepare a completion selection rationale for a field case.

Edge-building courses
- Sand-control engineering, well-intervention methods, and perforation dynamics.

## Skill 14 - Reservoir Engineering Basics

Why this skill: Reservoir engineers turn data into forecasts and guide field development — high leverage for economic decisions.

Roadmap
- Beginner: Material-balance and basic decline-curve analysis.
- Intermediate: Numerical simulation, reservoir characterization, PVT basics.
- Advanced: EOR methods, coupled well-reservoir optimization, uncertainty quantification.

12‑month plan
1. Month 0–3: Study DCA, material balance, and basic well testing concepts.
2. Month 4–8: Take a reservoir-simulation short course and practice building a simple model.
3. Month 9–12: Run a sensitivity study on recovery factors and prepare a short field-development note.

Courses & certifications
- University reservoir engineering modules, SPE reservoir courses, software vendor training (Eclipse, CMG).

Hands-on projects
- Build a simple two-layer reservoir model and test different production strategies.

Edge-building courses
- Data assimilation, uncertainty quantification, and machine-learning applications for production forecasting.

## Skill 15 - Well Integrity

Why this skill: Prevents loss of containment across the well lifecycle and is central to both HSE and production reliability.

Roadmap
- Beginner: Barrier concepts, casing/cement roles, basic integrity checks.
- Intermediate: [Well-integrity](15-well-integrity.md) risk assessment, pressure-testing programs, and corrosion-control basics.
- Advanced: Integrity management systems, permanent monitoring, and abandonment planning.

12‑month plan
1. Month 0–3: Learn barrier philosophy and common integrity failure modes.
2. Month 4–8: Participate in pressure-test planning and casing-evaluation case studies.
3. Month 9–12: Draft a [well-integrity](15-well-integrity.md) plan for a sample asset covering monitoring and inspection frequency.

Courses & certifications
- [Well-integrity](15-well-integrity.md) workshops, API standards review, and operator-specific integrity training.

Hands-on projects
- Prepare a barrier-failure-mode analysis and recommend redundant controls for a high-risk well.

Edge-building courses
- Corrosion engineering, permanent downhole monitoring systems, and integrated asset integrity management.
