ANPG verification & supplier registry — how to check company registration

Quick links
- Conteúdo Local (ANPG): https://anpg.co.ao/conteudo-local/
- Oferta Permanente: https://anpg.co.ao/permanentoffer/
- Plataforma IONA (data platform): https://iona.anpg.co.ao/anpg/geral/vdr

How to verify a company is registered/certified with ANPG
1. Start at the 'Conteúdo Local' page and follow links for 'Registo e Certificação de Empresas' or 'Empresas Certificadas'. These pages sometimes publish PDFs listing registered/certified companies.
2. Use the site search (https://anpg.co.ao/?s=empresa) to find news and PDF links referencing specific suppliers or 'Associadas'.
3. Check the 'Oferta Permanente' and 'Plataforma IONA' links for procurement information — these portals may require registration to download lists or data packages.
4. If a PDF list (e.g., "Associadas_CN_fv.pdf") is available, download and open it to extract company names and categories; use the PDF's table of contents for navigation.
5. When the web pages are dynamic or behind a portal, contact ANPG via the 'Contactos' page and request the supplier registry or a CSV export (provide a short explanation of your research purpose).

Practical verification tips
- If a company claims ANPG registration, ask for their registration/certification number and verify it via ANPG's contacts.
- Cross-check the company on LinkedIn (company page) and on the company's own Angola career pages.
- For smaller/local providers, verify tax ID and local address as part of due diligence.

Notes about automation limits
- ANPG pages and portals may use dynamic content or require authentication (IONA). Manual download or direct ANPG contact is often required for a complete supplier list.
