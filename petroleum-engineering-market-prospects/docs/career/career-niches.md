> Mapped to the Licenciatura em Engenharia de Petróleo curriculum

## Overview

A petroleum engineering degree unlocks **three career tracks**:
1. **Core Oil & Gas** — upstream operators, oilfield services, midstream
2. **Adjacent Technical** — refining, geoscience, petrochemicals, data/digital
3. **Energy Transition** — geothermal, carbon capture (CCUS), hydrogen, offshore wind, sustainability consulting

Angola is one of Africa's top two oil producers (alongside Nigeria), with **[Sonangol](../../data/company-directory/sonangol.md), [TotalEnergies](../../data/company-directory/totalenergies.md), Eni, Chevron, BP, Azule Energy** as main operators and a large ecosystem of oilfield service companies ([SLB](../../data/company-directory/slb.md), [Halliburton](../../data/company-directory/halliburton.md), [Baker Hughes](../../data/company-directory/bakerhughes.md), TechnipFMC, Saipem, Subsea7, Oceaneering, etc.) — all active hirers.

## Niche 1 — Reservoir Engineer

**What you do:** Evaluate underground formations, estimate recoverable reserves, design recovery strategies, model reservoir behavior over time, optimize production while minimizing decline.

**Your curriculum match:**
- Reservoir Engineering I & II ✅
- Reservoir Simulation & Modeling ✅
- Reservoir Monitoring & Management ✅
- Numerical Methods / Scientific Computing ✅
- Geostatistics ✅
- [Formation Evaluation](../skills/04-formation-evaluation.md) ✅

**Tools/software:** Petrel ([SLB](../../data/company-directory/slb.md)), Eclipse, CMG GEM/STARS, Reveal, OFM, ARIES

**Where to work in Angola/Africa:**
- Operators: [Sonangol](../../data/company-directory/sonangol.md) (E.P.), [TotalEnergies](../../data/company-directory/totalenergies.md) Angola, Eni Angola, Chevron Angola, Azule Energy, BP Angola
- Services: [SLB](../../data/company-directory/slb.md) ([Schlumberger](../../data/company-directory/slb.md)), Halliburton, [Baker Hughes](../../data/company-directory/bakerhughes.md)

**Career path:** Junior Reservoir Engineer → Reservoir Engineer → Senior RE → Subsurface Manager → Asset Manager

**Why it's a strong niche:** Reservoir engineers sit at the core of every field development decision. Every producing field needs them. Africa still has large maturing fields requiring enhanced oil recovery (EOR) expertise.

## Niche 2 — Drilling Engineer

**What you do:** Plan well trajectories, select drilling equipment and fluids, design casing programs, ensure [well integrity](../skills/15-well-integrity.md), manage drilling contractors, optimize rig performance, and control well cost.

**Your curriculum match:**
- Well Engineering ✅
- [Drilling Fluids](../skills/03-drilling-fluids.md) & Completion ✅
- Well Completion ✅
- Offshore Production Systems (Sistemas Marítimos — offshore context) ✅
- Engineering Economics (well cost optimization) ✅

**Tools/software:** WellPlan (Landmark), COMPASS, WellCat, Drillbench, [BHA](../glossary.md) design software

**Where to work in Angola/Africa:**
- Operators (well planning): [TotalEnergies](../../data/company-directory/totalenergies.md), Eni, Sonangol, Chevron
- Drilling contractors (rig-face role): Odfjell Drilling, Valaris, Transocean, Noble Corporation
- Services: [Halliburton](../../data/company-directory/halliburton.md), [Baker Hughes](../../data/company-directory/bakerhughes.md) ([drilling fluids](../skills/03-drilling-fluids.md), [directional drilling](../skills/05-directional-drilling.md))
- Local companies: [Sonamet](../../data/company-directory/sonamet.md), Paenal (fabrication/well services support)

**Career path:** Wellsite Engineer → Drilling Engineer → Sr. Drilling Engineer → Wells Manager → VP Wells

**Angola specifics:** Angola is a deep-water hub (Block 0, Block 15, Block 17, Block 31). Deep-water and ultra-deep-water drilling experience is highly valued.

## Niche 3 — Completions Engineer

**What you do:** Design the well completion (perforations, casing, tubing, wellhead), select completion fluids, plan stimulation operations (hydraulic fracturing, acidizing), design sand control, and ensure the well is ready to produce.

**Your curriculum match:**
- Well Completion ✅
- [Drilling Fluids](../skills/03-drilling-fluids.md) & Completion ✅
- [Artificial Lift](../skills/09-artificial-lift.md) ✅
- [Formation Evaluation](../skills/04-formation-evaluation.md) (choosing perforation intervals) ✅
- Offshore Production Systems ✅

**Tools/software:** Fracpro, ResFrac, FracCADE, IDEAS ([Halliburton](../../data/company-directory/halliburton.md)), well simulation tools

**Where to work in Angola:**
- Operators: [TotalEnergies](../../data/company-directory/totalenergies.md) (Block 17/32 — intelligent completions on Kaombo), [Azule Energy](../../data/company-directory/azule-energy.md), Chevron ([CABGOC](../../data/company-directory/chevron.md) — mature well workovers), Eni, [Sonangol](../../data/company-directory/sonangol.md)
- Services: [SLB](../../data/company-directory/slb.md) (sand control, perforating), [Halliburton](../../data/company-directory/halliburton.md) (cementing, completions), [Baker Hughes](../../data/company-directory/bakerhughes.md) (completion systems, packers), [Weatherford](../../data/company-directory/weatherford.md) (tubular running)
- Angola's sand-prone turbidite reservoirs make sand control (gravel pack, frac pack) the defining completions skill

**Career path:** Completions Engineer → Sr. Completions Engineer → Well Interventions Engineer → Wells Manager

**Your curriculum match:**
- Oil Flow (Escoamento de Petróleo) ✅
- Surface Production Systems ✅
- [Artificial Lift](../skills/09-artificial-lift.md) ✅
- Offshore Production Systems ✅
- Reservoir Monitoring & Management ✅

**Tools/software:** PROSPER, GAP (PETEX), Pipesim ([SLB](../../data/company-directory/slb.md)), WellFlo, OFM

**Where to work in Angola:** All operators with producing assets. [Sonangol](../../data/company-directory/sonangol.md), [TotalEnergies](../../data/company-directory/totalenergies.md), Eni, Azule Energy, Chevron. Also Weatherford ([artificial lift](../skills/09-artificial-lift.md) specialist).

**Career path:** Field Production Engineer → Production Optimization Engineer → Sr. Production Engineer → Production Manager

## Niche 5 — Reservoir Simulation Engineer (Digital/Subsurface Modeling)

**What you do:** Build and run numerical reservoir models (history-matching, forecasting, EOR screening), integrate geological and engineering data into simulators, support field development planning.

**Your curriculum match:**
- Reservoir Simulation & Modeling ✅
- Numerical Methods ✅
- Scientific Computing I & II ✅
- Geostatistics ✅
- Reservoir Engineering I & II ✅
- Linear Algebra (matrix solvers in simulation) ✅

**Tools/software:** Eclipse ([SLB](../../data/company-directory/slb.md)), CMG IMEX/GEM/STARS, Intersect, tNavigator, Petrel RE, Python/MATLAB for scripting

**Why this is a high-demand niche:** Simulation engineers with programming skills are increasingly rare and command premium salaries. The "Digital Oilfield" and AI-in-E&P trend means companies want engineers who can code as well as model reservoirs.

**Angola demand:** [TotalEnergies](../../data/company-directory/totalenergies.md) Block 17 full-field models contain 5–10 million cells. Azule Energy's Agogo pre-salt models are still being built from scratch. Chevron Block 0's 70-year-old field needs updated simulation for late-life waterflood optimization. Every major operator in Angola needs simulation engineers — and there are very few Angolan nationals qualified.

**Career path:** Reservoir Simulation Analyst → Simulation Engineer → Lead Simulation Engineer → Subsurface Technical Authority → Digital E&P Specialist

## Niche 6 — Petrophysicist / Formation Evaluation Specialist

**What you do:** Interpret wireline and [LWD](../glossary.md)/[MWD](../glossary.md) logs to characterize reservoir rocks (porosity, permeability, fluid saturations, lithology), support well placement decisions, and build petrophysical models.

**Your curriculum match:**
- [Formation Evaluation](../skills/04-formation-evaluation.md) ✅
- General Geology ✅
- Geochemistry ✅
- Sedimentary Basin Modeling ✅
- Basin Analysis ✅
- Geostatistics ✅

**Tools/software:** Techlog ([SLB](../../data/company-directory/slb.md)), IP (Interactive Petrophysics), Elan ([Halliburton](../../data/company-directory/halliburton.md)), Petrel Core & Geology

**Where to work:** [SLB](../../data/company-directory/slb.md) (world leader in wireline/petrophysics), [Halliburton](../../data/company-directory/halliburton.md), [Baker Hughes](../../data/company-directory/bakerhughes.md) — all operate heavily in Angola. Operators also hire petrophysicists in-house.

**Career path:** Petrophysics Analyst → Petrophysicist → Sr. Petrophysicist → Chief Petrophysicist / Subsurface Geoscientist

## Niche 7 — Geoscientist / Basin Analyst / Exploration Geologist

**What you do:** Analyze sedimentary basins for hydrocarbon potential, interpret seismic data, map geological structures, identify drill targets, and work upstream of drilling decisions.

**Your curriculum match:**
- General Geology ✅
- Basin Analysis ✅
- Sedimentary Basin Modeling ✅
- Geochemistry ✅
- Seismic Methods (optativa) ✅
- Geostatistics ✅

**Tools/software:** Kingdom (IHS), Petrel Geology, OpendTect, ArcGIS, Python for seismic processing

**Where to work in Angola:**
- [Sonangol](../../data/company-directory/sonangol.md) (AEPET — exploration arm)
- [TotalEnergies](../../data/company-directory/totalenergies.md) (major explorer in Angola)
- [Eni](../../data/company-directory/eni.md) (active explorer — Block 15/06, new blocks)
- AGR, TGS (data companies that provide geological studies)

**Angola specifics:** Angola's offshore ultra-deep blocks (Block 48, Block 49, new frontier blocks) are still being explored. Exploration geologists / basin analysts are in demand for new block studies, which are awarded via licensing rounds by the Agência Nacional de Petróleo, Gás e Biocombustíveis (ANPG).

**Career path:** Exploration Geologist → Sr. Geologist → Team Lead → Exploration Manager

## Niche 8 — HSE (Health, Safety & Environment) Engineer

**What you do:** Develop and implement safety management systems, conduct risk assessments (HAZID, HAZOP), ensure regulatory compliance, manage environmental impact, investigate incidents, develop emergency response plans.

**Your curriculum match:**
- Safety Engineering ✅
- Environmental Engineering ✅
- Sustainability ✅
- Engineering Economics (cost of safety systems) ✅

**Why it's always hiring:** HSE roles are non-negotiable in offshore oil & gas. Every operator and contractor must maintain minimum HSE staffing ratios by Angolan law (local content regulations).

**Where to work in Angola:** All operators + ALL service companies + [Sonangol](../../data/company-directory/sonangol.md) subsidiaries. Also regulatory bodies: ANPG, MINAMB (Ministry of Environment).

**Certifications that boost employability:** NEBOSH (International General Certificate — highly recognized globally), ISO 45001 Lead Auditor, IEMA Environmental Management.

**Career path:** HSE Technician/Coordinator → HSE Engineer → HSE Supervisor → HSE Manager → Group HSE Director

## Niche 11 — Energy Transition Engineer (CCUS / Geothermal / Hydrogen)

**What you do:** Apply subsurface and drilling expertise to decarbonization technologies — injecting CO₂ into depleted reservoirs (CCUS), developing geothermal heat/power systems, underground hydrogen storage, offshore wind foundations.

**Your curriculum match:**
- Reservoir Engineering (CO₂ injection, storage modeling) ✅
- Well Engineering (injection wells) ✅
- Environmental Engineering ✅
- Renewable Energy Topics (optativa) ✅
- Sustainability ✅
- Numerical Methods / Simulation ✅

**Why petroleum engineers are prized here:** "Geothermal energy companies are actively recruiting petroleum engineers right now. Your drilling expertise translates directly to geothermal energy development. Reservoir management skills apply to subsurface hydrogen storage." — environmentalscience.org

**Global market:** CCUS is growing rapidly (EU Carbon Border Adjustment Mechanism, net-zero commitments). Companies: [SLB](../../data/company-directory/slb.md) ([Schlumberger](../../data/company-directory/slb.md) Carbon Services), Halliburton NET Power, [Baker Hughes](../../data/company-directory/bakerhughes.md) Climate Technology, [TotalEnergies](../../data/company-directory/totalenergies.md) Renewables & Power.

**Angola context:** Still early-stage, but opportunities are emerging:
- **Gas flaring reduction:** Angola's gas utilization program requires operators to capture and monetize associated gas — creates demand for gas injection well design (a drilling/reservoir crossover)
- **Angola LNG (Soyo):** Captures gas from offshore blocks for LNG export — links upstream to energy transition
- **[Sonangol](../../data/company-directory/sonangol.md) sustainability mandate:** Sonangol's 2030 strategy includes renewable energy portfolio growth
- **[TotalEnergies](../../data/company-directory/totalenergies.md) Angola:** Parent company's "Multi-Energy" strategy means Angola operations will integrate renewables and CCUS within the decade
- **Depleted reservoir storage:** Block 0's mature reservoirs could be candidates for CO₂ storage — feasibility studies are expected by 2028–2030

**Career path:** Reservoir/Drilling Engineer → Energy Transition Specialist → CCUS Engineer → Climate Technology Lead

## Niche 12 — Petroleum Data Scientist / Digital Oilfield Specialist

**What you do:** Apply machine learning, data analytics, and simulation tools to oilfield problems — production optimization, predictive maintenance, drilling anomaly detection, reservoir characterization from big data.

**Your curriculum match:**
- Scientific Computing I & II ✅
- Numerical Methods ✅
- Statistics ✅
- Geostatistics ✅
- Linear Algebra ✅
- Calculus I–IV ✅ (math foundation for ML)

**Tools/software:** Python (pandas, scikit-learn, TensorFlow), Jupyter, Power BI, Spotfire, OSDU (Open Subsurface Data Universe), cloud platforms (AWS, Azure)

**Why this is a fast-growing niche:** The oil & gas industry is digitizing rapidly. Engineers who combine domain knowledge (what the data means) with coding ability (how to process it) are scarce and highly paid. This niche bridges petroleum engineering into tech companies (Microsoft, Google, AWS all have energy divisions) and consultancies (McKinsey, Accenture Energy).

**Self-study to unlock:** Python (free via Coursera, SPE learning), SQL, basic ML — these supplement your existing curriculum powerfully.

**Career path:** Petroleum Engineer → Production Data Analyst → Digital Oilfield Engineer → Data Science Lead (Energy) → AI/ML Engineer (Energy)

## Niche 15 — Local Content Consultant / Business Development (Angola Specific)

**What you do:** Help international oil companies comply with Angola's local content law (Lei do Conteúdo Local, Law 14/23), identify Angolan suppliers, manage ANPG compliance, develop supplier capabilities, advise on procurement strategies.

**Your curriculum match:**
- Introduction to Law ✅
- Entrepreneurship & Innovation ✅
- Engineering Economics ✅
- Full technical knowledge of services categories ✅ (you understand what each service company does)

**Why this is unique to you:** Your existing project (this very repository) has cataloged 80,000+ company-service category profiles from ANPG's local content database. You have a structural advantage in this niche over any generic business consultant.

**Angola opportunity:** ANPG (Agência Nacional de Petróleo, Gás e Biocombustíveis) requires all operators to use Angolan companies for qualifying services. This creates consulting demand for:
- Local content compliance audits
- Supplier development programs
- Joint venture facilitation (Angolan company + international company)
- ANPG reporting and documentation

**Career path:** Local Content Advisor (at operator or service company) → Local Content Manager → Independent Consultant → Business Development Director

# Part II — Oil & Gas Industry Sectors

The industry divides into three main streams plus a fourth cross-cutting segment. Understanding where each one sits is critical for targeting your job search — and for understanding where offshore work fits.

```
UPSTREAM  →  MIDSTREAM  →  DOWNSTREAM
   |
Oilfield Services (OFS) — cuts across all three
```

---

## Sector 1 — Upstream (Exploration & Production, E&P)

**What it covers:** Everything from finding hydrocarbons underground to bringing them to the surface and onto a tanker or pipeline.

**Sub-sectors:**

| Sub-sector | What happens | Key roles |
|---|---|---|
| **Exploration** | Seismic surveys, basin analysis, wildcat / appraisal drilling | Exploration Geologist, Geophysicist, Seismic Interpreter, Basin Analyst |
| **Development** | Field development planning, development drilling, facility design | Reservoir Engineer, Drilling Engineer, Completions Engineer, Subsurface Engineer, Facilities Engineer |
| **Production Operations** | Operating wells and facilities to produce oil/gas | Production Engineer, Operations Engineer, Process Engineer, [FPSO](../glossary.md) Operator, HSE Officer |

**Offshore relevance:** Upstream is the sector where **all offshore work lives.** Every drillship, jackup, semi-submersible rig, and [FPSO](../glossary.md) belongs to this sector. If you want to work offshore, upstream E&P is where you will spend your career.

**Angola upstream players:**

| Company | Role | Key assets |
|---|---|---|
| **[Sonangol](../../data/company-directory/sonangol.md)** | National oil company + operator | Block 0, Block 3/05, Block 4/05, partial interests across all blocks |
| **[TotalEnergies](../../data/company-directory/totalenergies.md)** | Largest international operator | Block 17 (Dalia, Pazflor, CLOV), Block 32 (Kaombo), Block 20 |
| **[Eni](../../data/company-directory/eni.md)** | Major operator | Block 15/06 (N'Goma [FPSO](../glossary.md)), Block 0, new licences |
| **[Chevron](../../data/company-directory/chevron.md)** | Major operator | Block 0 (Cabinda), Block 14, Block 39 |
| **[Azule Energy](../../data/company-directory/azule-energy.md)** | Largest E&P in Angola (BP + [Eni](../../data/company-directory/eni.md) JV) | Block 15 (Kizomba), Block 18, Block 31 |
| **BP** | Investor + operator stake | Block 31, Block 18 interests |

---

## Sector 2 — Oilfield Services (OFS)

**What it covers:** Specialist companies that provide the equipment, technology, and people that operators need to drill, complete, and produce wells. They are hired on a contract basis by operators — and they are typically the **easiest entry point** into the industry for fresh graduates.

**Sub-sectors:**

| Sub-sector | What it provides | Key companies in Angola |
|---|---|---|
| **Drilling Services** | [Directional drilling](../skills/05-directional-drilling.md), MWD/LWD, drilling fluids, bits, cementing | SLB, Halliburton, Baker Hughes, Weatherford |
| **Well Services** | Perforations, stimulation, wireline logging, coil tubing | [SLB](../../data/company-directory/slb.md), [Halliburton](../../data/company-directory/halliburton.md), [Baker Hughes](../../data/company-directory/bakerhughes.md) |
| **Subsea Services** | [ROV](../glossary.md) operations, subsea inspection, umbilicals, subsea trees | [Oceaneering](../../data/company-directory/oceaneering.md), TechnipFMC, Subsea7 |
| **Drilling Contractors** | Own and operate drilling rigs (jackups, semis, drillships) | Valaris, Transocean, Noble, Odfjell Drilling, Stena Drilling |
| **Production Services** | [Artificial lift](../skills/09-artificial-lift.md) (ESPs, gas lift), production chemicals | [Weatherford](../../data/company-directory/weatherford.md), SLB, Halliburton |
| **[FPSO](../glossary.md) Operators** | Build, own, and operate FPSOs on lease | [SBM Offshore](../../data/company-directory/sbmoffshore.md), Yinson, BW Offshore, MODEC, Teekay |
| **Installation & Construction** | Offshore pipeline lay, riser installation, hook-up | [Subsea7](../../data/company-directory/subsea7.md), Saipem, [McDermott](../angola/angolan-offshore-companies.md), [TechnipFMC](../../data/company-directory/technipfmc.md) |

**Why OFS is the best entry for offshore-focused graduates:** [SLB](../../data/company-directory/slb.md), Halliburton, and Baker Hughes run structured **Field Engineer Graduate Programs** (12–24 months rotational training) that typically start onshore but move offshore within 12–18 months. These programs are globally recognized and produce the most experienced field engineers in the industry. Entry is competitive but accessible for petroleum engineering graduates.

---

## Sector 3 — Midstream

**What it covers:** Transportation, processing, and storage of oil and gas between the wellhead and the refinery or export terminal.

| Asset type | What it does | Offshore presence |
|---|---|---|
| Export pipelines | Move crude oil or gas from offshore to shore | Offshore pipeline engineers, [flow assurance](../skills/10-flow-assurance.md) |
| [FPSO](../glossary.md) offloading | Crude offloaded to shuttle tankers directly at sea | FPSO operations team, mooring systems |
| LNG loading terminals | Offshore or nearshore LNG train and jetty | LNG engineers, operations |
| Gas compression platforms | Compress associated gas for re-injection or export | Platform operations, compression engineers |

**Angola midstream example:** Block 0's Sanha LPG floating facility, Angola LNG (onshore, Soyo), and the export pipelines running from offshore blocks to shore.

**Offshore relevance:** Moderate. Export pipeline and [FPSO](../glossary.md) offloading roles are genuinely offshore. LNG terminals are typically onshore, though FLNG (floating LNG) is an emerging technology.

---

## Sector 4 — Downstream

**What it covers:** Refining crude oil into products (gasoline, diesel, jet fuel, LPG, petrochemicals) and distributing them to end users.

| Sub-sector | Key roles |
|---|---|
| Refining | Process Engineer, Refinery Operator, Chemical Engineer, Maintenance Engineer |
| Petrochemicals | Process Engineer, Product Quality Specialist |
| Fuels distribution | Logistics Engineer, Commercial Analyst |

**Offshore relevance:** Very low. Downstream is entirely onshore (refineries, storage tanks, filling stations). The main overlap with your degree is your **Refinery Technology** course, which prepares you for process engineer roles at refineries ([Sonangol](../../data/company-directory/sonangol.md)'s Luanda refinery, or the new Lobito Refinery when commissioned).

**Angola downstream context:** [Sonangol](../../data/company-directory/sonangol.md) Refinação operates the Luanda refinery (65,000 bpd). The Cabinda mini-refinery and the planned Lobito Refinery (240,000 bpd) represent future growth for downstream roles in Angola.

## Angola's Offshore Infrastructure

Angola operates **deep-water and ultra-deep-water** fields — among the most technically demanding offshore environments in the world. The FPSOs operating there are state-of-the-art.

### Offshore Blocks & Key FPSOs

| Block | Water Depth | Operator | Key FPSOs / Facilities |
|---|---|---|---|
| **Block 0** | Shallow (< 100m) | [Chevron](../../data/company-directory/chevron.md) | Sanha LPG, Mafumeira, GirAssol jacket platforms |
| **Block 15** | Deep (600–900m) | [Azule Energy](../../data/company-directory/azule-energy.md) | Kizomba A, Kizomba B, Kizomba Satellites, Mondo, Saxi-Batuque FPSOs |
| **Block 15/06** | Deep (700–1,000m) | [Eni](../../data/company-directory/eni.md) | FPSO N'Goma, FPSO Armada Sterling III |
| **Block 17** | Ultra-deep (1,000–1,400m) | [TotalEnergies](../../data/company-directory/totalenergies.md) | Girassol, Dalia, Pazflor, CLOV FPSOs — Angola's flagship block |
| **Block 31** | Ultra-deep (up to 2,000m) | [Azule](../../data/company-directory/azule-energy.md) Energy (BP operated) | [FPSO](../glossary.md) Cidade de Niterói |
| **Block 32** | Ultra-deep (1,700–2,150m) | [TotalEnergies](../../data/company-directory/totalenergies.md) | Kaombo Norte FPSO, Kaombo Sul FPSO |
| **Block 20** | Deep | [TotalEnergies](../../data/company-directory/totalenergies.md) | Cinguvu (development phase) |

> Block 17 alone produces ~500,000 bopd — one of the highest-producing offshore blocks in Africa.

### Offshore Installation Types You Will Encounter

| Type | Description | Depth range | Seen in Angola |
|---|---|---|---|
| **Jackup rig** | Three-legged mobile drilling rig, legs stand on seabed | Up to 150m | Block 0 (Cabinda shelf) |
| **Semi-submersible rig** | Floating rig stabilized by pontoons | 300–3,000m | All deepwater blocks |
| **Drillship** | Ship-shaped vessel for drilling | 300–3,600m | Exploration wells (new blocks) |
| **[FPSO](../glossary.md)** | Floating Production Storage Offloading vessel — the dominant asset in Angola | 500–2,500m | Blocks 17, 15, 32, 31, 15/06 |
| **Subsea tieback** | Wells on the seabed, tied back to an [FPSO](../glossary.md) via risers | Any depth | All deepwater blocks |
| **Platform (fixed jacket)** | Steel structure fixed to seabed — used only in shallow water | < 150m | Block 0 |

## Entry-Level Offshore Jobs: Where to Start

These are the **realistic first roles** for a petroleum engineering graduate who wants to be offshore as early as possible.

### 1. Field Engineer Trainee (OFS Graduate Programs)

**Who hires:** [SLB](../../data/company-directory/slb.md), [Halliburton](../../data/company-directory/halliburton.md), [Baker Hughes](../../data/company-directory/bakerhughes.md), Weatherford  
**What you do:** Rotate through product lines ([drilling fluids](../skills/03-drilling-fluids.md), cementing, wireline, perforating, [directional drilling](../skills/05-directional-drilling.md)). Start onshore for training, move offshore within 12–18 months.  
**Schedule:** Rotational — varies by assignment. Typically 28/28 or 14/14 offshore later in the program.  
**Pay (entry):** $40,000–$70,000/yr + offshore allowances (Angola equivalent varies; these companies pay internationally consistent rates).  
**Career path:** Trainee → Field Engineer → Sr. Field Engineer → Field Supervisor → Account Manager / Operations Manager

> **Best starting point for offshore.** [SLB](../../data/company-directory/slb.md)'s Surface, Drilling, and Well Services divisions all operate heavily in Angola. A graduate who starts at SLB Angola and earns offshore experience can transfer anywhere in the world within the SLB network.

---

### 2. Mudlogger / Wellsite Geologist

**Who hires:** Integrated Exploration Systems (IES), Wellware, [Halliburton](../../data/company-directory/halliburton.md) CoreLab, Geoservices ([SLB](../../data/company-directory/slb.md)), independent [mudlogging](../skills/07-mudlogging.md) companies  
**What you do:** Live on the drillship or rig, monitor drilling parameters, analyze rock cuttings, detect gas shows, update the drilling log, communicate formation data to the operator.  
**Why it's a great first offshore role:** You are on the rig 24/7 during drilling — maximum offshore exposure. Lower barrier to entry than full field engineer roles.  
**Schedule:** 28/28 on drillships and deepwater rigs.  
**Your curriculum match:** General Geology, [Formation Evaluation](../skills/04-formation-evaluation.md), Geochemistry — all directly applicable.  
**Career path:** Mudlogger → Senior Mudlogger → Wellsite Geologist → Operations Geologist (onshore) → Exploration Geologist

---

### 3. MWD / LWD Field Engineer (Measurement / Logging While Drilling)

**Who hires:** SLB (PowerDrive, ArcVision), [Halliburton](../../data/company-directory/halliburton.md) (Sperry Drilling), [Baker Hughes](../../data/company-directory/bakerhughes.md) (AutoTrak)  
**What you do:** Run and monitor downhole measurement tools that send formation data and directional data to surface in real-time while drilling. Work on the rig floor alongside drill crews.  
**Offshore:** This role is almost entirely offshore on deepwater rigs and drillships.  
**Your curriculum match:** [Formation Evaluation](../skills/04-formation-evaluation.md), Well Engineering, General Geology, Numerical Methods (data processing).  
**Career path:** [MWD](../glossary.md)/LWD Operator → Sr. MWD Engineer → Formation Evaluation Specialist → Geosteering Specialist

---

### 4. Production Operator / Process Operator (FPSO)

**Who hires:** [SBM Offshore](../../data/company-directory/sbmoffshore.md), Yinson, BW Offshore, MODEC — FPSO operators. Also directly by [TotalEnergies](../../data/company-directory/totalenergies.md), Eni, Azule Energy operations teams.  
**What you do:** Operate equipment on the [FPSO](../glossary.md) production deck — separators, manifolds, pumps, gas compressors, water injection systems, export metering. Follow operating procedures, perform routine inspections, respond to alarms.  
**Why it matters:** This is where **production engineering meets real operations**. Production Operators who understand the engineering behind the equipment advance fast.  
**Schedule:** 28/28, shift work (12-hour shifts).  
**Your curriculum match:** Surface Production Systems, Offshore Production Systems, Oil Flow, Natural Gas Engineering.  
**Career path:** Process Operator → Senior Operator → Control Room Operator → Operations Supervisor → Production Foreman → OIM (Offshore Installation Manager — the captain of the [FPSO](../glossary.md))

**OIM track:** The Offshore Installation Manager is the highest operational role offshore — responsible for 200+ people, a $1B+ asset, and all production, safety, and emergency decisions. The path from operator to OIM typically takes 10–15 years but is extremely well-compensated ($200,000–$400,000+/yr with offshore allowances in deepwater Angola).

---

### 5. HSE Officer (Offshore)

**Who hires:** All operators and contractors — HSE is non-negotiable offshore.  
**What you do:** Implement permit-to-work system, conduct safety inspections, deliver toolbox talks, investigate near-misses, manage emergency drills, ensure regulatory compliance.  
**Why it's a viable entry:** HSE roles offshore are abundant and relatively easier to enter than pure technical roles. They give you time on the installation while you gain experience.  
**Your curriculum match:** Safety Engineering, Environmental Engineering, Sustainability.  
**Fast track:** Get NEBOSH IGC + OPITO certificates before applying. This package combined with an engineering degree makes you stand out significantly.  
**Career path:** [Offshore HSE](../skills/12-offshore-hse-safety.md) Officer → HSE Supervisor → HSE Manager → Group HSE Director

---

### 6. Subsea Engineer / ROV Pilot (After Foundation Experience)

**Who hires:** [Oceaneering](../../data/company-directory/oceaneering.md), TechnipFMC, Subsea7, Baker Hughes Subsea  
**What you do:** Operate ROVs (Remotely Operated Vehicles) to inspect and maintain subsea infrastructure (pipelines, wellheads, manifolds, risers, umbilicals), support installation operations, inspect subsea trees.  
**Schedule:** 28/28 on DSVs (Dive Support Vessels) or construction vessels.  
**Why it's exciting:** You work on the most technically advanced equipment in the offshore world. Subsea engineers in Angola work at 1,000–2,000m water depth.  
**Entry path:** Usually requires 2–3 years of surface experience first (production, facilities, or inspection). [ROV](../glossary.md) pilots can start earlier with specific ROV training (IMCA certification).  
**Career path:** [ROV](../glossary.md) Pilot → Senior ROV Pilot → Subsea Technician → Subsea Engineer → Subsea Operations Manager

## Offshore Rotation Patterns

| Pattern | Schedule | Common in |
|---|---|---|
| **28/28** | 28 days offshore, 28 days onshore | FPSOs in Angola, most deepwater operations |
| **14/14** | 14 days offshore, 14 days onshore | Jackups, some service company roles |
| **21/21** | 21 days on, 21 days off | Some drilling contractors |
| **Hitching** | Irregular, short trips (2–5 days) | Engineers doing specific interventions or audits |

> Most **Angola deepwater** operations run **28/28**. You spend exactly half the year on the [FPSO](../glossary.md) or rig, and half at home. This is financially very efficient — you earn while offshore and spend very little (all food, accommodation, and transport are covered by the operator).

## Mandatory Certifications Before Going Offshore

These are non-negotiable — you cannot board an offshore installation without them:

| Certification | What it is | Where to get it | Validity |
|---|---|---|---|
| **[BOSIET](../glossary.md)** (Basic Offshore Safety Induction and Emergency Training) | Firefighting, first aid, sea survival, helicopter crash — the mandatory offshore safety passport | OPITO-approved training centers (available in Luanda) | 4 years |
| **[HUET](../glossary.md)** (Helicopter Underwater Escape Training) | Escape from a submerged helicopter simulator in a pool | Included in [BOSIET](../glossary.md) or standalone | 4 years |
| **Offshore Medical Certificate** | Full physical exam confirming fitness for offshore duty | OPITO/company-approved medics | 2 years |
| **[H2S](../glossary.md) Awareness** | Hazardous gas safety (hydrogen sulfide) — common in oil production | Any safety training provider | Varies |
| **[Well Control](../skills/01-well-control.md) — [IWCF](../glossary.md) Level 2** | Blowout prevention fundamentals — required for any role near a wellhead | IWCF centers (accredited in Luanda and online) | 2 years |

**Cost note:** [BOSIET](../glossary.md) + Offshore Medical in Angola costs approximately $500–$1,500 depending on provider. This is an investment that unlocks offshore work — pay it before you start applying.

**Additional for drilling / well services roles:**
- [IWCF](../glossary.md) Level 3 (Surface Stack) or Level 4 (Combined) for Drilling Engineers and Wellsite Supervisors
- Confined Space Entry, Work at Height, Rigging & Slinging

## Offshore Salary Premium

Offshore work pays more than the equivalent onshore role because of the demanding schedule and environment. In Angola's IOC (International Oil Company) context:

| Role | Typical Offshore Premium | Additional Benefits |
|---|---|---|
| Field Engineer (service co.) | +30–50% over onshore equivalent | Flights, meals, accommodation all covered |
| Production Operator ([FPSO](../glossary.md)) | +40–60% | All living costs during rotation = high net savings |
| Drilling Engineer (offshore) | +50–80% | Per diem, offshore supplement |
| Operations Supervisor / OIM track | +80–120% | Company housing allowance often provided in Luanda |

> **Real financial impact:** An Angola offshore engineer earning $60,000/yr effectively spends $0 for 6 months of the year (all costs covered offshore). Their net savings rate is far higher than an equivalent onshore engineer earning the same gross salary.

## Offshore vs. Onshore: Quick Decision Matrix

| Factor | Offshore | Onshore |
|---|---|---|
| Pay | Higher (30–100% premium) | Base pay |
| Savings rate | Very high (no living costs 50% of year) | Normal |
| Technical exposure | Intense — real operations under pressure | More theoretical / office |
| Work schedule | 12h shift / 28 days on | 8–9h / 5 days/week |
| Career acceleration | Faster (hands-on, responsibility early) | Slower in many cases |
| Health & fitness | Managed (gym available, no alcohol, regular meals) | Up to you |

| Geographic mobility | High — experience transfers globally | More localized |
