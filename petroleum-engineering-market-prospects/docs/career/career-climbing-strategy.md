Career climbing strategy � Tier 3 ? Tier 2 ? Tier 1 (5�7 year path)

You've got your degree. You're certified. You're ready. Now comes the long game.

Angola's offshore industry is structured in tiers. Understanding these tiers and how to climb them is the difference between a stalled career and a trajectory that takes you from a junior technician earning USD 1,000/month to a Senior Engineer at a major operator earning USD 15,000/month with benefits.

---

## Understanding the Tiers

### Tier 3: The Angolan Foundation
**Who they are:** Angolan-owned companies providing essential services�logistics, fabrication, maintenance, manpower supply.

**Examples in Angola:**
- **Algoa Cabinda Fabrication** � construction, maintenance, assembly (Cabinda-based)
- **[SONILS](../../data/company-directory/sonangol.md)** � logistics base operations, supply chain (Luanda-based, largest)
- **Operatec** � engineering services, equipment maintenance
- **[Petromar](../angola/angolan-offshore-companies.md)** � fabrication yards (Ambriz)
- **Brimont, Cabship, Poliedro, Prodoil** � various specialized services
- **Newrest Angola** � catering and camp management
- **Certex Angola** � lifting and lifting inspection

**What they do:** Execute the nuts-and-bolts work. They're subcontractors to Tier-2 companies or direct vendors to operators.

**Who works here:** Recent graduates, local technicians, transitioned manual laborers.

**Your role:** Junior technician, field assistant, quality inspector, operations coordinator.

**Salary:** USD 800�1,500/month base + offshore rotation bonus (5�10% per hitch).

**Why you should start here:** This is where you buy **experience** with your time. You learn the industry's language. You understand how rigs work. You build relationships with operators' company men. You prove reliability. After 1�2 years, you're no longer a liability�you're an asset.

### Tier 2: The International Bridge
**Who they are:** Major multinational oilfield service and engineering companies holding multi-million-dollar contracts.

**Examples contracting in Angola:**
- **[SLB](../../data/company-directory/slb.md) ([Schlumberger](../../data/company-directory/slb.md))** � drilling, wells, reservoir services
- **[Halliburton](../../data/company-directory/halliburton.md)** � cementing, completions, production enhancement
- **[Baker Hughes](../../data/company-directory/bakerhughes.md)** � completions, rotating equipment, artificial lift
- **[TechnipFMC](../../data/company-directory/technipfmc.md)** � subsea, SURF, engineering
- **[Subsea 7](../../data/company-directory/subsea7.md)** � subsea installation, pipelay
- **[Saipem](../../data/company-directory/saipem.md)** � EPC, fabrication, installation
- **[SBM Offshore](../../data/company-directory/sbmoffshore.md)** � FPSO lease and operate
- **[Yinson](../../data/company-directory/yinson.md)** � FPSO operations (Agogo)
- **[Oceaneering](../../data/company-directory/oceaneering.md)** � ROV, IMR, subsea services

**What they do:** They hold the **master contracts**. They hire project teams, run multi-year programs, deliver technical expertise.

**Who works here:** Engineers with 1�5 years experience, graduates from structured programs ([SLB](../../data/company-directory/slb.md)'s Field Engineer track, [Halliburton](../../data/company-directory/halliburton.md) LEAD program).

**Your role:** Field Engineer Trainee, Senior Field Engineer, Supervisor, Project Engineer.

**Salary:** USD 2,500�8,000/month base (depending on level) + offshore rotation allowance (USD 500�1,500/month offshore).

**Why you should aim here:** World-class training. You learn from **experts** who've worked on 50+ wells. You develop repeatable technical skills. You build a peer network that lasts your entire career. You're positioned for Tier-1 recruitment.

### Tier 1: The Operators
**Who they are:** The companies holding the oil blocks. The decision-makers. The risk-takers.

**Examples in Angola:**
- **[TotalEnergies](../../data/company-directory/totalenergies.md) E&P Angola** � operator in Blocks 17, 32, 20/11, etc. (largest producer)
- **[Chevron](../angola/angolan-offshore-companies.md) ([CABGOC](../angola/angolan-offshore-companies.md))** � Block 0, other assets (most "Angolan")
- **[ExxonMobil](../angola/angolan-offshore-companies.md) ([Esso Exploration Angola](../angola/angolan-offshore-companies.md))** � Block 15 (technical reputation)
- **[Azule Energy](../angola/angolan-offshore-companies.md) (BP/[Eni](../angola/angolan-offshore-companies.md) JV)** � Agogo project and others (newer, growth-focused)
- **[Sonangol](../../data/company-directory/sonangol.md) E&P (National Oil Company)** � partner stakes in most blocks (local perspective)

**What they do:** They own the blocks. They set strategy. They approve expenditure. They hire from Tier 2 or promote internally.

**Who works here:** Engineers with 3+ years experience at Tier 2, or direct graduates via structured programs ([TotalEnergies](../../data/company-directory/totalenergies.md) Young Graduate, Chevron Graduate Internship).

**Your role:** Drilling Engineer, Production Engineer, Reservoir Engineer, Project Manager, Superintendent.

**Salary:** USD 4,000�18,000/month base + comprehensive benefits (health, pension, education, housing allowance).

**Why you should aim here:** **Stability.** Long-term career. Operator perspective is invaluable. You move from "executing work" to "deciding work."

## The 5�7 Year Climb: Phase by Phase

### Years 1�2: The Foundation Phase (Tier 3)

**Goal:** Buy experience and build reputation. You're not looking for the best salary; you're looking for credibility.

**Target Employers (Priority Order):**
1. **Algoa Cabinda Fabrication** � direct connection to [Chevron](../angola/angolan-offshore-companies.md) ([CABGOC](../angola/angolan-offshore-companies.md)). Best stepping stone to Chevron or SLB.
2. **[SONILS](../../data/company-directory/sonangol.md)** � largest, most structured. Good training. Exposure to all operators.
3. **Operatec** � smaller but quality-focused. Good for specialists (completions, subsea).
4. **[Petromar](../angola/angolan-offshore-companies.md)** � if you're interested in fabrication and EPC track ([TechnipFMC](../../data/company-directory/technipfmc.md), Saipem).
5. **Certex, Brimont, Cabship** � smaller, but real work and networking.

**Target Roles:**
- *Junior Operations Technician* � supports offshore operations teams
- *Field Technical Assistant* � hands-on work on rigs or [FPSO](../glossary.md)
- *Quality Assurance Inspector* � equipment inspection, testing
- *Logistics Coordinator* � supply chain, procurement experience
- *Equipment Maintenance Technician* � learn how subsea or surface equipment works

**Critical Actions:**

**? Get your paperwork right (Day 1):**
- [BOSIET](../glossary.md)/HUET certificate (valid)
- Offshore medical certificate (valid)
- [H2S](../glossary.md) Awareness certificate
- [IWCF](../glossary.md) Level 1 Well Control certification
- CV in Portuguese and English
- Police clearance (if required)

**? Land the job (Weeks 1�4):**
- Contact HR directly: email + phone call + LinkedIn message
- Keep your pitch simple: *"Recent petroleum engineering graduate, [BOSIET](../glossary.md)/[HUET](../glossary.md) valid, ready to mobilize."*
- Be flexible on salary. You're buying **experience**, not wealth. Accept USD 1,000�1,200/month if that's the offer.
- On interviews, emphasize: reliability, willingness to learn, safety mindset, language skills (Portuguese + English).

**? Perform from Day 1:**
- Show up on time. Always. Angola culture respects punctuality.
- Ask intelligent questions. Don't ask the same question twice.
- Keep a technical notebook. Write down what you learn every day.
- **Build relationships with the Company Man (operator's representative) you work alongside.** This person is your "gateway drug" to Tier 2.

**? Get your certs while working:**
- After 3�6 months, start studying for **[IWCF](../glossary.md) Level 2 (Supervisor)** certification. It's a 5-day course, ~USD 1,500. Ask your employer if they'll sponsor it (many Tier-3 companies will, as it increases your market value and theirs).
- Push for **[MWD](../glossary.md)/LWD Fundamentals** or **Drilling Fluids** training if your role allows it. This specialization will help you jump to Tier 2.

**? Network like your career depends on it (because it does):**
- After 6�9 months, identify someone at a Tier-2 company ([SLB](../../data/company-directory/slb.md), [Halliburton](../../data/company-directory/halliburton.md), [Baker Hughes](../../data/company-directory/bakerhughes.md)) who's impressed with your work.
- Invite them for coffee or lunch (offsite, not on the rig).
- Ask open-ended questions: *"What does a career path look like in your company? What do you look for in junior engineers?"*
- Stay in touch. Send them a message every 3 months. Show genuine interest in their work, not just their hiring power.

**? Document your wins:**
- Keep a log of wells you worked on, daily production data you contributed to, safety incidents prevented, cost savings achieved.
- In 2 years, you'll have a portfolio of 15�20 wells and projects. This is your **proof of work**.

**Outcome after Years 1�2:**
- You've worked on 15�20 wells or projects
- You have 2�3 endorsements from Company Men or Tier-2 supervisors
- You're confident offshore
- You're ready for Tier 2

### Years 3�5: The Bridge Phase (Tier 2)

**Goal:** Get structured training. Learn from experts. Position yourself for Tier 1.

**How to make the jump:**
This is critical. You don't apply cold to [Halliburton](../../data/company-directory/halliburton.md)'s LinkedIn page and expect a callback. You leverage your network.

**Strategy A: Leverage your Tier-3 relationships**
- Reach out to the Company Man or supervisor you built a relationship with in Years 1�2.
- Tell them: *"I've learned a lot here, and I'm ready to take on more technical responsibility. If you know of any Field Engineer Trainee positions opening up with [Service Company], I'd appreciate a referral."*
- A warm referral from someone that service company respects is worth 100 cold applications.

**Strategy B: Direct LinkedIn outreach**
- Search for [SLB](../../data/company-directory/slb.md) / Halliburton / [Baker Hughes](../../data/company-directory/bakerhughes.md) recruiters in Angola.
- Review their profile. Message them saying you're interested in Field Engineer roles and have Tier-3 experience.
- Attach a CV emphasizing: [IWCF](../glossary.md) Level 1/2, specific wells worked on, language skills, demonstrated safety culture.

**Strategy C: Attend industry events**
- SPE Angola events (Luanda, usually quarterly)
- IADC [well control](../skills/01-well-control.md) and drilling conferences
- Angola Oil & Gas symposia
- These are where service company recruiters literally hunt for junior talent.

**Target Employers (Priority):**
1. **[SLB](../../data/company-directory/slb.md) ([Schlumberger](../../data/company-directory/slb.md))** � largest, best training, most structured. Their **3-year Field Engineer Program** is the gold standard.
2. **[Halliburton](../../data/company-directory/halliburton.md)** � similar to SLB, strong in cementing/completions.
3. **[Baker Hughes](../../data/company-directory/bakerhughes.md)** � strong in completions and artificial lift.
4. **[TechnipFMC](../../data/company-directory/technipfmc.md)** � if you're interested in subsea/[SURF](../glossary.md) engineering (project-based but intense).
5. **[Subsea 7](../../data/company-directory/subsea7.md), Saipem** � EPC contractors; good if you want fabrication/installation experience.

**Target Roles (Entry to Tier 2):**
- *Field Engineer Trainee* � rotational. 28/28 schedule. USD 2,500�3,500/month + offshore allowance.
- *[MWD](../glossary.md)/[LWD](../glossary.md) Field Engineer* � specialized drilling. Fast-track to senior roles. USD 3,000�4,000/month.
- *Completions Field Engineer* � strong demand in Angola. USD 2,800�3,800/month.
- *Production Test Engineer* � if you like reservoir-focused work. USD 2,500�3,500/month.
- *Subsea Installation Engineer* � project-based; intense, well-paid. USD 3,500�5,000/month.

**Critical Actions:**

**? Negotiate carefully:**
- Tier 2 companies will offer you more money, yes, but the real value is **training.**
- Prioritize companies with structured training programs ([SLB](../../data/company-directory/slb.md)'s 3-year curriculum is legendary).
- Ask about: mentorship structure, overseas training opportunities, certification sponsorship.
- Try to negotiate a "sponsorship" agreement: the company will pay for your [IWCF](../glossary.md) Level 2 upgrade, any software training (OLGA, PROSPER), in exchange for a 2�3 year commitment.

**? Excel in training:**
- [SLB](../../data/company-directory/slb.md)'s Field Engineer training includes classroom + rig assignments. It's intense. Treat it like grad school.
- Your performance in the first 6 months is *critical.* This determines your trajectory in the company.
- If you're in the top 20% of your training cohort, you get premium assignments and mentorship.

**? Build a mentor (your *Padrinho*):**
- Within your first 6 months at Tier 2, identify a senior engineer (5�10 years experience) whose technical judgment you respect.
- Ask them to grab coffee or lunch every 2 months.
- Ask for advice on career, technical challenges, industry trends.
- **This relationship is worth USD 100,000 in your career over the next decade. Treat it accordingly. Don't ask for big favors. Just be a good mentee.**

**? Specialize:**
- By Year 3�4, you should be known for something specific: [MWD](../glossary.md) interpretation, gas lift optimization, subsea tree commissioning.
- Depth > breadth at Tier 2. Be the person everyone calls for [specific expertise].

**? Build your reputation with operators:**
- When you're deployed on a rig or [FPSO](../glossary.md), the **company man** (operator's representative) is watching you.
- If you impress them, they will ask your manager: *"When his contract is up, is he available for a Tier-1 position?"*
- This is how people get "poached" to operators.

**Outcome after Years 3�5:**
- You've worked on 30�50 wells at Tier 2
- You have specialized expertise ([MWD](../glossary.md), completions, subsea, etc.)
- You have 2�3 mentors / relationships with senior engineers
- You have relationships with operator company men who respect your technical judgment
- You're ready for Tier 1

### Years 5�7: The Destination Phase (Tier 1)

**Goal:** Transition to a direct-hire position with an operator. Stability. Strategic thinking. Long-term career.

**How it happens (the "seamless transition"):**
By Year 5, there's often an operator company man or engineer who's seen your work over 2�3 years. They trust you. They've thought, *"I want this person on my permanent team."* They advocate internally.

One day, HR calls: *"We have a Drilling Engineer position opening. Interested?"*

This is the target scenario. It's rare, but it happens to people who executed well in Years 1�4.

**If it doesn't happen organically:**

**Strategy A: Formal application**
- Monitor operator careers pages ([TotalEnergies](../../data/company-directory/totalenergies.md), Chevron, ExxonMobil all hire experienced engineers).
- Direct applications with a strong cover letter highlighting specific wells you've worked on in Angola.
- Emphasize: you know the geology, you know the operators' team, you've proven yourself in the field.

**Strategy B: Recruiter outreach**
- Tier-1 operators sometimes use external recruiters for senior engineer positions.
- Have your Tier-2 manager or mentor refer you to a recruiter.

**Target Roles (Entry to Tier 1):**
- *Drilling Engineer* � USD 4,500�7,000/month
- *Production Engineer* � USD 4,500�7,000/month
- *Reservoir Engineer* � USD 4,500�7,000/month
- *Project Engineer* � USD 5,000�8,000/month
- *Team Lead / Superintendent* (if you have 5+ years experience) � USD 8,000�15,000/month

**Critical Actions:**

**? Cultivate your *Padrinho* (mentor/sponsor):**
- By Tier-1, your mentor should be someone senior at an operator or someone you deeply respect at Tier 2 who has operator connections.
- They will advocate for you internally when opportunities arise.
- This is not a transactional relationship. It's built on mutual respect and genuine interest in your success.
- Check in quarterly. Ask for advice. Offer help if you can.

**? Develop strategic thinking:**
- Tier 1 is different. You're not executing work; you're deciding work.
- Start reading operator strategy documents, annual reports, block development plans.
- Understand business drivers: production targets, cost control, shareholder return.
- In interviews, show that you think like a business operator, not just a technician.

**? Negotiate aggressively (but professionally):**
- Tier-1 salaries are negotiable. They have room.
- Counter-offer their initial offer. Request benefits clarification (pension, housing allowance, education fund).
- Ask about career progression: will you be tracked for superintendent roles, manager roles, etc.?

**? Understand the politics:**
- Tier 1 is corporate. There's office politics, internal competition, strategic alignment.
- Spend your first 6 months understanding the power structure, decision-making processes, and key stakeholders.
- Choose your battles. Focus on technical excellence and relationship-building, not internal feuds.

**Outcome after Years 5�7:**
- You're a permanent Tier-1 engineer
- You have stability and long-term career growth
- You earn USD 5,000�15,000+/month depending on level
- You have comprehensive benefits (health, pension, education, housing)
- You're positioned for superintendent, manager, or director roles in the next 5�10 years

## Critical Skills to Develop (Throughout All Tiers)

### Technical
- **Pressure regimes** in Angola's pre-salt environment
- **Drilling operations** (ROP, torque, pressure, BOP, [well control](../skills/01-well-control.md))
- **Production optimization** (nodal analysis, [artificial lift](../skills/09-artificial-lift.md), flow assurance)
- **Well design** (casing, mud weight, completion strategy)
- **[Subsea systems](../skills/11-subsea-systems.md)** (trees, manifolds, umbilicals, control modules)
- **[FPSO](../glossary.md) operations** (separation, compression, export)
- **Software proficiency** (PROSPER, GAP, OLGA, reservoir simulation, spreadsheet mastery)

### Soft Skills
- **Communication** � writing, presenting, explaining technical concepts to non-engineers
- **Problem-solving** � systematic approach, quick decision-making under pressure
- **Relationship-building** � winning trust, mentoring junior engineers, managing up and down
- **Safety mindset** � rule-following, error prevention, transparency about risks
- **Bilingual fluency** � Portuguese and English. This is non-negotiable in Angola.
- **Adaptability** � comfort with change, new tools, new teams, new processes

### Certifications (over time)
- Year 1�2: [BOSIET](../glossary.md), IWCF Level 1, H2S, First Aid, OSHA 30
- Year 2�3: [IWCF](../glossary.md) Level 2 (Supervisor)
- Year 3�5: [Well Control](../skills/01-well-control.md) Instructor (if on drilling track), [Subsea systems](../skills/11-subsea-systems.md) cert (if on subsea track), [Flow Assurance](../skills/10-flow-assurance.md) modeling
- Year 5+: Management courses, MBA (optional but valued at Tier 1)

## Final Thoughts

This path is not fast. It's not glamorous. In Year 1, you'll be making USD 1,000/month while your university classmates in Luanda are in office jobs making USD 1,500 and never setting foot on a helicopter. That's hard.

But if you execute the plan�if you build relationships, specialize, and leverage your network�you'll be earning 10x what they make by Year 7. And you'll have stories they can't imagine.

The engineers who make it to senior roles in Angola did not get there by being the smartest. They got there by being **reliable, curious, and politically savvy.** They showed up. They asked good questions. They built relationships with people more senior. They moved when opportunities opened.

You can do this. Now go climb.

Networking here is about building genuine trust over time, not just collecting business cards.

*   **Key Industry Events:**
    *   **Angola Oil & Gas (AOG) Conference:** This is the premier event. The next edition is scheduled for **September 9-10, 2026, in Luanda**. This is where to go. Don't try to meet the CEO. ### ?? Part 5: Potential Pitfalls & How to Avoid Them

This path has real challenges. Being aware of them is the first step to overcoming them.

*   **Getting Stuck in Tier 3:** It can be comfortable. The pay might be decent, and you know the people. **How to Avoid:** Always have your next goal in sight. After 18-24 months, actively start applying to Tier-2 roles. Your Tier-3 experience is a stepping stone, not a destination.

*   **Burnout from Poor Rotation:** You might end up on a rig with a brutal 6-weeks-on/2-weeks-off schedule. **How to Avoid:** After proving your value for a year, you have leverage. Have a professional conversation with your supervisor. Frame it around safety and long-term performance: *"I'm concerned that the current rotation may lead to fatigue and affect my performance. I'd like to discuss options for a more sustainable schedule."*

*   **Navigating Expatriate Dynamics:** There may be times you feel overlooked in favor of an expat. **How to Handle:** Do not complain. Instead, use your unique advantage: you are Angolan. You know the local context, the culture, and the language (Portuguese and local dialects). When an issue arises that requires navigating local bureaucracy or community relations, you are the expert. Position yourself as an indispensable bridge between the international company and the Angolan reality.

*   **The "Fake Job Offer" Trap:** This is a real and pervasive problem in Angola. **How to Avoid:** Legitimate oil companies **never** ask for money for a job application, visa, or training. Be wary of job offers from generic email addresses (Gmail, Yahoo) instead of official company domains. If an offer seems too good to be true, it is. Always verify the job posting on the official company careers page. [Sonangol](../../data/company-directory/sonangol.md) has specifically warned the public about fake recruitment announcements.

## Appendix: Drilling Engineer Progression & Technical Readiness

> *Merged from offshore-work-lifestyle.md � year-by-year technical milestones, knowledge checklists, and how to stand out.*

### Part 1: The 5-Year Progression Timeline (Year-by-Year)

This is your ladder. Miss a rung, and you'll be playing catch-up.

*   **Year 1: Junior Drilling Engineer / Well Site Drilling Engineer**
    *   **Location:** 80% **Offshore (Rig)**, 20% Onshore (Luanda Office).
    *   **Primary Responsibilities:** You are a data sponge. You will master the IADC morning report. You will track the cost per foot (CPF) and watch Non-Productive Time (NPT) like a hawk. You will assist in [BHA](../glossary.md) make-up on the rig floor and learn to read a mud report and shaker screens.
    *   **Milestone for Promotion:** Complete your first full well as a fully responsible "night company man" (under supervision). Demonstrate you can spot a kick indicator before the driller does. Get signed off on [IWCF](../glossary.md) Level 2.

*   **Year 2: Drilling Engineer**
    *   **Location:** 60% Onshore (Luanda Office), 40% Offshore (Rig).
    *   **Primary Responsibilities:** You're now designing casing strings. You'll run simulations in **WellPlan** (torque/drag) and **StressCheck** (casing design). You'll write the detailed drilling program for the next well. You're also managing the bit program and doing the initial AFE (Authorization for Expenditure) cost tracking.
    *   **Milestone for Promotion:** Design and successfully execute a full 20-inch surface casing job, including cementing calculations, without major NPT. Be the primary author of a complete End-of-Well (EOW) report.

*   **Year 3: Senior Drilling Engineer**
    *   **Location:** 80% Onshore, 20% Offshore (Critical Operations Only).
    *   **Primary Responsibilities:** You are the well planner. You lead the peer review for your well design. You handle the more complex calculations, like ECD (Equivalent Circulating Density) management in narrow-margin deepwater wells. You start managing the relationship with the **Toolpusher** and the service company coordinators.
    *   **Milestone for Promotion:** Design a complex, multi-lateral well or an extended-reach well from scratch. Successfully manage a lost circulation event without losing the hole. [IWCF](../glossary.md) Level 4 certification.

*   **Year 4: Senior Drilling Engineer (Lead)**
    *   **Location:** 95% Onshore, 5% Offshore (Troubleshooting).
    *   **Primary Responsibilities:** You're the technical authority for your junior engineers. You lead the review of their casing designs and well plans. You're managing the AFE budget for a multi-well campaign. You present the drilling plan to the **Drilling Manager** and partners (like [Sonangol](../../data/company-directory/sonangol.md) P&P).
    *   **Milestone for Promotion:** Deliver a well under budget and under the planned time curve. Demonstrate you can mentor a junior engineer to competence.

*   **Year 5: Drilling Team Lead / Principal Drilling Engineer (or Transition to Production)**
    *   **Location:** 100% Onshore.
    *   **Primary Responsibilities:** You're looking at the bigger picture. You're involved in the [FEED](../glossary.md) (Front-End Engineering Design) study for the next field development. You're evaluating new drilling technologies and contractors. This is the year you can **pivot to Production Engineering** by volunteering for completion design or workover planning.
    *   **Milestone for Transition to Production:** Lead the design and execution of a well completion or a major workover. Volunteer for the next subsea tree installation. If Research is your goal, this is when you apply for a Master's degree (often sponsored by your Operator) focusing on Production Technology or Subsea Engineering.

### Part 2: The "Essential Knowledge & Abilities Checklist" (To Be Built *Before* You Go)

This is what separates the engineer who gets promoted from the one who gets stuck on the rig doing paperwork.

**2.1 Technical Foundation (Hard Skills)**
*   **[Well Control](../skills/01-well-control.md):** Understand [IWCF](../glossary.md) principles, kick tolerance, and BOP stack arrangements. You must know the difference between Driller's Method and Wait-and-Weight method in your sleep.
    *   **Build It:** Take the free **[IWCF](../glossary.md) Level 1 Awareness Course** online. Download and study the IWCF Drilling Well Control syllabus.
*   **Casing Design:** Understand burst, collapse, and tensile loads. Be able to sketch a casing program from a pore pressure/frac gradient plot.
    *   **Build It:** Get the textbook **"Applied Drilling Engineering" by Bourgoyne et al.** Read Chapters 6 and 7 cover-to-cover.
*   **[Drilling Fluids](../skills/03-drilling-fluids.md):** Interpret a mud report: density, YP, PV. Understand lost circulation material (LCM) types and when to use them.
    *   **Build It:** Free webinars from **MI-Swaco** (an [SLB](../../data/company-directory/slb.md) company) on drilling fluid basics.
*   **[Directional Drilling](../skills/05-directional-drilling.md):** Understand KOP (Kick-Off Point) selection, build rates, and [BHA](../glossary.md) design.
    *   **Build It:** Watch free videos on the **DrillingFormulas.com** website.
*   **Torque & Drag, Hydraulics:** ECD management is critical in Angola's deepwater, narrow-margin wells.
    *   **Build It:** Download the free **Dr. Shing's Hydraulics Calculator** Excel sheet and practice.
*   **Bit Selection & Dull Grading:** Learn the **IADC Dull Grading System**. A worn bit tells a story about the formation.
    *   **Build It:** Print out an IADC dull grading chart and keep it in your hard hat.
*   **Cementing:** Primary cementing calculations, top of cement (TOC) estimation.
    *   **Build It:** Practice calculations from **"Well Cementing" by Nelson & Guillot**.
*   **[Formation Evaluation](../skills/04-formation-evaluation.md):** Understand [LWD](../glossary.md) (Logging While Drilling) vs. Wireline. Know why you need to keep the hole clean for a logging run.
    *   **Build It:** Take a free online course on Petrophysics from **PetroWiki (SPE)** .

**2.2 Software & Automation Proficiency (The "Allowed" Automation)**

*   **Mandatory Software:**
    *   **Landmark OpenWells:** For morning reporting. This is non-negotiable.
    *   **WellPlan:** For torque/drag and hydraulics modeling.
    *   **Compass:** For directional well planning.
    *   **StressCheck:** For casing and tubing design.
*   **What Automation *Am I Allowed* to Do?**
    *   ? **SAFE & ENCOURAGED:**
        *   Build an **Excel Macro** to auto-generate hydraulics/ECD calculation tables for the morning report.
        *   Create a **PowerPoint Template** for End-of-Well (EOW) reports. A clean, clear EOW report is your best calling card.
        *   Write a **Python script** to clean and plot data from the Mud Logger's files (e.g., plot ROP vs. Depth).
        *   Automate **AFE tracking** sheets with Excel formulas.
    *   ? **DO NOT TOUCH:**
        *   **Do NOT attempt to automate real-time drilling parameter alarms or suggest mud weight changes via a script.** That is a Senior Drilling Supervisor's responsibility based on 20 years of experience, not a Python model. Doing this will get you fired.

**2.3 Physical & Mental Conditioning**

*   **Physical:** You will climb 10-20 flights of stairs per day in 35�C heat and 90% humidity. The vibration on the rig floor will exhaust you.
    *   **Regimen:** Start doing **stair climbing with a weighted backpack**. Focus on **core strength and grip strength**. If you can't hold onto a drill pipe sample for 30 seconds, you'll be useless.
*   **Mental:**
    *   **Sleep Hygiene:** The cabin is a tin can. **Invest in Bose QuietComfort or Sony WH-1000XM noise-canceling headphones.** They are worth their weight in gold. Bring **Magnesium Glycinate** supplements to help with sleep regulation. A **Kindle loaded with 20+ books** is mandatory. You will have days where the internet is dead.
    *   **The "Rig Fever":** After Day 18, everyone gets irritable. The best way to combat it is a strict routine: Work > Eat > Gym > Sleep > Repeat.

**2.4 The "Silent Skill Gap" (Standing Out Beyond Technical Competence)**

As the SPE blog points out, technical skills are now the baseline. You differentiate yourself with **context**: commercial, regulatory, and operational understanding.

*   **Communication:** Practice explaining what a "pack-off" is to your grandmother. If you can explain a complex well plan to a Finance Manager in 5 minutes using simple English (or Portuguese), you will be promoted. Practice in the mirror.
*   **Commercial Awareness:** Learn to read an **AFE (Authorization for Expenditure)** . Understand the difference between CAPEX (the well cost) and OPEX (the operating cost). Learn what **NPT (Non-Productive Time)** really costs: a drillship like the Valaris DS-9 costs over $400,000 per day. Every minute of downtime is a financial hemorrhage.
*   **Decision-Making Under Uncertainty:** Train your brain. If the ROP (Rate of Penetration) drops by 50% in the *Pinda* formation, what are the top 3 things you check *before* calling the OIM? 1) **Mud weight in/out** (Is it a kick?), 2) **Torque** (Is the string stalling?), 3) **Cuttings at the shaker** (Has the lithology changed?).
*   **Relationship Building:** The **Toolpusher** and **Driller** have been doing this since you were in primary school. Your degree is worthless if you don't show them respect. Listen more than you talk. Buy them a coffee from the galley. **Ask them: "In your experience, what's the best way to trip pipe in this hole section?"** That one question will earn you more respect than any Excel spreadsheet.

### ?? Part 2: Phase 1 - Foundation (Detailed Module Breakdown)

This is where you build the muscle memory of a drilling engineer. **You do not need to pay for expensive courses.**

**?? Module 1: Mastering [Well Control](../skills/01-well-control.md) (The Non-Negotiable)**

This is the one topic that will get you fired or get people killed if you ignore it. **Start here today.**

*   **Goal:** Understand kick detection, shut-in procedures, and the BOP stack function.
*   **Source 1:** **[IWCF](../glossary.md) Level 1 Well Control Awareness Course**. This is the industry-standard free online course created by the International Well Control Forum. It covers the lifecycle of a well, drilling rigs, and well control during drilling operations. It's perfect for students and takes about 4-6 hours to complete. **You will receive a certificate. Put it on your LinkedIn and CV.**
*   **Source 2:** **[IWCF](../glossary.md) Well Control & Pressure Control Calculations**. This is a separate free e-learning resource specifically for building confidence and accuracy in calculations (volumes, pressures).
*   **How to Train:**
    1.  **Step 1:** Complete the IWCF Level 1 course. Do not just click through. Take notes on the **BOP Stack Arrangement** and the difference between **Primary and Secondary Well Control**.
    2.  **Step 2:** **The "What-If" Game.** Every time you see a drilling diagram, ask: *"If the mud pump pressure drops by 200 psi, what are the top 3 things I check before touching anything?"*
    3.  **Step 3:** Download the free IWCF Calculations course and practice 5 problems a week.

**?? Module 2: The Drilling Engineer's Bible (Core Knowledge)**

You need a textbook. Not a PDF you save and forget. A book you read until the spine breaks.

*   **Goal:** Internalize casing design, hydraulics, bit selection, and directional [drilling fundamentals](../skills/02-drilling-fundamentals.md).
*   **Primary Source:** **"Applied Drilling Engineering" by Bourgoyne, Millheim, Chenevert, and Young (SPE Textbook Series, Vol. 2)** . This is the definitive text. It covers rotary drilling, [drilling fluids](../skills/03-drilling-fluids.md), cements, drilling hydraulics, bits, pore pressure/fracture resistance, casing design, and [directional drilling](../skills/05-directional-drilling.md).
*   **How to Obtain It:**
    1.  Check your university library (they often have digital access via OnePetro).
    2.  Search for it on **OnePetro.org** (the SPE's official repository).
    3.  Explore **Anna's Archive** for a free digital copy.
    4.  Search **Docsity** for student-uploaded study notes on the book.
*   **How to Train (Step-by-Step):**
    *   **Week 1-2:** Read Chapter 4 (Drilling Hydraulics). Do not just read. Open Excel and build a **Hydraulic Calculator** to compute pressure drop in the drill pipe and annulus. (See Module 3 for templates).
    *   **Week 3-4:** Read Chapter 7 (Casing Design). Sketch a typical casing program for Block 17 (36" Conductor, 20" Surface, 13 3/8" Intermediate, 9 5/8" Production Liner). Calculate the burst and collapse loads for the 9 5/8" section.
    *   **Week 5-6:** Read Chapter 8 (Directional Drilling). Understand the difference between **Measured Depth (MD)** and **True Vertical Depth (TVD)** . Learn what a **Kick-Off Point (KOP)** and **Build Rate** mean.

**?? Module 3: Building Your Own Tools (The Excel Wizardry)**

Senior engineers hate data entry. If you can automate it, you become their favorite person.

*   **Goal:** Build a suite of Excel spreadsheets that replicate expensive software.
*   **Source 1:** **Drilling Manual Hydraulics Calculator**. Download this free Excel template from `www.drillingmanual.com`. It includes sheets for casing, drill pipes, [MWD](../glossary.md), motors, and bit TFA calculations. **Do not just use it�reverse engineer it.** Open the formulas and figure out *why* the pressure drop increases when you change the mud weight.
*   **Source 2:** **Sargent's Drilling Utilities**. A freeware Excel program that performs day-to-day calculations for the "man on the rig."
*   **Source 3:** **Drilling Formulas Spreadsheets**. From `www.pipingguide.net`, download the "Drilling Spreadsheets" pack.
*   **How to Train:**
    1.  **Step 1:** Download the "Drilling Hydraulics Calculator" from Drilling Manual.
    2.  **Step 2:** Find a **Pore Pressure / Fracture Gradient Plot** for a generic deepwater well (search Google Images: "Deepwater Pore Pressure Plot").
    3.  **Step 3:** Using the Bourgoyne book formulas, build a **Casing Design spreadsheet** from scratch that takes the pore pressure data and tells you the minimum casing shoe depth.
