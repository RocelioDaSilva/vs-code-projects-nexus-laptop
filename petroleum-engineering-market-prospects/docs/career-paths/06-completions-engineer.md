# Career Path: Completions & Well Intervention Engineer

> A step-by-step guide from graduation to senior completions/well intervention engineer in Angola.
> Estimated timeline: 5–7 years from entry-level to senior roles.

---

## Why Completions & Well Intervention?

After a well is drilled, the **completion** determines whether it will produce efficiently. Completions engineers design the interface between the reservoir and the surface — perforations, sand control, tubing, packers, safety valves, and intelligent completions. In Angola's deepwater, a single completion can cost $20M–$50M+, making this a high-stakes, high-value discipline.

Well intervention is the other side of the coin: fixing, optimizing, and extending the life of existing wells. With Angola's fleet of 400+ producing wells aging, and production declining from its 1.8M bbl/d peak, intervention engineers are increasingly critical for maintaining output.

4. **[H2S](../glossary.md) Awareness** — Required for wellhead operations (~$500)
5. **Slickline / Wireline Basics** — Some providers offer short courses

- **[Sonamet](../../data/company-directory/sonamet.md)** — Workshop experience with subsea/surface completions equipment
- **[Petromar](../../data/company-directory/petromar.md)** — Well services support
- **Local well service companies** — Slickline, wireline, perforating operations

### Target Roles
- Completions equipment handler / workshop assistant
- Wireline trainee / slickline trainee
- Well services helper (perforating, setting plugs)
- Wellhead maintenance technician

### Skills to Study

### Action Items

## Phase 3: Specialization (Year 3–5)

### Goal
Transition into well engineering roles at operators or become a specialist completions engineer. Design completions, don't just run them.

### Target Roles
- Completions Design Engineer (operator wells team)
- Well Intervention Engineer / Campaign Lead
- Sand Control Specialist
- Intelligent Completions Engineer
- Plug & Abandonment (P&A) Engineer

### Specialization Tracks

#### Track A: Completions Design
- Design entire completion strings for new wells
- Optimize sand control: gravel pack vs. frac pack vs. standalone screens
- Specify tubing, packers, safety valves, chemical injection mandrels
- Interface with reservoir engineers on completion strategy
- Design intelligent completions with ICDs/AICDs/ICVs

#### Track B: Well Intervention & Workover
- Plan and execute production enhancement interventions
- Wireline operations: logging, perforating, plug setting/pulling
- Coiled tubing operations: cleanouts, stimulation, nitrogen lifts
- Workover planning: kill well, pull completion, re-complete
- Plug & Abandonment: permanent barrier installation, regulatory compliance

### Skills to Deepen
- **Intelligent Completions** — ICDs (Inflow Control Devices), AICDs (Autonomous), ICVs (Interval Control Valves)
- **Sand Control Design** — Gravel pack sizing (PSD analysis), frac pack design, screen selection
- **Tubing Stress Analysis** — Thermal expansion, ballooning, buckling in deepwater completions
- **Stimulation**: Matrix acidizing, acid fracturing (less common in Angola deepwater but growing)
- **P&A Engineering** — NORSOK D-010 compliance, permanent barrier design, cement plugs
- **Completion Economics** — Cost-benefit of intelligent completions vs. conventional

### Software to Master
| Software | Purpose |
|---|---|
| **WellCat (advanced)** | Full tubing stress analysis, load cases |
| **PROSPER / PIPESIM** | Complete well modeling with completion sensitivity |
| **Abaqus / ANSYS** | Advanced structural analysis (critical connections, sand screens) |
| **Python** | Automation of completion design calculations |
| **SSSM / TubeSim** | Tubing movement and stress analysis |

### Action Items
- [ ] Design a completion from scratch: wellbore → completion string → perforation → flow test
- [ ] Lead or co-lead a well intervention campaign (wireline or coiled tubing)
- [ ] Study intelligent completion case studies — Block 17 CLOV uses ICDs/ICVs
- [ ] Understand P&A regulations (NORSOK D-010, UK OGUK guidelines, ANPG requirements)
- [ ] Present completion optimization results at SPE or company technical forums
- [ ] Build relationships with operator wells teams for career transition

## Angola-Specific Context

### Completions Challenges in Angola Deepwater
| Challenge | Description | Impact |
|---|---|---|
| **Sand production** | Unconsolidated turbidite sands require sand control on most wells | Gravel packs are the norm; frac packs growing |
| **Long completions** | Multiple pay zones in thick reservoir sections | Long multi-zone completions with ICDs/ICVs |
| **Deepwater pressures** | High hydrostatic pressures complicate completion fluid design | Heavy brines (CaBr₂/ZnBr₂) needed |
| **Subsea trees** | All Block 17/31/32 wells have subsea Xmas trees | Completion must interface with [TechnipFMC](../../data/company-directory/technipfmc.md)/Aker tree designs |
| **HPHT wells** | Some Block 32 wells are High Pressure High Temperature | Specialized materials, elastomers, metallurgy required |
| **Gas lift integration** | Many wells use gas lift — mandrels must be in completion | Gas lift design integrated into completion design |
| **Water injection wells** | Pressure maintenance requires injection completions | Different completion design for injectors vs. producers |

### Key Completions Data for Angola
- **Primary sand control:** Gravel pack (most common), frac pack (growing), standalone screens
- **[Artificial lift](../skills/09-artificial-lift.md):** ESP (dominant), gas lift (secondary)
- **Intelligent completions:** Used on CLOV (Block 17), Kaombo (Block 32), and new developments
- **Intervention methods:** Wireline (most common), coiled tubing, snubbing (rare), workover rigs
- **P&A needs:** Increasing as Block 0 and older Block 15 wells approach end of life
