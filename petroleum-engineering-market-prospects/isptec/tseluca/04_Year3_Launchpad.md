# Year 3 Launchpad — Your Semester-by-Semester Action Plan

> You are in Year 3 of petroleum engineering at ISPTEC. You have approximately 4–6 semesters left before graduation. This file gives you a concrete action plan for each of those semesters — what to study, what certifications to pursue, which companies to target, and what to do in your free time to build the profile that gets you hired into an offshore rotation role.

## Year 3 — Semester 5 Action Plan

*Typical subjects: Engenharia de Reservatórios I, Mecânica das Rochas, Fluidos de Perfuração e Completação, Engenharia de Poços, Engenharia Econômica*

### This Semester's Priority: DECIDE YOUR PATH

This is the semester where you need to pick your career track. You now have enough technical background to make an informed choice. Go read [02_Six_Paths_Decoded.md](02_Six_Paths_Decoded.md) and score yourself on the path selection test.

**Decision deadline:** End of Semester 5. You have time to change later, but a decision now means focused study starting now.

### Academic Strategy

**Engenharia de Poços:** This is a career subject for drilling and completions engineers. Do not just pass it — master it. Understand:
- Well construction sequence (spud to TD)
- Casing design principles and casing seat selection
- Cementing: why and how primary cement is placed
- Wellbore trajectory: vertical, deviated, horizontal — the geometry and why it matters

**Fluidos de Perfuração e Completação:** This is the theoretical foundation for mud engineering. Pay particular attention to:
- Rheological models: Bingham, Power Law, Herschel-Bulkley
- Mud weight calculation and equivalent circulating density (ECD)
- The role of mud in well control (hydrostatic balance)
- Different mud systems: WBM (water-based) vs OBM (oil-based) — when and why

**Engenharia de Reservatórios I:** Foundation for reservoir and production paths. Focus on:
- Darcy's Law and permeability
- Rock and fluid properties: porosity, saturation, PVT behavior
- Pressure depletion and drive mechanisms
- The concept of reserves vs resources

**Mecânica das Rochas:** Essential for drilling (formation evaluation, wellbore stability) and completions (fracturing, sanding). Understand:
- Rock strength parameters (UCS, Mohr-Coulomb)
- Stress state around a wellbore
- Why wellbore breakout happens and how orientation helps

### Self-Study Schedule (Semester 5)

Allocate 4–6 hours per week of self-study beyond class. Suggested allocation:

| Activity | Hours/Week | Tool/Resource |
|---|---|---|
| Study your path's CORE skills tutorial | 2 hrs | This folder: `skills/` |
| Read 2 Rigzone or SPE articles related to your path | 1 hr | rigzone.com, onepetro.org |
| Practice unit conversions and pressure calculations | 0.5 hr | Any problem set |
| Build your technical vocabulary (English) | 0.5 hr | Glossary + Rigzone reading |
| LinkedIn: connect with 3 ISPTEC alumni per week | 0.5 hr | LinkedIn |
| Write one paragraph summarizing what you learned | 0.5 hr | Your technical journal |

### Certifications to Plan (Don't Get Yet — Plan)

This semester, **research costs and providers** for:

1. **BOSIET/HUET** — Offshore survival and helicopter underwater escape
   - Where to get in Angola: Check Sonangol service centers, international providers in Luanda
   - Cost estimate: $800–1,500 (includes medical)
   - Duration: 2 days
   - **Do not buy yet** — plan and save for it

2. **Offshore Medical Certificate**
   - Usually done alongside BOSIET
   - Required annually (some providers say every 2 years for fit candidates)

3. **IWCF Level 1** — Well control awareness
   - Can be done online (IWCF has online modules)
   - Cost: $500–1,200
   - **Do not buy yet** — plan and save

### Network Actions

- **Find 5 ISPTEC alumni who work in offshore roles.** Search LinkedIn for: "ISPTEC petroleum" + "Angola" + ("SLB" or "Halliburton" or "TotalEnergies"). Send a brief, professional message: introduce yourself, say you're in Year 3, ask if they'd be willing to talk about their career path for 20 minutes.
- **Attend at least one industry event:** ANPG runs registration and information events. SONANGOL hosts supplier days. Check ISPTEC's events board. Your goal: collect 3–5 business cards/LinkedIn connections from working professionals.

## Year 3 — Semester 6 Action Plan

*Typical subjects: Engenharia de Reservatórios II, Completação de Poços, Engenharia de Segurança, Elevação de Petróleo, Simulação e Modelagem de Reservatórios (or equivalent)*

### This Semester's Priority: START CERTIFICATIONS

You have now had one semester to save money and research providers. This semester you begin acquiring your certifications.

**Certification target for Semester 6:**
- [ ] Book and complete your **Offshore Medical** (do this first — it is the prerequisite for BOSIET)
- [ ] Complete your **BOSIET/HUET** course
- [ ] Begin self-study for **IWCF Level 1** (online preparation)

These three items, once complete, unlock almost every entry-level offshore application.

### Academic Strategy

**Completação de Poços:** Critical for completions and drilling paths. Deep-dive on:
- Completion design: tubing, packers, downhole safety valves (DHSV)
- Sand control: gravel pack design, expandable screens, standalone screens
- Perforating: shaped charge physics, underbalanced vs overbalanced perforating
- The completion string design: from perforations to wellhead

**Elevação de Petróleo (Artificial Lift):** Core for production engineering. Master:
- Natural flow vs artificial lift: when do you need it?
- Gas lift: principles, GLR optimization, gas lift mandrel installation
- ESP (electrical submersible pump): when to use, performance curves
- Nodal analysis: finding the operating point of the well system

**Engenharia de Segurança:** This directly prepares you for offshore work. Engage fully:
- Hazard identification: HAZOP, HAZID
- Risk matrix construction and risk ranking
- Bowtie analysis: threats, barriers, consequences
- Permit to Work systems

**Simulação e Modelagem de Reservatórios:** Even if you're not going reservoir, understanding simulation output helps you interpret the data that drives production decisions.

### Job Application Preparation (Start Now)

Begin building your CV this semester — not because you'll apply yet, but because the process of writing a CV forces you to identify gaps.

**CV structure for an ISPTEC petroleum engineering student (offshore target):**
1. **Name, contact, LinkedIn URL**
2. **One-line objective:** Petroleum Engineering graduate targeting [Drilling / Production / etc.] roles in Angola's offshore sector
3. **Education:** ISPTEC Licenciatura em Engenharia de Petróleos — expected graduation [month/year]. GPA if strong (above 15/20).
4. **Certifications:** BOSIET/HUET ✓ (Date), Offshore Medical ✓ (Date), IWCF Level 1 — In Progress
5. **Technical Skills:** Well construction design, drilling fluid calculations (mud weight, ECD), reservoir pressure analysis, gas lift principles — ONLY LIST SKILLS YOU CAN DEFEND IN AN INTERVIEW
6. **Projects / Lab Work:** List ISPTEC lab work with measurable outcomes: "Designed mud program for a 3,000m vertical well using WBM system; calculated required mud weight to maintain overbalance of 200 psi across reservoir"
7. **Languages:** Portuguese (native), English (professional — CEFR B2 target)
8. **Software:** Excel (advanced), [list any PE software you've used], Python (basic)

## Year 4 — Semester 7 Action Plan

*Typical subjects: Gerenciamento de Reservatórios, Sistemas de Produção Superfície, Sistemas Marítimos de Produção, Avaliação de Formações, Engenharia Ambiental, Gerenciamento de Projetos*

### This Semester's Priority: GET YOUR IWCF AND START APPLYING

By now you should have BOSIET + Offshore Medical. This semester, get your IWCF Level 1. And in the last 6 weeks of the semester, start applying to companies.

**Certification target for Semester 7:**
- [ ] Complete **IWCF Level 1** (online exam — study 2 weeks, take exam)
- [ ] Research and register for **NEBOSH IGC** if pursuing HSE path

### Academic Strategy

**Avaliação de Formações:** Critical for both drilling and reservoir paths. This is the subject that directly prepares you for MWD/LWD field engineer roles. Go deep on:
- Gamma ray log interpretation (shale vs sand indicator)
- Resistivity log interpretation (water vs hydrocarbon indicator)
- Porosity tools: neutron-density cross-plot
- Spontaneous potential log
- Reading a composite log and identifying reservoir intervals

**Sistemas Marítimos de Produção:** This directly covers FPSOs, subsea systems, and offshore production infrastructure — the physical reality of where you want to work. Do not miss classes. Take detailed notes on:
- FPSO process flow: wellstream inlet → separation → treatment → storage → export
- Subsea tree design and function
- Umbilical systems and subsea control
- Mooring and riser systems

**Sistemas de Produção Superfície:** Separator design, multiphase flow, wellhead equipment. Core for production path.

**Gerenciamento de Projetos:** This is a business skill but offshore engineers use it constantly. Learn the basics: WBS, critical path, earned value, risk register. ISPTEC will cover PMI basics.

### Company Applications (Start in Week 8 of Semester 7)

With BOSIET + IWCF Level 1 in hand, you are eligible to apply. Target:

**If Drilling path:**
1. SLB Angola — Field Engineer Trainee application (open year-round)
2. Halliburton Angola — LEAD program (annual recruitment, usually June–August)
3. Baker Hughes Angola — Graduate program

**If Production/FPSO path:**
1. TotalEnergies Angola — Young Graduate Program (competitive, apply early)
2. Azule Energy — Internship/Graduate program (150+ positions/year)
3. SBM Offshore — Trainee program

**If HSE path:**
1. Any operator or Tier 2 service company — apply broadly with NEBOSH IGC in progress

**If Reservoir path:**
1. Sonangol E.P. — Graduate intake (apply via Sonangol website)
2. ExxonMobil Angola — Graduate recruitment
3. TotalEnergies — Reservoir engineer graduate track

**Format:** Apply online through company career portals. Additionally, send a direct email to the Angola country HR or recruitment lead with your CV + cover letter. Cold email works in Angola's industry because it is small and direct.

## Year 4 — Semester 8 (Final Semester) Action Plan

*Típically: Projecto II (final year project), Estágio Supervisionado, optional subjects*

### This Semester's Priority: CONVERT AN INTERNSHIP OR GRADUATE JOB

### Your Final Year Project: Make It a Career Asset

Your Projecto II is a direct opportunity. Choose a topic that:
1. Is technically relevant to your chosen career path
2. Uses real data from Angola's offshore sector if possible
3. Demonstrates your ability to use professional software

**Strong project topics by path:**
- *Drilling:* Optimization of drilling fluid properties for ultra-deep water wells in Block 32; Wellbore stability analysis for a horizontal well in Angola's subsalt
- *Production:* Nodal analysis and artificial lift optimization for a mature FPSO well; Gas lift design for a deepwater well declining below bubble point
- *Reservoir:* Reservoir characterization and production forecast for a Block 17 analogue; History matching methodology for a turbidite reservoir
- *Subsea:* Flow assurance analysis for a deepwater tieback in Block 15; Cathodic protection design for a deepwater pipeline Angola
- *HSE:* Process hazard analysis (HAZOP) of an FPSO gas compression system; Incident causation analysis of Angola's offshore near-miss database

**Show your project to recruiters.** Put a 2-page summary on your LinkedIn. This is direct evidence of your technical capability.

### Estágio Supervisionado: Get It in Industry

The ISPTEC supervised internship (estágio) is your single most valuable pre-graduation asset. Push hard to do it at:
- An operator: TotalEnergies, Chevron, Azule Energy, Sonangol
- A Tier 2 service company: SLB, Halliburton, Baker Hughes
- A local company with genuine offshore exposure: Petromar, Sonamet, PAENAL

**How to secure an industry internship:**
1. Apply 4–6 months before the estágio period begins
2. Approach companies directly — do not wait for ISPTEC to arrange it
3. Leverage your alumni network from Semesters 5–6
4. Bring your CV, cover letter, and certifications to in-person meetings

If you cannot get an industry placement, the second-best option is a research institution with petroleum data: ANPG itself, INA (Instituto Nacional do Petróleo), or Sonangol's research arm.

## The Post-Graduation Sprint (Months 0–6 After Graduation)

You have your degree. Now:

**Week 1–2:**
- Update your CV with your degree, final project, and internship
- Update LinkedIn with graduation status. Add a headline: "Petroleum Engineer | Drilling [or your track] | Angola Offshore"
- Send follow-up messages to every industry contact you've made

**Week 3–4:**
- Submit applications to your 5 top target companies
- If you haven't done IWCF Level 2 yet, book it now
- If pursuing HSE: sit your NEBOSH IGC exam now

**Month 2–3:**
- Follow up on all applications (email, not just the portal)
- Apply to Tier 3 Angolan companies if Tier 2 applications are not progressing — Tier 3 experience beats unemployment

**Month 4–6:**
- If you have an offer: accept it and start your career
- If you don't have an offshore offer yet: accept any onshore petroleum engineering adjacent role (Sonangol E&P support, ANPG technical assistant, local E&P company, fabrication yard)
- Use your first job to gain experience, certifications, and continue networking toward your target company

**The key mental model:** You are on a trajectory, not in a race. Every legitimate petroleum engineering job moves you forward. Stay patient with the strategy and aggressive with the execution.

---

## The Critical Mistake to Avoid

The single most common failure mode for ISPTEC graduates with offshore ambitions is **waiting for permission to be ready.**

They wait to apply until they "know more."
They wait to study well control until they "have the textbook."
They wait to email companies until their CV is "perfect."
They wait for ISPTEC to arrange the internship.
They wait for the right moment to network.

There is no perfect moment. The engineers who make it offshore are the ones who start one semester earlier, apply six months before they feel ready, and network before they need to.

Start now.
