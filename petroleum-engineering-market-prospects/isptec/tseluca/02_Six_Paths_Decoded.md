# Six Paths Decoded — Choose Your Track, Then Go Deep

> There are six engineering career tracks that lead to offshore rotation in Angola. Each is a fundamentally different job, requires a different skill set, targets different companies, and has a different time-to-first-rotation. This file gives you enough detail on each to make a real decision — not a guess.
>
> **Your task:** Read all six. Then pick one. Commit to it for at least 3 years before reconsidering.

---

## The Decision Framework

Before reading the tracks, answer these three questions honestly:

**Question 1: Where do you want to physically work offshore?**
- **On a drillship** (loud, industrial, high-intensity, technical decisions under pressure) → Drilling or Completions
- **On an FPSO** (large vessel, process operations, steady rhythm, optimization-focused) → Production or Reservoir
- **On a construction/installation vessel** (project-based, highly variable, international) → Subsea
- **Any offshore installation** (you want offshore fast, role is secondary) → HSE

**Question 2: Which ISPTEC subjects did you actually enjoy?**
- Engenharia de Poços, Mecânica das Rochas → Drilling or Completions
- Engenharia de Reservatórios, Simulação → Reservoir
- Sistemas de Produção, Elevação de Petróleo → Production
- Sistemas Marítimos, Engenharia de Segurança → Subsea or HSE
- Fluidos de Perfuração e Completação → Drilling Fluids (Drilling track)

**Question 3: How long are you willing to wait for your first offshore rotation?**
- **Under 12 months:** HSE (6–12 months) or Drilling (6–18 months)
- **12–24 months:** Production, Completions
- **24–48 months:** Reservoir, Subsea

## Path 1 — Drilling Engineer

### The One-Sentence Version
You design and supervise the drilling of wells — the most expensive, high-stakes, technically complex operations in the entire oil industry.

### What You Actually Do
Every well drilled in Angola deepwater costs $30–80 million USD and takes 30–90 days. Your job is to plan how that well gets drilled (what casing program, what mud weight, what bit), then supervise the execution on the rig floor.

As a **field engineer** (your entry role, typically at SLB, Halliburton, or Baker Hughes): you operate a specific drilling service (directional drilling, measurement while drilling, drilling fluids). You monitor data, write daily reports, make real-time adjustments, and advise the Company Man (operator's representative on the rig).

As a **drilling engineer** (your Tier 2 mid-level role): you sit in an office in Luanda or on the rig and design the next well. Casing design, mud program, bit selection, wellbore trajectory, torque-and-drag modeling, cost estimation.

### Why Drilling Is the Most Common Entry Path for ISPTEC Graduates
SLB's Field Engineer Trainee (FET) program explicitly takes fresh graduates with zero offshore experience. They will train you from scratch on their tools. Your ISPTEC degree in petroleum engineering is the entry ticket. If you add BOSIET + Offshore Medical + IWCF Level 1 before applying, you go from applicant to shortlisted.

### What You Need to Start
1. BOSIET/HUET + Offshore Medical
2. IWCF Level 1 (basic well control awareness)
3. Basic knowledge of: well construction sequence, BHA components, mud properties
4. Applied to: SLB FET, Halliburton LEAD program, Baker Hughes Emerging Talent

### Minimum Study Commitment (Before First Job)
- [skills/01_Well_Control_Tutorial.md](skills/01_Well_Control_Tutorial.md): 10 hours study

## Path 2 — Production / FPSO Engineer

### The One-Sentence Version
You keep the oil flowing from reservoir to export tanker — optimizing every piece of process equipment on the FPSO to maximize production and minimize downtime.

### What You Actually Do
Angola produces ~1.1 million barrels of oil per day, almost entirely from FPSOs. The production engineer is responsible for:
- Monitoring well production rates and wellhead pressures
- Optimizing gas lift or ESP settings to maximize production per well
- Troubleshooting production problems: scaling, corrosion, hydrate formation, sand
- Managing choke positions and separator settings
- Writing production reports and conducting well tests

As a junior engineer, you are the "well watcher" and process monitor. You learn the FPSO's piping and instrumentation systems, understand the process flow from wellhead to export pump, and handle the daily paperwork. Senior engineers design field development changes, manage reservoir depletion strategy, and work on production optimization campaigns.

### Why Production Is Highly Attractive for ISPTEC Graduates
FPSOs are ANPG local content flagships. Operators like TotalEnergies and Azule Energy run formal graduate trainee programs that put Angolans into production engineer roles directly. Sonangol EP also has a production engineering function. The key is that production work requires understanding both surface facilities AND the reservoir — your full ISPTEC curriculum is relevant.

### What You Need to Start
1. BOSIET/HUET + Offshore Medical
2. Understanding of surface process flow: wellhead → choke → separator → metering → export
3. Basic knowledge of artificial lift (gas lift principles, ESP basics)
4. Applied to: TotalEnergies Graduate Program, Azule Energy internship, SBM Offshore, Yinson

### Minimum Study Commitment (Before First Job)
- [skills/03_FPSO_Operations_Tutorial.md](skills/03_FPSO_Operations_Tutorial.md): 12 hours study
- [skills/09_Production_Engineering_Tutorial.md](skills/09_Production_Engineering_Tutorial.md): 10 hours study

## Path 3 — Subsea Engineer

### The One-Sentence Version
You design, install, and maintain the underwater infrastructure that connects subsea wells to the FPSO — pipelines, risers, umbilicals, manifolds, trees.

### What You Actually Do
Angola's deepwater infrastructure involves subsea trees on the seabed at 500–2,500m water depth, connected to FPSOs by flexible risers, umbilicals, and flowlines (collectively called SURF — Subsea Umbilicals, Risers, and Flowlines). The subsea engineer's domain is this entire underwater system.

You will specialize in one or more of:
- **Design:** SURF design, riser analysis, cathodic protection
- **Installation:** Managing the offshore installation campaign from a construction vessel
- **Integrity:** Monitoring and maintaining installed subsea equipment, ROV inspections, intervention planning
- **Equipment:** Subsea trees, manifolds, Christmas trees, BOPs, control systems

### Why Subsea Takes Longer to Enter
Subsea engineering requires more specialized knowledge than drilling or production. The companies that hire (TechnipFMC, Subsea 7, Saipem, Oceaneering) want candidates who already understand SURF concepts, materials, and subsea equipment. A fresh graduate needs to supplement their ISPTEC curriculum with specific subsea self-study or start in a related Tier 3 role (fabrication yard at PAENAL or Sonamet) to build tangible experience.

### What You Need to Start
1. BOSIET/HUET + Offshore Medical
2. Strong mechanical engineering foundations (your Resistência de Materiais, Mecânica Geral subjects)
3. Understanding of: subsea tree components, umbilical design, riser concepts
4. Applied to: TechnipFMC Angola, Subsea 7 Angola, Oceaneering Angola, PAENAL (Tier 3 entry)

### Minimum Study Commitment (Before First Job)
- [skills/04_Subsea_Systems_Tutorial.md](skills/04_Subsea_Systems_Tutorial.md): 15 hours study

**Full deep dive:** [paths/03_Subsea_Path.md](paths/03_Subsea_Path.md)

## Path 4 — Reservoir Engineer

### The One-Sentence Version
You understand how oil and gas move through underground rock formations and use that understanding to maximize field recovery over decades.

### What You Actually Do
Reservoir engineers work on the fundamental question: how do we get as much oil out of the ground as efficiently as possible? This involves:
- Building reservoir simulation models (Eclipse, CMG, Petrel)
- Analyzing well test data to characterize reservoir properties
- Calculating recovery factors, production forecasts, and reserves estimates
- Designing well placement, depletion strategy, injection programs (WAG, water flooding)
- Monitoring field performance against predictions, updating models

### The Reality: Reservoir Is Mostly Onshore Work
Here is the honest truth: **reservoir engineering is the least offshore of the six tracks.** Most reservoir engineers work in Luanda offices. You travel offshore 2–4 times a year for well tests and sampling campaigns, but your primary workspace is your computer screen.

If you specifically want FIFO rotation life, reservoir engineering is not the strongest choice. However, if you want the highest technical prestige, the best long-term Tier 1 operator salaries, and you love simulation and data analysis — this is your path. Senior reservoir engineers at operators earn $10,000–18,000/month.

### What You Need to Start
1. Strong reservoir engineering fundamentals (your Engenharia de Reservatórios I & II subjects)
2. Exposure to at least one reservoir simulation software (CMG or Eclipse — free student versions exist)
3. Basic understanding of well testing: pressure transient analysis, skin factor, permeability
4. Applied to: Operator graduate programs (TotalEnergies, ExxonMobil, Azule Energy, Sonangol EP)

**Full deep dive:** [paths/04_Reservoir_Path.md](paths/04_Reservoir_Path.md)

## Path 5 — HSE / Safety Engineer

### The One-Sentence Version
You are responsible for ensuring that everyone on the installation goes home safely — designing, implementing, and auditing the safety management systems that prevent accidents in one of the world's most hazardous industrial environments.

### Why HSE Is the Fastest Path to Offshore

Every offshore installation legally requires dedicated HSE personnel. A drillship with 150 crew must have qualified safety officers on every shift. An FPSO must have an HSE team. This is not optional. The result: companies consistently hire HSE engineers and officers even when they are not expanding their technical engineering headcount.

An ISPTEC petroleum engineering graduate who adds:
- NEBOSH International General Certificate (IGC)
- BOSIET/HUET + Offshore Medical
- Basic Fire Safety and First Aid certifications

...is hirable as an HSE Officer within 6 months of graduation. This is not a backdoor — HSE is a legitimate, well-paid, career-advancing function that many engineers use as an entry point and then specialize or transition from.

### What You Actually Do
- Conduct toolbox talks and Job Safety Analyses (JSAs) before every non-routine task
- Manage the Permit to Work (PTW) system — the formal authorization process for all offshore work
- Investigate near-misses and incidents, write RCA (Root Cause Analysis) reports
- Monitor and enforce isolation procedures (LOTO — Lock Out Tag Out) for equipment maintenance
- Conduct offshore safety inductions for mobilizing crew
- Audit compliance with safety cases, regulations, and management system requirements
- Lead emergency response drills (fire, gas detection, man overboard)

### What You Need to Start
1. NEBOSH IGC (or IOSH Managing Safely as an interim step)
2. BOSIET/HUET + Offshore Medical
3. H2S awareness certificate
4. Basic First Aid / CPR

**Full deep dive:** [paths/05_HSE_Path.md](paths/05_HSE_Path.md)

## Path 6 — Completions / Well Intervention Engineer

### The One-Sentence Version
You design and execute the final phase of well construction — the completions that turn a drilled hole into a producing well — and then intervene in producing wells when they underperform.

### What You Actually Do
After the drilling team finishes drilling the well, the completions team takes over to:
- Install production tubing, packers, and downhole safety valves
- Perforate the productive reservoir intervals
- Execute gravel packs or sand control systems in unconsolidated formations
- Run completion tools and verify well integrity before production

After a well is producing:
- Workover operations: pulling and replacing completion equipment
- Coiled tubing interventions: acid stimulation, scale removal, fishing
- Wireline operations: perforating, plug-setting, downhole sampling

### The Angola-Specific Opportunity
Angola's deepwater subsea wells are completed with complex dual-barrier systems. The ANPG Local Content rules mean that Angolan completions engineers are in demand as operators and Tier 2 companies are required to nationalize their completions teams. This is a technically specialized path where a well-prepared ISPTEC graduate can differentiate quickly.

### What You Need to Start
1. BOSIET/HUET + Offshore Medical
2. IWCF Level 1 or 2 (well control — completions involves open hole environments)
3. Basic understanding of completion hardware: tubing, packers, perforating guns, safety valves
4. Applied to: Halliburton Completion Tools, Baker Hughes, SLB OneStim, Weatherford

**Full deep dive:** [paths/06_Completions_Path.md](paths/06_Completions_Path.md)
