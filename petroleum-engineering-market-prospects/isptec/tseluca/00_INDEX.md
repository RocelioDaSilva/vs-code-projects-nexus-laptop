# Path to Tseluca — The Complete ISPTEC Student Guide to Offshore Rotation

> **Who this is for:** You. A petroleum engineering student at ISPTEC, currently in Year 3 or 4, who wants to end up on an FPSO or drillship living the offshore rotational life — what Angolans call **tseluca**: the real offshore career, the FIFO money, the technical mastery, the life that justifies four or five hard years of engineering school.
>
> **What this folder does:** Every other guide in this repository gives you pieces. This folder puts it all together in a single, opinionated, step-by-step system. It tells you exactly which career path to take, every skill you need to learn for that path, detailed tutorials for each skill, which certifications to get, which companies to target, and what to do this semester, next semester, and in your final year.
>
> **The premise:** You are in Year 3. You have roughly 18–24 months until graduation. That is exactly enough time to position yourself for a Tier 2 service company job with offshore rotation — if you start now and follow a plan.

---

## What Is Tseluca?

"Tseluca" is the life. The 28-days-on-28-days-off rotation on a floating production unit or a drillship 100 km off the coast of Cabinda. It is:

- Flying from Luanda to a vessel by helicopter, landing on a helideck
- Working 12-hour shifts doing real engineering — solving real problems with real consequences
- Living on the vessel with 100–200 other professionals from 20 countries
- Getting paid in USD with accommodation, food, and transport covered by the company
- Having 14–28 days completely free every month to study, build relationships, travel, or invest

It is **not** a glamorous Instagram life — [06_Offshore_Reality.md in the guide folder](../guide/06_Offshore_Reality.md) will tell you that honestly. But for an Angolan engineer with an ISPTEC degree, it is one of the highest-impact career choices available. The technical skills you build, the professional network, the salary, and the international exposure are unmatched in Angola's domestic market.

This folder shows you how to get there.

## The Four Rules of This Guide

**Rule 1: Pick one path.** Every file in this folder assumes you've made a choice. Read [02_Six_Paths_Decoded.md](02_Six_Paths_Decoded.md), pick your track, and stop hedging. Generalists get passed over in Angola's offshore industry.

**Rule 2: Start the certifications immediately.** Your BOSIET + Offshore Medical are the minimum ticket to ride. Without them, no company can send you offshore — even if they want to. Start planning this cost now. It's $800–1,500 and you need it before you graduate.

**Rule 3: The skills tutorials here are not reading material — they're study material.** Take notes. Work through the calculations. Draw the diagrams from memory. The goal is not to have read the tutorial; the goal is to be able to explain the concepts in a technical interview without looking at notes.

**Rule 4: Network before you need a job.** ISPTEC alumni in offshore roles are your single most valuable asset. Find them on LinkedIn, at industry events, at ANPG conferences. They will refer you, coach you, and tell you which companies are actually hiring.

## The Numbers That Should Motivate You

Angola's offshore sector runs on roughly 80–120 active offshore rotational positions that turn over every 1–3 years. The local content requirements mean that Angolan nationals (ISPTEC graduates among them) get priority for these positions. Companies are legally required to hire local. The competition is other ISPTEC and UAN graduates — not international engineers.

The engineers who consistently win those positions are not the smartest people in the class. They are the ones who:
1. Chose a specific technical direction early
2. Built the required certifications
3. Did the self-study to be technically prepared before their first interview
4. Networked deliberately into the industry before they needed a job

This folder gives you the map. The work is yours to do.
