# Path: Completions / Well Intervention Engineer — From ISPTEC to Live Well Operations

> **Time to first offshore rotation:** 12–24 months post-graduation
> **Typical entry company:** Halliburton Completion Tools, Baker Hughes, SLB OneStim, Weatherford
> **Physical environment:** Drillship or jackup rig during completions; workover/intervention vessels for intervention
> **Schedule:** 28/28 or project-based rotation

---

## What Completions Engineers Actually Do

After the drilling team finishes drilling a well — reaching total depth, setting casing, cementing — the well is not yet a producing well. It is a cased hole. The completions engineer's job is to transform that cased hole into a functioning production system.

**Phase 1: Well Completions**

The completions engineer designs and executes the installation of the completion string — the production equipment that sits inside the casing and allows hydrocarbons to flow to surface in a controlled, safe way.

**Key completion components:**
- **Production tubing:** The pipe through which oil and gas flow to surface. Typically 3.5"–5.5" OD in Angola deepwater.
- **Packer:** A downhole tool that creates a seal between the tubing and casing annulus, isolating the production interval
- **Downhole Safety Valve (DHSV / SCSSV):** A fail-safe valve that closes if surface control is lost — the primary safety barrier in a producing well
- **Sand control system:** For unconsolidated sands (common in Angola's turbidite reservoirs): gravel pack, standalone screens, or expandable screens to prevent sand production
- **Gas lift mandrels:** Pre-installed sleeves in the tubing at specific depths to allow future gas lift injection
- **Production liner / perforated liner:** In some completions, the reservoir interval is completed with a pre-perforated liner rather than a casing + perforation approach

**Phase 2: Perforating**

In most Angola wells, the reservoir is penetrated by perforating charges — shaped explosive charges fired from a wireline or TCP (tubing-conveyed perforating) gun. The perforations create small (~1cm diameter) holes through the casing and cement into the formation, allowing reservoir fluids to flow into the wellbore.

The completions engineer designs:
- Perforation density (shots per foot/meter)
- Phase angle (180°, 120°, 90°, 60°)
- Interval selection (which formation zones to perforate)
- Overbalanced vs underbalanced perforating

**Phase 3: Well Testing and Cleanup**

After perforating, the well is flowed to clean up drilling fluid invasion and establish stable production. The completions engineer manages:
- Flow and build-up cycles
- Wellbore cleanup: reverse circulation of fluids, coiled tubing washout
- Production test: measuring flow rate, GOR, water cut, surface pressures
- Stimulation if needed: matrix acidizing to remove near-wellbore damage (skin)

## Well Intervention: The Other Half

Producing wells degrade over time. Production decline, mechanical failures, and reservoir depletion require **intervention** — operations on a live producing well to restore or optimize production.

**Types of interventions you'll execute:**

**Wireline Operations:**
- Slickline (wire): setting and pulling plugs, shifting sliding sleeves, measuring fluid levels, setting gauge hangers
- E-line (electrical line): perforating, logging, sampling, setting packers

**Coiled Tubing (CT) Interventions:**
- Scale removal: pumping acid or chelant through CT to dissolve calcite/barite scale in tubing or perforations
- Sand cleanout: circulating out sand accumulation from the wellbore
- Stimulation: matrix acid stimulation to remove skin and restore permeability
- Plug milling: drilling out bridge plugs left from previous workover operations

**Workover Operations:**
- Pulling and replacing the completion string
- ESP replacement (when an electric submersible pump fails)
- Sleeve shifting: opening or closing completion intervals to adjust well allocation
- Side track: drilling a new wellbore from within an existing well (e.g., to avoid a collapsed section or reach a new reservoir zone)

## Why Completions Is Particularly Valuable in Angola

Angola's deepwater wells have complex completion designs:
- **Dual-barrier completions** (required by ANPG for deepwater wells)
- **Subsea completion equipment** (subsea trees, production risers — the completion feeds directly into the subsea infrastructure)
- **Sand control in unconsolidated turbidites** — many Angola wells produce fine sand that would erode equipment without proper sand control
- **Multi-zone completions** — some wells produce from multiple reservoir sands simultaneously using selective interval completions

ANPG's local content requirements mean operators need Angolan completions engineers. This creates genuine demand for well-trained Angolan graduates in this specialization.

## The Completions Career Ladder

```
Year 0–1: Completions Engineer Trainee / Field Operations Trainee
  └── Learning completion tool catalogue: packers, tubing, DHSV, screens
  └── Assisting on completions runs on the rig
  └── Learning rig-floor operations specific to completions

Year 1–3: Completions Field Engineer / Wireline Field Engineer
  └── Running completion tools: packer setting, perforation runs, sand control jobs
  └── Interpreting completion data in real time
  └── Writing job reports, incident reports

Year 3–6: Senior Completions Engineer
  └── Designing completions programs for new wells
  └── Specifying equipment, managing service company work
  └── Acting as Company Man for completions phase

Year 6–10: Completions Lead / Well Engineering Manager
  └── Portfolio of wells, managing schedule and budget
  └── Technical authority for completion design decisions
  └── Operators: $10,000–16,000/month at this level
```

## Entry Routes: How to Actually Get In

### Route A: Halliburton Completion Tools (Primary)

Halliburton is the world's largest completion tools company. Their Completion Tools division designs and manufactures everything from simple packers to complex sand control screens to intelligent completion systems. Their Angola team needs field engineers.

**How to apply:** halliburton.com/en-US/careers — search Completion Tools + Angola + Graduate

### Route B: Baker Hughes Completions

Baker Hughes has a strong completions and intervention portfolio including gravel pack systems, sand exclusion screens, and downhole completion equipment. Their Angola operations have field engineer positions.

### Route C: SLB OneStim / Well Construction

SLB's completion services include wireline, perforating, and sand control. Their Field Engineer Trainee program covers completions as one of the specialization tracks.

### Route D: Weatherford

Weatherford specializes in well construction and completions services including tubular running services, liner hangers, completions tools, and intervention equipment. A smaller company than the big three but with genuine Angola exposure.

## Technical Interview Preparation: 15 Questions

1. What is the purpose of a production packer? How does it seal?
2. What is a DHSV (Downhole Safety Valve)? Why is it required in subsea completions?
3. What is sand control? Name three sand control methods and when you'd use each.
4. What is a gravel pack? How is it placed?
5. What is perforating? What is the difference between TCP (tubing-conveyed) and wireline perforating?
6. What is skin factor? How does the completions engineer reduce skin?
7. What is matrix acidizing? How does HCl react with calcite?
8. What is a workover? Name three reasons you'd workover a well.
9. What is coiled tubing? What are its advantages over wireline for intervention?
10. What is underbalanced perforating? What are its advantages?
11. Explain the completion string from reservoir to wellhead: what components are included and in what order?
12. What is a dual-barrier completion? Why is it required in deepwater?
13. What is a sliding sleeve? When would you open or close one?
14. What is a plug and abandonment (P&A) operation?
15. A well that was producing 2,500 bbl/day has declined to 800 bbl/day over 6 months, with no change in GOR or water cut. What completion interventions might restore production?
