# Path: Production / FPSO Engineer — From ISPTEC to FPSO Rotation

> **Time to first offshore rotation:** 12–24 months post-graduation
> **Typical entry company:** TotalEnergies Young Graduate Program, Azule Energy, SBM Offshore, Yinson, or Sonangol EP
> **Physical environment:** FPSO (Floating Production, Storage and Offloading vessel)
> **Schedule:** 28/28 rotation (standard for most Angola FPSOs)

---

## What Production Engineers Actually Do

Oil doesn't flow from a reservoir to an export tanker by itself. Every well must be optimized, every separator must be tuned, every pipeline must be managed. The production engineer is responsible for maximizing the rate at which oil moves from underground to the export pump — efficiently, safely, and with maximum recovery.

**Well performance management:**
- Monitor flowing wellhead pressure (FWHP), tubing head pressure (THP), and flow rates for each well
- Identify wells underperforming against prediction
- Diagnose root causes: wellbore damage, skin, artificial lift inefficiency, separator bottleneck
- Optimize choke position for each well within the FPSO processing capacity

**FPSO process operations:**
- Monitor separation train performance (inlet separators, high-pressure, low-pressure, test separator)
- Manage gas compression and lift gas allocation across wells
- Track water handling: produced water treatment, re-injection rates
- Manage export metering and custody transfer (you are accountable for every barrel of oil sold)

**Artificial lift management:**
- For gas lift wells: optimize gas injection rates per well using allocation curves; detect malfunctions (valve leakage, mandrel issues)
- For ESP wells: monitor motor current, intake pressure, pump efficiency; plan workovers for failing ESPs
- Build and maintain Nodal Analysis models (Prosper/IPM) for all wells

**Production optimization campaigns:**
- Identify low-hanging fruit: wells with high skin, tubing problems, incorrect choke settings
- Plan and execute well tests to measure skin, permeability, and PI
- Work with reservoir engineers to align surface optimization with depletion strategy

**Production reporting:**
- Daily Production Reports: actual vs allocated vs budgeted production per well and per field
- Monthly performance reports for operator management and block partners
- ANPG production reporting (regulatory requirement)

## The FPSO Explained: Your Office for 28 Days

The FPSO is a permanently moored vessel (or ship-shaped floating unit) that functions as a complete oil processing facility at sea. Angola's FPSOs are among the largest and most sophisticated in the world.

**Process flow on an FPSO (simplified):**

```
Subsea Wells
    │ (via flexible risers and flowlines)
    ▼
Inlet Manifold / Slug Catcher
    │ (removes slugs of liquid)
    ▼
HP Separator (High Pressure)
    ├── Gas → Gas Compression → Gas Lift / Fuel Gas / Export
    ├── Water → Produced Water Treatment → Overboard or Re-injection
    └── Oil → LP Separator
          ├── Gas → LP Compression
          └── Oil → Electrostatic Treater → Dewatering / Desalting
                └── Export Pump → Storage Tank → Export (to tanker or pipeline)
```

**Key equipment you must understand:**
- **Three-phase separator:** Separates oil, gas, and water using gravity + retention time + flow velocity
- **Electrostatic treater:** Applies electric field to break oil-water emulsion. Produces dry crude (BS&W < 0.5%)
- **Hydrocyclones / degassers:** Remove fine solids and dissolved gas from produced water
- **Gas compressors:** Centrifugal or reciprocating compressors handling produced gas
- **Chemical injection systems:** Corrosion inhibitor, scale inhibitor, hydrate inhibitor, emulsion breaker, biocide
- **Metering stations:** Fiscal metering for export (your accountability)

## The Production Career Ladder in Angola

```
Year 0–1: Production Engineer Trainee / Junior Production Engineer
  └── Learning the FPSO system: P&IDs, process flow, instrumentation
  └── Maintaining daily production logs
  └── Shadowing senior engineers during optimization campaigns

Year 1–3: Production Engineer
  └── Independently managing a subset of wells
  └── Running well tests
  └── Troubleshooting and optimizing specific wells

Year 3–5: Senior Production Engineer
  └── Managing full field production optimization
  └── Leading artificial lift campaigns
  └── Coordinating with reservoir engineers on depletion

Year 5–8: Lead Production Engineer / Production Superintendent
  └── Responsible for production targets across the FPSO
  └── Managing the production engineering team

Year 8–12: Asset Manager / Reservoir Management Lead
  └── Integrates subsurface (reservoir) with surface (production)
  └── Full field depletion decisions: well shutins, infill drilling
  └── Operators at this level: $12,000–18,000+/month
```

## Entry Routes: How to Actually Get In

### Route A: TotalEnergies Angola Young Graduate Program

TotalEnergies is the largest operator in Angola (Blocks 17, 32, 20). Their Young Graduate Program takes petroleum engineering graduates directly into production or operations engineer roles.

**What they want:**
- BOSIET/HUET + Offshore Medical
- Strong academic record (above average GPA)
- English proficiency
- Evidence of technical curiosity: SPE papers read, software experience, thesis quality

**Process:** Applications open approximately June–September each year. Competitive — expect 15–30 applicants per position. Interview includes technical case study: "You have an FPSO well producing 3,000 bbl/day last month. This month it's at 1,800 bbl/day. What do you do?"

### Route B: Azule Energy

Azule Energy (formed from the merger of Eni and BP operations in Angola) has one of the most active graduate recruitment programs in Angola. 150+ positions per year across all disciplines including production engineering.

**Key advantage:** Azule's accelerated development programs are specifically designed to nationalize technical roles within 3–5 years. Angolan graduates are strongly preferred.

### Route C: FPSO Contractors (SBM Offshore, Yinson, MODEC)

FPSO contractors operate the vessel for the operator under lease contracts. They need production operations engineers, facilities engineers, and process engineers. Entry is often less competitive than operator programs, and you can build strong FPSO operations experience that makes you attractive to operators later.

**SBM Offshore Angola:** Operates FPSOs including the Sepetiba (Block 32 area) and has maintained a significant Angola presence.

**Yinson:** Growing FPSO contractor with Angola presence (Helang FPSO for Sonangol).

### Route D: Sonangol E.P.

Sonangol EP (the national oil company's upstream division) has production engineers assigned to joint venture blocks. This is a stable, Kwanza-denominated position but provides genuine FPSO exposure and a strong foundation for future Tier 1 or Tier 2 moves.

## Required Certifications: Production / FPSO Path

| Certification | Level Required | When to Get It | Notes |
|---|---|---|---|
| **Offshore Medical** | Pass | Year 3 Semester 6 | Prerequisite for BOSIET |
| **BOSIET/HUET** | Pass | Year 3 Semester 6 | Mandatory for all FPSO work |
| **H2S Awareness** | Pass | Before first offshore trip | Mandatory on many FPSOs |
| **Fire Safety Basic** | Pass | Usually included in BOSIET | — |
| **BOSIET with Sea Survival** | Pass | Recommended | Some operators require the full FOET/BOSIET combo |
| **Well Control Awareness (IWCF L1)** | Pass | Year 4 | Not always required but valued — shows awareness |

**Process safety specific (highly valued for production path):**
- **NEBOSH Process Safety Management (PSM)** — shows understanding of FPSO process hazards
- **IChemE Safety Certificate** — chemical engineering safety, valued by process engineers on FPSOs

## Essential Software for Production / FPSO Engineers

| Software | What It Does | Free Access? |
|---|---|---|
| **Prosper (Petex IPM)** | Nodal analysis, well performance modeling, artificial lift design | Student version available |
| **GAP (Petex IPM)** | Gathering network modeling — models the FPSO inflow system | Student version available |
| **MBAL (Petex IPM)** | Material balance reservoir model | Student version available |
| **Excel + VBA** | Daily production reports, decline curve analysis | Free via Office |
| **Python + pandas** | Automated production data processing, trend detection | Free |
| **OLGA (Schlumberger)** | Multiphase flow simulation — for flow assurance and riser analysis | No free version |
| **PIPESIM (SLB)** | Single-phase and multiphase pipeline modeling | Student version sometimes available |

**Practical exercise:** Download the free Petex IPM student version (prosper.petex.com). Build a simple nodal analysis model: vertical well, 3,000m depth, 3,000 psi reservoir pressure, 500 md permeability. Plot the IPR and VLP curves. Find the operating point. Then change the choke size and see what happens to the operating point. This exercise demonstrates your Prosper skills and your understanding of nodal analysis in one go.

## Technical Interview Preparation: 20 Questions You Must Be Able to Answer

1. What is nodal analysis and why do you use it?
2. What is an IPR curve? A VLP (tubing performance) curve? Where do they intersect?
3. What is the bubble point pressure and what happens to production when reservoir pressure drops below it?
4. Explain gas lift: how does it work? What is the optimal GLR?
5. What is an ESP? When would you choose ESP over gas lift?
6. What is a three-phase separator and how does it work?
7. What does BS&W stand for? What is the acceptable level for export crude?
8. What is a well test? What information do you get from it?
9. What is production decline? What is the difference between exponential, harmonic, and hyperbolic decline?
10. What is skin factor? What does a positive skin mean? How do you reduce it?
11. What is a production deferral? How do you calculate production efficiency?
12. You have an FPSO well producing 4,000 bbl/day. The FWHP has increased by 200 psi over the last week. What could cause this?
13. What is the purpose of chemical injection on an FPSO?
14. What is an electrostatic treater and what is it used for?
15. What is a SCSSV (Surface Controlled Subsurface Safety Valve)? What is its purpose?
16. What is flow assurance? Name three flow assurance threats.
17. What is a well test separator? How is it different from the production separator?
18. What is a choke? Why do you adjust choke position?
19. What is produced water? How is it treated on an FPSO?
20. You are on FPSO night shift. Production suddenly drops by 2,000 bbl/day across all wells. What do you investigate?
