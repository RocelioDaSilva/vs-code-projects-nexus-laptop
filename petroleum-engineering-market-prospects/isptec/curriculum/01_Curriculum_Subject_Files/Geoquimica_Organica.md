## What You Learned

The origin of petroleum: kerogen types (I, II, III), thermal maturation (vitrinite reflectance, Tmax), source rock characterization (TOC, Rock-Eval pyrolysis), oil-source rock correlation, biomarkers, and the processes of generation, migration, and accumulation.

## Where This Subject Is Used in Industry

- **Exploration â€” Petroleum Systems Analysis:** Before drilling an exploration well, geoscientists assess: "Is there a mature source rock that generated hydrocarbons? Did those hydrocarbons migrate to a trap? Is the trap sealed?" Geochemistry answers the first two questions.
- **Source Rock Evaluation:** TOC (Total Organic Carbon) and Rock-Eval pyrolysis (S1, S2, HI, Tmax) from cutting/core samples tell you whether a rock is a good source rock and whether it is thermally mature (has generated oil or gas). These are measured in every exploration well.
- **Oil-Source Correlation:** Biomarker analysis (GC-MS of sterane, terpane, porphyrin fingerprints) can tell you which source rock generated a particular oil â€” critical for understanding migration pathways and predicting where undiscovered accumulations might be.
- **Basin Modeling:** Tools like PetroMod ([SLB](../../../data/company-directory/slb.md)) model the burial history and thermal evolution of basins over geological time. They predict when and where source rocks reached the oil window and gas window. Geochemistry provides the calibration data.

## Angola-Specific Context

Angola's petroleum system is extraordinarily prolific:
- **Source rock:** Neocomian (Early Cretaceous) lacustrine shales in the Kwanza and Congo basins â€” Type I kerogen (algal-rich), extremely high TOC (5â€“10%)
- **Maturation:** Deep burial under thick post-salt sediments pushed these source rocks into the oil window
- **Migration:** Hydrocarbons migrated upward through fault systems and accumulated in post-salt turbidite sandstones (Blocks 15, 17, 32) and pre-salt carbonates

Understanding this petroleum system helps you understand why Angola has so much oil and where undiscovered reserves might be â€” critical for explorers and for anyone who wants to understand the geological foundation of the industry they work in.
