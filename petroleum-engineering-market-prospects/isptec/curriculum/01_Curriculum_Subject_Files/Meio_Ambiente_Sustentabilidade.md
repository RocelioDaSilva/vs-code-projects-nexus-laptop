## What You Learned

Environmental science fundamentals, sustainability concepts, environmental impact assessment (EIA), pollution sources and control, waste management, climate change science, and the framework for balancing industrial development with environmental protection.

## Where This Subject Is Used in Industry

- **Environmental Compliance:** Every offshore project in Angola requires an **Environmental Impact Assessment (EIA)** approved by the Ministry of Environment before drilling can begin. Operations must comply with ANPG environmental regulations and international standards (IFC Performance Standards, Equator Principles).
- **Produced Water Discharge:** FPSOs must treat produced water to below 29 mg/L oil-in-water before discharging overboard (OSPAR Convention standard applied in Angola). This is a daily operational requirement.
- **Gas Flaring Reduction:** Angola has committed to reducing routine gas flaring. The World Bank's Zero Routine Flaring by 2030 initiative is relevant. Understanding why flaring occurs (associated gas with no processing/reinjection capacity) and how to eliminate it (gas reinjection, Angola LNG) comes from environmental science.
- **Drilling Waste Management:** Drill cuttings contaminated with oil-based mud cannot be discharged overboard in Angola's deep waters without treatment. Cuttings reinjection (CRI) or transport to shore for treatment are required.
- **Decommissioning:** When offshore infrastructure reaches end-of-life, it must be decommissioned with minimum environmental impact. This is a growing field as Angola's first-generation FPSOs approach 25+ years.

## Angola-Specific Context

Angola is increasingly focused on environmental performance:
- **Presidential Decree 38/09** establishes environmental protection regulations for petroleum operations
- **ANPG** monitors compliance with environmental conditions in production licenses
- **Angola LNG** was built partly to reduce gas flaring from Block 17 and other fields
- **Carbon neutrality targets** by international operators ([TotalEnergies](../../../data/company-directory/totalenergies.md) Net Zero 2050) affect Angola operations

Environmental roles are growing in Angola's oil sector. HSE/Environmental Officer positions are available with operators and service companies.
