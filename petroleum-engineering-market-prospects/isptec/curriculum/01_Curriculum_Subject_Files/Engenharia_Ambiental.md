## Where This Subject Is Used in Industry

- **Environmental Impact Assessment (EIA):** Every offshore development project in Angola requires an EIA submitted to the Ministry of Environment. The EIA covers: drilling discharges (cuttings, fluids), atmospheric emissions (gas flaring, turbine exhaust), produced water discharge, oil spill risk, and impact on marine fauna.
- **Produced Water Management:** Production wells in Angola produce water along with oil. This water contains hydrocarbons, chemicals, and naturally occurring radioactive material (NORM). The discharge limit in Angola is <29 mg/L oil in water. Meeting this limit requires treatment equipment (hydrocyclones, degassers, flotation units).
- **Gas Flaring Reduction:** Angola has been reducing gas flaring through the Angola LNG project and reinjection. Environmental engineers track and report flaring volumes â€” regulatory compliance with World Bank Zero Routine Flaring targets.
- **Oil Spill Response:** Environmental engineers develop Oil Spill Contingency Plans (OSCP), manage dispersant stocks, and coordinate response exercises.
- **Decommissioning:** As aging fields decline, facilities must be decommissioned responsibly. Environmental assessment of decommissioning options is a growing field.

## Angola-Specific Context

Angola's Decreto 38/09 governs environmental protection in petroleum operations. All operators must submit annual Environmental Reports to ANPG and the Ministry of Environment. Environmental compliance is not optional â€” it affects operating licenses. Companies like RSK, ERM, and Fugro provide environmental consulting services in Angola.
