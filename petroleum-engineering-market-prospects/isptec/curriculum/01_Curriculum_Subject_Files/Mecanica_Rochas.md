## What You Learned

Rock mechanical properties (Young's modulus, Poisson's ratio, UCS, tensile strength), in-situ stress (overburden, maximum/minimum horizontal stress), Mohr-Coulomb failure criterion, pore pressure, effective stress, and wellbore stability analysis.

## Where This Subject Is Used in Industry

- **Wellbore Stability:** The #1 application. When you drill a well, you remove the rock that was supporting the borehole wall. The remaining rock must support itself under the in-situ stress field. If the mud weight is too low â†’ borehole collapse (breakouts, cavings). If the mud weight is too high â†’ fracture (lost circulation). The safe mud weight window is calculated using rock mechanics.
- **Hydraulic Fracturing:** Fracturing rock intentionally requires knowing: minimum horizontal stress (closure stress), breakdown pressure, and fracture geometry. All from rock mechanics.
- **Sand Production Prediction:** Weak formations (low UCS) may produce sand during production, which damages equipment and blocks tubing. Predicting whether a formation will produce sand requires rock mechanical analysis.
- **Reservoir Compaction and Subsidence:** As reservoir pressure declines during production, the effective stress increases, causing rock compaction. This can cause surface/seabed subsidence (e.g., Ekofisk in Norway, 3m+ subsidence). Compaction can also drive production (compaction drive).
- **In-Situ Stress for Fault Seal Analysis:** Whether faults are sealing or leaking depends on the stress state on the fault plane â€” a rock mechanics problem.
