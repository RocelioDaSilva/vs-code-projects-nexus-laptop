## What You Learned

Single-phase and multiphase flow in pipes: pressure drop correlations (Beggs & Brill, Hagedorn & Brown), flow regimes (bubble, slug, annular, stratified), vertical lift performance (VLP), pipeline sizing, and the use of flow simulation software (PIPESIM, OLGA).

## Where This Subject Is Used in Industry

- **Nodal Analysis:** The operating point of a well is where the IPR (reservoir deliverability) intersects the VLP (tubing performance). Nodal analysis â€” the workhorse of production engineering â€” requires calculating pressure drop in the tubing using multiphase flow correlations.
- **Pipeline Design:** Sizing subsea flowlines for multiphase flow requires predicting pressure drop, liquid holdup, and flow regime. Under-sizing means insufficient capacity; over-sizing wastes capital.
- **[Artificial Lift](../../../docs/skills/09-artificial-lift.md) Design:** If the reservoir cannot deliver oil to surface at acceptable rates by natural flow, artificial lift (ESP, gas lift) is needed. The VLP calculation determines when natural flow stops and what lift method to use.
- **[Flow Assurance](../../../docs/skills/10-flow-assurance.md):** Slug flow in pipelines causes severe pressure and flow rate fluctuations â€” damaging topsides processing equipment. OLGA transient simulations predict slug characteristics and inform topside design (slug catchers).
