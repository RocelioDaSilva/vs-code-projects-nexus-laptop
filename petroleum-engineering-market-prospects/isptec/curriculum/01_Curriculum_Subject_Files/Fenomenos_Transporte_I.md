## What You Learned

Fluid mechanics: fluid statics, viscosity, laminar and turbulent flow, Reynolds number, Navier-Stokes equations, pipe flow (Hagen-Poiseuille, Darcy-Weisbach), boundary layers, and Bernoulli's equation. You learned how fluids move and how to calculate pressure drops.

## Where This Subject Is Used in Industry

- **Darcy's Law â€” The Foundation of Reservoir Engineering:** The flow of oil through porous rock is described by Darcy's Law: $Q = \frac{kA\Delta P}{\mu L}$. This is the most important equation in petroleum engineering and is a direct simplification of the Navier-Stokes equations for flow through porous media.
- **Drilling Hydraulics:** The drill bit is cooled and cleaned by mud flowing down the drill string and up the annulus. Calculating pressure drop through the drill string, across the nozzles (Bernoulli), and up the annulus (pipe flow) uses fluid mechanics directly. The rig's mud pumps must overcome this total pressure drop.
- **Pipeline Flow:** Transporting oil through subsea flowlines requires calculating pressure drop (Darcy-Weisbach) and flow regime (Reynolds number). Whether the flow is laminar or turbulent determines the friction factor and hence the pump power required.
- **[FPSO](../../../docs/glossary.md) Process Flow:** Process piping on the FPSO (from separator to heater to pump to export) is sized using pipe flow calculations.
- **Rheology:** [Drilling fluids](../../../docs/skills/03-drilling-fluids.md) are non-Newtonian (Bingham plastic or power-law fluids). Calculating pressure losses for non-Newtonian fluids requires extending the concepts from this course.
