## What You Learned

Thermodynamics of mixtures, phase equilibria (liquid-vapor, liquid-liquid), chemical potentials, Gibbs free energy, colligative properties, surface chemistry, reaction kinetics, and phase diagrams. You learned to predict when and why substances change phase.

## Where This Subject Is Used in Industry

- **PVT Analysis (Pressure-Volume-Temperature):** This is the single most important application. Reservoir fluids behave differently at reservoir conditions (high P, high T) vs. surface conditions. The bubble point, dew point, formation volume factor (Bo), solution GOR (Rs), and viscosity all depend on phase equilibria â€” directly from this course.
- **Equation of State (EOS):** Reservoir simulators model fluid behavior using equations of state (Peng-Robinson, SRK). These equations predict phase behavior of multicomponent hydrocarbon mixtures. The thermodynamic foundation is physical chemistry.
- **[Flow Assurance](../../../docs/skills/10-flow-assurance.md) â€” Hydrate Phase Diagram:** Gas hydrate formation is predicted using a phase diagram (pressure vs. temperature with hydrate stability boundary). - **Wax Appearance Temperature (WAT):** The temperature at which wax crystals begin to form in crude oil â€” a phase transition predicted by thermodynamic models.

## Angola-Specific Context

Each Angola crude oil has unique PVT properties that are measured in laboratories (usually by CoreLab or [SLB](../../../data/company-directory/slb.md)) and modeled using equations of state. The PVT report for a Block 17 reservoir is the foundation of all reservoir engineering, production engineering, and [flow assurance](../../../docs/skills/10-flow-assurance.md) work for that field. Without physical chemistry, you cannot understand what the PVT report tells you.
