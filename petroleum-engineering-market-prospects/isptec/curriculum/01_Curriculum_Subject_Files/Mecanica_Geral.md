## What You Learned

Statics (force equilibrium, moments, free body diagrams, trusses, frames), dynamics (kinematics, Newton's second law for systems, work-energy, impulse-momentum), and vibrations. You learned to analyze structures and moving systems.

## Where This Subject Is Used in Industry

- **Rig Structural Engineering:** Offshore drilling rigs (jackups, semi-submersibles, drillships) are massive structures subjected to wave, wind, and operational loads. Structural analysis of the derrick, substructure, and hull uses statics and dynamics from this course.
- **[FPSO](../../../docs/glossary.md) Structural Integrity:** FPSOs experience fatigue loading from waves over 20+ year service life. Hull structural analysis, turret design, and mooring system engineering use applied mechanics.
- **Drill String Dynamics:** As the drill string rotates and slides in the hole, it undergoes complex vibrations: axial (bit bounce), lateral (whirl), torsional (stick-slip). Understanding these vibrations requires dynamics. Vibration damage is a major cause of tool failure and poor drilling performance.
- **Crane Operations:** Calculating crane loads, boom angles, and sling tensions during offshore lifts is statics. Dynamic amplification from vessel motion is dynamics.
- **Pipeline Stress Analysis:** Pipelines on the seabed experience thermal expansion, pressure loads, and span loading â€” analyzed using beam theory and structural mechanics.
