## What You Learned

Fundamental English grammar, reading comprehension, and basic written and oral communication. You built the vocabulary foundation to read simple technical texts and communicate in a professional environment.

## Where This Subject Is Used in Industry

- **All Roles:** English is the working language of the international oil and gas industry. Even on Angolan rigs operated by [Sonangol](../../../data/company-directory/sonangol.md) or [TotalEnergies](../../../data/company-directory/totalenergies.md), the technical documentation (procedures, safety manuals, equipment manuals) is in English. Morning toolbox talks on FPSOs are conducted in English when international crew are present.
- **Drilling:** Daily Drilling Reports (DDR), well programs, and BOP test reports are written in English.
- **Production:** [FPSO](../../../docs/glossary.md) operating procedures from [SBM Offshore](../../../data/company-directory/sbmoffshore.md), Yinson, and MODEC are in English. Alarm descriptions on DCS/SCADA systems are in English.
- **HSE:** Permit to Work (PTW) forms, HAZOP reports, incident investigation reports â€” all English.

## Angola-Specific Context

Angola's oil industry is dominated by international companies ([TotalEnergies](../../../data/company-directory/totalenergies.md), Eni/Azule, Chevron, ExxonMobil) and OFS giants ([SLB](../../../data/company-directory/slb.md), [Halliburton](../../../data/company-directory/halliburton.md), [Baker Hughes](../../../data/company-directory/bakerhughes.md)). Your ability to read, write, and speak English fluently is **the single most important non-technical skill** for career progression. Many Angolan engineers with strong technical skills are passed over for promotion because their English report writing is weak.
