## What You Learned

Surface production facilities: separation (two-phase, three-phase), gas processing (dehydration, compression, sweetening), water treatment (produced water, injection water), oil treatment (dehydration, desalting), metering, storage, and export systems.

## Where This Subject Is Used in Industry

- **[FPSO](../../../docs/glossary.md) Process Engineering:** An FPSO is a floating oil processing factory. The production engineer / process engineer manages: wellhead manifold â†’ production separator â†’ gas compression â†’ oil dehydration â†’ water treatment â†’ storage â†’ offloading. Understanding each unit operation is this course.
- **Separator Sizing:** Three-phase separators separate oil, gas, and water based on gravity (Stokes' Law) and residence time. Sizing a separator for a given flow rate and fluid properties is a fundamental production engineering calculation.
- **Gas Compression:** Produced gas must be compressed for gas lift injection, gas export, or LNG feedstock. Understanding compressor types (reciprocating, centrifugal), staging, intercooling, and anti-surge control.
- **Produced Water Treatment:** Hydrocyclones, deoiling, flotation units, and filters treat produced water to meet the <29 mg/L oil discharge limit. As fields mature (increasing watercut), water handling capacity often becomes the production bottleneck.

## Angola-Specific Context

Angola's production comes predominantly from FPSOs ([FPSO](../../../docs/glossary.md) Girassol, FPSO Dalia, FPSO Pazflor, FPSO CLOV, FPSO Kaombo Norte/Sul, FPSO Mondo, etc.). Each FPSO was designed for specific process capacities. As fields mature and watercut increases, FPSO process bottlenecks (water treatment capacity, gas handling) limit production. Understanding surface processing is essential for production optimization.
