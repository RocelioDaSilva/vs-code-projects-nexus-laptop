## What You Learned

Advanced reservoir engineering: well test analysis (drawdown, buildup, Horner plot, type curve matching), inflow performance relationships (Vogel, Fetkovich), multiphase flow (Buckley-Leverett, fractional flow), water and gas injection design, and enhanced oil recovery methods.

## Where This Subject Is Used in Industry

- **Well Testing:** Pressure transient analysis (PTA) determines reservoir properties from well test data: permeability (k), skin factor (s), reservoir boundaries, and connectivity. Every new well is tested, and the data is analyzed using PTA software (Saphir/Kappa, Ecrin).
- **IPR Construction:** The IPR (Inflow Performance Relationship) â€” how much oil the reservoir can deliver at a given bottomhole pressure â€” is the starting point for production optimization. Vogel's equation is used for solution gas drive; straight-line PI for undersaturated oil.
- **Water Injection Design:** Most Angola fields use water injection for pressure maintenance. Designing the injection scheme (injection rate, pattern, water quality) uses fractional flow theory (Buckley-Leverett) and well-to-well interactions.
- **Decline Curve Analysis (DCA):** Arps decline models (exponential, hyperbolic, harmonic) forecast future production from historical trends â€” used for reserves estimation and economic evaluation.

## Angola-Specific Context

Angola's offshore fields are managed with aggressive water injection programs. [TotalEnergies](../../../data/company-directory/totalenergies.md)' Block 17 has been injecting seawater since first production of Girassol (2001). Managing watercut, optimizing injector placement, and maintaining sweep efficiency requires advanced reservoir engineering. This course provides the analytical tools.
