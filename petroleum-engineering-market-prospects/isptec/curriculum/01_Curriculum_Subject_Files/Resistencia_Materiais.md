## What You Learned

Stress and strain analysis, axial loading, torsion, bending, shear, Mohr's circle, beam deflection, column buckling (Euler), and combined loading. You learned to analyze whether structures and mechanical components will fail under load.

## Where This Subject Is Used in Industry

- **Casing Design:** The most direct application in petroleum engineering. Casing strings must resist: burst pressure (from well kick/formation pressure), collapse pressure (from cement or fluid column), axial tension (from the weight of casing string below), and thermal stress. Designing a casing string is a combined loading problem using the triaxial stress criterion (von Mises).
- **Drill String Design:** The drill string is under tension at surface and compression at the bit. It can buckle in the horizontal section, undergo fatigue at tool joints (bending stress from dogleg severity), and twist under torque. All of this is strength of materials.
- **Tubing Design:** Production tubing must resist burst, collapse, and tension â€” similar to casing but with additional thermal effects (tubing heats up during production, causing expansion and compressive stress).
- **Subsea Structures:** Manifolds, templates, and jumpers are designed for subsea loads (hydrostatic pressure, installation loads, thermal expansion) using structural analysis.
