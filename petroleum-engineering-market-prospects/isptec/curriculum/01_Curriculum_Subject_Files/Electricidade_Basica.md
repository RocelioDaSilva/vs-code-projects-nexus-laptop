## What You Learned

DC and AC circuits, Ohm's law, Kirchhoff's laws, power calculations (P = VI), three-phase systems, transformers, electric motors, and basic electrical safety. You learned how electrical systems are designed, operated, and protected.

## Where This Subject Is Used in Industry

- **[FPSO](../../../docs/glossary.md) Power Systems:** An FPSO is a floating power station. Gas turbine generators produce 30â€“100+ MW of three-phase AC power. This is distributed through transformers, switchgear, and motor control centers to power: ESP downhole pumps, gas compressors, water injection pumps, cranes, lighting, and accommodation. Understanding the electrical architecture of an [FPSO](../../../docs/glossary.md) is essential for production operators and maintenance engineers.
- **ESP Electrical Systems:** The ESP motor (downhole) is powered by a cable from the VSD (Variable Speed Drive) on the [FPSO](../../../docs/glossary.md) surface. The cable can be 3,000â€“5,000m long. Voltage drop, cable impedance, motor power factor, and overload protection â€” all from basic electricity â€” determine ESP performance and reliability.
- **Electrical Isolation (LOTO):** Before any maintenance work on electrical equipment offshore, the equipment must be electrically isolated and locked out. Understanding how to safely isolate a three-phase motor or a high-voltage transformer requires knowledge of electrical circuits and safety.
- **Hazardous Area Classification:** Offshore platforms and FPSOs have zones classified by explosion risk (Zone 0, 1, 2). Electrical equipment in these zones must be of appropriate protection type (Ex-d, Ex-e, Ex-i). This is based on electrical principles.
