# Five-Year Action Plan — From ISPTEC to Offshore Rotation

> This is not a vague motivational roadmap. It is a specific, month-by-month plan for getting from your final semester at ISPTEC to a Tier 2 offshore rotation within 36 months. Follow it precisely.

---

## The Three-Tier Model (Reviewed)

Angola's oil industry is structured in three tiers. Your career moves through them in sequence. Trying to skip tiers rarely works.

```
Tier 1 — Operators (TotalEnergies, Azule, Chevron, Sonangol)
         High salary. Slow to hire fresh graduates. Relationship-dependent.
              ↑ (transition after 4–6 years total experience)
Tier 2 — Service Companies (SLB, Halliburton, Baker Hughes, SBM, Subsea 7)
         Structured training. Accept fresh graduates. USD salary. Best starting point.
              ↑ (transition after 1–2 years at Tier 3 OR direct entry with certs)
Tier 3 — Local Companies & Logistics (SONILS, Algoa, Operatec, Petromar, SONAMET)
         Entry accessible. Kwanza salary. Limited training. But: offshore access.
```

The plan below gives you **two paths**:
- **Fast Path:** Direct Tier 2 entry (possible if you have certs, English, and strong academics)
- **Standard Path:** Tier 3 first, then Tier 2 (most common, more reliable)

## YEAR 0 — Final Semester (Months 1–6 Before Graduation)

**Goal:** Graduate with certifications in hand, applications already submitted.

### Month 1–2: Get Certified
- [ ] Book and complete **[BOSIET](../../docs/glossary.md) + HUET** at CFA Luanda — ~$700 USD
- [ ] Book and complete **Offshore Medical** at SOS International Luanda — ~$200 USD
- [ ] Create your **LinkedIn profile** — professional photo, ISPTEC education listed, headline: "Petroleum Engineering Student | [BOSIET](../../docs/glossary.md) Certified | Angola Offshore"
- [ ] Create a **Petrel student account** at learn.[slb](../../data/company-directory/slb.md).com (free with academic email) — start Petrel Fundamentals course

### Month 3: Build Your CV and Online Presence
- [ ] Write your CV (one page, see [08_Application_Playbook.md](08_Application_Playbook.md) for template)
- [ ] Get 2–3 professional references — at minimum: one ISPTEC professor, one engineer from any company you've interacted with
- [ ] Join **SPE International** (Society of Petroleum Engineers) — free for students at spe.org/student
- [ ] Attend any SPE Angola events or ISPTEC career days

### Month 4: First Applications
Submit applications to all of the following simultaneously:

| Company | Program | Application Link |
|---|---|---|
| [SLB](../../data/company-directory/slb.md) | Field Engineer Trainee | careers.slb.com |
| [Azule Energy](../../data/company-directory/azule-energy.md) | Professional Internship | azule-energy.com/careers |
| [Halliburton](../../data/company-directory/halliburton.md) | LEAD Program | jobs.halliburton.com |
| [Baker Hughes](../../data/company-directory/bakerhughes.md) | Emerging Talent | careers.bakerhughes.com |
| [TotalEnergies](../../data/company-directory/totalenergies.md) | Young Graduate Program | careers.totalenergies.com |
| [Sonangol](../../data/company-directory/sonangol.md) | Graduate Intake | sonangol.co.ao/emprego |
| [SONILS](../../data/company-directory/sonangol.md) | Any engineering role | sonils.ao |

**Simultaneously:** Connect with 10–15 Angola-based petroleum engineers on LinkedIn. Not to ask for jobs — to ask for 15-minute coffee chats to learn about their experience. 2 out of 10 will respond. Those 2 conversations are worth more than 50 cold applications.

### Month 5–6: Respond and Follow Up
- [ ] Follow up on all applications (email or LinkedIn message) at the 3-week mark
- [ ] Prepare for technical interviews: review [well control](../../docs/skills/01-well-control.md) basics, reservoir fundamentals, FPSO process overview
- [ ] Complete ISPTEC final project/dissertation — do not let this slip

## YEAR 1 — Entry Level (Months 7–18)

### Scenario A: You Got a Tier 2 Offer (Fast Path)

**Congratulations. You are in the 20% who got here directly.** This typically means you had strong English, certs ready, academic record, and targeted the right programs.

Your Year 1 at a Tier 2 company:

**Months 1–3 (Onshore Training Phase):**
- Absorb everything. You are in a structured training environment.
- [SLB](../../data/company-directory/slb.md) FET program: classroom + simulator training. Take notes obsessively.
- [Halliburton](../../data/company-directory/halliburton.md) LEAD: rotational exposure across divisions.
- Do not be the person who argues with the trainer because "the textbook says something different." The trainer has 15 years offshore. Listen.
- Start [IWCF](../../docs/glossary.md) Level 1 if your company doesn't schedule it automatically — ask about it in your first week.

**Months 4–12 (First Field Assignments):**
- You will be assigned to a real well or real [FPSO](../../docs/glossary.md) in a supervised capacity.
- Your job: **show up early, stay late, ask smart questions, never complain about the schedule.**
- Write your IADC reports/production reports carefully. Attention to detail in documentation is noticed.
- Objective for end of Year 1: **first offshore rotation completed, [IWCF](../../docs/glossary.md) Level 1 in hand.**

### Scenario B: You Got a Tier 3 Offer (Standard Path)

**This is the most common path. Do not be discouraged.** [SONILS](../../data/company-directory/sonangol.md), Algoa, [SONAMET](../../data/company-directory/sonamet.md) — these companies have launched hundreds of Angola careers.

Your Year 1 at a Tier 3 company:

**What "winning" looks like at Tier 3:**
- Get offshore within the first 3 months of joining. Push for it. If they assign you to office logistics for 6 months, politely but persistently request a rotation assignment.
- Every Tier 1 and Tier 2 engineer working on the same installation as you is a potential future employer. Introduce yourself. Do excellent work in their presence.
- Build your IWCF Level 1 — most Tier 3 companies won't sponsor it. Pay for it yourself. It's $1,000–1,500 and will pay back in months.

**Months 6–12 at Tier 3:**
- By Month 6, you should have been offshore at least once (even if only a 14-day rotation)
- By Month 9, re-approach your original Tier 2 applications with: "I have 6 months offshore experience, IWCF Level 1, [BOSIET](../../docs/glossary.md). I'd like to be reconsidered for [role]."
- This simple update letter has a very high conversion rate. Tier 2 companies prefer candidates who already have offshore exposure.

### Scenario C: No Offers Yet (Recovery Path)

**This happens. It doesn't mean your career is over.**

- Expand your application list: offshore catering (Newrest Angola), medical (SOS International offshore medics), inspection companies, environmental monitoring
- Consider a completely different entry: **Wellsite Geology** or **[Mud Logging](../../docs/skills/07-mudlogging.md)** — 2-week courses, get you offshore, pay reasonably, expose you to the drill floor. Many engineers start here.
- Re-audit your CV and cover letters with someone who has hiring experience
- Pursue [H2S](../../docs/glossary.md) awareness + IWCF Level 1 while waiting — these certifications change your application profile

## YEAR 2 — Proving Yourself

**Goal:** Establish a reputation as reliable, technically competent, and ready for more responsibility.

### What "the transition trigger" looks like

The switch from Tier 3 to Tier 2, or from trainee to engineer, is **almost never a job posting**. It is almost always triggered by one of:
1. Someone you worked with offshore recommends you to their HR
2. A service company engineer notices you on a job and offers you a tryout
3. You reapply to a Tier 2 program with offshore experience on your CV and this time you're selected

All three require the same thing from you: **do exceptional work on every rotation, be visible to the right people.**

### Tactical moves for Year 2

- [ ] Complete **[IWCF](../../docs/glossary.md) Level 2** (5-day course, ~$2,000). If your employer won't sponsor it, pay it yourself. This credential separates you from 80% of engineers at your level.
- [ ] Begin one online course that strengthens your track specialty:
  - Drilling: Petroleum Engineering certificate on Coursera (University of Texas)
  - Reservoir: ECLIPSE basics ([SLB](../../data/company-directory/slb.md) free training portal)
  - Production: PROSPER nodal analysis tutorial
  - HSE: NEBOSH IGC preparation
- [ ] Ask your supervisor for a performance review. Ask specifically: "What is the one thing I need to improve to be considered for the next grade/level?"
- [ ] Update LinkedIn with your offshore experience. Post 2–3 times per year about something you've learned offshore — this marks you as a credible technical voice.
- [ ] Re-activate your Tier 2 applications if you're still at Tier 3

## YEAR 3 — First Tier 2 Engineering Role

**Target:** By Month 30–36, you are employed at a Tier 2 service company in a staff engineer role, rotating offshore on a real schedule.

### What Tier 2 gives you that Tier 3 doesn't:

| Aspect | Tier 3 | Tier 2 |
|---|---|---|
| Salary | $800–1,500/mo | $2,500–5,000/mo + allowance |
| Training | Minimal / self-directed | Structured ([SLB](../../data/company-directory/slb.md) has a full curriculum) |
| Exposure | One operation type | Multiple clients, blocks, technologies |
| CV value | "Offshore experience" | "[SLB](../../data/company-directory/slb.md) / [Halliburton](../../data/company-directory/halliburton.md) trained engineer" |
| Career progression | Slow, non-structured | Defined grade levels (FE, SFE, Tech Expert) |

### At Tier 2: The first 6 months

- Be the engineer who understands the client's perspective (the operator). You are not just executing a service — you are a contractor working in someone else's field development. Act accordingly.
- Every interaction with the operator's Company Man or installation Supervisor is a potential reference letter for your Tier 1 move someday.
- Do not job-hop in year 3. Stability at Tier 2 for 2–3 years is what builds a real reputation.

## YEAR 4 — Specialization and Technical Deepening

**Goal:** Become the specialist in one technical area at your Tier 2 company.

In Year 4, your focus shifts from "getting experience" to "being the expert." This looks different per track:

- **Drilling:** [IWCF](../../docs/glossary.md) Level 2 complete, considered for Drilling Supervisor role
- **Production:** Building nodal analysis and decline curve analysis competence, lead for a well intervention campaign
- **Subsea:** Acting Subsea Engineer for a smaller project, managing [ROV](../../docs/glossary.md) interface
- **Reservoir:** Software proficiency in Petrel/Eclipse, leading decline curve work for a client
- **HSE:** NEBOSH complete, leading HAZOP sessions, representing HSE at SIMOPS meetings

Ask your employer to **second you** (loan you) to a Tier 1 operator for a project. This is how service engineers become operator engineers.

## YEAR 5 — The Tier 1 Move

**Goal:** By Year 5–6, transition to a Tier 1 operator in a staff engineer role.

### What Tier 1 operators want in a hire:

- Minimum 4–5 years of relevant Tier 2 experience in Angola specifically
- [IWCF](../../docs/glossary.md) Level 2 (for wells roles)
- No HSE incidents in personal record
- A track record on their specific operations — they want someone who has worked on their FPSOs or their wells before
- English fluency ([TotalEnergies](../../data/company-directory/totalenergies.md): also values French)
- Personal recommendations from current employees

### The Tier 1 application strategy:

1. Identify 3 target companies at Tier 1 (e.g., [TotalEnergies](../../data/company-directory/totalenergies.md) Angola + [Azule Energy](../../data/company-directory/azule-energy.md) + Sonangol E.P.)
2. Map your existing contacts: who do you know inside each? Cultivate those relationships in Year 4.
3. Watch for "Technical Lead," "Senior Engineer," or "Operations Supervisor" postings
4. Apply when you can honestly say "I have worked on your Block [X] operations in a service capacity for [Y] years"

**Salary reality at Tier 1 entry:**  
Base $6,000–10,000/month + offshore allowance. Total compensation $100,000–180,000/year.
