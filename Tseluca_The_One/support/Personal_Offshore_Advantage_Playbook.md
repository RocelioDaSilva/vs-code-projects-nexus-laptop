# Personal Offshore Advantage Playbook (Tseluca)

> Profile: ISPTEC petroleum engineering student who wants offshore rotational life, strong salary, and fast employability after graduation.
>
> Objective: Enter a petroleum-focused offshore role quickly, then compound salary and technical growth over the first 3 years.
>
> Date baseline: April 2026.

---

## 1) Strategy in One Page

You want three things at the same time:

1. Offshore FIFO/rotation lifestyle
2. Good salary trajectory
3. Low risk of long unemployment after graduation

The highest-probability route is:

1. Primary path: Production/FPSO or Drilling (petroleum-core + strong demand)
2. Employability hedge: HSE competency for faster entry opportunities
3. Target companies in order: Tier 2 service companies first, then operators/FPSO owners

Why this works:

- Service companies hire more graduates and train harder on operations
- Offshore certificates remove the main mobilization barrier
- Petroleum-core skills keep your career identity technical (not generic)

---

## 2) What To Learn Beyond ISPTEC

ISPTEC gives fundamentals. The market rewards applied execution. Build these layers beyond class:

### Layer A: Offshore Readiness (mandatory)

1. BOSIET/HUET + valid offshore medical
2. Basic permit-to-work and risk tools (JSA, LMRA, toolbox talk)
3. Offshore communication discipline (handover writing, shift reporting)

### Layer B: Petroleum Operations Depth (your advantage)

1. Well control fundamentals (kick detection, barriers, shut-in logic)
2. Drilling and completions basics (casing, cementing, fluids, well integrity)
3. FPSO production systems (separation, gas handling, water treatment, export)
4. Production engineering basics (IPR/VLP, nodal analysis, artificial lift logic)

### Layer C: Digital Productivity (selection differentiator)

1. Advanced Excel for engineering workflows
2. Python for petroleum data analysis (daily rates, decline, pressure trends)
3. Structured technical communication in English (interviews + reports)

---

## 3) Paid Certifications and Courses: What To Buy First

Use this order to avoid wasting money and to maximize employability per dollar.

### Priority 1 (buy first)

1. Offshore medical certificate
2. OPITO BOSIET with HUET

Where to do them:

- Angola providers listed in [Certifications_Angola.md](Certifications_Angola.md)
- If scheduling is limited locally, compare equivalent OPITO-approved centers in Namibia/South Africa and include travel in budget

Decision rule:

- Only pay providers with verifiable accreditation and certificate IDs

### Priority 2 (buy after Priority 1)

1. IWCF Level 1 (awareness/foundation)
2. H2S awareness (if required by target employer/project)

Where:

- IWCF-accredited training centers (remote/live options acceptable for Level 1)
- Confirm the credential appears in IWCF verification pathways

### Priority 3 (buy once interviews start)

1. NEBOSH IGC (if you want HSE-as-hedge and broader employability)
2. One targeted technical short course based on your chosen path:
   - Drilling: well control/drilling practices
   - Production: production operations/FPSO process fundamentals
   - Subsea: subsea systems/ROV systems fundamentals

Where:

- Recognized providers like PetroSkills, IADC/IWCF partner centers, and reputable vendor academies
- Prefer courses that issue verifiable certificates and practical assessments

## 4) Skills To Develop and How To Develop Them

### Skill Group 1: Technical Petroleum Skills

Build:

1. Well barrier philosophy and well control response
2. Drilling hydraulics and mud weight/ECD reasoning
3. Production troubleshooting mindset (rate drop diagnostics)
4. Basic formation evaluation interpretation

How:

1. Study relevant tutorials in [../skills](../skills)
2. Do 2 handwritten technical problem sets per week
3. Create one-page concept sheets from memory (no notes)
4. Explain one topic weekly to a peer in English

### Skill Group 2: Offshore Work Skills

Build:

1. Shift handover quality
2. Safety leadership habits
3. Fatigue and routine discipline

How:

1. Practice writing daily operational summaries (max 12 lines)
2. Run weekly mock toolbox talks (5 minutes, one hazard topic)
3. Follow a fixed sleep and fitness routine to simulate rotation resilience

### Skill Group 3: Career Execution Skills

Build:

1. CV and interview readiness for offshore roles
2. Networking and referral acquisition
3. Company intelligence and application timing

How:

1. Keep a master CV + path-specific variants (Drilling/FPSO/HSE-hybrid)
2. Message 3 alumni per week with specific questions
3. Track every target company in a weekly pipeline sheet

## 5) Best Role Positioning For Your Goal

To balance salary + speed + petroleum focus:

1. First choice: Drilling field-track or Production/FPSO junior track
2. Second choice: Completions/intervention support track
3. Hedge choice: HSE offshore-support role while keeping petroleum study active

Notes:

- HSE can get you offshore faster, but keep petroleum progression alive through parallel technical upskilling.
- Service companies are often the best first employer for structured offshore learning.

## 6) 12-Month Execution Plan (Starting Now)

### Months 1-3

1. Choose primary path (Drilling or Production/FPSO)
2. Finalize certification budget and savings schedule
3. Build technical study rhythm: 6-8 hours/week
4. Start alumni networking cadence (3 quality contacts/week)

Deliverables:

- One-page career thesis (why this path, why this company type)
- CV v1 + LinkedIn profile in English

### Months 4-6

1. Complete offshore medical + BOSIET/HUET
2. Finish one full skill tutorial stack tied to your path
3. Build 1 mini project portfolio item (Excel/Python + technical interpretation)

Deliverables:

- Certificate files organized and ready
- Portfolio item #1 published (PDF/LinkedIn post or GitHub notebook)

### Months 7-9

1. Complete IWCF Level 1
2. Run interview drills weekly (technical + behavioral)
3. Start early applications for internships/graduate pipelines

Deliverables:

- Interview answer bank (20 common questions)
- Target company pipeline with contact names and deadlines

### Months 10-12

1. Apply broadly in waves (Tier 2 first, then operators/FPSO)
2. Add one more portfolio artifact
3. If needed, start NEBOSH IGC as employability multiplier

Deliverables:

- 30+ quality applications over 2-3 cycles
- Referral-driven interviews in progress

---

## 7) Saving and Budget Plan For Certifications

Because you started a job to fund certifications, use a protected certification fund.

### Budget buckets

1. Mandatory core (medical + BOSIET)
2. Technical credential (IWCF)
3. Optional accelerator (NEBOSH or one technical short course)

### Execution rules

1. Save first, spend second (fixed monthly percentage)
2. Never buy two expensive courses in the same month
3. Verify provider legitimacy before payment
4. Keep a 15-20% reserve for travel, rebooking, or document costs

---

## 8) How To Stay Ahead of the Competition

Most graduates do one of two things wrong: they stay generic, or they delay execution.

Your advantage stack should be:

1. Early certificates completed before graduation
2. Narrow technical identity (Drilling or Production/FPSO)
3. Real mini portfolio proving applied competence
4. Referral network built before job season
5. Strong English technical communication

If you do these five consistently, you become easier to hire than candidates with only grades.

## 9) Weekly Scoreboard (Use Every Sunday)

Score yourself 0-2 on each item (0 = no progress, 1 = partial, 2 = done):

1. Study hours completed (target: 6-8)
2. Technical problem sets completed (target: 2)
3. Networking actions completed (target: 3)
4. CV/interview improvement action (target: 1)
5. Savings transfer completed (target: yes)
6. Application/company research actions (target: 3)

Target weekly score: 9+/12.

---

## 10) If You Want The Fastest Employability Without Losing Petroleum Focus

Use this sequence:

1. Core certs first (medical + BOSIET)
2. Apply to HSE and operations-adjacent offshore entry roles
3. Keep petroleum skills progression active in parallel
4. Transition internally or externally into drilling/production technical track within 12-24 months

This keeps you employed, offshore, and still on a petroleum-engineering growth path.
