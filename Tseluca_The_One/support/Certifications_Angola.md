# Certifications for Offshore Work — Angola Edition

> Complete guide to every certification you need for offshore work in Angola, where to get them in Luanda, what they cost, and in what order to get them. Updated for ISPTEC Year 3–4 students targeting offshore FIFO positions.

---

## Before You Read: The Certification Logic

Certifications serve two purposes:
1. **Legal requirement:** You cannot go offshore without them. No cert = no access.
2. **Competitive differentiation:** Certs on your CV signal seriousness and investment to employers.

Start with the legal-mandatory ones. Then layer on the technical ones that match your chosen path.

---

## Part 1: Mandatory Offshore Safety Certifications

These are REQUIRED before setting foot offshore. No exceptions.

### 1.1 Offshore Medical Certificate (Medical Fitness for Offshore)

**What it is:** A fitness-to-work medical examination conducted by an approved offshore medical provider. Confirms you are physically fit for offshore work.

**What is examined:**
- General physical examination (blood pressure, heart, vision, hearing)
- Blood tests (glucose, liver function, full blood count)
- Chest X-ray
- ECG (electrocardiogram)
- Urine analysis
- Eye test
- Audiogram

**Validity:** 2 years (renewable)

**Where to get it in Angola/Luanda:**
- **Clínica Multiperfil** — Rua Marechal Broz Tito, Luanda (approved by several operators)
- **Hospital Sagrada Esperança** — Approved international standards clinic
- **SOS International (SOS Angola)** — International SOS partner, widely operator-approved
- **Confirm with the employer** which medical provider is on their approved list before booking

**Cost estimate:** $150–350 USD (varies by provider and whether you pay in AOA or USD)

**Important:** Some Angola operators require their own company-approved medical provider. Check before completing this.

---

### 1.2 BOSIET — Basic Offshore Safety Induction and Emergency Training

**What it is:** The fundamental offshore survival course. Teaches you how to survive in an emergency on an offshore installation.

**Covers:**
- Fire fighting and fire prevention
- Sea survival (deploying a life raft, survival in the water)
- Helicopter underwater escape training (HUET) — the most famous part
- First aid basics
- Safety awareness for offshore environments

**Duration:** 4 days (some providers offer 3-day versions)

**Validity:** 4 years (refresher required every 4 years: FOET — Further Offshore Emergency Training)

**The HUET (Helicopter Underwater Escape Training):**
You get into a helicopter simulator on land, then it is submerged in a pool, inverted (turned upside down). You must escape underwater. This is a physical, swimming-capable test. If you cannot swim or have water phobia, address this BEFORE you enroll.

**BOSIET providers:**
- **OPITO-accredited is the international standard.** The following regional providers are closest to Angola students:
- **PETROTEC Training Center, Luanda** — The main Angola-based OPITO-accredited provider. Located in Luanda — this is your first choice.
  - Address: Verify current address and course schedule directly (schedules shift quarterly)
  - Tel: Contact via their website or the Angola OPITO representative
- **ICAT (Instituto de Capacitação Técnica), Luanda** — Check current OPITO accreditation status
- **Regional option: OPITO-accredited centers in Lagos, Nigeria or Cape Town, South Africa** — If Luanda courses are full or temporarily suspended
- **International option: Falck Safety Services, Survivex, HWSS (Survival Systems), etc.** — UK, Norway, Brazil; expensive if travel included

**Cost estimate:** $800–1,400 USD (Luanda provider); $1,500–2,500 (regional); $2,000–3,000+ (international with travel)

**Booking tip:** Angola offshore companies often run waiting lists for Luanda BOSIET courses. Book 2–3 months in advance. Some employers sponsor the cost once you're hired — ask during salary negotiation.

---

### 1.3 HUET (Helicopter Underwater Escape Training) — Standalone

Some providers separate HUET from BOSIET. If your BOSIET provider does not include a full HUET pool component, you may need a standalone HUET certificate.

**Check at time of BOSIET enrollment** whether the full HUET pool exercise is included.

**HEBS (Helicopter Emergency Breathing System) training:** Often bundled with HUET. You learn to use the small compressed air pony bottle while escaping a submerged helicopter. Confirm it's included in your course.

---

### 1.4 H2S Awareness Certificate

**What it is:** Training on the properties, hazards, detection, and response to hydrogen sulfide (H2S). Covers personal H2S detector, SCBA use, emergency evacuation procedures.

**Duration:** 1 day (classroom) or online

**Validity:** 2 years

**Where to get it in Angola:**
- **PETROTEC Training Center** — offers H2S awareness
- **Sonangol / company in-house programs** often include this in their induction
- **Online:** Some operators accept online H2S awareness certificates from providers like:
  - Enform, BCIT Safety (Canada) — check if accepted by Angola operator
  - H2S Alive (Energy Safety Canada) — widely recognized; online theory + practical component

**Cost estimate:** $50–150 USD (standalone course)

## Part 2: Path-Specific Technical Certifications

Get the mandatory certs first. Then add these based on your chosen career track.

### 2.1 IWCF Well Control Certifications (Drilling, Completions, Reservoir)

The IWCF (International Well Control Forum) certifies well control competency. Critical for anyone near a wellhead.

#### IWCF Level 1 — Well Control Awareness

**Who needs it:** Everyone working on a drilling rig or near a wellhead. Not specific to engineers — includes all rig personnel.

**Content:** Basic well control concepts, kick recognition, BOP awareness, emergency procedures (awareness level, not operational)

**Duration:** 2 days + exam

**Validity:** 2 years

**Where to get it:**
- **PETROTEC Training Center, Luanda** — check current course schedule
- **IWCF.org** — official website with approved test centers. Search Angola/sub-Saharan Africa.
- **Sonangol Pesquisa e Produção training programs** sometimes include IWCF L1 for trainees

**Cost estimate:** $300–600 USD

---

#### IWCF Level 2 — Subsea Well Control Supervisor

**Who needs it:** Drilling engineers, completions engineers, anyone who will be in a supervisory role near a wellhead on an offshore well.

**Content:** Full operational well control, kill procedures, BOP operations, deepwater-specific well control, kick simulation exercises

**Duration:** 5 days + exam (pass mark: 70% multiple choice + 70% on simulation exam)

**Validity:** 2 years (renewable by re-examination)

**Where to get it:**
- **PETROTEC Training Center** may offer Level 2 with simulator
- **INSTEC (Instituto Superior de Tecnologias), Angola** — engineering training
- **Regional and international:** IWCF test centers in Nigeria (Lagos), South Africa, UK, USA. Budget for travel if Luanda options are limited.
- **Online theory + in-person exam:** IWCF allows online theory preparation, but the final exam must be in-person with proctoring at an IWCF-approved test center

**Cost estimate:** $600–1,500 USD (course fees); add travel if going regional

**Study materials:** IWCF Study Guide (free at iwcf.org). "Applied Drilling Engineering" (SPE). "Well Control Workbook" from IWCF.

---

#### IWCF Level 3 — Driller

For when you're working as a driller. Typically achieved after 2–3 years of operational experience as a roughneck/assistant driller.

#### IWCF Level 4 — Toolpusher / Company Man

Senior level. Acquired after significant operational experience.

---

### 2.2 NEBOSH IGC (International General Certificate in Occupational Health and Safety)

**Who needs it:** HSE engineers (required), any engineer wanting to demonstrate safety management capability (highly respected across all paths)

**See full details in [07_HSE_Offshore_Tutorial.md](../skills/07_HSE_Offshore_Tutorial.md) Part 11**

**Online providers accessible from Angola:**
- **Astutis Online** (UK): £280–350 GBP, full online learning + online exam
- **Red Oak Training** (online): Good reputation, flexible schedule
- **Zoe Talent Solutions** (online): UAE-based, serves African students
- **British Safety Council** (online): NEBOSH-accredited course

**Cost estimate:** $350–600 USD depending on provider (online study option)

**Exam:** Online proctored exams (IG1: written paper; IG2: workplace practical assessment)

---

### 2.3 GWO (Global Wind Organisation) Basic Safety Training

**Who it's for:** Engineers targeting wind farm roles or marine vessel maintenance roles
**Coverage:** First aid, fire awareness, manual handling, working at heights, sea survival

**Not critical for oil and gas** unless specifically targeting offshore wind.

---

### 2.4 IOSH Managing Safely

**Who it's for:** Team leaders, supervisors, engineers who manage small teams and need structured safety management knowledge

**Duration:** 3 days (classroom) or 4–6 weeks (online)

**Online providers accessible from Angola:**
- IOSH direct (iosh.com)
- Astutis Online
- Cost: £250–400 GBP

**Value:** Well-regarded by operators for supervisory roles. Cheaper and faster than NEBOSH. Get NEBOSH if you want the full qualification.

---

### 2.5 CSWIP / PCN Inspection Certification

**Who it's for:** Completions, integrity, or subsea inspection roles. Inspectors who certify weld quality and material integrity.

**CSWIP 3.1 (Welding Inspector):** Foundation for inspection roles
- Requires classroom study + practical exam
- UK-based exams (TWI — The Welding Institute)
- Some Angola providers offer preparatory courses

**Not essential for a graduate engineer** unless targeting an inspection specialization.

---

### 2.6 SPE Membership — Student to Professional

**SPE (Society of Petroleum Engineers):** The global professional society for petroleum engineers.

**Student membership:** ~$25 USD/year. Gives you:
- Access to OnePetro (thousands of technical papers)
- Access to SPE Textbooks (digital)
- Attendance at local section events (Luanda Section — network with industry)
- Career resources and job board

**Get this in Year 3. Keep it for life.**

**Angola SPE Section:** Luanda section has meetings with local industry professionals. This is direct networking access to the people who hire.

## Part 4: Financing Your Certifications

**BOSIET + Medical + H2S = ~$1,300 USD minimum.** This is real money. Here is how ISPTEC students fund this:

### Company Sponsorship (Best Option)

Once you receive a job offer, the single most important negotiation point is:
**"Will the company sponsor my BOSIET and required offshore certifications as part of onboarding?"**

Most major Angola operators (TotalEnergies, SLB, Halliburton, Azule) include certification sponsorship in graduate hire packages. Service companies with graduate programs (SLB FET, Halliburton LEAD) routinely pay for BOSIET and IWCF L1 as part of induction.

**How to ask:** "I am committed to completing all required certifications. Can you confirm which certifications the company covers as part of the onboarding package?"

### Personal Investment (If Applying Without Sponsorship)

Get the **Offshore Medical and H2S Awareness** first ($300 total). These are the cheapest and are required for even basic offshore site visits and familiarization trips. Getting these before applying signals serious intent to employers.

BOSIET ($1,000): Save toward this. Angola banks (BFA, BAI, BIC) may offer personal loans for professional development. Ask specifically about "empréstimo formação profissional."

### ISPTEC Faculty or Departmental Funding

Ask your petroleum engineering department head whether ISPTEC has:
- Industry partnerships with SLB/TotalEnergies/Sonangol that include student certifications
- Emergency fund or departmental training budget for advanced students
- Connections to the Sonangol Scholarship fund (bolsas) for professional certification

### ANPG and Sonangol Foundation Programs

Both ANPG and the Sonangol Foundation (Fundação Sonangol) have periodically offered training support programs for Angolan petroleum engineering students. Check their current offerings at:
- anpg.ao
- fundacaosonangol.ao

## Part 5: The Recommended Certification Timeline

### Year 3 (now):
- ✅ Join SPE Student Chapter ($25)
- ✅ ANPG registration verification familiarization
- 📋 **Plan:** Identify the BOSIET provider in Luanda, get current schedule and cost

### Year 3 Summer / Year 4 Start:
- ✅ **Offshore Medical** — book and complete ($200)
- ✅ **H2S Awareness** — online or Luanda provider ($100)
- 📋 **Start saving for BOSIET**

### Year 4 (1st semester):
- ✅ **BOSIET** — complete at PETROTEC or equivalent ($1,000)
- ✅ **IWCF Level 1** — complete ($400)
- 📋 **Begin NEBOSH IGC self-study** if targeting HSE/production track

### Year 4 (2nd semester / Estágio period):
- ✅ **NEBOSH IGC** — complete online theory + exam ($450)
- ✅ **IWCF Level 2** — complete if drilling/completions track ($900)
- 📋 **Application sprints** (see [Company_Targeting_Guide.md](Company_Targeting_Guide.md))

### Post-graduation (Month 1–6):
- ✅ Any company-sponsored certs from employer induction
- ✅ Complete any remaining certs from the track-specific list
