# Company Targeting Guide — Angola Offshore Market

> For ISPTEC Year 3–4 students. Covers every major company operating offshore Angola: what they do, how they hire, what the interview is like, what they pay trainees, insider positioning tips, and the direct application routes to use. Updated for current Angola market conditions.

---

## How to Use This Guide

1. Read [02_Six_Paths_Decoded.md](../02_Six_Paths_Decoded.md) and decide your primary career track
2. Use the **Path-to-Company Matrix** at the end of this document to identify your top 3–5 companies
3. Go to each company's section in this document and understand exactly what they want
4. Apply to all 3–5 simultaneously — do not wait for one rejection before applying to the next

---

## Part 1: OPERATOR COMPANIES — The Top of the Food Chain

Operators own the oil blocks, make the development decisions, and contract service companies. They typically hire fewer graduates per year than service companies but offer the best long-term career progression, highest salaries, and most prestigious offshore rotations.

---

### 1.1 TotalEnergies Angola

**Block presence:** Block 17 (operator — Girassol, Dalia, Pazflor, CLOV FPSOs), Block 32 (operator — Kaombo Norte/Sul), Block 48, Block 20 (gas)

**Angola headcount:** ~800–1,200 direct employees + contractors

**What they do:** Operated production, reservoir management, subsea infrastructure management, FPSO operations, Angola LNG gas development

**Graduate program:** **Young Graduate Program (YGP)**
- Intake: typically 5–15 Angolan graduates per year (petroleum, electrical, mechanical engineering)
- Duration: 12–18 months rotational program across functions
- Location: Luanda HQ + possible FPSO rotation
- Salary: competitive, among the best for fresh graduates in Angola

**What they want:**
- ISPTEC or related petroleum engineering graduates (strongly preferred)
- Angolan nationality (high local content compliance priority)
- French language ability is a STRONG differentiator (TotalEnergies is a French company)
- Technical performance: final year GPA, thesis quality, any relevant publications
- Leadership signals: SPE chapter involvement, community activities

**How to apply:**
- **careers.totalenergies.com** — search Angola
- Email to **recrutamento.angola@totalenergies.com** with CV + cover letter (verify current address)
- LinkedIn: connect with TotalEnergies Angola HR and reference a specific opening
- **Attend SPE Luanda Section events** — TotalEnergies engineers regularly present. Introduce yourself.

**Interview process:**
1. CV screening
2. Technical test (petroleum engineering fundamentals, typically 60–90 minutes)
3. Panel interview (HR + technical engineer) — mix of English and Portuguese
4. Final interview with department head

**Insider tip:** TotalEnergies Angola values *Angolidade* (cultural commitment to Angola's development). In your cover letter, speak to your long-term commitment to Angola's energy sector, not just your technical skills.

---

### 1.2 Azule Energy (formerly Esso Angola + BP Angola)

**Block presence:** Block 15 (operator — Kizomba A, B, C FPSOs; Plutão, Saturno, Urano, Netuno), Block 31 (PSVM FPSO), Block 17 (minority partner)

**Background:** Azule Energy is a 50/50 joint venture formed in 2022 by ExxonMobil and BP combining their Angola assets. It is the largest oil producer in Angola (~700,000 bbl/day in peak years).

**Angola headcount:** ~900 direct employees + large contractor base

**Graduate program:** Check for current program — post-merger, the graduate program structure was being reformed as of 2023–2024. Watch for "Azule Energy Angola Graduate Program" announcements.

**What they want:**
- Technical excellence (ISPTEC petroleum or engineering graduates)
- Angolan nationality
- English proficiency is essential (both former parent companies are English-language)
- Problem-solving ability — interview process typically includes case studies

**How to apply:**
- **azule-energy.com/careers** — primary route
- LinkedIn — "Azule Energy Angola careers"
- ANPG-organized career events where Azule presents

**Interview process:** Similar to TotalEnergies. Technical test + panel interview.

**Insider tip:** Azule is still a relatively young company and is defining its culture. Showing adaptability, willingness to work across legacy ExxonMobil and BP systems, and technical depth are all valued.

---

### 1.3 Chevron Angola (CABGOC — Cabinda Gulf Oil Company)

**Block presence:** Block 0 (Cabinda offshore/onshore), Block 14 (deepwater), minority stakes in Blocks 2 and 4

**Angola headcount:** ~1,000+ (including Cabinda operations)

**What they do:** Block 0 is a mature, shallower water field. Less deepwater than TotalEnergies/Azule but still significant production. Cabinda operations include onshore elements (less common for ISPTEC students).

**Graduate program:** Chevron Angola has a local hiring program aligned with the Cabinda local content requirements. Check for annual graduate intake.

**What they want:**
- Technical graduation (petroleum, mechanical, chemical, electrical)
- Angolan and especially Cabinda-province candidates for Block 0 operations (local content)
- English proficiency
- Reliability and safety orientation

**How to apply:**
- **chevron.com/careers** (international portal)
- Local Luanda recruitment events
- ANPG and ISP relationships — Chevron engages with local institutions

---

### 1.4 Eni Angola

**Block presence:** Block 15/06 (operated — producing), Block 28 (exploration)

**Block 15/06:** Includes the N'Goma FPSO and significant subsea infrastructure. Relatively newer deepwater development.

**Graduate program:** Eni has an international "Eni School of Technology" framework. Angola recruitment is via Luanda office.

**How to apply:**
- **eni.com/en-IT/careers.html** (global portal)
- Luanda office direct contact: inquire via LinkedIn or the ANPG directory

---

### 1.5 Sonangol Pesquisa e Produção (Sonangol E.P.)

**What it is:** Angola's national oil company. Participates as co-owner in virtually every Angola block. Has its own operated wells (smaller production). Manages the ANPG registration and local content compliance for all operators.

**Why target Sonangol:**
- **The local content gateway:** Working for Sonangol or its subsidiaries is the most direct path to ANPG registration and operator experience
- **Connections:** Sonangol staff move between Sonangol and all operators on joint ventures — strong network
- **Career path:** Sonangol EP → operator joint venture technical committee role → operator secondment

**Graduate program:** **Sonangol Formation Program** (Programa de Formação Sonangol) — historically one of the most competitive graduate programs in Angola. Intake of 20–60 graduates per year across engineering specializations.

**What they want:**
- ISPTEC graduates specifically (Sonangol is an ISPTEC institutional partner)
- Angolan nationality (required)
- Good academic performance
- Long-term commitment to Angola's energy sector

**How to apply:**
- **sonangol.ao/emprego** or Sonangol HR Luanda
- ISPTEC career office — has direct Sonangol recruitment contact
- ANPG career events
- **Watch for:** Annual "Recrutamento de Jovens Engenheiros" announcement (typically posted through ISPTEC and Sonangol website)

**Insider tip:** Sonangol's graduate program may not pay as much as SLB or TotalEnergies initially, but the experience on joint venture technical committees puts you in the room with EVERY major operator in Angola. This is worth more than slightly higher pay in the first 2 years.

## Part 2: OILFIELD SERVICE COMPANIES — The Best Entry Route for Most Students

Service companies are where most ISPTEC graduates will start. They have larger intake, structured graduate programs, pay well, provide the most hands-on technical training, and their rotational programs put you offshore faster.

---

### 2.1 SLB (formerly Schlumberger) Angola

**What they do in Angola:** Drilling fluids, cementing, wireline logging, MWD/LWD, completion tools (OneSubsea), production optimization, reservoir simulation, seismic services. SLB provides services on virtually every Angola well.

**Angola headcount:** 300–600 direct employees (fluctuates with drilling activity)

**Graduate program:** **SLB Field Engineer Training (FET) Program**
- Duration: 18–24 months
- Start: 3–6 months in Luanda for admin/induction, then field rotations (offshore and onshore)
- What you become: Field engineer in one of SLB's product lines (cementing, wireline, drilling fluids, completions tools, etc.)
- Pay: $2,500–4,000 USD/month during training (Angola-level, includes offshore day rates)

**What they want:**
- Engineering degree (petroleum, mechanical, chemical, electrical — all relevant)
- Strong GPA (> 14 valores at ISPTEC)
- Communicates well in English
- Physically fit and willing to work offshore rotation
- Analytically strong — FET program is academically intense

**How to apply:**
- **slb.com/careers** (search "Angola" or "Africa")
- **LinkedIn:** Follow SLB Angola page. Connect with SLB Angola HR (search on LinkedIn)
- Email: **angola.recruitment@slb.com** (verify current HR contact via LinkedIn)
- ISPTEC career days: SLB regularly recruits at ISPTEC

**Interview process:**
1. Online application + CV screening
2. Technical online assessment (engineering fundamentals, problem-solving — 45–90 min)
3. Panel interview (HR + technical lead + local manager)
4. Background check + drug test + offshore medical
5. Offer + FET program start

**Insider tip:** SLB's hiring process is rigorous. The online assessment filters most candidates. **Prepare the assessment by practicing:** dimensional analysis, fluid mechanics basics, pressure calculation, math reasoning. Online practice resources: SLB "CareerFIT" (google this for examples of their assessments).

---

### 2.2 Halliburton Angola

**What they do in Angola:** Cementing (their historical strength), drilling fluids (Baroid brand), completion tools (WPS), wireline, drill bits (Security DBS), project management.

**Angola headcount:** 200–400

**Graduate program:** **Halliburton Landmark LEAD Program (Learning and Education for Accelerated Development)**
- Focus: More technical/engineering roles than SLB's field engineer focus
- Can lead to software roles (Landmark software: WellPlan, DecisionSpace), technical consulting, or field operations

**What they want:**
- Engineering graduate with strong academic record
- English proficiency essential
- Angolan nationality preferred for local hiring compliance

**How to apply:**
- **halliburton.com/en/careers** — search Angola
- LinkedIn: Follow Halliburton Angola, connect with HR
- ISPTEC career fairs

**Interview process:** Similar to SLB. Technical test + panel interview. Halliburton's technical test tends to focus on engineering problem-solving with less pure petroleum engineering content than SLB.

### 2.4 TechnipFMC Angola

**What they do:** Subsea trees, wellheads, subsea manifolds, umbilicals, flexible pipes, subsea processing systems. **Every major Angola deepwater FPSO has TechnipFMC/FMC subsea trees.**

**Angola-specific:** TechnipFMC has significant presence in Angola related to ongoing and brownfield subsea projects. They also have a local fabrication/assembly facility at **Sonils (Sonamet)** through partnerships.

**Graduate/trainee program:** Check TechnipFMC's current Angola recruitment. They periodically hire local engineers for subsea integrity, project engineering, and commissioning roles.

**How to apply:**

### 2.5 Subsea 7 Angola

**Angola presence:** Luanda base office. Vessels operate in all Angola blocks when installation campaigns are active.

**Roles for graduates:**

**How to apply:**

### 2.6 Oceaneering Angola

**Angola presence:** Luanda office. ROV work on all active deepwater blocks.

**Roles for graduates:**

## Part 3: FPSO OPERATORS — A Growing Sector

FPSO operators (SBM Offshore, Yinson, MODEC) own and operate the FPSOs under long-term leases with the oil companies. They hire their own process, maintenance, HSE, and marine engineers.

### 3.1 SBM Offshore Angola

**What they do:** Lease and operate FPSOs (Mondo, Saxi Batuque, Ngoma for Azule/ExxonMobil; others). SBM operates the vessel; the oil company operates the production well system.

**Roles:** Process engineer, maintenance engineer, marine superintendent, HSE engineer, topsides production technician

**Graduate/trainee path:** SBM has an Angolan Cadet / Trainee Officer program for marine and process roles. This leads to offshore rotation with competitive pay.

**How to apply:**
- **sbmoffshore.com/careers**
- LinkedIn SBM Offshore Angola
- Luanda office direct HR

---

### 3.2 Yinson Angola

**What they do:** FPSO operations (FPSO Helios on Block 15/06 for Eni). Yinson is a Malaysian-headquartered FPSO company expanding in Angola.

**Roles:** Similar to SBM — process, HSE, marine, maintenance

**How to apply:**
- **yinson.com/careers**
- LinkedIn Yinson Angola

## Part 4: LOCAL ANGOLAN COMPANIES — For First Experience and ANPG Credit

These companies are critical for ANPG local content compliance and are the most accessible entry point for ISPTEC graduates without international experience.

### 4.1 PAENAL (Porto Amboim Enterprises Angola)

**What they do:** Fabrication of subsea structures (manifolds, pipeline end structures, topsides modules) for offshore oil and gas projects. Located at Porto Amboim (Kwanza Sul Province).

**Why it matters:** PAENAL fabricates the steel structures that go onto the seabed for TotalEnergies, Azule, and other operators. Working here puts you close to the real hardware.

**Roles:** Structural engineer, QA/QC inspector, safety engineer, project coordinator

**ANPG credit:** High local content score.

**How to apply:**
- LinkedIn PAENAL Angola
- Direct application to Porto Amboim HR office
- ANPG-qualified company list often includes PAENAL for subcontracts

---

### 4.2 Sonamet

**What they do:** Offshore fabrication and installation services. Subsidiary of Sonangol. Has facilities in Lobito.

**Roles:** Structural fabrication engineer, piping inspector, safety engineer, project engineer

**How to apply:**
- sonamet.ao (website/HR)
- LinkedIn Sonamet

---

### 4.3 Petromar (Saipem Angola)

**What they do:** Marine and subsea installation services, pipelay, platform installation (historically in Block 0). Saipem's Angola subsidiary.

**Roles:** Marine engineer, offshore installation engineer, pipeline engineer

**How to apply:**
- saipem.com/careers (international Saipem portal)
- Local Luanda HR for Petromar

## Part 7: Cold Email Template

When applying directly (not via a job posting):

---

**Subject line:** Petroleum Engineering Graduate Application — [Your Name] — [Target Role e.g., Drilling Field Engineer Trainee]

**Body:**

Dear [Recruiter name or "Recruitment Team"],

My name is [Name], and I am a [Year 3/4] petroleum engineering student at ISPTEC in Luanda. I am writing to express my strong interest in a trainee or graduate position with [Company Name] in Angola.

I have focused my studies and preparation specifically on [your track, e.g., drilling engineering], having studied Engenharia de Poços, Fluidos de Perfuração, and completed self-study in well control (IWCF Level 1) and [relevant course/cert]. I have also completed [any project/internship/SPE activity].

Angola's deepwater sector — particularly [specific blocks or FPSOs where the company operates] — is exactly the environment I am preparing for. I have read [specific company technical paper or Angola project they are involved in] and found [specific thing you found interesting]. This is the work I want to do.

I attach my CV. I would welcome the opportunity to speak with your team about how I can contribute during a graduate or trainee placement.

Yours sincerely,
[Name] | ISPTEC Year [X] | [Phone] | [Email] | [LinkedIn URL]

---

**Cold email tips:**
1. **Personalize the middle paragraph.** Generic emails are deleted. Name a specific Angola project the company is involved in.
2. **Short is better.** 4–6 sentences. Recruiters in Angola receive many CVs and read short emails.
3. **Send in English.** Even for Portuguese-speaking companies. It signals professionalism and proves your English.
4. **Follow up once.** If no response in 2 weeks, send one follow-up. Do not send more than one follow-up.
5. **LinkedIn connection.** After the email, find the recruiter or HR person on LinkedIn and connect with a short note referencing your email.

## Part 8: How to Stand Out — The ISPTEC Student Advantage

Angola's local content regulations mean every company **wants** to hire Angolan engineers. The problem is that most ISPTEC graduates apply without having done additional preparation. The bar is often low. Here is what separates you:

1. **Certifications before you apply** (see [Certifications_Angola.md](Certifications_Angola.md))
2. **Software on your CV:** Prosper, CMG student version, Python for data analysis, AutoCAD — any of these puts you above 80% of applicants
3. **A specific, relevant final year project:** Choose a topic related to Angola (Block 17 reservoir simulation, FPSO gas lift optimization, local content analysis)
4. **SPE Luanda Section attendance and participation** — companies see your name on the attendee list
5. **English and French** — If you speak French at a professional level, you will skip the interview queue at TotalEnergies Angola
6. **Reference from an industry professional** — One professional reference from someone at the target company or sector is worth 10 applications
