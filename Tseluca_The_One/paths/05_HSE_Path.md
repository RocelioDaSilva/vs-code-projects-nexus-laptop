# Path: HSE / Safety Engineer — From ISPTEC to Offshore Safety Role

> **Time to first offshore rotation:** 6–12 months post-graduation (fastest realistic offshore entry for ANY track)
> **Typical entry company:** Any operator, any service company, any FPSO contractor — HSE is mandatory everywhere
> **Physical environment:** FPSO, drillship, construction vessel — your choice of environment
> **Schedule:** 28/28 or 21/21 rotation

---

## Why HSE Is the Most Underrated Path to Offshore

Most petroleum engineering students don't consider HSE as a career. They see it as a "non-technical" or "lesser" path. This is wrong, and understanding why changes the game for graduates who want offshore rotation fast.

**The reality:**
1. **Every offshore installation must have qualified HSE personnel** — it is a legal requirement under Angolan law, ANPG regulations, and every major operator's safety management system
2. **There are more HSE positions offshore than drilling or reservoir engineering positions** — an FPSO might have 2 drilling engineers during a campaign and 8 HSE officers permanently
3. **HSE is not "non-technical"** — it requires deep knowledge of process safety, risk analysis, fire and explosion dynamics, emergency response planning, and regulatory compliance
4. **HSE is a gateway** — many engineers start in HSE, build their offshore experience and network, then transition to their intended technical specialization after 2–3 years with genuine rig or FPSO experience on their CV

If your goal is offshore rotation within 12 months of graduation and you have the right certifications, HSE is your fastest route.

## What HSE Engineers Actually Do Offshore

### Daily Responsibilities

**Morning routine:**
- Review overnight incident log and safety alerts from other installations
- Issue Permit to Work (PTW) for the day's planned maintenance and operations activities
- Conduct toolbox talks with work crews before planned activities (simultaneous operations, hot work, confined space entry, working at height)

**During the day:**
- Walk the installation: check PPE compliance, housekeeping, fire extinguisher conditions, emergency lighting
- Attend safety-critical operation briefings (critical lifts, simultaneous operations SIMOPS)
- Investigate any incidents, near-misses, or unsafe acts — complete preliminary incident report
- Audit compliance with safety procedures: Permit to Work accuracy, isolation effectiveness, gas detector calibration

**Administrative:**
- Update safety statistics (LTI rate, incident frequency rate, near-miss count)
- Prepare for upcoming safety audits or client visits
- Review and update Job Safety Analyses (JSA) for upcoming operations
- Complete training records for crew members

**Emergency Response:**
- Lead or participate in emergency drills (fire, gas alarm, man overboard)
- On an actual emergency: follow the Emergency Response Plan, manage evacuation, liaise with the Offshore Installation Manager (OIM)
- Coordinate with onshore Emergency Response Team during a major event

### On a Drillship

HSE officers on drillships manage additional drilling-specific risks:
- Well control compliance: are all BOP maintenance records current? Are mud loggers following gas alarm procedures?
- H2S safety: planning, pre-job analysis, gas detector placement during sour zones
- Dropped object prevention: every tool lifted on a rig has potential drop energy calculation
- Night works and simultaneous operations with multiple service companies

### Long-Term Career Trajectory

```
Year 0–1: HSE Officer (offshore rotation)
  └── Learning the installation, the PTW system, the specific hazards
  └── Building your incident investigation and audit skills

Year 1–3: Senior HSE Officer
  └── Lead HSE Officer on an FPSO or drillship
  └── Incident investigation lead
  └── Mentoring junior HSE staff

Year 3–6: HSE Supervisor / HSE Lead
  └── Responsible for HSE performance of an entire installation
  └── Interface with operator HSE management
  └── Audits and compliance with operator safety case

Year 5–10: HSE Manager / Corporate HSE Role
  └── Managing HSE systems across multiple installations
  └── Setting safety standards, managing regulatory relationships
  └── Angola-based: $8,000–14,000/month at this level
```

## The Certifications That Get You In

Unlike any other track, HSE has a recognized international qualification path that is entirely accessible pre-graduation:

### NEBOSH International General Certificate (IGC) — Your Key Credential

The NEBOSH International General Certificate is the most widely recognized entry-level HSE qualification for offshore and industrial roles. It is the difference between "petroleum engineering student with safety interest" and "qualified HSE professional."

**What it covers:**
- Unit IG1: Management of International Health and Safety (open book exam)
- Unit IG2: Risk Assessment (practical — you submit a risk assessment from your workplace or study environment)

**How to get it:**
- Study: ~120 hours (can be self-paced online)
- Cost: $500–1,500 depending on provider and format (distance learning vs classroom)
- Pass rate: ~70% on first attempt for prepared candidates
- Providers: Available online from NEBOSH accredited centers; search for Angola/Africa region providers or international online providers

**When to start:** Year 4, Semester 7. Begin studying during the semester, sit the exam during exam breaks or after semester ends.

### IOSH Managing Safely (Stepping Stone)

A shorter, less demanding qualification that you can get before NEBOSH. Demonstrates HSE awareness. Useful as an interim while studying for NEBOSH. Duration: ~3 days. Cost: ~$200–400.

### Additional Offshore HSE Certifications

| Certification | Importance | When |
|---|---|---|
| BOSIET/HUET | MANDATORY | Year 3 Sem 6 |
| Offshore Medical | MANDATORY | Year 3 Sem 6 |
| H2S Awareness | MANDATORY | Before offshore |
| NEBOSH IGC | ESSENTIAL | Year 4 |
| GWO Basic Safety Training | HIGH | On the job |
| Incident Investigation (ICAM or TapRoot) | HIGH | First 2 years |
| NEBOSH Process Safety Management (PSM) | MEDIUM | Year 2–3 on the job |
| IEMA Certificate (Environmental) | MEDIUM | First 3 years |

## Entry Routes: How to Actually Get In

### Route A: Any Tier 2 Service Company — Wide Application

SLB, Halliburton, Baker Hughes, Subsea 7, TechnipFMC, Saipem all have permanent HSE headcount. They need HSE officers on their drilling and construction operations. Apply broadly with BOSIET + NEBOSH IGC (or in progress).

**Application message:** "Petroleum Engineering graduate, BOSIET/HUET certified, NEBOSH IGC [in progress/completed], seeking HSE Officer / Junior Safety Engineer role for offshore Angola operations."

### Route B: FPSO Operators and Contractors

TotalEnergies, Azule Energy, SBM Offshore, Yinson, MODEC all need HSE officers on their FPSOs. FPSO roles are typically 28/28 and the most stable offshore positions.

### Route C: Local Safety Consultancies (Pre-Offshore)

Some Angolan companies provide safety consultancy and training services. Working here for 6–12 months before targeting offshore gives you PTW experience, JSA writing practice, and incident investigation experience — all of which are valued by offshore HSE recruiters.

## Essential HSE Knowledge Areas

### Process Safety (FPSO-focused)

Process safety deals with preventing major accidents (fire, explosion, toxic release) in industrial facilities. Key tools:

**Bowtie Analysis:**
```
[Threats] → [Prevention Barriers] → [TOP EVENT] → [Recovery Barriers] → [Consequences]
   │                  │               Loss of        │                    │
   │                 PTW              Containment    Emergency             Fire
   │              Inspection                         Shutdown              Explosion
   │              Testing                            ESD valve             Toxic release
   │             Procedures                          Depressurization
```

**HAZOP (Hazard and Operability Study):**
A structured technique used during design to systematically identify hazards. Uses guide words (NO, MORE, LESS, REVERSE, OTHER THAN) applied to process parameters (flow, pressure, temperature, level) on each line and vessel.

**Fire and Gas System:**
FPSOs have fire detectors (heat, smoke, flame) and gas detectors (point, open-path) throughout. Understanding the detection logic, alarm levels (A1, A2), and automatic actions (ESD activation, deluge release) is fundamental HSE knowledge.

**Emergency Shutdown (ESD) Philosophy:**
- ESD-1: Wellhead shutdown (closes subsurface safety valves)
- ESD-2: Process shutdown (isolates production, closes FPSO inlet valves)
- ESD-3: Utility shutdown
- TR (Total Release): Evacuation alarm, turbine shutdown, full depressurization initiation

### Permit to Work System

The PTW is the formal authorization system for all non-routine activities offshore. Without a PTW, no maintenance, no hot work, no confined space entry happens. As an HSE officer, you are the PTW controller.

**Categories of permits:**
- **General Work Permit (Cold Work):** Low-hazard tasks in hazardous areas
- **Hot Work Permit:** Welding, grinding, open flame — requires fire watch and gas test
- **Confined Space Entry Permit:** Entry into tanks, vessels, sumps — requires atmospheric testing, rescue plan, standby man
- **Electrical Isolation Permit:** Working on energized electrical equipment — LOTO required
- **Excavation / Penetration Permit:** Breaking containment or penetrating decks/vessels

### Incident Investigation

Every incident (and near-miss) is investigated to find root causes. The standard methodology in Angola's offshore sector is typically either:
- **ICAM (Incident Cause Analysis Method):** Used by TotalEnergies and others
- **TapRoot:** Used by many US-operated facilities
- **5 Whys:** Simple root cause chain for minor incidents

You must be able to lead an investigation: gather evidence, interview witnesses, build a causal chain, identify systemic failures, write a corrective action plan.

## Technical Interview Preparation: 15 Questions

1. What is a PTW system? Why is it important offshore?
2. What is a JSA (Job Safety Analysis)? Walk me through how you write one.
3. What is the hierarchy of controls? Give examples at each level for a drilling operation.
4. What is a HAZOP? What guide words are used?
5. What is process safety? How is it different from occupational safety?
6. What is LOTO? Describe the isolation sequence for a pump.
7. What is H2S? What are the danger thresholds? What do you do if the H2S alarm sounds?
8. What is a bowtie diagram? Draw and explain one for a well blowout scenario.
9. What is an SECE (Safety and Environmentally Critical Element)?
10. What is a Safety Case? What does it contain?
11. Describe the ESD philosophy on an FPSO.
12. What is a confined space? What testing do you do before entry?
13. What is the difference between an LTI and a near-miss? Why do we track both?
14. You discover a crew member working without PPE in a hazardous area. What do you do?
15. A gas alarm sounds on the FPSO. Walk me through your immediate actions as the HSE officer on shift.
