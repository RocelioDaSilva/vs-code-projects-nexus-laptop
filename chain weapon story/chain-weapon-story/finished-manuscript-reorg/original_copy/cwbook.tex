% ══════════════════════════════════════════════════════════════════
%  CHAPTER STRIPE
%
%  One clean rectangle on the right edge.
%  Its Y-position slides down the page as chapters increase.
%  It is filled with the active character's colour.
%  No lines, no ticks, no numbers — nothing else on the margin.
% ══════════════════════════════════════════════════════════════════
\newcommand{\ChapterStripe}{%
  \begin{tikzpicture}[remember picture, overlay]
    \coordinate (TR) at (current page.north east);
    \coordinate (BR) at (current page.south east);
    % Bar width: 5 mm  (visible from book edge, not intrusive on page)
    \pgfmathsetmacro{\BarW}{0.50}
    % Slot height = full page / total chapters
    \pgfmathsetmacro{\SlotH}{\paperheight / \TotalChapters}
    % Block top and bottom in pt
    \pgfmathsetmacro{\BlockTop}{\paperheight - (\CurrentChapterNum - 1)*\SlotH}
    \pgfmathsetmacro{\BlockBot}{\paperheight - \CurrentChapterNum*\SlotH}
    % Draw the single rectangle
    \fill[\POVColor]
      ($(BR)-(\BarW cm,0)+(0,\BlockTop pt)$)
      rectangle
      ($(BR)+(0,\BlockBot pt)$);
  \end{tikzpicture}%
}

\lettrine[lines=3, lraise=0.05]{T}{he wind came down}
from the Grey Mountains carrying the smell of burnt cedar and
something older --- something that had no name in the common tongue,
but which every soldier recognised in the hollow of their ribs.
Aisen tightened the strap of his vambrace and said nothing.

Below, the valley curved like a wound in the earth, its floor dark
with the remnants of what had been three villages not a fortnight
past. The smoke still rose in thin, patient threads, as though the
ground itself had not yet decided whether to forgive.

{\color{Stone}\itshape ``We should not have come this way,''} said
the boy behind him. He did not turn. The boy said many true things.
That was what made him dangerous to keep near and impossible to
send away.

\SceneBreak

He counted the fires. Seven. No --- eight, if the far glow past the
treeline was theirs and not some shepherd's accident. Eight fires
meant a column, not a raid. A column meant orders. Orders meant
someone, somewhere, had decided this particular valley was worth
the cost of making it into a memory.

Aisen had spent seventeen years learning the difference between
decisions made in rooms and decisions that lived in the soil
afterward. He had yet to find a word for the distance between them.

The boy's name was Edric. He was sixteen, which meant he had never
known a year without the war, and also meant he was precisely old
enough to believe that this was a fact worth mourning. Aisen had
stopped mourning facts some time around his thirtieth season.
There were too many of them, and the mourning cost the same whether
or not you could change the thing you were mourning.

\SceneBreak

{\color{Stone}\itshape ``How many do you count?''} he asked.

Edric shifted behind him. {\color{Stone}\itshape ``Eight.
Unless the far one is a shepherd.''}

{\color{Stone}\itshape ``It isn't.''}

{\color{Stone}\itshape ``How do you know?''}

{\color{Stone}\itshape ``Shepherds don't build fires at the
treeline.''} He finally turned.
{\color{Stone}\itshape ``They build them where the animals are.''}

Edric looked at the fire. He was going to ask something else ---
Aisen could see it taking shape in the set of his jaw --- and then
he decided not to, which meant he had already guessed the answer.
He was almost proud of him for that.
