# Telekinesis — Numeric System & Guidelines

This file defines a compact, story‑friendly numeric system for telekinetic power (used in conjunction with the chain spear specs). Use these numbers to ground scenes, calculate costs, and create believable limits.

---

## Units & Scales
- **MU** — Mana Unit (abstract unit for magical cost).
- **Pool:** the total MU a character can store at a time.
- **Regen:** MU regained per minute while resting or meditating.

### Example Tiers (world scale)
- **Novice:** 30–80 MU pool (basic spells, slow regen).
- **Journeyman:** 150–350 MU pool.
- **Expert:** 400–1200 MU pool.
- **Archmage:** 1200+ MU pool.

### Aisen (baseline example)
- **Pool:** **120 MU**
- **Rest Regen:** **2 MU/min** (idle); **4 MU/min** after 10+ minutes of focused meditation.
- **Burst reserve:** can comfortably expend 30–40 MU in intense sequences before severe fatigue.

---

## Force Tiers (practical mapping of MU → effect)
- **Tier 0 (Subtle):** 0.5–1 MU — tactile sensing, boundary sense, thread probe.
- **Tier 1 (Light):** 1–5 MU — small pushes, fine adjustments, minor torque on unladen levers.
- **Tier 2 (Medium):** 5–15 MU — move small objects (5–30 kg) with purpose; apply joint torque; adjust momentum of thrown blades.
- **Tier 3 (Heavy):** 15–40 MU — move heavy objects (30–200 kg) or exert sustained force for several seconds.
- **Tier 4 (Extreme):** 40+ MU — heavy lifting and field‑changing force; requires large pools or multiple casters.

Note: exact mass figures are narrative approximations to help writers judge difficulty. Use the Tier model rather than strict Newtonian conversion unless you want technical realism.

---

## Standard Technique Costs (Aisen baseline, typical values)
- **Point Push/Pull:** 1 MU per impulse (Tier 1). Sustaining a hold costs 0.5 MU/min.
- **Torque Twist:** 2–6 MU (Tier 1–2) depending on target mass and leverage point.
- **Momentum Adjustment (nudge):** 3 MU per adjustment (Tier 2).
- **Trajectory Curving:** 3–6 MU per object altered (Tier 2).
- **Sustained Float:** 4 MU to hold ~5 kg indefinitely (Tier 2; scales roughly +0.8 MU per additional 5 kg).
- **Boundary Sense:** 0.2 MU continuous while active; be careful: running many background sensory threads adds up.
- **Thread Sense (probe):** 0.5–1 MU per probe; maintain at 0.2 MU/min.
- **Construct Tether (to detached chain segment):** creation 3 MU; maintenance 0.4 MU/min.

---

## Fatigue & Overload
- **Mild fatigue:** reaching 60% of pool — reaction time slows slightly, small tremors.
- **Severe fatigue:** 80–90% — headaches, nosebleed, blurred vision; accuracy drops by ~30%.
- **Collapse/Blackout:** exceeding pool (100%+ draw, e.g., through blood sacrifice, external boost) risks loss of consciousness and possible tissue damage.
- **Recovery:** rest regen applies, but physical exhaustion requires long rest. Regeneration is non‑linear: deeper depletion slows recover rate until the body rests.

---

## Interactions & Counters
- **Magical interference fields** reduce effective MU by a factor (e.g., −30–80%). Design those fields to create tactical constraints.
- **Enchanted resistance:** Certain materials (dense memory‑metal, Aelfar alloys) require +50–200% MU to affect.
- **Multiple tethers:** the human brain can only actively manipulate 1–2 high‑precision telekinetic threads concurrently without major efficiency loss; maintain only low‑cost background holds for more.

---

## Training, Efficiency Gains & Scaling
- **Years at academy:** basic efficiency gains 5–20% per year for focused practice on a technique (reduces MU cost by that percent).
- **Mastery:** a signature technique can be reduced to ~50% of base MU with specialized training and anchoring aids.

---

## Example Combat Calculation (Aisen baseline)
Sequence: telekinetically toggle 3 segments (4 MU per segment telekinetic), create 1 tether (3 MU), perform 3 point pushes (1 MU each), maintain tether for 2 minutes (0.4 MU/min × 2).

- Toggle cost: 3 × 4 = **12 MU**
- Tether create: **3 MU**
- Pushes: 3 × 1 = **3 MU**
- Tether maintain: 0.8 MU
- **Total immediate cost:** ~**18 MU**; two‑minute maintenance adds 0.8 MU.

This is ~15% of Aisen’s 120 MU pool and therefore sustainable as a tactical sequence with a short rest to recover.

---

## Failure Modes & Narrative Effects
- **Partial failure:** when interference cuts effective MU by 30% mid‑sequence — tethers droop, detached blades lag, and snap‑lock reliability drops (great scene beat).
- **Feedback:** attempting Tier 3 actions at low pool causes neural backlash: disorientation, sensory ringing, and blurred boundary sense for minutes.
