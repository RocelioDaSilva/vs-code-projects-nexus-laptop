# Combat Choreography — Master Reference

## ASSUMPTIONS & NOTATION

**Baseline:** Aisen pool ~120 MU (see TELEKINESIS-NUMERIC.md)

**Notation:**
- **MU:** Mana Units (costs)
- **Toggle:** telekinetic toggle of a chain segment (≈4 MU per 5-link segment for Aisen)
- **Push:** 1 MU point push
- **Tether:** 3 MU create / 0.4 MU/min maintain

These sequences show practical telekinesis + chain spear use with MU cost breakdowns, failure modes, and narrative beats.

---

## SEQUENCE 1 — ALLEY AMBUSH (Close Quarters Reaction)

**Context:** Narrow alley, two dagger attackers close from front and flank. Aisen has short‑pole ready; room for one step lateral movement.

### Tactical Beats (Fast, Reactive)
1. Boundary/Thread Sense ON (0.2 MU) — immediate micro‑alerts of contact and edge pressure
2. Segment Toggle ×3 (12 MU) — detach and arc three segments into short whip for reach and snap‑disruption
3. Create Tether to core (3 MU) — anchors telekinetic control to detached segment
4. Point Push on attacker A's wrist (1 MU) — torque to drop dagger
5. Trajectory Curving: redirect thrown dagger from attacker B (3 MU) — glance off wall
6. Rapid torque twist on whip strike (2 MU) + physical step-back sweep
7. Maintain tether 1 minute (0.4 MU) while securing alley

**Cost Summary:** Toggle 12 + Tether 3 + Push 1 + Curve 3 + Torque 2 + Maintain 0.4 = **~21.4 MU** (≈18% of pool)

**Fatigue:** Mild — reaction speed slightly reduced; no major backlash expected

**Link Integrity:** High‑speed snap/whip increases pivot wear — accelerated LIP wear if repeated

**Author Cues (Sensory):**
- Metallic whispering as segments peel free
- Faint tug like a heartbeat as tether takes hold
- Dagger's flight slowed and tipped by single millimetre

**Failure Mode (If Interrupted):**
- If interference field drops effective MU by 30%, curve fails
- Thrown blade embeds in wood, tether slips
- Aisen relies on physical parry — strong dramatic beat

## SEQUENCE 2 — OPEN FIELD DUEL (Momentum Exchange & Feint)

**Context:** One‑on‑one duel in open ground. Opponent uses mid‑range heavy polearm. Aisen uses reach and momentum to unbalance and create openings.

### Tactical Beats (Measured, Tactical)
1. Two‑segment extension to medium whip (toggle ×2 = 8 MU), core shaft kept for quick bracing
2. Momentum Adjustment on incoming swing (3 MU) — nudge opponent's pole tip off centerline as it passes
3. Feint: physically step into false opening while telekinetic torque twist (5 MU) adds spin to spear head, changing blade angle mid‑flight
4. Trajectory Curving on follow‑through (6 MU) — arc spear past armor seam to nick leg
5. Follow with physical reap: hook opponent's ankle with chain loop + pull (low MU tether assist 1 MU)

**Cost Summary:** Toggle 8 + Momentum 3 + Torque 5 + Curve 6 + Tether assist 1 = **23 MU immediate**

**Tactical Note:** Spread actions across rhythm — telekinetic adjustments reduce telegraphing while multiplying threat vectors

**Author Cues:**
- Spear's path unexpectedly crooked
- Eye‑glinting seam opens along opponent's boot as if guided by invisible fingers

**Failure Mode:**
- Opponent plants weighted counter‑step timed to momentum nudge
- Hook fails, Aisen momentarily off‑balance
- Good for comeback scene

## SEQUENCE 3 — SUSTAINED DEFENSE (Area Denial, High Cost)

**Context:** Holding ruined bridge against wave of light infantry. Aisen must create barrier and delay while allies withdraw. High MU, narratively sacrificial if needed.

### Tactical Beats (Sustained, Staging)
1. Create multiple tethers to two chain segments + dangling structural beam (3 MU × 3 = 9 MU)
2. Telekinetic holds: float and hold two heavy segments to form swinging curtain (sustained float ≈ 6–8 MU each) — ~14 MU for both
3. Repeated point pushes to stagger charging enemies (1 MU × 6 = 6 MU)
4. Create area drag: torque and drag soaked plank into choke point (torque 6 MU + curve 6 MU)
5. Maintain tethers and holds for 2–3 minutes (0.4 MU/min per × 3 = ~2.4 MU over 2 min)

**Cost Summary:** Create 9 + Holds 14 + Pushes 6 + Torque/Curve 12 + Maintain 2.4 = **~43.4 MU** over 2 minutes (≈36% of pool)

**Fatigue:** Significant — expect severe fatigue at sequence end (neural ringing, blurred boundary sense). If repeated or extended, severe fatigue → blackout risk

**Narrative Beats:**
- Chains hang like teeth across bridge
- Each held segment hums with white haze
- Soldiers slam against invisible curtain and stumble
- Time bought at visible cost

**Failure Mode:**
- If MU suddenly cut (field or wound), tethers drop mid‑swing
- Suspended planks crash unpredictably
- Great for last‑second rescue or costly sacrifice moment

## SEQUENCE 4 — URBAN SUPPRESSION (Non‑Lethal Area Control)

**Context:** Narrow market street where civilians funnel through. Aisen must delay looters and create corridor for evacuation.

### Tactical Beats
1. Rapid low toggles to create semi‑rigid curtain at shoulder height (toggle 4 segments = 16 MU)
2. Tether two segments as soft buffers (2 × 3 MU = 6 MU), maintain short pulsed pushes as people pass (1 MU × 6 = 6 MU)
3. Use gentle trajectory curving to guide debris away from fleeing civilians (3 MU × 2 = 6 MU)

**Cost Summary:** ~34 MU immediate; maintenance ~0.8 MU/min

**Design Philosophy:** Non‑lethal — emphasis on nudging and blocking rather than heavy strikes

**Narrative Value:** Shows Aisen's restraint and tactical creativity; humanizes his use of power

---

## SEQUENCE 5 — CAVALRY DISRUPTOR (Anti‑Mount Tactics)

**Context:** Fast attack by light cavalry along river road. Aisen cannot kill horses; must dismount riders and clog road.

### Tactical Beats
1. Telekinetic toggle to release two mid‑chain segments and spin them low as sweeping net (toggle telekinetically 2 = 8 MU)
2. Create two tethers to moving segments to hold in semi‑rigid sweep (2 × 3 MU = 6 MU)
3. Use point pushes to misdirect horse momentum at critical moments (1 MU × 3 = 3 MU)

**Cost Summary:** ~17 MU immediately; maintenance depends on duration (0.4 MU/min per tether)

**Risk:** High mass of horse means higher LIP wear if strikes contact. Use as area denial rather than repeated entanglement

**Tactical Note:** Effective crowd control without lethal intent; shows tactical flexibility

---

## SEQUENCE 6 — ZIPLINE RESCUE & CASUALTY EXTRACTION

**Context:** Collapsed bridge with civilians trapped across span. Aisen must ferry wounded using telekinetic tether and chain harness.

### Tactical Beats
1. Create strong tether anchor to structural beam (3 MU) and attach chain loop harness (3 MU)
2. Use sustained float to keep loop stable while moving single casualty (≈6–8 MU depending on mass)
3. Short point pushes assist return trips (1 MU each). Maintain anchor for duration (0.4 MU/min)

**Cost Summary:** ~12–16 MU per trip plus maintenance

**Narrative Value:** High maintenance cost over repeated trips = realistic sacrifice moment if Aisen keeps anchor too long

**Dramatic Beats:**
- Visible strain as tether holds steady
- Single civilian suspended over drop
- Aisen's focus narrowing with each repeated effort
- Physical exhaustion mounting

## AUTHOR NOTES & TUNING

### Sequence Usage Guide

**Sequence 1 (Alley Ambush):**
- Use for fast, reactive action scenes
- Aisen looks agile and resourceful
- Keep MU low so recovery is believable
- Good for establishing tactical competence early

**Sequence 2 (Field Duel):**
- Use for duels emphasizing cunning and misdirection
- Telekinesis here is subtle and surgical
- Shows mastery over raw power
- Effective against higher-skilled single opponents

**Sequence 3 (Sustained Defense):**
- Use sparingly — it's a dramatic, costly move
- Should visibly change character afterward
- High stakes, high cost, high reward
- Perfect for climactic last stand scenarios

**Sequence 4 (Urban Suppression):**
- Shows restraint and discrimination
- Useful for scenes where Aisen must protect civilians
- Demonstrates tactical flexibility
- Establishes moral boundaries

**Sequence 5 (Cavalry Disruptor):**
- Specialized anti-cavalry tactic
- Effective crowd control element
- Shows tactical innovation
- Preserves Aisen's non-lethal philosophy when possible

**Sequence 6 (Rescue):**
- Character-building through sacrifice
- High narrative value for emotional moments
- Shows willingness to exhaust resources for others
- Physical exhaustion creates vulnerability

### Tuning Parameters

- Adjust MU values upward for dramatic jeopardy (make Aisen stretch)
- Adjust MU values downward if want Aisen to appear more resilient
- Observe consistent fatigue consequences post-sequence
- Use visible sensory beats to signal degradation without showing numbers
- Link fatigue effects to cognitive impact (decision-making slows)

### Failure State Design

- Always give failure modes an escape route
- Failures should create new tactical challenges, not just "defeat"
- Use interruptions and field disruptions as dynamic elements
- Let Aisen improvise under pressure
- Make recovery require sacrifice (time, resources, or safety)
