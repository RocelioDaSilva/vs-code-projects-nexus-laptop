Compact timeline of formative events, skill milestones, and story beats for Aisen. Use the ages as anchors for scenes, flashbacks, and continuity checks.

0 — Born during a late frost in a small Hardland village; parents are working artisans.
1 — Early sensory attachments to tools and small metal objects; caregivers note unusual calmness when holding iron.
2 — First remembered moment: a small link fragment seems to 'twitch' under his fingers.
3 — Begins mimicking spear handling with a wooden shaft; shows steady hands and small, precise motions.
4 — Village elder introduces basic stance and footwork; Aisen shows unusual balance for his size.
5 — Helps at the smithing bench; fascinated by link pivots and how pieces return to line.
6 — First informal lesson in restraint and breathing from a local tutor—early seed of later telekinetic focus work.
7 — Small, involuntary tug on a spoon is noticed by family; local healer suggests testing for weave affinity.
8 — Enters basic weave calibration with a visiting examiner; passes preliminary sensory tests (thread probe response).
9 — Begins structured tutoring for Academy entrance; studies cadence, breath, and simple leverage.
10 — Trials and sponsorship negotiations; a minor benefactor offers partial support for Academy entry.
11 — Preparatory year — skills refined, basic telekinetic tasks (point push, boundary sense) practiced under supervision.
12 — Accepted to the Academy; relocation to campus, first exposure to formal Weavecraft and armory protocols.
13 — Assigned to a student house and begins formal combat curriculum; introduced to segmented chain prototypes.
14 — Shows aptitude with telekinetic thread probes and quick spatial math; instructors flag him for Master Wei's cohort.
15 — First hands‑on experience with memory‑metal under supervised conditions; shows aptitude for link rhythm and timing.
16 — Apprenticed to Master Wei (Chaincraft Specialist); begins dedicated chain‑spear techniques and precision telekinesis training.
17 — First field trip beyond campus (ridge patrols); practices coordinated holds and tether creation in controlled exercises.
18 — Frostfang Incident — Academy expedition ambushed by predators/hostiles. Aisen improvises telekinetic toggles and tethered segments to extract the group. Event leaves both acclaim and trauma; becomes a defining public moment.
19 — Recovery and consolidation year; Master Wei sharpens technique drills; Aisen begins designing signature maneuvers and low‑MU sequences.
20 — Public field tests and instructor assessments; receives first formal modifications to his chain spear hardware.
21 — Festival demonstration of controlled chain work; draws attention from a Patron House and a research coalition.
22 — Granted limited access to experimental memory‑metal sample for testing (strict oversight). Begins iterative maintenance/repair cycles with Forlorn Engineers.
23 — First real combat deployment with academy auxiliary unit; uses combined telekinesis/chain tactics in suppression actions—moral ambiguities surface.
24 — Training plateau: telekinetic pool stabilizes near baseline (practicalized at ~120 MU through conditioning and anchoring aids). Assumes more responsibility in field leadership drills.
25 — Promoted to junior instructor/assistant; leads small cohorts in chain drills and choreographed exercises; begins mentoring younger students.
26 — Enmeshed in Academy politics as field results clash with Conservator warnings; pressured to demonstrate practical utility of prototypes.
27 — Brief leave to repair hometown fences and relations; deals with local scavenger markets and the consequences of black‑market link flow.
28 — Leads an academy‑backed border patrol tasked with holding a vulnerable corridor; refines area‑denial sequences and sustained tether tactics.
29 — Final preparations and local evacuations as border pressures escalate; returns to home region to coordinate defenses and train villagers in basic countermeasures.
30 — Final Border‑Stand — Aisen uses signature chain‑spear and telekinetic constructs to hold a choke against a larger force. He expends a large portion of his pool; the sequence ends in severe injury or sacrificial stand (narrative decision: mortal wound vs. missing in the field). The act reshapes regional politics and his legend.

Skill & Power Growth (notes)
- Early childhood (0–8): nominal affinity, small sensory probes.  
- Early adolescence (9–14): MU pool shows steady growth; basic Tier 1–2 actions become reliable.  
- Mid adolescence (15–19): rapid practical gains via apprenticeship; emergent Tier 2–3 capability.  
- Early adulthood (20–24): pool and efficiency stabilize; signature techniques crystallize (Aisen baseline ≈120 MU).  
- Mid twenties (25–30): tactical refinement, leadership roles, and heavy fatigue costs for sustained, sacrificial sequences.
