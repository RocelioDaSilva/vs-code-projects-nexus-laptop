# Fourth‑Party Dossiers & Ecology

Purpose: quick-reference dossiers for non‑institutional actors, hostile enclaves, scavenger communities, and major fauna/ecological hazards. Use these to seed encounters, conflicts over resources (memory-metal, link stock), and environmental stakes.

Format per entry: Overview — Behavior & Ecology — Resources/Threats — Narrative Hooks — Encounter Rating.

---

1) The Forlorn Engineers (Scavenger Guild)
- Overview: Mobile smiths and salvage crews that comb battlefields and ruins for link scraps and memory-metal shards. They trade, fence, and clandestinely craft low-grade linkwork.
- Behavior & Ecology: Nomadic crews operating in small bands; expert at jury‑rigging and repairing damaged links. They avoid direct confrontation but will booby‑trap caches.
- Resources/Threats: Control black‑market link flow; can repair a broken chain spear for a price. Threat = booby traps, crude traps, corrosive solvents.
- Narrative Hooks: Aisen needs a rare link patch—must bargain or steal; a Forlorn crew is framed for a lab theft.
- Encounter Rating: Low→Medium (depends on number and local patronage).

2) Tusk Clans (Border Hunters)
- Overview: Hardland hunter‑bands who defend grazing corridors. Skilled with thrown bone‑lances and improvised chain nets.
- Behavior & Ecology: Seasonal migrations tied to frost cycles; aggressive if their pastures are threatened.
- Resources/Threats: Take memory-metal for talismans; practice quick ambush tactics; field poisons and smoke traps.
- Narrative Hooks: A hunting treaty is violated; a Tusk chieftain recognizes Aisen’s spear signature and demands recompense or duel.
- Encounter Rating: Medium.

3) Graybar Marauders (Roadwatch & Banditry)
- Overview: Opportunistic brigands who raid caravans and isolated academies' stores. They sometimes sell goods to Patron Houses.
- Behavior & Ecology: Operate in small mobile units; collaborate with patron spies when useful.
- Resources/Threats: Heavy crossbows, incendiary flasks, and knowledge of patrol timings.
- Narrative Hooks: Caravan with prototype chain links ambushed; an ally is captured and sold to a patron house.
- Encounter Rating: Medium→High.

4) Frosthorn Herds & Predators (Megafauna)
- Overview: Frosthorns are large grazing ungulates found in tundra ridges; predators (Frostfangs, Shale Wyrms) hunt them.
- Behavior & Ecology: Herds migrate after snowmelt; predators follow migration corridors and sometimes raid outposts.
- Resources/Threats: Herds attract hunters; their bones and hides are valuable. Predators are large, dangerous, and attracted to bright metal flashes (memory-metal).
- Narrative Hooks: A herd stampede through a training ground; a predatory Shale Wyrm snags a chain segment and drags an apprentice into the ice.
- Encounter Rating: High (environment + beast).

5) Mire‑Glass Colonies (Fey‑Remnants)
- Overview: Small, reclusive communities of semi‑sapient glass‑adapted sprites and tool‑users who alter wetlands. They sometimes bargain knowledge for metal.
- Behavior & Ecology: Control pockets of mana‑saturated marsh where memory‑bloom fungus grows; hostile to large metal extraction.
- Resources/Threats: Pulse‑sensitive flora, mana‑poison spores that interfere with low tiers of telekinesis.
- Narrative Hooks: A weaver attempts to harvest pulse memory fungus and awakens a colony; delicate diplomacy or deadly skirmish.
- Encounter Rating: Medium (magical hazards > physical threat).

6) Glassshore Wreckers (Coastal Salvage & Raiders)
- Overview: Coastal diggers who recover wrecks from tidal shoals; expert at treating corroded link elements and weaponizing kelp traps.
- Behavior & Ecology: Work seasonal tides; sell treated link bits to fences in the city. Skilled swimmers and corsair‑style raiders.
- Resources/Threats: Salt‑baked memory‑metal that gains brittle quirks; kelp‑entanglement traps that strain link integrity.
- Narrative Hooks: A chain spear contract calls for a salt‑season test; the Wreckers offer an illegal sharpening service with a dangerous side‑effect.
- Encounter Rating: Low→Medium.

---

Ecology & Environmental Hazards
- Flux Pockets: localized mana anomalies where telekinetic precision drops and MU costs spike by 30–80%. Found along old battlefields and near seam faults.
- Memory‑Bloom Wetlands: rare wetlands where pulse memory fungus grows on submerged links and bones. Harvest yields short‑term weave templates but risks mind‑fog.
- Silt Rot: a corrosive biological agent that degrades memory‑metal links over repeated exposure; common in swamp salvage zones.
- Seasonal Migration Maps: predators and large herds follow river thaw and seeding patterns. Winter routes open old war caches.

Interaction Notes (how this ties to chain spear & telekinesis)
- Memory‑metal is ecologically active: certain soils leach pulse patterns that modify link temperament (added torque, brittle snap, or unexpected resonance). Writers can use these effects to justify prototype failures or to set traps.
- Scavengers and Wreckers create the black market that supplies faulty or cursed link parts — a recurring source of plot friction for repair, betrayal, or upgrade scenes.
- Flux Pockets are an easy, scene‑scale way to force tactical improvisation (telekinesis costs spike mid‑fight; tethers fail; hold durations shorten).

Suggested Uses & Hooks
- Rescue mission: a Forlorn Engineers cache is raided; Aisen must decide whether to protect them or recover the stolen links.  
- Eco‑moral choice: harvesting pulse memory fungus could unlock a tactical advantage but harms a Mire‑Glass Colony.  
- Sabotage beat: a patron hires Graybar Marauders to sabotage a rival lab's field test, forcing a public Conclave hearing.

Author tips
- Use environmental hazards to vary combat: a stronger enemy can be made manageable by a localized silt rot or flux pocket, or vice versa.  
- Keep scavenger tech and predator behavior consistent: scavengers improvise; predators react to sound and metal flash, not morality.

End of dossier.
