# Academy Politics — Master Reference

## I. OVERVIEW

The Academy trains both martialists and weave practitioners. It functions as a military feeder, research center, and social elevator. Tension exists between conservatism, practicality, and experimental ambition.

Three governance spheres matter: 
- **Council** (policy)
- **Provost and Guards** (enforcement)
- **Research Divisions** (labs, armory)

Student houses and patrons provide external pressure.

---

## II. GOVERNANCE & STRUCTURE

### Council of Masters
- Senior faculty and the Dean
- Oversees curriculum, budgets, and tenure
- Sets institutional direction and resource allocation

### Provost and Guard
- Internal security and restricted access enforcement
- Compliance with rules and safety protocols
- Maintains institutional stability

### Research Divisions
- Semi-autonomous labs: Weavecraft, Kinetics, Materials
- Each lab competes for limited resources
- Cross-lab tensions over research priorities

### Student Houses and Guilds
- Cohorts by specialty managing apprenticeships
- Manage social order and peer advancement
- Connected to patron networks

---

## III. PRIMARY FACTIONS

### Conservators
- Defend tradition and archival control
- Resist rapid weaponization
- Favor institutional stability over experimentation
- **Power base:** senior masters, historians, heritage committees
- **Goals:** protect research integrity, limit external pressure

### Practical Arms (Instructor Guild)
- Linked to militias and border defense
- Favor live testing and field use
- Pragmatic approach to innovation
- **Power base:** military liaisons, combat instructors, field advisors
- **Goals:** produce effective soldiers, demonstrate practical applications

### Innovators
- Younger masters pushing experimental hybrid techniques
- Seek to blend traditional and new methods
- **Power base:** junior researchers, ambitious students
- **Goals:** advance capabilities, secure funding for experiments

### Patron Houses
- External sponsors who fund chairs and expect results
- Political players using Academy as soft power levers
- **Power base:** wealth, political connections, recruitment quotas
- **Goals:** secure advantages, influence institutional direction

## IV. THE EXAM: RESONANCE CALIBRATION TRIAL

### Purpose
Test a candidate's ability to harmonize a control ring with a resonance crystal under neutral conditions. A public trial that attracts patrons and influences reputation.

### Setup
- Sealed calibration bench (Council controlled)
- Supply crate of reserve crystals
- Monitoring crystal in gallery (for oversight)
- Public observation area

### Typical Failure Modes
- Poor calibration (user error)
- Misindexed crystal (manufacturing defect)
- Interference from local flux pocket
- Intentional sabotage (tampering)

## V. THE EXAM SABOTAGE — MECHANISM & FORENSICS

### The Sabotage Method

**How it works:**
- A dampening rune etched into underside of reserve crystal's housing
- Rune constricts crystal's micro‑phase response
- Causes sudden phase collapse when pulsed

**Immediate effect:**
- Control ring misreads feedback
- Trial candidate's ring oscillates
- Produces resonance burst destabilizing the bench
- Nullifies the test
- If used on live chain: triggers state misfire and LIP stress

**Planting evidence:**
- Second artifact introduced: silt‑rot compound smear
- Frames Forlorn Engineers (field kits contain similar residues)
- Creates false lead diverting investigation

### Discovery & Forensics

**Provost Maren's discovery:**
- Archival footage shows patron agent entering supply vault late at night
- Timestamps mismatch with official roster
- Orders discreet trace test on residues
- Finds micro‑etching consistent with intentional rune work

**Master Wei's contribution:**
- Dated lab note tracking vendor provenance
- Handwriting matches Patron House clerk's note
- Provides direct link to sponsor involvement

**Amara's testimony:**
- Oral witness to whispered agreement in market
- Memory cords contain mnemonic phrase
- Phrase matches Lucius Varro's steward's known speech patterns

## X. INSTITUTIONAL DYNAMICS

### Power Flows
- Council sets policy but depends on Provost for enforcement
- Research Divisions compete but need Council approval for budgets
- Patron Houses exert pressure through recruitment and funding
- Student Houses amplify or contain factional agitation
- Military liaisons demand practical results

### Pressure Points
- Budget cycles create annual conflicts
- Student advancement depends on patron recommendation
- Research priorities reflect political winds
- Security operations used as enforcement tools
- Information becomes leverage

### Reform Opportunities
- Transparency requirements post-sabotage
- Audits of supply chains
- Expanded commoner admissions
- Lab collaboration requirements
- Conflict resolution procedures
