# Economic Systems: Trade Networks, Class Economics & Resource Scarcity

## VALDIMERIAN ECONOMY

### Class-Based Economic Tiers

**Noble tier**: Land ownership + agricultural taxation + trade monopolies
- Income: Land rents, trade taxes, tribute from lower classes
- Wealth: Inherited; grows through advantageous marriage/trade deals
- Expenditure: Luxury goods, military maintenance, political intrigue
- Mobility: Downward only through spectacular failure; upward through lucky marriage

**Merchant tier**: Trade, crafting, service provisioning
- Income: Goods sales, service provision, guild fees
- Wealth: Earned; can grow quickly with good decisions
- Expenditure: Business investment, luxury goods (aspiring to noble lifestyle), family education
- Mobility: Can accumulate enough wealth to buy noble titles (one of few upward paths)

**Laborer tier**: Wage work, crafting, service
- Income: Wages (daily/seasonal, typically low)
- Wealth: Minimal; lives hand-to-mouth mostly
- Expenditure: Food, shelter, tools for trade
- Mobility: Upward rare but possible (apprenticeship → craftsperson → guild membership)

### Trade Network Structure

**Major trade routes**:
- **Grain corridor** (Valdimere → Therryn/Umara): Seasonal; high volume; price-dependent on harvest
- **Weapon trade** (Shukei ↔ all): Steady; premium prices
- **Luxury goods** (Umara spices/crafts → north): Regular; high markup; noble market
- **Raw materials** (Therryn metals/wood → all): Steady supplier role

**Trade hubs**: Frontier towns become exchange points (geographic advantage; no single nation dominance).

**Merchant guilds**: Control quality/pricing in regions; local monopolies (guild regulations).

---

### Economic Crisis Dynamics

**Taxation strain**: Covenant increasingly taxes; merchant profits squeezed; noble land-income decreases.

**Price controls**: Covenant sets prices (tries to stabilize economy; actually creates shortages + black markets).

**Currency instability**: Trust in money-backed-by-Covenant wavers; barter + black market increase.

**Aisen's father scenario**: 
- Tavern = moderate income (mixed clientele pays different prices)
- Economic crisis: customers spend less, loans default, prices rise (costs exceed income)
- Forced decision: sell stake to survive OR close business
- This economic pressure = personal motivation for Aisen

## SHUKEI ECONOMY

### Wealth Structure (Non-monetary emphasized)

**Wealth definition**: Reputation primarily; material wealth secondary.

**Prestige accumulation**:
- Combat victory = prestige (tournament winners recognized)
- Martial mastery = prestige (master fighters have status)
- Clan leadership = prestige (leads by respect, not wealth)
- Service = prestige (those who help clan gain standing)

**Material wealth**:
- Clan resources pooled (no individual private property typically)
- Personal items (weapons, tokens) can be owned
- Wealth primarily used for: weapons, training support, clan obligation payment

### Economic Flow

**Income sources**:
- Clan collective: hunting, farming, crafting
- Individual warriors: mercenary work (seasonal; conflict + non-war periods)
- Merchants: trade coordination
- Craftspeople: weapon/tool sales

**Economic obligation**:
- Warriors support clan (trained; ready to defend)
- Clan supports warriors (housing, food, training)
- Mutual obligation; not employment (no wage; reciprocal)

## THERRYN ECONOMY

### Knowledge-based Wealth

**Most valuable**: Intellectual contributions (new theories, inventions, strategies).

**Wealth acquisition**:
- Publishing discoveries (intellectual property; brings prestige + resources)
- Engineering services (design infrastructure; paid well)
- Teaching (tuition; can be substantial for master scholars)
- Patronage (wealthy sponsors seeking intellectual credibility)

### Economic Specialization

**Guild specialists**: Scholars (highest status) → engineers (practical application) → craftspeople (execution).

**Revenue streams**:
- Academic publications (knowledge sold)
- Engineering contracts (design work paid per project)
- Infrastructure operation (maintaining guild buildings/systems; guild member income)
- Patronage licensing (merchants/nobles pay for intellectual services)

### Market Dynamics

**Intellectual monopoly**: Own ideas for limited time (eventually others learn/replicate).

**Information asymmetry**: Those with better information have economic advantage (traders, politicians value Therryn intelligence).

**Innovation = wealth**: New discovery can generate wealth through applications + teaching others.

## UMARAN ECONOMY

### Relationship-Based Resource Distribution

**Wealth definition**: Relationship quality; obligation balance.

**Economic obligation**:
- If I have surplus; I support those in deficit (expected, not optional)
- If I'm in debt; I later support those who helped (reciprocal; can take years)
- Obligation tracking: informal but serious (community remembers who declined help)

### Resource Flow

**Income sources** (less relevant than relationship status):
- Collective farming/hunting
- Crafting (personal items for trade)
- Teaching (knowledge shared; expect reciprocal help later)

**Economic equality**: System actively works toward equality (surplus redistributed; not accumulated).

**Innovation suppression tendency**: System stable; innovation threatens equilibrium (risk-averse economically).

## COVENANT IMPACT ON ALL ECONOMIES

### Pre-war economic interventions:

**Valdimere**: Taxation increase → merchant margin squeeze; black market grows.

**Shukei**: Restrict ronin mercenary work (reduced income; forced clan integration or starvation).

**Therryn**: Restrict research topics (limits intellectual property development; knowledge sector disrupted).

**Umara**: Taxation disrupts obligation system (can't fulfill obligations; system breaks).

### War-economic impact:

**Resource scarcity**: Combat disrupts trade; supplies limited; rationing necessary.

**Population displacement**: Refugees; labor shortages in some areas, overcrowding in others.

**Production shift**: Peacetime goods (trade, crafting) → war goods (weapons, armor, shelter).

**Black markets boom**: Shortages create high-price trading (crime + legitimate overlap).

## POST-WAR ECONOMIC RECONSTRUCTION

### Immediate priorities (Months 1-6):

- Restore trade routes (enable surplus reestablishment)
- Address war debt (who pays? reparations? forgiveness?)
- Reintegrate displaced persons (housing, jobs)
- Decriminalize black markets (reintegrate shadow economy)

### Mid-term (Years 1-3):

- Rebuild infrastructure (restores productivity)
- Establish new economic governance (how distribute resources?)
- Address inequality increases from war (wealth concentrated in war-winners)
- Restart luxury trade (return to pre-war complexity)

### Long-term (Years 3-10):

- Stabilize new economic model (compatible with post-war governance)
- Address Convoy financial holdings (did Covenant commit theft during war?)
- Develop sustainable trade patterns (prevent future economic crises)
- Social integration through economic cooperation (economic = social health indicator)

## THEMATIC RESONANCE

**Central tension**: Economic systems determine who has power (financially) → political power. Post-war governance must address economic equality or replicate pre-war hierarchy.

**Pragmatism application**: Aisen would emphasize: fix economic incentives first; governance follows naturally if incentives right.

**Each nation's approach**: Different economic cultures (merit vs. relationship vs. prestige vs. knowledge) provide different post-war models.
