# Flux Corruption: Ecological Crisis Mechanics

## What is Flux Corruption?

**Nature**: A spatial/environmental phenomenon causing ecosystem breakdown, animal mutation/displacement, and reality distortion. Not a living organism but an environmental state.

**Origin**: Unknown (possibly intentional Covenant creation; possibly natural phenomenon Covenant exploits)

**Manifestation**: Zones where ecological rules break down; cause unpredictable.

## Primary Symptoms

### Plant Life
- **Dying despite adequate water/nutrients**: Growth mechanisms fail
- **Uncontrolled rapid growth**: Sometimes reverses (sudden death)
- **Mutation**: Plants develop animal-like characteristics or toxins
- **Seasonal disruption**: Wrong growth cycles; death in normally safe seasons
- **Reproduction failure**: Seeds don't germinate; plants can't propagate

### Animal Behavior
- **Migration patterns disrupted**: Herds abandoning safe territory
- **Aggressive mutation**: Predators becoming more dangerous; prey becoming aggressive
- **Uncontrolled reproduction**: Some species breed excessively; others stop reproducing
- **Territorial breakdown**: Animals abandoning inherited territories
- **Behavioral corruption**: Apex predators hunting inexplicably; prey predating each other

### Soil & Water
- **Nutrient depletion**: Soil loses fertility rapidly (years instead of normal cycles)
- **Water contamination**: Not poison but somehow making life difficult
- **Mineral accumulation**: Strange minerals depositing (sometimes glowing/magical)
- **Microbial die-off**: Symbiotic relationships breaking (plants can't access nitrogen)

### Weather Patterns
- **Unpredictable**: Rain doesn't fall when expected; comes when destructive
- **Temperature extremes**: Sudden freezes or heat waves out of season
- **Air quality degradation**: Breathing becomes difficult in affected zones
- **Wind disruption**: Storms appear/disappear without meteorological cause

## Geographical Spread (Current State)

### Severity Zones
- **Zone 1 (Frontier regions)**: Early, patchy corruption; 5-10% defective areas
- **Zone 2 (Border territories)**: Moderate corruption; 20-30% affected; visible to inhabitants
- **Zone 3 (Deep wilderness)**: Severe corruption; 50%+ areas essentially uninhabitable
- **Zone 4 (Unknown/remote)**: Corruption extreme; reality itself seems distorted

### Current Progress
- **Starting Point**: Deep wilderness; expanding outward toward civilization
- **Speed**: Accelerating; doubled affected area in past 5 years
- **Predicted**: If unchecked, civilization-threatening in 20-30 years
- **Actual Threat**: Likely faster than predicted (if deliberately engineered/accelerated)

### Nation-Specific Impact
- **Valdimere**: Frontier agricultural zones affected; yield declining
- **Shukei**: Islands showing early signs; uncertainty about water routes
- **Therryn**: Southern regions beginning to show effects (riverine areas)
- **Umara**: Savanna herds migrating away from traditional territories

## Ecological Consequences

### Food Crisis
- Crop failures expanding
- Fish populations declining/shifting
- Hunting becoming unreliable
- Starvation risk in 5-10 years if unchecked

### Water Crisis
- Contamination affecting drinking sources
- Irrigation systems unreliable
- Trade route disruption (rivers unnavigable)

### Predator/Monster Hypothesis
- Unclear whether Flux creates new monsters or displaces existing ones
- Evidence: Creatures appearing in civilized territories (previously unknown)
- Pattern: Appears to correlate with Flux zones

### Ecosystem Collapse Cascade
1. Base plants/animals affected → 2. Food web disrupted → 3. Predators migrate or die → 4. Competitors fill vacuums → 5. Novel predators appear → 6. Civilization threatened

## Important: Reversibility Question

**Can it be stopped?** Unknown. 

**Theories**:
1. Natural cycle (will reverse itself)
2. Environmental damage (requires active restoration)
3. Magical curse (requires spell-breaking)
4. Covenant creation (requires stopping them)
5. Combination of above

**Key Question for Story**: Is stopping Flux corruption possible? Or does story focus on *adaptation* rather than *reversal*?

## Discovery & Knowledge Progression

### Who Knows What?
- **Frontier peoples**: Experience it directly; understand symptoms not causes
- **Hunters/Farmers**: First to notice patterns; keep careful observations
- **Local governors**: Receive reports; often suppress/deny
- **Central authorities**: Generally unaware; information suppressed by bureaucracy
- **Scholars**: Begin investigating; theories proliferation without unified knowledge
- **Resistance**: May discover Covenant connection (if true)
- **Covenant**: Knows actual cause (either created it or exploits it)

### Information Flow
- **Bottleneck**: Official channels suppress/deny
- **Leakage**: Local people share with travelers/merchants
- **Convergence**: Scattered observations point toward universal phenomenon
- **Pattern Recognition**: Aisen + resistance synthesize scattered knowledge

## Kaelen's Post-War Role

Kaelen's plant magic + understanding of ecology = potential for:
1. **Assessment**: Determining actual scope and nature of corruption
2. **Restoration**: Using magic to help ecosystems recover
3. **Learning**: Nature-based approach might reveal cause
4. **Prevention**: If reversible, preventing future corruption

**Post-war vision**: Gardens as both practical (food production) and symbolic (restoration of balance).

## Mechanical Use in Story

**"Ecology as threat"** serves multiple functions:
- Creates urgency (people starving drives recruitment)
- Drives plot (investigating corruption reveals Covenant)
- Enables character arcs (Kaelen's restoration work)
- Tests morality (who benefits from crisis? Who suffers?)
- Reveals blame (who caused/exploits ecological failure?)
