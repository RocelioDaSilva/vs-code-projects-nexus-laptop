## FLUX MECHANICS: QUICK REFERENCE

### Flux Density Levels

| Level | Flux Density | Environment | Creatures Possible | Sustainability |
|-------|--------------|-------------|-------------------|-----------------|
| **Null** | 0 | Possible but unstable | None naturally | Requires artificial maintenance (Academy) |
| **Low** | Moderate fluctuation | Stable baseline | Generalists | Sustainable indefinitely |
| **Moderate** | Steady variation | Equilibrium range | Specialists | Sustainable w/respect for limits |
| **High** | Intense concentration | Stressed conditions | Combat-adapted | Unsustainable (requires external input) |
| **Critical** | Chaotic fluctuation | Void Zones | Metamorphic/unstable | Naturally unstable; creatures constantly adapting |

**Story Application**: 
- Settlement = Low flux = maximum sustainability
- Garrison = High flux = powerful but fragile
- Academy = Null flux = requires constant work

### Ley Line Network

**Three major ley lines pass through the region**, creating **four distinct areas**:

1. **Academy** - Set in natural Flux-null zone; artificially maintained
2. **Settlement** - At ley line intersection; moderate mixed Flux
3. **Garrison** - Downstream from ley line nexus; direct high Flux concentration
4. **Void Zones** - Ultimate Flux sink; point where ley lines concentrate to maximum

**Flow Direction**: Energy flows from void zones upstream toward lower-flux zones
- Academy must push back against this flow (requires constant magic)
- Settlement sits at balance point
- Garrison acts as buffer (absorbs excess Flux)

## WORLD-BUILDING COHERENCE: HOW EVERYTHING CONNECTS

### The Coherence Checklist

✓ **Creatures have ecology**: Not random; positioned in food chains, migration patterns, predator-prey relationships
✓ **Flux has physics**: Follows rules; concentrates toward void; creatures adapt to Flux density
✓ **Humans chose philosophies**: Each region made choices aligned with their environment; not arbitrary
✓ **Philosophies conflict**: Success in one region requires failure in another; this creates tension
✓ **Tension escalates**: Current trajectory suggests ecosystem approaching critical failure
✓ **Aisen sees all perspectives**: Acts as bridge between three philosophies
✓ **Stakes are global**: Not just personal conflict; world equilibrium at stake
✓ **Resolution unclear**: No easy synthesis; requires sacrifice from all regions

### How to Use This Framework in Writing

**When writing a scene involving creatures**:
1. Which region is the scene in?
2. What creatures naturally inhabit that region? (Check the regional file)
3. What is their behavior in that region? (Varies by Flux density)
4. What does their presence teach about regional philosophy?
5. How does this creature moment advance Aisen's understanding?

**When writing about Flux phenomena**:
1. What is the local Flux density? (Region determines this)
2. Is it natural or artificially maintained/stressed?
3. What creatures would this Flux level attract?
4. How would characters perceive Flux? (Settlement folk ≠ Garrison ≠ Academy perspective)

**When writing regional conflicts**:
1. Is this conflict regional (settlement vs. garrison?) or global?
2. What creature behavior demonstrates the philosophical difference?
3. How does creature ecology reflect the region's approach?
4. Is this conflict about resources, philosophy, or ecosystem sustainability?

## SUMMARY: WHY THIS LORE MATTERS

**At its core**, this regional creature system does three things:

1. **Creates world coherence**: Everything connects through Flux, creatures, and Flux-creature adaptation
2. **Enables character development**: Aisen's growth mirrors deepening creature/Flux understanding
3. **Generates plot momentum**: Ecosystem degradation, regional conflicts, philosophical tensions all drive story forward

**The deeper point**: 

This isn't just a magical world with creatures in it. This is a world where **the system IS the story**. Every scene involves Aisen learning more about how the system works and the implications of working within/against/beyond it.

By story's end, Aisen (and reader) understands:
- How creatures survive in their environment (Act I)
- Why humans can harm that survival (Act II)  
- What it would take to create sustainable symbiosis (Act III)

And the ultimate question: **Is sustainable symbiosis worth the cost of attempting it?**
