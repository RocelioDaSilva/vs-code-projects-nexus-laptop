# Therryn — Song Dynasty Meritocratic Empire (Overview & Systems)

## Core Identity
Song Dynasty China-inspired nation with examination-based meritocracy, scholarly authority, commercial sophistication, and trade-network prominence. Therryn values learning, artistic excellence, and commercial innovation.

## Governance & Magic System
**Authority**: Emperor → Scholar-officials (examination-passed) → Gentry → Merchants → Peasants → Diaspora  
**Magic Tradition**: Scholarly discipline; studied like mathematics; written lineages; different schools teach different approaches  
**Class Mobility**: Better than other nations; examination system theoretically meritocratic (practically class-biased remains)  
**Economy**: Sophisticated; merchant class wealthy; tax-based; internal trade complex  
**Trade**: Central position; crossroads of multiple routes; wealth from commerce  

**Key Advantage**: Information flows faster (merchant networks); innovation rewarded (scholarly competition); educated population  
**Key Vulnerability**: Complexity creates bureaucratic blindness similar to Valdimere; examination system can be corrupted; wealth inequality despite meritocracy claims

---

## Magic & Knowledge
**Approach**: Mathematical, geometric, philosophical (sacred geometry governs force flows)  
**Training**: Academic schools; studied through texts; debated among scholars  
**Innovation**: Encouraged through scholarly competition; new approaches valued if proven effective  
**Application**: Precise, elegant, sophisticated  
**Limitation**: Sometimes overcomplicated; practical soldiers struggle with theoretical approaches  

---

## Social Structure & Conflicts
| Tier | Role | Power | Upward Mobility |
|------|------|-------|---|
| 1 | Emperor & imperial family | Absolute | N/A |
| 2 | Scholar-officials | High (bureaucratic) | Examination passage (theoretically available) |
| 3 | Gentry | Moderate (land-based) | Intermarriage with officials; examination passage |
| 4 | Merchants | High (wealth) | Political advancement limited; economic growth possible |
| 5 | Peasants | Minimal | Examination passage (rare; requires patronage) |
| 6 | Diaspora | Negative (marginalized) | Nearly impossible |

**Tensions**:
- Merchants resent political powerlessness despite wealth
- Young scholars questioning established doctrines
- Examination system corruption (wealth affecting access)
- Regional gentry vs. central authority
- Maritime traders challenging inland merchant monopolies

---

## Story Relevance: Therryn
If Aisen encounters Therryn scholars: Immediate respect for his synthesis (combines traditions mathematically). Alternatively: Dismissal as unqualified amateur (class prejudice despite meritocratic claims). Therryn capital could host resistance meetings (cosmopolitan; foreign visitors less suspicious). Merchant networks crucial for supply chains.

# Umara — West African Merchant Networks & Oral Tradition (Overview & Systems)

## Core Identity
West African empires-inspired nation with decentralized merchant networks, griot-based authority, spiritual power structures, and oral knowledge preservation. Umara values relationships, storytelling, and ancestral wisdom.

## Governance & Magic System
**Authority**: Merchant princes + griots + spiritual leaders (no single hierarchy; relationship-based)  
**Magic Tradition**: Spiritual, relational, entheogenic; preserved through oral tradition and ritual; knowledge participatory  
**Class Mobility**: Complex; relationship-based advancement (patronage, adoptive family, griot recognition)  
**Economy**: Trade-network focused; wealth through merchant connections; communal resources  
**Trade**: Crossroads; supplies spices, textiles, knowledge from multiple regions  

**Key Advantage**: Fluid organizational structure (Covenant harder to infiltrate; networks distributed); information preserved through griots (harder to erase); emphasis on relationships (community resilience)  
**Key Vulnerability**: Decentralization can mean slow decisions; oral knowledge vulnerable if griots eliminated; spiritual traditions sometimes dismissed by "civilized" nations

---

## Magic & Knowledge
**Approach**: Spiritual, relational, ecological; learned through relationship and ritual, not books  
**Training**: Apprenticeship to griots/healers/spiritual practitioners; participatory knowledge  
**Innovation**: Adaptive; responds to ecological/social circumstances  
**Application**: Context-appropriate; ecological sensitivity  
**Limitation**: Sometimes slow to mobilize (consensus required); spiritual focus can seem impractical to military focus  

---

## Social Structure & Conflicts
| Tier | Role | Power | Upward Mobility |
|------|------|-------|---|
| 1 | Merchant princes & royalty | High (wealth/authority) | Birth + business success |
| 2 | Griots & spiritual leaders | Very high (cultural authority) | Recognition + apprenticeship |
| 3 | Guild merchants & crafts | Moderate-high (economic) | Business expansion; griot permission |
| 4 | Free laborers & traders | Minimal | Exceptional success in trade; spiritual calling |
| 5 | Servants, captives, enslaved | Negative | Rare (family adoption; freedom purchase) |
| 6 | Diaspora & refugees | Variable | Relationship-dependent |

**Tensions**:
- Merchant princes vs. spiritual authority (practical vs. spiritual)
- Coastal traders vs. inland kingdoms (different economies)
- Young people questioning ancestor-based traditions
- Slavery's moral contradiction with relationship-based culture
- External pressure to "civilize" (abandon traditions)

---

## Story Relevance: Umara
Amara's potential origin (griot family background). Umaran support crucial for: supply networks (trade routes safe), cultural legitimacy (griots preserve resistance stories), spiritual grounding (Umaran philosophy aligns with Aisen's synthesis emphasis on relationships). Post-war: Umaran approaches to decentralized governance model for reconstructed world.
