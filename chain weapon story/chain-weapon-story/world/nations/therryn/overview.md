# Therryn — National Overview

## Identity

Therryn is a vast continental empire governed by scholar-officials selected through a rigorous examination system. Its culture venerates intellectual achievement, mathematical elegance, and sacred geometry. It is the most technologically innovative nation in the world and the most subtly elitist.

**Real-World Inspiration**: Song Dynasty China + Ming bureaucracy + Enlightenment-era European universities.

## Geography

- **Core territory**: Temperate river valleys and fertile plains; extensive canal networks; large cities built around university complexes and market squares.
- **Border regions**: Mountain passes to the west (Andean Alliance), maritime ports to the south (trade routes to Shukei and Umara), steppe frontier to the north (Valdimere).
- **Key features**: The Grand Examination Halls (capital), the Library-Fortresses (provincial centers that store and protect accumulated knowledge), the Geometric Gardens (mathematical meditation spaces).
- **Flux geology**: Therryn sits on ancient geological formations with regular, predictable Flux gradients. This predictability is why their magic tradition favors mathematical modeling — the local Flux behaves like physics, not chaos.

## Core Values

1. **Knowledge as virtue**: The most admirable thing a person can do is discover something true. Ignorance is not a neutral state — it's a moral failing.
2. **Merit through examination**: All positions open to anyone who demonstrates competence. The examination system is the nation's moral core.
3. **Sacred geometry**: Mathematical principles aren't just tools — they're expressions of the fundamental order of reality. Understanding geometry is understanding the divine.
4. **Debate as practice**: Truth emerges from argument. Scholars who refuse to defend their ideas publicly are suspected of hiding errors.
5. **Publication as duty**: Knowledge hoarded is knowledge wasted. (In practice, this ideal is honored more in breach than observance.)

## Government Structure

### Imperial Court
- **Emperor/Empress**: Hereditary figurehead with theoretical supreme power. In practice, constrained by scholar-official consensus. An emperor who defies the examination system loses legitimacy.
- **Grand Council**: 12 senior scholar-officials (exam-selected, emperor-confirmed) who administer the empire. Each oversees a ministry: Revenue, Education, Defense, Works, Justice, Foreign Affairs, Agriculture, Commerce, Archives, Ceremonies, Health, Magic.
- **Provincial Governors**: Scholar-officials posted to provinces. Rotated every 3-5 years to prevent local power accumulation (a practice borrowed from Song China's "rule of avoidance").

### The Examination System
- **Three levels**: Provincial (qualifies for minor posts), Metropolitan (qualifies for governance), Palace (qualifies for Grand Council candidacy).
- **Content**: Mathematical principles, geometric proofs, historical analysis, ethical reasoning, magical theory, practical governance problems.
- **Schedule**: Provincial exams annually. Metropolitan every 3 years. Palace every 5 years.
- **Who can sit**: Theoretically anyone. Practically, only those with years of education. Exam preparation schools exist but cost money. Scholarship programs exist but are limited.
- **Pass rate**: Provincial ~15%. Metropolitan ~5%. Palace ~2%.
- **Result**: A governing class that is genuinely competent but systematically biased toward the already-educated.

### Power Dynamics
- **Scholar-officials vs. Emperor**: Ongoing tension. Scholars believe merit should govern; emperors believe blood should. Functional compromise: emperor reigns, scholars rule.
- **Scholar-officials vs. Merchants**: Merchants are wealthier but politically subordinate. They buy influence through patronage of scholars, funding of libraries, and endowing examination scholarships. The relationship is mutually dependent and mutually resentful.
- **Central vs. Provincial**: Rotation system prevents provincial power accumulation but also prevents officials from developing local expertise. Governors who are effective want to stay; the system says they must go.

## Military

### Structure
- **Scholar-designed, professionally executed**: Military strategy is developed by examination-passed strategists. Field execution is by professional soldiers who didn't pass the exam (or chose military service over scholarship).
- **Command hierarchy**: Grand Strategist (Grand Council member) → Provincial Commanders (exam-passed) → Field Officers (mixed exam/merit) → Soldiers (non-exam professionals).
- **Doctrine**: Emphasizes preparation, logistics, engineering, and formation discipline. Therryn armies are slow to deploy but devastating when properly positioned. They build fortifications, engineering works, and supply infrastructure that other armies raid but cannot replicate.

### Strengths
- Superior siege warfare (mathematical engineering)
- Best logistics in the world (systematic supply chain management)
- Excellent intelligence networks (scholar-officials are trained observers)

### Weaknesses
- Slow to react (strategic committees debate while enemies advance)
- Theory-practice gap (scholar-strategists overestimate the predictability of battle)
- Officer-soldier divide (educated commanders sometimes cannot communicate with uneducated troops)
- No warrior ethos (soldiers fight for pay, not identity; morale is weaker under stress than Shukei warriors)

## Religion & Philosophy

### Dominant Schools
1. **Structural Realism** (establishment): The universe operates on mathematical principles. Understanding those principles through geometry and observation is the highest form of worship. The examination system is a spiritual as well as political institution.
2. **Sacred Geometry** (scholarly elite): Mathematical beauty isn't just useful — it's evidence of divine design. The most elegant proof is the most sacred.
3. **Practical Ethics** (merchant-class, popular): Less interested in cosmic geometry; more interested in how to live well. Emphasizes reciprocity, business ethics, and community responsibility.
4. **Underground Heretical Schools**: Challenge Structural Realism's assumptions. Some argue that the examination system is an idol, not an instrument. Others explore forbidden magical applications that the establishment suppresses.

### Religious Practice
- **No clergy in the traditional sense**: Scholar-officials perform ceremonial functions. Knowledge IS the sacred act.
- **Seasonal observances**: Aligned with astronomical events. Solstice and equinox festivals involve geometric demonstrations, mathematical competitions, and public lectures.
- **Death rites**: The deceased's intellectual contributions are formally catalogued. A scholar's funeral includes a reading of their published works. A commoner's funeral acknowledges their practical contributions.
- **Coming-of-age**: Marked by first examination attempt (regardless of success). The act of sitting the exam is what matters.

## Magic Tradition: Mathematical Geometry

### Core Approach
Magic is treated as applied mathematics. Flux behaves according to principles that can be modeled, predicted, and optimized. Where Valdimere uses standardized formulas and Shukei uses embodied intuition, Therryn uses derivation from first principles.

### Strengths
- Can develop new techniques by deriving them from mathematical models (innovation)
- Extremely precise when properly calculated
- Can be taught through texts and proofs (scalable)
- Natural defense against chaotic/improvisational magic (their geometric shields are structurally optimal)

### Weaknesses
- Requires setup time (calculations must be verified)
- Fails catastrophically when assumptions are wrong (if the mathematical model doesn't match local Flux conditions, the technique doesn't just weaken — it inverts)
- Produces scholars who can derive techniques they can't physically execute (theory-practice gap)
- Over-reliance on geometric frameworks makes adaptation to non-geometric approaches difficult

### Relationship to Society
- Magic education is integrated into the examination system. The highest-level exams include practical magical demonstrations.
- Unexamined magic use isn't illegal (unlike Valdimere) but is considered vulgar — like doing arithmetic by finger-counting when you should be using algebraic notation.
- Magical innovation is prestigious. Discovering a new geometric formation can make a career.

## Current Crises

1. **Examination ossification**: The exam system is 400+ years old. What it tests has diverged from what governance requires. Officials who score highest are often least effective.
2. **Merchant-class resentment**: Wealthy merchants fund everything but hold no political power. Growing faction pushing for representation by wealth, not examination.
3. **Provincial disconnection**: Rotating officials never develop local knowledge. Provincial populations increasingly ignore central directives that don't fit local conditions.
4. **Covenant infiltration**: The Covenant finds Therryn easy to penetrate because scholarly competition creates natural secrecy. Scholars who "discover" forbidden techniques through "independent research" are actually being fed Covenant-curated knowledge.
5. **Military-scholarly divide**: Professional soldiers increasingly resent being commanded by theoreticians who've never seen battle. Low-ranking military rebellion is a genuine possibility.
