# Andean Alliance — National Overview

## Identity

The Andean Alliance is a federation of mountain communities organized by altitude — valley, terrace, and peak. It is the only nation where political structure directly mirrors geography. Communities cooperate through a vertical reciprocity system where each altitude zone produces what the others can't, creating mutual dependency that prevents any single level from dominating. In theory. In practice, peak communities have leveraged their spiritual status and magical access to claim political primacy.

**Real-World Inspiration**: Inca Empire (ayllu communal labor, mit'a rotation, quipu record), Aymara community governance, Andean ayni reciprocity system, Mapuche decentralized resistance.

## Geography

- **Core territory**: High mountain ranges, fertile valleys, terraced hillsides, and volcanic peaks. Altitude ranges from 1,500m (valley floor) to 5,500m (sacred peaks).
- **Vertical archipelago**: Communities at different altitudes produce different goods. Valley: grain, fruits, timber. Terrace: livestock, medicinal herbs, textiles. Peak: crystal mining, sacred site stewardship, magical reagents.
- **Key features**: Breath Terraces (permanent thin-Veil zones at specific altitudes; sacred and dangerous), the Crystal Spine (a mountain range with massive Flux-conductive mineral deposits), the Council Rock (a volcanic caldera where inter-band councils convene).
- **Flux geology**: Crystalline geology and high altitude create "resonant peaks" — natural Flux amplifiers. Crystal veins and ancient Aelfar mineral lacings concentrate Flux in predictable bands. The higher you go, the more magical access you have. This geological fact is the foundation of both the Alliance's power and its internal inequality.

## Core Values

1. **Vertical reciprocity (ayni)**: Every gift creates a debt. Every altitude zone gives what it has and receives what it needs. The system only works if everyone participates.
2. **Rotational service (mit'a)**: Instead of taxation, communities contribute labor on a rotating basis. Roads, terraces, irrigation works, and defenses are built by community labor pools.
3. **Geographic holiness**: The mountain is alive. Higher altitude = closer to the divine. Breath terraces are the most sacred sites. Disrespecting them is the gravest sin.
4. **Consensus governance**: No single ruler. Decisions require agreement across altitude bands. Speed is sacrificed for inclusion.
5. **Ancestral continuity**: The present is shaped by the past. Ancestors built the terraces, laid the roads, and discovered the crystal veins. Their investment obligates the living to maintain and extend.

## Government Structure

### Three-Band System

**Valley Band** (agricultural base, ~45% of population)
- Communities of 200-2,000 people organized around river valleys.
- Internal governance: Community assembly (all adults participate, consensus-based).
- Economic role: Grain, fruit, timber production. Surplus feeds terrace and peak communities.
- Political role: Numerically dominant but politically weakest (low altitude = low spiritual status).

**Terrace Band** (productive middle, ~40% of population)
- Communities built on the hillside terraces that define Andean landscape.
- Internal governance: Community assembly + elected terrace-wardens (rotational, 2-year terms).
- Economic role: Livestock, herbs, textiles. Also maintain the terrace infrastructure (irrigation, retaining walls, paths).
- Political role: The mediators. Neither valley nor peak; they translate between both. Disproportionate political influence despite middle status.

**Peak Band** (spiritual-magical elite, ~15% of population)
- Small communities at extreme altitude near Breath Terraces and crystal deposits.
- Internal governance: Council of Keepers (senior community members responsible for sacred site stewardship).
- Economic role: Crystal extraction, sacred-site maintenance, magical reagent provision.
- Political role: Spiritual authority. Peak communities claim divine mandate through proximity to thin-Veil zones. Smallest population, greatest influence.

### Inter-Band Council
- Meets at Council Rock (neutral site) for decisions affecting the entire Alliance.
- Representation: Equal (each band sends equal delegation regardless of population). This gives peak communities outsized per-capita representation.
- Consensus required. Any band can veto.
- **Result**: Valley communities (45% of population) have the same formal power as peak communities (15%). This is understood as spiritual parity compensating for numerical inequality. Valley communities increasingly see it as exploitation.

### Leadership Rotation
- Alliance-wide leadership (First Speaker) rotates between bands every 3 years.
- Peak First Speakers tend to focus on spiritual maintenance and sacred-site investment.
- Terrace First Speakers tend to focus on infrastructure and inter-band trade.
- Valley First Speakers tend to focus on agricultural policy and food distribution.
- **Pattern**: Each band uses its leadership turn to prioritize its own needs. This ensures no band is permanently neglected — but also ensures no long-term strategic planning is possible.

## Class Structure

Within each band, communities are egalitarian (no formal hierarchy). Between bands, altitude hierarchy exists:

### Peak-dwellers
- Spiritual elite. Crystal miners, Breath Terrace stewards, ceremonial leaders.
- Highest status. Their children receive the most magical training (Flux access at altitude).
- Small, tight-knit communities. Everyone knows everyone. Social pressure is intense.

### Terrace-dwellers
- Productive middle class. Herders, weavers, terrace maintainers.
- Moderate status. Respected for labor but not for spiritual attainment.
- Most politically active (they need both valley food and peak magic).

### Valley-dwellers
- Agricultural base. Farmers, carpenters, traders.
- Lowest status in the altitude hierarchy. Highest economic output.
- Resentment is growing. "We feed everyone and get least voice."

### Outsiders
- Non-Alliance peoples living in Alliance territory. Refugees, traders, displaced populations.
- No altitude assignment = no political standing.
- Can petition for community membership (requires 3-year residence and community vote).

## Economy

### Vertical Exchange

The fundamental economic mechanism:
- Valley sends UP: grain, fruit, timber, tools
- Peak sends DOWN: crystal artifacts, magical services, ceremonial authorization
- Terrace exchanges BOTH WAYS: textiles, livestock, herbs, labor

### Mit'a (Rotational Labor)
- Instead of monetary taxation, each community contributes labor on a rotating basis.
- Road maintenance: Each community maintains its segment of the path network.
- Major construction: Alliance-wide projects (bridges, fortifications, irrigation) draw labor from all bands in rotation.
- **Strength**: No monetary inequality from taxation. Labor is distributed proportionally.
- **Weakness**: Communities gaming rotation (sending weakest workers, delaying rotation, negotiating exemptions). Peak communities increasingly claim "spiritual labor" (meditation at sacred sites) as equivalent to physical labor (terrace building).

### Trade
- Internal: Vertical exchange system (non-monetary, obligation-based). Similar to Umaran reciprocity but geographically structured.
- External: Limited. The Alliance trades crystal artifacts and medicinal herbs with the Federation and Therryn for metal, textiles, and manufactured goods.
- **Currency**: No internal currency. External trade uses Federation compact marks or barter.

## Military

### Defensive Militia
- No standing army. Each community maintains a militia (all adults trained in basic combat + mountain survival).
- **Mountain warfare specialty**: Ambush, avalanche tactics, altitude exploitation. Andean fighters lure enemies into altitude where the invaders tire, sicken, and die while Andean natives are adapted.
- **Crystal weaponry**: Peak communities provide crystal-enhanced weapons and armor. Limited quantity, extremely effective.
- **Weakness**: No offensive capability. Cannot project force beyond the mountains. If attacked simultaneously from multiple passes, the militia cannot concentrate fast enough.

### The Condor Signal
- Emergency communication through trained Condor King-feather resonance. Activating a feather sends a Flux pulse detectable at any other feather within range (~100 miles). Simple signal: attack/all-clear/assemble. No complex messages.
- Fast for alerts; requires runner network for detailed orders.

## Religion & Spirituality

### Mountain Theology
- The mountain is alive. Not metaphorically — the Flux flowing through crystal veins is experienced as geological consciousness.
- Breath Terraces are communion points where human consciousness can interface with geological Flux patterns.
- Ancestors who served the mountain well are believed to merge with the crystal network after death.
- **Ceremony**: Seasonal ceremonies at Breath Terraces, synchronized with astronomical events. Solstice ceremonies are the largest — all bands send delegations to the highest accessible terrace.

### Shamanic Tradition
- Spiritual practitioners (not quite priests, not quite mages) who mediate between community and mountain.
- Training: 10-15 year apprenticeship beginning at age 8-10. Practitioners learn to enter Breath Terrace communion states safely.
- **Key function**: Detecting Flux anomalies before they become dangerous. A skilled shaman can sense crystal-vein stress and predict geological events (earthquakes, Flux storms, creature emergence) days in advance.

### Spiritual Crisis
- Valdimere-funded mining operations are fracturing crystal veins, disrupting Flux patterns, and damaging Breath Terraces.
- Shamans report increasingly confused communion results. The mountain's "voice" is fragmented.
- Peak communities interpret this as divine punishment for alliance with outsiders. Valley communities interpret it as evidence that mining agreements were exploitative.
- The theological debate masks a political contest: who controls crystal extraction, peak communities (traditional stewards) or the Alliance collectively?

## Current Crises

1. **Mining exploitation**: External interests (Valdimere, Federation) want crystal resources. Alliance communities disagree on whether to mine extensively (valley: yes, we need trade goods; peak: no, it damages sacred sites).
2. **Altitude inequality backlash**: Valley communities openly questioning peak privilege. "If we withhold grain for one season, the peaks starve. If they withhold crystals, we're inconvenienced."
3. **External military pressure**: Valdimere's frontier expansion is approaching Alliance mountain passes. The Alliance's defensive militia is untested against organized imperial military.
4. **Youth migration**: Young valley-dwellers leaving for Therryn and Federation cities where altitude doesn't determine social standing.
5. **Covenant geological manipulation**: Evidence (disputed) that the Covenant is deliberately destabilizing crystal veins to create conflict between bands and weaken Alliance cohesion.
