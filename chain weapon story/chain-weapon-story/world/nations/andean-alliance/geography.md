# Andean Alliance — Geography & Territory

## Core Geography

### The Three Bands
The Alliance territory is defined by extreme verticality — three distinct ecological zones stacked along a mountain range that runs 800 kilometers north-south.

| Band | Altitude | Climate | Primary Use |
|------|----------|---------|-------------|
| **Low Band** (Yunka) | 500–2,000m | Tropical/subtropical; heavy rainfall | Agriculture, coca cultivation, timber, river trade |
| **Mid Band** (Qucha) | 2,000–4,000m | Temperate; moderate rainfall; fertile valleys | Population centers, terrace farming, governance, markets |
| **High Band** (Urqu) | 4,000–6,000m | Cold/arid; thin air; extreme UV | Llama herding, mineral extraction, shrines, military watchtowers |

### Key Settlements

**Qhapaq Llaqta** (Capital, Mid Band, ~3,200m)
- Built into a mountain saddle; terraced city radiating outward from the central Inti Plaza
- Population: ~45,000; largest settlement in the Alliance
- Administrative hub of the Three-Band Government
- Connected to all major routes via the Qhapaq Ñan (Royal Road system)

**Tambo Wayra** (High Band Fortress, ~4,800m)
- Military observation post commanding the northern pass
- Permanently garrisoned by 200 Warak'a warriors
- Condor Signal relay station; can reach the capital in under 6 hours via signal chain

**Yunka Hatun** (Low Band Trade Port, ~800m)
- River port connecting Alliance territory to lowland trade networks
- Primary cacao, spice, and tropical fruit export point
- Most culturally diverse Alliance settlement; contact zone with outside nations

### The Qhapaq Ñan (Royal Road System)
- 1,200+ kilometers of maintained stone roads connecting all three bands
- Engineering marvel: switchback ascents, suspension bridges over gorges, relay stations (tambos) every 20km
- Maintained by mit'a labor rotation; road upkeep is a sacred duty
- Altitude-adjusted: road surfaces change material by band (packed earth in Yunka, stone in Qucha, flagstone in Urqu)

### Natural Defenses
- Western approach: Sheer cliff faces; only 3 viable passes, all fortified
- Eastern approach: Dense tropical forest (Yunka); impassable without guides
- Northern approach: Single narrow pass at 5,200m; altitude itself is a weapon
- Southern approach: River gorge system requiring bridge crossings the Alliance controls

### Flux Geology
- **Vein of the Earth**: A deep Flux vein runs the length of the mountain range, surfacing at volcanic hotspots
- High-altitude thin Veil zones create dangerous magic instability above 5,000m
- Mineral veins (copper, silver, gold) are intertwined with Flux deposits — mining requires shamanic supervision
- Volcanic thermal vents at three locations provide geothermal energy (heating, metalworking)
- **Altitude sickness + Flux exposure**: Non-adapted visitors experience combined altitude/magical sickness above 4,000m
