# Andean Alliance — Economy & Trade

## Economic Model: Vertical Exchange (Non-Monetary)

The Andean Alliance operates without internal currency. The economy is built on two reciprocal systems that predate the Federation's contract capitalism by centuries.

### Ayni (Reciprocal Exchange)
- **Principle**: Every gift creates an obligation; every service requires return
- **Scope**: Between individuals, families, and communities within a band
- **Tracking**: Communal memory (elders track obligations); no written ledger
- **Enforcement**: Social pressure; failure to reciprocate = loss of community standing
- **Function**: Distributes goods and services without accumulation; prevents wealth concentration

### Mit'a (Rotational Labor)
- **Principle**: Every able-bodied adult owes rotational labor to the community
- **Scope**: Infrastructure maintenance (roads, terraces, bridges), military service, communal projects
- **Duration**: Typically 3 months per year; varies by band and season
- **Exemptions**: Shamans (spiritual labor substitutes), nursing mothers, elderly, disabled
- **Function**: Maintains collective infrastructure without taxation or wage labor

### Vertical Exchange (Between Bands)
- Peak produces: crystals, high-altitude herbs, astronomical observations, condor feathers
- Terrace produces: wool, woven textiles, dairy, grain, warriors
- Valley produces: fruits, vegetables, fish, timber, trade goods (from external commerce)
- Exchange is ritualized: seasonal markets at band boundaries; exchange ratios set by council
- **Key tension**: Peak communities claim their crystals are "worth more" than valley food; valley communities claim their food is essential while crystals are luxury

### Yunka Hatun (Trade Port)
- Only location where foreign merchants are permitted
- Valley-band community; culturally distinct from highland Alliance
- Growing Federation merchant presence; permanent trading post
- Alliance administrators rotate quarterly to prevent corruption
- **Pressure point**: Federation demands expanded access; peak communities demand restriction

## Internal Economic Tensions

### 1. Vein Depletion Crisis
- Crystal veins — the Alliance's most valuable resource — are depleting
- Peak communities have mined for centuries; some veins are exhausted
- New veins require deeper, more dangerous mining
- Covenant has offered advanced mining technology in exchange for trade access
- **Dilemma**: Accept Covenant help and risk infiltration, or accept decline

### 2. Modernization Pressure
- Valley communities (especially Yunka Hatun) have adopted Federation trade practices
- Some valley merchants accumulate personal wealth — violating ayni principles
- Manufactured goods from the Federation displace artisan production
- Young people in valley communities prefer trade wages to mit'a labor

### 3. Food Security
- The Alliance is not fully self-sufficient in grain
- Terrace farming produces enough for normal years; drought years require imports
- Import dependency on Valdimere/Federation creates strategic vulnerability
- Peak communities refuse to expand terrace farming (would require deforesting sacred slopes)

### 4. Labor Shortage
- Mit'a rotation assumes stable population; young people are leaving for valley trade
- Peak mining is dangerous; fewer volunteers each generation
- Shamanic apprenticeship requires 10+ year commitment; young people can't wait
- Military readiness depends on terrace warriors; terrace population is declining relative to valley

## Economic Philosophy

The Alliance's economic philosophy is anti-accumulation:
- **Wealth is relationship, not possession.** Rich = well-connected through ayni. Poor = isolated.
- **Labor is communal, not individual.** Mit'a creates collective ownership of infrastructure.
- **Trade is necessary evil.** External commerce required for metal/grain; minimized as much as possible.
- **Growth is suspicious.** Expansion implies extraction; extraction depletes the mountain.

This philosophy creates resilience (no financial crisis, no debt, no inequality) but also fragility (no surplus, no investment, no adaptation to changing circumstances).

## Covenant Economic Strategy

The Covenant's approach to the Alliance is patient and indirect:
1. **Offer**: Advanced mining technology that could revitalize depleted crystal veins
2. **Price**: Permanent trade mission at Yunka Hatun + access to crystal output
3. **Target**: Valley modernizers who already want external trade
4. **Method**: Frame it as partnership, not infiltration; offer medical supplies alongside mining tools
5. **Goal**: Control crystal supply (which multiple nations depend on)

The Alliance's best defense is isolation — but isolation is becoming economically unsustainable.
