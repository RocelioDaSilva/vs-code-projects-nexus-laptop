# Shukei — Geography & Terrain

## Island Structure
- **Main islands** (4-5 large): Each controlled by different daimyo
- **Archipelago**: Hundreds of smaller islands; frontier/exploration frontier
- **Mountainous terrain**: Central highlands dominate; valleys create natural regional boundaries
- **Coastal regions**: Fishing, maritime trade, international contact points (carefully controlled)

## Regional Characteristics
- **Northern regions**: Colder; traditional strongholds; most conservative daimyo
- **Central highlands**: Monasteries, temples; spiritual authority
- **Southern coastal**: More exposure to foreign influence; wealth from maritime trade
- **Frontier islands**: Sparsely settled; occasional exploration/colonization

## Climate & Seasons
- Temperate island climate; seasonal distinctions important
- Typhoon season: Natural defense against naval invasion; economic disruption
- Cherry blossom / seasonal cycles: Cultural/spiritual significance
- Agricultural calendar determines social rhythms

## Natural Resources
- Timber: Rich forests; shipbuilding industry
- Fish: Abundant; fundamental to diet; cultural significance
- Minerals: Limited; some metals, rare stones
- Hot springs: Cultural/spiritual significance; healing reputation

## Defensive Advantages
- Island geography provides natural moat
- No land borders; military focus on naval defense
- Difficult terrain (mountains) prevents internal conquest
- Insularity = difficulty for outside threats to project power

## Ecological Considerations
- Island ecosystems fragile; overexploitation possible
- Would Flux corruption affect islands differently? (Water-borne? Isolated?)
- Animal migration patterns unique to islands
- Environmental crisis might force unprecedented interaction with mainland

## Trade Geography
- Maritime trade networks connecting to mainland
- Carefully controlled ports (not all daimyo benefit equally)
- Smuggling routes bypassing official trade
- Geographic position as crossroads or excluded (depending on policy)

## Story Relevance
Shukei's isolation means Covenant must work differently there. Insular nations are often most surprised by threats they don't expect because they don't monitor external environment carefully.
