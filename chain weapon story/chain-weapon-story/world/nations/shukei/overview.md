# Shukei — Island Feudal Hierarchy

## Core Identity
Edo-period Japan-inspired island nation with rigid feudal hierarchy, martial traditions, and cultural insularity. Shukei values honor, discipline, and tradition above all else. The nation views change with deep suspicion and maintains strict social stratification.

## Governance Structure
- **Shogun**: Supreme military and political leader (emperor parallel)
- **Daimyo**: Regional lords; semi-autonomous within framework
- **Samurai hierarchy**: Warriors who also serve bureaucratic functions
- **Local controllers**: Village elders, town magistrates (appointees of daimyo)
- **Isolation ministry**: Manages external relationships; controls information flow

## Geographic Position
Island nations with distinct regional cultures. Natural defensive advantages (sea borders). Internally diverse but externally isolationist. Geographic isolation reinforces cultural insularity.

## Philosophical Foundation
**Honor through Tradition**: Shukei believes that adherence to ancestral methods ensures survival and virtue. Change = dishonor; innovation = weakness.

**Hierarchy as Natural**: Society organized in strict layers. Each person has assigned role; excellence = perfect execution of role.

**Martial Virtue**: Military skill and willingness to die for honor = highest values. Weakness = submissiveness to hierarchy.

## International Reputation
- **Feared** for martial prowess and fanaticism
- **Distrusted** for insularity and refusal to negotiate
- **Misunderstood** (outsiders cannot grasp honor culture)
- **Suspicious** of foreign influence contaminating traditions

## Story Relevance
If Rin is Celestial or another character has Shukei background, this nation becomes personal. Shukei's insularity means they're slower to notice Covenant (until direct threat appears). Their rigid hierarchy creates vulnerability (replacement of elite = replacement of everything).
