# Valdimere — Geography & Physical Landscape

## Primary Regions

### Core Heartland
- River valley, heavily irrigated agricultural zone
- Urban centers with bureaucratic infrastructure
- Seat of imperial power and highest population density
- Well-maintained roads, bridges, fortifications

### River Systems
- **Delta region** (south): Trade ports, maritime commerce, mixture of river and sea navigation
- **Middle corridors**: Agricultural abundance; supports large population
- **Northern tributaries**: Mountain access, border defense, raw material extraction

### Mountain Passes
- Strategic frontier between Valdimere and neighboring lands
- Heavily fortified; military installations at key chokepoints
- Trade routes carefully controlled and taxed
- Buffer zone; anything uncontrolled beyond mountains = "barbarian lands"

### Frontier Borderlands
- Aisen's tavern sits in this region
- Mix of Valdimerian settlers and local indigenous populations
- Less centralized authority; regional governors have more freedom
- Crossroads where travelers congregate; neutral ground
- Ecological fragility (first areas to show Flux corruption)

## Climate & Seasons
- Continental: cold winters, hot summers
- Spring flooding expected and planned for (irrigation systems account for it)
- Autumn harvest drives economic cycles
- Winter movement restricted (travel planning key administrative task)

## Trade Routes
- Multiple river paths converging on delta ports
- Mountain passes connecting to other nations
- Overland caravans possible but heavily taxed
- Taxes recorded in triplicate; documentation obsessed

## Ecological Status
- Currently stable but showing early Flux corruption in frontier regions
- Agricultural productivity declining (concern for grain stores)
- Animals migrating away from Valdimerian heartland
- Scholars investigating but findings suppressed through bureaucratic channels

## Strategic Importance
Every geographical feature tied to military/economic strategy:
- Rivers = trade + defensive moats
- Mountains = border control + resource extraction
- Flatlands = farmland + army deployment space
- Delta = naval power + international commerce

Valdimere thinks of geography as infrastructure to be optimized, not terrain to adapt to.
