# Valdimere — Magic Tradition: Formulaic & Bureaucratized

## Core Philosophy
**"Magic is force. Force must be controlled. Control requires standardization."**

Valdimere treats magic like any other state resource: systematize it, measure it, allocate it, tax it. Unregistered magic is theft from the state.

## How Magic Works in Valdimerian System

### Official Channels
1. **Military Lodges** — Train combatants in standardized telekinesis
   - Systematic progression: basic → intermediate → advanced
   - Emphasis on consistency over innovation
   - Loyalty tested; rebels expelled/imprisoned
   
2. **Religious Orders** — Teach sanction-approved spiritual magic
   - Magic tied to imperial-approved religion
   - Heretical traditions suppressed
   - Control of spiritual narrative

3. **Guild Apprenticeships** — Craft magic taught to apprentices
   - Master→journeyman→master progression
   - Knowledge restricted to guild members
   - Prices set by guild; state-enforced monopoly

4. **Imperial Academy** — Advanced training for nobility/elite
   - Highest level of magical technique
   - Access restricted by birth/imperial selection
   - Where strategic magic is developed

### The Cost System
**All channeled magic is taxed**
- Small tax on apprentice-level spells
- Significant tax on military magic use
- Steep tax on experimental magic
- Unregistered magic is illegal seizure (confiscation, imprisonment, or death)

### Magical Emphasis by Tier
- **Tier 1**: Theoretical understanding; advanced techniques; experimental magic (imperial monopoly)
- **Tier 2**: Applied combat magic; sufficient for administration
- **Tier 3**: Standardized techniques; masters teach lodge/guild
- **Tier 4**: Basic techniques or none; labor-focused
- **Tier 5**: Sometimes underground unregistered magic; survival-oriented

## Telekinesis in Valdimerian Context

### Official Approach
- Taught as **precision force application**
- Focus on consistency: same gesture = same result, every time
- Military application: devastating in formation; weaker individually
- Precision valued only insofar as it increases efficiency

### Philosophy
**Power > artistry**. A Valdimerian mage optimizes for output, not elegance.

### Limitations
- Standardization breeds inflexibility
- Telekinesis described in terms of maximum force achievable at given tier
- Innovation viewed with suspicion (deviation from standard)
- Individual genius suppressed in favor of reliable competence

### Application Examples
- **Siege warfare**: Coordinated mages applying force to breach walls
- **Military control**: Suppressing crowd riots; maintaining order
- **Labor**: Moving heavy objects (approved use only)
- **Punishment**: Inflicting pain on prisoners (state-sanctioned torture)

## Unregistered Magic (Black Market)

### Rural Mystics
- Practice old, unnamed magic outside state channels
- Preserve pre-Valdimerian traditions
- Hunted by imperial authorities
- Provide services (healing, divination, cursing) for tithes

### Diaspora Traditions
- Refugee communities preserve forbidden practices
- Information networks transmit knowledge across generations
- Create parallel magical society within Valdimerian borders
- Tension between survival and invisibility

### Underground Resistance Magic
- Developing new techniques Covenant hasn't standardized
- Sharing knowledge freely (counter to state monopoly)
- Experimental synthesis (mixing traditions)
- Aisen's synthesis approach emerges from this tradition

## Key Tension

**Standardization vs. Reality**: Valdimerian bureaucracy documents "approved" magical applications. When reality contradicts documentation (e.g., new magical threats, ecosystem failures, Covenant corruption), bureaucracy denies reality and persecutes those who challenge records. This is the structural weakness the Covenant exploits.

## Historical Note

Valdimere's magical system wasn't always bureaucratic. Centuries ago, magic was diverse and decentralized. The "standardization" happened gradually—first through military reforms, then through economic control, finally through legal codification. This created both stability and stagnation.

The Covenant didn't create this system, but they *perfected* it—using bureaucratic structures to hide within visibility.
