# Mercantile Federation — National Overview

## Identity

The Mercantile Federation is a confederation of autonomous city-states and cantons bound together by interlocking contracts. It has no monarch, no sacred text, no founding myth — only agreements. Everything is contractual: citizenship, property, marriage, military service, even religious observance. Contracts are magically binding, which means law doesn't just punish violation — it physically prevents it.

**Real-World Inspiration**: Hanseatic League + Swiss Confederation + Venetian Republic + Dutch Republic + modern European Union.

## Geography

- **Core territory**: Temperate coastal and riverine regions. Major port cities connected by navigable waterways and maintained roads.
- **Political geography**: 14 cantons, each centered on a major city. Canton boundaries are contract-defined, not geographic. A canton can include non-contiguous territory if the contract specifies it.
- **Key features**: The Arbitration Hall (neutral city where inter-canton disputes are resolved), the Contract Vaults (magically-warded repositories for binding agreements), the Free Ports (trade zones exempt from most regulations).
- **Flux geology**: Artificially stabilized. The Federation adapted Aelfar technology to create engineered Flux nodes at contract-vault sites. These nodes power the magical enforcement of contracts. The most controlled magical environment of any nation.

## Core Values

1. **Contract is sacred**: A signed agreement is the fundamental unit of social trust. Breaking a contract is not just illegal — it's existentially threatening to a society built on contractual trust.
2. **Individual sovereignty**: Within the bounds of your contracts, you are free. What isn't contracted is unrestricted.
3. **Commerce as civilization**: Trade creates wealth, wealth creates comfort, comfort creates culture. The merchant is the true builder of civilization.
4. **Legal equality**: Before the contract, all parties are equal. (In practice, the party who drafts the contract has enormous advantage.)
5. **Pragmatic pluralism**: Different cantons have different customs, different religions, different social norms. The Federation doesn't standardize culture — it standardizes contracts.

## Government Structure

### The Federal Contract

The Federation itself is a contract — the Federal Compact — signed by the 14 founding cantons 300 years ago. The Compact defines:
- Common defense obligations
- Trade freedom between cantons
- Arbitration procedures for inter-canton disputes
- Amendment process (requires 10 of 14 cantons to ratify)

### Federal Assembly
- Each canton sends 2-5 delegates (proportional to population and trade volume).
- Meets quarterly. Supermajority required for most decisions.
- Can propose but cannot compel. Cantons that disagree with a federal decision can... disagree. Enforcement depends on other cantons' willingness to sanction.

### Canton Governance
Varies by canton. Most common structures:
- **Merchant Council**: Guild representatives govern (5 cantons)
- **Patriciate**: Hereditary merchant families (3 cantons)
- **Mixed Assembly**: Guild + citizen representatives (4 cantons)
- **Direct Democracy**: All citizens vote (2 cantons, smallest)

### The Arbitration Court
- 7 arbitrators selected from retired legal scholars. Serve 10-year non-renewable terms.
- Hears inter-canton disputes and contract cases with federal implications.
- Decisions are magically enforced through contract-vault enchantments.
- The most trusted institution in the Federation — precisely because it has no political power beyond its cases.

## Class Structure

### Tier 1: Patriciate & Senior Guild Masters (3%)
- Old money. Families that signed the original Federal Compact. Their names are on the founding contract — literally enshrined in magical law.
- Control the largest trading houses, contract-vault access, and arbitration appointments.

### Tier 2: Guild Officers & Professional Class (12%)
- Guild masters, licensed arbitrators, senior merchants, physicians, engineers.
- Economically comfortable. Politically influential within their canton.

### Tier 3: Guild Members & Artisans (35%)
- Skilled workers organized into guilds. Each guild has its own charter (a contract).
- Economic security comes from guild membership. Non-guild workers have no protections.

### Tier 4: Laborers & Unaffiliated (40%)
- Workers not organized into guilds. Manual laborers, seasonal workers, sailors, servants.
- No contract protections beyond basic citizenship (which provides minimal rights).
- Free Ports attract many: employment without guild restrictions, but also without guild protections.

### Tier 5: Contract-Bound & Refugees (10%)
- **Contract-bound**: People who signed exploitative contracts and are now legally obligated to serve. Not slavery in name — completely slavery in practice. The contract is magically binding; they cannot leave.
- **Refugees**: Non-citizens without contracts. No legal standing. Vulnerable to exploitation because any contract they sign binds them but no one is obligated to offer fair terms.

## Economy

### The Contract Economy

**Everything has a price. Everything has a contract.**
- Property: Contractual. You own what your contract says you own.
- Employment: Contractual. Terms defined by agreement. Hours, wages, conditions — all specified.
- Marriage: A contract. Divorce is contract termination, subject to exit clauses.
- Citizenship: Contractual. Each canton defines citizenship requirements. Most require guild membership or property ownership.

### Trade Infrastructure
- The most sophisticated commercial infrastructure in the world.
- Banking: Contract-backed banking with magically-enforced terms. Loans, insurance, investment vehicles.
- Currency: The "compact mark" — standardized across all 14 cantons. Backed by contract-vault reserves.
- Maritime fleet: Largest in the world. Trade routes spanning all coastal nations.
- Insurance: Federation invented maritime insurance (contractual risk-pooling). This enables high-risk trade that other nations can't finance.

### Economic Power
- Richest nation per capita.
- Controls international shipping routes.
- Federal banking system is the default mechanism for inter-national trade (even Valdimere and Therryn use Federation banks for large transactions).
- **Vulnerability**: Entire system depends on magical contract enforcement. If the contract-vaults are disrupted, the economy collapses overnight because trust was never social — it was always magical.

## Military

### Defense by Contract
- No standing federal army.
- Each canton maintains its own militia (contractual obligation of adult citizens).
- Federal defense is a contract: each canton contributes forces proportional to population and trade volume.
- **Mercenary tradition**: The Federation hires mercenaries through contracts. Its military is the best-equipped (funded by trade wealth) but the least motivated (fighting for pay, not identity).

### Strengths
- Best-equipped military in the world (wealth buys superior arms and armor).
- Naval superiority (largest fleet, most experienced sailors).
- Fortification engineering (contract-funded, well-maintained).

### Weaknesses
- No military identity (soldiers fight for contracts, not causes).
- Inter-canton rivalry impedes unified command.
- Mercenary dependence means loyalty is purchased, not earned. If the money stops, the army dissolves.

## Religion & Philosophy

### Contractualism
- Not a religion — a philosophical framework that functions as civic religion.
- Core belief: The contract is the only reliable social mechanism. Trust without contract is naive. Contract without enforcement is theater.
- **Variation by canton**: Some cantons maintain traditional religious practices alongside contractualism. Others have replaced religion entirely with legal philosophy.

### The Sacred Contract
- The Federal Compact is treated with religious reverence. Annual "Compact Day" celebrations include reading the original text aloud, renewing oath-contracts, and processing through the Arbitration Hall.
- Some citizens genuinely believe the Compact is divinely inspired. Most view it as a pragmatic achievement deserving respect.

## Current Crises

1. **Contract exploitation**: Sophisticated legal actors are writing contracts that technically comply with law but extractively exploit less-sophisticated parties. The arbitration system is overwhelmed with cases.
2. **Democratic deficit**: Most citizens have no voice in cantonal governance (guild-based representation excludes non-guild workers). Growing resentment among Tier 4 and 5.
3. **Military coordination failure**: When external threats emerge, the assembly debates defense contracts while the threat advances. The last border incursion took 6 weeks to generate a defense authorization.
4. **Contract-bound crisis**: The number of contract-bound persons (functionally enslaved through legal mechanisms) is growing. Abolitionist movements argue that magically-binding contracts violate natural rights. The legal establishment argues that freely-signed contracts are an expression of freedom, even if the outcome resembles servitude.
5. **Covenant legal infiltration**: The Covenant operates perfectly within Federation law. Their contracts are impeccable. Their terms are technically fair. Their integration into the banking system is complete. By the time anyone realizes the Covenant controls critical financial infrastructure, they control it through legal mechanisms that the Federation's own system protects.
