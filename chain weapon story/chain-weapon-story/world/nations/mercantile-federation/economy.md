# Mercantile Federation — Economy & Trade Systems

## Primary Economic Model: Contract Capitalism

Everything has a price. Everything has a contract. The Federation is the wealthiest nation per capita and controls international shipping routes, banking infrastructure, and trade finance.

### The Contract Economy
- **Property**: Contractual. You own what your contract says you own.
- **Employment**: Contractual. Terms defined by agreement — hours, wages, conditions specified.
- **Marriage**: A contract. Divorce is contract termination, subject to exit clauses.
- **Citizenship**: Contractual. Each canton defines requirements; most require guild membership or property ownership.

### Trade Infrastructure
- Most sophisticated commercial infrastructure in the world
- **Banking**: Contract-backed with magically-enforced terms. Loans, insurance, investment vehicles.
- **Currency**: The "compact mark" — standardized across 14 cantons. Backed by contract-vault reserves.
- **Maritime fleet**: Largest in the world; trade routes spanning all coastal nations.
- **Insurance**: Federation invented maritime insurance (contractual risk-pooling). Enables high-risk trade that other nations can't finance.

## Primary Economic Drivers

### Maritime Commerce (Dominant)
- Controls 60%+ of international shipping
- Port cities generate majority of Federation wealth
- Naval superiority enforces trade route access
- Maritime guilds (shipping, insurance, navigation) are the most powerful economic institutions

### Banking & Finance
- Federal banking system is the default mechanism for inter-national trade
- Even Valdimere and Therryn use Federation banks for large transactions
- Loan instruments, insurance products, investment vehicles — all contractual, all magically enforced
- Banking guild controls currency supply and credit availability

### Manufacturing
- Guild-organized production: textiles, glass, paper, precision instruments, ships
- Quality controlled by guild standards; innovation through guild-approved research
- Export of manufactured goods to all trading partners
- Specialty: magical infrastructure components (contract-vault equipment, Flux-stabilized instruments)

### Agriculture
- Temperate coastal agriculture; sufficient but not surplus
- Food security through trade (imports from Valdimere and Andean Alliance)
- Agricultural workers are Tier 4 (unguilded); no political representation

## Taxation System

| Item | Rate | Notes |
|------|------|-------|
| Trade transactions | 5-15% | Canton-specific; varies by good type |
| Guild membership | Annual fee | Plus 10-15% of earnings |
| Property ownership | Annual assessment | Based on contract-vault valuation |
| Maritime cargo | Per-cargo levy | Based on weight and destination |
| Contract registration | Per-contract fee | Covers magical enforcement costs |
| Federal contribution | Canton-assessed | Each canton contributes proportionally to federal budget |

### Revenue Distribution
- Canton treasuries retain 70-80% of collected taxes
- Federal budget funded by canton contributions (quarterly assessment)
- Guild treasuries supplement member services through dues
- Arbitration Court funded independently through case fees

## Currency & Finance

### The Compact Mark
- Silver-standard currency; fixed value maintained by contract-vault reserves
- Paper notes backed by vault deposits (fractional reserve system)
- Accepted throughout the trading world as reliable medium of exchange
- **Vulnerability**: Entire system depends on trust in contract-vault integrity

### Banking Innovation
| Instrument | Function | Who Benefits |
|------------|----------|-------------|
| Trade credit | Short-term loans for cargo purchase; repaid on delivery | Merchants (Tier 2-3) |
| Maritime insurance | Risk pooling for shipping losses; contractual payout | Ship owners, investors |
| Investment shares | Fractional ownership of trading ventures; contractual profit-sharing | Patriciate, wealthy guild masters |
| Life Contracts | Debt servitude; magically binding | Creditors (at the expense of debtors) |

### The Freeport Exception
- Freeport operates with reduced financial regulation
- No guild restrictions; no contract-vault enforcement within city limits
- Result: black market banking, unlicensed trade, innovation, fraud
- Federation tolerates Freeport because it serves as economic pressure valve

## Internal Economic Contradictions

### 1. Legal Equality vs. Structural Exploitation
- All parties technically equal before the contract
- But: contract-drafting sophistication concentrated among wealthy; poor sign agreements they don't fully understand
- Result: legal mechanisms produce outcomes indistinguishable from theft

### 2. Contract-Bound Crisis
- 10% of population in Life Contracts (growing)
- System designed to prevent escape: debt terms ensure repayment is nearly impossible
- Economically productive (bound labor is cheap) but morally indefensible
- Abolition would destabilize the entire contract-enforcement mechanism

### 3. Canton Competition as Inefficiency
- 14 cantons competing wastes resources on duplication and sabotage
- Each canton maintains separate military, administration, and trade apparatus
- Federal coordination is slow and partial; supermajority requirement prevents unified economic policy

### 4. Aelfar Infrastructure Dependency
- Contract enforcement depends on Aelfar-derived technology maintained empirically, not theoretically
- The Federation doesn't fully understand how the contract-vault mechanism works
- If disrupted (earthquake, sabotage, Covenant manipulation), the economy collapses overnight
- Trust was never social — it was always magical. Remove magic, trust evaporates.

## Covenant Economic Presence

### Current Infiltration
- Covenant-affiliated banks control ~25% of inter-national trade financing
- Acquiring controlling interests in maritime insurance guilds
- Members of the Financial Arbitrators Guild
- Positions on Arbitration Court nomination committees

### Strategy
- Control institutions that adjudicate contracts without changing any laws
- Create structural dependency on Covenant-controlled financial services
- Withdrawal of services would collapse trade credit, insurance, and banking
- Federation's own legal principles protect the mechanism of its subordination

### Why the Federation Can't Respond
- Covenant operations are legal; all contracts are enforceable
- Identifying "Covenant banking" is impossible (they use Federation institutions)
- Expelling Covenant agents would require breaking contracts — which undermines the entire system
- The Financial Arbitrators Guild warned the Assembly; the Assembly passed a non-binding resolution of concern
