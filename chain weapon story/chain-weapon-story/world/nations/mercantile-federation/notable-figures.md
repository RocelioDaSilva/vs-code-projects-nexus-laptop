# Mercantile Federation — Notable Figures

## Current Leadership

### Grand Arbiter Helena Voss
- **Age**: 63
- **Canton**: Goldmark (financial capital)
- **Role**: Chief Arbitration Court judge; most trusted institutional figure in the Federation
- **Personality**: Impeccably fair, rigidly legalistic, deeply conflicted about the system she upholds. Privately acknowledges the Life Contract crisis is indefensible; publicly rules that contracts freely signed are enforceable.
- **Position**: Strict constructionist — the Compact is the Compact; if you want change, use the amendment process (which requires 10 of 14 cantons).
- **Weakness**: Follows law over justice. If the Covenant's contracts are legal, she will enforce them.
- **Narrative relevance**: If the resistance needs Federation legal support, Helena is the key figure — but convincing her requires proving the Covenant broke a contract (not just that they're evil).

### Assembly Speaker Maximilian Thrace
- **Age**: 51
- **Canton**: Portmund (naval power)
- **Role**: Federal Assembly Speaker; manages inter-canton negotiations
- **Personality**: Charismatic, politically astute, frustrated by the supermajority requirement that paralyzes federal action. Privately supports military reform but can't get 10 cantons to agree.
- **Position**: Federal strengthening — wants more federal authority over canton defense, trade policy, and financial regulation. Opposes Covenant influence but can't prove it violates law.
- **Strength**: Best negotiator in the Assembly; has achieved more supermajority votes than any Speaker in 50 years
- **Narrative relevance**: Potential resistance ally IF the threat is framed in contractual terms. "The Covenant is making contracts under fraudulent pretenses" — that argument would mobilize him.

### Patrician Aldric van der Berg
- **Age**: 58
- **Canton**: Goldmark
- **Role**: Head of the van der Berg trading house; patriciate power broker; Federal Assembly delegate
- **Personality**: Calculating, tradition-bound, protective of family interests. Sees the Federation as a van der Berg creation (his family signed the original Compact).
- **Position**: Conservative patriciate — maintain current system; resist reform; manage Covenant relationship pragmatically.
- **Connection**: Father of Pieter van der Berg (Academy student, resistance-adjacent). Aldric's greatest fear: his son's idealism will destroy the family's position.
- **Narrative relevance**: Pieter's arc — choosing between family loyalty and resistance — is Aldric's arc in reverse.

### Guildmaster Margarethe Stern (Financial Arbitrators Guild)
- **Age**: 47
- **Canton**: Goldmark
- **Role**: Head of the Financial Arbitrators Guild; the person who warned the Assembly about Covenant infiltration
- **Personality**: Brilliant, paranoid, correct. Has mapped Covenant financial operations across 8 cantons. Nobody believes her assessment because the evidence looks like normal commerce.
- **Position**: Covenant is an existential threat; the Federation's own legal principles prevent response; the system must be modified to survive.
- **Weakness**: Her warnings sound like conspiracy theory to colleagues who can't distinguish Covenant banking from ordinary banking.
- **Narrative relevance**: Critical intelligence source. If the resistance can validate her evidence through external confirmation, she can mobilize the Financial Arbitrators Guild against Covenant operations.

## Freeport Characters

### "The Broker" — Unnamed Freeport Intermediary
- **Age**: Unknown (~40s)
- **Role**: Premier black-market contract broker in Freeport; arranges deals that can't go through official channels
- **Personality**: Amoral pragmatist; will arrange anything for anyone if the price is right
- **Significance**: Bridge between legitimate Federation commerce and underground resistance logistics
- **Covenant connection**: Has unknowingly brokered Covenant transactions; has also brokered resistance transactions. Serves whoever pays.

### Dr. Emilia Carsten (Freeport Healer)
- **Age**: 35
- **Role**: Unlicensed medical mage operating outside guild restrictions; serves Freeport's Tier 4-5 population
- **Personality**: Compassionate, exhausted, angry about guild restrictions that prevent qualified healers from practicing without guild membership
- **Position**: Abolitionist — active in movement to end Life Contracts; treats contract-bound patients for free
- **Narrative relevance**: Ground-level perspective on Federation inequality. If resistance needs Freeport support, Emilia is the trusted figure among the underclass.

## Historical Figures

### The Fourteen Signers
- 14 canton representatives who signed the Federal Compact 300 years ago
- Their descendants form the patriciate class
- Names inscribed in the contract-vault; magically preserved
- Annual Compact Day celebrates their achievement
- **Dark history**: Three of the fourteen cantons were coerced into signing through trade embargo; legal legitimacy still debated

### Admiral Katya Portmund (The Pirate Campaign, 3 years ago)
- Canton militia commander who unilaterally repelled a pirate fleet while the Assembly debated authorization
- Censured by the Assembly for acting without federal authority
- Celebrated by her canton as a hero
- **Legacy**: Proof that the Federation can act — but only when someone breaks the rules

## Covenant Operatives (Known/Suspected)

### Director Ernst Lehmann
- **Cover**: Senior banking executive at Goldmark's second-largest bank
- **Actual role**: Covenant financial operations coordinator for the Federation
- **Method**: All operations legal; all contracts enforceable; indistinguishable from ordinary banking
- **Status**: Identified by Margarethe Stern's analysis; no legal basis for action
- **Goal**: Acquire controlling interests in maritime insurance and Arbitration Court nominations

### Contract-Lawyers (Network)
- 8-12 Covenant-trained contract-lawyers operating across multiple cantons
- Draft contracts that extract maximum value while maintaining technical legality
- Target: Tier 4 workers and refugees; convert them to contract-bound status; bound workers serve Covenant-affiliated businesses
- **Protection**: Their contracts are legally impeccable; the Federation's own system defends them
