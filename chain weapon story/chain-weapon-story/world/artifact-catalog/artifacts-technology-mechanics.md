# Artifact & Technology Catalogs: Mechanical Effects & Story Integration

## VALDIMERIAN FORMULAE-BASED ARTIFACTS

### Tier 1: Common Artifacts (Accessible to most people)

**Light-stone** (Formulae-locked)
- Function: Emits light; brightness variable
- Mechanism: Formula reactivated daily; uses practitioner energy briefly
- Cost: Moderate (can be bought, relatively accessible)
- Story use: Lighting in dark scenes; shows class difference (wealthy have light-stones in homes; poor use oil lamps)
- Limitation: Requires licensed practitioner to recharge (dependency)

**Heat-seal Container**
- Function: Preserves food/materials; prevents spoilage
- Mechanism: Formula maintains temperature/dryness inside
- Cost: Expensive (requires craftwork + formula-setting)
- Story use: Evidence of wealth (who has preserved food available?); logistics (resource preservation)
- Limitation: Formula decays; replacement costs accumulate

**Communication crystal** (Licensed-restricted)
- Function: Sends short messages across distance (limited range; ~20 miles)
- Mechanism: Two crystals linked through formula; activation by practitioner
- Cost: Very expensive; state-controlled
- Story use: Military/noble communication; Covenant technology advantage; resistance must use runners instead (slower)
- Limitation: Only licensed practitioners can use; messages monitored/recorded

### Tier 2: Military/Professional Artifacts

**Enchanted armor** (Valdimerian standard)
- Function: Reduces damage physical (impact resistance)
- Mechanism: Formula woven through metalwork
- Cost: Extremely expensive; noble-only
- Story use: Combat advantage; wealthy nobles fight differently (more protected)
- Limitation: Degradation over time; requires renewal formula

**Detection ward**
- Function: Senses movement/intrusion in defined area
- Mechanism: Formula spanning space; triggers alarm
- Cost: Very expensive; state/military access primarily
- Story use: Garrison defense; resistance must carefully bypass (intelligence checkpoint)
- Limitation: Can be confused by multiple moving objects; false alarms

**Document-seal** (Tamper-proof)
- Function: Protects documents; shows tampering attempts
- Mechanism: Formula-lock; document sealed inside magically-warded container
- Cost: Very expensive; legal/state documents primarily
- Story use: Legal authority; shown through document presentation; Amara must forge/bypass for documentation
- Limitation: Can be broken but requires significant effort + specialized knowledge

### Tier 3: Strategic/Rare Artifacts

**Passage-key** (Portal creation)
- Function: Creates temporary portal between two marked locations
- Mechanism: Complex formula; requires significant practitioner energy
- Cost: Nation-state only; extremely rare
- Story use: Strategic military advantage (troop movement); Covenant has several; resistance must use terrain/conventional movement
- Limitation: Highly energy-intensive; limited uses per day

**Covenant command rune** (Control artifact)
- Function: Overrides other formulae in immediate area
- Mechanism: Master formula that establishes authority hierarchy over other spells
- Cost: Unique; Covenant-only
- Story use: Covenant's ultimate advantage; can neutralize other artifacts
- Limitation: Covenant's over-reliance on this becomes weakness if captured/destroyed

## SHUKEI MATERIAL ARTIFACTS

### Tier 1: Common Martial Equipment

**Refinement-steel** (Weapon enhancement)
- Function: Weapon doesn't dull; maintains edge (permanent)
- Mechanism: Material property (not formula); crafted through specific technique
- Cost: Moderate (high-quality weapon baseline cost)
- Story use: Warrior experience (weapons working well); professional vs. amateur visible through equipment
- Limitation: Can still break under extreme force; can be stolen

**Meditation anchor** (Carved stone)
- Function: Focuses practitioner during meditation; enhances spiritual centering
- Mechanism: Material shape + carving; psychological + material combined
- Cost: Low (crafted by any skilled worker)
- Story use: Warriors carry personal anchors (shows spiritual practice); can be traded between masters/students (relationship visible)

**Combat training dummy** (Wooden sculpture)
- Function: Practice target; realistic feedback on force
- Mechanism: Material engineering; weighted, balanced
- Cost: High (requires master craftsperson skill)
- Story use: Marks serious training space (presence of dummies shows warrior culture activity)

### Tier 2: Master-level Artifacts

**Crafted master sword** (Named weapon)
- Function: Enhanced performance (lighter, stronger, sharper than standard)
- Mechanism: Master craftsmanship; specific material + technique superior
- Cost: Very expensive; significant prestige item
- Story use: Master gives to student at advancement (relationship formalized); weapon carries history/honor
- Limitation: Can be damaged; irreplaceable if lost (emotional + practical consequence)

**Armor of tournament victors** (Enhanced durability)
- Function: Provides impact resistance during combat
- Mechanism: Crafted material; lamination technique
- Cost: Expensive; tournament winners + wealthy
- Story use: Visual status marker (who wears superior armor?); shows class inequality in "meritocratic" competition
- Limitation: Material fatigue after repeated impact; requires seasonal renewal

**Healing tea set** (Botanical + material)
- Function: Prepared tea promotes recovery/healing
- Mechanism: Material properties (specific ceramic) + botanical ingredients
- Cost: Moderate
- Story use: Masters offer tea to injured students (care + practical healing); shows mentorship relationships
- Limitation: Requires specific ingredients; effectiveness varies

### Tier 3: Legendary/Rare

**Tempest-blade** (Master creation)
- Function: In skilled hands, increases speed/precision impossibly (pushes physical limits, requires immense discipline)
- Mechanism: Perfect material + master craftsmanship + user spiritual alignment
- Cost: Effectively priceless (created once in generations)
- Story use: Referenced but not obtained (shows what mastery can achieve); aspirational
- Limitation: Not practical military weapon; too specialized; fragile in wrong hands

## THERRYN MATHEMATICAL ARTIFACTS

### Tier 1: Common Infrastructure

**Water-valve** (Mathematical precision engineering)
- Function: Controls water flow with mathematical precision
- Mechanism: Gearing + calculated angles
- Cost: Moderate (requires engineering)
- Story use: Shows Therryn infrastructure superiority (cities function efficiently)
- Limitation: Requires maintenance; can calculate incorrectly (engineering failure possible)

**Balance-scale** (Perfect measurement)
- Function: Weighs objects with mathematical precision
- Mechanism: Engineered balance; no formula needed
- Cost: Moderate
- Story use: Market transactions (ensures fairness); shows Therryn influence on commerce
- Limitation: Can be sabotaged; precision requires proper maintenance

**Numerical record** (Information storage)
- Function: Stores complex information in encoded numerical form
- Mechanism: Symbolic notation; can be translated + understood by trained readers
- Cost: Low (paper + ink)
- Story use: Shows information density possible; Therryn advantage in record-keeping
- Limitation: Requires training to decode; can be intercepted

### Tier 2: Academic/Professional

**Astronomical device** (Calculate celestial positions)
- Function: Calculates star/planet positions with high accuracy; used for navigation + calendar
- Mechanism: Mechanical gearing; mathematical precision
- Cost: Very expensive; rare
- Story use: Shows knowledge advantage; navigation with precision vs. guess-work
- Limitation: Requires skilled operator; can break; sensitive to damage

**Engineering blueprint** (Detailed planning artifact)
- Function: Stores complete construction specifications
- Mechanism: Symbolic language + mathematical notation
- Cost: Expensive (requires expert creation)
- Story use: Knowledge stored in portable form; easier to replicate infrastructure
- Limitation: Useless without trained readers; can be lost/damaged

**Calculating device** (Mechanical computation)
- Function: Performs mathematical calculations automatically (aided computation)
- Mechanism: Mechanical gearing; requires trained operation
- Cost: Extremely expensive; rare; state/academic mainly
- Story use: Advantage in strategic planning (calculations faster); resistance lacks this capability
- Limitation: Can produce incorrect results from operator error; very complex

### Tier 3: Rare/Strategic

**Astronomical observatory** (Infrastructure-scale)
- Function: Detailed celestial observation; enables accurate prediction of celestial events
- Mechanism: Precision lens + mechanical alignment
- Cost: Nation-state only; extremely rare
- Story use: Long-range planning advantage; Therryn can predict seasons perfectly (agricultural production optimized)
- Limitation: Fixed location; can be damaged/destroyed; takes years to rebuild

## UMARAN RELATIONAL ARTIFACTS

**Craft-connected tool** (Relationship to creation)
- Function: Tools work exceptionally well in hands of maker (psychological + material)
- Mechanism: No formula; relationship + familiarity = effectiveness
- Cost: Low (personal craftsmanship)
- Story use: Personal tools more effective than borrowed ones (shows importance of relationships)
- Limitation: Doesn't work well for unfamiliar users

**Kinship token** (Relationship reminder)
- Function: Small object carrying relational significance (reminder of relationship/obligation)
- Mechanism: Material + meaning (no magical mechanism)
- Cost: Varies (can be worthless material but priceless meaning)
- Story use: Characters carry tokens; represents families/bonds/obligations
- Limitation: Only meaningful to those in relationship; seems worthless to others

**Healing herb collection** (Botanical knowledge)
- Function: Plants with medicinal properties; knowledge of proper use
- Mechanism: Botanical properties + learned preparation
- Cost: Low to moderate
- Story use: Healers recognized by knowledge; can help without formula dependency

- Cost: Moderate (crafted with care)

- Cost: Low to moderate
- Story use: Shows relationship mapping; essential for obligation networks; loss = identity loss

### Tier 3: Rare/Ceremonial

**Council circle** (Relational infrastructure)

- Mechanism: Spatial + ceremonial design
- Cost: Significant (communal resource)

- Limitation: Only works if participants committed to consensus

## CRAFT SPECIALIZATIONS & PRODUCTION CHAINS

### Valdimerian Formulae-based

**Production**: Formula → licensed practitioner → artifact
**Bottleneck**: Licensing (Covenant controls formula distribution)
**Advantage**: Precision, scalability (once formula understood)
**Disadvantage**: Rigid (formula must be exact)

### Shukei Material-based

**Production**: Raw material → master craftsperson → artifact
**Bottleneck**: Master skill (takes decades to develop)
**Advantage**: Flexibility (masters innovate), durability
**Disadvantage**: Slow production, difficult to scale

### Therryn Mathematics-based

**Production**: Design → engineering → artifact
**Bottleneck**: Accurate design phase (errors in design = failure)
**Advantage**: Precision, replicability (same design = same output)
**Disadvantage**: Complexity, skill-requirement

### Umaran Relationship-based

**Production**: Community relationship → collective creation → artifact
**Bottleneck**: Consensus + participation (slower; requires agreement)
**Advantage**: Durability (cared-for objects last), ethical production
**Disadvantage**: Scale limits, time-intensive

## STORY DEPLOYMENT

**Act I**: Artifacts establish class differences (who has what technology).

**Act II**: Resistance must acquire/repurpose artifacts (Keth creates; Maven distributes).

**Act III**: Technology advantage shifts (artifacts lost/destroyed; improvisation necessary).

**Post-war**: Synthesis artifacts become standard (shows integration visibly).
