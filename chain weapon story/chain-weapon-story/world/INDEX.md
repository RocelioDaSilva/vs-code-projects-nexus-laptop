## 📝 KEY THEMES ACROSS FILES

### Standardization vs. Adaptation
- Valdimere: Standardization (bureaucracy) creates blindness
- Shukei: Rigidity creates fragility
- Therryn: Scholarly flexibility + institutional bias = adaptation possible
- Umara: Decentralized adaptation quick but sometimes uninformed

**Story Use**: Why different nations respond differently to Covenant threat

### Class Mobility
- Harder in rigid systems (Shukei)
- Possible in meritocratic systems (Therryn)
- Variable in relationship systems (Umara)
- Bureaucratically slow in centralized (Valdimere)

**Story Use**: Where resistance can recruit; how different people view revolution

### Magical Philosophy
- Formulaic (Valdimere) vs. Embodied (Shukei) vs. Scholarly (Therryn) vs. Spiritual (Umara)
- Synthesis approach threatens all existing monopolies

**Story Use**: Why Aisen's synthesis is revolutionary; cultural conflicts over validity

### Information & Denial
- Valdimere: Records override reality (bureaucratic denial)
- Shukei: Insularity prevents information flow
- Therryn: Information abundant but competitive (scholarly disputes)
- Umara: Information through relationships (slower but fuller picture)

**Story Use**: Who notices Covenant first? Who resists accepting it?

- Centralized vs. decentralized defensive strategies
