## Overview

This foundational batch establishes the cosmological framework, magical physics, and historical origins that underpin the entire world. It covers the Weave vs. Flux duality, the Aelfar civilization and Sundering, the Veil mechanism, and introductory concepts for magic system design. This is the bedrock upon which all subsequent worldbuilding is constructed.

---

## Key Topics

### 1. Cosmology: Weave vs. Flux

**The Weave** — Physical law and material reality
- Encompasses gravity, light, time, matter, momentum
- Operates on conservation principles
- Deterministic and observable
- The "default" state of the universe

**The Flux** — Raw magical energy overlay
- Can influence the Weave when properly channeled
- Follows different rules: abundant where Veil is thin, scarce where thick
- Magical potential that bends physics
- Additive layer (doesn't replace Weave, modifies it)

**Interaction**: Magic works by converting Flux (mana) into effects that still obey physical laws once manifested. Examples:
- Magic cannot create matter from nothing; it redistributes or transforms
- Magical fire still burns according to thermodynamics
- Telekinesis still respects mass and acceleration limits
- The cost of magic is partly in Flux consumption, partly in physical strain

---

### 2. Origins: The Aelfar Age

**Before the Sundering** (~2000+ years before protagonist's era)
- The Aelfar were an advanced civilization that understood both Weave and Flux deeply
- They built cities, rituals, and infrastructure using both magic and engineering
- They developed techniques to thin the Veil locally and access greater Flux
- Knowledge system was hierarchical; most citizens understood basics, elite understood deep theory

**Aelfar Knowledge Focus**:
- Resonance crystals (natural Flux batteries)
- Weave-Flux harmonics (how to make magic efficient)
- Dimensional manipulation and long-distance transit
- Lifespan extension through Flux techniques
- Environmental shaping (agriculture, architecture, climate control)

**The Hubris Error**:
- An elite faction attempted a major ritual to permanently thin the Veil
- Goal: unlimited Flux access and transformation of physical reality
- Execution: a multi-site synchronization ritual across the Aelfar territories
- Result: catastrophic failure (the Sundering)

---

### 3. The Sundering & The Veil

**What Happened**:
- The ritual backfired spectacularly
- Instead of thinning the Veil, it *tore* it in multiple places
- Raw, uncontrolled Flux poured into the world
- Aelfar infrastructure failed (crystal networks shattered; rituals destabilized)
- Most Aelfar cities were destroyed by Flux cascades or environmental collapse
- Survivors scattered; civilization collapsed within a generation

**The Veil Mechanism** (post-Sundering, ongoing)
- The Veil is a semi-permeable barrier between the Flux source and material reality
- Thickness varies by location: thin in some regions (high magic availability), thick in others (low magic)
- Holes and tears remain from the Sundering (called "Flux Tears")
- The Veil is slowly, naturally healing (very slowly — thousands of years)
- Where tears exist, raw Flux leaks, mutating fauna and empowering artifacts

---

### 4. Flux Tears & Environmental Consequence

**Flux Tears**:
- Persistent rifts where the Veil is absent or severely damaged
- Flux flows out uncontrolled in these areas
- Creates zones of high magical activity and unpredictability
- Creatures exposed to Flux Tears mutate or gain unexpected abilities
- Artifacts left in tears can become sentient or dangerously unstable

**Regional Impact**:
- The Shattered Coast (mainland side): multiple major tears; thin Veil; wild magic zone; source of Flux anomalies
- Central regions: Veil mostly intact; magic is possible but bounded
- Heartland: thicker Veil; magic is rare and learned through formal study
- Isolated valleys: variable; some thin, some thick

**Human Response** (early post-Sundering):
- Survivors learned to live with magic as environmental fact
- Developed techniques to work around tears (avoidance, protection, harvesting)
- Built settlements in stable regions
- Created warning systems for Flux anomalies

---

### 5. Magic System Foundations

**Core Principle**: Magic is not free. It costs mana (Flux). Using magic creates consequences.

**How Magic Works** (mechanics):
1. A caster gathers mana from the ambient Flux
2. Caster shapes mana via will, knowledge, or ritual
3. Mana converts into effect (manifests in Weave)
4. Effect follows physical laws (once created, gravity/etc. apply)
5. Side effects occur if mana is inefficient or over-extended

**Efficiency**:
- Efficient magic: aligned with existing physical processes (e.g., adding wind to a sail vs. creating flight from scratch)
- Inefficient magic: fighting physical laws (e.g., holding a boulder against gravity for hours)
- Conservation: mana converts to effect; excess heat, light, sound, or physical backlash if poorly controlled

**Limits**:
- Ambient Flux limits how much mana is available (thicker Veil = harder to cast)
- Personal mana pool (caster endurance) limits how much one person can channel
- Skill/knowledge limits what effects can be shaped
- Resistance: some materials, people, or places resist magic (dense matter, people with natural resistance, shielded areas)

---

### 6. Types of Magic (Foundational Categories)

**Ritualistic Magic**:
- Prepared in advance; requires materials, circles, stable conditions
- More efficient than improvised magic
- Slower but more controlled
- Examples: land wards, artifact charging, summoning

**Improvised Magic** ("Quick-cast")
- On-the-fly spellcasting without preparation
- Less efficient, faster, riskier
- Requires deep knowledge to avoid backlash
- Examples: creating light, heating water, pushing objects

**Artifact Magic**:
- Pre-enchanted objects hold mana/effects
- Can be used by non-mages if designed for that
- Limited by artifact durability and mana storage
- Examples: resonance crystals, enchanted weapons, ward stones

**Natural Magic**:
- Plants, animals, and places with inherent magical properties
- Not consciously "cast" but triggered by conditions
- Flows from Flux Tears or high-Flux regions
- Examples: creatures with magical abilities, Flux-touched plants, resonance groves

---

### 7. Aelfar Remnants & Artifacts

**What Remains**:
- Ruins scattered across the Shattered Coast and isolated pockets
- Most infrastructure collapsed; some protected structures survived
- Artifacts (weapons, tools, housing) ranging from intact to corrupted
- Written records (in Aelfar script; mostly unread by moderns)
- Knowledge fragments (passed orally; often inaccurate or mythologized)

**Artifact Categories**:
- **Functional**: Still operable (e.g., a preserved tool, a weapon)
- **Corrupted**: Damaged by Flux; unpredictable behavior
- **Sentient**: Some artifacts gained consciousness post-Sundering
- **Dangerous**: May actively harm or trap users

**Knowledge Loss**:
- Most Aelfar technical knowledge was lost in the collapse
- Surviving humans rediscovered some basics through trial and error
- Deep theory (Weave-Flux harmonics, dimensional math) largely lost
- Scholars study ruins and artifacts to reconstruct lost techniques

### 8. Post-Sundering Timeline: Early Human Civilization

**Age of Rebirth** (~1500 years before protagonist)
- Human survivors (and other species) coalesce into small communities
- Develop agriculture, trade, basic craftsmanship
- Early magic users emerge (hedge mages, shamans, scholars)
- Oral traditions form; myths develop around Aelfar and Sundering

**Age of Discovery** (~500 years before protagonist)
- Nations stabilize and expand
- Trade networks form
- Magic study becomes formalized (academies, guilds)
- Resonance crystals are harvested and used for power
- Technology and magic start to merge (hybrid systems)

**Modern Era** (protagonist's time)
- Six major nations are established with distinct magical traditions
- Academy founded as neutral research/training ground
- Magic is common among scholars and wealthy; rare among commons
- Flux Tears still exist; still causing problems; still being studied
- Ecological strain from heavy magic use (especially in industrializing areas)

---

### 9. Magic System Rules Summary

**Hard Rules** (inviolable):
1. **Conservation**: Mana converts to effect; no free lunch
2. **Resistance**: Some materials/people/places resist magic
3. **Fatigue**: Casters have personal limits; exceeding = burnout or death
4. **Consequence**: Large or careless magic causes side effects (heat, light, environmental damage)
5. **No Resurrection**: Dead cannot be brought back (violates conservation; always fails catastrophically)

**Soft Rules** (exploitable):
- Channeling mana through resonance crystals is more efficient than raw will
- Working with existing forces (wind, gravity, heat) costs less than creating new forces
- Ritual magic can be made arbitrarily efficient with preparation
- Some people have natural magical talent; others must study harder
- Cultural/regional variations in technique effectiveness

**Cultural Variations**:
- Heartland: formalized, militarized magic; used for enforcement and infrastructure
- Island Fortress: spirit-binding and personal power cultivation
- Celestial: scholarly, theoretical magic; focus on understanding Weave-Flux principles
- Sahelian: oral magic traditions; griots as living mana repositories
- Andean: shamanic, land-connected magic; plant and animal lore
- Federation: pragmatic, mercantile magic; focus on artifact production and trade
- Outlying areas: folk magic, hedge mages, folk remedies

### 10. Ecological & Political Consequences (Preview)

**Magic Pollution** (expanded in later batches):
- Heavy, industrialized magic usage in cities strains local Flux
- Creates artificial scarcity; weakens natural ecosystems
- Animals die or mutate in over-mined regions
- Leads to ecological degradation and refugee crises

**Political Tension**:
- Nations compete for access to Flux Tears and resonance crystals
- Magic users have disproportionate power vs. commons
- Class divides deepen: magic-haves vs. have-nots
- Institutions (academy, guilds) become power brokers
- Fourth Party (non-human magical entities) complicates relations

## Narrative Integration Notes

**For Story Development**:
- The Sundering is ancient history; most characters treat it as myth
- Aelfar ruins are treasure sources and sources of danger
- Magic system rules create tactical and dramatic constraints (can't resurrect, limited mana pools, etc.)
- Ecological crisis is slow-moving but accelerating (provides long-term stakes)
- Flux Tears are adventure sites and sources of anomalies

**For Protagonist**:
- He learns magic from multiple traditions (formal + practical)
- His chain weapon may have Aelfar-derived techniques
- His region's Veil thickness affects what magic is possible
- Ecological decay shapes the world he adventurs in

**For Worldbuilding Consistency**:
- If magic appears, check: Is it efficient or inefficient? What's the mana source?
- If artifact appears, consider: Is it Aelfar or modern? Functional or corrupted?
- If character uses magic, establish: Cost, skill level, cultural tradition
- If region is described, consider: Veil thickness, Flux availability, ecological state
