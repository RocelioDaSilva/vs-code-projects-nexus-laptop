# Seraphine — Character Notes

Role: Ancient antagonist — long-lived power with brittle empathy.

Arc summary:
- Act I: Distant presence; her age and reach are established as a structural fact.
- Act II: Her isolation and long-term planning create a durable but brittle authority.
- Act III: Aisen's ordinary tactics expose a seam; a hesitation at a key moment undermines her control and accelerates her fall from dominance.

Motivations:
- Preserve accumulated power and the systems that sustain her rule.

Key beats (what happens):
- Remains an institutional antagonist whose choices are strategic rather than personal.
- Hesitates in the field when confronted with memory and compassion; that hesitation has outsized consequences (tilts battle outcomes).
- After the confrontation she loses political and metaphysical leverage; her authority fragments into regional power rather than central control.

Relationships:
- Caspian: former servant; their dynamic humanizes her control-oriented world.

POV pairing suggestions:
- Use occasional Seraphine focalization to reveal how ancient power reasons; keep most scenes external to her to preserve mystery.

Voice/Notes:
- Icy, formal, ancient—avoid melodrama; show consequence through policy and delayed emotion.
