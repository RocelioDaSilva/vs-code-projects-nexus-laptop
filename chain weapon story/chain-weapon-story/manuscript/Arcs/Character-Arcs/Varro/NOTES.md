# Lucius Varro — Character Notes

Role: Prophetic/regressed figure — a voice that maps warnings to hindsight.

Arc summary:
- Act I: Eccentric seer who speaks in fragmented, time-bent fragments.
- Act II: Appears intermittently; warnings are noted but often discounted.
- Act III: His warnings land with force (Chapter 43); he observes consequences and his role is primarily to map missed opportunities.

Motivations:
- To name patterns and warn others even when ignored; he is driven less by power than by persistent seeing.

Key beats (what happens):
- Delivers enigmatic warnings that later read as prescient.
- In the climax, his sense of timeline registers the sequence of events that determine Aisen's fall (narrative causality; character-driven).
- Functions as moral-chronicle rather than active agent — his “victory” is recognition of the pattern.

Relationships:
- Aisen/Network: listeners who sometimes translate his fragments into action.

POV pairing suggestions:
- Use brief focal flips (Aisen hears Varro; Varro watches the world) to show the distance between seeing and changing.

Voice/Notes:
- Fragmented, lateral metaphors, temporally dissonant. Keep his language precise but dislocated.
