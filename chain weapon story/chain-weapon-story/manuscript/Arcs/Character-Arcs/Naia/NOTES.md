# Naia — Character Notes

Role: Border healer — living warning about the cost of exceeding limits.

Arc summary:
- Act I: Not present as a central figure.
- Act II: Demonstrates the bodily cost of heroism when she exceeds safe limits to save lives (Chapter 22).
- Act III: Serves as a cautionary exemplar and continued healer who must adapt to new limits; her experience informs the network's ethics regarding risk.

Motivations:
- Protect patients and communities even at personal cost.

Key beats (what happens):
- Pushes beyond recommended limits during mass-casualty events; loses full facility in some tasks (tremor, chronic impairment).
- Her sacrifice reframes the group's understanding of 'limit-breaks' from heroism to cost-accounting.

Relationships:
- Aisen: practical ally and moral lesson.

POV pairing suggestions:
- Pair with Aisen/Amara when showing the human consequences of sabotage and rescue operations.

Voice/Notes:
- Grounded, quietly outraged at systems that force impossible choices; speak with medical precision.
