# Caspian — Character Notes

Role: Complex antagonist/reluctant redeemer — formerly bound to Seraphine.

Arc summary:
- Act I: Appears as a phantom rescuer (Chapter 2) — a brief kindness that haunts Aisen.
- Act II: Shown in service to Seraphine; his actions are shaped by dependency, hunger, and obligation.
- Act III: The choice point — in the confrontation he reclaims agency and opens a path for Aisen's team, paying with his life.

Motivations:
- Protect a faint memory of kindness he once received.
- Avoid the cruelty that turned him into a tool.

Key beats (what happens):
- Early kindness in the blizzard establishes a private moral anchor.
- Period of internal conflict while serving Seraphine; private moments of compassion.
- Final sacrificial act during the headquarters confrontation: creates a corridor for escape, dies, and his death reframes compassion as a tactical force.

Relationships:
- Aisen: early rescuer/ghostly father figure in memory.
- Seraphine: the patron he serves; source of his compromises.

POV pairing suggestions:
- Pair with Aisen (memory/fleeting scene) and with Seraphine (service/ambivalence) to show moral friction.

Voice/Notes:
- Sparse dialogue, heavy interior regret. Make his kindness small, human, and credible.
