# Pieter van der Berg ÔÇö The Merchant's Son

**Role:** Aisen's friend, network financial manager, Federation cultural bridge  
**Tier:** Main Supporting Cast  
**First Appearance:** Ch 11 (Academy Initiation)  
**POV:** None (seen through Aisen, Amara, Vex)

---

## Demographics & Physical

- **Full Name**: Pieter Cornelis van der Berg
- **Age**: 16 at Academy entry; 21 at war's start; 26 in epilogue
- **Origin**: Mercantile Federation ÔÇö son of Alderman Hendrik van der Berg, a mid-tier guild merchant-prince
- **Build**: Stocky, broad-shouldered. Looks like he should be loading cargo, not studying magic.
- **Hair**: Reddish-brown, curly, perpetually in his eyes
- **Hands**: Ink-stained from ledgers. Calluses from abacus and rope, not weapons.
- **Clothing**: Federation-style waistcoat even at the Academy. Always overdressed by military standards.
- **Mannerism**: Counts on his fingers when estimating costs. Whistles tunelessly when nervous. Keeps a small leather-bound ledger in his breast pocket at all times.

---

## Background

- **Family**: Father is a Licensed Merchant ÔÇö allied with Covenant through monopoly licenses. Mother died during a trade route dispute (officially "bandit attack," actually Covenant economic warfare). Pieter doesn't know this at first.
- **Education**: Academy-trained in applied resonance (crystal grading, Flux-efficient logistics). Competent but not exceptional mage.
- **Motivation**: Came to Academy expecting to learn trade magic. Found friends instead. Stays for loyalty, not grades.
- **Federation Connection**: His family's trade network gives the resistance access to supply chains, crystal markets, and financial intelligence the Covenant can't easily trace.

---

## Personality

- **Core Trait**: Practical idealism ÔÇö believes in doing the right thing but always calculates the cost first
- **Strength**: Financial intelligence, logistics planning, social fluency across class boundaries
- **Flaw**: Naivety about human darkness. Trusts contracts and agreements because his world runs on them. Slow to recognize when people are negotiating in bad faith.
- **Humor**: Self-deprecating. "I'm the only person in this resistance whose most dangerous weapon is a receipt."
- **Under Pressure**: Becomes MORE organized. When others panic, Pieter starts making lists.

**Philosophy**: *"Loyalty is more valuable than profit ÔÇö when it matters. The trick is knowing when it matters. It always matters."*

## Narrative Arc

### Act I (Ch 11-13)
Academy arrival. Pieter is Aisen's first genuine friend ÔÇö not an ally, not a contact, just someone who likes him. Bonds over shared outsider status: Aisen from a frontier tavern, Pieter from merchant class (looked down on by noble students). Pieter helps Aisen navigate Academy economics (buying materials, securing workspace, bribing the right people for library access).

### Act II (Ch 14-44)
Becomes the network's unofficial quartermaster. Sources resonance crystals through family contacts. Launders intelligence costs through trade expenses. Creates a shadow accounting system that makes resistance spending look like legitimate Academy purchases.

**Key Moment (Ch 26)**: Pieter discovers his family's Licensed Merchant status comes from Covenant monopoly enforcement ÔÇö his father's wealth is built on the system Aisen fights. Confronts father via letter. Father responds: "The world is what it is. Profit within it." Pieter keeps funding the resistance with his father's money. Doesn't tell Aisen about the irony.

**Key Moment (Ch 35)**: Provides critical intelligence about Covenant supply chain vulnerabilities from his family's trade records. This information enables the Ch 30 Archive Infiltration. The merchant's son's knowledge of shipping routes is more valuable than any combat magic.

### Act III (Ch 45-85)
**The Merchant War (Ch 47)**: Pieter's family is caught in the economic warfare. Father's licenses revoked for "suspected resistance aid." Father blames Pieter. The family trade network collapses. Pieter must choose: save his family's business (by betraying the network's supply lines) or sacrifice his inheritance for the cause.

**He chooses the cause.** Converts remaining family assets into resistance logistics. Becomes Thrace's liaison to the shadow economy.

**Post-Aisen**: Pieter organizes the memorial fund. Creates a scholarship in Aisen's name at the rebuilt Academy ÔÇö funded by honest trade, not monopoly money. Becomes a Federation trade reformer: "Contracts should protect people, not own them."
