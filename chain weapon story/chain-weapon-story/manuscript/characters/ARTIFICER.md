# Artificer — Marrek Voss (working profile)

Note: name is a placeholder; rename as desired.

Core concept
- An industrious, practical maker trained in memory-metal, gears, and subtle fixtures. Marrek is an artificer of the guild style: he builds things people can trust and fixes things people break.

Background
- Apprentice in a city-forge linked to Academy contracts; prefers tinkering to theory. He was present during early node activity at an abandoned forge and salvaged a partial map fragment.
- Motivations: craft, curiosity, keep his hands occupied (and his mind away from losses). He believes in systems and makes his living by stabilizing them.

Signature Skills
- Memory‑metal smithing (state locks, toggles)
- Engineering anchors and mechanical portal housings (prototype gates, loop throws)
- Practical diagnostics: finds the one grain of sand that jams a state lock.

How he learns from allies
- From Aisen: improvised counters and how to weaponize mundane objects (dust pouches, spool tricks).
- From Kaelen: living anchors and how to integrate plant matter into cradles and seals.
- From Amara: routes and ledger-reading for supply-chain fixes; how to hide a component in a manifest.

Arc hooks
- Accidental contact with cosmic pattern: while repairing a flux‑warmed gear near a node, Marrek receives a brief, ecstatic vision of star-places. He does not understand it but finds his hands moving to carve star-motifs into a gate frame.
- If Option C is used (twist), these carvings are the same symbols later seen in the Warlock's rites.

Costs & Limits
- When working Flux into mechanisms he must pay an information cost: his hands ache; he forgets a small personal memory (a childhood recipe, a face). Repeated work without proper discharge ages him slightly and reduces his ability to hold complex informational states.

Sample Scenes
- Workshop night: Marrek shows Aisen how to reprofile a link while Aisen demonstrates how to use dust to jam a toggle. They swap small techniques.
- Discovery: Marrek notices a pattern in a broken plate that matches a star-carving he cannot explain.
