## What He Teaches Aisen

### **Telekinesis Fundamentals**
- Direct push and pull (linear force)
- Torque application (leverage through magical force)
- Multi-point control (handle multiple objects)
- Kinetic redirection (alter projectile paths)
- Force multiplication (concentrate power in small areas)
- Sustained hold and adhesion (keep objects floating)

### **Magical Philosophy**
- **Precision over power** – Low magical output, high precision control
- **Physics integration** – How magic interacts with natural laws
- **Ethical constraints** – Proper use of power; consequences of careless magic
- **Mental discipline** – Meditation, focus, understanding one's limits

### **Personal Values**
- Teaches Aisen that power without wisdom leads to ruin (from own experience)
- Emphasizes understanding before action
- Shows that failure and shame don't define a person permanently

## Relationship with Aisen
- **Dynamic**: Reluctant teacher → Trusted confidant (slow trust-building)
- **Key Element**: Aisen pays with tavern meals; cleric sees this as genuine value, not charity
- **Teaching Method**: Socratic—asks questions to help Aisen discover principles
- **Mutual Benefit**: Teaching Aisen gives cleric purpose; Aisen's youth and curiosity remind cleric why magic matters

- **Connection to Guard**: May know each other distantly; both carry shame and redemption arcs

## Philosophy

## Thematic Role
- **Represents**: Even those who've failed can teach; shame doesn't erase knowledge
- **Shows**: Magic users carry responsibility; carelessness has consequences
- **Embodies**: The cost of power; the possibility of redemption through humility

## Internal Conflict
