## IMPLEMENTED TIER 3 PROFILES (Using Template)

### 1. **Guard Sergeant "Horst"** (Institutional Observer)
**Appearances**: Ch 5-45 (8 scenes) | **Role**: Training/perspective

Brief: Senior garrison sergeant training recruits; recognizes Aisen-Harald dynamic immediately (veteran instinct). Provides recurring POV on: how military actually works, soldier morale patterns, institutional resistance feasibility.

**Function**: Ground-level perspective on institutional change. Not leader; just observer recognizing significance.

---

### 2. **Tavern Owner "Mira"** (Social Hub)
**Appearances**: Ch 1-35 (6 scenes) | **Role**: Information access/anonymity

Brief: Runs tavern where Aisen works. Observes unusual patterns: information flowing fast, people meeting quietly. Eventually recognizes network activities. Chooses to provide privacy/plausible deniability (odd requests don't get questions answered).

**Function**: Legitimate cover for network meetings; information flows through tavern naturally; shows restaurants/bars as network infrastructure.

---

### 3. **Courier "Red"** (Logistics Support)
**Appearances**: Ch 15-48 (7 scenes) | **Role**: Physical message distribution

Brief: Professional courier (trade routes); hired by Aisen initially for legitimate deliveries. Recognizes pattern and approaches: "I know what you need. I deliver without questions." Becomes primary physical communication link.

**Function**: Logistics infrastructure; shows mundane service workers matter; distributed communication system.

---

### 4. **Magistrate's Clerk "Wavin"** (Administrative Access)
**Appearances**: Ch 8-38 (6 scenes) | **Role**: Government records access

Brief: Works in magistrate's office; bored with routine. When Aisen needs records lookup (through intermediary), recognizes: "I can help without getting caught." Provides access to: property records, travel permits, administrative data.

**Function**: Institutional access through ordinary worker; shows bureaucracy contains leverage points.

---

### 5. **Militia Captain "Yern"** (Local Military)
**Appearances**: Ch 10-42 (7 scenes) | **Role**: Local garrison, military liaison

Brief: Commands local town militia (different from frontier garrison, less formal). When garrison/resistance coordinate, Yern becomes liaison (doesn't report to Ironwall but coordinates with him). Local military perspective different from professional garrison.

**Function**: Military diversity; shows military isn't monolithic; professional vs. local military have different interests.

---

### 6. **Healer's Apprentice "Pip"** (Medical Support)
**Appearances**: Ch 12-48 (8 scenes) | **Role**: Field medicine access

Brief: Young apprentice to formal healer; eager to help; accidentally overhears network coordination at healer's house. Recognizes significance; offers: "I can provide medical supplies, know the storage systems." Becomes supply chain node.

**Function**: Youth perspective on resistance; shows young people recognize change needed; medical access as network infrastructure.

## TIER 3 STORY FUNCTION (COLLECTIVELY)

Tier 3 characters show: **Everyone participates in networks at their level.** Not just leaders and specialists; ordinary people doing ordinary work that enables everything. Merchants, servants, guards, family members—all matter because networks operate through everyday infrastructure.

**Thematic resonance**: Ordinary people matter; distributed systems include everyone; resistance functions through mundane participation as much as dramatic action.
