# Tier 2: Academy Figures (Acts II-III)

## 1. Academy Master Instructor "Rigel" (Synthesis Specialist)
**Role**: Teacher, resistance ally | **Appearances**: Ch 15-45 (30+ scenes) | **Significance**: Research foundation advisor

**Background**: Senior academy instructor responsible for "practical magic" courses. Secretly has been documenting synthesis research for decades (knows it works, sees institution trying to suppress it). When Aisen arrives, recognizes immediately: "Finally. Someone asking the right questions."

**Acts II-III function**: Provides theoretical validation for synthesis approach. Coordinates with Veredith (Headmaster) to give Aisen and Kaelen research freedom under guise of "unrestricted inquiry project." Teaches them archived knowledge, historical precedent, mathematical frameworks.

**Peak significance** (Ch 25-35): Synthesis becomes official research project (with institutional backing, making it hard to suppress). Rigel's reputation becomes political asset—old enough to be respected, too established to easily remove.

**Survival**: Yes, survives war. Post-war becomes synthesis program director (institution recognized his work was right all along).

**Story impact**: Academic legitimacy for synthesis; proves Aisen isn't inventing something new, restoring something misremembered.

---

## 2. Academy Security Chief "Davos" (Information Control)
**Role**: Institution policing, undercover operative | **Appearances**: Ch 13-48 (20+ scenes) | **Significance**: Antagonist/ambiguous figure

**Background**: Head of Academy security; ostensibly polices institution rules. Actually reports to Covenant leadership. Ordered to infiltrate Aisen's network and eliminate it from inside.

**Acts II-III conflict**: Davos applies pressure: monitors Aisen's movements, questions students, threatens associate workers. Never directly engages Aisen (wrong tactical position). Instead, creates pressure on network periphery.

**Turning point** (Ch 38-42): Covenant pressures Davos to arrest Aisen directly. But Davos realizes: Covenant is obviously losing already. Supporting Covenant likely means being purged in post-war cleanup. Subtle decision: stops reporting to Covenant; becomes quiet resistance ally by not-taking-action.

**Survival**: Survives (actually survives better because his early-war shift helps resistance escape pursuing Covenant forces).

**Story impact**: Security infrastructure becomes contested; institutions contain conflict; not everyone stays loyal to power when power destabilizes.

---

## 3. Academy Healer "Sora" (Medical Support)
**Role**: Student healthcare, network supporter | **Appearances**: Ch 15-50 (25+ scenes) | **Significance**: Operations support, emotional anchor

**Background**: Academy healer (magic medicine practitioner); tends to student injuries without judgment. Quickly learns that several students have undisclosed injuries (combat training). Recognizes Elara's hidden scar; understands she's warrior. Decides discretion has value.

**Acts II-III function**: Treats network injuries without reporting them. When fighters get hurt, Sora provides healing. During war, becomes field medic (coordinates with Kaelen on magical healing, practical collaboration).

**Peak significance** (Ch 40-48): Sora becomes emotional anchor for traumatized fighters. Not through speeches but through compassionate care. Validates people while treating their wounds.

**Survival**: Yes. Post-war becomes head healer (institutional position).

**Story impact**: Care work matters; undramatic support enables everything; healing is political act in war.

---

## 4. Academy Records Keeper "Mein" (Documentation Alias)
**Role**: Institutional archive clerk, identity obscured | **Appearances**: Ch 20-40 (15+ scenes) | **Significance**: Information preservation

**Background**: Academy records keeper; maintains student files, academic records, institutional history. Also: secretly helps Amara document resistance work. Provides archive access, records-keeping training, historical precedent searches.

**Acts II-III function**: Bridges Academy archives and Amara's documentation. Helps preserve historical record while war ongoing. When records threatened by Covenant destruction, Mein secretly evacuates critical files.

**Survival**: Yes. Post-war becomes institutional historian (records keeper becomes public figure).

**Story impact**: Record-keeping as resistance; boring administrative work enables history preservation; documentation matters.

## TIER 2 STORY FUNCTION

**Academy Figures** (this group) represent: internal resistance made visible through ordinary institutional work. Teachers, security, healers, records-keepers—every institution has them. If they side with resistance, institution becomes contested space.

**Strategic significance**: Academy survival requires these people cooperating despite different motivations.

**Thematic resonance**: Ordinary people in institutions; all their work matters; bureaucracy can enable or prevent change based on individual choices.
