# Warlock — Astrael / "Star‑touched" (working profile)

Note: provisional name; subject to renaming.

Core concept
- A person bound, willingly or not, to a cosmic patron (the Goddess of the Cosmos). Their magic manifests as portals, short translocations, and an instinctive sense of spatial geometry on a cosmic scale.

Background
- May be a traveling mystic, an exile, or a quiet scholar who one night dreamed of stars and woke with a taste of starlight on their tongue. The patron's gift is partial and often expressed as involuntary portal flares.
- Motivations: questions about origin, awe for the cosmos, and a private hunger for the pattern that called to them.

Signature Powers
- Portal creation (temporary gates, short swaps), unexpected teleportation flares, astral glimpses (brief scenes of other places).
- The power is paracausal: it bends space and sometimes causality loosely; use carries heavy paradox costs.

How they learn from allies
- From artificers: how to anchor portals safely and how to craft mundane interfaces (gates framed with memory-metal) that reduce chaotic side effects.
- From plant mages: how living matter can soften a portal's signature (Kaelen's living anchors reduce entropy leakage).
- From Amara: how to hide or route portal manifests in shipping ledgers to avoid detection.

Costs & Consequences
- Paracausal debt: headaches, memory splinters, and entropy leaks manifest as cold burns or void-sheen on objects. When the patron's will acts, the warlock may lose small memories.
- The Goddess's touch is metaphorically loving: gifts of awe for beauty cost a little of the warlock's continuity.

Ambiguity option
- In Option C (twist), the Warlock's rites and the Artificer's carvings are the same; the Warlock's portal grammar matches the carvings the Artificer makes without either understanding the other's connection.

Sample Scenes
- Portal misfire: a gate opens mid-market; the Warlock collapses, and artisans nearby (Marrek) help stich the frame, learning the pattern in the process.
- Quiet confession: the Warlock explains the feeling of the goddess as both warmth and ache; the group helps them anchor the gift to reduce cost.
