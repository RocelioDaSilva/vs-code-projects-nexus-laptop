## Physical Description

- **Build**: Average; unremarkable; the kind of person who blends into any crowd
- **Hands**: Progress from kitchen-calloused to medical-precise
- **Clothing**: Evolves from kitchen uniform → field medic supplies → innkeeper's practical wear
- **Overall**: Her transformation is visible in her posture — from hunched and apologetic to upright and purposeful

## The Arc: Soup to Sanctuary

### Stage 1: The Accident (Act I background)
- Kitchen maid in palace or noble household
- Spills soup on a visiting noble — a moment of social catastrophe
- The incident pushes her out of invisible service into visible consequence
- This humiliation becomes the crack through which a larger life enters

### Stage 2: The Transformation (Act II-III)
- Displaced by conflict or institutional upheaval
- Trains as a field medic out of necessity, not calling
- Discovers aptitude for care under pressure
- Finds agency through service — not because she chose this path, but because she refused to stop when it found her

### Stage 3: The Anchor (Post-war)
- Opens an inn that becomes a refugee shelter
- Uses hospitality skills (from kitchen work) and medical knowledge (from the war)
- The inn becomes a place where displaced people find their first stable meal
- Preserves memory: keeps records, listens to stories, creates a living archive of the ordinary displaced

## Thematic Role

Lira is the story's answer to "what happens to everyone else?" While Aisen and the network fight systemic battles, Lira represents the thousands of ordinary people whose lives are overturned and who must build new purpose from wreckage. Her arc — from humiliation to competence to community — mirrors Aisen's at a smaller, more intimate scale.

She proves the story's thesis: ordinary people doing practical good is the foundation of any lasting change.

## Voice

- Early: Apologetic, quiet, afraid of attention
- Middle: Focused, practical, medical-precise
- Late: Warm, steady; the voice of someone who has found her place

### Characteristic Quote
> *"I used to be afraid of being noticed. Now I'm afraid of people being invisible."*
