## Core Concept

An industrious, practical maker trained in memory-metal, gears, and subtle fixtures. Marrek builds things people can trust and fixes things people break. He believes in systems and makes his living by stabilizing them.

## Arc Hooks

### The Star-Motif Mystery
While repairing a flux-warmed gear near a node, Marrek receives a brief, ecstatic vision of star-places. He doesn't understand it but finds his hands moving to carve star-motifs into a gate frame. If Option C (twist) is used, these carvings match symbols later seen in the Warlock's rites — connecting their powers through an unknown cosmic pattern.

### Costs & Limits
When working Flux into mechanisms, Marrek pays an **information cost**:
- His hands ache (chronic, worsening)
- He forgets small personal memories — a childhood recipe, a face, a name
- Repeated work without proper discharge ages him slightly
- Reduces his ability to hold complex informational states
- The cost is invisible to others but accumulates; by Act III he has gaps in his personal history

## Story Arc

- Marrek doesn't appear in the childhood/formation arc

### Act III: The Builder
- Provides critical infrastructure for network's war-phase operations
- Memory loss becomes visible to allies; raises questions about sustainability
- Post-war: Applies skills to reconstruction; builds systems that outlast the crisis

## Sample Scenes
