## Physical Description

- **Build**: Unremarkable; the kind of person you forget until the air around them shimmers
- **Eyes**: Normal most of the time; occasionally reflect distant starlight (patron's influence)
- **Skin**: Faint cold burns on hands and forearms from portal entropy leakage
- **Clothing**: Layers; practical but slightly off-pattern, as if assembled from different regions
- **Movement**: Occasional spatial stutters — a hand reaching slightly farther than expected, a step that covers too much ground
- **Overall**: Looks ordinary until they don't; the patron's gift is visible in moments, not constantly

## Core Concept

A person bound, willingly or not, to a cosmic patron (the Goddess of the Cosmos). Their magic manifests as portals, short translocations, and an instinctive sense of spatial geometry on a cosmic scale. The patron's gift is partial and often expressed as involuntary portal flares.

## Background

- May be a traveling mystic, an exile, or a quiet scholar who one night dreamed of stars and woke with a taste of starlight on their tongue
- The patron's gift arrived uninvited and remains partially uncontrolled
- Motivations: questions about origin; awe for the cosmos; a private hunger for the pattern that called to them
- Seeks understanding of the bond, not its removal

## Signature Powers

| Power | Description | Cost |
|-------|-------------|------|
| **Portal creation** | Temporary gates between locations; short-range swaps | Headaches, cold burns, entropy leakage |
| **Teleportation flares** | Involuntary short translocations when stressed or surprised | Uncontrolled; spatial disorientation |
| **Astral glimpses** | Brief visions of distant places or cosmic patterns | Memory splinters; temporal confusion |
| **Spatial geometry sense** | Instinctive awareness of distances, angles, positions | Passive; mild disorientation in enclosed spaces |

### Paracausal Nature
The power bends space and sometimes causality loosely. This is fundamentally different from standard telekinesis or elemental magic — it operates on rules that are not fully understood, even by the user.

## How They Learn from Allies

| Ally | What They Learn | Integration |

| **Artificers (Marrek)** | How to anchor portals safely; craft mundane interfaces (memory-metal gate frames) | Reduces chaotic side effects of portal creation |
| **Kaelen** | Living anchors; plant matter softens a portal's signature | Reduces entropy leakage; stabilizes gates |
| **Amara** | How to hide portal manifests in shipping ledgers | Avoids detection; operational security |

## Costs & Consequences

### Paracausal Debt
- **Headaches**: Chronic after heavy use; sharp and disorienting
- **Memory splinters**: Small memories lost — names, faces, tastes, routes
- **Entropy leaks**: Cold burns on skin; void-sheen on nearby objects
- **Patron override**: When the patron's will acts through them, the warlock loses autonomy briefly and forgets small personal details

### The Patron's Will
The Goddess of the Cosmos has her own agenda. Sometimes Astrael's portals open to places they didn't choose. Sometimes their hands carve star-symbols into surfaces (matching the Artificer's unexplained carvings). The patron's influence is subtle but unmistakable — and its purpose remains unclear.

## Story Arc

### Act II: The Unstable Gift
- Arrives in the network through circumstance or desperation
- Portal abilities are valuable but unreliable; causes both rescue and crisis
- Learns to stabilize powers through collaboration with Marrek and Kaelen
- Star-symbol connection with Artificer's carvings raises questions about cosmic pattern

### Act III: The Mystery Deepens
- Portals become more reliable but patron's interventions increase
- May serve as escape route, supply conduit, or battlefield mobility
- The cosmic pattern thread remains unresolved — a seed for larger mysteries
- Personal cost accumulates: by war's end, significant memory gaps

## Voice

- Speaks with slight hesitancy; sometimes mid-sentence spatial awareness interrupts thought
- Uses spatial metaphors: "The distance between intention and action is shorter than you think"
- When the patron's influence is active: voice drops, becomes rhythmic, uses words they don't recognize
- Private moments: quiet awe at what they can see; loneliness of a gift no one else shares

## Thematic Role

Astrael represents the cost of gifts that aren't chosen. Their power is extraordinary but their autonomy is compromised. They mirror Aisen's theme at a cosmic scale: what does it mean to have ability but not full control of its purpose?

## Connection to Artificer

The star-motifs Marrek carves unknowingly match the symbols in Astrael's patron-driven rites. This connection suggests a deeper cosmic pattern linking different magical disciplines — a thread that remains mysterious and potentially significant beyond the current story.
