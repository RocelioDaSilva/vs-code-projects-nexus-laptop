# Clone Master — The Transmigrated Self

## Profile
- **Epithet**: Self-Summoner / The Many-Selves
- **Archetype**: Clone Manifestation + Transmigrator (Rule Breaker tier)
- **Origin**: Transmigrator from another world (similar mechanics to *Natsubasa Baru* from Murata)
- **Power Source**: Inherent prodigy status (transmigrators bypass early-stage learning curves); belief and self-trust
- **Deployment**: Summons clones from a "goo" substrate; unsummons on command

## Core Narrative Arc: Learning to Love Yourself

This character's journey is fundamentally about **self-acceptance and trust in oneself**. In his past world, he suffered—inadequacy, failure, rejection—and carried that trauma forward. Now, with a power that requires absolute belief in himself, he begins a painful but beautiful arc:

1. **Early**: Clones feel like "other selves." He commands them, but with hesitation and doubt.
2. **Mid**: Realizes clones *mirror* his capabilities *and* his hesitations. What he sees them struggle with, *he* is struggling with.
3. **Late**: A moment where he sacrifices himself to save a clone—finally understanding that his clones *are* him, and that love means trusting all versions of himself.
4. **Apex**: Clones can now partially replace his body, fusing with him. He becomes part-clone, unlocking dematerialization.

## Clone Mechanics

### 1. **Summoning & Unsummoning**
- Clones materialize from a "goo substrate" — a primordial gel that pools near him
- Summoning is almost instantaneous; costs mana proportional to clone count and desired durability
- Unsummoning *recycles* the clone's mass back into the goo substrate (can be used strategically to create constructs mid-combat)
- **Max concurrent clones**: Starts at 3–4; grows as he learns to trust them

### 2. **Mirror Capabilities**
- Clones begin their existence mirroring the Master's abilities *exactly*
- Physical stats, mana capacity, technique knowledge — all identical
- **Plot point**: Early story, he doesn't realize this symmetry. He commands clones to "improvise" and watches them execute maneuvers he'd never considered. Over time, he realizes: *they are learning from him because they are him*

### 3. **Partial Manifestation & Dodge Tricks**
- A clone can be *partially* released (e.g., arm only, torso only) to create an opening or tank a hit
- Example: Incoming punch aimed at Master → Clone manifests arm in the path → arm stays solid, absorbs impact, allows Master a counter-window
- Cost: Partial manifestations are mana-sloppy; better to summon fully or not at all

### 4. **Combat Coordination via Shared Consciousness**
- Clones operate with near-perfect synchronization because they *are* the same consciousness spread across bodies
- No hesitation, no miscommunication — if one clone feints left, another is already repositioning to exploit the opening
- They don't need orders; they *know* what the others are doing because they *are* those others
- **Dramatic example**:
  - Clone A throws a punch that "misses"
  - Master, seeing the miss, wants to counter-attack
  - Clone B is *already there*, holding the punch, because Master saw the opening — and Clone B saw it simultaneously
  - Clone A pivots into a sweep, enabling Clone C to fling opponent toward a blade
  - No wasted movement; absolute efficiency of intent

### 5. **Goo Constructs & Body Morphing**
- Each clone can sacrifice itself mid-combat to create a goo construct (blade, shield, wall)
- Constructs are temporary; they dissolve after a few uses or when mana sustaining them runs out
- Clones can *morph* their body shape (elongate limbs, harden forearms like armor, reshape torso for different weapon grip)
- **Cost**: Sustained morphing is mana-expensive; usually better to dismiss and resummon

### 6. **Presence Masking & Clone Swap**
- **Late-story ability**: Master learns to "mask" his clones' presences, making all of them feel identical to sensory magic
- Enemies with precise sensing *might* detect clones, but cannot distinguish which is the "true" body
- Master can switch his consciousness/presence between clones, making it impossible to target the "real" one
- **Limited by**: Enemies with extreme sensory power (like Stellar Observer types) might still triangulate

## The Sacrifice & Transformation

**Catalyst Scene**:
- Master is losing a fight; one clone is about to be destroyed
- Instead of dismissing the clone to save mana, **Master sacrifices himself to shield the clone**
- The clone is shocked: "Why did you... we're the same. Your death is our death."
- Master: "Because I finally trust that you'll survive. That *I'll* survive."

**Consequence**:
- Clones discover they can **partially replace the Master's body with their own goo-flesh**
- Master wakes up: his left arm is now clone-tissue (goo-like, but functional); his heart is partially replaced
- He can now **dematerialize** — turn portions of his body into goo and reform elsewhere (costs mana; risky if miscalculated)
- His physiology is now **hybrid**: part human, part clone, part goo

### 7. **Dematerialization**
- Master (or clones) can convert their body into goo-form and re-solidify elsewhere
- Range: limited by sensory connection to the goo substrate (typically 20–50 meters)
- Cost: Moderate-to-high mana; longer distance = exponential cost
- **Risk**: If mana breaks mid-dematerialization, the reforming body might be incomplete or malformed
- Unique to Master because he's partially clone now; full humans cannot do this

## Limitations & Growth

1. **Early hesitation**: His doubt *manifests* in clone performance. Clones hesitate because *he* hesitates. This creates feedback loops where multiple semi-competent clones *feel* defeated because the Master doesn't fully trust them.

2. **Clone dependency**: Removing all clones forces Master to fight alone—and he discovers he's not actually stronger than them, just more "practiced." This realization humbles him.

3. **Goo production cap**: The substrate has a maximum "storage" of goo. If he depletes it by creating too many constructs, he must wait for biological regeneration (slow process).

4. **Consciousness fragmentation**: Commanding too many clones at once causes mental strain. If split across 8+ clones, he loses fine control and clones default to "hold position."

5. **Identity dissolution risk**: Over-relying on clones, he risks losing sense of which "version" is the original. Late-story, he struggles with the question: "If a clone dies, did I die? If I die, do the clones continue?"

## Relationship to the Party

- **Potential Ally**: Clone Master could join the ensemble (Aisen, Amara, Relâmpago, etc.)
- **Shared Learning**: His growth in self-trust mirrors Aisen's journey in trusting the group
- **Complementary Mechanics**: While Aisen chains people together (via links), Clone Master literally *is* multiple people—but needs to learn what Aisen already knows: unity requires vulnerability

## Voice & Character

- Early: Quick-talking, uses clones to avoid direct confrontation (sends clones to scout, to test opponents)
- Mid: Quieter, more introspective; after sacrifice moment, becomes more deliberate in speech
- Late: Confident but not arrogant; speaks as "we" sometimes, catching himself and correcting to "I"
- Deep fear: being alone, being singular, being incomplete

## Design Philosophy

Clone Master embodies **vulnerability as strength**. His power is strongest when he trusts himself completely—which means no walls, no filters, total self-acceptance. The journey is watching a transmigrator, burdened by a terrible past, learn to believe in the only thing that can save him: himself.

**Narrative Parallel**: If Caspian is learning to lose his humanity reluctantly (*forced* by magic), Clone Master is learning to *choose* a new form of humanity—one that includes plurality and goo-flesh—because he decided he was worth that risk.
