## Physical Description

- **Age appearance**: Deeply lined face; looks ancient and fragile but stronger than expected
- **Height**: Average; thin frame
- **Hair**: Grey-silver, shorn close
- **Eyes**: Deep brown, unusually bright; warm when engaged, distant when remembering
- **Skin**: Weathered; **burn scar from left arm extending up to neck** (from forbidden magic)
- **Build**: Thin and fragile-looking; moves with surprising steadiness
- **Movement**: Careful, deliberate; pauses mid-breath (pain management)
- **Clothing**: Simple clerical robes, worn and mended; walking staff always present
- **Overall**: A man who has paid a physical price for knowledge and carries it visibly

## Psychological Profile

### Core Drive
**Redemption and prevention.** Tarovin performed forbidden limit-breaking magic in his youth. It killed someone. He has spent his life since trying to prevent others from making the same mistake — and recognizing that the Covenant deliberately engineers temptation.

### Emotional Landscape
- **Default state**: Warm, nondescript presence; puts people at ease
- **With Aisen**: Fatherly affection mixed with terror that Aisen will repeat his mistakes
- **About the Covenant**: Recognizes them as deliberately manufacturing the conditions for limit-breaking
- **About his past**: The scar is his confession; he shows it rather than explains
- **Under pressure**: Ancient authority surfaces; voice hardens; stillness becomes commanding

### Key Beliefs
- "Power without understanding is tyranny; understanding without compassion is cruelty"
- Precision and constraint are virtues, not weakness
- Magic has real costs — physical, spiritual, personal
- The Covenant exploits the ambitious; only understanding protects against temptation

### Hidden Shame
Performed forbidden limit-breaking magic in his youth. The magical accident killed someone — possibly a fellow student, possibly an innocent. He abandoned his religious order afterward. Uses alcohol to manage grief and physical pain from the permanent damage.

## Background

### Former Life
- Scholar-cleric in a religious order focused on magical theory
- Brilliant student; pushed boundaries; performed forbidden limit-breaking
- The accident killed someone and permanently scarred him
- Abandoned his order; became itinerant; trades lessons for food and drink

### Wandering Years
- Travels between settlements teaching basic telekinesis
- Barters magic instruction for meals and shelter
- Uses alcohol to cope with grief and chronic pain
- Finds Aisen at The Crossing tavern; recognizes raw potential

## Telekinesis Framework

### 5-Level Progression System

| Level | Ability | Training Method | Cost |
|-------|---------|----------------|------|
| **1: Push/Pull** | Basic force application | Candle Nudge, Feather Flick | Minimal; fatigue |
| **2: Precision** | Fine control, multiple objects | Rotational Drill, Multi-Tether | Moderate; concentration headaches |
| **3: Sustained** | Extended duration, complex patterns | Extended holds, pattern drills | High; nosebleeds, exhaustion |
| **4: Combat** | Force applied under stress | Reactive drills, moving targets | Very high; physical strain |
| **5: Limit Break** | Beyond normal capacity | **FORBIDDEN** | **PERMANENT**: nerve damage, tremor, reduced capacity, memory loss |

### Teaching Drills (Assigned to Aisen)
1. **Candle Nudge**: Move a flame without extinguishing it
2. **Feather Flick**: Redirect a falling feather to a precise landing
3. **Rotational Drill**: Spin a small object at constant speed
4. **Multi-Tether Warmup**: Hold multiple objects simultaneously

### Core Principle
Low-power, high-precision. Tarovin teaches Aisen to do more with less — the same philosophy Harald applies to physical combat. This convergence creates Aisen's unique synthesis.

## Story Arc

### Act I: The Mysterious Itinerant (Age 55-65, Ch 6-12)
- **Arrival**: Appears at The Crossing tavern; trades telekinesis lessons for food and drink
- **The Candle Scene** (Ch 6, Aisen age 10-11): First formal magic lesson; demonstrates precision philosophy
- **Training deepens** (Ch 8-9): Reveals telekinesis framework; assigns progressive drills
- **Warning with scar reveal**: Shows Aisen the burn scar; explains the cost of limit-breaking without naming specifics
- **Departure**: Leaves The Crossing; writes recommendation alongside Harald's for Academy

### Act II: The Academy Anchor (Age 65-73, Ch 13-38)
- Accepts position as Academy Magical Coordinator
- Monitors for Covenant infiltration from within institutional structure
- Watches Aisen's development with mixture of pride and fear
- **Academy confrontation/farewell** (Ch 33): Warns Aisen directly about what he's discovered
- Growing tension between institutional role and knowledge of corruption

### Act III: The Sacrificial Witness (Age 73-75, Ch 33-40+)
- Confronts evidence of Covenant's systematic program to encourage limit-breaking in students
- **Final sacrifice** (Chapter ~41-43): Confronts Covenant's systematic program; his death is a witness-act
- His death is a witness-act — proving what the Covenant does, at the cost of his life
- Legacy: Aisen's precision philosophy carries Tarovin's teaching forward

## Voice & Mannerisms

### Speech Patterns
- Ancient authority when speaking on magic; measured, precise
- Warmer and more relaxed with Aisen; almost gentle
- When discussing forbidden magic: voice hardens, gains an edge
- Socratic like Harald, but with more direct warnings

### Physical Mannerisms
- Walking staff always present; uses it for emphasis
- Left arm tremor (permanent nerve damage from limit-breaking)
- Still, gathered posture; conserves physical energy
- Pauses mid-breath (pain management; chronic condition)

### Characteristic Quote
> *"I spent my youth chasing power and paid heavily... If my death now prevents others from paying what I paid, then my youth's mistake finally means something."*
