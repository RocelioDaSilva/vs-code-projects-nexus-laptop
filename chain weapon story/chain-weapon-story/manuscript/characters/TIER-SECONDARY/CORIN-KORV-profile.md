## Physical Description

- **Build**: Working-man's frame; not a fighter despite his father's martial background
- **Hands**: Large, weathered, scarred — the most frequently referenced physical detail; hands that have poured ten thousand drinks
- **Demeanor**: Quiet, watchful; moves behind the bar with habitual economy
- **Health decline**: Develops a persistent cough in later years from smoke and damp; worsens slowly

## Psychological Profile

### Core Drive
**Steady provision** — Corin's singular mission is to keep the tavern running, the family fed, and the world at bay. He inherited his father's pragmatism without his father's martial instinct.

### Internal Conflict
- Inherited a soldier's tavern but was never a soldier — he was a brewer, a host, a reader of people
- Financial pressure creates a slow erosion of dignity; one bad season could mean debt
- Hidden shame around financial mismanagement that nearly costs the family everything
- Too proud to show vulnerability; absorbs pressure silently

### Worldview
> *"A tavern is not a home. It's a stage. People come here to be someone else for a night. You don't judge them; you pour them another drink."*

This philosophy directly shapes Aisen's understanding that people are not always what they show — the foundation of his later intelligence work.

### Philosophy
> *"We protect what we love. Everything else is distraction."*

## Background

### Family History
- **Father**: Kaelen the Limper — veteran soldier from the Third Border War; founded the tavern on a land grant; died of old wounds
- **Wife**: Elara — daughter of a Sahelian trader; herbalist, bookkeeper, kitchen manager, informal healer
- **Son**: Aisen (the protagonist)
- **Daughter**: Mira — six years younger than Aisen; cheerful, socially adept

### Tavern Legacy
- Inherited The Wandering Stag at age 22 after Kaelen died
- Expanded: added a second sleeping room, a proper bar, and a stable
- Struck a deal with a Heartland merchant guild for waystation rights, guaranteeing regular traffic
- Stable but not wealthy — supported the family plus two part-time workers
- Owned the building and land (no rent), but taxes, guild fees, and occasional bribes ate into margins

### What He Taught Aisen
- To listen more than he spoke
- To pour another drink rather than confront a lie
- Basic spear and sword techniques (parental responsibility, not mastery)
- The "stage" philosophy: seeing through social performance
- That stability requires constant quiet effort

## Key Story Events

### Financial Crisis (Act I)
- Taxes unpaid; creditors aggressive; business faltering
- A mysterious wealthy patron arrives and saves the tavern from collapse
- Corin's reaction: mixture of gratitude and humiliation
- Creates tension in the family and seeds of obligation/mystery

### When Aisen Leaves for Academy
- Hires a stable boy (2 coppers/day); buys more butcher meat; profit margin shrinks to near zero
- Becomes quieter, working longer hours
- Family takes on debt for the first time — tuition loan secured against the tavern

### Death (~Age 55)
- Develops a worsening cough over years
- Dies while Aisen is away — approximately five years before Aisen's own death
- Aisen rushes home but arrives a day too late
- Aftermath: Elara retires to room above tavern; Mira takes over operations fully
- **Keepsake**: Aisen carries Corin's pocket knife for the rest of his life

## Character Arc

### Act I: The Quiet Foundation (Aisen ages 5-15)
- Present as steady background figure — keeps the tavern running
- Financial crisis creates family tension; Corin absorbs it silently
- Supports Aisen's departure for Academy despite the cost

### Acts II-III: The Absent Father
- Appears in Aisen's memories and motivations, not in person
- His death (off-page) becomes a turning point for Aisen's emotional arc
- Shifts Aisen from survival-mindset to protection-mindset
- Aisen's guilt at arriving too late shapes his urgency about the people he can still save

## Thematic Role

Corin embodies **the cost of ordinary life** — the quiet heroism of keeping a family fed in a world that doesn't care. His death while Aisen is away pursuing larger goals is the story's argument that macro-heroism always has micro-costs. The people who stay behind pay for the adventures of those who leave.

## Voice & Mannerisms

- Speaks economically; says more with silence than words
- Hands always working — wiping the bar, adjusting glasses, repairing something
- When angry, becomes quieter rather than louder
- Uses tavern metaphors for life advice

### Characteristic Quote
> *"You don't judge them; you pour them another drink."*
