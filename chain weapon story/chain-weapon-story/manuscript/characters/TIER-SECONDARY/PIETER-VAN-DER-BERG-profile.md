# Pieter van der Berg

## Core Identity
**Title:** Provincial Governor, Trade Chancellor  
**Age:** 54  
**Nation of Origin:** Mercantile Federation  
**Covenant Role:** Regional Economic Controller

## Appearance
Pieter is a round man made rounder by prosperity—the kind of fat that comes from good food and good wine rather than the appearance of actual softness. He has a merchant's face: angular enough to suggest shrewdness, warm enough to suggest he's not the kind of ruthless that ends badly. He dresses in expensive fabric that he wears like it's nothing special, which is exactly how a wealthy man dresses. His hands are never still—always moving, gesturing, conducting the world like a symphony.

## Core Conflict: Profit vs. Principle
Pieter made his fortune through trade—legitimate trade, mostly. He understood supply chains, market forces, opportunity costs. The Covenant approached him initially as a potential economic asset, then as someone to control through economic leverage. His real turning point comes when he realizes the Covenant's magical infrastructure is fundamentally destabilizing trade networks.

The decision to ally with the Crown (and through the Crown, with Aisen's evidence distribution) is not moral. It's economic. The Covenant's institutional corruption is bad for business. That's both the beauty and the tragedy of Pieter's character—he can be trusted to support the right thing, but only when the right thing is also the profitable thing.

## Strengths
- **Economic Acumen:** Understands market forces, supply chain vulnerabilities, and financial leverage
- **Negotiation:** Can find compromise positions that all parties believe they've won
- **Network:** Connected to merchants, nobles, and minor nobility throughout the realm
- **Pragmatism:** Willing to make hard choices if the calculation is clear

## Weaknesses
- **Shallow Commitment:** His loyalty extends only as far as his profit margin
- **Visibility:** Everything he does is visible to his business rivals and enemies
- **Luxury Dependence:** Has trouble functioning in environments without basic comforts
- **Short-Term Thinking:** Optimizes for next quarter rather than next decade

## Story Arc
Pieter represents the merchant class response to institutional collapse. His role in Act III involves mobilizing economic resources to support the transitional Crown and the alternative institutions. But his arc is about the discovery that some things have costs that can't be calculated in profit margins—and what happens when a pragmatist finally encounters something that doesn't have a market price.

## Narrative Function
- **Economic Realist:** Provides practical constraints on what institutions can accomplish with limited resources
- **Merchant Perspective:** Represents the non-magical middle class caught between institutional powers
- **Motivation Transparency:** His profit motive makes it clear why he's supporting the opposition—no romantic idealism

## Physical Description
Prosperous appearance, fine clothing, practiced charm. Moves with the confidence of someone accustomed to negotiation and influence.

## Key Attributes
- **Strength:** Controls information flow through trade networks. Understands economic pressure points and resource bottlenecks.
- **Limitation:** Financially motivated; loyalty follows profit. Can be bought or turned by better offers.
- **Core Conflict:** Wants to survive the war as a wealthy man but recognizes the system is collapsing.

## First Appearance
Chapter 31: "Deployment Begins" (arranging supply logistics)

## Key Scenes
- Negotiating supply lines with Aisen's network
- Playing multiple sides simultaneously during the Trade Council arc
- Making the pragmatic choice to protect assets rather than allies

## Character Arc
- **Beginning:** Pure capitalist; sees the war as business opportunity
- **Midpoint:** Realizes profit won't survive systemic collapse
- **Climax:** Must choose between maximizing profit or ensuring any legitimate commerce survives
- **Resolution:** Becomes architect of post-war economic frameworks

## Voice Example
"Money is language. War is a conversation where only economics talks clearly. Everyone else is lying about their intentions."

## Relationships
- **Thrace:** Allied merchant, though competing interests create tension
- **Aisen:** Transactional relationship; neither trusts the other fully
- **Mercantile Federation:** Conflicted loyalty; he questions whether the Federation will survive
