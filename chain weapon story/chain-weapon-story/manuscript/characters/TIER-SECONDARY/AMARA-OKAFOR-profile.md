## Physical Description

- **Age appearance**: Looks older than chronological age due to operational stress
- **Height**: Taller than average; athletic from caravan work
- **Hair**: Braided with small bells that chime when she moves
- **Eyes**: Dark, warm; observant; can shift from analytical to compassionate instantly
- **Skin**: Deep brown; ink-stained fingers from constant record-keeping
- **Build**: Athletic, lean from travel; moves quietly despite her height
- **Clothing**: Indigo cloth wrapped in Sahelian tradition; brass cuffs on forearms; practical layers
- **Carries**: Wooden giraffe carving (family totem); encoded journal; multiple writing instruments
- **Overall**: Commands attention through presence, not volume; looks like someone who knows more than she says

### Distinctive Sensory Details
- Bells in braids chime softly when she turns her head
- Indigo cloth rustles with deliberate movements
- Warm skin radiates calm in close proximity
- Faint scent of trade spices (cinnamon, cardamom) from caravan routes

## Psychological Profile

### Core Drive
**Preservation through connection.** "Stories are survival mechanisms." Amara believes information networks are the only defense ordinary people have against institutional power.

### Emotional Landscape
- **Default state**: Observant, warm, strategically friendly; puts people at ease to gather intelligence
- **With Aisen**: Profound respect and unspoken romantic affection — acknowledged internally but never acted upon
- **About the Covenant**: Cold analytical fury; sees them as information destroyers
- **About failure**: Carries guilt for not preventing Aisen's death; drives her post-war work
- **Under pressure**: Becomes more direct, not less warm; her emotional intelligence is her weapon

### Key Beliefs
- Stories keep people alive; erasure is the real weapon of power
- Networks beat hierarchies: "The Covenant thinks in hierarchies. We'll organize like a forest—connected but distributed."
- Intelligence is care: knowing someone's situation is the first step to helping them
- Personal stakes compromise operations (why she suppresses romantic feelings)

### The Suppressed Romance
Amara carries romantic feelings for Aisen that briefly surface in Act II but are deliberately suppressed for operational security. "We can't afford personal stakes. People in love make mistakes." This restraint makes her failure to warn him of his death even more devastating — she had the information but couldn't reach him in time.

## Background

### Sahelian Heritage
- Born into a family of griots (oral historians) who also operated as merchant-spies
- Trained in oral tradition encoding: hiding intelligence in songs, stories, and trade records
- Family trade routes double as information corridors
- Cultural philosophy: knowledge belongs to the community, not institutions

### Intelligence Training
- Learned codes and cipher systems from family tradition
- Mastered caravan logistics (cover for network operations)
- Built first informant network by age 16 using merchant contacts
- Recruits through trust and mutual need, not coercion

## Intelligence Network

### Network Architecture
| Layer | Composition | Function |
|-------|-------------|----------|

| **Refugee networks** | Displaced people, camp workers | Ground-truth intelligence |

- Trade manifests with hidden messages in inventory lists
- Bell-pattern signals (using braids as communication tool)
- Dead drops at caravan waypoints

## Story Arc

### Act I: The Merchant Guard (Age 12-15, Ch 12)
- First appears as a caravan guard on the road near The Crossing
- Meets Aisen at age 14; recognizes his unusual awareness and network potential
- Plants the seed of organized information-sharing as resistance
- Departs with the caravan but maintains contact through message network

### Act II: The Network Builder (Age 15-20, Ch 24-38)
- Builds a continent-spanning intelligence network using merchant routes
- Positions informants inside the Academy, military, and refugee camps
- Coordinates with Rin and Aisen's growing resistance cell
- **Intelligence explosion** (Ch 33-38): Network uncovers Covenant's systematic programs
- Romantic feelings surface briefly; deliberately suppressed for operational security

### Act III: The War Coordinator (Age 20-25, Ch 39-52)
- Coordinates simultaneous evidence distribution across multiple cities
- **Fails to warn Aisen** of his death — has the information but can't reach him in time
- Witnesses the aftermath; takes possession of Aisen's journal and field notes
- Takes over as de-facto network leader after Aisen's death
- **Post-war** (Ch 48-52): Works with local governments on accountability tribunals; travels recording refugee stories; trains younger griots; becomes the living archive of what happened

## Voice & Mannerisms

### Speech Patterns
- Direct; minimal small talk; every word carries information
- Dry humor about danger: "Another day, another attempted assassination. At least the food was good."
- Strategic questions rather than statements
- When emotional: shifts to Sahelian proverbs and griot cadence

### Physical Mannerisms
- Bells chime when she moves her head (unconscious tell when thinking hard)
- Ink-stained fingers tap surfaces rhythmically (encoding habit)
- Leans forward when listening; creates intimacy
- Touches wooden giraffe carving when making difficult decisions

### Characteristic Quote
> *"I keep stories because stories keep us... His story continues through everyone he touched."*
