## Physical Description

- **Age appearance**: Lean and weathered; looks every year of his age
- **Height**: Average to tall; carries himself with quiet authority
- **Hair**: Silver-grey, cropped short
- **Eyes**: Deep blue; observant, slow to judge
- **Skin**: Weathered, scarred hands from decades of weapon work
- **Build**: Lean, not barrel-chested; deceptively strong
- **Movement**: Economical; left leg wound causes slight favor but no dramatic limp
- **Clothing**: Simple guard-issue; practical; nothing decorative
- **Overall**: Looks like a man who has been tired for a long time but keeps standing

## Psychological Profile

### Core Drive
**Redemption through teaching.** Harald carries guilt from an unresolved past failure — a wife, child, or comrade he couldn't save. He channels that guilt into ensuring Aisen doesn't repeat his mistakes.

### Emotional Landscape
- **Default state**: Quiet, watchful, economical with words
- **With Aisen**: Warm but restrained; shows love through attention and patience
- **About power**: Cynical about institutions; hopeful about individuals
- **About the past**: Refuses to discuss specifics; manifests as silences and deflections
- **Under pressure**: Becomes more still, not more agitated

### Key Beliefs
- "Teaching is passing on questions, not answers"
- Strength is understanding, not force
- Ordinary people thinking deeply changes the world
- Power corrupts; individuals can resist

## Background

### Military Career
- 25+ years as Valdimere border guard
- Dishonorably discharged for refusing an unjust order (possibly Covenant-related)
- Self-taught philosopher; reads widely despite humble station
- Drifted to The Crossing after discharge; took up residence near the tavern

### The Unknown Failure
Harald carries grief from someone he lost — the specifics are never fully revealed. This shapes everything: his patience with Aisen, his insistence on questions over answers, his refusal to discuss his own past. The person he lost is a ghost that drives his teaching.

## Teaching Method

| Domain | Approach | Key Technique |
|--------|----------|---------------|
| **Spear** | Practice + questioning | "Why did that strike fail? What did your stance tell me?" |
| **Literacy** | Read together, argue meaning | Shared texts become shared thinking tools |
| **Philosophy** | Socratic dilemmas | Poses impossible questions; waits for Aisen to struggle |
| **Self-knowledge** | Reflection | Teaches Aisen to notice his own reactions and assumptions |

### Combat Teaching Principles
- Emphasizes **leverage over strength**; reading opponents by stance and breathing
- Core drills: sliding thrust, pivot step, retreating thrusts, planted-anchor drills
- Physics-minded approach: force vectors, body mechanics, positional advantage
- These principles become the foundation of Aisen's chain-spear synthesis

### 4 Key Teaching Moments
1. **Post-robbery trauma processing** (Ch 3-4): Helps young Aisen process first exposure to violence
2. **Spear training principles** (Ch 4-7): "Your body is a lever. The spear extends the lever. Understanding is the fulcrum."
3. **Literacy foundation** (Ch 4-12): Reading and arguing texts together; makes Aisen a thinker, not just a fighter
4. **Moral compass development** (throughout): Questions > answers; never tells Aisen what to believe

## Story Arc

### Act I: The Mentor (Age 40-50, Ch 3-12)
- **Recognition**: Sees raw potential in young Aisen during the robbery; chooses to train him
- **Training years**: Teaches combat, literacy, philosophy through daily practice
- **Method**: Socratic — refuses to give answers, insists Aisen find his own
- **Letter**: Writes Academy recommendation that opens Aisen's institutional path

### Act II: The Distant Teacher (Age 50-60)
- Steps back when Aisen enters the Academy — knows he can't protect him there
- Communicates through occasional letters; voice of conscience from afar
- May have underground connections to resistance figures

### Act III: Legacy (Age 55-60)
- Dies of old age before the war begins
- His methods and philosophy echo in Aisen's teaching of others
- The chain-spear synthesis is, at its root, Harald's physics-of-leverage applied to magic

## Voice & Mannerisms

### Speech Patterns
- Speaks slowly, deliberately; pauses for effect
- Questions far outnumber statements
- Dry, self-deprecating humor
- Never raises voice; authority comes from stillness

### Physical Mannerisms
- Left leg favors slightly
- Hands demonstrate techniques while talking
- Pauses mid-sentence to let Aisen think before continuing
- Touches Aisen's shoulder briefly as approval

### Characteristic Quote
> *"I've wasted a lot of my life... he'll still carry these questions forward. That's not redemption. But it's enough."*

## Legacy

What Harald leaves behind isn't technique — it's method:
- Socratic questioning as a way of life
- The spear as a principle (leverage, not force)
- Literacy as a gateway to independent thought
- The belief that ordinary people thinking deeply can change things
- A recommendation letter that sends Aisen into everything that follows
