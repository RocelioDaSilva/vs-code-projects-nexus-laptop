## Physical Description

- **Age appearance**: Looks her age; honest face, weathered by work
- **Height**: Average; compact and sturdy
- **Hair**: Practical cut; tied back for kitchen work
- **Eyes**: Same as Aisen's; observant but warmer, more openly emotional
- **Skin**: Ruddy; hands calloused from tavern work (dishes, barrels, cooking)
- **Build**: Stocky, strong arms from years of physical labor
- **Movement**: Efficient; moves through the tavern like she was born there (she was)
- **Clothing**: Work apron, simple dress; nothing decorative except the chain link
- **Carries**: A single chain link on a leather cord around her neck (Aisen's design; given before he left)
- **Overall**: The kind of person who holds everything together so others can leave

## Psychological Profile

### Core Drive
**Holding ground.** While others leave to fight, learn, or change the world, Mira stays. She keeps the tavern open, the community fed, and her father's legacy alive. Her strength is in endurance, not adventure.

### Emotional Landscape
- **Default state**: Practical, warm with regulars, busy with tasks
- **About Aisen**: Complex love-resentment; proud of him but angry he left her alone with responsibilities
- **About her father**: Deep grief masked by continuing his routines; she runs the tavern exactly as he did
- **About the war**: Terrified but pragmatic; tavern becomes a waystation for refugees
- **After Aisen's death**: Raw grief channeled into memorial; transforms resentment into fierce protectiveness of his story

### Key Beliefs
- Someone has to keep the fire burning while others go out into the cold
- Family means showing up, not writing letters
- The ordinary world matters as much as the heroic one
- A tavern is where people come to be human; that's worth protecting

### The Resentment
Mira never asked to be the responsible one. When their father died while Aisen was at the Academy, she handled everything alone — the burial, the tavern, the community. She loves her brother but carries an anger she can't fully articulate: he got to leave and become extraordinary while she stayed behind and became necessary.

## Background

### Childhood
- Grew up in The Crossing tavern alongside Aisen
- Older by ~5 years; took on caretaker role early
- Watched Aisen receive training from Harald and Tarovin while she managed the kitchen
- Saw her brother become special; knew she was useful

### The Tavern Years
- Took over tavern management as father aged
- Handled the full burden after father's death (while Aisen was away)
- Became the community's anchor during increasing border tensions
- Tavern served as informal refugee waystation during the war

## Story Arc

### Act I: The Older Sister (Age 10-19)
- Background presence in childhood chapters; helps run tavern
- Watches Aisen train while she works; quietly competent
- Gives Aisen the chain link necklace; receives promises he'll return
- Stays behind when Aisen leaves for the Academy

### Act II: The Keeper (Age 19-24)
- Manages tavern alone after father's death
- Handles practical grief while Aisen is unreachable
- Resentment builds through silence and unanswered letters
- Tavern becomes refugee waystation during escalating conflict

### Act III: The Memorial Carrier (Age 24-30+)
- Learns of Aisen's death through network contacts
- Grief transforms resentment into fierce protectiveness of his memory
- Places memorial plaque at the tavern: the first physical monument to him
- The Crossing becomes a pilgrimage site; she manages the visitors with practical grace
- Wears the chain link every day; keeps his story alive for everyone who asks
- Represents the ordinary cost of extraordinary lives

## Voice & Mannerisms

### Speech Patterns
- Practical, direct; doesn't waste words
- When talking about Aisen: voice catches before steadying
- Uses their father's phrases unconsciously
- Small, sharp humor — the kind that comes from years of tavern banter

### Physical Mannerisms
- Touches the chain link necklace when thinking about Aisen
- Dries her hands on her apron before important conversations
- Moves through the tavern without looking — knows every step by muscle memory
- Smiles with the gap between her teeth when genuinely amused

### Characteristic Quote
> *"He saved the world. I kept the fire burning so he'd have somewhere to come home to. He just... never came home."*

## Thematic Role

Mira represents the cost borne by those who stay behind. Every hero has someone who held the line at home, managed the ordinary, buried the dead, and kept the door open. She is the story's reminder that heroism requires a foundation, and that foundation is built by people like her.
