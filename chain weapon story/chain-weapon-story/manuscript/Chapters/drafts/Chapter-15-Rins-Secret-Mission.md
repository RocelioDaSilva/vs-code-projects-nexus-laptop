Rin arrived with a cover story and a hidden ledger. To everyone else she was a promising recruit; to those who knew, she was already a field agent. Her white-streaked hair marked her as someone who'd paid early and publicly for a private rite. 

Her mission: map the academy's ties to external sponsors, look for recruitment patterns and men who came to the halls under soft names but carried sharp intentions. She moved like attack weather—unnoticed until rain. 

In the library she met Kaelen, who smelled of earth and asked too many questions for a child. She found Amara in the mess hall, trading stories for a careful assessment. Rin planted seeds of small confidences and waited for responses, cataloguing who blinked at which phrase. 

Her report that night was sparse: several professors with suspicious patronage, a recruiter who lingered on students from border towns, and a promise that she would continue to watch. She was patient; patience was her form of resistance.
