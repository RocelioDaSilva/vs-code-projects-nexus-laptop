The experiment was framed as demonstration—how far could a student push their weave before the faculty stepped in? It began with bravado and applause and ended with a scream like breaking glass. 

The student's hands convulsed; the air around them shimmered with terrible imbalance. In the aftermath, a tremor lingered in their limbs. They were marked now: a whisper of damage that no tutor could easily erase. The faculty moved with a mix of protocol and secrecy—cleaning up, reporting for records, and shifting blame into language that would make sense to donors. 

Aisen watched from the gallery, a small notepad hidden, and saw the real lesson: the academy traded bodies for spectacle. The young student's tremor became the node of understanding Aisen required—power extracted has a price, and the institution that extracts it calls it necessary.
