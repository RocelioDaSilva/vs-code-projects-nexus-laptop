Tarovin had always taught in small doses—lessons hidden as parables. When the purge reached into the library, he made a choice: distract guards with a staged contraband scandal while copies of critical documents slipped out hidden in mundane deliveries. 

He paid in a measured way. Captured and bound, he faced interrogation with the calm of a man who knew the value of time. In the moments before his final silence he pressed a small bundle into Aisen's hands: a trove of unedited ritual records and a map of Covenant nodes. 

Tarovin's death was not theatrical. It was the quiet conclusion of a lifetime of small resistances. He left Aisen with tools and with a model of how sacrifice could be effective—not only in martyrdom but in the preservation and distribution of knowledge. 

Aisen's grief crystallized into resolve; Tarovin's lesson became a blueprint for the next phase.
