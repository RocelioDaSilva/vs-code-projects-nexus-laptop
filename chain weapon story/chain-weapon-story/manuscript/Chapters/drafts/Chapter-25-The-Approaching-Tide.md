Several threads converged: Lysandra arrived under scholarly cover but carried resistance coordinates; Varro murmured riddles from the margins; Caspian received orders he hated; Corvin tightened lines at the border. Each moved like weather toward a storm.

Aisen and allies consolidated small victories—safe houses, document drops, sympathetic soldiers—but the scale of the Covenant's machine meant their actions were a single pebble in a wide river. The chapter shows preparation: maps, false manifests, and a list of people to warn. 

They resolved to act within their scale: save what they could, leak what they must, and be ready for blowback. The tide approached; they would either learn to ride it or be swept under.
