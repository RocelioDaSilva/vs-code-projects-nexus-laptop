Master Tholen's archive smelled of dust and patient ink. He moved among stacks like someone who kept secrets in folded corners. Aisen came with a crude question and left with a thread pulled—references to events erased, footnotes removed, a map of how history had been edited. 

Tholen did not shout. He slid a small folio across the table: copies of pre-Covenant records, marginalia that named early treaties, and a note that suggested the Covenant's narrative had been curated. "People who own the past own the present," Tholen said in a voice like parchment. 

Aisen felt the book's weight in his hands and understood the scale of control. Information was not neutral; it was a lever. Tholen offered a cautious alliance: he would continue to supply fragments at risk to his position. The library had become a quiet resistance hub.
