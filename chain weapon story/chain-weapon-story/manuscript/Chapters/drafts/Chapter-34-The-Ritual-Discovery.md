Lin's job was simple: move boxes, staple lists, and sometimes return books to shelves. He found a thin ledger tucked beneath a false bottom: ritual notes describing forced limit-break ceremonies and their intended outcomes—binding loyalty through sacrifice. 

He took the pages to Tholen in secret. The librarian's face hardened as he read; the record confirmed their fears: the Covenant had built rituals that required human cost to harness certain powers. 

The discovery turned academic curiosity into moral outrage. For Aisen's group, it meant the enemy's control wasn't just political but metaphysical—a structural use of suffering as fuel. They now had documents that, if circulated, could erode public support. But circulation carried risk. 

They planned with care, each step weighed by the potential for retaliation.
