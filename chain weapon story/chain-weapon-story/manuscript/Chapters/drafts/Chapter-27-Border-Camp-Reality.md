The camp was a city of ration lines and hushed grief. Refugees pressed in with stories that read like cataloged loss—homes gone, wells poisoned, children missing. Aisen moved through the rows carrying messages and supplies, learning the human equations that fed systems of control. 

Naia worked two tents away, each wound she dressed another tally against the day's cruelty. She moved with weary competence, sometimes faltering and then steadying. 

Aisen watched the bartering of favors and the tiny economies that formed in the shadows of formal orders. He saw officers take advantage of chaos and heard whispered plans of escape. The reality of the border was not heroism but endurance, and endurance revealed both the Covenant's leverage and the spaces where resistance could breathe.
