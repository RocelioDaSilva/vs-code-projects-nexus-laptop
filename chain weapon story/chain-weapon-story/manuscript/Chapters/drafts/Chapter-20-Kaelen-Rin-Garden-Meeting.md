Kaelen found Rin in the academy garden, sleeves dirt-smudged and eyes alert. She had come with questions disguised as curiosity and left with a friendship that smelled of soil and secrecy. 

They traded stories in whispers: Kaelen spoke of roots and patient tending; Rin described shadows and coded recruitment tactics. Where the academy insisted on categorization, their exchange felt like improvisation—two different logics finding overlap. 

They agreed to small experiments: Kaelen would hide seeds in public spaces to see who noted them; Rin would watch which handlers commented on careless students. Their methods were complementary: Kaelen's durability softened Rin's edge, and Rin's strategic mind taught Kaelen to read human patterns as he read seasons.

A quiet trust formed. It would become part of the network that later moved information like veins moved sap through trees.
