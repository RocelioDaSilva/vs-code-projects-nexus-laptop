Varro's presence in the chaos was like a cracked clock striking time out of sync. He spoke of futures like weather patterns, of choices as knots that pulled ripples through years. His language was tangled with grief and clarity. 

When the coin flipped the outcome set: Aisen's fall. Varro's face registered a private confirmation—his worst timeline had snapped into place. The truth had been spoken, but not in a way that could be changed now. 

In his last rational moments, Varro muttered the mechanism of collapse: single decisions amplified, a series of small sacrifices compounding into inevitability. For those who survived, his warnings became a map of missed opportunities. For Varro, the victory was only in the knowledge that somehow, the timeline had been seen and recorded.
