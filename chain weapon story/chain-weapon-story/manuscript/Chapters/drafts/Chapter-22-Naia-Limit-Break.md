Naia had been a healer at the border for years, a woman who measured resilience by small successes: a fever broken, a child fed enough to sleep. During a brutal campaign she pushed beyond recommended limits to save dozens trapped under bombardment. The cost was immediate—her hands trembled, and she later found tasks she once did reflexively required long thought. 

The chapter shows the aftermath: a healer who cannot fully recover her previous skill, a body paying for the attempt to exceed human limits. It becomes a cautionary episode for Aisen and his friends: there are actions that change you irrevocably. 

In the evening, Aisen visited Naia and left with a clearer understanding of what "breaking" meant: not heroism but damage, a trade-off the Covenant used casually.
