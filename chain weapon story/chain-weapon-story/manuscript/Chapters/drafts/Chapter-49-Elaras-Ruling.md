If power had once tempted Elara to flee responsibility, her experience turned her toward governance. She assumed a local leadership role, not as monarch but as steward—implementing protections and strategies Aisen valued: networks for ordinary people, mutual aid, and legal protections where possible. 

Her rule is pragmatic and humble. She resists grand gestures in favor of small policy changes that limit abuses. She keeps Aisen's methods at the center of training for rescue squads, and ensures that the costs of resistance are shared. 

Her scene is about quiet competence and distributed authority. She honors the past by making structures that prevent the same tragedies.
