News travels slower in the margins but it travels. In a refugee encampment a woman named Emma hears that the quiet boy who taught strangers to survive has fallen. The reaction is not grand mourning but the settling of a fact into people's daily calculus—someone paid a price so they could live. 

Conversations shift from immediate needs to meaning: what does one life change? People amplified his practical acts—how to form a line, how to use a rope, how to hide the wounded. Aisen's death becomes a teaching moment, a manual in small acts.
