Corvin met Aisen in the field, two forces that had never understood one another now in direct conflict. The battle was brute and beautiful: iron and chain singing against each other, control versus choreography. 

For a moment Corvin saw the boy he once glimpsed—a small figure who had learned to use observation as armor. Corvin's hollow choices flashed before him: survival purchased by pieces of himself. He could continue to enforce the Covenant's orders, or he could redirect his remaining force. 

He chose partial redemption. In a decisive gesture, he turned his destructive momentum away from the fleeing and toward a fortified battery that threatened civilian lives. The act cost him everything. 

He died not fully redeemed but altered; his final motion was less about victory and more about repair. The field would remember him as both monster and man.
