Familiar faces return in small ways: Marta runs a bakery that funds a small scholarship; Lira teaches children to read maps; Gareth trains engineers to repair wells. Each micro-scene is a vignette showing how ordinary lives continued and how Aisen's lessons persisted. 

They are quiet reminders that change is incremental: a repaired bridge, a well dug, a child taught to observe. The narrative stitches these moments into a fabric that is resilient and human.
