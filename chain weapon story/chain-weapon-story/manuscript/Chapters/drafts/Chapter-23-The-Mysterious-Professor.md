The professor taught modern ritual theory and smiled as if scholarship alone could fix the world. Aisen noticed the small things: appointments made at odd hours, an insistence on certain books being removed from the shelf during closed hours. 

When Aisen followed a trail of receipts and quiet conversations, he found the thin seam between public teaching and private procurement. The professor requested ritual texts that had no obvious academic use and asked Tholen for copies with a casual tone that implied authority. 

It was a small discovery but dangerous: this faculty member was a node in something larger. Aisen cataloged details, not accusations—who took what, when, and where. He began leaving questions in the margin of his notebooks; the professor's pattern fit too well with the Covenants' earlier recruitment methods. 

The truth lurked in the quiet paperwork.
