They gathered in a circle that had no king: Kaelen tending a pot of root-broth, Rin with coded slips, Lysandra with maps, Elara bound and practical. Each carried the residue of battle—scar, tremor, loss—but all carried the same knowledge: systems outlive individuals. 

The conversation was businesslike. Grief was acknowledged, sacralized briefly, then filed for fuel. They spoke in logistics: how to keep safe houses operating, how to continue leaks without a central node, how to teach Aisen's techniques so they wouldn't be fetishized. 

Each member read the world through what they had learned from him: Kaelen's patience, Rin's precision, Amara's network craft, Elara's protective pragmatism. They agreed not to make him a martyr but a method book. 

The chapter closes with action: new cells formed, lessons written, and a plan that Aisen's ordinary tactics would become infrastructure—a way of being resistant rather than a single life of sacrifice.
