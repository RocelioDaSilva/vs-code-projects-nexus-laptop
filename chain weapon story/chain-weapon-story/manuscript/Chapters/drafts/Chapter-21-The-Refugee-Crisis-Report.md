The Academy's sheltered halls filled with the kind of faces that did not fit neatly into lectures: burned hands, hollow eyes, children with stories of lost rivers. Emma, a refugee who had walked two borders with her family, told her story in a crowded hall. Her voice was flat and precise—details that made the crisis feel like a ledger of losses. 

Aisen and allies listened and cataloged: ecosystems collapsing, supply lines co-opted, official reports minimizing numbers while privately orders tightened. The human cost became data in their notebooks—names, ages, places. The Academy's comfortable distance was pierced by these arrivals; students who had read about crisis now saw it up close. 

The chapter ends with a small resolution: the group agreed to help one family find safe passage. The act was small but necessary, a reminder that knowledge combined with action can change an outcome.
