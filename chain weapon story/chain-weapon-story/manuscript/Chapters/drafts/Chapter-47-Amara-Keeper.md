Amara assumes a new role: keeper of stories and of systems. She collects testimonies, records methods, and protects the network's procedural memory. Her ledger becomes an instrument of continuity—who is owed what favor, which safe houses are reliable, and how to teach Aisen's tactics without cult. 

She also acts as a griot: sharing the truth in controlled doses among merchant networks and refugee gatherings. Her stewardship is quiet and deliberate. 

Her final act in the chapter is to carve the wooden giraffe—Aisen's odd talisman—into many small tokens dispersed among trusted hands, a symbol that ordinary care persists.
