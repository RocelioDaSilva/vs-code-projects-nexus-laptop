Found Log: Archive Fragment — Copilot Interaction

They keep these logs in the Academy’s lower stacks, bound between thin sheets of vellum and printed on recycled ledger paper: discussions between human hands and the small, polite daemon that lives in the glass boxes. The header reads simply: "Reconstructed Conversation — GitHub Copilot (archive)."

Aisen read the entry with thumb damp from the rain, the lamps in the stacks humming overhead. The log described how an automated assistant had parsed the DeepSeek talks in five‑thousand line sweeps, saved summaries, and built a bestiary — a catalog of creatures that breathed around the edges of the Veil. The way the record described the assistant’s choices made the work feel less like a machine and more like a collaborator: it favored Kairos material, prioritized a scene, and then followed up with a pragmatic, almost clinical decision when the source ended.

Aisen let the page fall open where the assistant had written: “Attempted read of source 45001–50000 returned no content — source effectively ended.” He smiled. The machines were honest about their limits.

Beneath the summary sat a folded excerpt the archivists had inserted: the agent recommended starting with a scene called "Aldric’s Execution." Aisen felt the paper weigh heavy in his hands. That was a scene they’d argued over for months—too raw to open, too central not to.

He copied the transcript into his ledger and wrote a single line beneath it: "Use the daemon’s eye, not its hands." Then he closed the book and walked toward the ladder that led to the northern stacks, planning which pages to pull next.

— Archive fragment end.
