# Resonance Crystals & Magical Artifacts

## Overview

**Resonance crystals** are natural or engineered objects that store and amplify magical energy. They are the backbone of artifact magic and civilization-scale magical infrastructure.

Understanding crystals is critical for:
- Worldbuilding (how are cities powered?)
- Character resources (where does emergency mana come from?)
- Tactical elements (can artifacts be destroyed to disable magic?)
- Aelfar lore (they invented resonance crystal networks)

---

## PART I: FUNDAMENTALS

### What Are Resonance Crystals?

**Definition:** Naturally occurring (or engineered) structures that align with the Flux, allowing them to store, amplify, transmit, or transform magical energy.

### Natural vs. Engineered

#### Natural Crystals
- **Where found:** High-Flux regions, near Flux Tears, inside mountains, deep underground
- **Formation:** Flux saturation over centuries; crystal lattice becomes magically conductive
- **Rarity:** Valuable; often guarded or claimed by power groups
- **Unpredictability:** Varied properties; no two are identical
- **Power level:** Can range from Novice-tier (30 MU storage) to Archmage-tier (1000+ MU storage)

#### Engineered Crystals
- **Aelfar origin:** Pre-Sundering engineers learned to grow crystals with desired properties
- **Method:** Submerge seed crystal in pure Flux (requires Flux Tear access); apply template through ritual; grow over months
- **Control:** Properties are standardized and repeatable
- **Durability:** Engineered crystals are more stable than natural ones
- **Cost:** Requires rare materials and skilled engineers; very expensive

## PART II: CRYSTAL TYPES & PROPERTIES

### Storage Crystals (Mana Batteries)

**Purpose:** Hold magical energy for later use.

| Type | Capacity | Duration | Cost | Use Case |
|------|----------|----------|------|----------|
| **Novice** | 30–80 MU | Weeks before leakage | 20–50 silver | Personal emergency reserves; student practice |
| **Journeyman** | 150–350 MU | Months | 100–300 silver | Military supply; city administration |
| **Expert** | 400–1200 MU | Years (with care) | 500–2000 silver | Siege magic support; major institutions |
| **Archmage** | 1200+ MU | Decades | 5000+ silver | Kingdom-level infrastructure; irreplaceable |

**Mechanics:**
- Mana leaks from storage slowly (5–10% per week) unless properly sealed
- Crystal can be "overcharged" (stored MU exceeds design capacity); risks shattering
- Extracting mana is faster than natural regen; a Journeyman can pull 20–30 MU/min from a crystal

**Aisen's access:** Academy maintains a small cache of Journeyman-tier crystals. Aisen can request emergency restocking (1–2 per expedition).

### Amplification Crystals (Power Multipliers)

**Purpose:** Reduce the MU cost of specific spell types.

| Type | Spell Type | Discount | Effect | Rarity |
|------|-----------|----------|--------|--------|
| **Flame amplifier** | Fire magic | 25–40% cost reduction | Fire spells burn hotter and spread faster | Moderate |
| **Thunder amplifier** | Lightning magic | 25–40% cost reduction | Lightning travels farther; higher arc precision | Moderate |
| **Water amplifier** | Water/ice magic | 25–40% cost reduction | Water control affects larger volumes | Common |
| **Kinetic amplifier** | Telekinesis/momentum | 20–30% cost reduction | Movement spells more responsive; momentum transfers more efficient | Rare (Aisen's specialty) |
| **Universal amplifier** | Any magic | 10–15% cost reduction | Works for all spell types but less potent than specialized | Very rare |

**Mechanics:**
- Amplification crystal must be in physical contact with caster or integrated into weapon/armor
- Multiple amplifiers can stack (e.g., fire + kinetic = 50–70% cost reduction for fire telekinesis)
- Amplifiers degrade with use (~10% effectiveness loss per year)

**Aisen's access:** His chain spear should be integrated with a **kinetic amplifier** (if not already). This reduces toggle costs from 4 MU → ~3 MU per segment.

### Transmission Crystals (Communication & Coordination)

**Purpose:** Send information (voice, images, sensations) across distances.

| Type | Range | Latency | Capacity | Cost |
|------|-------|---------|----------|------|
| **Short-range** | <1 km | Instant | 1 message/min | 50 silver |
| **Medium-range** | 1–10 km | <1 second delay | 10 messages/min | 200 silver |
| **Long-range** | 10–100 km | 2–5 second delay | 5 messages/min, simpler format | 800 silver |
| **Kingdom-range (network)** | 100+ km (requires relay network) | 10–30 sec delay | Very limited | 5000+ silver per node |

**Mechanics:**
- Two paired transmission crystals needed (one sender, one receiver)
- Requires initial synchronization ritual (1 hour; 50 MU spent)
- Can be intercepted or jammed by strong interference
- Network transmission crystals require relay stations (every 50–75 km)

**Cultural significance:**
- Heartland uses transmission networks for military command
- Island Fortress rejects them (seen as dishonorable spying)
- Sahelian Spirit-bonded can communicate with spirits instead (seen as more trustworthy)

### Sensing Crystals (Detection & Analysis)

**Purpose:** Detect magical activity, identify spells, monitor areas.

| Type | Detection | Range | Cost | Rarity |
|------|-----------|-------|------|--------|
| **Magic sensor** | Active spelled areas | 50–100 meters | 150 silver | Moderate |
| **Intention reader** | Pre-casting intent (warnings) | 20–50 meters | 500 silver | Rare |
| **Aura analyzer** | STA classification of mage pool | 10–20 meters | 300 silver | Moderate |
| **Contamination detector** | Flux corruption, curse markers | Variable | 200 silver | Moderate |

**Mechanics:**
- Sensing crystals are passive (no MU cost to operate) but require nearby mana source to function
- Can be "fooled" by cloaking spells or anti-magic fields
- Military uses them at garrison gates; academies use them to monitor student safety

## PART III: CRYSTAL NETWORKS & INFRASTRUCTURE

### Aelfar Legacy Networks (Pre-Sundering)

**What they were:**
- Massive pyramid structures containing huge resonance crystals
- Linked together continent-wide through transmission networks
- Automated systems for power distribution, water control, climate regulation
- Some structures visible as ancient ruins; some still function partially

**Why most failed:**
- The Sundering shattered connection networks (physical and energetic links broke)
- Crystals destabilized; some shattered, others went dormant
- Key facilities lost maintenance; systems degraded
- Some were deliberately disabled by post-Sundering survivors

**Current status:**
- A few Aelfar sites still have functional or semi-functional crystals
- Heartland has captured and repurposed some (integrated into siege magic systems)
- Andean mountains contain untouched Aelfar installations (respected as ancestral)
- Federation negotiates with entities to "inherit" Aelfar crystal access

**Tactical significance:**
- A functional Aelfar pyramid could power a kingdom for decades
- Competing for Aelfar sites is an ongoing source of international tension
- Maps of Aelfar sites are classified intelligence in all nations

### Modern Crystal Networks

#### Heartland Imperial Network
- **Purpose:** Military command, siege power distribution, administrative coordination
- **Layout:** Capital node, regional nodes (major cities), garrison nodes (100+ per region)
- **Redundancy:** Partial mesh (broken links don't collapse entire system)
- **Reliability:** ~85% uptime; failures are political disasters

#### Celestial Meditation Hubs
- **Purpose:** Support large-scale meditation formations
- **Function:** Stabilize thought-spaces; reduce casting costs during synchronized sessions
- **Integration:** Usually underground, hidden from non-practitioners
- **Limitation:** Completely inactive when not in meditation use

#### Sahelian Spirit-Anchor Stones
- **Purpose:** Create zones where spirits feel "welcomed"
- **Function:** Reduce mana cost of spirit-bonding rituals; attract beneficial entities
- **Placement:** Usually in sacred groves, at tribal gatherings, near burial grounds
- **Note:** Not "powered" like other crystals; they are anchoring points rather than batteries

#### Federation Contract-Vaults
- **Purpose:** Store contracts magically; enforce terms
- **Function:** Bind agreements; track debts; execute consequences if terms broken
- **Security:** Heavily guarded; breaking into a contract-vault is catastrophic crime
- **Capacity:** Can hold thousands of simultaneous contracts

## PART IV: CRYSTAL CRAFTING & MAINTENANCE

### Growing Crystals

**Requirements:**
- Seed crystal (small raw crystal taken from Flux Tear or previous growth)
- Pure Flux environment (access to Flux Tear or amplified chamber)
- Template (ritual inscription defining desired properties)
- Time (2–12 months depending on target size)
- Supervision (crystal can destabilize; requires expert monitoring)

**Cost breakdown:**
- Seed crystal: 50–200 silver
- Flux access (rental or permission): 100–500 silver
- Template preparation: 50–150 silver (varies by complexity)
- Expert supervision: 200–800 silver (per month)
- **Total for Journeyman storage crystal: 400–1500 silver**

**Who can do it:**
- Aelfar engineers (pre-Sundering; extinct)
- High-tier artificers (Heartland, Island Fortress, Federation)
- Celestial masters (with philosophical alignment)
- Sahelian shamans (with spirit permission)
- Andean instinct-crafters (rare, experimental)

### Recharging Crystals

**Method 1: Ambient Flux (Slow)**
- Place crystal in high-Flux area (near Flux Tear, thin Veil region)
- Regen rate: 5–20 MU/day depending on Flux density
- No cost, but slow and location-dependent

**Method 2: Direct Mana Injection (Fast)**
- Mage touches crystal and channels mana directly
- Injection rate: 5–10 MU/min per mage
- Cost: Mages experience mild fatigue but can be rotated
- Time: A Journeyman crystal needs ~15–30 mages for 1 hour to recharge fully

**Method 3: Ritual Recharge (Efficient)**
- Group of mages participate in synchronized ritual (like Heartland formation)
- Efficiency: 20–30% better than direct injection
- Time: ~1 hour for Journeyman crystal from empty
- Limitation: Requires ritual space and prepared participants

### Crystal Degradation

**Factors:**
- **Age:** Natural decay over years (crystals lose 0.5–2% effectiveness per year)
- **Overcharging:** Storing more MU than rated capacity causes micro-fractures
- **Combat damage:** Physical impacts can shatter crystals instantly
- **Magical backlash:** Failed spells can contaminate crystal, rendering it useless
- **Flux saturation:** Too much ambient Flux can "burn out" a crystal

**Lifespan:**
- **Poorly maintained:** 5–10 years before unusable
- **Well-maintained:** 30–50 years
- **Master-crafted:** 100+ years (Aelfar-quality)

## PART V: CRYSTAL IN COMBAT & TACTICS

### Crystal-Enabled Tactics

#### Siege Magic (Heartland Favorite)
- Massive storage crystals power formation-cast spells
- Siege crystal can sustain wall-hardening spells for days
- Capturing/destroying siege crystal cripples siege capability

#### Artifact Weapons (Island Fortress)
- Signature weapons integrated with amplifier crystals
- Each master's weapon contains unique crystal configuration
- Losing weapon = losing ~50% of combat effectiveness

#### Ritual Support (Celestial)
- Meditation formation sustained by nearby transmission/coordination crystals
- If crystals are disrupted, entire formation collapses
- Masters protect crystal locations above almost everything

#### Spirit-Anchor Advantages (Sahelian)
- Anchor stones allow repeated bonding at same location (cheaper ritual cost)
- Destroying an anchor stone offends all spirits in region; magic becomes harder for everyone
- Acts as deterrent against warfare in sacred lands

### Anti-Crystal Tactics

**Sensor detection & destruction:**
- Target transmission/sensing crystals to blind enemies
- Destroying just 1–2 key crystals can cascade system failures
- Scouts often hunt for hidden crystal caches

**Magical saturation:**
- Flood area with ambient Flux; overwhelms delicate crystals
- Requires Flux Tear access; extreme tactic

**Theft & relocation:**
- Stealing enemy crystals is classic military objective
- Guarding crystal supplies is major logistical burden

**Direct shatter:**
- Crack crystals with physical impact or magical backlash
- One skilled act can destroy months of preparation

- Shattering enemy's crystal network is viable victory condition

**Social status:**
- Having personal crystals marks him as trained/legitimate academic (not street mage)
- Protecting his crystals is responsibility (losing them means losing access to advantages)

## PART VII: QUICK REFERENCE

### Crystal Capacity & Regen

| Tier | Capacity | Weekly Leakage | Recharge Time (single mage) | Silver Cost |
|------|----------|---|---|---|
| Novice | 30–80 MU | 5–10% | 5–15 hours | 20–50 |
| Journeyman | 150–350 MU | 5–8% | 15–40 hours | 100–300 |
| Expert | 400–1200 MU | 3–5% | 40–120 hours | 500–2000 |
| Archmage | 1200+ MU | <3% | 120+ hours | 5000+ |

### Crystal Types Quick Reference

- **Storage:** Mana battery; ~5–30 MU/min extraction rate
- **Amplification:** Reduces spell cost (15–40% discount); specific to spell type
- **Transmission:** Long-distance communication; requires paired crystal
- **Sensing:** Detects magic/intentions; passive operation
- **Anchor:** Sahelian spirit locations; no cost, location-dependent effect

### Integration Considerations

- Amplifier crystal in weapon = permanent +20–30% efficiency for that spell type
- Too many crystals integrated = weapon becomes heavy and magical "noisy" (easier to detect)
- Crystal damage in combat is catastrophic (lose equipment + lose backup mana source)

## SUMMARY

Resonance crystals are magical infrastructure. They enable civilizations to scale magic, store power, and create distributed systems. However, they are also **vulnerabilities**—breaking a crystal network can cascade into total system failure. Aisen's independence from crystals (he doesn't rely on them, just uses them as backup) is another mark of his genius: he can operate in any environment, with or without magical infrastructure.
