# The Hard Magic System — Core Rules & Framework

## Overview

Magic in the Chain Weapon world operates on **rigid, internally consistent laws**. The system values **costs, limits, and consequences** over arbitrary power. Magic cannot appear where there are no rules; power must be earned through understanding and sacrifice.

---

## PART I: FUNDAMENTAL PRINCIPLES

### The Duality: Weave vs. Flux

#### The Weave (Physical Law)
- The fabric of material reality: gravity, momentum, thermodynamics, causality, conservation
- Deterministic and observable
- Operates on universal principles that never change
- Examples: objects fall, energy is conserved, time moves forward

#### The Flux (Raw Magic)
- The overlay of magical energy that can **temporarily bend** Weave rules
- Flows from tears in the Veil (remnants of the Sundering)
- Abundance varies by location: thin Veil (high Flux) vs. thick Veil (low Flux)
- Cannot create matter from nothing; can only redistribute or transform existing matter

#### Interaction Between Weave & Flux
- Magic works by converting Flux (mana) into effects that still obey Weave laws **once manifested**
- A telekinetic push still respects mass and acceleration
- A fireball still burns according to thermodynamics
- The cost of magic is partly in Flux consumption, partly in physical strain

**Key Insight:** Magic doesn't break the rules of physics; it *temporarily suspends them* while mana is actively channeled. When the spell ends, the Weave snaps back to normal—and the energy difference creates consequences (side effects, entropy debt, environmental damage).

## PART II: MANA SYSTEM

### Basic Terminology

| Term | Definition |
|------|-----------|
| **MU** | Mana Unit (abstract unit of magical cost) |
| **Pool** | Total MU a character can store at once |
| **Regen** | MU recovered per minute while resting or meditating |
| **Ambient Flux** | Available mana in a given location (depends on Veil thickness) |
| **Veil Thickness** | Determines how much ambient Flux is accessible (thin = high magic, thick = low magic) |

### Power Tiers (World Scale)

| Tier | Pool | Regen | Examples | Role |
|------|------|-------|----------|------|
| **Novice** | 30–80 MU | 1 MU/min | Conscripts, basic students, untrained commoners | Laborers, minor enchantment support |
| **Journeyman** | 150–350 MU | 2–3 MU/min | Licensed city mages, Academy graduates, sworn mages | Officers, specialists, skilled craftspeople |
| **Expert** | 400–1200 MU | 4–6 MU/min | Battle-mages, senior instructors, specialized practitioners | Military command, major rituals, advanced research |
| **Archmage** | 1200+ MU | 8+ MU/min | State-level appointees, legendary figures, civilization-shapers | Political power, irreversible rituals, reality-altering effects |

### Aisen (Baseline Protagonist)
- **Pool:** 120 MU (Journeyman tier; exceptional control but modest reservoir)
- **Regen:** 2 MU/min idle; 4 MU/min after 10+ minutes of focused meditation
- **Burst Reserve:** 30–40 MU comfortably expended in intense sequences before severe fatigue
- **Specialization:** Telekinesis + chain spear control (not raw offensive magic)

### How Magic Gets Cast

**Step 1: Mana Gathering**
- Caster draws ambient Flux from the environment
- Veil thickness determines how much is available
- Thick Veil = difficult casting; thin Veil = abundant mana

**Step 2: Intention & Knowledge**
- Caster shapes mana through will, training, or ritual
- Without knowledge of the effect, mana cannot be shaped effectively
- Improvised magic (quick-cast) requires deep understanding to avoid backlash

**Step 3: Manifestation**
- Shaped mana converts into effect in the Weave
- Once manifested, the effect obeys physical laws
- Telekinetic force still respects mass and acceleration

**Step 4: Consequence**
- Side effects occur based on spell efficiency
- Efficient magic: aligned with existing processes (minimal backlash)
- Inefficient magic: fighting physical laws (severe backlash, environmental damage)

## PART III: FORCE TIERS & PRACTICAL COSTS

### Force Tiers (MU to Effect)

| Tier | MU Range | Example Effects | Physical Manifestation |
|------|----------|-----------------|------------------------|
| **Tier 0 (Subtle)** | 0.5–1 MU | Sensory probes, boundary sense, thread manipulation | Barely perceptible; mostly mental/informational |
| **Tier 1 (Light)** | 1–5 MU | Small pushes, fine adjustments, minor torque on objects | Nudges objects under 10 kg; precise control |
| **Tier 2 (Medium)** | 5–15 MU | Move small objects (5–30 kg), sustained force, momentum adjustment | Lifts a person, swings a weapon, accelerates a sphere |
| **Tier 3 (Heavy)** | 15–40 MU | Move heavy objects (30–200 kg), sustained force over seconds, area effects | Pushes a boulder, holds a wall, creates environmental changes |
| **Tier 4 (Extreme)** | 40+ MU | Field-changing force, large-scale effects, requires large pools or multiple casters | Siege magic, terrain transformation, catastrophic power |

**Note:** Mass figures are approximate narrative guidelines. Use Tier model rather than strict Newtonian calculation unless writing technical realism scenes.

### Standard Technique Costs (Aisen Baseline)

#### Telekinesis Costs

| Technique | MU Cost | Sustain Cost | Notes |
|-----------|---------|--------------|-------|
| **Point Push** | 1 MU | 0.5 MU/min hold | Tier 1; single impulse or sustained grip |
| **Torque Twist** | 2–6 MU | 0.2 MU/sec hold | Tier 1–2; depends on target mass and leverage |
| **Momentum Adjustment** | 3 MU | — | Tier 2; nudging a moving object's trajectory |
| **Trajectory Curving** | 3–6 MU | — | Tier 2; curved path for projectile |
| **Sustained Float** | 4 MU | +0.8 MU per 5 kg | Tier 2; holding ~5 kg indefinitely; scales upward |
| **Boundary Sense** | 0.2 MU/min | Continuous | Tier 0; passive awareness of objects/edges in space |
| **Thread Probe** | 0.5–1 MU | 0.2 MU/min | Tier 0; single sensory thread; multiple threads stack cost |
| **Segment Toggle** | 4 MU | — | Chain spear specific; tightens/loosens 5-link module |
| **Construct Tether** | 3 MU | 0.4 MU/min | Tier 2; create and maintain telekinetic link to detached object |

#### Example Combat Sequence

**Scenario:** Aisen toggles 3 chain segments, creates 1 tether to a detached blade, performs 3 point pushes, maintains tether for 2 minutes.

| Action | Cost | Subtotal |
|--------|------|----------|
| Toggle 3 segments | 3 × 4 = 12 MU | 12 MU |
| Create 1 tether | 3 MU | 15 MU |
| 3 point pushes | 3 × 1 = 3 MU | 18 MU |
| 2-min tether maintenance | 0.4 × 2 = 0.8 MU | 18.8 MU |
| **Total immediate cost** | — | ~18 MU |
| **Pool used** | 18 / 120 = 15% | Sustainable |

This sequence is within Aisen's burst capacity and sustainable after a brief 5-minute rest.

## PART IV: LIMITATIONS & CONSTRAINTS

### Veil Thickness Effects

| Veil State | Ambient Flux | Casting Difficulty | Regen Adjustment | Examples |
|------------|-------------|-------------------|------------------|----------|
| **Very Thin** | Abundant (80%+) | Trivial | +50% | Flux Tears, ancient Aelfar sites, storm zones |
| **Thin** | High (50–80%) | Easy | +25% | Coastal regions, magically active areas, wild zones |
| **Normal** | Moderate (30–50%) | Standard | +0% | Most settlements, Academy, stable regions |
| **Thick** | Low (10–30%) | Difficult | −25% | Mountain interior, Heartland bureaucratic zones, shielded areas |
| **Very Thick** | Scarce (0–10%) | Extremely Hard | −50% | Deep underground, anti-magic fields, sealed chambers |

### Magical Interference & Shielding

- **Interference fields** reduce effective MU by 20–80% (field strength dependent)
- **Enchanted resistance:** Certain materials (dense memory-metal, Aelfar alloys, ward stones) require +50–200% MU to affect
- **Personal resistance:** Some people (rare natural resistance) reduce incoming magic effectiveness by 20–50%

### Attention & Control Limits

- **Active manipulation threads:** A single person can maintain 1–2 high-precision telekinetic threads without efficiency loss
- **Background threads:** Can maintain passive sensory threads (boundary sense, thread probes) at low cost, but they accumulate
- **Overload point:** More than 3–4 active threads causes fatal error: all threads collapse, immediate fatigue, possible backlash

### Physical Fatigue System

| Fatigue Level | Pool Used | Symptoms | Mechanical Effect |
|---------------|-----------|----------|-------------------|
| **Fresh** | 0–40% | None | Full accuracy and speed |
| **Mild fatigue** | 40–60% | Slight tremor, delayed reaction | −10% to precision; slower casting |
| **Moderate fatigue** | 60–80% | Nosebleed, blurred vision, headache | −20% to precision; mistakes on complex spells |
| **Severe fatigue** | 80–100% | Vision loss, disorientation, muscle failure | −30% to precision; high failure risk; collapse likely if pushing further |
| **Overload** | 100%+ | Blackout, seizure, tissue damage, possible death | Forced unconsciousness; recovery takes hours or days |

**Recovery:** Regen is non-linear. Shallow depletion (40–60%) recovers at listed regen rate. Deep depletion (80%+) requires long rest (8+ hours) before regen accelerates.

## PART V: TYPES OF MAGIC

### Ritualistic Magic
- **Preparation:** Requires circles, materials, stable conditions, time
- **Advantage:** More efficient than improvised; high precision; can affect large areas
- **Disadvantage:** Slow; location-dependent; fails if interrupted
- **Examples:** Warding a building, charging resonance crystals, summoning, large-scale healing

### Improvised Magic ("Quick-cast")
- **Preparation:** None; casting on-the-fly
- **Advantage:** Fast; flexible; responsive to changing conditions
- **Disadvantage:** Less efficient; riskier; requires deep knowledge to avoid backlash
- **Examples:** Creating light, pushing an object, quick healing, emergency telekinetic catch

### Artifact Magic
- **Mechanism:** Pre-enchanted objects hold mana or effects
- **Activation:** Can be used by non-mages if designed for that purpose
- **Advantage:** No casting knowledge required; repeatable; stable
- **Disadvantage:** Limited by artifact durability; mana storage capacity; non-upgradeable
- **Examples:** Resonance crystals, enchanted weapons, ward stones, message orbs

### Natural Magic
- **Trigger:** Plants, animals, and places with inherent magical properties
- **Origin:** Often flows from Flux Tears or high-Flux regions
- **Mechanism:** Not "cast" but triggered by conditions (sunlight, blood, time)
- **Examples:** Memory-blooms (grant sensory visions), Silt Rot (corrodes metal), Void Zones (reality anomalies)

## PART VI: MAGIC DETECTION & SENSITIVITY

### Who Can Sense Magic?

| Type | Baseline Ability | Training Boost |
|------|-----------------|-----------------|
| **Untrained commoner** | Faint sensation if very strong magic | None |
| **Novice student** | Light telepathic contact; major spells obvious | 20–40% detection range |
| **Journeyman mage** | All active spells clearly sensed; can identify type | 60–80% detection range; can estimate MU cost |
| **Expert mage** | Passive magic sensed; can predict casting | Full detection; can sense pre-casting intention |
| **Archmage** | Disturbances across cities; precognitive warnings | Can sense across regions |
| **Aisen (telekinesis specialist)** | Can sense "threads" of active telekinesis within 30–50 meters; can feel boundary sense from others | Superior spatial awareness; can disrupt threads |

### Magic Sense Mechanics

- **Active casting** is always detectable; the more MU spent, the more obvious
- **Passive effects** (maintained spells, artifacts) can be sensed by trained mages
- **Expertise gap:** A Journeyman mage cannot easily detect an Archmage's subtle magic unless actively looking
- **Interference:** Strong ambient Flux (thin Veil regions) makes detecting individual spells harder

## PART VII: COST-BENEFIT FRAMEWORK

Every spell has a cost structure. Understanding cost shapes tactical and narrative choices.

### Mana Cost vs. Outcome

| Magic Type | MU Investment | In-Scenario Benefit | Hidden Cost |
|------------|-------------|-------------------|-------------|
| **Quick healing** | 15–30 MU | Mends non-lethal wound | Caster ages; arthritis or gray hair develops |
| **Teleportation** | 40–60 MU | Instant displacement | Spatial distortion; gravity anomalies nearby; risk of shearing if objects in path |
| **Fireball** | 20–40 MU | Large area damage | Extreme heat cost; environment cools drastically; frost develops nearby |
| **Charm/Control** | 30+ MU | Control enemy for minutes | Caster's memory loss equal to duration controlled |
| **Time reversal** | 50+ MU | Undo ~10 seconds | Caster loses memories of reversed period; timeline "corrects" with violent events |
| **Summoning** | 60+ MU | Manifestation of entity | Summoned entity demands price; unknown consequences possible |

### Efficiency Gains Through Training

- **Novice→Journeyman:** 15–25% efficiency improvement per year through focused practice
- **Signature techniques:** Can reduce cost to ~50% of baseline with specialized training and anchoring aids
- **Mastery payoff:** A master of one spell type becomes nearly unbeatable in specific scenarios

## PART VIII: COMMON FAILURE MODES

### Casting Failure

| Failure Type | Cause | Effect |
|--------------|-------|--------|
| **Partial failure** | Magical interference mid-cast | Spell weakens; secondary effects misfire; unpredictable outcome e.g., tether drips, detached blade lags |
| **Feedback backlash** | Attempting too-high Tier action at low pool | Neural disruption; sensory ringing; disorientation for minutes |
| **Overload collapse** | Exceeding pool limit | Instant blackout; recovery takes hours; possible tissue damage |
| **Interference cascade** | Multiple spells interfering in same area | All spells fail; casters take fatigue damage; area becomes magically "dead" for minutes |

### Environmental Consequences

| Environmental Effect | Cause | Duration |
|----------------------|-------|----------|
| **Void Zone** | Prolonged magic inefficiency; entropy debt accumulation | Permanent or very long-term (centuries) |
| **Flux Pocket** | Localized Veil tear or temporary thinning | Hours to days; MU costs +30–80% in pocket |
| **Magic dead zone** | Overuse of anti-magic fields or massive dispelling | Temporary (days); no casting possible |
| **Temporal distortion** | Excessive time-bending magic | Hours; objects/people age unpredictably |

## SUMMARY

The hard magic system operates on **consistency, cost, and consequence**. Magic is never free. Casters choose between:
- Power now vs. consequences later
- Precision (lower cost) vs. brute force (higher cost)
- Efficiency (training required) vs. raw talent (limited)

This creates tactical depth and meaningful character choices. Aisen's genius is not raw power but understanding costs and exploiting bent rules with minimal expenditure—placing a string exactly where the Flux bends and breaks.
