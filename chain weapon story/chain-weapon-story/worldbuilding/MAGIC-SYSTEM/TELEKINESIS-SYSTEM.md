# Telekinesis — Specialized System & Aisen Integration

## Overview

Telekinesis is Aisen's primary magical discipline: moving objects through space using will and mana (MU). Unlike offensive magic (fireballs, ice shards) or ritualistic magic (wards, summoning), telekinesis is **precision control-based**—about finesse, sustained manipulation, and tactical adaptation.

Aisen is a **Journeyman-tier specialist** in telekinesis, not a prodigy. His advantage is **efficiency and spatial understanding**, not raw power.

---

## PART I: FUNDAMENTAL MECHANISMS

### The Thread Concept

Telekinesis works through **"threads"**—invisible, persistent mental connections to objects or forces.

| Thread Type | Purpose | Cost | Range |
|------------|---------|------|-------|
| **Boundary sense thread** | Passive awareness; map space around caster | 0.2 MU/min | 20–50 meters |
| **Sensory probe thread** | Active investigation; touch distant objects mentally | 0.5–1 MU create; 0.2 MU/min maintain | 10–30 meters |
| **Manipulation thread** | Direct control; move or twist objects | Variable (see force tiers) | 5–20 meters |
| **Construct tether** | Link to detached objects (chain segments, blades) | 3 MU create; 0.4 MU/min maintain | 3–10 meters |

### Thread Capacity

**Active threads (high-precision):** 1–2 simultaneous without efficiency loss.
**Background threads (low-intensity):** 3–5 passive sensory threads acceptable; cost accumulates.
**Overload point:** >4 active threads causes collapse—all threads fail, instant fatigue, backlash risk.

**Example:** Aisen can maintain a boundary sense thread (passively mapping space) + 1 manipulation thread (moving the chain) comfortably. Adding a second manipulation thread causes fatigue penalties.

## PART II: FORCE TIERS FOR TELEKINESIS

### Tier 0: Subtle (0.5–1 MU per action)

**Use cases:** Sensory work, fine manipulation, information gathering.

| Technique | Cost | Effect | Example |
|-----------|------|--------|---------|
| **Boundary sense** | 0.2 MU/min | Map objects/edges within 30–50 m | Know where table edges are in dark room |
| **Thread probe** | 0.5–1 MU | Touch distant object mentally; basic sensory feedback | Test if cloth is silk or wool from 5m away |
| **Vibration sense** | 0.5 MU/min | Feel distant vibrations through air/ground | Sense footsteps 20m away |

**Failure risk:** Very low. Tier 0 is almost always reliable.

---

### Tier 1: Light (1–5 MU per action)

**Use cases:** Push small objects, adjust trajectories, fine torque on lightweight levers.

| Technique | Cost | Physical Effect | Example |
|-----------|------|-----------------|---------|
| **Point push** | 1 MU | Nudge object <10 kg with ~5 kg force | Push a door open; deflect a throwing knife |
| **Sustained hold** | 0.5 MU/min | Hold object aloft (e.g., 5 kg) indefinitely | Float a candle to light a high shelf |
| **Fine adjustments** | 1–2 MU | Micro-movements for precision | Align a sword tip with enemy's eye slot |
| **Torque twist (light)** | 2–3 MU | Rotate delicate objects (vase, lock mechanism) | Open a locked chest without a key; disarm a trigger |
| **Momentum nudge** | 1–2 MU | Adjust trajectory of slow-moving projectile | Curve a thrown pebble by 10 degrees |

**Failure risk:** Low if conditions are ideal; moderate if interrupted or opposed.

**Aisen's signature:** Most of Aisen's quick reactions use Tier 1 (1–3 MU per push, costing ~1–5% of pool per action). This makes him incredibly efficient in combats—he never "burns out" early.

---

### Tier 2: Medium (5–15 MU per action)

**Use cases:** Move person-sized objects, sustained force, area manipulation, chain combat sequences.

| Technique | Cost | Physical Effect | Example |
|-----------|------|-----------------|---------|
| **Push (medium)** | 5–10 MU | Move 30–100 kg object decisively or person-sized target | Shove enemy backward 3 meters; knock debris clear |
| **Sustained float** | 4 MU base + 0.8 MU per 5 kg | Hold object aloft for minutes (person ~60 kg = 13 MU total for indefinite hold) | Float a water barrel while moving; lift an injured ally |
| **Trajectory curving** | 3–6 MU | Curved path for projectile; change course mid-flight | Arc an arrow around a column; curve a spear in a spiral |
| **Momentum transfer** | 6–10 MU | Move momentum between two objects without contact (momentum bridge) | Transfer swing momentum from whip to blade; add power to thrown stone |
| **Sustained grapple** | 8–12 MU/sec | Hold object/person in place against resistance | Lock enemy limbs for 3–5 seconds of immobility |
| **Construct tether** | 3 MU + 0.4 MU/min | Permanent link to detached chain segment or blade; moves with caster's will | Detach chain segment and control it 10m away |

**Failure risk:** Moderate. Environmental interference, enemy resistance, or distraction can disrupt Tier 2 effects.

**Aisen's signature:** Chain spear combat is built on Tier 2—he creates tethers to detached chain segments (3 MU create, then 0.4 MU/min maintain) and manipulates them in coordinated patterns. A typical 2-minute chain combat uses 15–25 MU total.

---

### Tier 3: Heavy (15–40 MU per action)

**Use cases:** Massive objects, extreme forces, battlefield-scale manipulation, desperation sequences.

| Technique | Cost | Physical Effect | Example |
|-----------|------|-----------------|---------|
| **Push (heavy)** | 15–25 MU | Move 100–300 kg object or multiple people; apply extreme force | Push through a barricade; move a horse away; force open fortified door |
| **Sustained heavy lift** | 15+ MU, scaling | Hold heavy object aloft (200+ kg) for extended time | Hold collapsing ceiling section while allies escape |
| **Area pressure** | 20–35 MU | Create localized force field over 10–20 m² (not full enclosure) | Push enemies away from a defensive position; create "safe zone" |
| **Destructive twist** | 25–40 MU | Shatter or disassemble large structure through applied torque | Break castle gate hinges; snap a tree; shred a steel door |
| **Sustained grapple (heavy)** | 20+ MU/sec | Lock ship-sized object or multiple opponents in place | Immobilize a carriage; hold a raging beast |

**Failure risk:** High. Any interference, counter-magic, or physical obstruction can cause catastrophic failure (backlash, loss of control, spillover damage).

**Cost analysis:** Tier 3 typically requires 30–50% of Aisen's pool in one action. He avoids this unless desperate or supported by allies with regen auras.

---

### Tier 4: Extreme (40+ MU per action)

**Use cases:** Reality-altering effects, field-scale forces, civilization-level magic. Requires Expert+ mages or multiple Journeymen in formation.

| Technique | Cost | Example |
|-----------|------|---------|
| **Field inversion** | 50+ MU | Reverse gravity over square block; reshape landscape |
| **Collective lift** | 100+ MU (Archmage+ or formation) | Levitate city structure; raise island from sea |
| **Permanent restructuring** | Variable, extreme | Remake forest terrain; build mountain; reshape ocean current |

**Aisen cannot access Tier 4.**

## PART III: AISEN'S SIGNATURE TECHNIQUES

### 1. Chain Spear Toggling

**Concept:** The chain spear is divided into 5-link **modules**. Aisen tightens or loosens each module telekinetically, changing the weapon's state (rigid staff → flexible whip → detachable segments).

| Action | Cost | Effect | Duration |
|--------|------|--------|----------|
| **Tighten 1 module** | 4 MU | 5-link chain becomes stiff, rigid like a metal rod | Instant; maintained automatically |
| **Loosen 1 module** | 4 MU | 5-link chain becomes flexible like a whip | Instant; requires continuous MU to maintain looseness (0.1 MU/sec) |
| **Disconnect 1 module** | 3 MU (first time); 0.1 MU (maintain separation) | Module detaches; can be manipulated independently | Until re-attached |

**Strategic use:** Early in a fight, Aisen toggles 2–3 modules tight (8–12 MU), keeping the chain staff-like and efficient. When he needs precision or area control, he loosens modules and detaches segments (total 15–20 MU cost).

**Example sequence:**
- Tighten 2 modules: 8 MU (chain is now rigid staff, good for blocking)
- Loosen 1 module + create tether: 4 + 3 = 7 MU (loosened module becomes whip segment; tether maintains it at distance)
- Total: 15 MU (12.5% of pool); sustainable for extended combat

---

### 2. Construct Tether (Detached Chain Segments)

**Concept:** Once a chain segment is detached, Aisen creates a persistent **tether**—a mental link that lets him control the detached segment like a remote limb.

| Aspect | Detail |
|--------|--------|
| **Creation cost** | 3 MU per tether |
| **Maintenance cost** | 0.4 MU per minute per tether |
| **Range** | 4–10 meters (Trained tier; degrades with distance) — see [TELEKINESIS-NUMERIC.md](../../magic-system/TELEKINESIS-NUMERIC.md) for full tier table |
| **Max simultaneous tethers** | 2 (without efficiency loss); 3–4 possible with fatigue penalties |
| **Control resolution** | Can perform Tier 1–2 actions (push, whip, curve) with tethered object |

**Why this matters:** A single detached chain segment can be controlled like a secondary whip or blade. Aisen can tie up 2–3 enemies with simultaneous detached segments while wielding the main chain staff. Cost scales with complexity, but it's extremely efficient for area control.

**Example 2-minute combat:** Create tether to 2 segments (6 MU), maintain for 2 minutes (0.4 × 2 × 2 = 1.6 MU), perform 4 pushes across the tethers (4 MU). Total: ~11.6 MU (9.6% of pool). Add 2 toggles for chain repositioning (8 MU), and the full 2-minute sequence costs ~20 MU (16.6% of pool)—very sustainable.

---

### 3. Boundary Sense Mapping

**Concept:** Active awareness of space and obstacles. Aisen maintains a persistent sensory thread that maps the area around him, giving him tactical intelligence.

| Capacity | Cost | Detail |
|----------|------|--------|
| **Radius** | 30–50 meters | Decreases with Veil thickness / interference |
| **Sustained cost** | 0.2 MU/min | Very cheap; often maintained passively |
| **Resolution** | Rough (distance, size, movement) | Not fine details; cannot identify individuals visually |
| **Multiple probes** | 0.5–1 MU each; 0.2 MU/min each | Can maintain 2–3 probes for specific investigation |

**Tactical advantage:** During combat, Aisen "sees" enemy movements before they happen (through boundary sense). He anticipates dodges, parries, and repositioning. This makes him seem almost prescient—but it's just sensory awareness.

**Cost:** A 10-minute combat with continuous boundary sense costs 2 MU (very cheap). Adding focused probe threads to investigate specific enemies costs 1–2 MU extra.

---

### 4. Momentum Transfer Bridge

**Concept:** Create a temporary telekinetic "bridge" between two moving objects, allowing Aisen to transfer momentum from one to another.

**Example:** Aisen's whip is swinging at low power. He creates a momentum bridge between his own telekinetic push and the whip's tip. His push is added to the whip's swing, dramatically increasing impact.

| Aspect | Detail |
|--------|--------|
| **Creation cost** | 2–4 MU (depends on momentum magnitude) |
| **Duration** | Instantaneous (1 action) |
| **Effect** | Combine two force vectors into one; net force is additive |
| **Backlash** | Creates heat/shock at bridge points; hand may feel burned |

**Why this matters:** Aisen can amplify his weak attacks into powerful ones without spending massive MU on direct force application. It's an **efficiency exploit**—getting extra power through clever manipulation rather than raw casting.

- **Total: 19.4 MU (~16% of pool)**

- 2 chain toggles (maintain formation): 8 MU

## PART V: FAILURE MODES & COUNTERS

### Aisen-Specific Vulnerabilities

| Vulnerability | Cause | Tactical Counter |
|---------------|-------|------------------|
| **Thread collapse** | Strong magical interference; exceeds thread capacity | Opponent casts area dispel; Aisen loses all active tethers at once |
| **Sensory blindness** | Boundary sense jammed by dense Flux or counter-magic | Place in Flux Pocket; Aisen becomes blind to distance, relies on instinct |
| **Momentum bridge fail** | Interrupted mid-action; bridge destabilizes | Create unpredictable physical obstacle; bridge fails and energy rebels (backlash) |
| **Tether snap** | Detached segment struck with extreme force; link breaks | Heavy blow to detached chain segment; tether severs; 3 MU wasted, re-establish costs another 3 MU |
| **Fatigue cascade** | Deep pool depletion; regen slows; penalties stack | Prolong fight past Aisen's 45–60 min sustainable limit; he becomes slower and less precise |

### Defensive Positions

Aisen is vulnerable to:
1. **Pure brute force:** A Tier 3 punch from a rule-breaker overwhelms his Tier 1–2 blocks
2. **Ranged barrages:** Multiple simultaneous attacks exceed thread capacity
3. **Anti-magic zones:** Thick Veil regions where telekinesis is extremely costly
4. **Magical feedback:** Spells designed to damage casters directly (backlash loops)

Aisen is strong against:
1. **Single opponents:** He can manage 1–2 enemies indefinitely through efficiency
2. **Group tactics (3–5 enemies):** Boundary sense + area denial with tethered segments
3. **Reactive scenarios:** Quick toggles and point pushes are very cheap
4. **Sustained fights:** His regen is good; he outlasts most opponents

## PART VI: TRAINING & PROGRESSION

### Years 1–2 (Novice)
- Learn to sense ambient Flux
- Create first threads (unreliable)
- Can maintain boundary sense for ~5 minutes before exhaustion
- Pool: 40–60 MU; cannot attempt Tier 2 without severe backlash

### Years 3–5 (Journeyman)
- Master basic thread control
- Reliable Tier 1 casting; can experiment with Tier 2
- Can maintain multiple threads simultaneously (with effort)
- Pool: 100–150 MU (Aisen at 120 MU is on the high end)

### Years 6–15 (Expert)
- Signature techniques refined; nearly automatic
- Tier 2 casting is routine; Tier 3 is possible but costly
- Can teach others; develop unique applications
- Pool: 200–400 MU

### Years 15+ (Archmage)
- Tier 3–4 casting; reality-level manipulation
- Can lead formations or armies
- Technique development and innovation
- Pool: 400+ MU

**Aisen's position:** Middle Journeyman (5 years post-Academy, age 22). He's highly efficient but not prodigious. His skill comes from practice, not talent.

## SUMMARY

Telekinesis is Aisen's primary weapon not because it's the most powerful magic, but because it's **the most efficient**. Every action costs measurable MU, encouraging tactical choices and resource management. Aisen's genius is recognizing how to spend the least MU for the maximum effect—making him deadly in prolonged conflicts despite not being a prodigy.
