## File Summaries

### 1. HARD-MAGIC-RULES.md (Core System)
**Length:** ~8,000 words  
**Purpose:** Foundational rules of magic in this world

**Covers:**
- Weave vs. Flux duality (physical law vs. magical overlay)
- Mana units (MU) and power tiers (Novice to Archmage)
- Force tiers (Tier 0 subtle → Tier 4 extreme)
- Standard technique costs (telekinesis, healing, etc.)
- Limitations (Veil thickness, interference, control limits)
- Types of magic (ritualistic, improvised, artifact, natural)
- Magic detection and sensing
- Cost-benefit framework

**When to read:**
- Before designing a magic system interaction
- When checking if a spell cost is consistent
- When determining what's possible in a region (Veil thickness)
- As reference for explaining magic to readers

**Key takeaway:** Magic is hard-ruled. Every spell has measurable cost. No free power.

### 2. TELEKINESIS-SYSTEM.md (Aisen's Specialty)
**Length:** ~9,000 words  
**Purpose:** Deep-dive on telekinesis mechanics and Aisen's techniques

**Covers:**
- Thread concept (threads = persistent mental connections)
- Force tiers 0–4 with examples
- Aisen's signature techniques (chain spear toggling, construct tethers, boundary sense, momentum bridges)
- Efficiency metrics and cost breakdowns for common combat scenarios
- Failure modes and counters
- Training progression (years 1–15+)
- Quick reference tables

**When to read:**
- Before any combat scene involving Aisen or telekinesis
- When designing a telekinetic opponent
- For inspiration on how to describe telekinetic effects
- To understand why Aisen is efficient (he's not powerful, just smart)

**Key takeaway:** Telekinesis is Aisen's realm. It's cheap, precise, and tactical. His genius is knowing how to spend the least MU for maximum effect.

### 3. CULTURAL-POWER-SYSTEMS.md (Nation Magic)
**Length:** ~8,500 words  
**Purpose:** How each major nation approaches magic; worldbuilding depth

**Covers:**
- **Heartland Imperial System:** Institutional, formation-based, bureaucratic
- **Island Fortress System:** Personal, duel-based, honor-driven
- **Celestial System:** Philosophical, meditation-based, understanding-focused
- **Sahelian System:** Relational, spirit-bonding, cooperative
- **Andean System:** Birthright, instinctive, high-Flux regions
- **Federation System:** Contractual, transactional, agreement-based
- Comparative analysis table
- How Aisen fits into the spectrum
- Why he's strong vs. each system; where he's vulnerable

**When to read:**
- Before writing scenes involving mages from other nations
- When designing antagonists (choose a system, follow its rules)
- For understanding political/cultural tensions around magic
- To show that Aisen's methods are unusual (he's neither Heartland institutional nor Island Fortress individualistic)

**Key takeaway:** Magic philosophy shapes nations. Understanding a nation's magic system explains their values, laws, and conflicts.

### 4. RESONANCE-CRYSTALS.md (Magical Artifacts)
**Length:** ~7,500 words  
**Purpose:** How magical infrastructure works; artifact mechanics

**Covers:**
- Crystal types and properties (storage, amplification, transmission, sensing)
- Natural vs. engineered crystals
- Aelfar legacy networks (pre-Sundering)
- Modern crystal networks by nation
- Personal crystal items (emergency reserves, weapon integration)
- Crystal crafting and recharging mechanics
- Crystal degradation and maintenance
- Crystal tactics in combat
- Aisen's crystal access and limitations

**When to read:**
- When describing magical infrastructure or sieges
- If Aisen needs emergency mana (crystal reserves)
- For worldbuilding flavor (Aelfar ruins, crystal networks)
- To understand limits of magical support systems (crystals are finite, can be destroyed)

**Key takeaway:** Crystals are magical infrastructure. They enable civilization-scale magic but create vulnerabilities. Aisen's independence from crystals is another mark of his genius.

### 5. MAGIC-COSTS-AND-CONSEQUENCES.md (High Stakes)
**Length:** ~7,000 words  
**Purpose:** Magical costs beyond MU; consequences shape character arcs

**Covers:**
- Six types of costs: mana, fatigue, environment, personal sacrifice, social, medical
- Fatigue progression system (fresh → mild → moderate → severe → critical)
- Specific costs (healing = aging, memory magic = lost memories, blood sacrifice = death)
- Environmental consequences (heat, gravity, void zones, temporal distortion)
- Consequence scales (immediate, short-term, medium-term, long-term)
- Aisen-specific consequences (thread collapse, tether severance, efficiency trap, psychological numbness)
- Using costs as plot device
- Aisen's consequence arc through story phases

**When to read:**
- When raising stakes in conflict (magic use = permanent damage)
- Before any healing scene (reader should understand the cost)
- For exploring character trauma and sacrifice
- To justify why casters don't just "cast harder" (costs become unsustainable)
- Before climactic magical sequences

**Key takeaway:** Magic has weight. Every powerful spell demands payment. Understanding costs creates narrative depth and emotional stakes.

## Checklists for Common Writing Tasks

### Writing a Combat Scene with Aisen

1. **Before scene:** Determine Aisen's current MU pool and fatigue level
2. **Opening:** Note boundary sense status (is he "aware" of environment?)
3. **For each action:** 
   - Determine technique (toggle, tether, push, etc.)
   - Look up MU cost in [TELEKINESIS-SYSTEM.md](TELEKINESIS-SYSTEM.md#part-ii-force-tiers-for-telekinesis)
   - Deduct from pool
   - Track fatigue (if >60%, apply precision penalties)
4. **Escalation:** As pool depletes, Aisen slows down; desperation increases
5. **Conclusion:** Check recovery time; does fatigue linger into next scene?

### Writing a Healing Magic Scene

1. **Before healing:** Determine severity of injury (minor vs. mortal)
2. **Healing cost:** Look up cost in [MAGIC-COSTS-AND-CONSEQUENCES.md](MAGIC-COSTS-AND-CONSEQUENCES.md#healing-reversing-entropy)
3. **personal cost:** Healer ages ~5 years per major wound
4. **Narrative impact:** Show the aging (hair color, joint stiffness, weariness)
5. **Emotional beat:** Healer is paying a permanent price; make reader feel that weight

### Writing an Enemy Mage

1. **Choose cultural system:** [CULTURAL-POWER-SYSTEMS.md](CULTURAL-POWER-SYSTEMS.md) picks their power philosophy
2. **Determine specialization:** What's their signature technique?
3. **Set their limitations:** What drains their MU fastest? What costs are they avoiding?
4. **Weakness:** Every system has gaps. How does Aisen exploit them?
5. **Equipment:** Do they have resonance crystals? What amplifiers? (see [RESONANCE-CRYSTALS.md](RESONANCE-CRYSTALS.md))

### Designing a Magical Conflict (City-Scale)

1. **Determine primary magic system:** Which culture controls the region?
2. **Infrastructure:** What crystal networks exist? (see [RESONANCE-CRYSTALS.md](RESONANCE-CRYSTALS.md#part-iii-crystal-networks-infrastructure))
3. **Costs at scale:** When hundreds cast, what environmental costs occur? (see [MAGIC-COSTS-AND-CONSEQUENCES.md](MAGIC-COSTS-AND-CONSEQUENCES.md#type-3-environmental-consequences-entropy-debt))
4. **Vulnerability:** How can one person (Aisen) disrupt city-scale magic? (crystals, caster formation, morale)

## Common Questions Answered

**Q: How much MU does a telekinetic push cost?**  
A: 1 MU (Tier 1). See [TELEKINESIS-SYSTEM.md](TELEKINESIS-SYSTEM.md#tier-1-light-1-5-mu-per-action).

**Q: Can Aisen cast in a Flux Pocket?**  
A: Yes, but MU costs are +30–80% higher. See [HARD-MAGIC-RULES.md](HARD-MAGIC-RULES.md#veil-thickness-effects).

**Q: What happens if a healer heals too many people?**  
A: They age rapidly. Early: gray hair, arthritis. Later: frailty, organs fail. See [MAGIC-COSTS-AND-CONSEQUENCES.md](MAGIC-COSTS-AND-CONSEQUENCES.md#healing-reversing-entropy).

**Q: Can Aisen save someone with a healing spell?**  
A: Technically yes, but he'd age 5+ years. A better question: Why wouldn't he? See Aisen's character motivation.

**Q: How does Heartland formation magic work?**  
A: 30+ mages link through resonance circles; amplify single spell by 10x. See [CULTURAL-POWER-SYSTEMS.md](CULTURAL-POWER-SYSTEMS.md#formation-enhancement).

**Q: What's the strongest magic Aisen can use?**  
A: Tier 3 (15–40 MU) is his realistic ceiling. Tier 4 requires depleting his entire pool; not sustainable. See [TELEKINESIS-SYSTEM.md](TELEKINESIS-SYSTEM.md#tier-3-heavy-15-40-mu-per-action).

**Q: Can crystals break in combat?**  
A: Yes. Physical impact or magical backlash can shatter crystals. See [RESONANCE-CRYSTALS.md](RESONANCE-CRYSTALS.md#crystal-degradation).

**Q: What's a Void Zone?**  
A: A region where magic is permanently broken due to repeated inefficient casting. No one can cast there. See [MAGIC-COSTS-AND-CONSEQUENCES.md](MAGIC-COSTS-AND-CONSEQUENCES.md#void-zones-repeated-magic-misuse).
