# Institutional Systems by Nation

## Purpose

The previous audit identified that no nation had fully developed judicial, healthcare, communication, or military logistics systems. This document fills those gaps with realistic, culture-consistent institutional detail.

---

## I. JUDICIAL SYSTEMS

### Valdimere: Magistrate Courts

**Structure**: Emperor → High Tribunal (12 senior magistrates, capital) → Provincial Courts (1 per province, magistrate + 2 assessors) → District Courts (1 per district, single magistrate) → Village Arbitration (elder mediation, non-binding until magistrate confirms).

**How it works**:
- All legal proceedings require written documentation. Oral testimony is transcribed; unsigned testimony is inadmissible.
- Cases ascend by severity: village arbitration handles disputes under 50 marks; district courts handle criminal matters and disputes up to 500 marks; provincial courts handle capital crimes and major civil cases; High Tribunal handles cases of state interest.
- **Class bias**: Magistrates are Tier 2-3. Tier 4 defendants face judges who socially outrank them. Tier 5 defendants literally cannot file cases (no documentation = no legal standing).
- **Corruption mechanism**: Magistrate appointment is theoretically merit-based but patronage-influenced. A magistrate who rules against their patron's interests gets reassigned to a frontier district (effectively punishment).

**Distinctive features**:
- **Precedent archives**: Every ruling is recorded and archived. Lawyers cite precedent. This creates a slow, conservative legal system that resists change but provides predictability.
- **Inquisitorial system**: Magistrates investigate; there are no defense attorneys. The magistrate is theoretically neutral. Practically, investigation resources favor the prosecution.
- **Flux severance**: The ultimate punishment. Reserved for heresy and treason. Magical practitioners have their Flux access permanently severed through a ritual that seals their tether. Functionally, it's the magical equivalent of execution — magic-dependent individuals rarely survive long.

**Justice gap**: Approximately 40% of criminal acts go unreported because victims lack documentation to file claims. The system functions well for those inside it and is completely absent for those outside it.

### Shukei: Lord's Justice

**Structure**: Shogun → Daimyo courts → Samurai adjudication → Commoner mediation (informal).

**How it works**:
- **Samurai vs. commoner**: Two entirely separate legal systems. Samurai disputes are resolved through honor-code adjudication (mediated by a senior samurai or the daimyo). Commoner disputes go through village headman mediation.
- **Samurai courts**: The daimyo hears cases personally or delegates to a senior retainer. Evidence includes testimony, physical proof, and character witnesses. Honor is weighed: a samurai's word against a commoner's word always prevails unless physical evidence contradicts.
- **Commoner courts**: Informal. Village headman mediates. If mediation fails, the dispute escalates to the local samurai administrator, who typically decides quickly and without appeal.
- **Cross-class cases**: A commoner who accuses a samurai of wrongdoing faces a system designed to dismiss such claims. A samurai who harms a commoner faces no legal consequence unless the daimyo personally intervenes.

**Distinctive features**:
- **Honor execution** (seppuku): A samurai found guilty of serious dishonor may choose ritual suicide to preserve family honor. This is "mercy" — the alternative is execution by the daimyo, which disgraces the entire family.
- **Masterless judgment**: Ronin exist outside the legal system entirely. They can be killed by any samurai without consequence. They cannot bring cases. They have no legal personhood.
- **No appeal**: Daimyo decisions are final. The Shogun can theoretically override but almost never does (it would undermine daimyo authority).

**Justice gap**: Commoners have no meaningful legal protection against samurai. The system acknowledges this and calls it "natural order."

### Therryn: Examination Court System

**Structure**: Emperor → Supreme Court (Grand Council members sitting as judges) → Provincial Appeal Courts → District Examination Courts → Local Scholar-Arbiters.

**How it works**:
- Judges are examination-passed scholar-officials. Legal reasoning is treated as intellectual exercise — the most elegant legal argument carries weight alongside factual evidence.
- **Written proceedings**: Everything is written. Oral argument supplements written briefs. Cases can take months because both parties produce extensive written analyses.
- **Precedent AND principle**: Unlike Valdimere's precedent-only system, Therryn courts apply precedent modified by "first principles analysis." A judge can override precedent if they derive a more logically consistent ruling from foundational principles. This creates intellectual excitement and legal unpredictability.

**Distinctive features**:
- **Public debate**: Major cases are argued publicly. Spectators attend like lecture audiences. A brilliant legal argument can make a lawyer's career regardless of the case outcome.
- **Examination-based access**: Only examination-passed scholars can argue cases. Commoners who can't afford representation get assigned "public scholars" (typically junior exam-passers with minimal experience).
- **Intellectual property courts**: Unique to Therryn. Handle patent disputes, plagiarism accusations, and knowledge-ownership claims. These courts consume enormous resources.

**Justice gap**: Legal complexity means only the educated can navigate the system. A farmer with a legitimate complaint against a local official faces a legal process they literally cannot understand without a translator.

### Umara: Consensus Resolution

**Structure**: No courts. No judges. No formal legal system.

**How it works**:
- **Community mediation**: Disputes are brought to the elder council. Both parties speak. Elders consult historical precedent (griot memory). Community members contribute perspectives.
- **Restoration focus**: The goal is not punishment but relationship repair. The question isn't "who's guilty?" but "how do we restore the broken relationship?"
- **Griot testimony**: Griots provide historical context ("The last time two families disputed over water rights, here's what happened"). This functions as precedent but is oral, interpretive, and potentially biased.
- **Escalation**: If community resolution fails, a mediator from a neighboring community is invited. If that fails, a regional council convenes. Each escalation level is shameful — it means the community couldn't handle its own problems.

**Distinctive features**:
- **Exile as ultimate punishment**: The worst thing that can happen to an Umaran is ejection from the relationship network. In a society where identity is relational, exile is social death.
- **No incarceration**: There are no prisons. Punishment takes the form of obligation (increased duties, public apology, compensation through labor) or banishment.
- **Restorative rituals**: Formal reconciliation ceremonies witnessed by elders, recorded by griots. Both parties acknowledge the breach and agree on restoration terms.

**Justice gap**: The powerful (elders, merchant princes) have disproportionate influence on "consensus." True consensus is rare; manufactured consensus is common. Those without strong network-relationships have no effective voice.

### Celestial Kingdom: Monastic Tribunal

**Structure**: Senior monk council → Regional monastery courts → Community lay-monk arbitration.

**How it works**: Spiritual authority adjudicates. Disputes are framed as spiritual imbalances. The guilty party has "lost their way" and the sentence is typically penance (meditation, labor, pilgrimage). Serious crimes (murder, treason) are treated as spiritual catastrophe requiring extended isolation and supervised meditation.

**Justice gap**: Lay grievances against monastic authority are adjudicated by monastic authority. No independent review.

---

### Federation: Contract Arbitration

**Structure**: Federal Arbitration Assembly → Canton Courts → Licensed Arbitrators → Contract-Specified Dispute Resolution Clauses.

**How it works**: Most contracts include their own dispute resolution mechanisms. When they don't, licensed arbitrators (examination-passed legal specialists) mediate. Arbitration decisions are magically binding — the same enchantment that binds contracts binds rulings.

**Justice gap**: The arbitration system is as good as the contract. Well-drafted contracts protect both parties. Poorly-drafted contracts (typically signed by the less sophisticated party) protect the drafter.

---

### Andean Alliance: Council Judgment

**Structure**: Inter-band council → Band council → Community assembly.

**How it works**: Disputes between individuals go to community assembly. Disputes between communities go to band council. Disputes between bands go to inter-band council. Consensus required at every level.

**Justice gap**: Altitude prejudice infects judgments. Peak community claims tend to prevail over valley community claims when councils meet at peak-altitude locations (which they usually do, because sacred sites are on peaks).

## II. HEALTHCARE SYSTEMS

### Valdimere
- **Licensed healers**: Magical healing requires registration. Licensed healers work through hospitals (every provincial capital has one) and guild-approved practices.
- **Unlicensed healers**: Hedge healers, herbalists, folk practitioners. Technically illegal if they use magical techniques. Tolerated in rural areas where licensed healers won't go.
- **Quality**: Licensed healing is excellent but expensive and rationed. Tier 1-3 get prompt access. Tier 4 waits. Tier 5 doesn't exist to the system.
- **Disability**: Documented. Magical disability (Flux severance aftermath, entropy damage) is classified separately from physical disability. Disabled persons retain their tier but face employment barriers.
- **Mental health**: Not recognized as medical. Behavioral problems are moral failures. Trauma is treated with rest and prayer.

### Shukei
- **Warrior medicine**: Battlefield surgery, bone-setting, wound treatment. Excellent for combat injuries. Warriors receive immediate, skilled treatment.
- **Commoner medicine**: Herbalists and folk healers. Quality varies enormously. No standardization.
- **Disability**: Warriors who are disabled in service are honored but retired. Their family receives clan support. Commoners who are disabled receive community aid if the family requests. No formal system.
- **Mental health**: Warriors with combat trauma are expected to meditate through it. Those who can't are considered weak. Suicide among traumatized warriors is normalized.

### Therryn
- **Scholar-physicians**: Medicine is a scholarly discipline. Physicians pass examinations. Research-oriented.
- **Quality**: Best theoretical medicine in the world. Therryn scholars have mapped anatomy, pharmaceutical properties, and disease transmission accurately. Implementation is uneven — rural areas have few physicians.
- **Disability**: Intellectual disability especially stigmatized (it challenges the meritocratic foundation). Physical disability treated medically if treatable, accommodated if not.
- **Mental health**: Recognized as a medical category (advanced for this world). Treatment involves rest, herbal compounds, and structured intellectual engagement. Limited effectiveness but the conceptual framework exists.

### Umara
- **Community healers**: Every community has at least one healer. Training through apprenticeship. Integration of herbal, magical (spirit-well communion), and psychological treatment.
- **Quality**: Highly effective within their knowledge base. Limited by available resources and seasonal constraints (spirit-well access varies).
- **Disability**: Fully integrated into community obligation network. Disabled persons receive support proportional to their need. Their obligations are adjusted, not removed (maintaining dignity and participation).
- **Mental health**: Treated as relational disturbance. Healing involves restoring broken relationships, community reintegration, and griot counseling (narrative therapy — the griot helps the person reframe their experience through storytelling).

## III. COMMUNICATION SYSTEMS

### Valdimere
- **Imperial courier service**: Mounted riders on maintained roads. Core provinces: 3-5 day message delivery. Frontier: 1-3 weeks.
- **Magical communication**: Licensed practitioners can send short-range Flux signals (10-mile radius). Expensive. Reserved for military and government.
- **Tavern networks**: Informal information exchange through traveler gossip. Surprisingly effective for commercial intelligence but unreliable for accuracy.

### Shukei
- **Messenger runners**: Trained runners between daimyo seats. Fast (island geography = shorter distances) but vulnerable to interception.
- **Signal fires**: Mountain-to-mountain visual communication. Fast for alerts; useless for complex messages.
- **Isolation Ministry censorship**: All communications theoretically monitored. In practice, only inter-island communications are surveilable. Intra-island conversation is effectively uncontrolled.

### Therryn
- **Printed dispatches**: The printing press enables mass communication. Government announcements are printed and distributed through scholar networks.
- **Postal system**: Most sophisticated in the world. Literate population + printing technology + maintained roads = reliable mail service. Most citizens can send and receive written messages.
- **Scholarly journals**: Regular publications that distribute research findings. Functions as academic communication network.

### Umara
- **Griot network**: Griots travel between communities carrying information. Slow but high-fidelity (griots are trained to transmit accurately).
- **Drum communication**: Long-distance drum signals for urgent alerts. Limited vocabulary but fast. Effective range: 5-20 miles depending on terrain.
- **Trade route gossip**: Merchants carry news along trade routes. Faster than griots for commercial information, less reliable for accuracy.

## IV. MILITARY LOGISTICS

### Valdimere
- **Standing army**: Professional soldiers organized into provincial regiments. Standing force: ~200,000 (3% of population).
- **Supply chain**: State-managed grain reserves, military foundries, standardized equipment. Logistics administered by military bureaucracy separate from civilian government.
- **Conscription**: Available but rarely used. Voluntary recruitment preferred (better morale). Conscription deployed only in existential crisis.
- **Weakness**: Bureaucratic supply chain means equipment reaches frontier 2-4 weeks after request. By then, the tactical situation has changed.

### Shukei
- **Clan levies**: Each daimyo maintains a warrior force. National military = sum of clan levies called by Shogun.
- **Supply chain**: Clan-managed. Each lord provisions their own warriors. Quality varies enormously between wealthy and poor clans.
- **Mobilization**: Fast (personal loyalty = immediate response) but coordination between clans is slow (inter-daimyo rivalry impedes unified command).
- **Weakness**: No centralized logistics. A campaign that outlasts one season exhausts smaller clans' supplies.

### Therryn
- **Professional army with scholar command**: Soldiers recruited from Tier 4. Officers examination-passed or merit-promoted.
- **Supply chain**: The best in the world. Mathematical optimization of supply routes, warehouse positioning, and resource allocation.
- **Siege capability**: Superior. Therryn siege engineers calculate fortification stress points mathematically. Their siege weapons are precision instruments.
- **Weakness**: Slow to deploy (strategic committee deliberation). Excellent at prepared warfare, poor at reactive combat.

### Umara
- **No standing military**: Warriors emerge from communities for specific conflicts and return to civilian life.
- **Supply chain**: Non-existent in the conventional sense. Each warrior provisions themselves. Communities provide food along the march route through obligation networks.
- **Guerrilla capability**: Excellent. Individual initiative, terrain knowledge, and decentralized command make Umaran forces nearly impossible to pin down.
- **Weakness**: Cannot sustain prolonged conventional warfare. No siege capability. No heavy equipment.
