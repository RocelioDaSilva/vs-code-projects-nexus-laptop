# Technology & Artifacts Documentation

This folder organizes documentation of technological systems, magical artifacts, innovations, and mechanical devices within the Chain Weapon Story world.

## Purpose

Store documentation for:
- **Artifact catalogs** (magical items, their creation, properties)
- **Technology types** (crafted vs. magical; practical vs. ceremonial)
- **Innovation systems** (how technology develops in different nations)
- **Weapon mechanics** (chain spears, specialized tools)
- **Magical artifacts** (Resonance crystals, enchanted items)
- **Creation processes** (how are artifacts made?)
- **Technology limitations** (what CAN'T be created?)

## Technology by Nation

### Shukei Technology
- **Specialization:** Trade, craftsmanship, practical engineering
- **Signature items:** High-quality standard weapons (chain spears, swords), merchant scales, durable travel equipment
- **Innovation rate:** Steady incremental improvement
- **Military advantage:** Reliable, well-made standard equipment

### Valdimere Technology
- **Specialization:** Fortification, military engineering, durability
- **Signature items:** Heavy armor, siege equipment, mountain-adapted tools, anti-magic artifact locks
- **Innovation rate:** Effective in established domains
- **Military advantage:** Defensive structures, sustainable weapons

### Therryn Technology
- **Specialization:** Maritime, navigation, water systems
- **Signature items:** Ships, navigation instruments, water purification, marine creature gear
- **Innovation rate:** Island-specific innovations
- **Military advantage:** Naval capability, coastal fortifications

## Documentation To Add

When developing technological systems:
- [ ] Complete artifact catalogue (name, creator, properties, location)
- [ ] Weapon specifications (chain spear variations, creation process, maintenance)
- [ ] Material properties (what materials exist, what are they good for?)
- [ ] Creation limitations (what can't be made? Why?)
- [ ] Artifact degradation (do magical items decay? How?)
- [ ] Cost & rarity matrix (how expensive/available is technology?)
- [ ] Innovation timeline (when did key inventions emerge?)

## Scene Considerations

Technology should:
- **Feel grounded** (magical but with limits and costs)
- **Create story possibilities** (enable new plots, create new obstacles)
- **Show cultural values** (technology reflects what nations prioritize)
- **Have maintenance requirements** (nothing is free or permanent)
- **Evolve with story** (technology state at start vs. end differs?)
- **Support characterization** (what someone uses shows who they are)

## Key Questions for Technology

When developing tech systems:
- [ ] What is the magical power source? (Flux, Resonance crystals, other?)
- [ ] Who can create this technology? (Artisans, mages, both?)
- [ ] How long does creation take?
- [ ] What are the failure modes? (What happens if it breaks?)
- [ ] Can it be repaired or does it need replacement?
- [ ] Is it mass-producible or one-of-a-kind?
- [ ] Does it have an innate cost (to create, to use, to maintain)?
- [ ] What prevents this from solving all problems?
