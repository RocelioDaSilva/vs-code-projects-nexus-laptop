# Cross-Cultural Class Mobility Comparison

## The Central Question

**How do people move from lower to upper tiers? Why does it differ by nation?**

---

## Mobility Pathways by Nation

### VALDIMERE: Bureaucratic Progression

**Ascent Path**:
1. **Tier 4→3**: Military service (successful soldiers promoted) or guild apprenticeship
2. **Tier 3→2**: Demonstrate exceptional competence; seek noble patronage; bureaucratic appointment
3. **Tier 2→1**: Intermarriage with nobility (rare) or imperial appointment (rarer)

**Barriers**:
- Documentation requirement (tier 5: no records = legally non-existent)
- Guild gatekeeping (who can apprentice?)
- Noble patronage necessity (can't advance without sponsor)
- Bureaucratic slowness (advancement possible but glacially slow)

**Advantage for Ordinary People**: System theoretically meritocratic (competence noticed eventually)  
**Disadvantage**: Bureaucratic bottlenecks; institutional inertia  
**Time to Ascend**: Tier 4→3 = 10-20 years (military service); Tier 3→2 = 20-30 years  

**Key Example**: Harald (military officer) discredited → expelled → works outside system → becomes teacher (parallel hierarchy formed)

---

### SHUKEI: Blood Determines Path

**Ascent Path**:
1. **Tier 4→3**: Nearly impossible; maybe 1 in 10,000 (exceptional talent + daimyo notice + official adoption)
2. **Tier 3→2**: Possible only through daimyo appointment (military service) or samurai adoption (rare)
3. **Tier 2→1**: Only through birth into samurai family; no external route

**Barriers**:
- Birth hierarchy (starting position pre-determined)
- Samurai adoption process (extremely selective; requires extraordinary circumstances)
- Daimyo discretion absolute (caprice as powerful as policy)
- Honor code prevents most tier 4 from attempting (would cause scandal)

**Advantage for Ordinary People**: If samurai need skilled worker (craftsperson, scholar), adoption possible  
**Disadvantage**: System fundamentally designed to prevent mobility; stability built on immobility  
**Time to Ascend**: Tier 4→3 = if occurs, sudden (adoption) not gradual; Tier 3→2 = 20+ years of military service  

**Alternative Path—Tier 5 (Ronin)**: Disgraced samurai become masterless; create parallel meritocracy (skill-based authority)

**Key Example**: Ronin warrior system allows progression based on combat ability alone (threatens hierarchy by existing outside it)

---

### THERRYN: Examination System (Theoretically Meritocratic)

**Ascent Path**:
1. **Tier 4→3**: Pass examination (requires literacy, education, test-taking skill)
2. **Tier 3→2**: Pass higher examinations OR business success as merchant
3. **Tier 2→1**: Further examination success OR imperial appointment

**Barriers**:
- Education requirement (who can afford schooling?)
- Examination structure (tests designed to favor literate, wealthy)
- Merchant-class prejudice (wealth doesn't equal political power)
- Generational advancement (peasant→tier 3 possible; peasant→tier 2 unlikely in one lifetime)

**Advantage for Ordinary People**: System explicitly meritocratic; examination open to anyone (in theory)  
**Disadvantage**: Class bias embedded in education access; wealthy children better prepared for exams  
**Time to Ascend**: Tier 4→3 = 5-10 years (if passes exam quickly); Tier 3→2 = often generational  

**Class Paradox**: Merchant class wealthy but politically powerless; scholar-officials poor but politically powerful

**Key Example**: Poor scholar studies for 20 years; passes examination; becomes minor official; family gains respect; children could advance further

---

### UMARA: Relationship-Based (Patronage & Adoption)

**Ascent Path**:
1. **Tier 4→3**: Recognition by griot, merchant prince, or spiritual leader; adoption into network
2. **Tier 3→2**: Advancement within network through demonstrated excellence or inherited status
3. **Tier 2→1**: Not really "ascending" but becoming center of own network (emerges organically)

**Barriers**:
- Patronage requirement (need someone to vouch for you)
- Relationship-building necessity (cannot climb alone; must be integrated)
- Generational networks (families have connections; outsiders don't)
- Diaspora status (refugees have fewer connections; harder to advance)

**Advantage for Ordinary People**: If talented + personality-compatible, rapid advancement possible (relationship-driven)  
**Disadvantage**: System depends on luck (who notices you?) + personality (can you build relationships?)  
**Time to Ascend**: Tier 4→3 = rapid if recognized (months-years); or never if unnoticed (decades working without advancement)

**Alternative**: Exceptional storytellers, healers, or traders noticed quickly; integrated into upper tiers

**Key Example**: Amara (griot family) born into Tier 2; through skill + family training, becomes intelligence coordinator (functionally Tier 1)

## Comparative Ease of Ascent

| Path | Ease | Speed | Stability | Fairness |
|------|------|-------|-----------|----------|
| **Valdimere** | Medium | Slow (20+ yrs) | High (official) | Medium (merit + patronage) |
| **Shukei** | Very Hard | Extremely slow or sudden | High (rigid) | Low (birth-based) |
| **Therryn** | Medium | Medium (10-20 yrs) | High (merit-tested) | Medium (education-biased) |

## Resistance Recruitment by Nation

### VALDIMERE
- **Target**: Ambitious tier 4 (military) or frustrated tier 3 (merchants, guilds)
- **Appeal**: "Prove your merit; we'll recognize you differently than bureaucracy"
- **Risk**: Defection risk if bureaucracy offers advancement instead

### SHUKEI
- **Target**: Ronin, outcasts, rejected samurai; ambitious tier 3
- **Appeal**: "Your birth doesn't determine worth; join our meritocracy"
- **Risk**: Loyalty uncertainty (system that rejects hierarchy might collapse into chaos)

### THERRYN
- **Target**: Scholar-reformers, merchant princes, ambitious peasants
- **Appeal**: "Education for all; examination system for all; transparent advancement"
- **Risk**: Covenant could offer to reform the examination system (double-agent temptation)

### UMARA
- **Target**: Talented outsiders, ambitious traders, spiritual revolutionaries
- **Appeal**: "Your relationships matter; build network with us"
- **Risk**: Network-based loyalty might be compromised (individual patronage conflicts)

## Post-War Consideration

**Question**: What comes after victory? Integrated mobility system across nations?

**Options**:
1. **Federalist**: Each nation maintains own system (high treaty)
2. **Synthesized**: Blend best mobility practices (examination + relationship + merit + opportunity)
3. **Revolutionary**: Eliminate class entirely; horizontal organization  
4. **Regressive**: Return to pre-war systems (safest for entrenched powers)

**Theme**: Winning the war is easy (defined enemy); building fair system afterward is hard (requires reimagining everything).
