# Federation — Folklore & Flux‑Geology

Overview

The Federation treats power as contractual and transactional. Their folklore often anthropomorphizes agreements as living things—debts, promises, and signatures become entities that must be fed.

Geology & The Veil

- Federation regions are notable for artificially stabilized nodes: contract‑vaults and binding stones that concentrate Flux in regulated pockets. These pockets are intentionally narrow and managed, which changes the ecology: creatures that can exploit the "paper trail" or live off signatures thrive.
- Because pockets are engineered, they produce predictable, bounded anomalies rather than wild Tears; these sites yield beings that embody law and obligation.

How folklore maps to physics

- Myths about "breakers of oaths" encode dangers of contract abuse; rituals are part legal formality, part mana mitigation.
- Contractual nets often have physical anchors (vault nodes) that, when damaged, spill controlled flux into the soil, producing unusual fauna.

Major Folklore Beasts (examples)

1) Clause Wyrms
- Description: Serpentine creatures born from broken promises; they gnaw at signatures and consume obligations.
- Flux Affinity: Medium (they digest ethereal contracts to grow)
- Mechanics: Where Clause Wyrms eat a contract, the legal effect is literally erased until the binding archive reasserts itself (a magical audit ritual can restore the lost clause).
- Narrative hooks: A powerful land grant disappears overnight; Aisen is hired to retrieve the stolen clause from the Wyrm's lair.

2) Notary Sentinels
- Description: Construct guardians that enforce the letter of a contract; immobile until an oath is invoked.
- Flux Affinity: Low (they are run on stored contractual MU)
- Mechanics: They can immobilize parties who breach terms by imposing literal shackles—useful as both deterrent and story obstacle.
- Narrative hooks: A bargaining scene turns violent when a Notary Sentinel activates because one party slanders the other's oath.

3) Ledger Moths
- Description: Pale insects that feast on signatures and can cause records to become unreadable or to animate as spectral witnesses.
- Flux Affinity: Low–Medium
- Mechanics: In large numbers they animate marginalia into hallucinations or phantom testimony.
- Narrative hooks: A court case is derailed by Ledger Moths—Aisen must find the nest and the informal ledger that proves innocence.

Practical notes for writers

- Federation folklore supports plots about reputation, debt, and the moral cost of deals. Magic and law are intertwined; violating ceremonial form often has real magical side effects.
