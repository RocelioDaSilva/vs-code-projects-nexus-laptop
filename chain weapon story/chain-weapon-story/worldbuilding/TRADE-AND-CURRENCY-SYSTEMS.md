# Trade Routes, Currency & Cross-National Economics

**Purpose**: Consolidate economic worldbuilding scattered across nation files into one reference. For per-nation economic models, see [SOCIETAL-SYSTEMS-COMPARATIVE.md](SOCIETAL-SYSTEMS-COMPARATIVE.md).

## Currency Systems

| Nation | Primary Currency | Basis | Notes |
|--------|-----------------|-------|-------|
| **Valdimere** | Crown Mark (stamped coin) | Gold/silver standard | State-minted; counterfeiting = treason; merchant guilds handle change |
| **Shukei** | Koku (rice measure) | Commodity-backed | One koku = ~180 liters rice; prestige economy runs parallel to material |
| **Celestial Kingdom** | Scholar's Seal (paper note) | State-backed fiat | Backed by examination system's credibility; forgery-warded with minor Flux |
| **Therryn-Umara** | Cowrie Shell & Verbal Contract | Trust-backed | Griot networks verify transactions orally; physical currency = supplementary |
| **Andean Alliance** | Quipu Knot (labor credit) | Labor-time standard | No physical money; obligations recorded in knotted rope; communal ledger |
| **Mercantile Federation** | Trade Florin (gold coin) | Gold standard | Most widely accepted cross-border currency; Federation banks issue credit |

### Cross-Border Exchange
- The **Trade Florin** functions as the de facto international currency due to Mercantile Federation banking networks.
- **Valdimere-Shukei** trade uses direct commodity exchange (weapons for silk) with Florin settlement for imbalances.
- **Therryn-Umara** griot-verified contracts are accepted by most nations, but require a griot present for both parties.
- **Andean quipu** labor-credits are not recognized outside the Alliance; Andean traders convert to Florins at border markets.

## Major Trade Routes

### The Heartland Corridor (Valdimere ↔ Celestial Kingdom)
- **Goods**: Valdimere exports weapons, processed metals, military equipment. Celestial Kingdom exports knowledge-artifacts, alchemical ingredients, precision instruments.
- **Route**: Overland through Academy territory (which collects transit tariffs).
- **Narrative relevance**: This route is how the Academy funds itself; disrupting it threatens institutional power.

### The Spice Sea Route (Therryn-Umara ↔ Mercantile Federation)
- **Goods**: Spices, textiles, griot-services (storytelling, contract verification) from Therryn-Umara. Manufactured goods, banking services from Federation.
- **Route**: Coastal shipping with island resupply at Shukei ports.
- **Narrative relevance**: Amara's intelligence network piggybacks on these merchant ships. Trade gossip = intelligence.

### The Highland Path (Andean Alliance ↔ Valdimere)
- **Goods**: Raw materials (minerals, rare earth, highland herbs) from Andean. Military contracts, protection services from Valdimere.
- **Route**: Mountain passes; seasonal (impassable in winter).
- **Narrative relevance**: Tupac's people control the passes. Economic leverage = military leverage.

### The Silk Bridge (Shukei ↔ Celestial Kingdom)
- **Goods**: Silk, spirit-bound weapons, martial training from Shukei. Scholarship, examination manuals, magical theory from Celestial.
- **Route**: Sea crossing; small ports; tightly controlled.
- **Narrative relevance**: Rin's spy network uses cultural exchange as cover for intelligence work.

## Economic Consequences in the Story

### How the Covenant Controls Through Economics
- Tax collection franchised to local strongmen who keep a percentage → creates loyalty without direct oversight.
- Monopoly on magical material supply (resonance crystals, Flux-conductive metals) → practitioners dependent on Covenant supply chain.
- Academy transit tariffs fund Covenant operations indirectly → even nations that resist the Covenant fund it through trade.

### How the Resistance Disrupts Economics
- Amara's merchant contacts redirect supply chains around Covenant chokepoints.
- Pieter's financial knowledge identifies which noble families are leveraged (debt = vulnerability to recruitment).
- Andean refusal to export minerals starves Covenant weapon production.

### Post-War Economic Challenges (Ch 71-85)
- Trade Florin loses value as Federation fragments → currency instability.
- Reconstruction requires coordinated resource allocation without centralized authority.
- Black markets from the war era resist integration into legitimate economy.

## Writing Notes
- Characters should reference economic realities naturally: Pieter counts costs, Amara mentions trade routes, Tupac references highland-pass leverage.
- Economic pressure drives political decisions — nobles ally with whoever guarantees trade access.
- The Covenant's economic power is more dangerous than its military power. Destroying the army doesn't destroy the trade monopolies.
