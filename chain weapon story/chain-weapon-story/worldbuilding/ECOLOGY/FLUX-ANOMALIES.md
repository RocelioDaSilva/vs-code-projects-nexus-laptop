# Flux Anomalies & Geological Mechanisms

Overview

Flux is not uniformly distributed. Geological structures, ancient Aelfar work, and human rituals create patterns of thin and thick Veil. Understanding these mechanisms explains why magical beasts appear where they do.

Key anomaly types

1) Flux Tears
- Origin: Large ritual overloads (the Sundering) or catastrophic failures in engineered crystal networks.
- Behavior: Large, persistent rifts; raw Flux pours uncontrolled, producing wild magic zones and long‑term mutation sites.
- Ecology: Flux Tears produce high mortality followed by new stable ecosystems dominated by mutated fauna and resilient flora.

2) Flux Pockets
- Origin: Localized build‑up in seams, volcanic vents, or abandoned Aelfar caches.
- Behavior: Temporary high‑flux zones (hours–days) that shift with tides, storms, or after heavy casting.
- Ecology: Spawn ephemeral creatures (e.g., Yofitsu, Sand‑woven Djinns) that disperse once the pocket collapses.

3) Resonant Basins
- Origin: Slow leakage from storage crystals and distributed networks (Heartland mesh).
- Behavior: Low‑grade but persistent seepage; produces long‑term environmental change and specialist fauna (Ledger Wyrms).
- Ecology: Creates chronic problems (void pockets, slow mutation) that require maintenance rituals.

4) Breath Terraces & Aeroliths
- Origin: High altitude crystalline outcrops and thermal updraft features (Andean/Celestial regions).
- Behavior: Predictable thin‑veil bands favored by aerial and high‑altitude fauna (Cloud‑Longs, Condor Kings).

5) Contract Nodes
- Origin: Engineered pockets around Federation vaults and legal anchors.
- Behavior: Narrow, managed flux; attracts fauna that feed on record‑energy (Ledger Moths, Clause Wyrms).

Mechanisms that create anomalies

- Tectonic faulting near Aelfar installations exposes crystal veins to ambient flux and can generate tears over decades.
- Repeated inefficient casting raises local entropy debt; if not drained, entropy concentrates and manifests as environmental anomalies (voids, flux blooms).
- Industrial extraction of memory‑metal (poorly regulated) increases micro‑fractures around seams, accelerating leak rates.

Writers' reference values (narrative mechanics)

- Flux Pocket magnitude: Small (+30–80% MU cost for precise work), lasts hours–days.
- Resonant Basin leakage: Chronic (MU availability +10–25% locally; long term degradation risk).
- Flux Tear intensity: Extreme (ambient Flux +200%+; unpredictable, often creates permanent anomalies).

Countermeasures & narrative hooks

- Ritual drainage: Heartland maintenance ritual (synchronous crystal cycling) reduces basin leakage over weeks.
- Sealing: Aelfar‑derived seals (rare) can close pockets for decades but require archmage‑level work.
- Ethical cost: Sealing a tear often kills living beings thriving in its flux—moral dilemma for protagonists.
