# Bigotry Systems: How Prejudice Emerges from Structure

## Foundational Principle

**Bigotry is not a character flaw. It is a systemic feature.**

Every form of prejudice in this world arises from a rational (from the bigot's perspective) response to structural conditions. This doesn't make bigotry moral — it makes it comprehensible. And comprehensible bigotry is harder for characters to fight because they can't dismiss it as ignorance.

The purpose of this document is to map — for each society — what kinds of bigotry exist, why they emerged, what purpose they serve, who benefits, and how they clash with newer values trying to displace them.

---

## I. VALDIMERE — Bureaucratic Bigotry

### A. Class Bigotry: "Your Papers Define Your Worth"

**What it looks like**: Upper-tier citizens speak about lower tiers with casual dismissal. Not hatred — indifference. A Tier 5 (unregistered) person attempting to file a complaint is not abused; they're simply not acknowledged. "You don't exist in the records," the clerk says, and genuinely means it — not as cruelty, but as administrative fact.

**Why it emerged**: When documentation is the basis of legal reality, the undocumented become non-persons through systemic logic, not malice. The bureaucratic system was designed for efficiency — to manage a large empire with limited administrative capacity. Registering everyone was impractical, so the system drew a line. Those below the line were excluded not because anyone hated them, but because they didn't fit the filing system.

**Who benefits**: 
- Tier 1-3 citizens benefit from a system that guarantees their documented rights.
- Bureaucrats benefit from a smaller population to manage.
- The Crown benefits from tax revenue collected only from registered persons (simpler accounting).

**How it self-perpetuates**: 
- Registration requires documentation. Documentation requires previous documentation (birth certificates need parent's records). Those once excluded can never catch up.
- Children born to Tier 5 parents are automatically Tier 5 — not by law, but by absence of records.
- Tier 4 citizens defend the system because it distinguishes them from Tier 5. The most passionate defenders of the registration system are those just above the cutoff.

**The psychologically devastating part**: Tier 5 people often internalize their non-person status. They don't rebel — they accept that they're nobody. A system that creates invisibility rather than persecution is harder to organize against because the victims don't feel victimized; they feel absent.

### B. Anti-Magic Bigotry: "Unregistered Power is Unregistered Threat"

**What it looks like**: Licensed magical practitioners are respected professionals. Unlicensed practitioners are criminals — regardless of skill, intent, or result. A hedge healer who saves a child's life is prosecuted if unlicensed; a licensed mage who botches a spell gets administrative review.

**Why it emerged**: Valdimere's founding crisis involved uncontrolled magic causing mass destruction (the Flux Tearing). The registration system was a response to genuine danger. Controlling magic meant controlling who could practice it. Over centuries, the safety measure became a power structure.

**Who benefits**: Licensed mages maintain monopoly on legal practice (and the income that comes with it). The Crown maintains surveillance over magical capability. The Covenant operates through the licensing system — they control who gets licensed and what they learn.

**How it clashes with new values**: 
- **Progressive argument**: The licensing system was designed for an era of crisis. The crisis is over. Restricting magic now doesn't prevent danger — it prevents innovation and creates a black market in unlicensed healing that hurts the poor.
- **Conservative counter**: "The crisis is never over. Uncontrolled magic destroyed the world once. The licensing system is the only reason it hasn't happened again."
- **The irony**: Both sides are partially right. The system does prevent some dangers. It also prevents the synthesis approach that would make magic safer and more accessible. The Covenant uses this genuine ambiguity to maintain the status quo.

### C. Anti-Frontier Bigotry: "Civilization Ends at the Last Magistrate's Office"

**What it looks like**: Core-province citizens view frontier settlers as semi-civilized. Frontier communities are blamed for their own hardships. "If they wanted safety, they'd live in the provinces."

**Why it emerged**: The empire expanded faster than its bureaucracy. Frontier regions don't have full administrative coverage. Without documentation, frontier people are culturally adjacent to Tier 5. The bureaucratic mindset equates "beyond our system" with "lesser."

**Who benefits**: Core provinces retain disproportionate resources. Officials avoid dangerous frontier postings. The narrative of frontier inferiority justifies allocating defense spending to core regions.

**Value clash**: Young soldiers posted to frontier garrisons return with frontier-sympathetic views ("These people are braver than any capital bureaucrat"). This creates a military-civilian cultural split where frontier veterans resent the capital and capital dwellers resent frontier "barbarism."

### D. Anti-Foreign Bigotry: "Their System is Inferior Because It Isn't Ours"

**What it looks like**: Valdimerian officials treat foreign knowledge traditions with polite contempt. Shukei embodied magic is "superstition." Therryn mathematical magic is "impractical elegance." Umaran relational magic is "primitive."

**Why it emerged**: Standardization is Valdimere's core value. Any non-standardized approach is inherently suspect because it can't be controlled, measured, or documented. This isn't cultural chauvinism — it's bureaucratic incompatibility treated as cultural inferiority.

**The critical nuance**: Valdimerian anti-foreign prejudice isn't about hating foreigners as people. It's about distrusting their *systems*. A Shukei warrior who adopts Valdimerian forms is welcomed. A Shukei warrior who insists on embodied technique is tolerated socially but excluded professionally.

## II. SHUKEI — Honor-Based Bigotry

### A. Birth Bigotry: "Your Blood Determines Your Ceiling"

**What it looks like**: A commoner of extraordinary ability is acknowledged, admired — and never promoted above their birth station. A samurai who is mediocre maintains rank above the extraordinary commoner. Public discourse praises "natural hierarchy" while private conversation acknowledges the absurdity.

**Why it emerged**: Shukei's island geography created small, isolated communities where survival depended on stable social roles. When every person has an assigned function and performs it, the community survives storms, famines, and raids. When people switch roles, confusion creates vulnerability. The birth-determination system was a survival mechanism for island life.

**Who benefits**: Samurai class maintains monopoly on power, weapons, and honor. Daimyo maintain control over their territory. The system benefits those at the top while providing those at the bottom with clarity (you know your role, your obligations, and your limits).

**How it self-perpetuates**:
- The honor code means challenging the hierarchy dishonors your family. The cost of rebellion isn't just personal — it falls on everyone who shares your name.
- Commoners who demonstrate extraordinary ability are explained as "exceptions that prove the rule." The system absorbs contradictions rather than changing.
- The warrior class maintains educational monopoly. Even if a commoner is talented enough, they don't have access to training.

**The psychological mechanism**: Shukei's birth bigotry works because it provides meaning. A farmer who is a farmer because of divine order has purpose. A farmer who is a farmer because the system is rigged has resentment. The religious-philosophical framework transforms arbitrary hierarchy into cosmic order.

### B. Ronin Bigotry: "Masterless is Meaningless"

**What it looks like**: Ronin are treated as contagious. Communities that shelter ronin lose status. Businesses that employ ronin are suspect. Other warriors who associate with ronin are tainted.

**Why it emerged**: The entire Shukei system depends on loyalty bonds. A masterless warrior proves that bonds can be broken. Their existence is an existential threat to the system's foundational logic. If a warrior can exist without a master, then maybe masters aren't necessary.

**Who benefits**: Daimyo and the Shogun. Every ronin who is shunned reinforces the message: leave the system and you become nothing. This uses the ronin as a scarecrow to keep loyal warriors obedient.

**Value clash**: Ronin create parallel meritocracies where skill determines standing regardless of birth. Young warriors who interact with ronin see a world where ability matters more than lineage. The clan system responds by increasing isolation — fewer opportunities for young warriors to encounter ronin.

### C. Anti-Merchant Bigotry: "Those Who Create Nothing Deserve Nothing"

**What it looks like**: Merchants are legally above commoners but socially below farmers. A wealthy merchant who builds a fine house is mocked by a penniless samurai. Trade is necessary but shameful.

**Why it emerged**: In a warrior society that prizes production (farming, crafting, fighting), trade is seen as parasitic. Merchants move goods but create nothing. This is the same logic that feudal Japan applied: warriors protect, farmers grow, artisans make — merchants merely broker.

**Who benefits**: The warrior class, which monopolizes prestige. Farmers, who are told their labor is more honorable than merchant wealth (keeping them content in poverty). The system itself, which suppresses the one class with enough wealth to challenge the hierarchy.

**Value clash**: Young Shukei merchants who trade with Therryn and Federation merchants discover that wealth equals political power elsewhere. They return home to find their coin worthless against birth status. This generates a double resentment: against Shukei for devaluing them and against foreign systems for showing them what they're missing.

### D. Anti-Outsider Bigotry: "The Islands Are Pure; The World Is Corrupt"

**What it looks like**: Foreign ideas, goods, and people are treated as pollutants. Not metaphorical — the religious framework defines contact with outsiders as a form of spiritual contamination requiring purification rituals.

**Why it emerged**: Island isolation worked. For centuries, Shukei's closed system produced stability, cultural coherence, and martial excellence. Foreign contact historically brought disease, destabilization, and demands for change. The isolation policy is a trauma response to genuine historical harm.

**How it clashes**: The current era requires engagement. External threats, trade dependencies, and Covenant infiltration mean isolation is no longer viable. But the cultural machinery for processing foreign information doesn't exist — it was deliberately dismantled by the Isolation Ministry. When foreign ideas do arrive, they're processed through a fear-distortion lens.

## III. THERRYN — Intellectual Bigotry

### A. Education Bigotry: "Ignorance is a Moral Failing"

**What it looks like**: Therryn doesn't hate its uneducated population. It pities them — which is worse. A farmer who can't read is spoken about with the same tone used for the disabled. "It's not their fault, the poor things." Education is so thoroughly equated with human worth that its absence is treated as a deficiency.

**Why it emerged**: When the examination system defines who governs, knowledge becomes the basis of legitimate authority. If knowledge equals authority, then ignorance equals unworthiness. This logic is impeccable within the system — and devastating for those outside it.

**Who benefits**: Scholar-officials maintain power monopoly while claiming moral authority. Their privilege isn't arbitrary (like Shukei birth) or bureaucratic (like Valdimere documentation) — it's "earned." This makes it almost impossible to challenge. You can argue against inherited privilege. How do you argue against merit?

**The insidious psychology**: In Valdimere, a peasant knows the system is rigged. In Shukei, a commoner knows their birth is arbitrary. In Therryn, a failed examination candidate believes they deserved to fail. The system has colonized their self-assessment. This is the most psychologically damaging form of bigotry because the victim agrees with the bigot.

**How it self-perpetuates**: 
- Education costs money → wealthy families produce scholars → scholars make policy that benefits the educated → cycle repeats.
- Failed candidates become examples of "fair but demanding" system → reinforces perception of meritocracy.
- Those who succeed through examination credit the system that promoted them → "I passed because the system works."

### B. Theory-Practice Bigotry: "If You Can't Prove It, It Doesn't Count"

**What it looks like**: Practical knowledge — farming techniques, healing traditions, martial arts refined through centuries of use — is dismissed unless validated by mathematical proof. A traditional healer whose methods work is "superstitious" until a scholar derives the mathematical principle behind the treatment.

**Why it emerged**: Therryn's magic tradition is mathematical. Mathematical magic works brilliantly in Therryn's predictable Flux geology. The leap from "our magic is mathematical and effective" to "all valid knowledge must be mathematical" is short and seductive.

**Who benefits**: Scholars who produce mathematical proofs hold monopoly on "legitimate" knowledge. Practical knowledge that can't be formalized is devalued, keeping practitioners subordinate to theoreticians.

**Value clash**: Umaran relational magic works without mathematical framework. Shukei embodied techniques work without proofs. The existence of effective non-theoretical approaches is threatening. Therryn's response: ignore, deny, or reframe as "pre-theoretical understanding that will eventually be properly modeled."

### C. Anti-Merchant Bigotry: "Money Isn't Knowledge"

**What it looks like**: Wealthy merchants who fund the entire scholarly system are treated as necessary evils. A merchant who quotes a philosopher is condescended to: "How charming that they read." Scholar-merchant intermarriage is common but socially embarrassing for the scholar.

**Why it emerged**: The examination system ranks knowledge above wealth. Since merchants can't buy examination results (usually), their wealth is politically impotent. But scholars need merchant money. The result is mutual dependency managed through mutual contempt.

**Value clash**: Growing merchant class demanding political representation. "We fund everything. We should have a voice." Scholars respond: "Understanding, not funding, qualifies you to govern." The merchant counter: "Your understanding couldn't balance a ledger."

### D. Anti-Foreign-Methodology Bigotry: "Their Magic Isn't Really Magic"

**What it looks like**: Therryn scholars acknowledge that Valdimerian formulaic magic and Shukei embodied magic produce results. But they categorize these as "mechanical application" (Valdimere) or "instinctive fumbling" (Shukei) rather than genuine understanding. Only mathematical derivation from first principles counts as real knowledge.

**Why it emerged**: If your entire civilizational identity rests on "we understand the math behind reality," then people who achieve the same results without math are threatening. Either the math isn't necessary (destroying your cultural foundation) or they got lucky (dismissible).

## IV. UMARA — Relational Bigotry

### A. Network Bigotry: "You Are Nobody If Nobody Knows You"

**What it looks like**: Strangers, refugees, and newcomers are treated with guarded hospitality. Not hostility — but exclusion from meaningful participation. A refugee can eat at the communal table but can't speak at the council until they've built sufficient relationship history. This can take years.

**Why it emerged**: The obligation system requires trust, and trust requires relationship history. Extending full community privileges to strangers would be economically irrational — they might take without giving. The exclusion is a rational risk management strategy.

**Who benefits**: Established families maintain power through network density. Their children inherit relationships. Newcomers must build from scratch — ensuring that existing power structures reproduce across generations even without formal hierarchy.

**The cruel irony**: Umara genuinely believes in equality and hospitality. The exclusion of newcomers isn't recognized as bigotry because it's framed as "building trust." But if trust-building takes a decade, the newcomer is effectively second-class for a decade. The system is exclusionary while sincerely believing it's welcoming.

### B. Age Bigotry: "Youth is Ignorance"

**What it looks like**: Young adults with good ideas are told to wait. "You'll understand when you're older." Innovations proposed by people under 40 are reflexively questioned not on merit but on the proposer's age.

**Why it emerged**: Oral tradition preserves knowledge through elder memory. In a system without written records, the old ARE the library. Disrespecting the old means losing the library. Habitual deference to age was a knowledge-preservation strategy.

**Who benefits**: Elders maintain disproportionate influence. Communities remain conservative (which was historically advantageous in stable conditions).

**Value clash**: The current crisis requires rapid adaptation. Younger Umarans who've traveled to Therryn or Valdimere see faster decision-making and argue for reform. Elders see this as cultural betrayal. "Speed destroyed Valdimere's frontier. You want us to be fast like them?"

### C. Anti-Written-Record Bigotry: "Writing Kills Memory"

**What it looks like**: Umarans who adopt writing (especially after contact with Therryn merchants) are viewed with suspicion. "If you need to write it down, you don't really know it." Written records are seen as a crutch that atrophies the mind.

**Why it emerged**: Griot authority depends on oral transmission monopoly. If knowledge can be written, anyone can access it. Griots lose their unique social function. The anti-writing prejudice is framed as cultural values but functions as professional protectionism.

**Who benefits**: Griots maintain monopoly on historical narrative. Communities maintain cultural distinctiveness against Therryn textual imperialism.

**Value clash**: Younger Umarans learning to write discover that written records are more reliable than memory (griots make errors, sometimes deliberately). This threatens the foundation of griot authority and — by extension — the entire cultural memory system.

### D. Anti-Individualist Bigotry: "Your Need Doesn't Outweigh Our Community"

**What it looks like**: Community members who pursue individual goals (personal wealth, solo travel, private ambitions) are pressured to return to communal priorities. "You're being selfish" is the most serious social criticism possible.

**Why it emerged**: Survival in the savannah required collective action. Individuals who prioritized personal goals over group survival endangered everyone. The anti-individualism bias was survival-rational.

**Value clash**: Individuals with extraordinary abilities (heroes, innovators, independent thinkers) are constrained by communal pressure. The community that produced Amara (the intelligence coordinator) also tried to prevent her from leaving. Her departure was experienced as betrayal even though her work ultimately served Umaran interests.

## V. CELESTIAL KINGDOM — Spiritual Bigotry

### A. Meditation Hierarchy: "Depth Determines Worth"

**What it looks like**: Those who meditate deeply are spiritually superior. Those who don't are spiritually immature. The monastery-educated speak about lay populations with gentle condescension.

**Why it emerged**: Celestial magic is meditation-based. Deeper meditation = more powerful magical ability. The spiritual hierarchy maps directly onto magical capability. If magic is real power and meditation produces magic, then meditation depth becomes the legitimate basis for authority.

### B. Anti-Material Bigotry: "Worldly Concern is Spiritual Weakness"

**What it looks like**: Interest in commerce, military matters, or physical pleasure is viewed as attachment to the material world. Monks who advocate for practical trade policies are told they've "lost their way."

**Why it emerged**: Monastic renunciation was a genuine spiritual practice. But when the spiritual class governs, their discomfort with material reality becomes policy. Infrastructure decays. Trade atrophies. Military preparedness is neglected. "The cosmos will provide" — except it won't.

**Value clash**: Lay citizens who want economic development, military security, and physical comfort are told these desires reflect spiritual immaturity. The resentment is growing.

## VI. FEDERATION — Contractual Bigotry

### A. Legal Literacy Bigotry: "If You Can't Read a Contract, You Can't Govern Yourself"

**What it looks like**: Citizens who can't navigate the legal system are treated as functionally children. They need legal guardians. They can't own property without co-signers. They can't testify without legal representation.

**Why it emerged**: When contracts are magically binding, an uninformed signature can literally enslave someone. Legal literacy requirements were a protection mechanism — preventing exploitation through ignorance.

**How it became bigotry**: The protection mechanism became a gate-keeping mechanism. Legal literacy education is expensive. Those who can't afford it are permanently second-class. The system protects them from exploitation by preventing them from participating.

### B. Anti-Communal Bigotry: "Obligation Without Contract is Chaos"

**What it looks like**: Federation citizens view Umaran communal obligation as primitive and dangerous. "You mean their debts aren't written down? Anyone could claim anything." The concept of trust-based commerce is literally incomprehensible to a culture where contracts define reality.

## VII. ANDEAN ALLIANCE — Geographic Bigotry

### A. Altitude Hierarchy: "Higher is Holier"

**What it looks like**: Peak communities consider themselves spiritually closest to the divine. Valley communities are "lowlanders" — productive but earthbound. Terrace communities see themselves as the bridge.

**Why it emerged**: Flux concentration increases with altitude. Peak-dwellers genuinely have more magical access. When magical capability correlates with geography, geographic hierarchy becomes spiritual hierarchy.

**Value clash**: Valley communities produce most of the food. Peak communities produce most of the magic. Each needs the other; each looks down on the other. The terrace communities mediate — but their mediator status grants them disproportionate political influence.

## VIII. CROSS-CUTTING BIGOTRY PATTERNS

### A. Universal Pattern: Magic-Based Discrimination

In every nation, magical ability correlates with social status. Those with more magical access occupy higher social positions. The specific mechanism varies:
- Valdimere: Licensed vs. unlicensed
- Shukei: Samurai training access vs. commoner exclusion
- Therryn: Mathematical understanding vs. practical knowledge
- Umara: Spirit-well proximity vs. distance
- Celestial: Meditation depth
- Federation: Contracted vs. uncontracted
- Andean: Altitude

**The synthesis implication**: Aisen's synthesis approach (combining all four traditions, accessible to anyone) threatens EVERY nation's power structure because it removes the magical basis for hierarchy. This is why synthesis is feared — not because it's dangerous, but because it's democratizing.

### B. Universal Pattern: The Exception Exemption

Every nation has a mechanism for absorbing exceptional individuals without changing the system:
- Valdimere: "You're registered now. See, the system works."
- Shukei: "You're adopted into a samurai family. Exceptional, but the hierarchy holds."
- Therryn: "You passed the exam despite poverty. Proof the system is fair."
- Umara: "You built your network. The system welcomed you."
- Celestial: "Your meditation is deep. You were always spiritually advanced."

**The function**: Every exception reinforces the system. If exceptional individuals can succeed, the system is vindicated. The 99% who don't succeed are invisible.

### C. Universal Pattern: Rational Persistence

Every form of bigotry in this world persists because:
1. **It benefits someone powerful** (maintains existing hierarchy)
2. **It's mentally efficient** (simpler classifications require less thought)
3. **Changing it costs more than maintaining it** (reform threatens stability)
4. **The victims partially agree** (they've internalized the system's values)

This is the core narrative insight: prejudice persists not because people are evil but because the system makes prejudice rational.

## IX. VALUE CLASHES: OLD vs. NEW

### In Every Nation, The Same Tension

**Traditional values** (old): Stability, continuity, known categories, tested methods, ancestor wisdom.
**Progressive values** (new): Adaptation, innovation, expanded categories, experimental methods, present-moment problem-solving.

The clash isn't ideological — it's generational, experiential, and existential.

### Nation-by-Nation Clash Points

#### Valdimere
- **Old**: "Documentation is safety. Registration protects civilization."
- **New**: "Documentation is control. Registration is surveillance."
- **Flashpoint**: Young bureaucrats who've seen frontier collapse want to reform the registration system to include Tier 5 populations. Senior officials see this as diluting the system's integrity.
- **Outcome trajectory**: Neither side can win cleanly. Reforming registration doesn't address frontier resource allocation. Maintaining registration doesn't address demographic collapse.

#### Shukei
- **Old**: "Birth hierarchy is divine order. Honoring your role honors your ancestors."
- **New**: "Birth hierarchy wastes human potential. The ronin prove that skill transcends birth."
- **Flashpoint**: Tournament outcomes where commoner-born ronin defeat high-born samurai. The old guard explains these as "flukes." The young guard sees them as evidence.
- **Outcome trajectory**: The honor system will crack from within as young samurai who've witnessed ronin excellence can't unsee what they've seen. But the crack will be slow because the cost of acknowledging it falls on the individual (dishonor to family).

#### Therryn
- **Old**: "The examination system is sacred merit. It's worked for four hundred years."
- **New**: "The examination system is hereditary privilege disguised as merit. It reproduces itself."
- **Flashpoint**: When a merchant-funded examination candidate outperforms a landed gentry candidate. The system worked — but the process reveals that money, not merit, enabled the success.
- **Outcome trajectory**: The merchant class is one generation from having enough educated children to challenge the scholarly establishment from within. When merchant-educated scholars dominate the Grand Council, the examination system's relationship to wealth will be undeniable.

#### Umara
- **Old**: "Relationships define identity. Community before individual. Memory before innovation."
- **New**: "We need written records. We need faster decisions. We need formal structures."
- **Flashpoint**: When a griot's memory contradicts written evidence provided by a Therryn-educated Umaran. The community must choose between oral tradition and documentation. Neither choice preserves full trust.
- **Outcome trajectory**: Some communities will adopt writing. Others will reject it. The resulting cultural split will weaken the network that holds Umaran society together — exactly when unity is most needed.

#### Celestial
- **Old**: "Spiritual attainment is the highest good. Material concern is distraction."
- **New**: "We're starving while the monks meditate. Spiritual attainment doesn't grow food."
- **Flashpoint**: When a foreign invasion targets a Celestial border community and the monastic council's response is extended meditation for "cosmic guidance" rather than military mobilization.
- **Outcome trajectory**: Lay rebellion or external conquest. The spiritual bypass can't survive contact with material crisis.

#### Federation
- **Old**: "Contracts are sacred. Legal process is freedom."
- **New**: "Contracts are cages. The wealthy write rules that serve themselves."
- **Flashpoint**: When a magically-binding contract is shown to be exploitative but legally unbreakable. The victim serves the contract while the arbitration system debates reform.
- **Outcome trajectory**: Legal reform from within (slow, incremental) or revolutionary rejection of the contract system (catastrophic, because the magical infrastructure depends on contractual stability).

#### Andean
- **Old**: "The mountain teaches. Altitude is wisdom. The old ways sustained us."
- **New**: "The mountain is just geology. Altitude is geography. New threats require new responses."
- **Flashpoint**: When peak communities refuse to share magical resources with valley communities during an ecological crisis, citing "spiritual prerogative." Valley communities begin questioning the altitude hierarchy.
- **Outcome trajectory**: Geographic redistribution crisis. If altitude privilege falls, the Alliance's organizational principle collapses. What replaces it?

## X. NARRATIVE APPLICATION

### For Aisen

Aisen's trajectory through these systems is:
1. **Recognition** (Tavern): Sees that bigotry is rational
2. **Expansion** (Brokers): Sees that every faction's bigotry makes sense from their position
3. **Disillusionment** (Academy): Sees that systemic bigotry absorbs individual excellence without changing
4. **Acceptance** (Godwar): Works within bigoted systems rather than against them
5. **Legacy** (Post-death): The synthesis vision threatens all bigotry systems simultaneously — not by fighting prejudice but by removing its structural foundation (magic-based hierarchy)

### For Supporting Characters

Each supporting character should embody their culture's specific bigotry and value clash:
- **Harald** (Shukei-origin): Ronin who lives the birth-hierarchy contradiction daily. Respected for skill; rejected for masterlessness.
- **Tarovin** (Therryn-trained): Scholar who recognizes the examination system's hypocrisy but can't abandon the framework that gave him identity.
- **Amara** (Umaran): Intelligence coordinator who left community (individualist "betrayal") to serve community interest (relational obligation).
- **Rin** (Celestial): Fled spiritual hierarchy for material freedom. Carries guilt for abandoning spiritual practice. Questions whether practical action is "lower" than meditation.
- **Elara** (Valdimerian): Licensed healer who knows the licensing system prevents better healing. Maintains license for survival while practicing unlicensed techniques privately.

### For World-Building Scenes

When depicting any society, the bigotry should be:
- Casual, not dramatic (it's how people think, not a speech they give)
- Rational from inside the system (never make the bigot stupid)
- Costly to challenge (changing your mind costs you something real)
- Carried by sympathetic characters (the reader should understand why they think this way)
