# Ren's Spar

The courtyard was empty. All the other students were at dinner.

Ren's father stood across from her, sword drawn. He had come to visit the Academy unannounced. He had asked to see her fight.

"Show me what they have taught you," he said.

She bowed. She raised her wooden blade.

He was faster. Stronger. He had been training since he could walk—the family sword tradition stretching back generations. She was fast for her age, good for an Academy student.

She was not good compared to him.

He disarmed her on the third exchange. Her wooden sword clattered to the ground.

"Again," he said.

She attacked. Again. Again. Again.

Each time, he moved her down. Each time, she picked up her blade and returned.

After the seventh disarmament, her father finally lowered his sword.

"You are not worthy of the family sword," he said. His voice was not unkind. It was simply true.

Ren stood, breathing hard, her hand marked where the blade had struck.

"I know," she said. "But I will be."

She picked up her wooden blade. She bowed. She walked away.

Her father did not smile. But he did not look away. For just a moment, she heard something in his silence—not approval, not yet.

But something that might become approval, if she kept walking back. If she kept returning. If she refused to break.

That was enough. For now, that was enough.
