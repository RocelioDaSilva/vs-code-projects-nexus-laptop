# Caspian Collapses

The beast was massive—a hulk of corrupted flesh and bone, its hide thick enough to deflect arrows. Caspian's ice shards bounced off. Aisen's chain could not find a grip.

"We need more power," Caspian said.

"No," Aisen replied. "We need a better angle."

But Caspian was already casting. He drew from his well—deep, too deep. Ice formed around the beast's legs. More ice. A cage. A tomb.

The beast stopped moving.

Caspian stopped breathing.

He collapsed. His eyes were open. His hands were trembling. His lips moved, but no sound came out.

Aisen was beside him in an instant. "Caspian. Caspian!"

No response. The vampire's skin was pale—paler than usual. His pulse was weak.

*Mana exhaustion.*

Aisen had seen it before. He had felt it himself. But never this severe.

He dragged Caspian behind a broken wall. He poured water into the vampire's mouth. He waited.

Minutes passed. Then: a cough. A gasp.

"Don't," Aisen said. "Ever. Do that. Again."

Caspian's eyes focused. He tried to smile. It was weak.

"Noted."

But they both knew he would do it again. The cost was always worth it, until it wasn't.
