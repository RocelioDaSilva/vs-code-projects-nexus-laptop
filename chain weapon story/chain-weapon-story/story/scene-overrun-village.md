# The Overrun Village

The smell hit him first. Rotting flesh. Smoke. Something sweet underneath—corpses, left too long in the sun.

Aisen had seen death before. He had seen Frostfang. He had seen the aftermath of battles. But this was different.

This was a village. Not a battlefield.

Homes were crushed. Walls torn open. A child's doll lay in the mud, its painted face cracked. A cooking pot was still on the fire, the stew burned to charcoal.

No bodies. The beasts had taken them. Or the survivors had buried them. He did not know which was worse.

He found a man sitting against a broken well. The man's leg was bandaged—poorly. Blood had soaked through. His eyes were open. He was not looking at anything.

"How many?" Aisen asked.

The man did not answer. Then: "Thirty-seven. Before."

"Before what?"

"Before the Veil thinned. Before the beasts came. Before the nobles sent their army to the border instead of here."

The man laughed. It was a hollow sound.

"They said it was a monster surge. Natural. But I saw them. Their eyes were glowing. Flux-touched. Someone drove them here. Someone wanted us gone."

Aisen said nothing. He had no proof. But he believed the man.

He left his waterskin. He left his last ration bar. He walked away.

Behind him, the man began to weep.
