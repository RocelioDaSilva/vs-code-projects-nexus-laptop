# KAIROS: THE LOOP MONTAGE

## Fragment: Eternal Repetition

**Loop 1**

Kairos lunged. The general sidestepped with a smile. The sword went through his abdomen, clean and precise.

Pain. Then darkness.

Then light.

He was standing at the cell door again. The general was smirking.

**General:** "Again."

---

**Loop 3**

He tried feinting left. The general countered with a laugh. The blade found his chest.

Reset.

---

**Loop 7**

He managed to land a punch. The general's head snapped back. For one moment, Kairos thought—

The general's hand moved. Kairos was on the ground. The sword was in his back.

Reset.

---

**Loop 15**

He had learned the general's rhythm now. He was faster. He understood the footwork. He almost—

Almost meant nothing. The sword went through his throat.

Reset.

---

**Loop 47**

He tried grabbing a stone. Tried to make a weapon. The general saw it, laughed, broke his arm, ran him through.

Reset.

---

**Loop 112**

Kairos begged. *"Please. Let me go."*

The general laughed. *"Your goal is to kill me. Begging doesn't count."*

Reset.

---

**Loop 152**

He had memorized every scar on the general's face. He knew the pattern of stubble growth. He knew the general took two steps to the left before attacking. He knew none of it mattered.

The sword was in his belly.

Reset.

---

**Loop 203**

He stood still. Did not move. Did not try.

The general frowned. *"What's wrong? Given up?"*

Kairos did not answer. He was trying to remember his name. It felt like a word he had read somewhere.

The general killed him anyway.

Reset.

---

**Loop 241**

He was no longer sure which attempt this was. He was no longer sure he was counting correctly. Was it 241? Was it 2,000? Had he been here so long that numbers had stopped meaning anything?

He died.

Reset.

---

**Loop 311**

He had stopped trying hours ago. Days. Time had become weird. He did not move when the general approached. He just stood there.

The sword was a punctuation mark.

He woke up to do it again.

---

**Loop 398**

His memory was breaking. He could see it happening, like watching a building collapse in slow motion. There were too many versions of this moment, too many timelines bleeding into each other. He remembered dying in different ways. He remembered succeeding and failing. He remembered things that had never happened yet.

Or had already happened. He couldn't remember.

He died.

Reset.

---

**Loop 401**

Who was he? What was his name? He could almost reach it, but it was slipping away like water through his fingers.

Kairos. His name was Kairos. He said it aloud. The sound of his own voice was strange. Had he spoken in how long?

The general ran him through.

Reset.

---

**Loop 518**

He had been here for... weeks? Months? The notion of time had stopped making sense. Each loop felt like it was compressed into a single second. Each day felt like it lasted forever.

He tried to remember what the sun felt like. He couldn't.

He died.

Reset.

---

**Loop 659**

He could barely move. His muscles remembered all the ways he had died. They were confused about how to still work. His body was a graveyard of failed attempts.

He died anyway.

Reset.

---

**Loop 800+**

There was no counting anymore. There was only: failure, death, awakening, failure, death, awakening.

There was only the general's smile.

There was only the sword.

There was only standing at the cell door, waiting to fail again.

---

**Loop ???**

Did he still have a face? He couldn't remember. He had died so many ways. Had his face survived? Did anybody's face survive this?

He was crying. Or maybe he was laughing. He couldn't distinguish between the two anymore.

The sword was coming.

Reset.

---

**Loop ???**

He didn't move.

The general killed him.

Reset.

He didn't move.

The general killed him.

Reset.

He didn't move.

The general killed him.

Reset.

He didn't move.

The general killed him.

Reset.

---

**Loop: The Break**

Kairos was no longer sure he was Kairos. There was a shape of a person standing here, but the interior was empty. The map of who he was had been replaced with loops, with death, with failure.

The shape tried to beg. The shape tried to fight. The shape tried to surrender. None of it mattered. The shape died.

The shape woke up.

The shape stood at the cell door, already terrified of what would happen next, already dead in anticipation, already dying retroactively from loops that hadn't happened yet.

---

**Then: A Voice**

"Your goal is not to kill him. Your goal is to escape this room."

Kairos—or what was left of Kairos—looked up.

A stranger stood in the shadows. Wiry. Calm. Seeing him.

"Set the goal," the stranger said.

---

**Loop: The Final One**

Kairos set the goal: *I will escape this room.*

The Flux activated for the last time.

The door was open. The general was not there. The path was clear.

Kairos walked into the corridor.

The stranger was waiting.

**Kairos:** "Why?"

**Aisen:** "Because no one should be trapped forever."

And for the first time in what might have been years, might have been seconds, Kairos felt something besides fear move through him.

It was so small, so fragile.

It was gratitude.

---

**END OF KAIROS: THE LOOP MONTAGE**
