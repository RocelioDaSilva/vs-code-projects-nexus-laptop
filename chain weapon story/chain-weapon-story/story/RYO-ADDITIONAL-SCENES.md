# RYO: ADDITIONAL SCENES

## Scene 1: The Recognition – Years Before the Campfire

*Ages 17-18: Adventuring years*

Aisen was tracking a creature—something between a lynx and a fox, Flux-touched, hunting farmers' goats on the settlement's edge. He had been following blood droplets and paw prints for three days when he found someone standing directly in front of him.

The man was young, maybe Aisen's age. He had the look of someone who had been walking very far and had a thousand miles still to go. His eyes were the wrong color—one gray, one blue, shifting between them sometimes like a fault in the world.

*"You're going to find that creature in the next valley,"* the stranger said. *"It's going to try to run up the cliff wall. It will succeed. You'll follow. You won't catch it, but you'll prove to the farmers that it can't be killed here. They'll hire a mage to create a boundary ward. The creature will go hungry for two months, during which time its mate will give birth underground. Five kits. All of them will die except one."*

Aisen did not move his hand from his chain.

*"How do you know this?"*

*"Because I lived this three times. Different approaches. Different timings. Same outcome. The one survivor is going to be the mother of fifteen creatures that will haunt that valley for decades. You can kill the creature now, save the offspring problem. Or you can let it live, and deal with the consequences generations from now. Either way, you'll be making a choice someone already made."*

*"You sound like a heretic."*

*"I sound like someone who knows too many versions of the same story."*

The stranger held Aisen's eyes. For a moment, Aisen saw the fracture in him—not metaphorical, but real. There were multiple people looking out of those mismatched eyes, and they were not quite synchronized.

*"The creature is pregnant,"* the stranger continued. *"She's going to die having the kits no matter what you do. So the real choice is whether you want her death to have meant something."*

*"And you've already made this choice?"*

*"At least three times. I keep making different decisions. It hasn't helped."*

The stranger smiled, but it was a broken thing.

*"Are you Flux-bent?"* Aisen asked.

*"I'm something that was bent so hard it broke and reformed into something new. Name's Ryo. And no, I'm not going to hurt you, and yes, I'm probably wanted for questioning by more than one faction. And yes, I know exactly what you are because I can see the way you carry fear like a second skin, the same way I do."*

Aisen released his chain slightly.

*"Aisen."*

*"I know. I've seen you around. You're the one who doesn't make friends."*

*"Too dangerous."*

*"Yeah. I'm learning that too."*

They stood in silence for a moment. The valley wind carried the scent of the pregnant creature—fear-sweat, approaching labor, something like resignation.

*"The kits die,"* Aisen said. *"So I should kill her now."*

*"You could. Or you could go back and tell the farmers the truth: the creature isn't going to stop hunting their goats. They need to either live with the loss or relocate. It's not a magical problem. It's an economics problem."*

Aisen almost smiled. *"You're a heretic."*

*"So are you. You're just slower about it."*

Ryo turned and walked back the way he had come. After a few steps, he paused.

*"When you figure out how to be two people at once without going insane, come find me. We can compare notes."*

Then he was gone, and Aisen was left alone with the creature's presence ahead and the strange certainty that he had just met someone very much like himself—if himself had been broken differently.

## Scene 2: The Lightning Protocol – First Battle

*Ages 19-20: War years*

Aisen first saw Ryo's power when they were caught in a Garrison ambush. It was night. Cavalry was moving toward them. A dozen soldiers, maybe more, moving fast.

Ryo was standing beside him when the first lightning struck.

But it was wrong lightning—too many points of origin, all striking the same moment, all in rhythmic pattern. Like someone playing an instrument made of electricity.

Four soldiers fell. Their armor was scorched. They were not dead—they would recover. But they would not be fighting that night.

*"First form,"* Ryo said quietly. *"Single lightning, single strike, single direction. Takes everything I've got. Once per day, maybe twice if I'm desperate."*

More lightning. The cavalry was confused—electricity in the ground now, electricity in the air. The horses were panicking.

*"Second form,"* Ryo continued. *"Four simultaneous strikes. Four different targets. Same energy, divided. Takes a few hours to recover from. Can do it twice if I'm not sleeping much."*

The cavalry regrouped. They were backing off, trying to reposition. Now Aisen saw Ryo's body begin to glow—not brightly, but distinctly. Four constructs of pure electricity formed around him, roughly humanoid, holding shape through pure will.

*"Third form,"* Ryo said. *"Four battle-forms. They're not conscious. They're just... me. Distributed. I fight through all of them simultaneously. It feels like being torn apart and sewn together while I'm still bleeding. It lasts maybe two minutes before the strain breaks something in my head and I have to stop."*

The four constructs moved with inhuman speed. The cavalry broke and ran. In the space of thirty seconds, it was over.

Ryo collapsed to his knees. When he looked up, Aisen saw blood running from his nose.

*"That,"* Ryo said, *"is why I'm trying to find a way to stop being two people. Because when I split myself that much, I can't remember which version of me is supposed to feel like the real one."*

Aisen helped him up without speaking. Some things did not need words.

## Scene 3: The Recognition – Battlefield Recognition

*Ages 21-22: War deepening*

By the time they truly became allies, Ryo had learned to control something in himself. Not the splitting—that was permanent—but the fear of it.

They were defending a bridge when the moment came.

The Garrison had sent their best—soldiers trained specifically to counter Flux-users. Aisen was falling back, his chain getting tangled in a broken cart. Ryo was three positions over, maintaining the bridge's defensive line.

Then Aisen saw the killing blow coming. A soldier with a weapon designed to sever Flux-connections was approaching from the side. Aisen could not move fast enough to stop it.

Ryo *split*.

Not into four constructs, but into two—the child-self and the warrior-self, occupying the same space for just a moment, making a decision together that neither could have made alone.

Lightning struck the soldier with the severing-weapon. And then Ryo was laughing, bleeding, collapsing against the bridge railing.

*"Did you see that?"* he gasped. *"Did you see it?"*

*"See what?"*

*"They worked together. The two parts of me. They cooperated. They saved you. Both of them. At the same time. Without destroying each other."*

Aisen helped him sit.

*"That's new?"*

*"It is. I think... I think I might be able to actually fuse. Not erase one self or suppress the other. But actually merge them into something new. Something that has both their memories but isn't torn apart by having them."*

Aisen understood what that meant. It meant Ryo might become whole. It also meant that the act of fusion would be like dying while alive.

*"When?"* Aisen asked.

*"I don't know yet. Soon, maybe. I can feel it getting closer. The two versions of me are learning each other's language. Soon we'll be able to say what we need to say, and then there won't be any reason not to try."*

Ryo looked at him with one gray eye and one blue eye, and they flickered.

*"When I do it, it might not work. I might just break differently. I might cease to exist entirely. There's no way to know until I try."*

*"I'll be there if you want."*

Ryo smiled—a real smile, not the fractured one.

*"Yeah. I do want that. Promise?"*

*"Promise."*

## Scene 4: The Chasm – Ryo's Fusion Moment

*Ages 22-23: Approaching climax*

The night before Aisen's final stand, Ryo came to him with a question.

*"Do you think I'll survive it?"*

*"The fusion?"*

*"Yeah."*

Aisen considered. *"I don't know. I don't think anyone has ever done this before. You might. You might become something new. You might just... stop existing and something else starts."*

*"Would that be so bad? If I stopped and something else started?"*

*"Only if what starts isn't as worth knowing as what stops."*

Ryo laughed. *"That's cruel and kind at the same time."*

*"That's what we do."*

Ryo closed his eyes. When he opened them again, both were the same color—gray, steady, no longer flickering.

*"I think they merged,"* he whispered. *"Not the full fusion yet. But they're speaking the same language now. I can feel both of them in my head at the same time without the splitting pain. It's like... it's like finding the frequency where two different songs can play together and make something beautiful."*

Aisen waited. This was Ryo's moment.

*"I'm going to do it tomorrow. After you... after everything. I need to know you survived, first. I need to know that the person I'm fusing into will have someone to recognize what I become."*

*"And if I don't survive?"*

*"Then I might still fuse, but it would mean something different. It would hurt."*

Aisen stood and embraced him. All four constructs stood there, all iterations of Ryo, and only one person. 

It was enough.
