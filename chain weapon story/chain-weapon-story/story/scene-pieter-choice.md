# Pieter's Choice

His father's letter arrived on a warm afternoon. Pieter read it once. Then again.

*A trade deal. A fortune. A family name elevated.*

All he had to do was stop associating with a lowborn tavern boy with no future.

His father waited for a response. Instead, Pieter walked to the Academy's administrative hall. He found his father waiting there, having traveled to deliver the terms in person.

"The answer is no," Pieter said.

His father's face reddened. "You don't understand what you're refusing. This family has built—"

"I understand perfectly. But I'm refusing."

"He's a commoner, Pieter. He has no future. He will drag you down."

Pieter thought of Aisen. The way he shared his food even when he had little. The way he listened without judgment. The way he carried a chain that had belonged to his father and treated it with more respect than Pieter's father treated anything.

"No," Pieter said again. "I'll find another way."

He turned and walked back toward his quarters. His father did not follow.

Behind him, he heard his father's voice, low and bitter: "Then you are no son of mine."

Pieter did not stop. He did not cry. But he knew, in that moment, that he had chosen who he wanted to be. And it was not the man his father was.

Years later, when Aisen was gone, Pieter would think of that choice. He would know it was the right one. But he would also know that it had cost him everything.

It had been worth it.
