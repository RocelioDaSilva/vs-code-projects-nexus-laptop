Summary:
Emma leads a group of refugees fleeing a region where the ecosystem has broken down. Plants are dying despite plentiful rain; animals have become aggressive and mutated. She describes watching her village slowly suffocate as the land died around them. They fled not for war, but for survival.

Key details:
- Emma is a weathered farmer, strong and grieving, responsible for a child and an elder.
- She bears a distinctive burn mark on her neck from a corrupted animal attack.
- She speaks of a "sickness in the earth" that no one seems to understand, offering local color and testimony to the ecological crisis.

Dramatic function:
- This scene reveals the ecological collapse from a human perspective and provides grounding for later Academy research scenes where Aisen and others piece together the Flux corruption.
- Emma's voice returns in later refugee camp scenes as camps grow and coalesce.
