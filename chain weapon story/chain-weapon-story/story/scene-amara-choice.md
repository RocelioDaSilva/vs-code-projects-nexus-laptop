# Amara's Choice

Amara's hands were still. The letter in front of her was short. Three sentences. It was enough.

*The gardener's boy passes messages to a Sahelian merchant. The merchant pays in silver. The silver buys medicine for the boy's dying mother.*

She had recruited him herself. She had promised him safety. She had promised him that no one would know.

Now she knew. And she had to choose.

She could burn the letter. No one would ever know she had seen it. The boy would continue his work. The Covenant would continue to receive information. Students would die.

Or she could deliver the letter to Master Wei. The boy would be arrested. Questioned. Executed. His mother would die without medicine. And her network would learn that Amara could not protect her sources.

*What would Aisen do?*

The question surprised her. Aisen was pragmatic. He would weigh the cost. One life versus many. He would choose the many.

But Aisen was not here. And Amara was not Aisen.

She picked up the letter. She walked to the fireplace. She held it over the flame.

The paper curled. Blackened. Turned to ash.

She would warn the boy. She would tell him to stop. She would find another way to get medicine for his mother.

And if he betrayed her? If more students died?

She would carry that weight. That was her choice.
