Scene: Chain‑Spear Training — Mechanics and Ritual

Setting: The Hall yard during off‑hours; teachers and students practice the chain spear form—drills focusing on momentum, catch, and release.

Summary/Draft:

Training begins with fundamentals: footwork and timing, the coil and release of the weighted chain. Instructors teach how to store momentum in the arm and release it with a snap that redirects force rather than absorbing it. This is illustrated with paired drills: one student catches the incoming chain in a practiced brace, transferring energy into an anchored stump and returning it with a gentle flick.

The scene alternates technical description with character beats: a mentor correcting Aisen’s stance, a rival mocking a failing technique, and a quiet moment where Aisen invents a small modification—an off‑axis catch that lets the chain slip past armor joints. The instructor calls it clever and dangerous in equal measure.

Use: This scene offers a place to explain the Chain‑Spear’s unique mechanics without a lecturing exposition. It also seeds a personal signature move that can later be decisive in combat scenes.
