Scene: Frostfang Trek — The Cold that Tests Bonds

Setting: The Spine’s lower ridges during the Frostfang expedition — freezing wind, thin ley currents, and creaking ice.

Summary/Draft:

The group moved in a ragged line, wrapped in pack cloaks and breathing in steam. The ley lines here were thin, and the air held a faint electric tang that made skin prickle. Lead guide Joren checked the compass—both mundane and flux—for signs of unstable nodes.

The trek begins as a logistical challenge: cram supplies, scout for shelters, and ration heat‑stores. But the ever‑present danger is the shifting ice: a fissure opens that swallows a rear pack. The team must improvise a rescue using resonance hooks and graviton‑tethers, a scene that shows practical use of Flux in field conditions.

At night, the small fire’s light reveals frost‑catchers: strange crystalline codices that sing when wind passes through. Someone spots a distant shimmer—an ice‑scale of a Frost Leviathan—or only the mind’s fear. Tension rises when two members disagree on route choice; a personality fracture mirrors the physical cracking of the ice.

Use: This trek demonstrates logistical stakes, group dynamics, and how the environment tests both equipment and character. It’s an opportunity to show ritualized survival techniques, such as the chaining of heat stores, the use of Flux to bind wounds, and the moral cost of leaving a slow companion behind.
