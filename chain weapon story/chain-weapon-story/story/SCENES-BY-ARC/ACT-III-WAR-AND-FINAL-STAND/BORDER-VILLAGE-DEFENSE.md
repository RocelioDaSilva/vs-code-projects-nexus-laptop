Scene: Border Village Defense — Improvised Strategy

Setting: A small border village on the Silverrun tributary; makeshift barricades, watchtowers, and villagers with farming tools.

Summary/Draft:

At dawn the village wakes to the unnatural sound of drums: raiders approach along the low fen. The protagonist’s training group has been dispatched to assist—mostly unproven recruits and a handful of veterans. The defenders must turn farming tools into weapons and create choke points along the wooden bridges.

The scene shows community tactics: collapsing a ford as a trap, using resonant beacons to mask movement, and field‑repurposed chain spear trip lines. One sequence focuses on a young defender who uses a modified trapping hook to disarm a cavalry charge, showing ingenuity.

The moral center of the scene is the villagers’ cost: burned cottages, displaced families, and the law’s delayed response. The protagonists learn that heroism is improvisation and that large institutions are often too slow.

Use: This scene demonstrates low‑tech defense, social stakes, and opportunities for the protagonists to grow as tactical leaders.
