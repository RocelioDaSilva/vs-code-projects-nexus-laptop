Summary:
Naia, a healer attached to border forces, is performing magic at the edge of her capacity. She's trying to save as many soldiers as possible, but she's being pushed beyond safe limits. One day, she breaks her limits—burns out her mana reserves—and collapses. She survives but with permanent damage: her healing magic is now much weaker. She must confront a reality where she can't save everyone and can't even fully save herself.

Key details:
- Naia is young (mid-20s) and idealistic.
- After her limit-break she develops a tremor in her hands and permanent nerve damage affecting her precision.
- She becomes bitter and depressed; her healing work turns mechanical and haunted.
- The scene underscores the human cost of extended crisis and the ethical boundary between sacrifice and self-preservation.
