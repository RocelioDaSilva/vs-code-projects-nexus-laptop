## THE WORKFLOW: How to Use These Tools

### PHASE 0: BEFORE YOU START WRITING ACT I (Planning Stage)

**Goal**: Ensure your foundation is solid before investing writing time.

**Tools to Use**:
1. **SANDERSON-SELF-EVALUATION-GUIDE.md** — Read PART 1 (Core Premise) completely
2. **Verify your 1-Sentence Pitch**:
   - "Chain Weapon Story is [familiar] but [unfamiliar]"
   - Share with someone unfamiliar with your story
   - Does it intrigue them? If not, revise
3. **Read PART 2 (Character Sliders)** on your three sliders
   - Proactivity: Should Aisen go from reactive → proactive across story ✓
   - Likability: Do you have 3+ scenes showing why people like him?
   - Competence: Is his unique superpower (systems thinking) going to be visible?

**Checklist Before Starting Act I**:
- [ ] You can state your pitch in one sentence
- [ ] You understand your three sliders and where you want Aisen on each
- [ ] You've identified your three major plot types
- [ ] You understand your core promises (ordinary, synthesis, systems, protection costs)

**Time Investment**: 2-3 hours reading + thinking

---

### PHASE 1: DRAFTING ACT I (Chapters 1-12 / Ages 5-15)

**Goal**: Complete first draft of act without self-editing.

**Tools to Use**:
1. **Before each chapter**: Quick check against SANDERSON-SELF-EVALUATION-GUIDE.md PART 3 (Plot Structure)
   - What's the inciting incident for this chapter?
   - How does it escalate stakes?
2. **After drafting each scene**: Open SCENE-EVALUATION-CHECKLIST.md
   - Use "Universal Scene Checklist" minimum (10 min per scene)
   - Mark PASS/FLAG; document decisions
   - Save audit as `audits/[SceneID]-audit-1.0.md`

**Writing Targets**:
- Estimated Act I: 3,000-5,000 words per chapter × 12-15 chapters = 36,000-75,000 words
- Pace: Chapter per week or 2 chapters per week if you're fast
- Don't stop to revise major issues; note them and keep writing

**Sample Week**:
- Monday: Draft Chapter (4,000 words)
- Tuesday: Run SCENE-EVALUATION-CHECKLIST on major scenes from Chapter
- Wednesday: Draft next chapter
- Thursday: SCENE-EVALUATION-CHECKLIST
- Friday: Integrate notes; prep next chapter
- Weekend: Buffer/catch-up or minor revisions only

**Checklist After Act I Draft**:
- [ ] All 12-15 chapters drafted to completion
- [ ] No major revisions; only notes saved
- [ ] Scene audits created for all major scenes
- [ ] Timeline verified against MASTER-CHRONOLOGY.md (Aisen ages 5-15 ✓)

**Time Investment**: 6-12 weeks depending on writing speed

---

### PHASE 2: ACT I CONTINUITY PASS (Revision 2.0)

**Goal**: Fix major plot holes and character inconsistencies you noticed while writing.

**Tools to Use**:
1. **Read SANDERSON-SELF-EVALUATION-GUIDE.md** PART 6 (Revision Process, steps 2.0)
2. **MANUSCRIPT-DIAGNOSTIC-TEMPLATE.md**:
   - Fill out all questions in sections 1-3 (Premise, Character, Plot)
   - This will identify your major continuity gaps
3. **Create Continuity Fix List**:
   - Example: "Ch.5 says Caspian disappears; Ch.7 contradicts this"
   - Sort by severity (plot-breaking vs. minor inconsistency)

**Major Tasks**:
- [ ] Timeline consistency (Aisen's age progression matches outline ✓)
- [ ] Character consistency (Elara's motivations coherent across chapters)
- [ ] Plot logic (Does B logically follow from A?)
- [ ] World consistency (Tavern geography, magic rules, Covenant behavior stable)

**Don't Touch Yet**:
- Prose-level editing (save for 3.0)
- Dialogue heavy rewrites (save for 3.0)
- Scene additions (only if absolutely required for plot to make sense)

**Checklist After Act I Continuity Pass**:
- [ ] MANUSCRIPT-DIAGNOSTIC completed; all major gaps identified
- [ ] Continuity issues fixed
- [ ] Timeline verified
- [ ] Character arc progression from Act I clear entering Act II
- [ ] Save as `audits/ACT-I-DIAGNOSTIC-2.0.md`

**Time Investment**: 1-2 weeks

---

### PHASE 3: ACT I POLISH (Revision 3.0)

**Goal**: Lean prose. Cut 10-15%. Show > tell. Concrete language.

**Tools to Use**:
1. **SANDERSON-SELF-EVALUATION-GUIDE.md** PART 4 (Prose Quality)
   - Read "Window Pane Principle" and "Pyramid of Abstraction" sections
2. **SCENE-EVALUATION-CHECKLIST.md** — Prose sections:
   - Run each major scene through "Dialogue-Heavy" and universal prose checks
3. **Focus Areas**:
   - Replace passive voice: "The decision was made" → "Aisen decided"
   - Add concrete details: Replace "He felt afraid" with physical sensation
   - Tighten dialogue: Remove unnecessary tags; use beats instead
   - Cut 10-15%: Every loop, look for sentences that don't earn their space

**Major Tasks**:
- [ ] Read Act I aloud (at least key chapters). Listen for rhythm
- [ ] Highlight passive voice; replace with active
- [ ] Find 5-10 "showing" opportunities; rewrite to show-not-tell
- [ ] Test dialogue: Read without names; can you identify speakers?
- [ ] Cut pass: Remove 10-15% of words

**Checklist After Act I Polish**:
- [ ] Prose is clear and direct (not flowery)
- [ ] 70%+ concrete details, 30%- abstract
- [ ] Dialogue voices distinct
- [ ] Word count reduced 10-15%
- [ ] Save Polish version as `story/ACT-I-3.0-polish/`

**Time Investment**: 1-2 weeks

---

### PHASE 4: ACT I ALPHA FEEDBACK (Revision 4.0)

**Goal**: Get external eyes before moving to Acts II+III.

**Tools to Use**:
1. **SANDERSON-SELF-EVALUATION-GUIDE.md** PART 6, section 4.0 (Alpha Readers)
2. **Create Alpha Feedback Form**:
   - What was your emotional experience? (pace, engagement)
   - Which character felt most real? Why?
   - At what point did you predict the ending?
   - What confused you?
   - Did the ending match the opening's promises?
3. **Select 2-3 Alpha readers**:
   - Someone who loves character-driven fantasy
   - Someone familiar with Sanderson's work
   - Someone who'll give honest critique

**Major Tasks**:
- [ ] Share Act I with alpha readers
- [ ] Give 2-3 weeks for reading + feedback
- [ ] Collect feedback; look for *patterns*
- [ ] Not: "Fix every comment" → Yes: "If 2+ readers mention X, it's a real problem"

**After Alpha Feedback**:
- [ ] Make structural changes based on patterns (not individual preferences)
- [ ] Document why you did/didn't incorporate each feedback point
- [ ] Verify: Did fixes make Act I stronger? (Read key scenes again)

**Checklist After Alpha Feedback**:
- [ ] Act I has been improved based on reader feedback
- [ ] Structural issues addressed
- [ ] Character confusion reduced
- [ ] Ready to draft Act II with confidence
- [ ] Save feedback summary as `audits/ACT-I-ALPHA-FEEDBACK-4.0.md`

**Time Investment**: 4-6 weeks (including reader turnaround)

## DO NOT MOVE TO ACT II UNTIL:

✓ You've completed PHASES 0-4 (or accepted you'll do Act II blind and catch issues in subsequent revision)

**Reason**: Brandon Sanderson strongly recommends NOT writing Acts II-III until Act I is solid. Otherwise, you'll find after writing 100,000 words that your foundation was flawed, forcing massive rewrites.

**Exception**: If you're a discovery writer who needs to write to find the story, go ahead to Act II but plan for major rewrites later.

## COMMON PITFALLS & HOW THESE TOOLS PREVENT THEM

### Pitfall 1: "My Act II is boring"
**Cause**: Often missing "Yes, but..." / "No, and..." escalation. Each scene outcome is flat.
**Prevention**: Use SCENE-EVALUATION-CHECKLIST section on "Escalation & Stakes" after every scene. Ensure each major outcome escalates.
**Diagnostic**: Run MANUSCRIPT-DIAGNOSTIC section 3.3 ("Momentum in Middle"). Zero "Stall" outcomes allowed.

### Pitfall 2: "Characters sound identical"
**Cause**: Dialogue lacks individual voice patterns and verbal tics.
**Prevention**: Before drafting dialogue-heavy scenes, reference CHARACTER-VOICE-BANK.md and write sample dialogue for each character.
**Diagnostic**: SCENE-EVALUATION-CHECKLIST "Voice Consistency" section. Can you identify speakers without names?

### Pitfall 3: "Prose is purple/overwrought"
**Cause**: Too much abstract language; not enough concrete detail.
**Prevention**: Use SCENE-EVALUATION-CHECKLIST "Concrete Grounding" section. Aim for 70% concrete, 30% abstract.
**Diagnostic**: After draft, count sentences. Rebalance if needed.

### Pitfall 4: "My climax doesn't feel earned"
**Cause**: Earlier acts didn't escalate stakes or show Aisen paying costs.
**Prevention**: Use MANUSCRIPT-DIAGNOSTIC sections 2 (Personal Cost Visibility) and 3 (Climax Clarity) to verify setup.
**Diagnostic**: Ask "Could Aisen's sacrifice feel cheap?" If yes, you need more cost/escalation earlier.

### Pitfall 5: "Readers are confused by my ending"
**Cause**: Opening promises weren't clearly established or fulfilled.
**Prevention**: Use SANDERSON-SELF-EVALUATION-GUIDE section 1 (Promise Fulfillment Matrix) to map promises → payoffs.
**Diagnostic**: After drafting, verify every major promise from opening is addressed by climax.
