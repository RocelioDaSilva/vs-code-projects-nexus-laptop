# SCENE-BY-SCENE EVALUATION CHECKLIST: Sanderson Principles Applied

**Purpose**: Quick checklist to run on each scene during drafting or revision  
**Use**: Paste into document before finalizing any scene  
**Status**: Active tool; update as you discover new evaluation questions  
**Revised**: April 2, 2026 (now based on Sanderson lectures)

---

## HOW TO USE THIS CHECKLIST

1. **After drafting a scene**, open this checklist.
2. **Select the scene type** (Character-focused, Action, Exposition, Dialogue-heavy, etc.)
3. **Run through the relevant checks** below.
4. **Mark as PASS or FLAG**.
5. **For each FLAG, decide: Fix it or Remove it?**
6. **Save your audit** as `audits/[SceneID]-audit-[version].md` when done.

---

## UNIVERSAL SCENE CHECKLIST (All Scenes)

### STRUCTURAL INTEGRITY

- [ ] **POV Anchor Clear**: Scene opens with interaction that establishes whose head we're in. (See: WRITING-GUIDELINES-PERSPECTIVE.md)
- [ ] **POV Consistent**: No head-hopping inside the scene (even into allies' minds).
- [ ] **Scene Purpose Defined**: Can you state in one sentence why this scene exists? (To reveal X about character; to escalate Y plot; to show Z relationship)

### PROMISE ALIGNMENT

- [ ] **Genre Tone Maintained**: Does this scene feel like the story's opening promised? (If opening was cozy tavern, is this scene maintaining grounded pragmatism or veering into epic spectacle?)
- [ ] **Theme Reinforced**: Does this scene touch on at least one core theme? (Prejudice as system, synthesis vs. perfection, protection-as-cost, ordinary-people-matter)

### ESCALATION & STAKES

- [ ] **Escalation Present**: Does this scene end with stakes *higher* than it began? (Using "Yes, but..." or "No, and..." logic from earlier checklist)
- [ ] **Momentum**: After this scene, does reader want to read the next one?
- [ ] **No Stalling**: If this is a "quiet" scene, does it still escalate something (relationship, understanding, obligation)?

## CHARACTER FOCUS SCENES

Use this for scenes primarily about character moments, relationships, revelations.

### CHARACTER SLIDERS (Proactivity, Likability, Competence)

**AISEN-FOCUSED SCENES**:
- [ ] **Proactivity**: Is Aisen making a meaningful choice in this scene, or just reacting to events? (Target: He initiates or decides >50% of what happens)
- [ ] **Likability**: Does reader see why allies like him? (Small kindness, clear goal, respect from others, growth?)
- [ ] **Competence**: Is Aisen's unique skill (pattern-reading, adaptation, synthesis) visible in this scene?

**SUPPORTING CHARACTER SCENES** (Elara, Rin, Caspian, etc.):
- [ ] **Proactivity**: Does this character make a choice that matters? (Not just responding to Aisen's actions?)
- [ ] **Agency**: Does this scene show why they're part of the network—not just as helpers, but as actors?
- [ ] **Character Arc Progress**: Does this scene move their arc forward? (If not, does it deserve page time?)

### PROMS Framework

- [ ] **Past**: Does dialogue/action reveal something shaped by character's past?
- [ ] **Relationships**: Does character show how they feel about people in the scene?
- [ ] **Obligations**: Are their moral codes, duties, or social rules visible?
- [ ] **Motivations**: Is it clear what they want *right now, in this scene*?
- [ ] **Sensibilities**: Can you sense their unique way of seeing the world?

**True if ≥3 PROMS elements present. If ≤2, the character depth is thin.**

### VOICE CONSISTENCY

- [ ] **Voice Distinct**: If dialogue tags removed, can you identify the speaker? (Use CHARACTER-VOICE-BANK.md as reference)
- [ ] **Vocabulary Match**: Does character use words consistent with their background/education/personality?
- [ ] **Speech Pattern**: Do they have a characteristic way of phrasing—questions, declaratives, hesitations?
- [ ] **Emotional Expression**: How does this character show emotion—through words, silence, action, deflection?

**If any flag: Revise dialogue to deepen voice.**

## ACTION/CONFLICT SCENES

Use this for combat, confrontation, chase, high-stakes sequences.

### CLARITY & STAKES

- [ ] **Geography Clear**: Reader can visualize where this is happening. Not cinematic overload, but clear spatial sense.
- [ ] **Stakes Explicit**: Before action starts, does reader *know* what winning/losing means?
- [ ] **Concrete Details**: Dialogue/internal monologue grounded in sensory details, not abstraction?

### SYSTEM THINKING (Aisen's Competence)

- [ ] **If Aisen is present**: Does his solution use *systems thinking*, not just brute force? (He reads geometry, physics, psychology of opponent—not just strength vs. strength)
- [ ] **Synthesis Visible**: If magic or weapon is involved, does he combine tools cleverly?
- [ ] **Physical Realism**: Do consequences feel grounded? (Injury lasts; exhaustion matters; limit-breaks cost)

### LIMIT-BREAKING PROTOCOL

- [ ] **If character breaks limits**: Cost is defined *before* power surge. Not hand-waved.
- [ ] **Cost is Visible**: Consequences ripple immediately (not just "he'll feel it later").
- [ ] **Future Impact**: Marked in documents so consequences carry through story.

**Example**: If Aisen pushes magic past his limit, define: Does he lose 10% permanent mana? Memory gaps? Aging? Document it clearly.

### EMOTIONAL WEIGHT

- [ ] **Not Glorified**: Violence/sacrifice is shown as costly, not thrilling. (Aisen doesn't revel in combat; he endures it)
- [ ] **Character Response**: Does POV character react emotionally after the action? (Adrenaline crashes; guilt; relief; resolve)

## DIALOGUE-HEAVY SCENES

Use this for conversations, negotiations, group meetings.

### MIRCO Framework (Motive, Individuality, Realism, Conflict, Objective)

Each character's speaking line should pass:

- [ ] **Motive**: Why are they saying this *right now*? What do they want from this conversation?
- [ ] **Individuality**: Distinct voice; can identify by speech pattern?
- [ ] **Realism**: Sounds like how people talk (not real, but believable)?
- [ ] **Conflict**: Tension present—even if subtle (agreement can have tension: "Yes... but" vs "Yes, definitely")?
- [ ] **Objective**: What is the scene itself accomplishing in the story?

**Fail test**: If you can't answer "Motive" for a line, consider deleting it.**Fail test**: If all characters answer the same way (everyone clever, everyone direct), differentiation is weak.

### Dialogue Mechanics

- [ ] **Tags Early**: Dialogue tag (who speaks) appears in first half of speech or before, not after.
- [ ] **Show Emotion Through Beat**: Where possible, replace tags with action beats. "He said tiredly" → [He rubbed his eyes.] "I'm tired."
- [ ] **"Said" Used 90%**: Check for bookedisms ("exclaimed," "whispered," "ejaculated")—replace with "said" or action.
- [ ] **Interruptions**: If characters interrupt each other, does it show conflict naturally? (Not overdone)
- [ ] **Realistic Rhythm**: Dialogue has pauses, incomplete thoughts, topic shifts—not every line advances plot perfectly.

### Information Loading

- [ ] **No Exposition Dumps**: Characters don't explain systems/backstory unless it serves their motivation in *this scene*.
- [ ] **Subtext Present**: What they *don't* say matters as much as what they do.
- [ ] **Question & Answer Balanced**: Conversation feels natural (not question-answer-question-answer interview format).

## EXPOSITION/WORLD-BUILDING SCENES

Use this for scenes where the story explains how the world works.

### Concrete Grounding

- [ ] **Show Through Example**: Don't explain prejudice; show character experiencing it.
- [ ] **Character Perspective**: World-building comes from character's POV character discovering/observing, not author explaining.
- [ ] **Physical Anchors**: Tie world concepts to place (magic at academy, prejudice at tavern, systems through social observation).

### Pace & Interest

- [ ] **Not a Textbook**: Information is *revealed*, not *lectured*. Character learns alongside reader.
- [ ] **Microconflict**: Even quiet scenes have small tension—character doubts, learns something unexpected, has to revise assumptions.
- [ ] **Escalation**: Does understanding something new raise stakes? (He learns X about system; now realizes Y is more dangerous)

---

## QUIET/DOWNTIME SCENES

Use this for tavern nights, preparation, recovery, character moments without major plot advancement.

### PURPOSE CLARITY

- [ ] **Scene Earns Its Page**: Why does story *need* this moment? (Relationship deepening, character voice establishment, system revelation, emotional release)
- [ ] **Not Filler**: If you deleted it, something would be lost—not just detail, but something important.

### Micro-Escalation

- [ ] **Even Quiet Scenes Escalate**: Small revelation, complication, or choice that raises stakes or deepens character.
- [ ] **Concrete Sensory Detail**: Tavern smells, food tastes, texture of worn chair—reader sits in the scene.
- [ ] **Emotional Texture**: Warmth, tension, melancholy—reader feels the mood.

### Character Interaction

- [ ] **Relationship Shift**: Does how characters interact show change from their previous meeting? (Not major, but *something* shifted)
- [ ] **Dialogue Reveals Without Telling**: Avoid "characters sit and discuss their feelings"—show it through banter, avoidance, unexpected honesty.

## PACING CHECK — Scene Placement

Run this for each scene to verify it's in the right position.

| Question | Answer | Action |
|---|---|---|
| What is the **intensity level** of this scene? (1=calm, 10=climax) | ___ | Should it be here, or moved closer to/further from climax? |
| How does this scene's intensity **relate to previous scene**? | Higher/Same/Lower | Should it escalate from previous, or provide breathing room? |
| Does this scene's outcome **enable the next scene**? | Yes/No/Unclear | If No, does it deserve to be here? |
| Could this scene be **combined with another**? | Yes/No | If Yes, would combining tighten pacing? |
| **Reading experience**: Does this scene feel like it drags? | Yes/No/Slightly | If Yes slightly, add escalation within. If Yes significantly, cut. |

---

## MASTER CHECKLIST SUMMARY

**A scene PASSES if it hits ≥80% of relevant checks above.**

**A scene FAILS if it:**
- Has no clear purpose
- Stalls momentum without reason
- Features all characters sounding identical
- Includes exposition without character motivation
- Has multiple POV head-hops
- Fails to escalate stakes despite appearing to

**When in doubt, ask: "Would the story be better or worse without this scene?" If worse, revise. If same, cut.**

## CONTINUOUS IMPROVEMENT

As you draft and receive feedback, add new questions to this checklist. 

**Examples of additions after alpha feedback:**
- "Do readers understand why Aisen makes this choice?" (If multiple alphas miss it, add check)
- "Does the Covenant feel like a credible threat?" (If multiple alphas think it's vague, add specificity check)
- "Are there enough quiet moments to emotionally ground the war scenes?" (If pacing feels rushed, add balance check)

**Goal**: This checklist becomes *your* quality bar over time, reflecting what matters most in your story's voice and execution.
