## Summary
The Provincial Corridor — eastern districts governed loosely under the Crown's nominal authority — is being systematically squeezed: merchant tariffs block supply chains, military deployment routes displace civilian populations, and Covenant-backed administrative changes realign district governance so that local leaders have no legal standing to resist Imperial directives. The Rebellion is not a single uprising; it is a slow combustion of accumulated cost, and Kael is inside it from the beginning.

**Core political reality**: The Crown does not want rebellion. The Covenant benefits from instability it can attribute to provincial lawlessness, which justifies expanded Covenant administrative oversight. The military, caught between two principals, deploys with incomplete orders and produces atrocities by institutional inertia rather than malice.

## Scene Map (7 escalating beats)

### Beat 1 — PUBLIC TEST (Ch 14B)
**Scene**: Already written. Kael in Ashen Ford, provincial intelligence work, contact in culvert. Orev compromised by stationed patrol. Kael estimates the damage and begins contingency routing.
**Personal cost**: Kael burns a nine-year contact route. Orev's safety is in question.
**Status**: Exists (14B-The-Provincial-Cost.md).

### Beat 2 — PRIVATE COST (Ch 18–20)
**Scene**: Amara's rider to Districts 5–6 confirms civilian non-evacuation. She files the hold. The deployment proceeds thirty-six hours later despite the hold — a superior officer overrides it citing "operational calendar compliance." The villages are not evacuation-complete when operations begin.
**Personal cost**: Amara is demoted one grade in the provisional assessment, officially for "individual action outside command protocol." She accepts it. She writes a full report anyway, filed six copies in six different office chains.
**Who pays**: Amara (career setback). The civilians in Districts 5–6.
**Dialogue anchor**: "How many?" Amara asks the courier about confirmed displaced. The number is wrong and they both know it.
**Status**: Amara's rider is in Ch 19B. The demotion scene needs to be written in Ch 18/20.

### Beat 3 — BETRAYAL CATALYST (Ch 26)
**Scene**: A village is destroyed. Not by direct military order — by the intersection of an imprecise operation boundary, a panicked local garrison, and the absence of civilian evacuation. Kael is three kilometers away. He hears it. He arrives at the aftermath.
**Personal cost**: Kael has a specific limitation: he does not allow himself grief in the field. He can feel it but the job is not done. He files the scene in the section of his working memory labeled *costs* and keeps moving. But the costs section is getting full and he is becoming harder.
**Who pays**: The village. Kael, in the long accumulation.
**Political mechanics**: The operation boundary imprecision was not an accident. The Covenant-backed administrative change to district governance had, thirty days earlier, altered the "civilian zone" designation for that area in a technical document revision that no one outside the administrative chain had seen.
**Status**: To draft — PRIORITY SCENE. Kael POV, ~3,500 words.

### Beat 4 — ESCALATION (Ch 32–35)
**Scene**: The provincial leaders who have been cooperative with Imperial administration hold an emergency session. Three of the seven vote to request Covenant mediation — which means inviting Covenant administrative representatives into governance positions. Four vote against. The session is not secret; it is formal. The four who voted against begin receiving "compliance inquiries" within a week.
**Personal cost**: The one cooperative provincial leader Kael has been working with — the one whose quiet resource-sharing has kept the eastern network supplied for two years — is removed from office via the compliance process.
**Who pays**: The removed leader (and his family). Kael's network loses its best institutional contact.
**Status**: Outline only.

### Beat 5 — COMPROMISE ATTEMPT (Ch 44)
**Scene**: Aisen, now operating at a higher network level, attempts to broker a quiet arrangement: he will bring provincial grievance documentation to a sympathetic Northern House intermediary (Elara's channel) in exchange for the provincial resistance standing down on three planned disruption operations. The arrangement requires both sides to trust a third party neither has fully verified.
**Personal cost**: Aisen is asking people who have been burned before to trust an arrangement they cannot independently verify. Two provincial contacts refuse. One agrees. The one who agrees is the oldest and most cautious contact in the network, and her agreement is the scene's emotional weight.
**Dialogue anchor**: "You're asking me to believe you, which I can manage. You're asking me to believe the arrangement, which requires me to believe people I have never met. That is a different thing." — The provincial contact. Aisen has no clean answer.
**Status**: Outline only.

### Beat 6 — COMPROMISE FAILS (Ch 48–50)
**Scene**: The disruption operations Kael was supposed to hold back — one goes forward anyway, carried out by a cell that was not under the arrangement's coverage and didn't know about it. The Covenant uses this to declare the arrangement void and accelerate administrative takeover of two districts.
**Personal cost**: Aisen's broker credibility is damaged. The contact who trusted him is exposed when the Covenant links the arrangement back to her channel. She is not caught immediately, but she goes to ground and the eastern network loses its most experienced coordinator for six months.
**Who pays**: The coordinator. Aisen, by guilt and by strategic loss.
**Status**: Outline only.

### Beat 7 — FALLOUT / IRREVERSIBLE CHANGE (Ch 50–55B)
**Scene**: The Provincial Rebellion moves from slow combustion to open fire in Districts 4 and 7. Kael is now running operations that are explicitly military rather than intelligence. The cost is different. The cost is immediate. He is very good at it. That is also a cost.
**Who pays**: Everyone. The arc does not end cleanly — it hands off into Act III's broader war arc with the provinces still partially occupied and the human cost still accumulating.
**Status**: Outline only.
