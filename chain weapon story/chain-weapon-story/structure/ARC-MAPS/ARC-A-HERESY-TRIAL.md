# Arc A: The Heresy Trial (Chapters 17–24)

## Summary
The Covenant brings a formal Heresy Trial against the Academy's research program — specifically targeting the synthesis-magic methodology Aisen and Kaelen have pioneered. Nominally about doctrinal compliance; actually about the Covenant preventing decentralized magic research that would reduce their institutional leverage over the provinces.

**Core political reality**: The trial is won or lost in the preparation period (Ch 17–20), not the hearing room. Evidence is suppressed, witnesses are pressured, and a mole inside the network (Ghost) is feeding the prosecution's team advance knowledge of the defense strategy.

---

## Scene Map (6 escalating beats)

### Beat 1 — PUBLIC TEST (Ch 17–18)
**Scene**: The Covenant files a formal research review request — standard procedure, precedented, impossible to refuse without looking guilty. Academy administration agrees to cooperate. Internal reality: some administrators are complicit; others genuinely believe cooperation will resolve the issue.
**Personal cost**: Kaelen is required to open his greenhouse research to Covenant inspectors. He complies. The breath-vine specimens are handled by inspectors who do not understand what they are observing, and one specimen — the recovering glass-blight plant — is damaged during inspection.
**Who pays**: Kaelen. Not career-ending, but personal. Eleven months of specific work.
**Dialogue anchor**: "You may observe. You may not interfere with active biological processes." — Kaelen to the inspector.
**Status**: Outline only.

### Beat 2 — PRIVATE COST (Ch 19–20)
**Scene**: Rin intercepts a message that suggests the prosecution has a copy of the defense preparation strategy before the defense team has finished writing it. This confirms Ghost is inside the network. She tells no one.
**Personal cost**: Rin knows she could stop the leak right now by revealing what she knows — but revealing means exposing her method, which ends her intelligence capacity permanently. She chooses method over this single leak. A student is questioned by Covenant investigators and removed that same day.
**Who pays**: The removed student, and Rin by guilt.
**Dialogue anchor**: "Write what you see. The wall doesn't care what you think of it." — Rin, internal monologue only.
**Status**: Seeds planted in Ch 17B (Vex survey) and Ch 21B (Rin ghost discovery).

### Beat 3 — BETRAYAL CATALYST (Ch 21–22)
**Scene**: The Public Hearing. Aisen is summoned to testify. The prosecution enters evidence that could only have come from the defense preparation file. The defense team realizes, in real time, they have a leak. Aisen looks at the prosecution table and calculates. The evidence was from three days ago. Three people saw the file.
**Personal cost**: Aisen has to decide whether to name a suspect from limited information or to protect the network by appearing to not notice the leak. He chooses the latter. This costs him — the Hearing goes badly.
**Faction logic**: Covenant prosecution team has been authorized to use Ghost intelligence precisely because they expected the defense to be strong. They needed to break it early.
**Personal motive**: Ghost is not ideologically Covenant. Ghost is afraid of something specific — to be identified in Ch 34B (Rin follow-up).
**Who pays**: Aisen (strategic setback). Junior research students whose work is now suspect.
**Status**: To draft — PRIORITY SCENE. See note below.

### Beat 4 — ESCALATION (Ch 23)
**Scene**: Following the hearing, the Covenant issues a provisional citation against the Academy research program. Two senior faculty are "invited" to a review board. The invitation is non-optional. The Academy administration, split between compliance-willing and resistance-willing factions, fails to present a unified response. The compliance faction, out of genuine fear and some complicity, accepts the invitation on behalf of the faculty.
**Personal cost**: One faculty member who has been Kaelen's sponsor retires immediately, citing health. No one believes the health reason.
**Who pays**: The Faculty sponsor. Kaelen, who loses his institutional protection.
**Status**: Outline only.

### Beat 5 — COMPROMISE ATTEMPT (Ch 24)
**Scene**: Elara arrives at the Academy on a formal visit from the Northern House, which is technically above the Covenant's jurisdiction. She uses the visit to trigger a procedural challenge — the Heresy Trial cannot proceed while a Great House representative is formally requesting its documentation under the Inter-House Review Protocol. This stalls the trial by approximately thirty days.
**Personal cost**: Elara has used a significant political card early. The Covenant will remember it. Aldric Fenn watches her with a new assessment in his eyes during the corridor meeting afterward.
**Who pays**: Elara — the thirty-day window is the win, but the relationship with the Covenant is now openly adversarial.
**Political mechanics**: The Inter-House Review Protocol is real, legal, and has not been invoked against the Covenant before. It is not a permanent solution. It is a thirty-day breather.
**Status**: Outline only.

### Beat 6 — FALLOUT (Ch 24, closing)
**Scene**: The trial is formally paused. The research program continues under restricted conditions. Ghost is not caught. Rin has five candidates and no proof. The Covenant consolidates its hold on the Provincial assessors and begins building the next approach, which is not a trial but a supply disruption (handing off to Arc D).
**Who pays**: Everyone, but diffusely. The threat has not been removed; it has been temporarily redirected.
**Status**: Outline only.

## Ghost — Narrative Logic
Ghost is not a Covenant loyalist. Ghost is afraid of something specific that the Covenant has leverage over — a family member in a provincial garrison, a debt, a previous record. The Lie Ghost tells is: *staying invisible keeps the people I protect safe.* The Truth is: staying invisible is harming more people faster than exposure would. This mirrors Rin's Lie at a lower structural level — two characters maintaining secrecy for protective reasons, both wrong in related ways.

Ghost's identity should not be revealed until Ch 34B (Rin's follow-up B-chapter). The revelation should feel inevitable in retrospect but not telegraphed from Ch 21B.
