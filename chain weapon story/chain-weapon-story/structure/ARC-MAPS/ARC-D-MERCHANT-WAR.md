# Arc D: The Merchant War (Chapters 26–58)

## Summary
The Covenant's economic wing has been methodically cornering the Provincial Corridor's supply chains: tariff structures that benefit Covenant-affiliated merchant houses, route disruptions that strand independent merchant traffic, and documentation requirements that favor large institutional traders over local networks. This is not an accident — it is a deliberate economic strategy, and it is working. By Chapter 40, Covenant-affiliated houses control approximately 60% of the food and material supply into the eastern provinces.

The Merchant War is slow, mundane, and lethal. It does not make dramatic scenes easily. The task is to find the human-scale consequence of each economic event and focus the narrative there.

---

## Scene Map (6 escalating beats)

### Beat 1 — THE MECHANISM (Ch 26–28)
**Scene**: Kael, during a provincial supply run, discovers that a previously-reliable local merchant — a woman named Yara who has been running grain routes for twelve years — is no longer operating. Her warehouse is being managed by a Covenant-affiliated forwarding house. He talks to her. She is not dead, not imprisoned, not threatened in any obvious way. She sold the route because she could not afford the new documentation compliance costs, and the Covenant forwarding house made a fair offer. She sounds almost fine. She sounds like someone who has made peace with a decision that has a large and invisible cost.
**Personal cost**: Yara. The human scale of economic displacement.
**Vex connection**: Vex, who has been tracking building modifications at the Academy, identifies three warehouses in the city that have undergone structural modification consistent with expanded cold-storage capacity — much more than a forwarding house would need for its stated business. She files this as a structural maintenance note. It is evidence of something she does not yet have words for.
**Status**: Outline only.

### Beat 2 — FIRST ESCALATION (Ch 29)
**Scene (PRIORITY — first to draft)**: The Blockade. A Covenant forwarding consortium formally lodges a documentation violation complaint against seventeen independent merchant runs in the Provincial Corridor. The complaint is technically valid — the merchants are not using the new compliance forms — and it results in a sixty-day hold on their routes. The hold is legal. The timing is calculated: it covers the main autumn supply window. The provinces will receive roughly 40% of their expected autumn goods.
**Personal cost**: Multiple small merchants. Also Amara, who calculated the supply gap in her assessment and is watching her calculation be confirmed in real time while her deployment-hold report sits in a filing archive marked *submitted.*
**Dialogue anchor**: Amara to a supply officer: "This is the number I projected in March." Supply officer: "Lieutenant Amara, the hold is within legal parameters." Amara: "Yes."
**Status**: To draft — PRIORITY SCENE, ~3,500 words, Amara POV.

### Beat 3 — SABOTAGE (Ch 34–36)
**Scene**: A provincial contact of Kael's — not a rebel, a shopkeeper — attempts to run a supply shipment through an informal route that bypasses the blockaded roads. The shipment is seized by a Covenant customs officer at a checkpoint that was established on that route three weeks after the shopkeeper began planning the run. Someone fed the checkpoint location to the Covenant.
**Personal cost**: The shopkeeper's entire capital investment is seized. His family has three weeks of reserve food.
**Vex dual-POV B-moment (Ch 36B planned)**: Vex, separately, traces the checkpoint's location back to a structural survey she filed — the survey of the Academy annex, the clean version, which she submitted through official channels. The checkpoint matches the route flagged in an administrative note attached to her survey at the Facilities office. She did not write that note. Someone added it after submission.
**Who pays**: The shopkeeper. Vex — by recognizing that her clean survey was used as intelligence.
**Status**: Ch 36B outline only. Core Kael scene outline only.

### Beat 4 — COUNTER-MOVE (Ch 40–42)
**Scene**: Vex, Amara, and Kael — operating through separate channels — independently arrive at the same conclusion: the Covenant economic strategy depends on the compliance documentation system, and the documentation system has a specific structural vulnerability. The compliance forms require a licensed certification official to sign off, and there are only eleven licensed certification officials in the entire Provincial Corridor, all of whom are now employed by or affiliated with Covenant-backed firms.
**Plan**: Aisen's network identifies three people with the legitimate credentials to become certification officials. The credentialing process takes sixty days. They run it.
**Personal cost**: The three people who go through credentialing put themselves in a visible institutional position. Visible is dangerous. They know this. They do it anyway.
**Status**: Outline only.

### Beat 5 — RETALIATION (Ch 46–50)
**Scene**: The Covenant challenges the credentials of the newly-certified officials via a technical objection — a paper trail issue in one applicant's credentialing record. The challenge is sustained. The counter-move fails.
**Personal cost**: The three people who credentialed are now known and have no institutional protection. One disappears from her town within a week after the ruling. The other two are not threatened directly — but they stop answering correspondence.
**Who pays**: The three credentialing volunteers. Aisen (strategic failure and guilt). The supply situation in the provinces deteriorates further.
**Status**: Outline only.

### Beat 6 — BREAKTHROUGH (Ch 54–58)
**Scene**: The counter-strategy that eventually works is not legal — it is a parallel supply network that does not use Covenant-registered routes at all, operating through the Beast-Kin territories (Arc E, which Elara has been negotiating). The breakthrough is a direct result of the Alliance secured in Arc E — the Beast-Kin have their own supply lines that the Covenant has no jurisdiction over.
**Personal cost**: The arrangement requires the provincial network to accept Beast-Kin traders into human-controlled market zones on equal commercial footing. This is politically explosive among some provincial residents. Kael has to argue for it. It is the argument he finds hardest.
**Dialogue anchor**: Kael to a skeptical provincial contact: "The alternative is your children eating what the Covenant allows them to eat." Contact: "This is not what we fought for." Kael: "What you fought for doesn't have a route right now. This does."
**Status**: Outline only — depends on Arc E resolution.

## Thematic note
The Merchant War's purpose narratively is to make the political feel material. Tariffs and documentation procedures kill people as surely as swords — more slowly, less visibly. Every scene in this arc should contain a named human being whose specific situation is being described in terms of food, shelter, route, capital. The Covenant is not evil in cartoonish terms; it is an institution optimizing for institutional goals, and the optimization is incompatible with human welfare in the provinces.
