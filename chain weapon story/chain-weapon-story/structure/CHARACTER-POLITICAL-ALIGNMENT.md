**Cynthia**
- Primary: Resistance network recruiter

- Primary: Resistance network (Kaelen's network)

- Primary: Military institutional reform (independent)
- Secondary: Resistance network allied (but separate)

- **Tension**: Reform success depends on factors outside her control (Registry decisions, commander receptiveness)

**Serath** (early arc)
- Primary: Crown military traditionalist
- Secondary: Defects to Resistance/Provincial Confederation alliance
- **Transition**: Changes faction affiliation midway through Act III

**Elara**
- Primary: Crown faction (legitimate appearance)
- Secondary: Noble House Valorin (private authority)
- **Tension**: Must maintain democratic facade while enabling authoritarian consolidation

## FACTION INTERACTION MATRIX

### Who Works With Whom
```
                Crown  Merchant  Provincial  Resistance  Reform  Houses  External
Crown           —      Conflict    Conflict    Conflict   Oppose  Allied  Varied
Merchant        ↔        —         Conflict    Conflict   Oppose  Mixed   Varied
Provincial      ↔        ↔          —          Oppose    Support  Oppose  Varies
Resistance      ↔        ↔          ↔           —         Allied  Oppose  Oppose
Reform          ↔        ↔          ↔           ↔         —       Oppose  Conflict
Houses          ↔        ↔          ↔           ↔         ↔       —      Compete
External        ↔        ↔          ↔           ↔         ↔       ↔      —

↔ = Conflict/Opposed
Allied = Working together
Mixed = Some houses ally, some oppose
— = Self (faction with itself)
```

### Conflict Intensity by Faction Pair
1. **Highest**: Crown ↔ Resistance, Merchant ↔ Provincial (irreconcilable goals)
2. **High**: Crown ↔ Provincial, Resistance ↔ Merchant (competing systems)
3. **Medium**: Reform ↔ Shukei, Houses ↔ Houses (economic/political competition)
4. **Variable**: External powers ↔ all (depends on national interests)

## PHILOSOPHICAL INSIGHT BY FACTION

**Crown**: "Stability requires centralized decision-making; democraticy is window dressing"  
**Merchant**: "Economic leverage is the only reliable power; control money, control politics"  
**Provincial**: "Local autonomy is freedom; imposed unity is oppression"  
**Resistance**: "Distributed information prevents any single power monopoly"  
**Reform**: "Systems improve through evidence-based analysis, not ideology"  
**Houses**: "Individual family power transcends ideology; adapt to whatever system emerges"  
**External**: "Chaos in others' territories = opportunity for our influence"

**The tragedy**: All positions are defensible. All are partly right. No faction can prove another fundamentally wrong through argument alone. Power persists through rational self-interest, not moral superiority.

## FINAL NOTE

**This map is a complexity tool, not a simplification.**

It intentionally shows that:
- Characters have competing loyalties
- Institutions have multiple masters
- Factions have internal subdivisions
- Alliances are temporary and conditional
- No faction wins completely
- Politics operate beneath surface narratives

Use it to make the political landscape feel **real** and **complex**, not like a simple good-vs-evil binary.
