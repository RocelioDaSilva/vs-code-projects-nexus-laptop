# Side Character Moments: Micro-Scene Bank & Systemic Reveal

## Overview
This document contains a curated bank of 40-80 micro-scenes (vignettes, 500-1200 words each) featuring side characters, servants, minor merchants, refugees, and ordinary people. These scenes fill downtime in the narrative, reveal systemic corruption through lived experience, and create a sense of a densely populated world where even unnamed characters have lives, concerns, and agency.

**Purpose of Micro-Scenes**:
1. **Systemic reveal**: Show how the Covenant's corruption, the ecological crisis, and class struggle affect ordinary people.
2. **Downtime filling**: Avoid time skips by showing daily life during Aisen's quieter periods.
3. **Dramatic irony**: Reader learns about conspiracies and plot points before characters do.
4. **Familiarity through recurrence**: Same characters, appearing in multiple scenes, become recognizable.
5. **Cultural depth**: Show different regions and classes living their daily lives.

**Scene Tagging System**:
Each micro-scene is tagged with:
- `[downtime]` — fills narrative gap; no major plot event.
- `[recurrence]` — character has appeared before or will appear again.
- `[location:<slug>]` — geographic region or institution.
- `[character:<name> (<role>)]` — protagonist of the micro-scene.
- `[theme:<topic>]` — systemic issue revealed.

## Tavern Micro-Scenes (6 scenes)

### Scene 1: The Empty Coin Jar
**[downtime] [recurrence] [location:Tavern] [character:Marta (Barmaid)] [theme:Economic Decline]**

**Context**: Placed during Aisen's age 8-10, when his father's business is struggling due to ecological crisis affecting travel.

**Summary**: Marta counts coins at closing time. The jar isn't as full as it should be for the season. She mentions to Aisen's father that fewer merchants are passing through—the routes are becoming dangerous due to beast encroachment. She worries about her siblings back in the farm village; food is becoming scarce.

**Key details**:
- Marta has a chipped tooth from an old accident; she's self-conscious about smiling.
- She's been at the tavern for 6 years; Aisen has grown up watching her.
- She reveals that her family's farm has been "bought out" by a noble's agent at a low price—implied Covenant influence.
- She asks Aisen's father for an advance on her wages; he reluctantly refuses, saying he barely has enough.

**Dramatic irony**: Readers see the economic squeeze before Aisen understands it. This becomes relevant later when similar squeezes occur across the kingdom, and we realize the Covenant is deliberately destabilizing regions.

**Later recurrence**: Marta appears in Scene 47 (age 14-15), as a refugee at an Academy shelter, telling a similar story of loss.

### Scene 2: The Merchant's Shadow
**[downtime] [recurrence] [location:Tavern] [character:Thorne (Traveling Merchant)] [theme:Intelligence Networks]**

**Context**: Age 9-11; Aisen overhears conversations.

**Summary**: Thorne, a regular merchant, sits nervously at the bar. He orders extra ale and keeps glancing at the door. He tells Aisen's father that he's heard travelers are being "asked questions" on the roads—questions about who they've met, what they've seen, what they've heard. Some merchants have disappeared entirely.

**Key details**:
- Thorne wears a distinctive blue cloak and always carries a worn leather journal.
- He's from the Heartland, but travels extensively.
- He hints that there's an organization asking these questions—something new, something with reach.
- He decides not to travel the northern route anymore; he'll stay closer to the capital.

**Dramatic irony**: Readers understand the Covenant is building surveillance networks. Later, when we follow Amara's PoV, we'll learn this is exactly what the Covenant's agents are doing—mapping traders, building information webs.

**Later recurrence**: Thorne appears in Scene 38, caught by the Covenant and forced to become an informant.

### Scene 6: The Aftermath of Aisen's Departure
**[downtime] [recurrence] [location:Tavern] [character:Various Regulars - Collective Scene] [theme:Community Loss]**

**Dramatic irony**: Readers understand that Aisen is leaving behind a community that cares for him—people who have become familiar. This adds weight to his absence and the dangers he'll face. Some of these people will appear later in the story as refugees or casualties.

### Scene 7: The Library Keeper's Observation
**[downtime] [recurrence] [location:Academy] [character:Master Tholen (Head Librarian)] [theme:Knowledge Control]**

**Context**: Placed during the protagonist's early Academy years (Age 17-18).

**Summary**: Master Tholen organizes books in the restricted section. He notices that certain books on archaeological magic (Covenant origins) have been checked out by teacher-members of the Covenant and never returned. He also notices that new books on "proper" power hierarchies have been added to the curriculum section. He begins quietly cataloging which books are being hidden and who is doing the hiding.

**Key details**:
- Tholen has been at the Academy for 40 years; he has a perfect memory for books.
- He wears glasses held together with wire and has ink stains on his collar.
- He's cautious but not cowardly; he's gathering information.
- He treats Aisen with unusual interest (perhaps sensing something in him).

**Dramatic irony**: Readers understand the Covenant is rewriting Academy history. Later, Aisen will seek Tholen's help in uncovering this.

**Later recurrence**: Tholen appears in Scene 39 and Scene 65, as Aisen's ally in the resistance.

**Context**: Age 15-16 (during Aisen's border travels with Harald).

**Summary**: Emma leads a group of refugees fleeing a region where the ecosystem has broken down. Plants are dying despite plentiful rain; animals are becoming more aggressive and mutated. **Key details**:
- Emma is a farmer, weathered, strong, grieving.

**Dramatic irony**: Readers learn about the ecological crisis through lived experience. Later, when Aisen studies this crisis at the Academy, he'll understand what Emma was experiencing.
