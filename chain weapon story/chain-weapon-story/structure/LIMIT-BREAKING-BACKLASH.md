# LIMIT-BREAKING & BACKLASH — Specification

Purpose
-------
This document formalizes how characters may exceed their normal limits, the immediate and lasting costs of doing so, and how those consequences should appear in the narrative. The goal is consistency: every limit‑breaking action must have measurable mechanical effects and narratively meaningful, often permanent, costs.

Cost Categories
---------------
- Physical: tissue damage, broken bones, permanent loss of mobility or sensory function.
- Cognitive/Psychic: memory loss, dissociation, seizures, personality shifts.
- Metaphysical: loss of access to Weave/Flux, soul‑anchoring damage, unintended transformations.
- Environmental/Social: dead zones, social stigma, political repercussions.

Severity Scale
--------------
- Minor — immediate pain, healing days→weeks, small permanent scar or reduced stat (-5% to -10%).
- Moderate — structural damage, months recovery, noticeable permanent loss (functional impairment, -10% to -30%).
- Severe — long-term disability, permanent loss of a discrete system (e.g., precision telekinesis), altered social status, narrative hooks (-30% to -70%).
- Catastrophic — irreversible bodily/metaphysical change, local/region-level consequences, or permanent world-change.

Decision Rules
--------------
1. No plot armor: limit‑breaking creates real constraints that affect future scenes.
2. Costs must be narratively useful (choices, trust, political fallout).
3. Severity should scale with intent and consumed resource (time, life force, Flux strain).
4. Recovery is possible but costly, slow, and often incomplete.

Common Limit‑Break Actions (examples)
-------------------------------------
| Action | Mechanic | Immediate Cost | Long‑term Cost | Narrative Hook | Recovery |
|---|---|---:|---|---|---|
| Telekinetic Overload — "Push Beyond" | Sustain telekinetic field beyond safe output | Hemorrhage, fainting, temporary paralysis | Focal seizures, reduced max output | Loses fine motor control at crucial moments | Months; risk permanent deficit |
| Chain‑State Snap | Force a chain state change through physical/Flux shock | Deep lacerations, entanglement | Scar tissue, chronic pain, a state disabled | Weapon behaves differently; forces tactical pivot | Surgery/ritual; rarely full restoration |
| Weave Rending | Tear local Flux to amplify effect | Localized voiding, sensory bleed | Regional instability; accelerated aging in user | Places become scarred; politicized event | Often irreversible; containment rituals help |
| Blood Bargain | Offer life/years for power surge | Immediate surge; blackouts | Shortened lifespan, mutated traits | Visible mark; moral cost; NPC reaction | Cannot be undone; rare counter‑rituals exist |
| Soul‑Anchor Stress | Force or sever an anchor (POV/tether manipulation) | Dissociation, fragmentation | Memory gaps, identity erosion | Character doubts memories/loyalties | Long rituals; some memories irretrievable |
| Elastic Recoil | Forcing chain inertia beyond tolerance | Broken bones, joint damage | Chronic nerve pain, limp | Limits physical agility; forces new combat style | Physical therapy; incomplete recovery |

Guidelines for Authors
----------------------
- Quantify: attach small numeric tags when helpful (editorial aids, e.g., -10% precision). Not required in prose.
- Anchor: place a short sensory line in the scene that signals the cost ("His left hand stopped answering him.").
- Payoff: use permanent costs later (choices, reduced options, visible scars, social consequences).

Sample Vignettes
-----------------
- Pre‑Academy — Minor: Aisen forces a small telekinetic Push to save a child from a falling cart. He collapses afterward, lives with headaches for a week, and loses a small degree of precision in his off‑hand for months.
- Academy — Moderate: During a spar Aisen attempts a Chain‑State Snap to disarm a senior cadet. The maneuver mangles his dominant wrist; he keeps the spear but loses one chain state until a risky ritual months later.
- Late Arc — Catastrophic: To close a Flux breach, Aisen performs a Weave Rending. The region becomes a dead scar; Aisen's ability to sense Flux is forever diminished and a loved one is lost to timestream slippage.

Recovery & Healing
------------------
- Physical recovery follows narrative needs but should respect the severity scale.
- Metaphysical recovery requires external resources (rituals, rare reagents, institutional aid) and should be narratively gated.

Verification Checklist (quick)
----------------------------
1. Is the cost signaled clearly in scene text? 2. Does the cost have mechanical and narrative impact later? 3. Is severity consistent with the action? 4. Are recovery routes plausible and costly?
