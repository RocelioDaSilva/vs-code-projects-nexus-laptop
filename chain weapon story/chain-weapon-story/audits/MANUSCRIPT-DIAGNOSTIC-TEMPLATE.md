# MANUSCRIPT DIAGNOSTIC: Act Completion Checklist

**Purpose**: Run this comprehensive diagnostic after completing each Act (1, 2, 3) to identify systemic strengths and weaknesses before revision  
**Use**: After full act draft done; before revision pass  
**Output**: Diagnostic report identifying priority improvements  
**Based on**: Brandon Sanderson's 7 Universal Story Categories + Chain Weapon Story specifics  

---

## HOW TO USE THIS DIAGNOSTIC

1. **After completing Act draft**, print or open this document.
2. **Answer each section carefully** (not quick yes/no—think deeply).
3. **Tally results** at the end.
4. **Review priority areas** and update project plan.
5. **Save diagnostic** as `audits/ACT-[I|II|III]-DIAGNOSTIC-[version].md`.

## DIAGNOSTIC SECTION 1: PREMISE & PROMISE DELIVERY

### Questions for Act I

**Question 1.1: Opening Tone**
- In chapter 1, what tone does the story establish?
  - [ ] Pragmatic, grounded (tavern daily life, ordinary stakes)
  - [ ] High fantasy spectacle (magic, destiny, epic)
  - [ ] Dark/grimdark (everything is suffering)
  - [ ] Other: _________

- Does this tone *continue consistently* through Act I's climax (robbery)?
  - [ ] Yes, completely
  - [ ] Mostly (slight shifts but direction consistent)
  - [ ] Somewhat (noticeable tone shifts)
  - [ ] No (acts like different book)

**Diagnostic Note**: If tone shifts significantly mid-act, readers will feel disorientation. If opening promises grounded pragmatism but Act I ends in epic spectacle, you've made an implicit genre-shift promise. Verify this is intentional.

**Question 1.2: Core Promises Made**
- Does your story establish these promises clearly by Act I climax?
  - [ ] Aisen is ordinary, not chosen (no secret bloodline, destiny, or OP power)
  - [ ] Synthesis of small tools beats singular perfection
  - [ ] Ordinary people matter; systems are the real antagonist
  - [ ] Protection requires sacrifice and costs things
  - [ ] The world is intelligible (systems can be understood)

**Diagnostic Note**: If ≥3 promises are unclear, your opening is doing too much. Cut elements; focus on 3-4 core promises.

**Question 1.3: Ending Alignment**
- Now that Act I is drafted, will Act III climax (Aisen's sacrifice) *feel like* a natural continuation of Act I's promises OR will it feel like betrayal?
  - [ ] Natural continuation (promises are consistent)
  - [ ] Mostly consistent (surprise but earned)
  - [ ] Somewhat disconnected (climax feels like different book)
  - [ ] Betrayal (reader will feel tricked)

**Diagnostic Action**: If misalignment, identify which Act I promise needs reinforcing in Act II.

---

### Questions for Act II & III

**Question 1.4: Midpoint Reversal** (Act II only)
- Does Act II have a clear midpoint where something Aisen believed is *reversed* or *complicated*?
  - [ ] Yes, clear midpoint reversal around chapter 24-26
  - [ ] Somewhat (reversal exists but unclear when)
  - [ ] No (things just keep escalating)

**Diagnostic Note**: Sanderson calls this the "liar revealed" moment. Something Aisen trusted turns out to be half-truth. If missing, Act II will feel like repetitive escalation rather than new chapter of understanding.

**Question 1.5: Act III Promise Fulfillment**
- By act III climax, has every major promise from Acts I+II been addressed?
  - [ ] Yes (climax directly tests all major promises)
  - [ ] Mostly (climax tests 3-4 of 5+ promises)
  - [ ] Some (climax tests 1-2 promises)
  - [ ] No (climax feels disconnected from early promises)

**Diagnostic Action**: If Act III climax tests <3 major promises, rewrite climax to compound the tests. Aisen's sacrifice should be the ultimate expression of his core conflict: Protection requires sacrifice.

## DIAGNOSTIC SECTION 2: CHARACTER HEALTH

### Aisen Protagonist Check

**Question 2.1: Proactivity Progression**
- Map Aisen's proactivity across three acts. Is he increasingly *driving* events vs. *reacting*?

  Act I: [ ] Mostly reactive | [ ] Mixed | [ ] Mostly proactive
  Act II: [ ] Mostly reactive | [ ] Mixed | [ ] Mostly proactive
  Act III: [ ] Mostly reactive | [ ] Mixed | [ ] Mostly proactive

**Diagnostic Target**: Arc should be Reactive → Mixed → Proactive. If not, you're not showing growth.

**Question 2.2: Likability & Agency**
- List 3 scenes where reader sees why allies like/trust Aisen: 
  1. _____________ (Act: _)
  2. _____________ (Act: _)
  3. _____________ (Act: _)

- List 3 scenes where Aisen makes a choice that costs him personally:
  1. _____________ (Act: _)
  2. _____________ (Act: _)
  3. _____________ (Act: _)

**Diagnostic Note**: If those lists are sparse (you're struggling to fill them), Aisen is too much a loner or too competent-without-heart. Add scenes showing him care, teach, or sacrifice for others.

**Question 2.3: Competence Clarity**
- List 5 scenes where Aisen's unique competency (pattern-reading, synthesis, adaptation) saves the day:
  1. _____________ (Act: _, scene outcome: ______)
  2. _____________ (Act: _, scene outcome: ______)
  3. _____________ (Act: _, scene outcome: ______)
  4. _____________ (Act: _, scene outcome: ______)
  5. _____________ (Act: _, scene outcome: ______)

**Diagnostic Action**: If lists have <3 items filled, his superpower isn't visible enough. Aisen should have at least one "moment" per act where his weird way of thinking solves what brute force can't.

### Supporting Character Check

For each major ally (Elara, Rin, Caspian, Kaelen, etc.):

**Question 2.4: Character Arc Visible**
- Character: ________

  Do they change by end of act? [ ] Clear arc | [ ] Partial | [ ] No change
  
  Can you state their change in one sentence? 
  "_________ learned that _________."

  Is this change *earned through their choice* or just *pushed by plot*?
  [ ] Earned | [ ] Partially earned | [ ] Plot-pushed

**Diagnostic Action**: If change is plot-pushed, write one scene where character chooses to change (costs them something).

**Question 2.5: Voice Distinctness**
- For top 4 allies, test voice distinctness.
- Write a dialogue line for each without names. Can you (or a reader) identify the speaker?
  
  Elara: "_____________________" → Identifiable? [ ] Yes | [ ] Maybe | [ ] No
  Rin: "_____________________" → Identifiable? [ ] Yes | [ ] Maybe | [ ] No
  Caspian: "_____________________" → Identifiable? [ ] Yes | [ ] Maybe | [ ] No
  Kaelen: "_____________________" → Identifiable? [ ] Yes | [ ] Maybe | [ ] No

**Diagnostic Action**: If 2+ are "Maybe" or "No," use CHARACTER-VOICE-BANK.md to strengthen voice differentiation before revision.

## DIAGNOSTIC SECTION 3: PLOT STRUCTURE HEALTH

### Plot Type Coverage

**Question 3.1: Multi-Plot Verification**
- Map your three plot types (primary driving plot + secondary + tertiary). Are all active?

  Primary Plot Type: _____________ (Is this escalating each act? [ ] Yes | [ ] No)
  Secondary Plot Type: _____________ (Is this intersecting with primary? [ ] Yes | [ ] No)
  Tertiary Plot Type: _____________ (Is this adding depth/theme? [ ] Yes | [ ] No)

**Diagnostic Note**: Sanderson warns: Too many plot types and they strangle each other. One primary + one secondary is ideal. If three main plots are all demanding equal page time, clarify which is actually primary.

### Inciting Incident Quality

**Question 3.2: Act Inciting Incidents**
- Act I Inciting Incident (Chapter 3): The Robbery
  - Is it *personal* (threatens Aisen directly)? [ ] Yes | [ ] No
  - Does it raise a question about the story (not just "what happens next")? [ ] Yes | [ ] No
  - Example question it raises: ___________________________

- Act II Inciting Incident (Academy arrival or Covenant discovery):
  - Is it *personal AND systemic*? [ ] Yes | [ ] Partially | [ ] No
  - Does it complicate the stakes from Act I? [ ] Yes | [ ] No
  - Example: Does reader now care about not just Aisen's survival but the broader threat?

- Act III Inciting Incident (War acceleration):
  - Is time pressure visible? (Situation becomes *urgent*) [ ] Yes | [ ] No
  - Is Aisen's final choice now forced? (Can't delay) [ ] Yes | [ ] No

**Diagnostic Action**: If any II lacks these qualities, rewrite it. Inciting incidents are *the most important scenes* for pacing.

### "Yes, But..." / "No, And..." Escalation

**Question 3.3: Momentum in Middle**
- Pick the middle scene of each act (rough midpoint). What was the outcome?
  - Act I Midpoint (Ch ~5): Outcome was... [ ] Success escalating stakes | [ ] Failure worsening situation | [ ] Stall
  - Act II Midpoint (Ch ~21-22): Outcome was... [ ] Success escalating stakes | [ ] Failure worsening situation | [ ] Stall
  - Act III Midpoint (Ch ~38-40): Outcome was... [ ] Success escalating stakes | [ ] Failure worsening situation | [ ] Stall

**Diagnostic Target**: *Zero "Stall" outcomes.* Every major scene should escalate (either via "Yes, but..." or "No, and...").

**Diagnostic Action**: If you have stalls, those scenes need outcome revision. Rewrite so something *meaningfully changes* for the worse OR better.

### Climax Clarity

**Question 3.4: Act Climax Definition**
- Act I Climax: What is the single *choice* or *moment* that defines the climax?
  (Don't say "the robbery"—say "Aisen chooses to help father" or "decides Harald's worth trusting")
  
  Answer: _______________________________

- Act II Climax: Single choice/moment?
  
  Answer: _______________________________

- Act III Climax: Single choice/moment?
  
  Answer: _______________________________

**Diagnostic Action**: If you can't complete these in one punchy sentence, your climax is a *sequence* rather than a *moment*. Sanderson recommends having ONE central moment that everything converges on. Identify it; tighten everything toward it.

### Sub-Plot Vitality

**Question 3.5: Subplot Checklist**
- List all major subplots (e.g., Elara's political rise, Rin's spy network, Caspian's redemption):

  1. _________________________ | Escalates each act? [ ] Yes | [ ] No | Active in all three acts? [ ] Yes | [ ] Partially | [ ] No
  2. _________________________ | Escalates each act? [ ] Yes | [ ] No | Active in all three acts? [ ] Yes | [ ] Partially | [ ] No
  3. _________________________ | Escalates each act? [ ] Yes | [ ] No | Active in all three acts? [ ] Yes | [ ] Partially | [ ] No
  4. _________________________ | Escalates each act? [ ] Yes | [ ] No | Active in all three acts? [ ] Yes | [ ] Partially | [ ] No

**Diagnostic Action**: Any subplot with "No" on escalation or ≥2 "Partially/No" on presence needs attention. Either escalate it or cut it; don't let it drift.

## DIAGNOSTIC SECTION 4: PROSE QUALITY

### Clarity & Concreteness

**Question 4.1: Pyramid of Abstraction**
- Pick three representative scenes (one from each act).
- Read the first 3 paragraphs of each scene.
- Count: How many sentences are *concrete* (specific sensory detail) vs. *abstract* (explanation, emotion, meta-thinking)?

  Scene 1: __ concrete / __ abstract sentences (Target: 2-3 concrete per abstract)
  Scene 2: __ concrete / __ abstract sentences
  Scene 3: __ concrete / __ abstract sentences

**Diagnostic Target**: 70% concrete, 30% abstract. If you're at 50% concrete, you're too explanatory; add sensory details.

**Diagnostic Action**: Pick one abstract-heavy scene. Rewrite first paragraph entirely in concrete details (sights, sounds, smells, textures) *before* any explanation. Reader sits in the moment first; understanding comes later.

### Dialogue Quality

**Question 4.2: Dialogue Voice Test**
- Print dialogue from three major character-pair scenes (no names/attributions).
- Read aloud without character tags.
- Can you (and a neutral reader) identify who's speaking?

  Scene 1 (Aisen + Elara): [ ] Easily | [ ] With thought | [ ] Confused
  Scene 2 (Aisen + Rin): [ ] Easily | [ ] With thought | [ ] Confused
  Scene 3 (Aisen + Antagonist): [ ] Easily | [ ] With thought | [ ] Confused

**Diagnostic Target**: ≥2 should be "Easily." If <2, voice differentiation is weak.

**Question 4.3: Dialogue Mechanics**
- Scan 5 random dialogue exchanges (10 lines total).
- Count: How many dialogue tags appear early in the line (before or first half)? __ / 10
- Count: How many use "said/asked"? __ / 10

**Diagnostic Target**: 
- Early tags: ≥7/10
- "said/asked": ≥9/10

**Diagnostic Action**: If below target, do dialect/detail pass specifically on dialogue tags and attributions.

### Show vs. Tell

**Question 4.4: Show/Tell Ratio**
- Pick one emotional scene (death, betrayal, reconciliation, sacrifice).
- Read it. Highlight every line that *tells* emotion: "Aisen felt afraid" / "She was angry" / "He loved her"
- Count: __ telling lines out of ~[total lines in scene]

**Diagnostic Target**: <10% lines are pure tell. If >20%, you're not showing enough through action/dialogue/physical sensation.

**Diagnostic Action**: Rewrite telling lines as showing: 
- "Aisen felt afraid" → [His hands shook. He couldn't meet their eyes.]
- "She was angry" → [She slammed the table.] "You lied to me."

## DIAGNOSTIC SECTION 5: SYSTEM & THEME INTEGRATION

### Prejudice-as-System Theme

**Question 5.1: Prejudice Visibility**
- How many major scenes in Act I show prejudice as a *rational system*, not just bigotry?
  
  Count: __ scenes (Target: ≥3)
  
  Examples:
  1. _____________________________
  2. _____________________________
  3. _____________________________

**Diagnostic Note**: Prejudice should show as *efficient inertia*—people maintain it because it costs less to believe than to change. Not stupidity; rationality from limited perspective.

**Question 5.2: System Understanding Visual**
- How many scenes show Aisen *analyzing* how a system works (Academy hierarchy, Covenant structure, magic tradition)?
  
  Count: __ scenes (Target: ≥2 per act)
  
  Examples from Act I: ____________________
  Examples from Act II: ____________________
  Examples from Act III: ____________________

**Diagnostic Action**: If count is low, add scenes where Aisen explicitly deduces how something works. This is his superpower; make it central to his problem-solving.

### Magic System Consistency

**Question 5.3: Telekinesis Rules**
- How many scenes explain or show telekinesis limits/costs?
  
  Count: __ scenes
  
  Is the *cost framework* (physical, cognitive, metaphysical, social) consistent?
  [ ] Yes, very consistent | [ ] Mostly consistent | [ ] Inconsistently applied

**Diagnostic Action**: Before revision, create a one-page "Telekinesis Cost Reference" listing what happens when Aisen pushes limits at 110%, 120%, 130%, etc. This ensures consistency.

### Chain Weapon Synergy

**Question 5.4: Chain Spear Mechanics Visibility**
- How many times does Aisen *choose* a specific chain state (Tightened, Loosened, Connected, Disconnected)?
  
  Count: __ scenes (Target: ≥1-2 per act, increases toward Act III)
  
  Is each choice *meaningful* to outcome, or just description?
  [ ] Choices matter | [ ] Mostly matter | [ ] Cosmetic

**Diagnostic Action**: If weapon feels decorative, ensure Aisen's solution choices *require* specific weapon configuration. Chain state should affect strategy visibly.

  [ ] Yes, carry forward | [ ] Partially | [ ] Forgotten by next act

**Diagnostic Note**: If Acts I-II cost him nothing permanently, Act III sacrifice will feel unearned. Show him paying prices throughout.

  [ ] Yes, absolutely | [ ] Mostly | [ ] Somewhat | [ ] Barely

**Diagnostic Target**: By Act II climax, reader should be afraid for Aisen. If not, go back and deepen likability/stakes.

**Question 6.3: Ally Investment**

  [ ] Yes, clear favorites | [ ] Mostly | [ ] Some | [ ] Not really

  Which allies feel *real* and not just supporting cast?
  1. _________________
  2. _________________
  3. _________________

**Diagnostic Action**: If <3, add scenes showing allies as full humans (flaws, wants, fears), not just Aisen's helpers.

## DIAGNOSTIC SECTION 7: PACING & FLOW

### Chapter Pacing Distribution

**Question 7.1: Act Pacing**
- Estimate total chapters per act (rough count from outline).
  - Act I chapters: __ (Target: 12-15)
  - Act II chapters: __ (Target: 18-25)
  - Act III chapters: __ (Target: 15-20)

**Diagnostic Note**: If Act II is <15 chapters, it may feel rushed. If >30, it may sag.

**Question 7.2: High/Low Intensity Distribution**
- In Act II (longest act), roughly how many chapters are *high intensity* (conflict, revelation, action) vs. *low intensity* (quiet, character, recovery)?
  
  High intensity: __ chapters
  Low intensity: __ chapters
  Balanced? [ ] Yes | [ ] Too much high | [ ] Too much low

**Diagnostic Target**: Rough 60% high, 40% low. All high = readers fatigue. All low = boring.

**Diagnostic Action**: If imbalanced, map out quiet chapters as deliberate "breathing room" between intense sequences.

### Momentum Arc

**Question 7.3: Three-Act Momentum**
- Act I momentum: [ ] Builds toward climax | [ ] Flat | [ ] Peaks then drops
- Act II momentum: [ ] Consistently rising | [ ] Peaks early, sags middle, rises end | [ ] Flat
- Act III momentum: [ ] Relentless toward climax | [ ] Stalls before ending | [ ] Anticlimactic

**Diagnostic Target**: Ideally all three show rising momentum toward their climax. 

**Diagnostic Action**: If Act II peaks early, you need a major reversal at midpoint to re-escalate. If Act III stalls, your climax sequence needs to *accelerate*, not release tension early.
