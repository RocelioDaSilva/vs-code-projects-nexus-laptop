## Changes Applied

**Problem:** Sensory void, expository dialogue, Seraphine explaining rather than *being* present  
**Research applied:** Reedsy's "Show, Don't Tell" principles; sensory grounding through environment; Sanderson's dialogue-as-action  
**Changes:**
- Added ozone-taste ward pressure, temperature changes, white-wall disorientation
- Seraphine's dialogue cut by ~40%, replaced with physical presence and subtext

- Room environment manipulates around her (chair appearing, window materializing)

## Writing Craft Research Applied (from Internet Sources)

### Reedsy Blog: "Show, Don't Tell" (reedsy.com/blog/show-dont-tell/)
- **Setting through action:** Characters interact with environment rather than describing it
- **Dialogue reveals character:** Subtext > exposition; trust readers to interpret
- **Strong details, don't overdo it:** 2-3 precise sensory details > 10 generic ones
- Applied to: Ch 37 (ward environment), Ch 41-44 (rain, bread, chalk dust, grain warehouse)

### Reedsy Blog: "Pacing in Writing" (reedsy.com/blog/pacing-in-writing/)
- **Short sentences for urgency:** Applied in Ch 37 Seraphine confrontation
- **Dialogue speeds pace:** Applied in Ch 41 (Kael exchange), Ch 42 (Maren exchange)
- **Introspection slows pace:** Preserved in Ch 20 (Aisen's moral analysis of Serath's offer)
- **Cliffhangers:** Ch 37 ends on "They both did" — Seraphine exit as implicit threat

### Sanderson's Laws of Magic
- **First Law:** Reader understanding proportional to narrative use of magic → Ch 37's ward environment shown through Aisen's Flux core reaction rather than explained
- Applied: Wards as *felt pressure* not *described mechanism*

### Swain's Scene/Sequel (already used in strong chapters)
- Verified all new scenes follow Goal → Conflict → Disaster / Reaction → Dilemma → Decision
- Ch 41: Goal (get strategic clarity) → Conflict (Amara disagrees) → Decision (provincial resistance)
- Ch 42: Goal (comply with Covenant) → Conflict (curriculum directive) → Decision (bureaucratic delay weapon)
- Ch 43: Goal (coordinate cells) → Conflict (risk of exposure) → Decision (isolated cells, no topology)
- Ch 44: Goal (convergence) → Conflict (visibility risk) → Resolution (friction model works)
