## The Chain Spear — High‑level mechanics
This weapon is the narrative and mechanical centerpiece. Design goals: versatility, tactical depth, clear failure modes.

- States (fundamental):
  - Tighten: rigid/staff mode for thrusts, anchors, and bracing.
  - Loosen: whip/coil mode for range, entangling, and area denial.
  - Connect: segments unify to form composite tools (pole, flail, cage, platform).
  - Disconnect: independent segments perform separate tasks simultaneously.

- Components and tech:
  - Segmented core with tension control and break/lock points (mechanical + Flux nodes).
  - Blade clusters at terminals and midlinks for puncture/cutting when rigid.
  - Mana/telekinesis conduits: small nodes to allow the wielder to apply precise telekinetic vectors to selected chain sections.

- Combat uses (examples):
  - Anchor + vault: tighten a segment, plant like a pole to vault or redirect momentum.
  - Multi‑target lockdown: disconnect segments to bind several opponents or create barriers.
  - Catch & convert: use loosened links to snag a weapon, then tighten to snap or disarm.
  - Construct formation: combine links into short temporary shields, ladders, or braces.

- Design constraints (narrative tension):
  - Stress thresholds: armor/links bend or break if overloaded—planned failure points enable dramatic costs.
  - Telekinetic synergy is precise, low‑raw, high‑utility; the wielder cannot brute‑force mass moves.

## Tactical Telekinesis (the protagonist's signature utility)
Core philosophy: telekinesis as applied physics — leverage, torque, pressure, and momentum redirection rather than lifting giant masses.

- Signature techniques:
  - Focused Joint Torque: concentrate force along an opponent's joint vector to disable a limb.
  - Rotational Impulse: apply off‑axis force to create spins that unbalance heavy opponents.
  - Momentum Brake / Catch: match and cancel an incoming object's momentum by applying an opposing vector precisely at the object's center of mass.
  - Pressure Narrowing: compress a target’s moving surface area to increase pressure locally (e.g., collapse a doorway seal or redirect a falling beam to pin an enemy).
  - Microthermal by slowing particles: reduce local particle motion to create frost or brittleness as a tactical debuff (high cost and situational).
  - Fluid manipulation via Bernoulli principles: small, targeted adjustments to flow to create blades/jet effects with minimal mana.

- Tactical constraints:
  - Range, mass, and precision tradeoffs — greater precision reduces effective mass moved; moving large masses requires time and is noisy/vulnerable.
  - Concentration cost: precise applications demand mental focus; the user is vulnerable while executing complex vectors.
  - Sensory dependency: requires line of sight or keen spatial sense to apply precise effects; darkness/obstructions reduce effectiveness.

## Power System: Flux & Weave (summary)
- Weave: the layered fabric of reality (structural rules of things — conservation, material constants).
- Flux: the flow that manipulates the Weave; learned like physics or ritual, and interpreted by different cultures.
- Access control: knowledge, institutions, and socio‑political licensing gate advanced techniques (military and academy secrecy creates black markets and assassination incentives).

- Therryn: meritocratic, scholarly Flux; examination culture; technological codification of magic.
- Umara: networked, ancestral Flux traditions; griots and community‑based power distribution.

## Anomalies, Weaknesses & Counterstrategies (summary)
- Regessors: change the script; introduce unpredictable variables.
- Transmigrators: exploit local physics and cultural ignorance.
- Reincarnators: use modern tactics and bait nostalgia.
- Prodigies: outlast and force fundamentals; stress endurance.
- Lucky Encounter Havers: remove randomness; force skill-only controlled arenas.
- Noble Blood: target dependency and gear; create asymmetric conditions.
- Chosen/Prophecy: attack the prophecy’s framing; manipulate followers.
