## Story Overview (Spoiler-Free)

### The World
Magic exists but destabilizes reality. Society suppresses individual mages through an institution called the Academy, which is infiltrated by a cult (the Covenant) seeking total control. The ecological consequences of magical abuse are eroding the frontier.

### The Protagonist
Aisen is ordinary—no magic gift, no noble birth, no destiny. He learns spear combat from a disgraced guard and telekinesis from a wandering scholar. He combines both into something new: a chain spear system. At 17, he enters the Academy and discovers it's far more political and dangerous than he expected.

### The Arc
Aisen navigates Academy politics, discovers evidence of the Covenant's manipulation, and becomes instrumental in exposing institutional corruption. But there are costs. Permanent costs. The story uses multiple POV characters to show how systems of oppression affect ordinary people, not just the hero.

### Why Now?
The story was completed as a "proof of concept" first draft to demonstrate:
- That an ordinary protagonist can be compelling
- That consequences don't require graphic violence
- That endings don't require romance or triumph
- That multiple POV perspectives can build systemic understanding

## How to Give Feedback

### Format
Please provide feedback as comments with:
1. **Location:** Chapter number + rough position (beginning/middle/end)
2. **Type:** Plot / Character / Prose / World / Other
3. **Comment:** What worked or didn't work
4. **Suggestion (optional):** If you have an idea for improvement

### Examples
- "Ch. 5, middle: Corvin's dialogue felt rushed. Suggest slowing down his reveal."
- "Ch. 28, end: The battle choreography was confusing. Not clear who was where."
- "Ch. 46: This hit hard. Aisen's death felt earned despite being sad."

### Collect Feedback
- Email back with line-by-line comments in a text file, or
- Use the PDF annotation tools if you print it, or
- Write a summary email with top 3-5 impressions
