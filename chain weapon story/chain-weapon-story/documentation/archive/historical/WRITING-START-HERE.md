## STEP-BY-STEP WRITING PROCESS

### **Phase 1: Preparation (Before Writing)**

**Step 1**: Read **STORY-MASTER-STRUCTURE.md** completely (1-2 hours)
- Understand three-act structure
- Know character arcs
- Understand thematic progression
- Identify all major pivot points

**Step 2**: Read **SCENE-DRAFT-PROMPTS.md** chapters 1-5 (30 minutes)
- Get sense of writing tone and detail level
- Understand prompt format
- Prepare for Act I opening

**Step 3**: Set up reference documents
- Keep open: **story-overview.md** (character descriptions)
- Keep open: **MAGIC-SYSTEM-REFERENCE.md** (for physics descriptions)
- Keep open: **PRE-ACADEMY-TIMELINE.md** (for age/season consistency)

### **Phase 2: Act I Draft (Chapters 1-12)**

**For Each Chapter**:

1. **Open SCENE-DRAFT-PROMPTS.md** to that chapter's prompt
2. **Review checklist**:
   - [ ] PoV character clear?
   - [ ] Anchor point identified?
   - [ ] 2-3 key scenes outlined?
   - [ ] Micro-scene for downtime chosen?
   - [ ] Emotional arc mapped?
3. **Identify micro-scene placement** (from SIDE-CHARACTER-MOMENTS.md)
4. **Draft chapter** (follow prompt but write naturally; prompt is guide, not cage)
5. **Post-write checklist**:
   - [ ] Timeline consistent (age, season, year)?
   - [ ] 3+ named characters with traits?
   - [ ] Emotional progression clear?
   - [ ] No exposition (shown through action)?
   - [ ] Thematic element present?
6. **Log any coin flips or randomness decisions** (record in RANDOM-EVENTS-LOG.md)
7. **Save** to `Drafts/` folder with naming: `Chapter-##-Title.md`

**Expected Act I Writing**: 40-50 pages / 35,000-45,000 words

### **Phase 3: Act II Draft (Chapters 13-38)**

**Same process as Act I**:
1. Follow SCENE-DRAFT-PROMPTS.md
2. Maintain consistency with Act I characters/tone
3. **Key checkpoints**:
   - Ch. 18-25: **Tournament arc** interleaved; ensure pacing and character moments
   - Ch. 26-32: **War begins**; shift to military/border POVs; multiple character threads
   - Ch. 33-38: **Conspiracy exposed**; information spread; tension mounting
4. **Character recurrence**: Multiple side characters should appear 2-3 times now
5. **Thematic shift**: Move from "specialness is lie" (Act I) to "systems hide truth" (Act II)

**Expected Act II Writing**: 50-65 pages / 45,000-60,000 words

### **Phase 4: Act III Draft (Chapters 39-52+)**

**Climactic and resolution phases**:
1. Ch. 39-43: **Confrontation and death**
   - Major battle scenes
   - Character deaths (Caspian, possibly Corvin)
   - Coin flip resolution for Aisen's death
   - Psychological intensity high
2. Ch. 44-52+: **After Aisen** (story continues without protagonist)
   - Network reconvenes
   - World rebuilds
   - Side characters shine
   - Legacy themes develop
3. **Thematic culmination**: "Ordinary people matter; death doesn't end impact"

**Expected Act III Writing**: 50-60 pages / 45,000-55,000 words

## KEY REMINDERS WHILE WRITING

### **Structural Principles**:
1. **PoV anchors follow interactions** — Shift perspective only after meaningful handshake between characters
2. **Micro-scenes fill downtime** — Never skip years with vague transitions; use micro-scenes
3. **Recurrence builds familiarity** — Characters appear 3+ times, readers recognize them by traits
4. **No plot armor** — Protagonist can die, fail, lose
5. **Randomness controls events** — Coin flips at major pivots; author writes HOW not WHAT

### **Technical Consistency**:
1. **Timeline accuracy** — Every chapter has age/season/year; never violate
2. **Memorable traits** — Use same distinctive details every time character appears
3. **Magic mechanics** — Describe telekinesis in physics terms; chain weapon state transitions explicit
4. **Dialogue specificity** — Characters have speech patterns; avoid generic conversation
5. **Thematic resonance** — Each chapter advances one of the three act themes

### **Narrative Quality**:
1. **Show, don't tell** — Never explain; reveal through action, dialogue, observation
2. **Emotional authenticity** — Characters' feelings rooted in their circumstances
3. **Systemic revelation** — Corruption, class struggle shown through lived experience
4. **World density** — Even minor characters have names, traits, lives beyond plot
5. **Consequence reality** — Actions have lasting, tangible results
