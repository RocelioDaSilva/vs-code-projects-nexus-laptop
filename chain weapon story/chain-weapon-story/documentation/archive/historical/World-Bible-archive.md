## I. World Overview
### Cosmology
- The Weave: the fabric of physical law (gravity, inertia, energy).
- The Flux: the parallel flow of raw magical potential.
- The Veil: a semi‑permeable boundary between Weave and Flux. Thin Veil zones cause strong, unstable magic.

### Magic + Physics
- Magic is energy conversion: mana (Flux) is converted into effects on the Weave.
- Law of Resonance: spells that work with physical phenomena are far cheaper and more reliable.
- Matter over Magic: once a magical effect alters matter, it obeys physical laws like any manufactured object.
- Resistance: dense or enchanted materials can block or damp precise magic.

### Geography
- Spine of the World: resource‑rich mountain chain; strategic passes and resonance crystals.
- Silverrun River System: trade arteries; site of Aldric's Crossing and the Academy.
- Shattered Coast: Sundering scars and Aelfar ruins; thin Veil and high anomaly density.

## II. Historical Timeline (Key Domino Events)
- Aelfar Age: The first civilization to systematically shape Flux—ends in the Sundering.
- Sundering: catastrophic event that tore the Veil, scattering Aelfar ruins and producing wild magic zones.
- Formation of Heartland, Federation, Island polities; rise of trade and technological/magical integration.
- First Great War: Flux Tear(s) and the Void Covenant provoke the Grand Alliance.
- Treaty of Silverrun: founding of the Academy to train cross‑national talent and prevent future cataclysms.
- Magical industrialization: rampant use of magic for infrastructure and war; ecological debt; wild beast evolution.
- Contemporary: Rising friction among Alliance members, ecological collapse on the frontier, the Fourth Party revelations.

## VI. Weapons & Systems (Reference)
### Hunting Foundation
- Tools: boar spear, snares, deadfalls, bow, gut knives, bolas, nets, fishing traps.
- Skills: tracking, stalking, anatomy, trap mechanics, patience, terrain exploitation.

### Spear Techniques
- Core: standard thrusts, parries, sweeps, hooks, butt strikes.
- Advanced: sliding thrust, pivot lever, deflection roll, one‑handed control, spear‑and‑shield, vaulting and pole work.

### Telekinesis (Low power, high precision)
- Techniques: point push/pull, torque twist, momentum adjustment, boundary sense, thread sense, vibration reading, trajectory curving, joint lock.
- Limits: short range, one focused manipulation at a time, mana fatigue, interference by dense/enchanted materials.

### Chain Spear (Memory‑metal system)
- Structure: segmented memory metal links, resonance crystals, control ring, terminal blades.
- States: tighten (rigid), loosen (flexible), connect, disconnect (detachable segments). Gradients and mixed patterns enable complex constructs.
- Techniques: rigid thrust, whip crack, harpoon, snap‑lock, flail spin, spring launch, segmented volley, chain shield, catching parry, blade orbit, rigid cage, net weave, tripwire, spring snare.

### Combos & Tactical Patterns
- Harpoon + Torque: wrap + telekinetic twist to dislocate.
- Whip‑Snap Transfer: telekinetic impulse at whip tip for extra penetration.
- Spring Vault: coil under foot + release for launch.
- Blade Dance: orbiting detached blades + primary chain attacks.
- Trap Funnel: rigid anchors to channel enemies into snare arrays.

## VII. The Fourth Party & Ecological Crisis
- Overuse of magic draws down ambient life force; crops, animals, and ecosystems degrade.
- Wild zones host increasingly adaptive, hostile magical fauna; only the most resilient survive.
- The Fourth Party: beast‑kin, elemental enclaves, remnant constructs — not monolithic evil, often victims with knowledge of restoration.
- Political exploitation: the Fourth Party is used to justify sustained militarization and territorial expansion by factions like the Heartland.
