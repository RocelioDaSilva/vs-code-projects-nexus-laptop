# Andean Alliance — Geography & Territory

## Core Geography

### The Three Bands
The Alliance territory is defined by extreme verticality — three distinct ecological zones stacked along a mountain range that runs 800 kilometers north-south.

| Band | Altitude | Climate | Primary Use |
|------|----------|---------|-------------|
| **Low Band** (Yunka) | 500–2,000m | Tropical/subtropical; heavy rainfall | Agriculture, coca cultivation, timber, river trade |
| **Mid Band** (Qucha) | 2,000–4,000m | Temperate; moderate rainfall; fertile valleys | Population centers, terrace farming, governance, markets |
| **High Band** (Urqu) | 4,000–6,000m | Cold/arid; thin air; extreme UV | Llama herding, mineral extraction, shrines, military watchtowers |

### Key Settlements

**Qhapaq Llaqta** (Capital, Mid Band, ~3,200m)
- Built into a mountain saddle; terraced city radiating outward from the central Inti Plaza
- Population: ~45,000; largest settlement in the Alliance
- Administrative hub of the Three-Band Government
- Connected to all major routes via the Qhapaq Ñan (Royal Road system)

**Tambo Wayra** (High Band Fortress, ~4,800m)
- Military observation post commanding the northern pass
- Permanently garrisoned by 200 Warak'a warriors
- Condor Signal relay station; can reach the capital in under 6 hours via signal chain

**Yunka Hatun** (Low Band Trade Port, ~800m)
- River port connecting Alliance territory to lowland trade networks
- Primary cacao, spice, and tropical fruit export point
- Most culturally diverse Alliance settlement; contact zone with outside nations

### The Qhapaq Ñan (Royal Road System)
- 1,200+ kilometers of maintained stone roads connecting all three bands
- Engineering marvel: switchback ascents, suspension bridges over gorges, relay stations (tambos) every 20km
- Maintained by mit'a labor rotation; road upkeep is a sacred duty
- Altitude-adjusted: road surfaces change material by band (packed earth in Yunka, stone in Qucha, flagstone in Urqu)

### Natural Defenses
- Western approach: Sheer cliff faces; only 3 viable passes, all fortified
- Eastern approach: Dense tropical forest (Yunka); impassable without guides
- Northern approach: Single narrow pass at 5,200m; altitude itself is a weapon
- Southern approach: River gorge system requiring bridge crossings the Alliance controls

### Flux Geology
- **Vein of the Earth**: A deep Flux vein runs the length of the mountain range, surfacing at volcanic hotspots
- High-altitude thin Veil zones create dangerous magic instability above 5,000m
- Mineral veins (copper, silver, gold) are intertwined with Flux deposits — mining requires shamanic supervision
- Volcanic thermal vents at three locations provide geothermal energy (heating, metalworking)
- **Altitude sickness + Flux exposure**: Non-adapted visitors experience combined altitude/magical sickness above 4,000m

# Celestial Kingdom — Geography & Territory

## Core Geography

### The High Plateau
The Kingdom occupies an elevated plateau enclosed by mountain ranges on three sides and a vast glacial lake system to the south. Altitude: 3,000–6,000m. The thin air and extreme conditions define every aspect of life.

| Zone | Altitude | Character | Primary Use |
|------|----------|-----------|-------------|
| **Lake Rim** | 3,000–3,500m | Relatively hospitable; warmer microclimate near lakes | Agriculture, largest settlements, trade stations |
| **Monastery Belt** | 3,500–4,500m | Moderate; optimal for meditation practice | Monastery-cities, governance centers, spiritual academies |
| **Summit Zone** | 4,500–6,000m | Extreme cold, thin air, Veil-thin zones | Hermitages, crystal mining, vision quests, military observation |

### Key Settlements

**Bodhi Dzong** (Capital-Monastery, ~4,200m)
- Fortress-monastery built into cliff face; 800+ years old
- Population: ~15,000 (monastic + lay community)
- Seat of the Triune Council; largest library of meditation texts in the world
- Architecture: white-washed walls, golden roofs, prayer corridors connecting 200+ meditation chambers

**Mirror Lake City** (Southern Trade Hub, ~3,100m)
- Built on stilts along the shore of the Great Mirror Lake
- Population: ~12,000; most cosmopolitan Celestial settlement
- Southern pass access; primary contact point with outside world
- Rin Celestara's likely point of departure when sent to the Academy

**Crystal Peak Hermitage** (~5,800m)
- Highest permanent habitation; ~200 residents
- Aerolith mining and thin-Veil vision practice
- Air so thin non-adapted humans cannot function beyond 24 hours
- Produces the finest aerolith crystals used in meditation and magical practice

### The Six Passes
Six mountain passes connect the plateau to surrounding territories. Natural military chokepoints.

| Pass | Direction | Status | Notes |
|------|-----------|--------|-------|
| Southern Gate | South to Mirror Lake | Open year-round | Primary trade route; most guarded |
| Western Crossing | West to Valdimere frontier | Open 8 months | Tension point; Valdimere military patrols nearby |
| Northern Thread | North to Andean Alliance | Open 7 months | Spiritual exchange route; lightly used |
| Eastern Descent | East to lowlands | Open 9 months | Rin's intelligence network communication route |
| Cloud Pass | Northeast | Open 5 months | Dangerous; avalanche-prone; emergency military route |
| Pilgrim's Way | Southeast | Open 6 months | Sacred route; no military traffic allowed by tradition |

### Flux Geology

**Aeroliths**: Crystalline Flux deposits unique to high-altitude thin-Veil zones. Properties:
- Amplify meditation effects; extend awareness duration
- Store impressions (magical/emotional recordings) for centuries
- Fragile; shatter under impact; fragments retain partial functionality
- Only form above 4,500m in specific geological conditions
- **Strategic resource**: The Covenant wants aerolith access; main leverage point

**Thin-Veil Zones**: Above 5,000m, the boundary between physical and magical reality becomes permeable. Effects:
- Enhanced magical sensitivity; reduced control
- Spontaneous vision events (disturbing for non-meditators)
- Weather manipulation becomes easy but unpredictable
- Creatures from the magical ecosystem appear more frequently
- **Military advantage**: Non-adapted invaders experience disorientation, fear, hallucinations

# Mercantile Federation — Geography & Territory

## Core Geography

### The Fourteen Cantons
The Federation occupies a lowland river-delta and coastal zone, intersected by navigable waterways that define its political boundaries and trade infrastructure.

| Region | Cantons | Character | Primary Economy |
|--------|---------|-----------|----------------|
| **Coastal Core** (4 cantons) | Arbitria, Portmund, Tidevale, Ironhaven | Harbor cities; naval bases; international trade | Maritime commerce, shipbuilding, banking |
| **River Interior** (5 cantons) | Lockbridge, Millstream, Broadwash, Copperfield, Goldmark | Inland waterway cities; manufacturing | Textiles, metalwork, agriculture, river freight |
| **Border Marches** (3 cantons) | Northgate, Eastwall, Southfen | Frontier zones; defensive | Military provisioning, border trade, fortification |
| **Free Cities** (2 cantons) | Freeport, Neutralis | Special-status; exempt from certain federal laws | Arbitration, diplomacy, black-market regulation |

### Key Settlements

**Arbitria** (Federal Capital, Coastal)
- Population: ~180,000; largest city in the known world
- Seat of the Arbitration Court and Federal Assembly
- The Contract Vault — underground fortress holding all magically-binding federal agreements
- Architecture: canal-threaded; every district accessible by water; bridges are jurisdictional boundaries
- **Narrative relevance**: The financial infrastructure the Covenant is infiltrating

**Freeport** (Free City, Coastal)
- Population: ~60,000; most diverse settlement in the Federation
- No guild restrictions; any profession legal within city limits
- Neutral ground for inter-canton disputes and international negotiations
- Black market regulated (taxed and tolerated) rather than suppressed
- **Narrative relevance**: Resistance operations could use Freeport as neutral staging ground

**Goldmark** (River Interior)
- Population: ~25,000; Federation's primary banking center
- Contains 40% of the Federation's liquid capital reserves
- Guild of Financial Arbitrators based here; most powerful guild in the Federation
- **Vulnerability**: If the Covenant controls Goldmark's banking guilds, they control inter-national trade

### Waterway Network
- 2,000+ kilometers of navigable rivers and canals
- Standardized lock-and-bridge system connecting all 14 cantons
- Canal-boat freight is the primary commerce method (cheaper than overland)
- Bridge tolls and lock fees are significant revenue sources; hotly contested between cantons
- **Strategic value**: Destroying or blocking waterway infrastructure would paralyze Federation economy within weeks

### Flux Geology

**Artificially Stabilized**: The Federation's Flux environment is unique — not natural but engineered.
- Ancient Aelfar infrastructure (precursor civilization) stabilized Flux flows through embedded crystalline networks
- Federation engineers discovered and adapted this system; most controlled magical environment in the world
- **Implication**: Magic in Federation territory is reliable and predictable; rare in the rest of the world
- **Vulnerability**: If the stabilization network is disrupted, magical chaos follows — contracts stop enforcing, banking fails
- The stabilization system is not fully understood; Federation engineers maintain it empirically, not theoretically

### Natural Defenses (Weak)
- Low-lying terrain; no natural barriers except rivers
- Three invasion routes: northern overland, eastern overland, southern naval approach
- Historically defended through bribery, alliance-shifting, and mercenary armies rather than geography
- **Federation saying**: "We don't need walls. We need contracts."

# Shukei — Geography & Terrain

## Island Structure
- **Main islands** (4-5 large): Each controlled by different daimyo
- **Archipelago**: Hundreds of smaller islands; frontier/exploration frontier
- **Mountainous terrain**: Central highlands dominate; valleys create natural regional boundaries
- **Coastal regions**: Fishing, maritime trade, international contact points (carefully controlled)

## Regional Characteristics
- **Northern regions**: Colder; traditional strongholds; most conservative daimyo
- **Central highlands**: Monasteries, temples; spiritual authority
- **Southern coastal**: More exposure to foreign influence; wealth from maritime trade
- **Frontier islands**: Sparsely settled; occasional exploration/colonization

## Climate & Seasons
- Temperate island climate; seasonal distinctions important
- Typhoon season: Natural defense against naval invasion; economic disruption
- Cherry blossom / seasonal cycles: Cultural/spiritual significance
- Agricultural calendar determines social rhythms

## Natural Resources
- Timber: Rich forests; shipbuilding industry
- Fish: Abundant; fundamental to diet; cultural significance
- Minerals: Limited; some metals, rare stones
- Hot springs: Cultural/spiritual significance; healing reputation

## Defensive Advantages
- Island geography provides natural moat
- No land borders; military focus on naval defense
- Difficult terrain (mountains) prevents internal conquest
- Insularity = difficulty for outside threats to project power

## Ecological Considerations
- Island ecosystems fragile; overexploitation possible
- Would Flux corruption affect islands differently? (Water-borne? Isolated?)
- Animal migration patterns unique to islands
- Environmental crisis might force unprecedented interaction with mainland

## Trade Geography
- Maritime trade networks connecting to mainland
- Carefully controlled ports (not all daimyo benefit equally)
- Smuggling routes bypassing official trade
- Geographic position as crossroads or excluded (depending on policy)

## Story Relevance
Shukei's isolation means Covenant must work differently there. Insular nations are often most surprised by threats they don't expect because they don't monitor external environment carefully.

# Valdimere — Geography & Physical Landscape

## Primary Regions

### Core Heartland
- River valley, heavily irrigated agricultural zone
- Urban centers with bureaucratic infrastructure
- Seat of imperial power and highest population density
- Well-maintained roads, bridges, fortifications

### River Systems
- **Delta region** (south): Trade ports, maritime commerce, mixture of river and sea navigation
- **Middle corridors**: Agricultural abundance; supports large population
- **Northern tributaries**: Mountain access, border defense, raw material extraction

### Mountain Passes
- Strategic frontier between Valdimere and neighboring lands
- Heavily fortified; military installations at key chokepoints
- Trade routes carefully controlled and taxed
- Buffer zone; anything uncontrolled beyond mountains = "barbarian lands"

### Frontier Borderlands
- Aisen's tavern sits in this region
- Mix of Valdimerian settlers and local indigenous populations
- Less centralized authority; regional governors have more freedom
- Crossroads where travelers congregate; neutral ground
- Ecological fragility (first areas to show Flux corruption)

## Climate & Seasons
- Continental: cold winters, hot summers
- Spring flooding expected and planned for (irrigation systems account for it)
- Autumn harvest drives economic cycles
- Winter movement restricted (travel planning key administrative task)

## Trade Routes
- Multiple river paths converging on delta ports
- Mountain passes connecting to other nations
- Overland caravans possible but heavily taxed
- Taxes recorded in triplicate; documentation obsessed

## Ecological Status
- Currently stable but showing early Flux corruption in frontier regions
- Agricultural productivity declining (concern for grain stores)
- Animals migrating away from Valdimerian heartland
- Scholars investigating but findings suppressed through bureaucratic channels

## Strategic Importance
Every geographical feature tied to military/economic strategy:
- Rivers = trade + defensive moats
- Mountains = border control + resource extraction
- Flatlands = farmland + army deployment space
- Delta = naval power + international commerce

Valdimere thinks of geography as infrastructure to be optimized, not terrain to adapt to.
