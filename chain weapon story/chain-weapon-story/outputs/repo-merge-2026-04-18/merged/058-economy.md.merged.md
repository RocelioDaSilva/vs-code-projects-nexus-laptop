# Andean Alliance — Economy & Trade

## Economic Model: Vertical Exchange (Non-Monetary)

The Andean Alliance operates without internal currency. The economy is built on two reciprocal systems that predate the Federation's contract capitalism by centuries.

### Ayni (Reciprocal Exchange)
- **Principle**: Every gift creates an obligation; every service requires return
- **Scope**: Between individuals, families, and communities within a band
- **Tracking**: Communal memory (elders track obligations); no written ledger
- **Enforcement**: Social pressure; failure to reciprocate = loss of community standing
- **Function**: Distributes goods and services without accumulation; prevents wealth concentration

### Mit'a (Rotational Labor)
- **Principle**: Every able-bodied adult owes rotational labor to the community
- **Scope**: Infrastructure maintenance (roads, terraces, bridges), military service, communal projects
- **Duration**: Typically 3 months per year; varies by band and season
- **Exemptions**: Shamans (spiritual labor substitutes), nursing mothers, elderly, disabled
- **Function**: Maintains collective infrastructure without taxation or wage labor

### Vertical Exchange (Between Bands)
- Peak produces: crystals, high-altitude herbs, astronomical observations, condor feathers
- Terrace produces: wool, woven textiles, dairy, grain, warriors
- Valley produces: fruits, vegetables, fish, timber, trade goods (from external commerce)
- Exchange is ritualized: seasonal markets at band boundaries; exchange ratios set by council
- **Key tension**: Peak communities claim their crystals are "worth more" than valley food; valley communities claim their food is essential while crystals are luxury

## External Trade

### Exports
| Product | Value | Destination | Risk |
|---------|-------|------------|------|
| Crystals (raw + worked) | Very high | Federation, Valdimere | Depletion; Covenant interest |
| High-altitude herbs (medicinal) | High | All nations | Sustainable if managed |
| Woven textiles (terrace artisan) | Moderate | Federation, Therryn | Cultural loss through commodification |
| Condor feathers (ceremonial) | Symbolic | Celestial Kingdom (diplomatic gifts) | Non-commercial; diplomatic exchange only |

### Imports

|---------|------|--------|------|
| Metal (iron, copper, tin) | Critical | Federation, Valdimere | Dependency; trade leverage |
| Grain (wheat, rice) | Important | Valdimere, Federation | Food security vulnerability |
| Tools (manufactured) | Moderate | Federation | Technological dependency |
| Medicine (non-herbal) | Growing | Federation, Therryn | Cultural resistance (shamanic tradition vs. external medicine) |

### Trade Relations by Nation
| Nation | Relationship | Volume | Tension |
|--------|-------------|--------|---------|
| Mercantile Federation | Primary trade partner | High (crystals for metal/grain) | Federation wants deeper access; Alliance limits to Yunka Hatun port |
| Valdimere | Secondary, strategic | Moderate | Valdimere wants border access; Alliance resists |
| Celestial Kingdom | Diplomatic, minimal trade | Low | Mutual respect; similar isolationism |
| Shukei | Negligible | Minimal | Geographic distance; cultural disconnection |
| Therryn | Scholarly exchange | Low | Therryn herbalists visit; limited economic impact |

### Yunka Hatun (Trade Port)
- Only location where foreign merchants are permitted
- Valley-band community; culturally distinct from highland Alliance
- Growing Federation merchant presence; permanent trading post
- Alliance administrators rotate quarterly to prevent corruption
- **Pressure point**: Federation demands expanded access; peak communities demand restriction

## Internal Economic Tensions

### 1. Vein Depletion Crisis
- Crystal veins — the Alliance's most valuable resource — are depleting
- Peak communities have mined for centuries; some veins are exhausted
- New veins require deeper, more dangerous mining
- Covenant has offered advanced mining technology in exchange for trade access
- **Dilemma**: Accept Covenant help and risk infiltration, or accept decline

### 2. Modernization Pressure
- Valley communities (especially Yunka Hatun) have adopted Federation trade practices
- Some valley merchants accumulate personal wealth — violating ayni principles
- Manufactured goods from the Federation displace artisan production
- Young people in valley communities prefer trade wages to mit'a labor

### 3. Food Security
- The Alliance is not fully self-sufficient in grain
- Terrace farming produces enough for normal years; drought years require imports
- Import dependency on Valdimere/Federation creates strategic vulnerability
- Peak communities refuse to expand terrace farming (would require deforesting sacred slopes)

### 4. Labor Shortage
- Mit'a rotation assumes stable population; young people are leaving for valley trade
- Peak mining is dangerous; fewer volunteers each generation
- Shamanic apprenticeship requires 10+ year commitment; young people can't wait
- Military readiness depends on terrace warriors; terrace population is declining relative to valley

## Economic Philosophy

The Alliance's economic philosophy is anti-accumulation:
- **Wealth is relationship, not possession.** Rich = well-connected through ayni. Poor = isolated.
- **Labor is communal, not individual.** Mit'a creates collective ownership of infrastructure.
- **Trade is necessary evil.** External commerce required for metal/grain; minimized as much as possible.
- **Growth is suspicious.** Expansion implies extraction; extraction depletes the mountain.

This philosophy creates resilience (no financial crisis, no debt, no inequality) but also fragility (no surplus, no investment, no adaptation to changing circumstances).

## Covenant Economic Strategy

The Covenant's approach to the Alliance is patient and indirect:
1. **Offer**: Advanced mining technology that could revitalize depleted crystal veins
2. **Price**: Permanent trade mission at Yunka Hatun + access to crystal output
3. **Target**: Valley modernizers who already want external trade
4. **Method**: Frame it as partnership, not infiltration; offer medical supplies alongside mining tools
5. **Goal**: Control crystal supply (which multiple nations depend on)

The Alliance's best defense is isolation — but isolation is becoming economically unsustainable.

# Celestial Kingdom — Economy & Trade Systems

## Primary Economic Model: Subsistence-Plus

The Celestial Kingdom operates a **monastery-centered redistribution economy**. There is no free market. Production is communal; distribution is managed by monastic institutions.

### Agricultural Production
- **Highland agriculture**: Barley, buckwheat, root vegetables, medicinal herbs
- **Livestock**: Yak (primary), mountain goat, highland sheep
- **Yield per hectare**: Lower than lowland nations due to altitude, but medicinal herbs command premium prices in external trade
- **Growing season**: 4-5 months (limited by altitude and climate)
- **Food security**: Marginal; one bad harvest creates famine risk; 4-month winter isolation requires stockpiling

### Monastery Economics
- Monasteries receive community labor and agricultural surplus
- They redistribute resources according to need (food to lay communities, medical care, education)
- Monastery reserves include: preserved food, aerolith stockpiles, medicinal herb stores, textile stocks
- **Imbalance**: Monasteries accumulate wealth (aerolith trade income, foreign teaching fees) while lay communities maintain subsistence

### Taxation System
| Item | Rate | Method |
|------|------|--------|
| Agricultural surplus | ~25% | In-kind contribution to nearest monastery |
| Community labor | Rotational | Each household contributes 30 days/year to monastery/community projects |
| Trade goods | Variable | Monasteries take first pick of external trade goods; remainder distributed |
| Aerolith extraction | 100% | All aeroliths are monastery property; lay miners receive food/shelter only |

## Trade

### Exports
| Export | Value | Destination | Notes |
|--------|-------|-------------|-------|
| Medicinal herbs | High | All nations | High-altitude plants with unique magical properties; most valuable export |
| Aerolith artifacts | Very High | Federation, Valdimere | Crystalline Flux-conductive material; limited supply, enormous demand |
| Meditation instruction | High | Wealthy foreigners | Multi-year teaching programs; 5-10 foreign students per year at 10x local cost |
| Textiles (highland wool) | Moderate | Regional trade | Yak wool and highland sheep; cold-weather specialty |

### Imports
| Import | Source | Necessity | Notes |
|--------|--------|-----------|-------|
| Metal (iron, copper, tin) | Valdimere, Federation | Critical | Highlands have almost no metal deposits; dependent on trade |
| Grain (wheat, rice) | Valdimere, Andean Alliance | Important | Supplementary food; essential during poor harvests |
| Manufactured goods | Federation | Moderate | Tools, glass, paper; increasingly desired by lay communities |
| Textiles (cotton, silk) | Therryn, Umara | Luxury | Status goods for monastery decoration and ceremonial use |

### Trade Vulnerability
- **Pass dependence**: 6 major mountain passes; 4 close seasonally (winter snows). If remaining 2 are blocked, Kingdom is cut off from all trade.
- **Metal dependency**: No domestic metal production. If trade stops, tool/weapon production halts within 1-2 years.
- **Food margin**: One bad harvest + trade disruption = famine. Historical occurrence: twice in past century.

## Currency & Finance

### Internal Economy: No Currency
- The Kingdom operates without formal currency internally
- Exchange based on: obligation, reciprocity, monastic distribution
- Barter for informal exchanges between households
- Monastic records track obligations and contributions (spiritual accounting)

### External Trade: Federation Compact Marks
- External transactions use Federation currency (practical necessity)
- Monasteries manage all foreign currency; lay communities have no independent access
- Growing problem: aerolith income concentrates foreign currency in monastic hands
- Lay merchants in lower passes increasingly conduct unauthorized trades using Federation marks

## Labor Economy

### Communal Labor (Mit'a Parallel)
- All lay adults contribute 30 days/year to communal projects
- Projects include: terrace maintenance, path repair, monastery construction, pass fortification
- Monastics exempt (meditation = their contribution); Sentinels contribute military patrol
- System functions well at community scale; breaks down for Alliance-wide projects

### Artisan Specialization
- Metalworkers (most valued — work with scarce imported material)
- Weavers (yak wool; highland specialty)
- Builders (stone construction at extreme altitude; specialized knowledge)
- Herbalists (overlap with spiritual practice; medicinal preparation)
- Aerolith processors (monastery-controlled; lay workers supervised by monks)

### The Aerolith Labor Problem
- Aerolith extraction is physically dangerous (thin air, unstable geology, Flux exposure)
- Lay miners do the work; monasteries claim the product
- Miners receive food and shelter but no share of export profits
- Growing resentment: "We breathe thin air and break rocks; they sell crystals to foreigners"
- Potential flashpoint if external trade increases aerolith demand

## Internal Economic Contradictions

1. **Spiritual wealth vs. material poverty**: Monasteries sit on valuable aerolith reserves while lay communities struggle with subsistence. The monastic response ("material wealth is irrelevant to spiritual progress") rings hollow when spoken by those with full storerooms.

2. **Trade growth vs. isolation doctrine**: Aerolith and herb exports are the Kingdom's only source of metal, grain, and manufactured goods. Expanding trade requires more pass engagement with outsiders — which the Monastic Council views as spiritual contamination.

3. **Youth migration as economic loss**: Every young person who leaves represents decades of agricultural labor and communal contribution. The Kingdom cannot replace this labor without either increasing workload on remaining communities or accepting foreign workers (which violates isolation principles).

4. **Covenant dependency trap**: Covenant-funded research programs bring material resources that the Kingdom desperately needs. Cutting ties means losing equipment, supplies, and scholarly prestige. Maintaining ties means deepening a dependency that enables Covenant intelligence extraction.

## Story Relevance

- **Rin's perspective**: She sees the economic dysfunction clearly — the Kingdom's most valuable asset (meditation-based magical capability) is trapped inside an economic system that can't distribute its benefits
- **Covenant leverage**: They understand the Kingdom's vulnerabilities (metal dependency, food margin, aerolith monopoly) and use them to create institutional dependency
- **Resistance opportunity**: Help the Kingdom develop alternative trade relationships that reduce Covenant dependency without violating all isolation principles

# Mercantile Federation — Economy & Trade Systems

## Primary Economic Model: Contract Capitalism

Everything has a price. Everything has a contract. The Federation is the wealthiest nation per capita and controls international shipping routes, banking infrastructure, and trade finance.

### The Contract Economy
- **Property**: Contractual. You own what your contract says you own.
- **Employment**: Contractual. Terms defined by agreement — hours, wages, conditions specified.
- **Marriage**: A contract. Divorce is contract termination, subject to exit clauses.
- **Citizenship**: Contractual. Each canton defines requirements; most require guild membership or property ownership.

### Trade Infrastructure
- Most sophisticated commercial infrastructure in the world
- **Banking**: Contract-backed with magically-enforced terms. Loans, insurance, investment vehicles.
- **Currency**: The "compact mark" — standardized across 14 cantons. Backed by contract-vault reserves.
- **Maritime fleet**: Largest in the world; trade routes spanning all coastal nations.
- **Insurance**: Federation invented maritime insurance (contractual risk-pooling). Enables high-risk trade that other nations can't finance.

## Primary Economic Drivers

### Maritime Commerce (Dominant)
- Controls 60%+ of international shipping
- Port cities generate majority of Federation wealth
- Naval superiority enforces trade route access
- Maritime guilds (shipping, insurance, navigation) are the most powerful economic institutions

### Banking & Finance
- Federal banking system is the default mechanism for inter-national trade
- Even Valdimere and Therryn use Federation banks for large transactions
- Loan instruments, insurance products, investment vehicles — all contractual, all magically enforced
- Banking guild controls currency supply and credit availability

### Manufacturing
- Guild-organized production: textiles, glass, paper, precision instruments, ships
- Quality controlled by guild standards; innovation through guild-approved research
- Export of manufactured goods to all trading partners
- Specialty: magical infrastructure components (contract-vault equipment, Flux-stabilized instruments)

### Agriculture
- Temperate coastal agriculture; sufficient but not surplus
- Food security through trade (imports from Valdimere and Andean Alliance)
- Agricultural workers are Tier 4 (unguilded); no political representation

## Taxation System

| Item | Rate | Notes |
|------|------|-------|
| Trade transactions | 5-15% | Canton-specific; varies by good type |
| Guild membership | Annual fee | Plus 10-15% of earnings |
| Property ownership | Annual assessment | Based on contract-vault valuation |
| Maritime cargo | Per-cargo levy | Based on weight and destination |
| Contract registration | Per-contract fee | Covers magical enforcement costs |
| Federal contribution | Canton-assessed | Each canton contributes proportionally to federal budget |

### Revenue Distribution
- Canton treasuries retain 70-80% of collected taxes
- Federal budget funded by canton contributions (quarterly assessment)
- Guild treasuries supplement member services through dues
- Arbitration Court funded independently through case fees

## Currency & Finance

### The Compact Mark
- Silver-standard currency; fixed value maintained by contract-vault reserves
- Paper notes backed by vault deposits (fractional reserve system)
- Accepted throughout the trading world as reliable medium of exchange
- **Vulnerability**: Entire system depends on trust in contract-vault integrity

### Banking Innovation
| Instrument | Function | Who Benefits |
|------------|----------|-------------|
| Trade credit | Short-term loans for cargo purchase; repaid on delivery | Merchants (Tier 2-3) |
| Maritime insurance | Risk pooling for shipping losses; contractual payout | Ship owners, investors |
| Investment shares | Fractional ownership of trading ventures; contractual profit-sharing | Patriciate, wealthy guild masters |
| Life Contracts | Debt servitude; magically binding | Creditors (at the expense of debtors) |

### The Freeport Exception
- Freeport operates with reduced financial regulation
- No guild restrictions; no contract-vault enforcement within city limits
- Result: black market banking, unlicensed trade, innovation, fraud
- Federation tolerates Freeport because it serves as economic pressure valve

## Internal Economic Contradictions

### 1. Legal Equality vs. Structural Exploitation
- All parties technically equal before the contract
- But: contract-drafting sophistication concentrated among wealthy; poor sign agreements they don't fully understand
- Result: legal mechanisms produce outcomes indistinguishable from theft

### 2. Contract-Bound Crisis
- 10% of population in Life Contracts (growing)
- System designed to prevent escape: debt terms ensure repayment is nearly impossible
- Economically productive (bound labor is cheap) but morally indefensible
- Abolition would destabilize the entire contract-enforcement mechanism

### 3. Canton Competition as Inefficiency
- 14 cantons competing wastes resources on duplication and sabotage
- Each canton maintains separate military, administration, and trade apparatus
- Federal coordination is slow and partial; supermajority requirement prevents unified economic policy

### 4. Aelfar Infrastructure Dependency
- Contract enforcement depends on Aelfar-derived technology maintained empirically, not theoretically
- The Federation doesn't fully understand how the contract-vault mechanism works
- If disrupted (earthquake, sabotage, Covenant manipulation), the economy collapses overnight
- Trust was never social — it was always magical. Remove magic, trust evaporates.

## Trade Relations

### Exports
| Export | Value | Destination | Notes |
|--------|-------|-------------|-------|
| Manufactured goods | High | All nations | Precision instruments, glass, textiles |
| Financial services | Very High | All nations | Banking, insurance, trade credit |
| Ships | Very High | Coastal nations | Best shipbuilding in the world |
| Compact marks | Medium | All trading partners | Currency used internationally |

### Imports
| Import | Source | Necessity | Notes |
|--------|--------|-----------|-------|
| Grain | Valdimere | Essential | Supplementary food supply |
| Metals | Valdimere, Andean Alliance | Important | Raw materials for manufacturing |
| Aeroliths | Celestial Kingdom | Valuable | Magical components for contract infrastructure |
| Tropical goods | Andean Alliance, Umara | Luxury | Spices, medicinal herbs, dyes |
| Weapons | Valdimere | Strategic | Federation has limited arms manufacturing |

## Covenant Economic Presence

### Current Infiltration
- Covenant-affiliated banks control ~25% of inter-national trade financing
- Acquiring controlling interests in maritime insurance guilds
- Members of the Financial Arbitrators Guild
- Positions on Arbitration Court nomination committees

### Strategy
- Control institutions that adjudicate contracts without changing any laws
- Create structural dependency on Covenant-controlled financial services
- Withdrawal of services would collapse trade credit, insurance, and banking
- Federation's own legal principles protect the mechanism of its subordination

### Why the Federation Can't Respond
- Covenant operations are legal; all contracts are enforceable
- Identifying "Covenant banking" is impossible (they use Federation institutions)
- Expelling Covenant agents would require breaking contracts — which undermines the entire system
- The Financial Arbitrators Guild warned the Assembly; the Assembly passed a non-binding resolution of concern

# Valdimere — Economy & Trade Systems

## Primary Economic Drivers

### Agricultural Production
- **Heartland crop surplus** feeds population and generates export revenue
- Grain quotas extracted as tax; surplus sold through state trade monopoly
- Flour milling controlled by state (mill tax extracted)
- Food security drives military power (stable, fed armies)

### Trade Routes
- **River commerce** (internal): Highly taxed; fees collected at every checkpoint
- **Border crossings** (external): Tariffs on all goods; foreign merchants negotiate with crown
- **Caravan routes** (overland): Dangerous, expensive; safer option paid more for convoy protection
- **Delta ports** (maritime): Major wealth concentration; naval power projection point

### Taxation System
| Item | Tax Rate | Notes |
|------|----------|-------|
| Agricultural harvest | 30% | State tithe; collected in kind or currency |
| Merchant trade | 15-25% | Varies by guild; higher for non-Valdimerian goods |
| Magical services | 20-40% | Depends on tier; unregistered magic: confiscation |
| Land ownership | Varies | Annual dues to regional governor |
| Guild membership | Annual fee | Plus percentage of earnings (10-15%) |
| Trade caravan pass | Per-route; high | Negotiated with marcher lords + crown split |

### Labor Economy
- Peasant labor: Bound to land; owe labor service as part of tax obligation
- Guild apprentices: Unpaid; work for training + room/board
- Military conscription: Required service (length varies by region/need)
- Slave labor: Minimal; captured prisoners used for dangerous work

### Currency & Finance
- Valdimerian coin: Silver standard; fixed value officially (reality = inflation)
- Money lenders: Licensed through guilds; high interest (20-40%)
- Imperial treasury: Centralized control; emperor allocates funds
- Debt enslavement: Unpayable debts lead to servitude

## Manufacturing & Crafts

### Controlled Industries
- **Weapons**: State monopoly; only military lodges produce quality blades
- **Armor**: Guild-controlled; restricted to military/nobility
- **Magic items**: Rare state monopoly; extremely expensive
- **Textiles**: Guild-controlled; imported goods taxed heavily

### Guild Craft Hierarchy
- **Master**: Owns shop; sets prices; takes majority profit
- **Journeyman**: Skilled worker; paid wages; hopes to become master (difficult)
- **Apprentice**: Unpaid labor; learns trade; most never advance

### Innovation & Technology
- State controls weapon development
- Innovation requires imperial approval
- Craftspeople working on new designs must register (and pay bureaucratic fees)
- Failure to innovate within spec = expelled from guild

## Internal Contradictions

### Systemic Problems
1. **Overtaxation** — Tiers 4-5 pay unsustainable percentages; starvation common
2. **Currency devaluation** — Inflation corrodes savings; poor suffer most
3. **Monopolistic pricing** — Guilds control prices; consumers have no choice
4. **Economic stagnation** — Conservative trade practices; lack of innovation
5. **Wealth concentration** — Upper tiers accumulate; lower tiers starve

### Ecological Crisis Impact
- Frontier agricultural yields declining (Flux corruption)
- Animal resources depleted (migration away from Valdimere)
- Trade routes becoming less safe (monsters from Flux)
- Military spending increases (defending borders); tax burden rises

### The Fiscal Cliff
- Current trajectory unsustainable
- State predicts revenue shortfall in 5-10 years
- Response being planned: more taxation, more conscription, possibly aggressive conquest
- Creates desperation → recruitment opportunity for resistance

## Economic Relevance to Story

### Aisen's tavern:
- Sits on trade route; derives income from travelers
- Subject to taxes and fees; struggles during shortages
- Marcher lord offers "protection" (extortion) during crisis
- Economic crisis → financial desperation → mysterious patron's intervention

### Covenant economic role:
- May control certain trade routes (hidden ownership)
- Manipulates grain prices (hoarding→shortage→buying cheap when desperate)
- Uses economic power to recruit desperate people
- Economic crisis masks their conspiracy (blame poor governance, not sabotage)

### Resistance economic strategy:
- Targets trade routes (disrupts Covenant+ supplies)
- Supports alternative economies (black market, diaspora networks)
- Redistributes confiscated wealth (builds loyalty among poor)
- Post-war: Rebuilds economic system around fairness (not monopoly)
