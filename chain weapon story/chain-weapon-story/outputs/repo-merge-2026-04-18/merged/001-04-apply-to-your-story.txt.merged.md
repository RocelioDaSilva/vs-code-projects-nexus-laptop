## Source: writingtutorials/01-Brandon-Sanderson-Hard-Magic/04-apply-to-your-story.txt

Apply to your story:

- Add a nosebleed, tremor, or other physical cost every time Aisen uses telekinesis near his limit.
- Show Caspian's hand wither when he uses blood ice.
- Have a minor character break their limit and suffer permanent damage (e.g., lose a hand) to foreshadow Aisen's death.
- Never let Aisen solve a major problem with magic alone. He must use physics (leverage, momentum, terrain) to reduce the cost.

## Source: writingtutorials/02-Joe-Abercrombie-Gray-Morality/04-apply-to-your-story.txt

Apply to your story:

- Give Aisen a moment where he chooses pragmatism over compassion, and the reader disagrees with him.
- Let a "good" character (e.g., Amara) make a selfish choice that hurts an innocent.
- Add dark humor to bleak scenes (e.g., Pieter says, "Well, that could have gone worse," after a near-death experience).
- After a victory, show the hidden cost: a friend crippled, a village burned, Aisen blamed.

## Source: writingtutorials/03-NK-Jemisin-Systemic-Worldbuilding/04-apply-to-your-story.txt

Apply to your story:

- Add one scene showing the ecological cost of magic (e.g., a village overrun by Flux-corrupted beasts).
- Show a noble using their power to harm a commoner without consequence.
- Include a servant's perspective (e.g., Nella the palace servant) as an interlude.
- Have Aisen witness a beast that is not a monster—just a victim of the thinning Veil.

## Source: writingtutorials/04-Patrick-Rothfuss-Prose-Mysteries/04-apply-to-your-story.txt

Apply to your story:

- Add sensory details to every scene (smell, touch, temperature).
- Plant three mysteries in the first act to be resolved in the third.
- Choose a recurring motif (broken chain link, wooden fox, etc.) and use it at least five times.
- Write one paragraph of pure, evocative description (no dialogue, no action, just atmosphere).

## Source: writingtutorials/06-Robin-Hobb-Character-Interiority/04-apply-to-your-story.txt

Apply to your story:

- Add a scene where Aisen is alone, grieving, vulnerable.
- Show a flashback triggered by a smell or sound (e.g., smoke triggers Aldric's execution).
- Let Aisen fail to save someone. Let him carry the guilt.
- Show him almost trusting someone, then pulling back.
