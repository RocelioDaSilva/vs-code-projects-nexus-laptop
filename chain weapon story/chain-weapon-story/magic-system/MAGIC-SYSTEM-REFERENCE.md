## Core Principles (Rule Summary)

1. Telekinesis is low-output, high-precision in this world. It cannot lift vast masses, but it can apply focused forces with surgical effect.
2. Mana cost scales with *control complexity* (precision, sustained hold, multi-point coordination), not strictly with raw force.
3. Physical principles apply: torque (τ = F × r), momentum conservation (p = m × v), and pressure (P = F / A) are the mental metaphors and practical calculations practitioners use.
4. Breaking a limit creates permanent, story-significant costs: nerve damage, reduced capacity, accelerated aging, or permanent sensory loss.

## Core Techniques (Definitions)

- Point Force: Focused push/pull on a single small target area. Low cost if momentary; high cost if held.
- Torque Application: Apply rotational force around an axis. Useful to pop joints, unscrew locks, or change footings.
- Momentum Transfer: Add or subtract small momentum to an object that is already moving; works best on items connected to the wielder's chain.
- Boundary Sense: A passive, localized perception bubble. Feels shape and motion of objects entering immediate personal space.
- Construct Tether: A weak telekinetic tether to a disconnected object (e.g., a dropped blade). Maintained at cost; severed by heavy impacts or anti-magic fields.
- Impulse Push: A very short, forceful nudge timed to coincide with an impact (e.g., a telekinetic flick at the moment a blade strikes to deflect it).

## Mechanics & In-World Constraints

- Range: Novice 1–4 m; Trained (Aisen) 4–10 m; Expert up to 15 m. Fine control decays with distance. → **Canonical numbers**: [TELEKINESIS-NUMERIC.md](TELEKINESIS-NUMERIC.md)
- Mass Limit: Can affect small objects easily; affecting heavier objects requires connecting them to the wielder (through chain or contact) to reduce required force.
- Multi-Point Control: Holding multiple objects or tethers multiplies cognitive load and mana cost linearly (approx. cost × N points). Sustaining N points rapidly drains capacity.
- Disruption: High-impact forces, magical interference, or direct trauma to the caster will interrupt delicate telekinetic maneuvers and may cause backlash.

## Cost Table (Narrative shorthand)

| Action | Relative Mana Cost | Typical Consequence if Abused |
|---|---:|---|
| Point tug/push (instant) | Low | Fatigue if repeated rapidly |
| Sustained hold (1+ sec) | Medium | Arm tremor, shallow nerve ache |
| Multi-point tether (2-4 points) | Medium–High | Cognitive fog, headaches |
| Torque on living limb (dislocation) | High | Nerve shock, temporary numbness |
| Large momentum transfer to heavy object | Very High | Permanent capacity loss risk |

## Training Exercises (Progression)

1. Candle Nudge: Lift/tilt candle flame precisely to align wick—focus and breath control.
2. Feather Flick: Apply micro-impulses to a feather to alter its drift—timing practice.
3. Rotational Drill: Spin a small coin on a table using torque, then stop it—fine rotational control.
4. Multi-Tether Warmup: Maintain 2 tethers (blade + ring) for 10s, recall softly—mana budgeting.
5. Combat Integration: Time an impulse push to alter a training spear’s trajectory mid-thrust.

## Cultural Approaches

- Scholarly (Celestial Halls): Telekinesis as applied physics. Emphasis on theory, careful notation, and sanctioned drill practices. Syllabus-like training.
- Clerical (Tarovin-style): Telekinesis as precise ritual—focus on constraints, mantra-like breathing, and barter-based teaching.
- Guild (Merchant/Maritime): Telekinesis for utility—rigging, lifting, and small repairs; emphasis on economy of action.
- Folk/Border: Practical tricks, often secretive, passed by hunters and smiths for utility rather than prestige.

## Forbidden Use & Limit-Breaking

- Techniques that attempt to apply sustained force to heavy masses, or to manipulate many independent points simultaneously, are classified as Limit-Break methods and yield permanent cost.
- Covenant institutionalized some limit-breaking to create fuel for ritual magic (sacrifice of capacity for immediate power). This is a major moral and plot point.

## Anti-Telekinesis Measures

- Dampening fields (crystals, artifacts) reduce effective range and precision.
- Strong impacts and shockwaves scatter delicate control and may cause backlashes.
- Enchanted armor with pressure-diffusion makes high-precision impact techniques (e.g., torque) much less effective.

## Sample Scene Uses (how to write it)

- Tactical Nudge: A small push on a spear tip at the exact moment it would hit a guard’s armor seam—explain as microsecond timing and vector redirection.
- Pivot Break: Lock the chain around a wrist, apply torque mentally, describe the bone-joint resisting then popping—show sensory backlash on caster.
- Recall & Redirect: A detached blade arcs toward a foe; caster tethers it and nudges the tip to change angle by 15° to avoid guard.

## Tagging & In-Story Metadata

- For text consistency, when describing telekinesis use physical metaphors: torque, fulcrum, vector, impulse, pressure. Avoid vague 'force' jargon.
- Note consequences immediately: after a heavy move, mention ache, tremor, or temporary sensory loss to remind readers of cost.

## Blood Mastery & Clone Magic (Rule Breaker Systems)

### Bloodheart — Blood as Weapon
- **Reference**: [characters/BLOODHEART.md](../characters/BLOODHEART.md)
- **Core**: Blood generation & manipulation as primary power; inscribed blade as life-linker
- **Key Techniques**: Heart Unrolling, Blood States (mist/liquid/hard), Whips & Chains, Stat Boost via Infusion
- **Philosophy**: Constraint (low mana use) enables mastery; blood quality matters more than magical reserves
- **Narrative Role**: Mentor to those learning that "mastery comes from sharpening fundamentals, not accumulating power"
- **Mechanical Note**: Bloodheart never meets Caspian—deliberate separation of blood practioners to preserve mystery and future stakes

### Clone Master — Self as Multiplicity
- **Reference**: [characters/CLONE-MASTER.md](../characters/CLONE-MASTER.md)
- **Core**: Manifest clones of self; clones mirror master's capabilities; journey toward self-trust and acceptance
- **Key Techniques**: Summon & Unsummon, Mirror Capabilities, Partial Release, Goo Constructs, Presence Masking, Self-Sacrifice & Transformation (hybrid form), Dematerialization (post-transformation)
- **Philosophy**: Strength comes from believing in and loving all versions of yourself; vulnerability unlocks new power
- **Narrative Role**: Arc demonstrating that identity is fluid; ensemble cooperation mirrors learning to accept multiplicity within self
- **Mechanical Note**: Clone Master is a transmigrator (inherited prodigy status); arc is Natsubasa Baru-like (escape from past trauma to self-acceptance)

### Aisen — Flux Multiplier & Momentum Master
- **Reference**: [characters/AISEN-EXPANDED.md](../characters/AISEN-EXPANDED.md)
- **Core**: Master of Flux multipliers; base stats amplify through stacked low-cost boosts; momentum shifting for combat efficiency
- **Key Techniques**: Momentum Shifting (cancel-acceleration), Layered Stat Multiplication, Physical Base Amplification, Precision Information Reading, Telekinetic Multiplication
- **Philosophy**: Mastery of basics scales better than mastery of complexity; efficiency beats abundance
- **Narrative Role**: Linchpin of ensemble; teaches adaptive thinking; forms trinity with Healer and Artificer
- **Mechanical Note**: Appears weaker than nobles with higher mana pools but defeats them through understanding

### Hydromancer — Water as Flow
- **Reference**: [characters/HYDROMANCER.md](../characters/HYDROMANCER.md)
- **Core**: Water manipulation via Bernoulli principle; pressure zones enable efficiency; sensory network through water
- **Key Techniques**: Pressure Blade, Projectile Ejection, Spinning Construct, Current Reading, Stream Manipulation, Micro-Precision Blade, Pressure Cushion
- **Philosophy**: Don't control water; guide what water already wants to do; constraint in environment (need water) breeds understanding
- **Narrative Role**: Traveling ally; demonstrates that efficiency and mastery are achievable in any specialty
- **Mechanical Note**: Depends on water availability; near-useless in desert but overwhelming in aquatic/wet terrain
