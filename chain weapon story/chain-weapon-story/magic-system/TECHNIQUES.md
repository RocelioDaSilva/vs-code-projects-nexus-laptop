# Technique Cards — Expanded Spell & Technique Writeups

Purpose: Full, ready-to-use technique cards for authors: effect, range, information cost, narrative cost, failure modes, counters, and scene hooks.

Use: Drop these into combat or discovery scenes. Each card includes a short "how to show it" note and references to sample scenes in `story/`.

## Telekinesis — Impulse Push
- Class: Telekinesis (Point Action)
- Effect: A microsecond impulse that alters an object's trajectory by a few degrees or imparts small additional momentum to a weapon.
- Range: ~2–7 m (close-range precision)
- Information Cost: Low (single informational vector)
- Narrative Cost: Hand tremor or mild headache if used repeatedly; fatigue after >5 uses in quick succession.
- Failure Mode: Mistimed impulse (no effect) or backlash if caster is struck during the push (sharp pain, blurred boundary sense).
- Counters: High-impact shocks, anti-telekinesis crystals, sudden displacement of anchor points.
- Scene Hook: Use for a decisive nudge that makes a spear glance off armor. See `story/scene-stringline-ambush.md` for how a micro-action can reveal a physical rule.
- How to show: Describe a precise mental vector, a pulse through the caster's palm, and the target's trajectory changing by an impossible sliver.

## Telekinesis — Torque Application
- Class: Telekinesis (Rotational Force)
- Effect: Apply rotational force around a pivot to dislocate, unscrew, or spin an object.
- Range: 1–5 m
- Information Cost: Medium (requires axis/vector information)
- Narrative Cost: Brief nerve shock in caster's arm; burn on sustained use.
- Failure Mode: Over-rotation causing structural failure (unintended shrapnel) or caster tremor.
- Counters: Pressure-diffusing armor, multi-anchor targets (prevents lever action).
- Scene Hook: Use the chain as a fulcrum to twist a gauntlet free or snap a joint. See `story/caspian_first_encounter.md` for related physics-based injury tone.
- How to show: Use physical metaphors—fulcrum, lever, torque—describe the chain's rotation and the caster's focused breath.

## Telekinesis — Multi-Point Control
- Class: Telekinesis (Sustained)
- Effect: Maintain control of multiple objects/points simultaneously (floating chain segments, multi-weapon ballet).
- Range: 2–7 m
- Information Cost: High (cost scales linearly with control points)
- Narrative Cost: Cognitive fog, headache, blurred peripheral awareness; long sessions risk permanent sensory loss.
- Failure Mode: One point loses hold → cascade failure across other points; catastrophic if holding explosives or limbs.
- Counters: Cognitive disruption (smoke, loud shock), severing connections (cut chain), anti-magic dampeners.
- Scene Hook: Aisen controlling disconnected chain segments, then losing one during an impact and scrambling. See `story/scene-node-expedition.md` for tension when systems fail.
- How to show: Mark the number of contact points in prose and show the caster's mental juggling (counting, breath control).

## Telekinesis — Construct Tether
- Class: Telekinesis (Tether)
- Effect: Create a weak telekinetic tether to a nearby object; can pull/push with limited force.
- Range: 1–6 m
- Information Cost: Medium
- Narrative Cost: Tethering for long durations causes arm numbness, and repeated tethers degrade recall precision.
- Failure Mode: Heavy impacts sever the tether; sudden trauma causes backlash to caster's neural sense.
- Counters: Heavy shock, pressure diffusion on the object, physical anchoring.
- Scene Hook: Use to recall a dropped blade or anchor a thrown link; the tether snaps during combat for dramatic injury.
- How to show: Describe the tether like a thread of thought stretching from caster to object—thin, vibrating, alive.

## Telekinesis — Momentum Bridge
- Class: Telekinesis (Transfer)
- Effect: Siphon or inject momentum between objects without direct contact.
- Range: Short (2–4 m)
- Information Cost: High (requires matching momentum vectors)
- Narrative Cost: Localized heat at bridge endpoints; caster feels a recoil of force.
- Failure Mode: Mismatch generates momentum echoes—objects twitch later or drift.
- Counters: Place conductive metal links at the bridge points to short-circuit transfer; create chaotic motion (noise) to prevent vector matching.
- Scene Hook: Add telekinetic push to a chain blade mid-swing; the caster is left with a hot sting. See `story/scene-stringline-ambush.md` for small-moment physics.
- How to show: Explain momentum like a passing of weight; show heat or a crackle at contact points.

## Teleportation — Short Swap
- Class: Translocation (High-Cost)
- Effect: Swap two small volumes of space (person-sized). Allows near-instant reappearance across short distances.
- Range: Requires line-of-sight or pre-mapped coordinates.
- Information Cost: Very High (molecular/positional precision)
- Narrative Cost: Immediate entropy debt: cold/frost at donor site, nerve haze for caster, risk of partial reconstitution injuries.
- Failure Mode: Spanning objects produce shearing (see Loop Anchor); anchors in either volume can cause partial reconstitution.
- Counters: Physical anchors across likely arrival sites; dampening fields that reduce precision.
- Scene Hook: The string example—teleporter arrives wounded by an anchored loop. See `story/scene-stringline-ambush.md` for the archetype.
- How to show: Make the reappearance visceral—air stutters, skin prickles, an aftertaste of cold.

## Teleportation — Loop Anchor (String Technique)
- Class: Translocation Counter
- Effect: A mundane physical loop or anchored cord spans arrival surface and forces a chaotic resolution on teleportation.
- Range: N/A (physical placement at destination)
- Information Cost: None for the anchor (the caster placing it may expend low mana only to throw/accurately place it)
- Narrative Cost: None for the user placing it; the teleporter pays heavy cost if anchored.
- Failure Mode: If the anchor is incorrectly placed or the teleporter avoids the exact spot, non-lethal or no effect.
- Counters: Teleporters who check destination surfaces, pre-clear landing zones, or use wide-area arrival.
- Scene Hook: Aisen rigs a window with fishing line and learns the rule. See `story/scene-stringline-ambush.md`.
- How to show: Focus on the mundane detail; the horror comes from the mismatch between high magic and low object.

## Memory‑Lock — Link Toggle
- Class: Structural Memory (State Lock)
- Effect: Force a memory-metal link or enchanted gear to adopt or release a stored state (locked vs. released).
- Range: Touch or short tether
- Information Cost: Medium
- Narrative Cost: Slight neural fatigue; repeated toggles cause minor sensory loss in the hand.
- Failure Mode: Foreign particulates (sand, dust) jam the mechanism; toggles can get stuck.
- Counters: Throw abrasive dust into joints, temperature shock (freeze/overheat) to make links brittle or immobile.
- Scene Hook: Aisen learns to throw fine dust into an armored joint to seize an enemy's enchanted plate.
- How to show: Describe tiny grains clogging a smooth mechanism, the link resisting with a metallic cough.

## Memory‑Lock — Jam (Sand/Dust Pouch)
- Class: Mundane Counter (Weaponized Particle)
- Effect: Throw fine particulates into moving enchanted joints to produce jamming.
- Range: Short throwing distance (5–10 m)
- Information Cost: None
- Narrative Cost: Minimal to user; use responsibly (creates mess, can harm innocents)
- Failure Mode: Wind disperses particles; enclosed armor resists.
- Counters: Protective seals, ritual grease, sealed bearings.
- Scene Hook: Aisen carries pouches of dust; tosses them into a canyon of bronze to seize a relock. See `story/scene-node-expedition.md` for tactile detail.
- How to show: Focus on grit under nails, the metallic complaint as moving parts seize.

## Tagging Link — Splash Tag
- Class: Tracking (Utility)
- Effect: Throw a weak tracking tag into an area; it clings to the first object a target contacts and reports presence.
- Range: Throwing distance + magnetic/adhesive reach
- Information Cost: Low
- Narrative Cost: Tag maintenance; tags degrade over time and require calibration.
- Failure Mode: Tag sticks to irrelevant debris; high clutter reduces reliability.
- Counters: Rapid environment cleansing, decoys, removing touched items quickly.
- Scene Hook: Tagging a field, then tracing a summoner through a leaf they brushed—used in recon beats and ambush setup.
- How to show: Describe the tag as a living bead that waits to bite into motion, then later beeps in a pocket.

## Flux Corruption — Corrupt Wound
- Class: Affliction (High Risk)
- Effect: Channel Flux into a biological target to warp tissue, create sustained pain, or bind a beast.
- Range: Touch or short (requires contact)
- Information Cost: High and unpredictable
- Narrative Cost: Leaves Flux residue on the environment, accelerates nearby decay, and marks the caster with sympathy.
- Failure Mode: Backlash can spread corruption to allies; the caster may become a locus for predatory anomalies.
- Counters: Silver, sanctified alloys, and dampening relics.
- Scene Hook: Corvin learns silver repels his beast; a corrupt wound shows an object's unnatural sheen. See `story/scene-node-expedition.md` for environmental cost cues.
- How to show: Emphasize rot, cold blue veins, and the way light refracts oddly off corrupted tissue.

## Blood Mastery — Heart Unrolling (Bloodheart Signature)
- Class: Blood Manipulation (Rule Breaker Tier)
- Effect: Pierce own chest cavity with inscribed blade; pull weapon upward to throat level; chest opens and unrolls heart-tissue into a larger, organ-based blade.
- Range: Melee (partial) or extended forms up to 10–15 m via blood whips
- Information Cost: Medium-to-High (requires intimate knowledge of own physiology)
- Narrative Cost: Immediate vulnerability window (1–2 seconds of convulsing/gasping); short duration before needing to retract into human form; severe depletion of biological reserves
- Failure Mode: Cavity jam (scar tissue buildup over time reduces reliability); incomplete transformation (heart only partially unrolls, causing internal hemorrhage); over-extension leads to rapid blood depletion and shock
- Counters: Prevent access to chest (full-torso binds); target the cavity site with entropy magic or anti-blood reagents; interrupt during vulnerable window
- Scene Hook: Bloodheart uses unrolling only when certain of victory; it's a tell that an opponent is already doomed or that Bloodheart has resolved to end it. See `characters/BLOODHEART.md` for lore.
- How to show: Visceral detail—the chest splitting, breath failing, organs reorganizing into a new shape. Make it beautiful and horrifying in equal measure.

## Blood Mastery — Blood States (Liquid, Mist, Hard)
- Class: Blood Manipulation (Projectile)
- Effect: Throw blood at varying states: liquid (spreads on impact), mist (disperses), hard (coagulated construct). Each state has different ballistic and structural properties.
- Range: 5–25 m (depends on state)
- Information Cost: Medium (requires understanding of blood composition and state-phase transitions)
- Narrative Cost: Drain on biological reserves; high-speed throws cause fatigue; mist forms dissipate quickly and waste blood
- Failure Mode: State transition fails mid-flight (liquid hardens too slowly, construct fragments); target immune to blood (sealed/magically protected)
- Counters: Dispersion nets (catch mist), hardened shields (block constructs), heat magic (prevents proper coagulation)
- Scene Hook: Bloodheart throws hardened blood-blades mid-combat, or summons mist to obscure the battlefield. The inscriptions on his primary blade enhance control. See `characters/BLOODHEART.md` for technique details.
- How to show: Describe blood's viscosity and weight; emphasize how blood behaves unlike water (it clings, spreads in complex patterns, has memory).

## Blood Mastery — Stat Boost via Infusion
- Class: Blood Manipulation (Self-Enhancement)
- Effect: Channel blood flow into specific limbs to temporarily enhance strength, speed, or endurance without expending mana (uses biological regeneration instead).
- Range: Self
- Information Cost: Low (requires basic vascular mapping)
- Narrative Cost: Limbs overheat; sustained boosts cause muscle tears; overuse leads to hemorrhagic shock
- Failure Mode: Infusion targets wrong limb (weakness in place of strength); veins rupture if timing is off
- Counters: Sudden cold (freezes blood, breaks circulation), arterial strikes (severing main vessels)
- Scene Hook: Bloodheart accelerates forward in a crucial moment; the reader sees his arm bulge and redden as blood surges. Short bursts make him unstoppable; long strings of boosts make him vulnerable.
- How to show: Emphasize the physical aftermath—sweat, tremor, the smell of iron. Each boost is visible and costs him.

## Blood Mastery — Whips & Chains
- Class: Blood Manipulation (Extended Control)
- Effect: Create extended tendrils of hardened blood that function as whips, chains, or binding restraints. Conductive (can amplify sensory input); stretchy (up to 15 m); multi-strand possible.
- Range: Up to 15 m per tendril
- Information Cost: High (requires simultaneous control of multiple blood constructs)
- Narrative Cost: Extreme fatigue; loss of sensation in the hands; extended use risks autonomic system failure
- Failure Mode: Constructs weaken if caster loses focus or mana dips; cold hardens them too much (lose flexibility); impact can shatter tendril if insufficient blood is dedicated
- Counters: Rapid heating (melts constructs), blade cleaners (cut through strands), shock (disrupts control cohesion)
- Scene Hook: Bloodheart uses a whip to redirect an ally's falling weapon or to snare an opponent at distance. The whips showcase his precision and control. See `characters/BLOODHEART.md` for integration.
- How to show: Detail the *weight* of blood even when hardened; describe the sound of it cracking through air (different from a leather whip).

## Clone Manifestation — Summon & Unsummon
- Class: Clone Magic (Rule Breaker Tier)
- Effect: Manifest clones of self from a "goo substrate"; each clone mirrors the caster's capabilities exactly. Unsummon on demand to recycle mass back into substrate.
- Range: Emerge within 10–20 m of substrate pool
- Information Cost: Low initially; scales with number of simultaneous clones
- Narrative Cost: Mana-intensive; early-story limit is 3–4 clones; grows as caster learns self-trust. Sustaining clones in combat is draining.
- Failure Mode: Too many clones leads to consciousness fragmentation (clones lose coordination); forced unsummon mid-combat leaves caster vulnerable; substrate depletion prevents new manifestations
- Counters: Isolate clones from substrate (they become mortal); AOE attacks that target multiple positions; entropy magic that corrupts the goo
- Scene Hook: Early story, clones feel "lesser" and caster hesitates when using them. By mid-story, clones move with perfect, unsettling synchronization. See `characters/CLONE-MASTER.md` for arc.
- How to show: Describe clones emerging from the goo like skin forming over a wound; the moment is unsettling and beautiful. Track which clone is "lead" vs. mirroring.

## Clone Manifestation — Mirror Capabilities
- Class: Clone Magic (Scaling Power)
- Effect: Each clone possesses identical stats, mana capacity, technique knowledge, and reflexes to the master. Clones improve as master improves.
- Range: N/A (inherent to clones)
- Information Cost: Baseline information cost (the caster provides all knowledge to clones via shared consciousness)
- Narrative Cost: None, except the existential anxiety of watching yourself fight
- Failure Mode: Clones inherit limitations too—if caster is injured or tired, clones become less effective; caster self-doubt manifests as clone hesitation
- Counters: Target caster's sense of self; introduce paradox (make caster unsure which clone is "real")
- Scene Hook: Turning point in arc: caster watches clones solve a problem he couldn't and realizes *they ARE him*. This realization is cathartic and terrifying. See `characters/CLONE-MASTER.md` for narrative arc.
- How to show: When a clone does something brilliant, have the master feel it resonate in his own body—a phantom pain, a sense-echo.

## Clone Manifestation — Partial Release
- Class: Clone Magic (Tactical Dodge)
- Effect: Manifest only a portion of a clone (arm, torso, leg) to block incoming attacks, create openings, or dodge with minimal mana loss.
- Range: Instant manifestation
- Information Cost: Medium (requires precision body-mapping)
- Narrative Cost: Partial manifestations are sloppy and feel wrong; overuse causes phantom pain in the corresponding real limb
- Failure Mode: Partial clone absorbed back into substrate before intended (leaves gap in defense); wrong portion activates (arm instead of torso, missing the defense)
- Counters: Strike unpredictably; force caster to predict wrong; over-extend the partial clone so it can't retract
- Scene Hook: Incoming punch → clone arm manifests and absorbs impact → master counter-attacks while clone arm is sacrificed. Efficient but expensive.
- How to show: Describe the sudden, wet sound of a clone limb forming; the opponent's shock that something solid appeared from nothing.

## Clone Manifestation — Goo Constructs & Morphing
- Class: Clone Magic (Resource Manipulation)
- Effect: Sacrifice a clone mid-combat to create temporary constructs (blades, shields, walls, anchors); clones can morph their bodies (elongate limbs, harden forearms, reshape torso). Constructs last until mana sustaining them depletes or construct is destroyed.
- Range: Varies (constructs deploy from clone's location)
- Information Cost: Medium (requires imagined blueprint of construct shape)
- Narrative Cost: Sacrificing a clone feels like self-harm; morphing causes temporary disorientation and sensory lag
- Failure Mode: Morph is unstable and collapses mid-combat; construct texture is weak and shatters easily; using too much goo for constructs means fewer clones available
- Counters: Temperature shock (destabilizes goo), time pressure (constructs decay quickly), predicting construct forms
- Scene Hook: Clone morphs into a long-reach weapon, sacrifices itself to create a blade trap. Valuable for demonstrating creative problem-solving and the grim math of clone sacrifice. See `characters/CLONE-MASTER.md`.
- How to show: Describe the *wrongness* of a limb stretching or torso reshaping; the goo ripples like water but never loses cohesion.

## Clone Manifestation — Presence Masking & Swap
- Class: Clone Magic (Late-Story Ability)
- Effect: Master learns to mask all clone presences so they feel identical to sensory magic. Master can switch consciousness/presence between clones, making true self impossible to determine without extreme sensory precision.
- Range: Within substrate connection (30–50 m)
- Information Cost: Very High (requires deep manipulation of existential signatures)
- Narrative Cost: Identity becomes unclear—master isn't sure which clone is "really him"; long-term masking risks permanent identity dissolution
- Failure Mode: Mask fails under scrutiny (extreme sensory magic pierces it); switching at wrong moment leaves caster disoriented (brief lag time)
- Counters: Extreme sensory specialists (Stellar Observer types might still detect); introduce noise so caster can't track own position
- Scene Hook: Late-story combat: opponent strikes what they think is master—turns out to be clone. Master was 20 m away the whole time. See `characters/CLONE-MASTER.md` for arc placement.
- How to show: Make the reveal shocking but fair; the reader should be able to re-read the scene and see the clues they missed.

## Clone Manifestation — Self-Sacrifice & Transformation
- Class: Clone Magic (Breakthrough)
- Effect: Master willingly sacrifices himself to protect a clone. Clones discover they can *replace* portions of master's body with goo-flesh. Master survives but becoming hybrid (partially clone). Unlocks dematerialization.
- Range: Requires close proximity (within 5 m)
- Information Cost: Extremely High (consciousness merger)
- Narrative Cost: Master is no longer fully human; identity is permanently altered; psychological integration required
- Failure Mode: Merger is incomplete (master wakes up malformed, missing limbs); consciousness doesn't integrate cleanly (master suffers dissociation)
- Counters: N/A — this is a breakthrough. Counters emerge afterward in form of vulnerabilities to anti-goo magic.
- Scene Hook: This is THE turning point. Master's sacrifice in a desperate moment unlocks the ability. It should come at the exact moment where all seems lost. See `characters/CLONE-MASTER.md` for narrative positioning.
- How to show: Make it beautiful and horrifying. The merger should feel like rebirth and violation simultaneously. Show clone hesitation before merging—they're afraid of changing their master.

## Clone Manifestation — Dematerialization (Hybrid Ability)
- Class: Clone Magic (Post-Transformation)
- Effect: Master (now part-clone) can convert portions or full body into goo-form and re-solidify elsewhere within sensory range of substrate.
- Range: 20–50 m (depends on distance and mana cost)
- Information Cost: High (requires reconstructing molecular position)
- Narrative Cost: Risk of incomplete reformation; sensory disorientation during transition; every use costs mana and biological reserves
- Failure Mode: Incomplete reformation (missing fingers, warped face); misstep during dematerialization (land inside stone, melting partially into objects)
- Counters: Dampening fields that sever substrate connection; anti-goo wards that repel the master's reformed body; attacks during dematerialization (while in goo form, vulnerable)
- Scene Hook: Mid-combat, master is cornered. Dematerializes as an escape or repositioning tool. First use should come as a shock (even to the reader). See `characters/CLONE-MASTER.md`.
- How to show: Describe the body melting into gel, the conscious sensation of losing cohesion, then reforming. It's not teleportation—it's dissolution and rebuilding.

## Flux Optimization — Momentum Shifting (Aisen Signature)
- Class: Flux (Acceleration Casting)
- Effect: Apply acceleration spell to body to gain velocity; *cancel spell midway* rather than sustain to completion. Result: imparted velocity; mana partially refunded.
- Range: Self (applies to full body or limbs)
- Information Cost: Low per application; costs scale with frequency of cancel-applications
- Narrative Cost: Requires intense mental focus; repeated use causes cognitive fatigue and disorientation
- Failure Mode: Mistimed cancellation (spell completes anyway, full cost applied); over-cancellation causes brief paralysis (nervous system resets)
- Counters: Cognitive disruption (lights, sound); anti-acceleration wards; neural dampeners
- Scene Hook: Aisen moves faster than nobles with higher-cost speed buffs because he understands the acceleration curve. See `story/scene-aisen-meets-healer.md` for introduction.
- How to show: Describe the sudden spike of movement, the moment of *stopping*, the faint shimmer of released mana. Make it feel like a controlled stutter.

## Flux Optimization — Layered Stat Multiplication
- Class: Flux (Scaling Enhancement)
- Effect: Stack multiple low-cost stat boosts on same target (limb, stat, action). Each boost multiplies independently; total effect is cumulative multiplier.
- Range: Self (can extend to allies via artifacts)
- Information Cost: Low per layer; scales linearly with number of layers
- Narrative Cost: Each layer adds minor info-cost; stack too high = paradox cascade (information debt spikes unexpectedly)
- Failure Mode: Layers destabilize if base stat is too weak; cascade causes temporary stat loss worse than starting position
- Counters: Anti-multiplier wards; dispel magic targeting single layers; paradox injection that destabilizes entire stack
- Scene Hook: Aisen combines strength boost (×1.15) + dexterity boost (×1.2) + momentum from acceleration (×1.1) for compound effect. Appears to use less mana than equivalent advanced spell.
- How to show: Show small magical effects stacking visibly; light from multiple sources combining; final effect that feels disproportionate to mana spent.

## Flux Optimization — Physical Base Amplification
- Class: Mundane + Flux (Training Integration)
- Effect: Train body relentlessly *without* magic; build base stats through physical practice. When Flux boosts apply, they multiply a higher foundation.
- Range: Self
- Information Cost: None (purely physical)
- Narrative Cost: Physical exhaustion, injury, time investment
- Failure Mode: Over-training causes injury that magic can't instantly heal (psychological barrier against pushing further)
- Counters: Target the trained physique with ailments that reduce willingness to move; create situations where rest is impossible
- Scene Hook: Aisen's "superhuman" feats are partly Flux magic but also partly decades of mundane training. Opponents mistake him for a mage when he's partially a fighter.
- How to show: Show Aisen doing simple physical exercises (repetitive, unglamorous) interspersed with magical moments; juxtapose discipline with flash.

## Flux Optimization — Precision Information Reading
- Class: Flux (Sensory Enhancement)
- Effect: Apply low-cost perception boost for brief moments; gain heightened sensory acuity. Use these windows to read opponent's body language, breathing patterns, magical signature.
- Range: Limited by natural sensory range (sight, hearing, proprioception)
- Information Cost: Low per use; repeated use causes "information strain" (temporary blindness, headaches, sensory numbness)
- Narrative Cost: Characters who know this technique can recognize when someone is using it (visible tells: dilated pupils, twitching muscles)
- Failure Mode: Information overload if perception boost activates during sensory shock (loud noise, bright light); can cause temporary paralysis
- Counters: Sudden sensory shocks; camouflage of intent (hide breathing patterns, muscle tension); misdirection
- Scene Hook: Aisen activates perception boost just before expected attack; dodges with perfect timing. Appears precognitive but actually just well-informed.
- How to show: Brief moment of intense focus from Aisen; visible sensory processing (eyes tracking micro-movements); then precisely-timed response.

## Flux Optimization — Telekinetic Multiplication
- Class: Telekinesis + Flux (Compound Action)
- Effect: Combine physical strike with telekinetic impulse applied at moment of impact. Telekinetic nudge targets armor seams, joint weak points, or pressure zones.
- Range: Strike range (melee) + telekinetic range (2–7 m fine control)
- Information Cost: Medium (requires precise timing and spatial awareness)
- Narrative Cost: Requires simultaneous control of two independent systems (physical body + telekinetic field)
- Failure Mode: Mistimed telekinetic impulse (applies before or after strike, negating bonus); over-precision causes backlash
- Counters: Flexible armor (no seams to target); moving targets (timing becomes harder); anti-telekinesis fields
- Scene Hook: Aisen punches with a tiny telekinetic nudge that makes armor deflection impossible. Appears stronger than he is.
- How to show: Strike connects, but somehow bypasses armor. Microscopic detail—the way impact propagates unexpectedly.

## Water Mastery — Pressure Blade (Hydromancer Signature)
- Class: Water Manipulation (Rapid Defense Formation)
- Effect: Coat hands/body with thin water film; create localized high-pressure zones at blade edge. Water solidifies into razor edge (without freezing) via Bernoulli pressure differentials.
- Range: Close (arm's reach)
- Information Cost: Low (requires understanding of Bernoulli principle)
- Narrative Cost: Water film evaporates quickly; sustained use dries skin (minor damage over time)
- Failure Mode: Water disrupted by sudden heat; pressure destabilized by impact; formation collapses under heavy striking force
- Counters: Heat magic (vaporizes water), sudden cold (freezes edge, becomes dull), impacts that exceed pressure tolerance
- Scene Hook: Hydromancer blocks incoming strike while washing hands in river. Defense appears impossible yet feels inevitable. See `characters/HYDROMANCER.md` for philosophy.
- How to show: Describe water acting unlike water—solid without ice, sharp without hardness; the physics feel slightly *wrong*.

## Water Mastery — Projectile Ejection (Pressure Launch)
- Class: Water Manipulation (Ranged Attack)
- Effect: Compress water into high-speed projectiles; sustain trajectory via Bernoulli effect. Water shot spreads on impact (difficult to block traditionally).
- Range: 10–30 m depending on pressure maintained
- Information Cost: Medium (requires pressure vector calculations)
- Narrative Cost: Aquatic depletion; rapid ejections cause sensory feedback pain (pressure sense overloads)
- Failure Mode: Pressure release before launch (explosion backward); trajectory curves unpredictably if pressure zones destabilize mid-flight
- Counters: Dispersal nets; hardened shields; anti-water wards; high wind (breaks pressure zones)
- Scene Hook: Hydromancer creates distance by launching water at near-supersonic speeds. Each projectile spreads on impact, making dodging difficult.
- How to show: Water moving faster than should be possible; opponent scrambling to defend against something that spreads and changes shape mid-flight.

## Water Mastery — Spinning Construct (Autonomous Barrage)
- Class: Water Manipulation (Centrifugal Deployment)
- Effect: Create fast-spinning plate/wheel of water; centrifugal force continuously ejects smaller droplets/streams. Construct operates autonomously; caster redirects via pressure zone adjustment.
- Range: 15–20 m radius (construct operates within this zone)
- Information Cost: Medium initial cost; very low sustained cost (Bernoulli effect does the work)
- Narrative Cost: Minimal sustained cost once construct is stable; creating multiple constructs multiplies cost
- Failure Mode: Construct destabilizes if caster loses focus; surrounding water disruption (rain, splashes) can degrade construct integrity
- Counters: Large-scale water disruption; dampening fields; extreme temperature change (freezes or boils the construct)
- Scene Hook: Construct spins in place while Hydromancer handles other threats. Opponent trapped in continuous bombardment while caster conserves mana.
- How to show: Water spinning with visible momentum, ejecting smaller streams in spray patterns. The construct feels *alive* but clearly isn't.

## Water Mastery — Current Reading (Sensory Network)
- Class: Water Manipulation (Extended Perception)
- Effect: Extended perception through water medium; "feel" vibrations, pressure changes, movements through any connected water.
- Range: 30–50 m radius in water-rich environments; drastically reduced in arid zones
- Information Cost: Passive/low if water is static; scales with complexity of information extracted
- Narrative Cost: Sustained reading causes pressure sense numbness; overuse risks permanent sensory damage
- Failure Mode: High-turbulence environments (rapids, storms) create noise that obscures useful information
- Counters: Arid environments (no water to read through); sophisticated camouflage (still movement, controlled breathing); sensory dampening
- Scene Hook: Opponent cannot hide in water or wet environments; Hydromancer has 360° awareness. Provides tactical advantage in aquatic terrain but weakness in deserts.
- How to show: Descriptions of Hydromancer "sensing" through water before seeing; blind but aware; suddenly knowing where threats are without looking.

## Water Mastery — Stream Manipulation (Bernoulli Guidance)
- Class: Water Manipulation (Environmental Control)
- Effect: Don't move entire water flow; move pressure *boundaries* that guide water to desired paths. Redirect rivers, split waterfalls, create temporary dams via pressure zone networks.
- Range: Scales with water volume; can theoretically affect infinite flow if pressure zones are maintained
- Information Cost: Very high initial setup; plummets once equilibrium reached (water does most of the work)
- Narrative Cost: Initial setup drains significant mana; sustained cost becomes negligible
- Failure Mode: Pressure zone collapse (water returns to natural flow); environmental disruption (earthquake, major flood) can disrupt entire system
- Counters: Disrupt one pressure zone (cascade failure); introduce massive additional water volume (overwhelms pressure differential); natural disasters
- Scene Hook: Hydromancer redirects river around allies, creates escape route by opening water channels, or traps enemies by surrounding with water. Environmental-scale power but only while pressure zones hold.
- How to show: Water flowing *wrong*, but in ways that feel inevitable once explained; read like natural phenomenon that *should* always happen.

## Water Mastery — Micro-Precision Blade
- Class: Water Manipulation (Surgical Cutting)
- Effect: Generate pressure differentials at millimeter scale; create ultra-fine water streams or blades for surgical cuts. Can slice stone, metal, or flesh with hydraulic precision.
- Range: Contact to ~1 m
- Information Cost: Very High (extreme precision required)
- Narrative Cost: High mana per cut; exhaustion after multiple cuts in succession
- Failure Mode: Loss of precision under stress; pressure blade destabilizes mid-cut
- Counters: Target the thin blade with opposite pressure (cancels out); move the target during cut (breaks precision)
- Scene Hook: Hydromancer makes incisions with millimeter accuracy. Can target specific targets in armor or sever particular materials without affecting adjacent areas.
- How to show: Describe the impossible precision; water cutting through materials as easily as air passes through water.

## Water Mastery — Pressure Cushion (Adaptive Armor)
- Class: Water Manipulation (Defensive Formation)
- Effect: Create sphere of balanced pressure around body; external attacks compress the cushion while internal pressure resists deformation. Acts as armor that adapts to incoming force, diffusing damage across larger area.
- Range: Close (body armor)
- Information Cost: Medium sustained cost
- Narrative Cost: Spikes when absorbing impacts; prolonged defense causes pressure sense exhaustion
- Failure Mode: Imbalanced pressure (one side compressed too much); armor collapses if pressure differential becomes too high
- Counters: All-directional attacks (can't defend everywhere); pressure-reversal magic; sustained pressure that outlasts caster's endurance
- Scene Hook: Hydromancer tanks hits that would kill unarmored mage. Can release pressure explosively for counter-attack.
- How to show: Water visibly rippling around body; impacts creating concentric rings of pressure; the caster standing calm inside chaos.
