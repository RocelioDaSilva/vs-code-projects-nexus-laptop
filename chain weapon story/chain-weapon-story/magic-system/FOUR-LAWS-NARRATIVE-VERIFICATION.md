# FOUR LAWS OF FLUX — NARRATIVE VERIFICATION AUDIT

## Overview

This audit verifies that the Four Laws of Flux are **implicitly coherent** throughout the manuscript. Each law should be demonstrated through scene consequences, not explicit explanation.

**Key Principle:** Readers should *feel* these laws through magic system effects, not have a character explain them.

> **Canonical Hierarchy**: The magic system operates on two conceptual layers:
> - **4 Meta-Laws** (this document): Universal constraints on ALL magic — Conservation, Entropy Exchange, Symmetry Breach, Information. These are the *costs and limits* that govern magic use regardless of domain.
> - **6 Flux Domains** (see WORLDBUILDING-INDEX.md): The six categories of reality that Flux can manipulate — Gravity, Energy, Information, Causality, Entropy, Momentum. These describe *what magic can do*.
> - Every use of any Flux Domain is subject to all four Meta-Laws. A gravity manipulation still costs energy (Conservation), generates heat/corruption (Entropy Exchange), cannot give one person disproportionate power indefinitely (Symmetry Breach), and carries knowledge-cost if it touches forbidden patterns (Information).

---

## The Four Laws (Reference)

### Law 1: Conservation
All energy in the Flux is conserved. You cannot create power from nothing. Mana doesn't disappear—it transforms, flows, depletes, regenerates, but never violates conservation of energy.

**Consequence if violated:** Impossible magic attempts, backfire, power bleeding into uncontrollable forms.

---

### Law 2: Entropy Exchange
Every use of magic generates entropy (heat loss, magical "waste," corruption). This manifests as:
- Heat/temperature loss in surrounding area
- Aging or deterioration of the caster or environment
- Void Zones (areas where entropy has accumulated so heavily that the Flux collapses)
- Fatigue, mana depletion, physical burnout

**Consequence if violated:** Caster burns out, Void Zones spread, environment decays.

---

### Law 3: Symmetry Breach
One consciousness should not have disproportionate power. Vast magical ability creates an imbalance in the Flux itself. This manifests as:
- Rule-breakers (anomalies whose existence destabilizes the system)
- Interference patterns (powerful mages create "noise" in the Flux that disrupts other magic)
- Heresy designation (too much power = forbidden)
- Burnout (system rejects the imbalance)

**Consequence if violated:** Anomalies emerge, society targets them, power becomes unstable and self-destructive.

---

### Law 4: Information
Knowledge has a cost. Forbidden knowledge specifically has a *Flux cost*—it corrupts the one who holds it, and the system actively punishes its spread.

**Consequence if violated:** Heresy punishment, Flux-sickness, memory erosion, forced integration becomes option to stop the spread.
