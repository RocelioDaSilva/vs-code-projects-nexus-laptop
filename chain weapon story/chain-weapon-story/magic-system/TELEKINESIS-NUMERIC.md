## 1. Effective Range by Skill Tier

| Tier | Label | Practical Range | Fine Control Range | Notes |
|------|-------|----------------|--------------------|-------|
| Novice | 1 | 1–4 m | 0–2 m | Can nudge small objects; no sustained hold |
| Trained | 2 | 4–10 m | 2–6 m | Aisen's typical working range for combat |
| Expert | 3 | 8–15 m | 5–10 m | Requires full concentration; cost multiplies |
| Archmage | 4 | 15–30 m | 10–20 m | Accessible only to elite or formation casters |

**Aisen's canonical range**: Tier 2–3 boundary. Combat effective range ≈ 8 m; maximum tested range ≈ 12 m at high cost.

> **Resolution note**: Earlier drafts state "2–7 m" (MAGIC-SYSTEM-REFERENCE.md) and "5–10 m" (TELEKINESIS-SYSTEM.md). Both are correct within context:
> - 2–7 m = novice-to-trained range (the range where *most* practitioners operate)
> - 5–10 m = trained-to-expert range (Aisen's primary working range)
> This table supersedes all previous standalone values.

## 3. Living-Body Rule (Canonical)

- **External force**: Allowed. Telekinesis may push, pull, restrain, or apply torque to a living body from outside (e.g., shove a person, pin an arm, apply twisting force to a joint). Cost is elevated (Tier 2–3).
- **Internal puppetry**: Forbidden (not just illegal — physically impossible without Covenant-restricted blood-ritual). Telekinesis cannot enter and manipulate nerves, muscles, or internal organs directly.
- **Dislocation technique**: Applying torque to a wrist or knee joint (locked via chain) is Tier 3 cost (~20–30 MU), causes nerve shock to *caster* as backlash, and is narratively significant.

## 4. Multi-Point Control Cost Multiplier

| Points Held | Approximate Cost Multiplier |
|-------------|----------------------------|
| 1 | ×1 (baseline) |
| 2 | ×1.8 |
| 3–4 | ×2.5–3 |
| 5–6 | ×4+ |
| 7+ | Exceeds typical capacity; causes cognitive overload |

**Narrative use**: Aisen's signature 4-clone technique in the Ryo vision sequence would cost ≈ ×3 multiplier on top of a Tier 2 baseline — around 35–45 MU total, representing peak effort.

## 5. Disruption Thresholds

| Disruption Source | Effect |
|------------------|--------|
| Hard physical impact on caster | Breaks all active tethers instantly |
| Anti-magic dampening field (crystal) | Reduces effective range by 50%; doubles MU cost |
| Magical interference / enemy counterspell | Degrades control precision; may snap tethers |
| Extreme cognitive load (7+ points) | Causes involuntary release; headache, temporary blindness |
| Backlash from living-limb torque | Nerve shock in caster's forearm; 10–30 sec partial numbness |
