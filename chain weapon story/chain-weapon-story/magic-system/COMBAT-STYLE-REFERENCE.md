# FIGHTING STYLES: PROSE & MECHANICS REFERENCE

## BRUTE STRENGTH
**Core Belief:** Overwhelming force crushes all.

**Sentence Structure:**
- Long build-up sentences that create momentum
- Short impact sentences that land hard
- Repetition of heavy verbs

**Key Verbs:**
slammed, crushed, shattered, stomped, drove, caved, burst, razed

**Sensory Focus:**
- Sound: crack, boom, roar, thunder
- Physical: weight, mass, pressure, ground shaking
- Describe impact BEFORE contact when possible

**Pacing:**
Slow wind-up → sudden impact (contrast creates power)

**Prose Example:**
The beast did not lunge. It moved. Twenty feet collapsed into nothing. The air itself was shoved aside by its mass. Corvin felt impact before seeing it—a wall of pressure, then fire across his chest. He was airborne. Bark shattered beneath him. The beast was already winding up another swing.

**Weaknesses to Show:**
- Slow recovery after heavy attacks
- Cannot adjust mid-swing
- Exhaustion visible in breathing

**Characters:** Corvin (beast form), Orin, brute nobles

---

## FINESSE
**Core Belief:** Precision defeats power.

**Sentence Structure:**
- Short, clipped sentences
- Each strike is a separate beat
- No wasted syllables

**Key Verbs:**
flicked, danced, slipped, traced, breathed, ghosted, wove, feathered

**Sensory Focus:**
- Sight: angles, gaps, sight lines
- Silent: strikes too fast to hear clearly
- Emphasis on WHAT WAS MISSED, not what was hit

**Pacing:**
Rapid, rhythmic, impossible to predict

**Prose Example:**
Rin did not attack. She offered. A needle flicked from her braid. He dodged. Another. He parried. The third came from behind—she had planted it five seconds ago. He didn't see it. He didn't hear it. He felt it. A bead of blood. She was already gone, already moving toward the next position, already calculating what came next.

**Weaknesses to Show:**
- Cumulative damage—small cuts add up, but one heavy blow shatters
- Requires mobility space
- Fragile if cornered

**Characters:** Rin, young Caspian, elite assassins

---

## STRATEGY
**Core Belief:** The right move at the right time wins.

**Sentence Structure:**
- Conditional language: If X, then Y
- Setup is described separately from payoff
- Cause and effect are explicit

**Key Phrases:**
"If he stepped there..." "Because she did X..." "Aisen had already..."

**Sensory Focus:**
- Observation: what is noticed, what is placed
- Timing: the pause before the strike
- Quiet intensity

**Pacing:**
Slow burn. Each action places a piece. Then sudden execution.

**Prose Example:**
Aisen did not attack. He waited. The enemy's pattern was three strikes then a pause. One. Two. Three. Now. His chain snapped forward—not at the enemy, but at the loose stone he had noticed ten minutes ago. The stone tripped the enemy's back foot. Just a stumble. Just a moment. That was all Aisen needed. His chain was already moving to the new angle.

**Weaknesses to Show:**
- If setup is interrupted, the plan fails
- Requires information (can be fed false info)
- Vulnerable during setup phase

**Characters:** Aisen, Kael, battle mages, generals

---

## TRICKERY
**Core Belief:** Deception is the deadliest weapon.

**Sentence Structure:**
- Misdirection: describe one action, reveal it was a feint
- Surprise twist mid-sentence
- Unreliable narration—the reader is lied to

**Key Phrases:**
"No—" / "He thought..." / "It was not..." / "Behind him..."

**Sensory Focus:**
- Confusion of senses: he saw X, but felt Y
- Layered actions: the feint and the real attack
- Disorientation

**Pacing:**
Rapid, disorienting, full of reversals

**Prose Example:**
Caspian raised his left hand. The enemy braced for ice. But the spear of ice was not the attack—it was the reflection. The real ice had already frozen the ground beneath the enemy's feet. He slipped. Caspian was already behind him. When had he moved? The spear was in his hand. No—it was in the enemy's chest. Had it always been there?

**Weaknesses to Show:**
- Falls apart if seen through (once you know the trick, it doesn't work twice)
- Relies on opponent's ignorance
- Area attacks defeat misdirection

**Characters:** Caspian (early), deceivers, illusionists

---

## ENDURANCE
**Core Belief:** I can take anything you dish out.

**Sentence Structure:**
- Repetition creates a grinding rhythm
- Short, relentless sentences
- Lists of accumulated damage

**Key Phrases:**
"Again." / "Once more." / "He did not stop." / Damage stacking: "His shield was cracked. His arm was numb. His vision blurred."

**Sensory Focus:**
- Damage accumulation: describe each wound
- Breathing: the endurance fighter breathes normally while opponent labors
- Contrast between opponent's exhaustion and fighter's steadiness

**Pacing:**
Slow, relentless, grinding to a halt

**Prose Example:**
The blade cut his arm. He did not flinch. The second cut his side. He kept moving. The third—he caught it. His hand bled around the steel. He pulled. The enemy stumbled. His forehead met the enemy's nose. Bone cracked. The enemy fell. Corvin stood. He was bleeding from three wounds. He did not sit down. He did not breathe hard. He simply waited for the next one.

**Weaknesses to Show:**
- Can be outlasted by pure overwhelm
- Lacks burst damage (hard finishes are difficult)
- Slower than opponents who gamble everything

**Characters:** Corvin (human form), wall knights, tanks

---

## OVERWHELM
**Core Belief:** Volume of attacks defeats any defense.

**Sentence Structure:**
- Run-on sentences that don't let the reader breathe
- Lists: "A blade, then another, then another, then another"
- No pause between actions

**Key Verbs:**
flooded, buried, saturated, cascaded, relentless

**Sensory Focus:**
- Sound: constant, overlapping
- Sight: too many targets to track
- Defense: failing under weight

**Pacing:**
Manic, rapid-fire, no breathing room

**Prose Example:**
Four copies of Ryo flickered into existence and they did not wait, they attacked, blades of lightning arcing from four angles at once, again and again and again, a storm of steel and light that did not stop, did not pause, did not let him breathe, again and again and again, blocking one meant two got through, blocking two meant he was backed into the corner, backed into the wall, there was no escape, just the endless rain of strikes.

**Weaknesses to Show:**
- Mana intensive (runs out)
- Predictable patterns can be exploited
- One well-placed counter can break rhythm

**Characters:** Ryo, swarmers, multi-summon casters

---

## ADAPTATION
**Core Belief:** I become what the fight needs.

**Sentence Structure:**
- Shifting sentence structure to match shifts in style
- Transitional phrases: "Now he was..." / "Then it became..."
- Rhythm changes mid-scene

**Key Phrases:**
"Now X, now Y, now Z" / "The chain reconfigured" / "He shifted"

**Sensory Focus:**
- Variety: no single focus
- Learning: the fighter notices and adjusts
- Flexibility

**Pacing:**
Constantly shifting, never settling

**Prose Example:**
Aisen's chain was a staff. The enemy closed. Now it was a whip—distance maintained. The enemy pushed forward. Now it was a net—two segments wrapping around the enemy's ankles. He stumbled. Now it was a spear. Aisen drove the tip into the enemy's shoulder. The enemy fell. Aisen stepped back. His chain reconfigured. Already he was planning the next phase. Already he was shifting again.

**Weaknesses to Show:**
- Cannot master any single style completely
- Vulnerable during reconfiguration
- Can be overwhelmed before adaptation

**Characters:** Aisen, shapeshifters, generalists

## QUICK COUNTERPLAY REFERENCE

| Beats | Because | Tell |
|-------|---------|------|
| Brute | Finesse | Speed bypasses power |
| Finesse | Endurance | Precision fails against wall |
| Strategy | Trickery | Deception breaks plans |
| Trickery | Brute | Area attacks defeat feints |
| Endurance | Overwhelm | Volume exceeds absorption |
| Overwhelm | Strategy | Patterns exploited |
| Adaptation | Specialist | Forced into unsustainable mode |

## THE FOUR-PHASE STRUCTURE (Every Time)

**Phase 1: TESTING**
- Tentative verbs: tested, probed, feinted
- Short exchanges
- Distance maintained
- Prose: Light, observational

**Phase 2: ESCALATION**
- Longer sentences
- Force verbs: slammed, crushed, drove
- Environment begins to suffer
- Prose: Building momentum

**Phase 3: REVERSAL**
- Short, sharp sentences
- Single moment of clarity
- Action becomes economical
- Prose: Sudden, precise

**Phase 4: RESOLUTION**
- Quiet aftermath
- Focus on cost: damage, exhaustion, consequence
- No celebration
- Prose: Sparse, reflective

---

## HOW TO USE THIS CARD

**When starting a fight scene:**
1. Identify each fighter's primary style (pick from the 7 above)
2. Note the prose signature for that style
3. Decide which phase of the fight you are writing
4. Write using appropriate sentence structure, verbs, and sensory focus
5. Check counterplay table if relevant

**Example:**
- Aisen (Adaptation + Strategy) vs. Brute Noble
- Primary style: Adaptation
- Prose signature: Shifting sentences, transitional phrases
- Phase 1: Test using short sentences, note enemy's power
- Phase 2: Escalate—longer sentences as noble pushes forward
- Phase 3: Reversal—short sharp sentences as Aisen exploits weakness
- Phase 4: Resolution—quiet aftermath, show damage

**Do not mix styles randomly.** Each character should have a consistent voice.
