# ACT I: Formation & Mentorship (Chapters 1-12, Age 5-15)
## Master Writing Guide

### Thematic Arc
**Innocence → Disillusionment → Pragmatism**

Aisen grows from a tavern child to a trained young man with combat skills, telekinetic precision, and a moral compass formed by two mentors. Every chapter pairs Aisen with a secondary character through pair-based fluid POV.

---

## Key Writing Challenges

### 1. Child Voice Without Childishness
Aisen ages from 5 to 15 across Act I. The narration must evolve:
- **Age 5-8**: Sensory-dominant. Short sentences. Wonder and confusion. "The man's hands were red. Not paint-red. Different."
- **Age 9-12**: Growing analytical capacity. Longer observations. Beginning to question.
- **Age 13-15**: Adolescent sharpness. Irony. Testing boundaries. Internal monologue becomes strategic.

**Technique (Robin Hobb — Close POV)**: Stay in Aisen's sensory experience. He doesn't understand adult motivations; he registers their effects. When Harald is sad, Aisen sees "the way his hand stopped moving on the spear" — not sadness itself.

### 2. Mentor Relationships Without Exposition Dumps
Harald and Tarovin teach through action. Avoid:
- ❌ "Harald explained that leverage mattered more than strength"
- ✅ "Harald pressed two fingers against the shaft. The spear tilted. 'Where did the force go?'"

**Technique (Sanderson — Hard Magic)**: Establish training drills as visible, repeatable systems. The reader learns the magic/combat alongside Aisen. Candle Nudge, Feather Flick, sliding thrust — concrete exercises that build understanding.

### 3. Violence With Weight
The robbery (Ch 3) is Aisen's first exposure to real violence. It must feel consequential:
- Don't skip the aftermath (Hobb: trauma-as-lens)
- Harald's response models healthy processing (he doesn't glorify or minimize)
- The weight carries forward — Aisen references it in later chapters

**Technique (Abercrombie — Consequences)**: Every act of violence in Act I should have visible aftermath. Bodies have weight. Blood has smell. Recovery takes time.

### 4. Pair-Based Fluid POV
Each chapter pairs Aisen with someone. The perspective shift happens through sensory/object anchors, not hard scene breaks:
- Object anchor: "Aisen set down the cup. On the other side of the bar, the cup's shadow moved differently for Harald."
- Sensory anchor: Sound, temperature, or smell bridges between two characters' perspectives.

## Chapter-by-Chapter Writing Notes

### Block 1: Foundation & First Violence (Ch 1-3)
| Chapter | Pair | Core Technique | Key Lesson |
|---------|------|---------------|------------|
| 1 — Tavern Opening | Aisen + Father | Rothfuss: sensory immersion | Establish the tavern as a full sensory world |
| 2 — Caspian's Visit | Aisen + Caspian | Rothfuss: slow reveal | Mystery without exposition; Caspian reveals nothing but implies everything |
| 3 — The Robbery | Aisen + Harald | Abercrombie: consequences | First violence; first mentor recognition; weight of harm |

### Block 2: Mentorship & Training (Ch 4-9)
| Chapter | Pair | Core Technique | Key Lesson |
|---------|------|---------------|------------|
| 4 — Harald's Training | Aisen + Harald | Sanderson: hard magic (combat systems) | Show the spear drills as learnable, visible mechanics |
| 5 — Elara's Ambush | Aisen + Elara | Jemisin: marginalized perspectives | Cross-class bond; princess and tavern boy |
| 6 — Tarovin's Candle | Aisen + Tarovin | Sanderson: limitations > powers | Telekinesis introduced through constraint, not spectacle |
| 7 — Winter & Snow | Aisen + Caspian | Hobb: slow character change | Deepen the mystery; emotional bonding through shared cold |
| 8 — Kaelen's Grove | Aisen + Kaelen | Jemisin: ecological worldbuilding | Forest magic as ecosystem, not spell |
| 9 — Apprenticeship | Aisen + Tarovin | Sanderson: expand before adding new | Deepen telekinesis before introducing new systems |

### Block 3: Departure (Ch 10-12)
| Chapter | Pair | Core Technique | Key Lesson |
|---------|------|---------------|------------|
| 10 — Border Crisis | Aisen + Corvin | Martin: political machinations | War as systemic, not personal; introduction of larger conflict |
| 11 — First Synthesis | Aisen solo | Sanderson: expand what you have | Chain-spear proto; combining Harald's leverage with Tarovin's precision |
| 12 — Amara's Caravan | Aisen + Amara | Martin: intelligence as weapon | Information networks introduced; knowledge over power |

## Revision Checklist for Act I

- [ ] **Voice ages correctly**: Compare Ch 1 narration to Ch 12 — is the growth audible?
- [ ] **Mentors teach through action**: Ctrl+F for "explained" and "told" — replace with demonstration
- [ ] **Violence has aftermath**: Every violent scene has physical and emotional recovery
- [ ] **POV transitions use anchors**: No hard scene breaks within chapters; sensory/object bridges only
- [ ] **Magic builds incrementally**: Telekinesis Level 1 before Level 2; spear basics before synthesis
- [ ] **Pair-character dynamics distinct**: Each secondary character brings a different energy to their chapter
- [ ] **Foreshadowing planted**: Caspian's true nature, Covenant references, Harald's unnamed loss
- [ ] **Tavern is a full world**: Smells, sounds, regular patrons, daily rhythms established early
