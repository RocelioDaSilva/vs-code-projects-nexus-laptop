Sanderson's Three Laws of Magic:

1. First Law: An author's ability to solve conflict with magic is directly proportional to how well the reader understands the magic.

2. Second Law: Limitations > Powers. (Weaknesses and costs are more interesting than abilities.)

3. Third Law: Expand what you already have before adding something new.

Your story's magic is physics-based (Flux/Weave). You already have limits (range, mana, entropy debt). Now enforce them visibly.
