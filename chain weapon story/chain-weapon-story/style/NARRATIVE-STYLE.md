# NARRATIVE STYLE GUIDELINES

## Voice & Narrative Perspective

### Primary POV: Aisen (3rd person limited)
- **Tone**: Observant, pragmatic, slightly detached but caring
- **Internal monologue**: Logical; asks questions; doesn't inflate situations
- **Emotional expression**: Understated; shows through action/notation not explanation
- **Vocabulary**: Clear but not simplistic; complex ideas explained through practice

**Example voice**: "He recognized the pattern. The guard's overconfidence was rational—most fighters would fold at his reach. Aisen had folded many times. He shifted the spear three degrees and threw.

### Occasional Secondary POVs (Elara, Rin, Kaelen, Amara)
- **Limited use**: Maybe once per act to show network perspective
- **Still pragmatic**: Each character uses their own observation
- **Function**: Show that Aisen's impact ripples; they're not passive

**Constraint**: Keep these rare; reader is most comfortable with Aisen perspective

---

## Dialogue Style

### Aisen's Speech
- Asks more questions than states facts
- Doesn't explain philosophy directly; lets others figure out implications
- Practical; direct; rarely flowery
- **Example**: "What do you think happened when she found out?" (not: "I suspect she was upset")

### Harald's Speech (Mentor)
- Socratic; deflects questions back
- Philosophical but grounded in immediate experience
- Authority through calmness not declaration
- **Example**: "That was a good strike. Why do you think it worked?"

### Tarovin's Speech (Magical mentor)
- More explicit about philosophy than Harald
- Still questions but answers sometimes
- Carries weight of regret occasionally surfacing
- **Example**: "You're thinking about power. Better to think about understanding. Power without it is just... violence."

### Other characters
- Each has distinct voice reflecting background
- Elara: Confident, direct, strategic
- Rin: Analytical, guarded, precise
- Amara: Warm, storytelling-inclined, connecting-focused
- Kaelen: Wondering, connected-to-nature references, sometimes uncertain
- Lysandra: Grounded, cultural perspective, respectful

### Dialogue principles
- Minimal tags; reader can track speakers through voice/rhythm
- Subtext over exposition; characters don't explain
- Interruptions; people talk over each other in real conversation
- Silence used; not every exchange resolved in dialogue

## Sensory Description

### Touch (Primary for Aisen)
- Combat descriptions emphasize physical sensation
- Telekinesis described as physical feel not visualization
- Temperature, texture, impact described specifically
- **Example**: "The chain sang. Cold metal. His hands knowing where each segment was without watching."

### Sight (Secondary)
- Not overly detailed
- Focuses on what Aisen notices (patterns, threats, expressions)
- Skips flowery description; practical observation
- **Example**: "The governor's left eye twitched. Recognition that his lie didn't land."

### Sound (Utilized)
- Combat sounds: Physical impacts, breathing, movement
- Dialogue inherent; tone carries meaning
- Silence as sound; what's NOT said
- **Example**: "The room was quiet. That was the problem. Fear makes people quiet."

### Smell (Occasional)
- Pragmatic; indicates place/time/season
- **Example**: "The tavern smelled like harvest season—dried herbs, root vegetables, earlier fires."

### Taste (Rare)
- Only when significant
- Usually in aftermath scenes
- **Example**: "Blood in his mouth. Iron. That means he bit his lip. That means his hands were shaking."

### Proprioception (Emphasized)
- How Aisen's body knows where it is in space
- Critical for combat descriptions
- Shows mastery through body knowledge
- **Example**: "His center was three inches behind his feet. He could feel the space there."

## Metaphor & Imagery

### System metaphors (Frequent)
- Prejudice as: machine, river, architecture, gravity
- Networks as: webs, rhizomes, constellations, mycelium
- **Effect**: Helps reader understand Aisen's analytical perspective

### Natural metaphors (Moderate)
- Growth, seasons, weather, ecosystems
- Especially when Kaelen perspectives included
- **Effect**: Grounds story in ecological reality

### Combat metaphors (System-aware)
- Combat described through physics/leverage not poetic violence
- **Avoid**: "Like a dancer" (flowery); use "Lever arm × force = rotation"
- **Effect**: Emphasizes Aisen's analytical approach

### Avoid:
- Clichés (storm, darkness, light)
- Overwrought emotional metaphors
- Nature descriptions that feel romantic instead of real
- Magic descriptions that sound like video games

---

## Description Economy

### Rule: Description serves information or emotion, never decoration
- **Information**: "The tavern's eastern entrance faced the garrison. Harald would notice if customers changed."
- **Emotion**: "The room smelled like loss. Impossible, but it did."
- **Decoration (avoid)**: "Golden afternoon light slanted through windows, painting shadows that danced like lovers."

### Setting descriptions
- Function-focused: What does this space do? How does it enable what happens here?
- Sparse but specific: One or two notable details beat lavish description
- **Example good**: "The Academy hall was built to echo. Footsteps traveled. Privacy was architectural lie."
- **Example avoid**: "Soaring vaulted ceilings, carved with intricate patterns of..." (reader zones out)

### Character descriptions
- Rarely more than 2-3 sentences
- Focus on distinctive feature that matters to story
- Avoid: Full inventory (hair color, exact height, clothing brand)
- **Example**: "Lysandra moved like the mountain had taught her. Ground-centered. Patient."

---

## Dialogue vs. Narrative Balance

### Act I
- Heavy dialogue: Mentorship conversations shape voice
- Narrative explanation: Setting/worldbuilding requires context
- Balance: ~40% dialogue, 60% narrative

### Act II
- Moderate dialogue: Active scenes need less conversation
- Narrative acceleration: More action, less talked-about-action
- Balance: ~35% dialogue, 65% narrative

### Act III
- Less dialogue: War doesn't pause for conversation
- Narrative action: Events happen rather than discussed
- Balance: ~25% dialogue, 75% narrative
- **Exception**: Death scene + aftermath = more dialogue/internal monologue

## Internal Monologue Style

### Aisen's thinking
- **Not confessional**: Doesn't explain feelings
- **Pattern-recognizing**: Notices contradictions, connections
- **Self-aware**: Recognizes his own biases (pragmatism can be excuse)
- **Practical**: "What happens next?" more than "How do I feel?"

**Example internal monologue**:
"Three choices. Attack the merchant master (revenge, immediate consequences). Report to Harald (delayed, safer). Say nothing (cheapest option, consequences later). Each was rational. Each cost something different. He chose the one that kept him moving."

### Avoid
- Excessive self-analysis
- Emotional wallowing
- Explanations of things reader already understands
- Purple prose that doesn't match character voice

---

## Magical Description

### Telekinesis
- Described through physics, not mysticism
- **Not**: "Azure energy flowed from his fingertips"
- **Instead**: "He felt the weight distribution shift. Lever arm extended. Force × distance = torque applied."
- **Rare exception**: Non-scientific traditions use different language (Shukei embodied, Umaran relational)

### Magic system specificity
- Different traditions describe same phenomena differently
- Valdimerian: Formulaic (systematic description)
- Shukei: Embodied (sensation-based)
- Therryn: Mathematical (geometric description)
- Umaran: Relational (community-connected language)
- **Effect**: World feels plural; no single "correct" magic language

### Limit-breaking
- Described with cost explicitly
- **Not**: "He pushed all his power through" (glosses over consequences)
- **Instead**: "He broke his limit. His hands would shake for three days after. Not a day was worth it. He did it anyway."
- **Effect**: Validates Tarovin's warnings; magic has real costs

## Combat Description

### Philosophy over spectacle
- Emphasize positioning, leverage, reading opponent
- Minimize slow-motion blow-by-blow
- Let pacing reflect urgency
- **Example good**: "He shifted. Harald's feint passed. Three-beat rhythm in ripostes. Aisen recognized the pattern, stepped left, drove."
- **Example avoid**: "The tips of their spears clashed with a sound like bells, metal singing its ancient song of war..."

### Violence without glorification
- Physical impacts precise (what breaks, what aches)
- Consequences clear (characters remember injuries)
- Never celebrates violence
- Shows cost even in victories

### Different fighters described differently
- Aisen: Physics-based, analytical
- Harald: Experienced, economical
- Lysandra: Mountain-grounded, adaptive
- Caspian: Bestial, overwhelming, barely restrained
- **Effect**: Combat style reveals character

---

## Emotional Restraint

### Core principle: Show emotion through action/notation, rarely through statement
- **Not**: "He was devastated when Harald died."
- **Instead**: "He kept Harald's teaching notes for three weeks before opening them. When he did, his hands trembled. That's when he realized: The tremor wouldn't stop."

### Grief in particular
- Never melodramatic
- Often expressed as continued work (keeping going)
- Shown through small physical details
- Articulated through action not explanation

### Love (rare, understated)
- Never explicit; implied through choices/actions
- Aisen-Elara bond never stated as romantic; reader infers
- Shown through trust, protection, mutual understanding
- Not flowers and declarations

## Naming & Terminology

### Consistency with established names
- Characters keep same names (not name evolution)
- Places specific: Tavern (unnamed, functional); Academy (institutional); Border garrison (fortress-like)
- Magic traditions: Formulaic, Embodied, Mathematical, Relational (descriptive not poetic)

### Covenant terminology
- Never called "organization" or "society"
- Using proper name (Covenant, Seraphine's Order) rather than pronouns when possible
- Operatives have titles not names (The Fused Knight, Ice Vampire Queen, etc.)
- **Effect**: Dehumanizes enemies without caricaturing

### Nation names
- Valdimere, Shukei, Therryn, Umara (established; used consistently)
- Northern Kingdom (Elara's home; clarifies political alignment)
- Forest (Kaelen's origin; intentionally vague)
- Celestial empire (Rin's origin; mysterious)
- **Effect**: Some places clear, some mysterious; matches knowledge distribution

---

## POV Consistency & Limitation

### What Aisen doesn't know, reader doesn't know
- Covenant deliberations: Not shown (he learns through effects)
- Other character internal monologue: Not shown (reader knows only through dialogue/action)
- Historical events: Learned through mention not exposition
- **Effect**: Reader experiences world as Aisen does; conspiracy as gradually revealed

### Appropriate exceptions
- Act II rare secondary POV (Rin, Elara, Kaelen) breaks this; signals importance
- Act III death scene: Time of Aisen's death, reality narrows to immediate sensory
- Aftermath: Shift to Elara POV (now she's the acting one)

## Summary: Style as Content

**Style principle**: The how matches the what. Because this is story about understanding systems and pragmatic acceptance, the narrative style is:
- Clear, not flowery (matches Aisen's thinking)
- Analytical, not emotional (shows his perspective)
- Grounded, not fantastical (respects weight of reality)
- Restrained, not heightened (refuses to dramatize)
- Distributed, not singular (network perspective embedded)

Reader should experience story as Aisen does: With attention to systems, pragmatic acceptance of complexity, and recognition that understanding doesn't equal power.
