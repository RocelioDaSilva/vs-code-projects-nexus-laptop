## Angola Completions Market Context

Every one of Angola's 600+ deepwater producing wells required a completion costing $10–40M per well. The completion design determines whether a $80M well produces 5,000 bbl/d or 15,000 bbl/d for the next 20 years. In Angola's sand-prone turbidite reservoirs, sand control is the defining completions challenge.

### Angola-Specific Completions Challenges

- **Sand control in turbidites:** Angola's Oligocene/Miocene turbidite sands are poorly consolidated — sand production destroys ESPs; gravel pack and frac-pack are the standard solution
- **Intelligent completions adoption:** Kaombo is Angola's showcase for ICVs (Inflow Control Valves) + fiber optic DTS/DAS — enabling real-time reservoir management from the [FPSO](../glossary.md)
- **Deepwater workover cost:** Every intervention requires a drillship ($300–500K/day). A simple ESP replacement can cost $15–30M. Completions must be designed to minimize intervention frequency.
- **Scale management:** Barium sulfate (BaSO₄) scale is prevalent in Block 17 and Block 15 — requires downhole scale squeeze treatments on a regular schedule
- **P&A (Plug & Abandonment):** As Angola's oldest fields mature (Block 0 started in the 1960s), well P&A becomes a major market. Completions engineers with P&A experience are in growing demand.
- **HPHT completions:** Block 20 (pre-salt) presents high-temperature completion design challenges — metallurgy, seal reliability, elastomer selection

## 1. What Does a Completions Engineer Actually Do Day-to-Day?

A completions engineer designs and installs the hardware that connects the reservoir to surface — everything that goes into the well after the drilling crew reaches total depth. This includes production tubing, packers, sand control screens, inflow control devices, safety valves, perforating guns, and [artificial lift](../skills/09-artificial-lift.md) equipment.

- **Morning**: Review completed operations from the night shift. Check whether the gravel pack/frac pack on Well X achieved target skin factor. Review packer pressure test results.
- **Mid-day**: Design the completion string for the next well — select tubing size, packer type, SCSSV (subsurface safety valve) depth, sand control method. Run thermal stress analysis.
- **Afternoon**: Coordinate with service companies ([SLB](../../data/company-directory/slb.md), [Halliburton](../../data/company-directory/halliburton.md), [Baker Hughes](../../data/company-directory/bakerhughes.md)) on equipment deliverables. Review perforating design (charge type, shot density, phasing).
- **Evening**: Update well intervention candidate list. Prepare cost estimates for a workover on Well Y (ESP replacement + scale squeeze).

**On a rig (operations completions engineer):** Supervise completions installation on the rig floor. Manage the running of production tubing, packers, screens. Monitor pressures during packer setting, perform wellbore cleanup. 12-hour shifts, 28/28 rotation.

**In the office (design completions engineer):** Design completions hardware selection, run FEA (finite element analysis) on safety-critical equipment, write completions basis of design, evaluate vendor bids, and manage the completions budget.

## 5. Common Interview Questions

### Technical
1. Walk me through a deepwater sand control completion design (gravel pack vs. standalone screen vs. expandable screen).
2. What factors determine your selection of tubing size for a completion?
3. Explain the difference between openhole and cased-hole gravel packs. When would you use each?
4. How do you design a perforating program? What parameters do you consider (charge type, phasing, shot density, penetration)?
5. Describe the function and setting mechanism of a hydraulic production packer.
6. What is an intelligent completion and what are its components (SCSSV, DHSV, ICV, gauges)?
7. Explain the concept of skin and how completions design can minimize it.
8. What are the failure modes for an ESP installed as part of a completion?
9. Walk through a well intervention using coiled tubing — when would you choose CT vs. wireline vs. snubbing?
10. What is annular pressure build-up (APB) and how do you mitigate it in deepwater completions?

### Behavioral
11. Describe a completions operation that didn't go as planned. How did you troubleshoot?
12. How do you manage competing demands between drilling schedule pressure and completions quality?
13. Tell me about a time you improved completion run-life or reduced non-productive time.
14. How do you evaluate vendor equipment reliability data?

## 6. Portfolio Projects

1. **Completion Design Report** — Full completion design for a deepwater well: tubing, packer, SCSSV, sand control, perforating, [artificial lift](../skills/09-artificial-lift.md). Include a completion schematic drawing.
2. **Sand Control Selection Study** — Compare gravel pack, standalone screen, expandable screen, and frac pack for a given reservoir. Decision matrix with cost, risk, productivity.
3. **Perforating Design Optimization** — Calculate optimum shot density and phasing for given formation properties. Show effect on skin and productivity.
4. **Well Intervention NPV Calculator** — Build a Python/Excel tool that ranks intervention candidates by incremental production × oil price vs. intervention cost.
5. **Intelligent Completion Data Analysis** — Analyze downhole gauge data (pressure, temperature) to diagnose zonal contribution. Use public datasets if available.
6. **Completion Failure Analysis** — Take a public case study of a completion failure and perform root cause analysis.

## 12. 5-Year and 10-Year Outlook

### 5-Year (2026–2031)
- **Strong demand.** Every new well needs a completion. Angola, Guyana, Brazil, Namibia have major completion campaigns.
- **Intelligent completions growth** — ICVs, in-well sensing, autonomous zonal control becoming standard for new deepwater wells.
- **Multistage completion technology** — Moving from shale into conventional wells.
- **Digital completions** — Real-time downhole data driving adaptive completion strategies.

### 10-Year (2026–2036)
- **P&A boom** — Thousands of wells need decommissioning (North Sea, GOM). Completions engineers are essential for safe abandonment.
- **Geothermal completions** — High-temperature, corrosive environments. Challenging completion design.
- **CCS completions** — CO₂ injection well completions require specialized materials (corrosion-resistant alloys, CO₂-resistant seals).
- **Self-healing completions** — Smart materials that autonomously repair downhole failures was emerging research.

## 16. Critical Soft Skills

- **Attention to detail** — A wrong packer setting depth or incorrect seal material specification can ruin a $20M+ completion.
- **Equipment knowledge** — You must know the physical hardware: how a packer sets, how a safety valve functions, how a screen filters.
- **Cross-functional coordination** — Interface with drilling (wellbore readiness), production (lift method), reservoir (inflow requirements), subsea (tree compatibility).
- **Vendor management** — You negotiate with [SLB](../../data/company-directory/slb.md), [Baker Hughes](../../data/company-directory/bakerhughes.md), Halliburton on equipment specs, delivery schedules, and costs.
- **Problem-solving under pressure** — Rig time costs $500K–$1M/day. When equipment fails downhole, you troubleshoot fast.
- **Technical writing** — Completions basis of design, equipment specifications, equipment failure reports.

## 22. Common Mistakes

1. **Not understanding the reservoir.** Completions connect reservoir to surface. If you don't understand the formation, you design the wrong completion.
2. **Over-engineering completions.** Every added component is a potential failure point. Simpler is often better.
3. **Ignoring lessons learned databases.** Previous completion failures in similar reservoirs should dictate your design.
4. **Not learning multiple sand control methods.** Gravel pack, frac pack, screens — know them all, not just one.
5. **Staying in the field too long without design experience.** Running tools is important, but designing completions is where the career ceiling is.
6. **Not tracking completion performance data.** Run-life, skin, failure modes — this data drives your next design.

## 25. Emerging Technologies

- **Intelligent completions (ICVs + fiber optic DTS/DAS)** — Real-time zonal control and monitoring. [TotalEnergies](../../data/company-directory/totalenergies.md), Shell, Equinor deploying widely.
- **Dissolvable materials** — Frac plugs, ball seats that dissolve after use. Eliminates drill-out operations.
- **Autonomous inflow control** — AICDs that respond to fluid type (oil vs. water vs. gas) without surface control.
- **3D-printed downhole equipment** — Custom connectors, centralizers, flow control devices.
- **Digital twin completions** — Real-time models of completion performance linked to production data.
- **Expandable tubulars** — Expandable screens, liners that deform in-place. Reduce well cost.
- **CO₂-resistant completion materials** — For CCS wells. New alloys and seals for CO₂ environments.
