Glossary � Terminology for Angola offshore petroleum engineering

Quick reference for technical, organizational, and industry terms you'll encounter in Angola offshore.

---

## Offshore Safety & Certifications

**BOSIET / HUET**
Basic Offshore Safety Induction and Emergency Training. HUET = Helicopter Underwater Escape Training. A mandatory 5�7 day course teaching offshore survival, lifeboat operations, emergency procedures, and how to escape a submerged helicopter. Must be current before boarding any offshore installation. Valid 5 years.

**IWCF [Well Control](skills/01-well-control.md)**
International [Well Control](skills/01-well-control.md) Forum certification. Levels 1 (Fundamentals), 2 (Supervisor), 3 (Instructor). Level 1 is mandatory for drilling and completions engineers. Covers pressure control, kick response, BOP operations, well kill procedures. Valid 3 years; renewal via 1-day refresher.

**H2S Awareness**
Hydrogen Sulfide awareness and safety training. Teaches recognition, symptoms, escape procedures for H2S exposure (rare in Angola but present in some pre-salt zones). 1-day course; valid 2�3 years.

**Offshore Medical**
A comprehensive medical examination required to work offshore. Covers cardiovascular, respiratory, hearing, vision, psychological fitness. Must be current (usually 1�2 years). Issued by IADC-approved clinics.

**OIM / OIC**
Offshore Installation Manager (or Offshore Installation Contractor). The most senior person on a rig/FPSO responsible for all operations, safety, and personnel.

**Company Man / Well Site Leader**
The operator's on-site representative managing the well execution for the oil company. Your direct interface with the decision-maker.

**HAZOP (Hazard and Operability Study)**
A structured risk analysis technique used to identify potential hazards in a process or operation. A multi-disciplinary team systematically examines each process node using guide words (No, More, Less, Reverse, etc.) to find deviations from design intent. Required for all major modifications to FPSO topsides and subsea systems. HSE engineers often facilitate HAZOP workshops.

## Drilling Operations

**ROP (Rate of Penetration)**
Speed at which the drill bit advances through rock. Measured in meters per hour (m/hr). Higher ROP = faster drilling = lower costs, but requires careful pressure management.

**WOB (Weight on Bit)**
The downward force applied to the drill bit via the drill string. Measured in thousands of pounds (klbs). Too little and the bit won't cut; too much and you damage the bit or wellbore.

**Torque**
Rotational force applied to the drill string as it turns. Indicates friction. High torque can signal a stuck pipe, hole sloughing, or bit dulling. Measured in thousand foot-pounds (kft-lbs).

**RIG (Drilling Rig)**
The vessel or platform used to drill wells. Examples in Angola: **Drillship** (like *Valaris DS-7* or *[Sonangol](../data/company-directory/sonangol.md) Quenguela*), **Semisubmersible** (*Odfjell Drilling rigs*), **Jack-up** (rarely used in deepwater Angola).

**Drillship**
A mobile offshore drilling unit shaped like a ship, designed for deepwater operations. Dynamically positioned (DP) to stay in place without anchors. Used in Angola's pre-salt deepwater.

**DP (Dynamic Positioning)**
A computer-controlled system that automatically maintains a vessel's position using thrusters and propellers. Critical for drillships and construction vessels in deepwater Angola where anchoring is impossible. DP classes: **DP-1** (single system), **DP-2** (redundant — required for most drilling), **DP-3** (triple redundancy — for critical operations).

**SIMOPS (Simultaneous Operations)**
Multiple operations happening at the same time on one installation — e.g., drilling while producing, crane operations during well testing. Requires rigorous risk assessment and coordination. Common on Angola FPSOs where production continues during workover campaigns.

**BOP (Blowout Preventer)**
A stack of specialized valves at the wellhead controlling flow from the well. Prevents uncontrolled release of hydrocarbons (a "blowout"). Mandatory for all wells.

**Kick**
An uncontrolled influx of hydrocarbons or formation fluids into the wellbore. Detected by a rise in pit volume or a surge in well pressure. A kick is the first sign of a potential blowout.

**[well control](skills/01-well-control.md)**
The process of detecting and safely managing a kick. Involves closing the BOP, establishing circulation through a choke line, and increasing mud weight to kill the well.

**Mud / Drilling Fluid**
Circulated through the wellbore to cool the bit, carry cuttings to surface, and maintain pressure control. Can be **WBM** (Water-Based Mud) or **OBM** (Oil-Based Mud; common in Angola pre-salt for stability).

**ECD (Equivalent Circulating Density)**
The effective pressure exerted by circulating mud. High ECD can cause losses or stuck pipe. Calculated in pounds per gallon (ppg) or kilograms per cubic meter (kg/m�).

**MWD / LWD**
**M**easurement **W**hile **D**rilling / **L**ogging **W**hile **D**rilling. Sensors in the drill string sending real-time data: gamma ray, resistivity, inclination, temperature. Guides drilling and identifies reservoir boundaries.

**Casing**
Large-diameter steel pipes set at intervals in the wellbore to prevent collapse and isolate formations. **Conductor** (shallowest), **Surface**, **Intermediate**, **Production** (deepest).

**Completion**
The process of preparing a well for production. Includes perforating the reservoir, installing sand control, tubing, packers, and safety valves. **Upper completion** = surface equipment; **Lower completion** = downhole.

**Well Intervention / Workover**
Returning to a producing well to perform maintenance, stimulation, repairs, or zone changes. Can be light (wireline), medium (coiled tubing), or heavy (rig-based).

## Production & FPSO Operations

**FPSO (Floating Production Storage Offloading)**
A vessel that receives crude oil from subsea wells, separates oil/gas/water, compresses gas, and stores/exports oil. Angola's main production infrastructure for deepwater fields (e.g., *Girassol, Dalia, Pazflor, Agogo, Kaombo Norte/Sul*).

**Production Engineer**
Optimizes daily production rates, diagnoses well performance issues, designs [artificial lift](skills/09-artificial-lift.md), manages flow assurance. Often office-based in Luanda with regular offshore trips.

**Process Engineer**
Manages FPSO topsides equipment: separators, compressors, heaters, water treatment. Ensures efficient processing and export. Based onboard or onshore.

**Well Testing / DST (Drill Stem Test)**
A procedure to evaluate reservoir potential during drilling. Produces oil/gas to surface, measures rates and pressures, analyzes fluid samples. Project-based offshore deployment (2�4 weeks per test).

**Production Technologist**
Analyzes well and processing data, optimizes facilities design, troubleshoots process issues. Often combined with Production Engineer role.

**[Artificial Lift](skills/09-artificial-lift.md)**
Systems used when a reservoir has insufficient natural pressure to flow to the surface. Examples: **Gas Lift** (inject gas), **ESP** (Electrical Submersible Pump), **PCP** (Progressive Cavity Pump). Designed and optimized by ALS ([Artificial Lift](skills/09-artificial-lift.md) Specialist).

**[Flow Assurance](skills/10-flow-assurance.md)**
Prevents and mitigates deposition of hydrates, wax, asphaltene, and solids in subsea flowlines. Uses thermal modeling (OLGA), chemical injection, pipeline design optimization.

**Nodal Analysis**
Mathematical approach to optimizing well and surface equipment systems. Identifies production bottlenecks and calculates productivity gains. Software: PROSPER, GAP.

## Subsea & SURF

**Subsea / SURF (Subsea Umbilicals Risers Flowlines)**
Infrastructure on the seafloor connecting wells to platforms/FPSOs. Includes: **subsea trees** (wellheads), **manifolds** (connection hubs), **flowlines** (pipes), **umbilicals** (steel tubes carrying hydraulics/fiber optics).

**Subsea Tree**
A complex assembly of valves and controls on the seafloor at each well. Controls flow, isolation, and safety. Can weigh 50+ tons in deepwater Angola.

**Subsea Manifold**
Joins multiple flowlines and control systems. Routes hydrocarbons from multiple wells toward the FPSO.

**Umbilical**
Steel-reinforced bundle containing hydraulic lines, fiber-optic cables, and power lines controlling subsea equipment from the platform. Critical for deepwater field control.

**T&I / Hook-up & Commissioning**
**T**ransport, **I**nstallation, Hook-up & Commissioning. The phase where subsea installations are deployed and brought into service. Involves heavy-lift vessels, DP coordinators, and offline engineering.

**Riser / Flowline**
**Riser** = pipe running from seafloor to platform, supporting weight and resisting fatigue. **Flowline** = horizontal or low-angle pipe on seafloor carrying hydrocarbons between wells and manifolds.

## Geology & Reservoir

**Pre-salt / Post-salt**
**Pre-salt** = strata below the salt layer (Angola's primary target; deepwater, high pressure, high temperature). **Post-salt** = above the salt layer (shallower, lower pressure). Angola pre-salt fields are technically challenging but highly productive (Girassol, Dalia, Block 17/32).

**Sand / Shale / Granite / Volcanic Ash**
Common rock formations encountered while drilling. **Sand** = permeable, often productive. **Shale** = impermeable, often seals. **Granite** = igneous basement, very hard. **Volcanic Ash** = thin, abrasive strata (pre-salt Angola).

**Reservoir**
Porous, permeable rock formation containing oil and/or gas. Must be sealed by overlying shale or salt to trap hydrocarbons.

**Seal / Trap**
**Seal** = impermeable rock preventing hydrocarbon escape. **Trap** = structural or stratigraphic configuration holding hydrocarbons (dome, fault block, salt diapir).

**Pore Pressure / Overburden Pressure**
**Pore pressure** = pressure of fluids inside rock pores (indicates pit depth, compaction). **Overburden** = weight of overlying rock. Imbalance causes instability (kicks or lost circulation).

**Pressure Regime / Gradient**
The subsurface pressure environment expressed as ppg (pounds per gallon mud weight equivalent). **Normal** = ~0.465 ppg/100ft. **Overpressured** (Angola pre-salt) = >0.465 ppg/100ft; requires heavier mud and careful pressure management. **Underpressured** = rare.

**Petrophysics / [Formation Evaluation](skills/04-formation-evaluation.md)**
Interpretation of wireline logs (gamma ray, resistivity, porosity, density) to identify reservoir properties: porosity, saturation, permeability, net pay. The engineer who does this is a **Petrophysicist** or **Well Evaluation Engineer**.

**P10 / P50 / P90 (Probabilistic Reserves)**
Statistical estimates of recoverable hydrocarbons. **P90** = 90% probability of at least this amount (conservative/proved). **P50** = 50% probability (most likely/probable). **P10** = 10% probability (optimistic/possible). Used in reserves reporting and investment decisions. Angola operators use these extensively for field development plans.

**PRMS (Petroleum Resources Management System)**
The SPE/WPC/AAPG/SPEE industry standard for classifying and reporting petroleum reserves and resources. Defines categories: Proved (1P), Proved + Probable (2P), Proved + Probable + Possible (3P), plus Contingent and Prospective Resources. All Angola block operators report reserves under PRMS.

**Greenfield / Brownfield**
**Greenfield** = a new development on a previously undeveloped area (new wells, new infrastructure from scratch). **Brownfield** = modifications, expansions, or optimization of an existing producing field. Angola has both: greenfield exploration in Kwanza and Namibe basins, brownfield optimization on mature Block 0 and Block 17 assets.

## Project & Development

**AFE (Authorization for Expenditure)**
Formal approval document for well/project spending. Often signed by multiple parties (operator, partners, governments). Can require weeks or months to obtain.

**FEED / FEL**
**F**ront-**E**nd **E**ngineering **D**esign (Phase 2 of engineering). Also referred to as FEL2 (Front-End Loading). Produces 30�50% design basis for projects.

**EPC**
**E**ngineering, **P**rocurement, **C**onstruction. A major contract model where a service company designs, buys equipment, and builds/installs the facility. Examples in Angola: [Saipem](../data/company-directory/saipem.md), [TechnipFMC](../data/company-directory/technipfmc.md), Subsea 7.

**CAPEX / OPEX**
**CAPEX** = Capital expenditure (drilling, infrastructure, one-time costs). **OPEX** = Operating expenditure (routine maintenance, labor, consumables).

**NPV (Net Present Value)**
The present value of future cash flows minus the initial investment, discounted at a chosen rate. The primary metric for petroleum project economics. A positive NPV means a project creates value. Used by Angola operators to rank development options and sanction field investments.

**IRR (Internal Rate of Return)**
The discount rate at which a project's NPV equals zero. Represents the project's expected annualized return. Angola deepwater projects typically require IRR > 15–20% to gain sanction given the high CAPEX and political risk premium.

**TIC (Total Installed Cost)**
Total cost to build and install a subsea system or field infrastructure.

**Tie-in / Tie-back**
Connection of a new well or field to existing infrastructure (flowlines, platforms). Reduces cost vs. standalone facilities.

## Organization & Contracting

**Operator**
The company holding the oil block license and responsible for field development and operations. Examples: [TotalEnergies](../data/company-directory/totalenergies.md), Chevron, ExxonMobil.

**Service Company / Contractor**
A company hired by the operator to perform specific work (drilling, completions, subsea installation, FPSO operations, etc.).

**Subcontractor**
A company hired by a service company or operator to perform specialized sub-scopes (fabrication, inspection, catering, waste management).

**Joint Venture / JV**
Two or more companies partnering on a contract or asset. Examples: **[Sonadrill](../data/company-directory/sonangol.md)** = Sonangol + Seadrill (drilling); **[Azule Energy](angola/angolan-offshore-companies.md)** = BP + Eni (operator).

**Local Content / Conte�do Local**
Angola's legal requirement (Presidential Decree 271/20) mandating that a percentage of workforce, procurement, and services come from Angolan companies or nationals. Typically 60�80% by phase.

**ANPG**
**Ag�ncia Nacional do Petr�leo, G�s e Biocombust�veis** � Angola's national oil & gas regulator. Oversees licensing, local content compliance, safety, environmental regulations. Publishes the list of approved service categories and supplier registrations.

**Oferta Permanente**
Permanent offer list published by ANPG showing supplier companies, their registrations, and service categories. Used by operators to identify vetted vendors.

## Roles & Job Titles

**Company Man / Well Site Leader**
Operator's on-site manager responsible for well execution, safety, and decisions.

**Toolpusher / Rig Manager**
Most senior drilling person on the rig, reporting to the OIM. Responsible for drilling operations.

**Drilling Supervisor**
Mid-level engineer overseeing drilling operations, mud systems, safety. Works with the Toolpusher.

**Field Engineer Trainee / Junior Field Engineer**
Entry-level position for graduates. Rotational. Learns all aspects of drilling or production operations.

**Senior Field Engineer / Field Engineer II**
Experienced field engineer (2�5 years) with technical authority on rig/FPSO.

**Company Man / Well Site Supervisor**
Senior engineer representing the operator on the rig during well drilling or completion.

**Project Engineer / Drilling Engineer (office-based)**
Plans wells, designs casings, mud programs, completion strings. Office-based in Luanda or regional hub.

**Superintendent / Manager**
Supervises a team of field engineers or manages a specific functional area (drilling, production, subsea operations).

## Shorthand & Slang (Angola Offshore)

**Mobilization / Demob**
Going offshore (mobilization) or leaving offshore (demobilization).

**Rotation / Hitch**
A period of work offshore. Angola standard is **28/28** (28 days offshore, 28 days onshore).

**Rest Day**
One day per week offshore where you don't work. You're still on the installation but have limited duties.

**Mudline**
The seafloor. Referenced in [subsea systems](skills/11-subsea-systems.md) (e.g., "mudline BOP" = blowout preventer at the seafloor).

**Topsides**
The above-water structures on an FPSO or platform (processing equipment, accommodations).

**Subsea**
All infrastructure below water. Opposite of "topsides."

**Shale Shaker**
Equipment separating drilling cuttings from mud before recirculation. Used to monitor formation changes.

**Flare**
Burning off excess gas from an FPSO or platform (common during commissioning or emergency venting).

**Stuck Pipe / Pack-off**
Drill string becomes lodged in the hole due to high friction, wellbore collapse, or formation swelling. Major problem requiring careful management to free the pipe.

**Trip Out / Pull Out**
Removing the entire drill string from the hole (usually to change the drill bit or address problems). Time-consuming and expensive.

**Spud**
To begin drilling a new well. "Spud date" = planned drilling start.

**TD (Total Depth)**
The deepest point drilled in a well. Target TD for Angola pre-salt wells often 3,500�4,500 meters.

**Kick / Influx**
An uncontrolled entry of formation fluids (oil, gas, water) into the wellbore. Detected by pit volume increase or pressure surge. Requires immediate [well control](skills/01-well-control.md) response.

**Blowout**
Uncontrolled release of hydrocarbons from the wellbore. The worst possible scenario offshore. All training aims to prevent this.

### ?? Your Path to Offshore Rotation

Your most direct path to a **full offshore rotation** is through **Drilling, Completion & Well Intervention** (Category 1 above). As a Field Engineer with a major service company like [SLB](../data/company-directory/slb.md) or [Halliburton](../data/company-directory/halliburton.md), you can expect a consistent 28/28 rotation from the start of your career.

For a path into **Production and Research**, the route is slightly more office-based initially but will lead to **Reservoir Services** or **Engineering & Project Management**. A position with an operator like [TotalEnergies](../data/company-directory/totalenergies.md) or Azule Energy in their subsurface or projects team will provide the long-term career flexibility you're looking for, with opportunities for offshore trips as needed.
