## What You Learned

Crude oil characterization (API gravity, sulfur, TAN), atmospheric and vacuum distillation, thermal cracking, catalytic cracking (FCC), hydrocracking, reforming, alkylation, isomerization, desulfurization (HDS), and product blending. You learned how crude oil is transformed into usable products.

## Where This Subject Is Used in Industry

- **Refinery Process Engineering:** Operating and optimizing distillation columns, reactors, heat exchangers, and separation units. Process engineers monitor yields, energy consumption, product quality, and throughput.
- **Crude Oil Valuation:** Different crude oils have different values depending on their refinery yield (light vs. heavy products). Angola's various crudes (Girassol, Dalia, Cabinda, Nemba) each have specific refinery yields.
- **Product Quality:** Meeting fuel specifications (gasoline octane, diesel cetane, sulfur limits) requires understanding the refining process chain.
- **LPG, Naphtha, and Petrochemical Feedstocks:** Refineries produce feedstocks for the petrochemical industry â€” understanding this value chain expands career options.
