## Where This Subject Is Used in Industry

- **Drilling Operations â€” Daily Use:** Drilling fluid (mud) is the lifeblood of drilling operations. The mud engineer tests properties every 6 hours (density, viscosity, pH, chlorides, fluid loss) and adjusts the formulation. The driller adjusts pump rate based on mud properties. Understanding [drilling fluids](../../../docs/skills/03-drilling-fluids.md) is essential for the entire drilling team.
- **[Well Control](../../../docs/skills/01-well-control.md):** If formation pressure exceeds mud hydrostatic pressure, a kick (influx) occurs. The mud weight is the primary [well control](../../../docs/skills/01-well-control.md) barrier. Weighting up the mud to kill a kick is the first response â€” using barite and the kill sheet calculations.
- **Wellbore Stability:** The mud system interacts with shales (clay swelling, osmotic effects in OBM vs. WBM) and affects borehole stability. OBM was developed specifically because shale instability with WBM was causing massive hole problems.
- **Formation Damage:** The wrong completion fluid can damage the near-wellbore formation (fines migration, clay swelling, emulsion blocking), reducing productivity by 50% or more. Properly designed completion brines minimize this.

## Angola-Specific Context

Angola deepwater wells predominantly use Synthetic-Based Mud ([SBM](../../../data/company-directory/sbmoffshore.md)) â€” a type of OBM using synthetic base fluid instead of diesel. SBM provides excellent wellbore stability in reactive shales and has lower environmental impact than traditional OBM. SLB (M-I SWACO) and [Halliburton](../../../data/company-directory/halliburton.md) (Baroid) are the main mud service providers in Angola. Mud engineering is an entry-level career path at both companies.
