## What You Learned

[Artificial lift](../../../docs/skills/09-artificial-lift.md) methods: Electrical Submersible Pumps (ESP), gas lift, sucker rod pumps, progressive cavity pumps (PCP), jet pumps, and plunger lift. Design methodology, selection criteria, and performance optimization for each method.

## Where This Subject Is Used in Industry

- **ESP Design and Operation:** ESPs are the dominant [artificial lift](../../../docs/skills/09-artificial-lift.md) method in Angola deepwater. Design involves: selecting pump stages (flow rate and head), motor power, cable selection, gas separator, and VSD settings. Operating involves: monitoring motor temperature, vibration, current, pump intake pressure, and adjusting speed to optimize production.
- **Gas Lift Design:** Gas lift injects gas into the tubing to reduce the fluid density (hydrostatic head), allowing the reservoir to flow. Gas lift valve sizing, injection depth selection, and gas allocation optimization are design tasks. Some Angola wells use gas lift â€” especially high GOR wells.
- **[Artificial Lift](../../../docs/skills/09-artificial-lift.md) Selection:** Which method for which well? ESPs for high-rate wells (2,000â€“30,000 bbl/d), gas lift for wells with gas available and moderate rates, rod pumps for onshore low-rate wells. Angola deepwater = ESPs in 90% of cases.
- **ESP Failure Analysis:** When an ESP fails, root cause analysis determines: was it electrical (cable, motor), mechanical (pump wear, shaft failure), or operational (gas slugging, scale, sand)?

## Angola-Specific Context

Angola's deepwater wells initially flow naturally due to high reservoir pressure. As reservoir pressure declines (after years of production), the wells require [artificial lift](../../../docs/skills/09-artificial-lift.md) â€” almost always ESPs.
