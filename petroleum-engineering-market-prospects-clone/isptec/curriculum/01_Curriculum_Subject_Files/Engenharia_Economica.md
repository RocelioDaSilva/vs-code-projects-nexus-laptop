## What You Learned

Time value of money, Net Present Value (NPV), Internal Rate of Return (IRR), payback period, depreciation, inflation, sensitivity analysis, and economic evaluation of engineering projects.

## Where This Subject Is Used in Industry

- **Well Economics:** Before drilling a $60M deepwater well, the operator calculates: expected production profile, oil price assumptions, operating costs, and taxes â†’ generates a cash flow model â†’ calculates NPV and IRR. If NPV < 0, the well is not drilled. This decision uses engineering economics directly.
- **Field Development Planning (FDP):** The choice between: (a) 10 wells + 1 [FPSO](../../../docs/glossary.md) vs. (b) 15 wells + 1 larger [FPSO](../../../docs/glossary.md) is an economic optimization. Each option has different capital cost, production profile, operating cost, and NPV. Engineering economics provides the framework.
- **Project Sanctioning:** Oil companies sanction (approve for investment) major projects based on economic metrics: NPV at a given discount rate (typically 10â€“15%), IRR, and payback period. Angola's Block 32 Kaombo project ($16B investment) was sanctioned based on economic analysis.
- **Lease or Buy Decisions:** Should a company lease a drilling rig for $300K/day or buy a rig for $600M? This is a classic engineering economics problem.
- **Production Optimization:** "Should we spend $15M on a well workover to increase production by 2,000 bbl/day?" requires calculating the incremental NPV of the additional production vs. the workover cost.
