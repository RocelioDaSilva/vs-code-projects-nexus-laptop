# Metodologia Cientifica

**Ano:** 2o | **Semestre:** 1o | **Sector:** All (Upstream, Midstream, Downstream)

---

## What You Learned

Scientific method, hypothesis formulation, literature review, research design, data collection and analysis methods, citation standards, academic writing structure (abstract, introduction, methodology, results, conclusion), and critical evaluation of sources.

## Where This Subject Is Used in Industry

- **Problem Solving:** When an ESP fails on an Angola well after only 6 months (expected life: 3 years), the production engineer follows a systematic investigation process identical to the scientific method: observe the problem, formulate hypotheses (scale? vibration? electrical failure?), collect data (pull the ESP, inspect, test), analyze, and conclude.
- **Root Cause Analysis:** Incident investigation in offshore operations uses structured, evidence-based analysis - the same logical framework you learned in methodology.
- **Literature Review / Benchmarking:** Before designing a well, engineers review offset well data, SPE papers, and internal company knowledge bases. This is a literature review applied to engineering.
- **Technical Paper Writing:** If you contribute to a technical paper or conference presentation (SPE, AAPG), you need to know how to structure a paper, cite sources, and present data with scientific rigor.
- **Project Proposals:** Writing a proposal for a new well, a production optimization project, or a technology pilot requires: problem statement, literature review (what has been tried before), proposed methodology, expected results, and economic justification.

## Angola-Specific Context

ISPTEC requires Projecto I and Projecto II - thesis projects that follow the scientific method. These projects prepare you for the kind of structured problem-solving expected in industry. Many Angola operators ([TotalEnergies](../../../data/company-directory/totalenergies.md), Eni) encourage engineers to publish SPE papers from their work - having scientific methodology skills makes you a candidate for these opportunities.

*"If I were tasked with improving the run-life of ESPs across 15 wells on an Angola [FPSO](../../../docs/glossary.md), how would I structure an investigation using the scientific method - what data would I collect, what hypotheses would I test, and how would I present my findings?"*
