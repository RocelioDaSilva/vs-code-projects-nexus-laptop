## Where This Subject Is Used in Industry

- **Seismic Exploration:** This is the single most important application. Seismic surveys send acoustic waves (pressure waves â€” longitudinal) into the earth. These waves reflect off geological boundaries (impedance contrasts) and are recorded at the surface. Processing these reflections creates an image of the subsurface â€” the primary tool for finding oil.
- **Acoustic Logging:** Sonic (acoustic) wireline and [LWD](../../../docs/glossary.md) tools measure the velocity of sound waves through rock. Compressional wave velocity (Vp) and shear wave velocity (Vs) are used to calculate porosity, rock mechanical properties, and detect gas.
- **Cement Bond Logging:** CBL/VDL tools send acoustic waves through casing and cement. The attenuation of the signal indicates the quality of the cement bond â€” essential for [well integrity](../../../docs/skills/15-well-integrity.md).
- **Fiber Optic Sensing (DTS/DAS):** Distributed Temperature Sensing and Distributed Acoustic Sensing use light waves in fiber optic cables installed in wells. These provide continuous real-time temperature and acoustic measurements along the entire well â€” a growing technology in Angola.
- **Ultrasonic Testing:** Non-destructive testing ([NDT](../../../docs/glossary.md)) of welds, pipes, and equipment uses ultrasonic waves â€” same principles as seismic but at much smaller scale.
