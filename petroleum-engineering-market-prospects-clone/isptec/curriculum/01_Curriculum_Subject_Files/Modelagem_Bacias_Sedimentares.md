## What You Learned

Quantitative basin modeling: burial history reconstruction, thermal history (heat flow, vitrinite reflectance calibration), hydrocarbon generation and expulsion modeling, migration pathways, and the use of basin modeling software (PetroMod or similar).

## Where This Subject Is Used in Industry

- **Source Rock Maturation:** The key output of basin modeling is: "When did the source rock enter the oil window? How much oil was generated? When was it expelled?" This determines whether the petroleum system is charged.
- **Hydrocarbon Migration:** Basin models simulate the migration of oil and gas from source to trap â€” through carrier beds, along faults, or through the salt. This predicts which structural and stratigraphic traps are filled.
- **Risk Assessment:** Exploration wells cost $50â€“150M in Angola deepwater. Basin modeling quantifies the probability that a prospect is charged with hydrocarbons â€” directly affecting the drilling decision.
- **Temperature Prediction:** Basin models predict temperature at depth â€” critical for well design (tool ratings, mud system, cement design) and especially for predicting HP/HT conditions.

## Angola-Specific Context

Basin modeling of the Kwanza and Lower Congo basins has been critical for understanding Angola's petroleum system. PetroMod models show that the Neocomian lacustrine source rocks reached peak oil generation in the Miocene, charging the post-salt turbidite traps that host Angola's major fields (Girassol, Dalia, Pazflor, CLOV, Kaombo).
