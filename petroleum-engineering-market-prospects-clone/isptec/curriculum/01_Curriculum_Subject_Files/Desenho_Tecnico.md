## What You Learned

Orthographic projection, isometric drawing, section views, dimensioning, tolerances, and the ability to read and create standardized engineering drawings. You learned to translate 3D objects into 2D representations and vice versa.

## Where This Subject Is Used in Industry

- **Drilling Engineering:** Reading wellbore schematics â€” the standard document that shows the well from surface to TD, with all casing strings, cement tops, and completion equipment drawn to scale. Every well has one; every drilling engineer must read it instantly.
- **[FPSO](../../../docs/glossary.md) / Facilities Engineering:** Reading P&IDs (Piping and Instrumentation Diagrams) â€” the "circuit diagrams" of process plants. A P&ID shows every pipe, valve, instrument, and vessel on the [FPSO](../../../docs/glossary.md). Process engineers, HSE officers, and operators use P&IDs daily.
- **Subsea Engineering:** Subsea layout drawings show the positions of wellheads, manifolds, flowlines, and umbilicals on the seabed. These are the "maps" that [ROV](../../../docs/glossary.md) pilots follow.
- **Completions:** Completion string diagrams show every component from packer to SCSSV to tubing hanger â€” drawn as a cross-section.
- **Midstream/Downstream:** Pipeline route drawings, refinery plot plans, and equipment general arrangement (GA) drawings.
