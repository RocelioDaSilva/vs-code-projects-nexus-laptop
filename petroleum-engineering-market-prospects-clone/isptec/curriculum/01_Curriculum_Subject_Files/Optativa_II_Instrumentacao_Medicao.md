## What You Learned

Measurement principles for pressure, temperature, flow, and level sensors. Instrument calibration, accuracy, precision, signal transmission (4-20 mA, HART, fieldbus), process control (PID), and safety instrumented systems (SIS).

## Where This Subject Is Used in Industry

- **Flow Metering:** Measuring oil, gas, and water flow rates accurately is critical for production allocation (determining how much each well/partner produces), fiscal metering (custody transfer for sales), and reservoir management. Instruments: Coriolis meters, ultrasonic meters, multiphase flow meters, orifice plates.
- **Pressure and Temperature Monitoring:** Downhole gauges, wellhead gauges, process pressure/temperature sensors â€” continuous monitoring of hundreds of instruments on an [FPSO](../../../docs/glossary.md). Understanding measurement principles helps engineers evaluate data quality.
- **Process Control:** [FPSO](../../../docs/glossary.md) and platform process control uses PID loops: controlling separator level, gas compressor speed, water injection rate. The DCS (Distributed Control System) manages all process control.
- **Safety Instrumented Systems (SIS):** Emergency Shutdown (ESD) and High Integrity Pressure Protection Systems (HIPPS) use safety-rated instruments. Understanding SIL (Safety Integrity Level) ratings is essential for safety engineering.
- **Fiscal Metering:** Oil sales from Angola FPSOs require custody transfer meters with Â±0.25% accuracy. Measurement uncertainty directly affects revenue â€” a 0.1% error on 200,000 bbl/d is 200 bbl/d = $15,000/day.

## Angola-Specific Context

Every Angola [FPSO](../../../docs/glossary.md) has thousands of instruments monitored by the DCS. Instrument technicians are among the most in-demand technical roles offshore. For engineers, understanding instrumentation helps in production optimization, troubleshooting, and alarm management. Modern Angolan FPSOs (Kaombo) use advanced multiphase flow meters that measure oil/gas/water from each well without a test separator.
