## What You Learned

Numerical reservoir simulation: governing equations (continuity + Darcy), grid construction (Cartesian, corner-point), fluid model initialization, wells and completions modeling, history matching, prediction runs, and use of commercial simulators (Eclipse, CMG, tNavigator).

## Where This Subject Is Used in Industry

- **Field Development Planning:** Reservoir simulation is the primary tool for evaluating development options: How many wells? Where to place them? Water injection or gas injection? What production plateau? The simulator forecasts production under different scenarios.
- **History Matching:** Adjusting model parameters (permeability, fault transmissibility, aquifer strength, relative permeabilities) until the simulation reproduces observed historical production data. A history-matched model is trusted for prediction.
- **Production Forecasting:** Forecasting plateau rate, decline rate, watercut evolution, and cumulative production over field life â€” the basis for reserves booking and economic evaluation.
- **Well Placement Optimization:** Running multiple simulation scenarios to determine the optimal location, trajectory, and completion design for infill wells.
- **IOR/EOR Screening:** Evaluating potential for enhanced oil recovery (WAG, polymer, COâ‚‚ injection) using simulation.
