## What You Learned

Process safety management, risk assessment (HAZID, HAZOP, bow-tie), safety integrity levels (SIL), fire and explosion protection, personal safety (PPE, PTW), emergency response planning, and safety legislation.

## Where This Subject Is Used in Industry

- **HSE Department:** Every offshore installation has an HSE team. Safety engineers conduct risk assessments, manage the Permit to Work system, investigate incidents (root cause analysis), and maintain safety management systems.
- **Process Safety:** Preventing major accidents (blowouts, fires, explosions) through design (safety instrumented systems, pressure relief), procedures (HAZOP, LOPA), and barrier management. The Deepwater Horizon disaster was a process safety failure.
- **Permit to Work (PTW):** No work happens offshore without a permit. Understanding hazards (working at height, confined space, hot work, electrical isolation) and the control measures for each is daily safety management.
- **Emergency Response:** Fire, abandon ship, man overboard, Hâ‚‚S release, medical evacuation â€” every offshore worker must know these procedures. Safety engineers design and drill these scenarios.
- **SIMOPS Management:** Simultaneous Operations (producing while drilling, crane operations during offloading) create complex hazard interactions that require rigorous safety assessment.

## Angola-Specific Context

Angola's operators must comply with ANPG safety regulations and international standards (ISO 31000, API RP 75). The safety record in Angola's offshore industry has improved significantly, but the risks remain high.
