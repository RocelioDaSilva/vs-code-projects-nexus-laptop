# Offshore Execution Checklist (Angola + Africa)

> Goal: Get into offshore rotational petroleum-engineering work fast, with strong employability and salary upside.
>
> Use this as a weekly execution checklist. Tick items only when evidence is complete.

---

## 1) Master Checklist (Do in This Order)

### Phase A: Direction and Market Positioning (Week 1-2)

- [ ] Choose primary path: Drilling or Production/FPSO
- [ ] Choose backup path: HSE-support or Completions-support
- [ ] Build target company list (minimum 20: service + operator + FPSO)
- [ ] Set certification budget and monthly savings plan

Where:

- Path guidance: [../02_Six_Paths_Decoded.md](../02_Six_Paths_Decoded.md)
- Company selection: [Company_Targeting_Guide.md](Company_Targeting_Guide.md)
- Market demand context: [Angola_Employability_Market_Intelligence.md](Angola_Employability_Market_Intelligence.md)

How:

1. Commit to one primary track in writing.
2. Create a spreadsheet with company name, role type, contact, deadline, status.
3. Set a fixed monthly transfer to certification savings before other spending.

---

### Phase B: Mandatory Offshore Access Certifications (Week 2-12)

- [ ] Offshore medical certificate completed
- [ ] OPITO BOSIET (with HUET) completed
- [ ] H2S awareness completed (if required by target roles)
- [ ] Digital copies + printed copies organized

Where (Angola first):

1. Offshore medical (Luanda):
   - Clinica Multiperfil
   - Hospital Sagrada Esperanca
   - International SOS Angola
2. BOSIET/HUET:
   - PETROTEC Training Center (Luanda, check OPITO status)
   - ICAT (Luanda, verify current accreditation before payment)

Where (regional Africa backup):

1. Nigeria (Lagos): OPITO-approved providers
2. South Africa (Cape Town/Johannesburg): OPITO-approved providers
3. Namibia (Walvis Bay/Windhoek): training market is smaller, verify latest OPITO listings before travel

How:

1. Verify provider accreditation on the awarding body website before paying.
2. Ask provider for exact certificate title, validity period, and verification method.
3. Confirm your target employer accepts that exact certificate/provider.
4. Book 6-10 weeks ahead during peak demand.

---

### Phase C: Technical Employability Credentials (Week 8-20)

- [ ] IWCF Level 1 completed
- [ ] NEBOSH IGC started or completed (recommended employability multiplier)
- [ ] Optional: IOSH Managing Safely completed
- [ ] Optional by path: IWCF Level 2 plan defined (timing + budget)

Where:

1. IWCF:
   - Angola centers listed in [Certifications_Angola.md](Certifications_Angola.md)
   - Regional test centers in Nigeria and South Africa
   - Official center finder: iwcf.org
2. NEBOSH/IOSH:
   - Astutis (online)
   - British Safety Council (online)
   - Other NEBOSH/IOSH accredited partners

How:

1. Register only through accredited learning partners/test centers.
2. Block weekly study slots in calendar (minimum 4-6 hours/week).
3. Use official syllabus and sample assessments first.

---

### Phase D: Portfolio and Interview Proof (Week 4-24)

- [ ] Build CV master version + 2 tailored versions
- [ ] Build LinkedIn profile in English with target headline
- [ ] Complete 2 technical mini-projects (Excel or Python)
- [ ] Prepare interview bank (20 technical + 20 behavioral answers)

Where:

- Skills and tools: [../skills/12_Software_and_Python_Tools.md](../skills/12_Software_and_Python_Tools.md)
- Technical modules by path: [../03_Skills_Universe.md](../03_Skills_Universe.md)

How:

1. Project 1: drilling/production calculations dashboard.
2. Project 2: data interpretation case (rates, pressure, decline, anomaly notes).
3. Publish outputs as PDF + short LinkedIn technical summary.

---

### Phase E: Applications and Networking (Week 10 onward)

- [ ] 30+ quality applications submitted across 2-3 waves
- [ ] 3 alumni/professional outreach messages per week
- [ ] 1 networking event per month (SPE/ANPG/company event)
- [ ] Referral tracker maintained

Where:

- Company portals and hiring routes: [Company_Targeting_Guide.md](Company_Targeting_Guide.md)
- SPE membership/events: spe.org and SPE Luanda section

How:

1. Apply in batches (service companies first, then operators/FPSO owners).
2. Track every application outcome and follow up after 7-10 days.
3. Ask for referral only after a clear technical conversation.

## 2) Free vs Paid vs Certified Sources

## Free Sources (high-value, start now)

1. SPE webinars and selected technical content
2. OnePetro abstracts and open papers
3. NPTEL engineering courses (fundamentals)
4. YouTube channels for drilling, well control, production operations (use selectively)
5. Vendor technical notes (SLB, Halliburton, Baker Hughes public resources)

How to use free sources correctly:

- Use a structured learning plan, not random browsing.
- Capture notes in problem-solution format.
- Convert each topic into one interview-ready explanation.

## Paid Sources (fast-track learning)

1. PetroSkills short courses (drilling/production/completions)
2. NEBOSH IGC providers (online)
3. IOSH Managing Safely providers
4. IWCF prep + exam packages
5. OPITO BOSIET/HUET packages

How to choose paid courses:

1. Only buy courses tied to immediate hiring value.
2. Prioritize internationally verifiable certificates.
3. Compare total cost: tuition + exam + travel + re-sit cost.

## Certified Sources (verification-first list)

1. OPITO (BOSIET/HUET)
2. IWCF (well control)
3. NEBOSH (HSE)
4. IOSH (safety management)
5. SPE (professional membership and technical standing)

Verification rule:

- Never rely on marketing claims alone. Confirm accreditation on the official awarding body website before payment.

## 6) Weekly Operating Rhythm (Repeat Every Week)

- [ ] 6-8 hours technical study
- [ ] 2 technical problem sets solved
- [ ] 3 networking messages sent
- [ ] 5 quality applications or 2 deep referral actions
- [ ] 1 interview practice session
- [ ] 1 savings transfer for certifications

Score rule:

- 5/6 done = good week
- 6/6 done = elite week

## 7) Anti-Scam and Quality Control Checklist

- [ ] Accreditation verified on official body website
- [ ] Certificate title and validity confirmed in writing
- [ ] Employer acceptance confirmed before payment
- [ ] Full fee breakdown received (including retake or practical fees)
- [ ] Provider cancellation/refund policy reviewed

If any of these are missing, do not pay yet.
