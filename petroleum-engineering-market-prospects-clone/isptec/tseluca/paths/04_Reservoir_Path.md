# Path: Reservoir Engineer — From ISPTEC to Operator Technical Core

> **Time to first offshore rotation:** 24–48 months post-graduation
> **Typical entry company:** TotalEnergies, ExxonMobil, Azule Energy, or Sonangol EP
> **Physical environment:** Luanda office (primary) + FPSO or drillship (well test campaigns, 2–6 trips/year)
> **Honest note:** If you want maximum FIFO rotation, this is not the fastest path. If you want the highest technical prestige and ultimate earning potential in Angola's oil sector, this is it.

---

## What Reservoir Engineers Actually Do

The reservoir engineer answers the most fundamental question in oil and gas: **How much oil is in the ground, how fast can we produce it, and how do we get the most out of the reservoir over the life of the field?**

Angola's deepwater turbidite reservoirs — the sandstone formations at 1,500–3,000m depth that contain billions of barrels of oil — are complex geological systems. Understanding them requires combining geological data, well test data, production data, and numerical simulation into an integrated model that can forecast the future.

**Reserves estimation and classification:**
- Calculate Proved (1P), Probable (2P), and Possible (3P) reserves using volumetric and material balance methods
- Update reserves estimates annually based on production history and new well data
- Report to management and joint venture partners (operators report reserves to government regulators including ANPG)

**Reservoir characterization:**
- Interpret well logs (gamma ray, resistivity, porosity) to map reservoir distribution
- Analyze core data for rock properties
- Run pressure transient analysis (PTA) from well tests to determine permeability, skin, reservoir boundaries
- Build static geological models in Petrel

**Reservoir simulation:**
- Build dynamic simulation models (Eclipse, CMG) that model how fluids move through the reservoir over time
- History match the model to actual production data
- Run forecast scenarios: depletion at current rate, infill drilling, waterflood, WAG injection
- Calculate recovery factor: how much of the oil-in-place can we actually produce?

**Depletion strategy:**
- Design the optimal well pattern, injection program, and production rate to maximize recovery factor
- Advise on when to drill infill wells
- Manage reservoir pressure through injection (water or gas injection)
- Identify bypassed pay zones for perforation

**Well testing and sampling:**
- Plan and supervise drill stem tests (DST) on new exploration wells
- Plan and supervise production well tests on producers for Pressure Transient Analysis
- Analyze PVT (Pressure-Volume-Temperature) samples to characterize reservoir fluids

## Why Reservoir Engineering Is Worth the Wait

Reservoir engineers at Tier 1 operators are the people who make the decisions that determine whether a block is economic. They sit in Luanda planning meetings where $100 million infill drilling decisions are made. They are the technical authority on the subsurface.

**Salary trajectory for reservoir engineers in Angola:**

| Level | Years | USD/Month |
|---|---|---|
| Junior Reservoir Engineer | 0–3 | $3,500–6,000 |
| Reservoir Engineer | 3–7 | $6,000–10,000 |
| Senior Reservoir Engineer | 7–12 | $10,000–15,000 |
| Reservoir Engineering Lead | 12+ | $14,000–22,000+ |

These salaries are paid in USD by Tier 1 operators. They represent the highest individual contributor technical salaries in Angola's petroleum sector. A senior reservoir engineer at TotalEnergies or ExxonMobil Angola earns more than most Angolan bank executives.

## The Offshore Component

While reservoir engineering is primarily office-based, you do go offshore:

**Well tests (DST / production test):** When a new exploration well is drilled and the operator wants to test the reservoir, the reservoir engineer designs the test (what flow rates, what shut-in periods, what samples to collect) and goes offshore to supervise and interpret the real-time data. This might be a 2–4 week offshore trip.

**PVT sampling:** Collecting fluid samples from subsurface using wireline tools or during a well test. The reservoir engineer specifies the sampling procedure and may go offshore to supervise the collection.

**FPSO visits:** For production monitoring, well allocation tests, and meter verification. Short visits (3–7 days) to check that actual production matches your reservoir model assumptions.

If you want rotation-heavy offshore work, this is not your track. If you're comfortable with 4–10 offshore trips per year and want the technical intellectual depth of reservoir modeling, this is exceptional.

## Required Technical Software

This is the most software-intensive of the six tracks. You must invest time in these tools before your first job interview at a reservoir engineering level:

| Software | Purpose | Access |
|---|---|---|
| **Eclipse (SLB)** | Industry-standard black oil and compositional reservoir simulator | University access via SLB programs |
| **CMG (Computer Modelling Group)** | Alternative reservoir simulator (increasingly common) | Free student version at cmgl.ca |
| **Petrel (SLB)** | Static geological modeling and reservoir characterization | Student access programs |
| **Prosper / MBAL (Petex)** | Material balance, well performance, nodal analysis | Free student version |
| **Saphir (KAPPA)** | Pressure transient analysis (PTA) for well test interpretation | No free version; training courses available |
| **Excel + VBA** | Decline curve analysis, reserves calculations, material balance | Free |
| **Python** | Production data analysis, automated decline fitting | Free |

**Minimum software target before your first reservoir interview:** CMG student version with a completed basic simulation run. This shows you understand the workflow.

## Entry Routes: How to Actually Get In

### Route A: Operator Graduate Programs (Primary Route)

TotalEnergies, ExxonMobil Angola, and Azule Energy all have graduate reservoir engineering programs. These are competitive but deliberately designed to hire Angolan nationals per ANPG local content requirements.

**Application strategy:**
1. Apply during your final year (Semester 7–8)
2. Lead with your simulation project (Projecto II, if reservoir-focused)
3. Mention CMG or Eclipse experience, even if basic
4. Show your understanding of material balance and decline curve analysis

### Route B: Sonangol E.P.

Sonangol EP's technical team has reservoir engineers. The entry level is competitive but the Kwanza-denominated salary is lower than Tier 1 international operators. However, Sonangol provides:
- Exposure to Angola's entire upstream portfolio (they have interests in almost every block)
- Strong ANPG relationships
- A foundation for later moves to TotalEnergies or ExxonMobil

### Route C: Consultancy / Service (Non-direct, slower)

Some graduates enter through reservoir consulting firms (Core Laboratories, Gaffney Cline, RPS) that do work in Angola. This provides technical depth but delays operator entry. Not recommended as a first choice but viable if operator applications don't succeed immediately.

## The ISPTEC Subjects That Directly Prepare You

| ISPTEC Subject | Reservoir Connection |
|---|---|
| Engenharia de Reservatórios I | Fundamental reservoir physics — Darcy's Law, rock properties, fluid properties |
| Engenharia de Reservatórios II | Advanced — drive mechanisms, material balance, pressure maintenance |
| Simulação e Modelagem de Reservatórios | Dynamic simulation — the core skill |
| Avaliação de Formações | Log interpretation — reservoir characterization |
| Gerenciamento e Monitoração de Reservatórios | Reservoir management — depletion, IOR/EOR |
| Geoestatística | Geostatistical modeling of reservoir properties |
| Geologia Geral + Introdução à Análise de Bacias | Stratigraphic understanding — why turbidites exist |
| Estatística Básica | Statistical analysis for uncertainty quantification |
| Computação Científica I & II | Numerical methods, which underpin all simulation |

These subjects are your direct preparation. If you excel in these subjects, you have a genuine technical foundation for reservoir engineering.

## Technical Interview Preparation: 15 Questions

1. What is Darcy's Law? What does it tell you about permeability?
2. What is a pressure transient test? What is the skin factor?
3. What is a material balance equation? What does it assume?
4. What is a drive mechanism? Name the main types and describe each.
5. What is recovery factor? What is a typical recovery factor for an Angola deepwater turbidite?
6. What is the difference between reservoir simulation and material balance?
7. What is history matching and why is it important?
8. What is gas-oil ratio (GOR) and what does a rising GOR indicate?
9. What is water cut and what does it mean for production economics?
10. What is a DST (drill stem test)? What information does it provide?
11. What is PVT analysis? What are the main PVT properties you need for a reservoir model?
12. What is EOR (Enhanced Oil Recovery)? Name three EOR methods.
13. What is WAG injection? Why is it used?
14. What is a decline curve? Describe Arps' decline curve parameters (Di, b, qi).
15. What is a reserves audit? What is the difference between 1P, 2P, and 3P reserves?
