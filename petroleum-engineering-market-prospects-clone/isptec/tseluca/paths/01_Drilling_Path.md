# Path: Drilling Engineer — From ISPTEC to Drillship

> **Time to first offshore rotation:** 6–18 months post-graduation (fastest realistic path with proper preparation)
> **Typical entry company:** SLB, Halliburton, or Baker Hughes Field Engineer Trainee program
> **Physical environment:** Drillship (deepwater) or jackup rig (shallow water)
> **Schedule:** 28/28 or 21/21 rotation

---

## What Drilling Engineers Actually Do

Angola drills 8–20 wells per year across its deepwater blocks. Each well is a $30–80 million operation taking 30–90 days. The drilling engineer is the technical architect and supervisor of that operation.

**At the well planning stage (onshore, typically Tier 1 or senior Tier 2):**
- Design the well: casing program, mud weight schedule, bit selection, wellbore trajectory
- Build the Drilling Program — the operational bible that governs every step of the well
- Run Torque and Drag (T&D) models to verify the drill string will work through the planned trajectory
- Calculate wellbore hydraulics: equivalent circulating density (ECD), pressure losses, flow rates
- Estimate the cost and schedule (AFE — Authorization for Expenditure)

**At the wellsite (offshore, field engineer or company man):**
- Monitor real-time drilling data on a WITSML feed: ROP, WOB, torque, drag, ECD, pit volume
- Make operational decisions: bit change timing, parameter adjustments, kick detection response
- Write the IADC Daily Drilling Report (DDR) — accounts for every 15 minutes of rig time
- Coordinate the service company specialists (MWD engineer, mud engineer, cementing engineer)
- Respond to downhole problems in real time: stuck pipe, lost circulation, gas kick

**As a field engineer at SLB/Halliburton/Baker Hughes:**
You are NOT the Company Man (that's the operator's representative). You are the technical expert for your company's specific service. If you're a Directional Driller, you control the drill bit trajectory. If you're an MWD engineer, you operate the downhole measurement tools. If you're a mud engineer, you manage the drilling fluid properties.

## The Drilling Career Ladder in Angola

```
Year 0–1: Field Engineer Trainee (Tier 2 service company)
  └── Learning the specific service your company provides
  └── On rotation: shadowing, assisting, learning the rig
  └── Offshore 60–80% of the time

Year 1–3: Field Engineer (MWD/LWD, Directional, Fluids, Cementing)
  └── Responsible for your service. You are the person on shift.
  └── Making technical decisions in real time
  └── Offshore 70–90% of the time

Year 3–5: Senior Field Engineer / Specialist Engineer
  └── Training junior engineers on rotation
  └── Complex well programs (multilateral, high-angle, HPHT)
  └── Or: transition to Tier 1 as junior drilling engineer

Year 5–8: Drilling Engineer (Tier 1 Operator or Senior Tier 2)
  └── Well design role. Onshore office + rig supervision visits
  └── Building wells from AFE to TD

Year 7–12: Senior Drilling Engineer / Drilling Supervisor
  └── Supervising multiple simultaneous wells
  └── People management + technical authority

Year 10+: Wells Manager / Drilling Superintendent
  └── Strategic oversight. Budget authority. Negotiating rig contracts.
  └── Angola-based companies: $12,000–20,000+/month at this level
```

## Entry Routes: How to Actually Get In

### Route A: SLB Field Engineer Trainee (Most Common for ISPTEC Graduates)

SLB (formerly Schlumberger) runs a dedicated Field Engineer Trainee (FET) program that accepts fresh graduates. This is the single most common entry point for Angolan petroleum engineering graduates into offshore drilling.

**What SLB offers:**
- 6–12 month onboarding: classroom training in Houston, Paris, or SLB's regional training centers + on-the-job training
- Your first rotation is typically 6–9 months after joining
- You specialize in one of: Drilling & Measurements (D&M), Drilling Fluids (M-I SWACO), or Completions
- After 3 years, you are a qualified Field Engineer with a marketable international credential

**How to apply:**
1. Go to slb.com/careers
2. Search "Field Engineer Trainee" + "Angola" or "Africa"
3. Apply with your CV + cover letter
4. The interview process: online assessment → technical phone screen → in-person panel interview

**What they want to see in your CV:**
- BOSIET/HUET (or booked/in progress)
- IWCF Level 1 (or in progress)
- Specific ISPTEC subjects: Engenharia de Poços, Fluidos de Perfuração, Mecânica das Rochas
- Any lab work with measurable results
- Technical English proficiency

**What they will ask in the interview:**
- "Explain the well construction sequence from spud to completion."
- "What is the purpose of the BOP and what types of rams does it have?"
- "A kick has been detected. Walk me through the shut-in procedure."
- "What is ECD and why does it matter?"
- "Describe the difference between WBM and OBM drilling fluids."

See the skill tutorials in this folder to prepare these answers.

### Route B: Halliburton LEAD Program

Halliburton's Learning and Education for Accelerated Development (LEAD) program is equivalent to SLB's FET. Halliburton specializes in:
- Baroid division: Drilling fluids (mud engineering)
- Sperry Drilling: Directional drilling and MWD
- Halliburton Cementing

**Key difference from SLB:** Halliburton tends to have a more structured first-year training period and places more emphasis on certifications early. They will expect you to have, or be very close to completing, your IWCF Level 2 before your first offshore rotation.

### Route C: Baker Hughes Graduate / Emerging Talent Program

Baker Hughes specializes in drill bits, drilling systems, and chemicals. Their Angola operation is smaller than SLB or Halliburton but their graduate program is real. They also operate the BHGE measurement tools (Inteq MWD systems).

### Route D: Tier 3 → Tier 2 (Slower But Reliable)

If you cannot get into a Tier 2 FET program directly:
1. Join a Tier 3 local company with genuine drilling-adjacent work (SONILS yard, PAENAL fabrication, Petromar logistics)
2. Use that 12–18 months to: complete IWCF Level 2, build practical knowledge of drilling equipment, network with Tier 2 field engineers who visit the yard
3. Apply to Tier 2 programs with 12–18 months of relevant experience on your CV

This route adds 12–18 months to your timeline but substantially increases your Tier 2 acceptance probability.

## Required Certifications: Drilling Path

| Certification | Level Required | When to Get It | Cost Estimate | Provider |
|---|---|---|---|---|
| **Offshore Medical** | Pass | Year 3 Semester 6 | $300–500 | Offshore medical clinics, Luanda |
| **BOSIET/HUET** | Pass | Year 3 Semester 6 | $600–1,200 | See support/Certifications_Angola.md |
| **IWCF Level 1** | Pass | Year 4 Semester 7 | $500–1,000 | Online (iwcf.org) |
| **IWCF Level 2 (Surface)** | Pass | Year 1 on the job | $1,200–2,500 | IWCF centers |
| **H2S Awareness** | Pass | Before first offshore trip | $300–600 | Online or in-person |
| **Fire Safety Basic** | Pass | Usually done during BOSIET | Included | — |
| **First Aid Basic** | Pass | Year 4 | $100–200 | Red Cross / workplace providers |

**Strongly recommended (adds significant CV value):**
- IADC WellSharp (equivalent to IWCF, sometimes preferred by North American-oriented operators)
- SLB, Halliburton, or Baker Hughes internal certifications (you get these when you join)

## Essential Software for Drilling Engineers

You don't need to be an expert on these before joining, but being able to mention familiarity will differentiate your CV:

| Software | What It Does | Free Access? |
|---|---|---|
| **COMPASS (Landmark)** | Wellbore survey and trajectory planning | Student license sometimes available |
| **WellPlan (Landmark)** | Torque & drag, hydraulics modeling | Student license sometimes available |
| **Drillbench (Kongsberg)** | Managed pressure drilling, well control simulation | No free version |
| **WITSML viewers** | Real-time drilling data monitoring | Free viewers available online |
| **Microsoft Excel** | Hydraulics calculations, cost tracking | Included in Office |
| **Python / pandas** | Drilling data analysis | Free |

**Practical exercise:** Build an Excel spreadsheet that calculates:
- Hydrostatic pressure at any depth given mud weight
- ECD given circulating pressure loss and mud weight
- Kill mud weight given shut-in drillpipe pressure (SIDPP) and original mud weight

If you can build this from first principles, you are interview-ready for hydraulics questions.

## The Angola-Specific Advantage: Local Content

The ANPG's Decreto Legislativo Presidencial 2/18 and related regulations require oil companies operating in Angola to:
- Give preference to Angolan-national employees over expatriates for technical roles
- Submit workforce nationalization plans showing % Angolan technical staff increasing over time
- Pay training levies if they cannot meet nationalization targets

**Practical effect for you:** An Angolan petroleum engineering graduate with BOSIET + IWCF + relevant technical knowledge is in high demand because companies need to fill their Angolan technical headcount. You are not competing with international engineers for these roles — you are the preferred candidate by law.

## Technical Interview Preparation: 20 Questions You Must Be Able to Answer

1. Describe the complete well construction sequence from spudding to TD
2. What is the purpose of each casing string (conductor, surface, intermediate, production)?
3. What is ECD? How do you calculate it? Why does it matter in narrow-margin wells?
4. What are the signs of a kick? What do you do when you detect one?
5. Describe the Driller's Method for well killing
6. What is the difference between WBM and OBM drilling fluids? When do you use each?
7. What is plastic viscosity and yield point? How do you measure them?
8. What is a BHA? What are the common components?
9. What does an MWD tool measure and how does it transmit data?
10. What is directional drilling? What is a build section vs a tangent section?
11. What is stuck pipe? What are the two main types and how do you free them?
12. What is lost circulation? How do you deal with it?
13. Explain torque and drag in a wellbore and why it matters for casing design
14. What is managed pressure drilling (MPD) and why is it used in deepwater?
15. What is the difference between a subsea BOP stack and a surface BOP?
16. What is primary cementing and why is it important?
17. What is a kick tolerance? How is it calculated?
18. Describe the LMRP (Lower Marine Riser Package) and its function
19. What is a drilling program? What does it contain?
20. You are on shift on a drillship. The mud engineer reports a 3-barrel pit gain. What do you do?
