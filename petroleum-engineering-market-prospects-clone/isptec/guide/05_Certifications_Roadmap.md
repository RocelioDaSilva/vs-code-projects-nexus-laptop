# Certifications Roadmap — What You Need, In Order, With Costs

> No certification = no offshore access. This is not a soft requirement. You cannot board a helicopter to an offshore installation without at least the first two certifications on this list. Everything else can wait. These cannot.

---

## The Non-Negotiable Minimum (Get These Before Applying)

### 1. BOSIET + HUET (Basic Offshore Safety Induction & Emergency Training)

**What it is:**  
A 3–5 day mandatory safety course that teaches you how to survive an offshore emergency. It covers:
- Offshore survival (sea survival, life rafts, firefighting)
- Helicopter Underwater Escape Training ([HUET](../../docs/glossary.md)) — the most important part: how to escape a submerged helicopter in a controlled tank simulation
- Firefighting and fire prevention
- First aid basics
- Emergency procedures (abandon ship, man overboard)

**Why it's mandatory:**  
No operator or service company will allow you to board any offshore installation without a current [BOSIET](../../docs/glossary.md) certificate. This is global industry standard, enforced by OPITO (the offshore training standards body). Angola's operators follow it without exception.

**Validity:** 4 years (with an OPITO-approved refresher called Further Offshore Emergency Training / FOET extending validity)

**Cost:**  
- In Angola (CFA Luanda): ~$500–$900 USD  
- Internationally (UK, Norway, South Africa): $900–$1,500 USD  
- *Do it in Angola — CFA Luanda (Centro de Formação Ambiental) is OPITO-approved and significantly cheaper*

**Where to do it in Angola:**  
**CFA — Centro de Formação Ambiental, Luanda** (primary OPITO-approved provider in Angola)  
- Location: Near [SONILS](../../data/company-directory/sonangol.md) base, Luanda  
- Contact: +244 222 XXX XXX (verify current number via [Sonangol website](https://www.sonangol.co.ao/) or ANPG)  
- Some international operators also accept [BOSIET](../../docs/glossary.md) done in South Africa (OPITO-approved centres in Cape Town)

**Pro tip:** If you do BOSIET in Angola, your certificate is identical to one done in Aberdeen or Houston. OPITO certification is globally recognized. Angola employers will accept it without question.

**Timing:** Get this BEFORE you apply to any Tier 2 company. Having it in hand signals you're serious and ready to mobilize.

### 2. Offshore Medical Certificate

**What it is:**  
A medical fitness assessment performed by an OPITO-approved or company-approved doctor that certifies you are physically fit to work offshore. It checks:
- Blood pressure (must be controlled — typically below 160/100 onsite)
- Vision and hearing
- Cardiovascular health (ECG for candidates 35+)
- BMI and weight (survival suit sizing, lifeboat escape)
- No epilepsy, severe diabetes, or disqualifying conditions
- Drug and alcohol screening (urine test)

**Why it's mandatory:**  
An offshore installation is hours from emergency medical care by helicopter. Companies cannot afford to evacuate employees with preventable medical emergencies. Your certificate is required before boarding any offshore installation.

**Validity:** 2 years (with annual review if you have certain controlled conditions)

**Cost:**  
- In Angola: ~$150–300 USD at approved medical centres  
- Abroad: $300–700 USD

**Where to do it in Angola:**  
- SOS International Luanda (approved for most major operators)
- International SOS Luanda clinic
- Ask the HR department of your target company — they often have approved clinic partnerships

**Pro tip:** Do this at the same time as your BOSIET, or immediately after. Both are required to appear on any mobilization manifest.

## The Next Priority (Get These Within 6–12 Months of Starting Work)

### 3. IWCF Well Control — Level 1 (Fundamentals)

**What it is:**  
International [Well Control](../../docs/skills/01-well-control.md) Forum certification. Level 1 ([Well Control](../../docs/skills/01-well-control.md) Fundamentals) is a 3-day classroom and computer-based exam that covers:
- Pressure and hydrostatic calculations
- Formation pressure and wellbore pressure balance
- How and why kicks happen (unwanted influx of formation fluids into the wellbore)
- Basic BOP (Blowout Preventer) operations
- Well kill methods (driller's method, wait-and-weight)

**Who needs it:** Anyone working in a drilling or completions role. If you're on a drillship or working on a well in any capacity, [IWCF](../../docs/glossary.md) Level 1 is required.

**Validity:** 3 years (renewed via a 1-day refresher)

**Cost:** ~$800–$1,500 USD (varies by provider and location)

**Level 2 (Supervisor):** More advanced. Required if you're acting as a supervisor or Company Man. Takes 5 days. Plan to get this by Year 2–3 of your career.

**Where to do it:**  
- [Halliburton](../../data/company-directory/halliburton.md) Training Centre, Luanda (often the most accessible for Angola-based engineers)
- [SLB](../../data/company-directory/slb.md) Training Centre, Luanda  
- [IWCF](../../docs/glossary.md)-approved providers in South Africa, UK (online theory, practical in-country)

---

### 4. H2S Awareness

**What it is:**  
A 1-day awareness course on hydrogen sulfide — a toxic, colorless gas that occurs naturally in some reservoirs and production facilities. At concentrations above 50 ppm, [H2S](../../docs/glossary.md) can cause rapid incapacitation and death.

Angola's offshore fields have relatively low [H2S](../../docs/glossary.md) concentrations compared to, say, the Middle East or North Sea. But pre-salt exploration zones and some production streams do contain H2S, and all offshore installations carry H2S monitors.

**Who needs it:** Not strictly mandatory for all roles, but required by most operators before boarding. Takes half a day. Cheap. Get it.

**Cost:** ~$100–$300 USD  
**Validity:** 2–3 years

---

### 5. Cédula Marítima (Seafarer's Book)

**What it is:**  
A Seafarer's Identification Document issued by IMPA (Instituto Marítimo e Portuário de Angola) or the equivalent maritime authority. Some employers — particularly [FPSO](../../docs/glossary.md) operators and marine contractors — require this before assignment to a floating vessel.

**Note:** Not required by all employers for all roles. Check with your specific employer. If they don't ask for it, you don't need it immediately.

**Cost:** ~$50–100 USD (local maritime authority fees)  
**Where to get it:** IMPA, Luanda

## Later-Stage Certifications (Year 2–5)

These certifications accelerate your career significantly. Plan for them, but don't let them block your first job.

### 6. IWCF Level 2 (Well Control Supervisor)
- More advanced [well control](../../docs/skills/01-well-control.md): complex kill scenarios, deepwater specifics, subsea BOP operations
- Required for: Company Men, Drilling Supervisors, Senior Completion Engineers
- Cost: ~$1,500–2,500 USD | Validity: 3 years
- **Ask your Tier 2 employer to sponsor this** — most will if you commit to 2 more years

### 7. NEBOSH International General Certificate (HSE Track)
- Globally recognized safety qualification
- Online/blended study (~200 hours), written exam
- Cost: ~$600–$1,200 USD (course + exam) | Valid: indefinitely (with CPD)
- Highly recommended for: HSE track engineers

### 8. Software Certifications
- **Petrel Fundamentals** ([SLB](../../data/company-directory/slb.md)): Free for students at learn.slb.com
- **PROSPER / GAP** (Petroleum Experts): Contact PE for student license
- **WellPlan** (Landmark/[Halliburton](../../data/company-directory/halliburton.md)): Often provided internally when you join Halliburton
- Getting any of these before your first job is a genuine differentiator

### 9. OPITO FOET (Refresher)
- Renews your [BOSIET](../../docs/glossary.md) when it expires at 4 years
- 1-day course. Much cheaper than original BOSIET (~$200–350)
- Never let your BOSIET expire — you become unmobilizable

```
BEFORE APPLYING:
  ├── BOSIET / HUET ────────────── 5 days, ~$700, do at CFA Luanda
  └── Offshore Medical ──────────── 1 day, ~$200, do at SOS International Luanda

FIRST 6 MONTHS AT WORK:
  ├── H2S Awareness ──────────────── 1 day, ~$150, any OPITO provider
  ├── IWCF Level 1 (if drilling) ─── 3 days, ~$1,000, ask employer to sponsor
  └── Cédula Marítima (if needed) ── 1 day, ~$75, IMPA

YEAR 1–2:
  ├── IWCF Level 2 ───────────────── 5 days, ~$2,000, employer-sponsored
  └── Relevant software course ───── Self-study / online

YEAR 2–4:
  ├── NEBOSH IGC (HSE track) ──────── 6 months self-study, ~$900
  └── Track-specific courses ──────── Varies (Petrel, OLGA, WellPlan, etc.)
```

## What Happens If You Don't Have These

Without BOSIET + Offshore Medical, you cannot board a helicopter to an offshore installation. This means:
- You cannot do any offshore role
- You are automatically excluded from ~70% of Angola's petroleum engineering jobs
- Companies cannot hire you for rotational positions regardless of your qualifications

This is not bureaucratic inconvenience. It is a genuine safety system that protects the 150 people on that installation with you.

**Cost reality check:** [BOSIET](../../docs/glossary.md) + Offshore Medical = ~$900–1,100 total. This is the single best ROI investment of your career. A single offshore rotation can pay more in one month than your investment.
