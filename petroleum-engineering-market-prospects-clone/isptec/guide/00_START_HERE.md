## The Core Idea: Three Tiers, One Path

Angola's offshore industry works in **three tiers**. Understanding this structure is everything:

```
TIER 1 — OPERATORS             (TotalEnergies, Chevron, ExxonMobil, Azule Energy, Sonangol)
  └── They OWN the oil blocks. They hire the most senior engineers.
  └── Entry: 3–7 years experience OR direct graduate programs (competitive)
  └── Salary: USD 4,000–18,000/month

TIER 2 — SERVICE COMPANIES     (SLB, Halliburton, Baker Hughes, TechnipFMC, Subsea 7, Saipem...)
  └── They EXECUTE the work. They run the drills, maintain the FPSOs, install the subsea.
  └── Entry: 0–2 years experience. Many have graduate trainee programs.
  └── Salary: USD 2,500–8,000/month + offshore allowance

TIER 3 — LOCAL ANGOLAN FIRMS   (SONILS, Algoa Cabinda Fabrication, Operatec, Petromar...)
  └── They SUPPORT the service companies. Essential connective tissue.
  └── Entry: Fresh graduates. No certifications required at some.
  └── Salary: USD 800–1,500/month (in Kwanzas at most)
```

**Your path as an ISPTEC graduate:**

```
Graduation → Tier 3 (1–2 years) → Tier 2 (2–4 years) → Tier 1 (destination)
```

This is not a rule. It's a strategy. Some graduates go straight to Tier 2 ([SLB](../../data/company-directory/slb.md) hires fresh graduates via their Field Engineer Trainee program). A few go straight to Tier 1 via Azule Energy's internship. But the **safest, highest-probability path** is the three-tier climb.

## One Final Note

This industry is hard to get into. Angola's offshore sector is competitive, relationship-driven, and safety-obsessed. No one cares about your GPA. They care whether you show up on time, keep your certifications current, follow procedures under pressure, and make the team safer and more effective.

The good news: **you already have the technical foundation.** The ISPTEC curriculum covers everything you need to understand what happens on a rig or [FPSO](../../docs/glossary.md). What you need to add is: the right certifications, the right relationships, and the right strategy.

This guide gives you all three.
