> Understanding this market is not optional. Every career decision you make — which company to target, which specialization to choose, which certification to get first — depends on knowing how Angola's offshore industry is structured.

### Block 17 — Why It Dominates Everything

Block 17 is operated by TotalEnergies and accounts for roughly **40% of Angola's total production**. It contains six [FPSO](../../docs/glossary.md) units: Girassol, Dalia, Pazflor, CLOV, Kaombo Norte, and Kaombo Sul. Every major service company has contract vehicles on Block 17. It is the single most important block to understand as a job seeker.

## The Infrastructure: FPSOs and Drillships

### What is an FPSO?

A **Floating Production, Storage and Offloading** vessel is a ship-shaped platform that:
1. **Produces** oil and gas from subsea wells connected by flowlines
2. **Processes** the wellstream (separates oil, gas, and water)
3. **Stores** crude oil in tanks (typically 1–2 million barrels capacity)
4. **Offloads** oil to shuttle tankers every 5–10 days

An [FPSO](../../docs/glossary.md) is permanently moored at the field. It does not move. **This is where production engineers, HSE engineers, process engineers, and subsea engineers work on rotation.**

### Drillships and Jack-Ups

**Drillships** (like the Valaris DS-17 or Noble Bob Douglas) are used to **drill new wells** in deepwater. These are where drilling engineers, [MWD](../../docs/glossary.md)/LWD specialists, mud engineers, and completions engineers spend their rotations.

**Jack-up rigs** operate in shallow water (Block 0, Block 14 shallow areas). They're less common in Angola but still active.

## The Regulatory Framework

### ANPG — Agência Nacional de Petróleo, Gás e Biocombustíveis

ANPG is Angola's petroleum regulator. It does three things that matter for your career:

1. **Licenses oil blocks** — decides who operates what
2. **Enforces Local Content** — Presidential Decree 271/20 requires operators and service companies to hire Angolans, use Angolan suppliers, and develop local capacity. **This is why your ISPTEC degree is valuable.** Companies have legal targets for Angolan employment.
3. **Certifies service companies** — a company must be ANPG-registered to work in Angola. The service categories it registers under determine what work it can bid for.

**Key [ANPG service categories](../../docs/angola/anpg-company-service-categories.md) for engineers:**
- Category 17: Drilling, Completion & Well Intervention (highest demand)
- Category 12: Maritime Operations ([FPSO](../../docs/glossary.md) operations)
- Category 20: Subsea Production Systems & [SURF](../../docs/glossary.md)
- Category 13: Reservoir Services
- Category 14: HSE Services

For the full ANPG category analysis, see [docs/anpg-company-service-categories.md](../../docs/angola/anpg-company-service-categories.md).

## Why Your Timing Is Good (2026 Context)

Several factors make 2026 a strong entry year:

1. **Agogo development ([Azule Energy](../../data/company-directory/azule-energy.md) / Block 32):** New FPSO coming online 2024–2026 = new positions across all disciplines
2. **Block 20/11 ([TotalEnergies](../../data/company-directory/totalenergies.md)):** Development drilling ongoing = drilling engineer demand
3. **Local content enforcement tightening:** ANPG is actively monitoring Angolan hiring ratios. Operators face fines if ratios drop. Your degree is a compliance asset.
4. **Kaombo expansion:** [TotalEnergies](../../data/company-directory/totalenergies.md) continuing well workover and production optimization campaigns
5. **Pre-salt exploration:** New frontier. Geoscience and reservoir engineering roles increasing.

## Key Takeaway

Angola does not have a small or declining offshore industry. It has one of the most technologically complex deepwater production ecosystems in the world. It runs 20+ FPSOs. It drills with drillships worth USD 600M+ each. It has a mandatory local content framework that makes Angolan graduates valuable.

**Your degree is not a disadvantage here. It is the baseline requirement.**

The question is not whether you can get into this industry. The question is how fast, and into which tier.
