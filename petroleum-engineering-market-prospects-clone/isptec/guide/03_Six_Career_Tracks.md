# Six Career Tracks — Pick Your Specialization

> You cannot be "a petroleum engineer" in Angola's offshore industry. You must be **a drilling engineer**, or **a production engineer**, or **a subsea engineer**. Generalists get passed over. Specialists get hired.
>
> This file describes all six main tracks in enough detail for you to make an informed choice. Choose one. Then go deep.

---

## How to Choose

Ask yourself three questions:
1. **What did I enjoy most in the ISPTEC curriculum?** (Not what you were best at — what you *enjoyed*)
2. **Do I want to be on a drillship (wells-focused) or an [FPSO](../../docs/glossary.md) (production-focused) or a construction vessel (subsea)?**
3. **What is the fastest path to offshore rotation from where I am today?**

Use the comparison table below, then read the section for your top two options.

## Track 1 — Drilling Engineer

### What You Do Day-to-Day

You plan and supervise the drilling of wells. Every well drilled in Angola deepwater costs **USD 30–80 million** and takes 30–90 days. Your job is to make that well faster, safer, and cheaper without compromising integrity.

**On the drillship:**
- Monitor real-time drilling parameters: ROP (rate of penetration), WOB (weight on bit), torque, pump pressure, ECD
- Make decisions: when to change the bit, how to respond to a kick, what mud weight adjustment to make
- Write the IADC daily report: account for every 15 minutes of rig time (non-productive time = money lost)
- Communicate with the operator's Company Man onshore (or onsite)
- Supervise the [MWD](../../docs/glossary.md)/LWD specialist, the mud engineer, the cementing crew

**On your off-days (onshore):**
- Design the next well: casing program, bit selection, mud program, completion design
- Work with reservoir engineers to position the well trajectory for optimal reservoir contact
- Prepare drilling programs (procedural documents that govern every step of the well)
- Review post-well reports and extract lessons learned

### The Angola-Specific Reality

Angola is deepwater with complex geology. Pre-salt carbonate reservoirs are being explored. Shallow water (Block 0) uses jack-ups. Deepwater (Block 17, 32) uses drillships. The formation pressures are high in some zones. Drilling here is technical.

### ISPTEC Subjects That Help You Most
→ Engenharia de Poços, Fluidos de Perfuração e Completação, Mecânica das Rochas, Completação de Poços

### Entry Path
1. Get [BOSIET](../../docs/glossary.md) + Offshore Medical + IWCF Level 1
2. Apply to [SLB](../../data/company-directory/slb.md) Field Engineer Trainee (FET) program — they accept fresh graduates
3. Alternatively, start at Tier 3 ([SONILS](../../data/company-directory/sonangol.md)) as a drilling support technician for 12 months
4. Transition to [SLB](../../data/company-directory/slb.md), [Halliburton](../../data/company-directory/halliburton.md), or [Baker Hughes](../../data/company-directory/bakerhughes.md) as a Field Engineer Trainee

### Salary Progression
- Trainee (0–1 yr): $2,500–3,500/month
- Field Engineer (1–3 yrs): $3,500–5,500/month
- Senior/Specialist (3–7 yrs): $5,000–9,000/month
- Drilling Supervisor / Company Man (7+ yrs): $8,000–15,000/month

## Track 2 — Production / FPSO Engineer

### What You Do Day-to-Day

You keep the oil flowing. Angola's production comes from FPSOs — the floating facilities that receive oil from subsea wells, process it, and store it for export. Your job is to maximize production rate and minimize downtime.

**On the [FPSO](../../docs/glossary.md):**
- Monitor well performance: production rates, GOR (gas-oil ratio), watercut, wellhead pressures
- Identify wells that are underperforming — recommend interventions
- Manage the [FPSO](../../docs/glossary.md) process: separators, compressors, gas lift injection, produced water treatment
- Coordinate with subsea engineers when flowlines show anomalies
- Raise production uplift requests (requesting interventions like coiled tubing, gas lift valve changes)
- Manage daily production allocation reports: which well produced how many barrels

**Onshore / office role:**
- Nodal analysis to optimize gas lift injection rates
- Decline curve analysis to forecast production 12–24 months ahead
- Prepare well intervention proposals (coiled tubing, ESP replacement)

### The Angola-Specific Reality

Angola's FPSOs are aging. Girassol (Block 17) first oil was 2001. These mature fields have increasing watercut and declining GOR. Production engineers are busy managing production decline and designing enhanced recovery programs. New FPSOs (Agogo for [Azule Energy](../../data/company-directory/azule-energy.md), Kaminho for [TotalEnergies](../../data/company-directory/totalenergies.md)) are coming online — new opportunities.

### ISPTEC Subjects That Help You Most
→ Escoamento de Petróleo, Elevação de Petróleo, Engenharia de Reservatórios I & II, Sistemas de Produção Superficial, Sistemas Marítimos de Produção

### Entry Path
1. Get [BOSIET](../../docs/glossary.md) + Offshore Medical
2. Target FPSO operator companies: [SBM Offshore](../../data/company-directory/sbmoffshore.md) (operates Pazflor, CLOV, Kaombo), [Yinson](../../data/company-directory/yinson.md) (Agogo), [Azule](../../data/company-directory/azule-energy.md) Energy
3. Alternatively: [TotalEnergies](../../data/company-directory/totalenergies.md) Young Graduate Program (18 months, competitive)
4. [SLB](../../data/company-directory/slb.md) Production Solutions team also hires production engineers

### Salary Progression
- Junior Production Engineer (0–2 yrs): $2,500–4,000/month
- Production Engineer (2–5 yrs): $4,000–7,000/month
- Senior Production Engineer (5–10 yrs): $7,000–12,000/month
- Production Superintendent (10+ yrs): $12,000–18,000/month

**For the full production career roadmap:** [docs/career-paths/02-production-engineer.md](../../docs/career-paths/02-production-engineer.md)

## Track 3 — Subsea Engineer

### What You Do Day-to-Day

Angola's 600+ deepwater producing wells are connected to FPSOs via subsea equipment: trees, manifolds, flowlines, risers, and umbilicals. Nothing is on the seabed without a subsea engineer who designed it, installed it, and maintains it.

**During construction/installation projects (offshore):**
- Supervise the installation of subsea equipment from a pipelay or construction vessel
- Manage [ROV](../../docs/glossary.md) (remotely operated vehicle) operations during subsea interventions
- Coordinate hook-up and commissioning of new [subsea systems](../../docs/skills/11-subsea-systems.md)
- Read and interpret as-built surveys, inspection reports, [ROV](../../docs/glossary.md) video

**During production (offshore or onshore):**
- Monitor subsea equipment health: tree pressures, valve positions, flowline temperatures
- Plan and execute subsea interventions (subsea tree maintenance, jumper replacements)
- Manage integrity of umbilicals, flexible risers, and flowlines (fatigue, corrosion, inspection)

### The Angola-Specific Reality

Angola has one of the largest concentrations of subsea infrastructure in the world. Block 17 alone has 6 FPSOs and 140+ subsea wells, all connected by hundreds of kilometres of subsea equipment. The Kaombo development has one of the world's longest tie-back distances. Subsea engineers are in high demand — but the entry bar is higher (you need to understand the full [SURF](../../docs/glossary.md) system).

### ISPTEC Subjects That Help You Most
→ Sistemas Marítimos de Produção, Resistência de Materiais, Ciências de Materiais e Corrosão, Mecânica Geral, Escoamento de Petróleo

### Entry Path
1. Get [BOSIET](../../docs/glossary.md) + Offshore Medical
2. Target [TechnipFMC](../../data/company-directory/technipfmc.md) (Angola) for subsea trees and manifolds
3. Target [Subsea 7](../../data/company-directory/subsea7.md) or Saipem for installation and IMR work
4. [Oceaneering](../../data/company-directory/oceaneering.md) for ROV-based subsea intervention (ROV pilot-technician trainee path)
5. Start in hook-up & commissioning (lower technical bar, gets you on construction vessels)

### Salary Progression
- Junior Subsea Engineer (0–2 yrs): $3,000–5,000/month
- Subsea Engineer (2–5 yrs): $5,000–8,000/month
- Senior Subsea Engineer (5–10 yrs): $8,000–13,000/month
- Subsea Project Manager (10+ yrs): $12,000–20,000/month

**For the full subsea career roadmap:** [docs/career-paths/04-subsea-engineer.md](../../docs/career-paths/04-subsea-engineer.md)
**Deep-dive (26 questions answered):** [docs/career-deep-dives/career_subsea_engineer.md](../../docs/career-deep-dives/career_subsea_engineer.md)

## Track 4 — Reservoir Engineer

### What You Do Day-to-Day

You manage the subsurface asset. Every barrel of oil that Angola produces comes from a reservoir that someone designed a recovery strategy for. You are that person. Reservoir engineering is the highest-salaried discipline in petroleum engineering — and the hardest to enter directly from university.

**Day-to-day work:**
- Build and update reservoir simulation models (Eclipse, CMG, Petrel)
- Estimate reserves (P10/P50/P90) for reporting to the regulator (ANPG)
- Analyze well test data to determine reservoir properties (permeability, skin, boundaries)
- Design waterflooding and gas injection patterns for improved recovery
- Make infill well recommendations (where to drill next for maximum recovery)
- Perform production history matching in simulation models

**The offshore component:** Reservoir engineers typically work onshore, with occasional trips to the [FPSO](../../docs/glossary.md) for well tests or sampling campaigns. Less rotation, more office. But: higher salary ceiling.

### The Angola-Specific Reality

Angola's turbidite reservoirs are notoriously complex — highly heterogeneous, thin-bedded, with uncertain connectivity. Block 17 reservoirs have been studied for 25+ years and still surprise engineers. Pre-salt carbonates are the next frontier. Reservoir engineers with Angola-specific knowledge are rare and valuable.

### ISPTEC Subjects That Help You Most
→ Engenharia de Reservatórios I & II, Simulação e Modelagem de Reservatórios, Gerenciamento e Monitoração de Reservatórios, Geoestatística, Avaliação de Formações

### Entry Path
1. This track typically requires strong academic performance + software skills
2. Apply directly to [Sonangol](../../data/company-directory/sonangol.md) E.P. graduate program (focuses on reservoir management)
3. Or: join [SLB](../../data/company-directory/slb.md)'s Reservoir Characterization group or [Halliburton](../../data/company-directory/halliburton.md)'s Landmark division
4. Or: build experience in a related role ([formation evaluation](../../docs/skills/04-formation-evaluation.md), well testing) at Tier 2, then move into reservoir engineering

### Salary Progression
- Junior Reservoir Engineer (0–2 yrs): $3,500–5,500/month
- Reservoir Engineer (2–5 yrs): $5,500–9,000/month
- Senior Reservoir Engineer (5–10 yrs): $9,000–14,000/month
- Reservoir / Asset Manager (10+ yrs): $14,000–22,000/month

**For the full reservoir career roadmap:** [docs/career-paths/03-reservoir-engineer.md](../../docs/career-paths/03-reservoir-engineer.md)

## Track 5 — HSE / Safety Engineer

### What You Do Day-to-Day

Every offshore installation in Angola — every [FPSO](../../docs/glossary.md), every drillship, every construction vessel, every fabrication yard — must have a dedicated HSE team. By law. ANPG enforces it. This makes HSE the single most consistently hiring discipline in Angola's oil sector.

**Offshore:**
- Conduct daily safety walks (hazard identification and near-miss reporting)
- Lead toolbox talks before every job
- Manage the Permit to Work (PTW) system
- Investigate incidents and near-misses using root cause analysis
- Enforce SIMOPS (simultaneous operations) procedures
- Monitor helicopter deck, crane operations, confined space entries

**Onshore:**
- Develop HSE management systems and procedures
- Conduct risk assessments (HAZID, HAZOP) for new projects
- Prepare emergency response plans
- Manage environmental compliance (produced water, flaring, waste)
- Interface with ANPG on HSE reporting obligations

### The Angola-Specific Reality

Angola's HSE culture has matured significantly under ANPG pressure. Total recordable incident rates are scrutinized. Every operator has annual HSE targets. Angolan HSE officers are valued because: (1) local content requirements, (2) Portuguese communication on mixed-nationality crews, (3) understanding of local labor law. Entry is the easiest of all six tracks.

### ISPTEC Subjects That Help You Most
→ Engenharia de Segurança, Meio Ambiente e Sustentabilidade, Engenharia Ambiental, Química Geral

### Entry Path
1. Get [BOSIET](../../docs/glossary.md) + Offshore Medical + H2S Awareness (minimum)
2. Consider NEBOSH International General Certificate (~$600–1,200 online) — differentiates you
3. Apply to any operator or service company for HSE Officer / Safety Observer role
4. Tier 3 local companies ([SONILS](../../data/company-directory/sonangol.md), Algoa) are easiest entry

### Salary Progression
- HSE Observer/Assistant (0–1 yr): $1,500–2,500/month
- HSE Officer (1–4 yrs): $2,500–5,000/month
- Senior HSE Officer (4–8 yrs): $5,000–8,000/month
- HSE Manager (8+ yrs): $8,000–14,000/month

**For the full HSE career roadmap:** [docs/career-paths/05-hse-safety-engineer.md](../../docs/career-paths/05-hse-safety-engineer.md)

## Track 6 — Completions / Well Intervention Engineer

### What You Do Day-to-Day

Drilling a well is only half the job. Completing it — installing the production tubulars, perforating the reservoir, placing sand control screens, and commissioning the well to flow — is the other half. Well intervention is what happens when something goes wrong after the well is producing.

**During a completion campaign (offshore):**
- Run completion assemblies (screens, gravel packs, packers) into the wellbore
- Execute perforating operations and [formation evaluation](../../docs/skills/04-formation-evaluation.md)
- Perform [well integrity](../../docs/skills/15-well-integrity.md) tests
- Oversee stimulation operations (if required — rare in Angola due to unconsolidated sands)

**During interventions:**
- Plan and execute coiled tubing operations (scale removal, gas lift valve changes)
- Manage wireline operations (log surveys, plug setting, perforation)
- Design and execute bullheading or squeeze operations

### The Angola-Specific Reality

Angola's turbidite reservoirs produce sand-prone fluids. Sand control (gravel packing, frac packing) is the defining challenge for completions engineers. Every producing well has a completion designed to keep formation sand out. With 600+ producing wells across multiple FPSOs, completion and intervention work is constant.

### ISPTEC Subjects That Help You Most
→ Completação de Poços, Engenharia de Poços, Fluidos de Perfuração e Completação, Elevação de Petróleo

### Entry Path
1. [IWCF](../../docs/glossary.md) Level 2 is strongly recommended (required by most operators for completions work)
2. Start in a drilling support role at [SLB](../../data/company-directory/slb.md) or [Halliburton](../../data/company-directory/halliburton.md), then transition to completions
3. [Baker Hughes](../../data/company-directory/bakerhughes.md) has a strong completions division in Angola (sand control, completion tools)
4. [Halliburton](../../data/company-directory/halliburton.md)'s Production Enhancement team runs cementing and stimulation — good entry

### Salary Progression
- Completions Trainee (0–2 yrs): $3,000–4,500/month
- Completions Engineer (2–5 yrs): $4,500–7,000/month
- Senior Completions Engineer (5–10 yrs): $7,000–11,000/month
- Completions Superintendent (10+ yrs): $11,000–16,000/month

## Which Track Is Right for You?

| If you... | Choose... |
|---|---|
| Want offshore rotation ASAP with the fastest entry | HSE (easiest) or Drilling (via FET program) |
| Love the subsurface, enjoyed Reservoir Eng subjects most | Reservoir or Drilling |
| Prefer the production side, enjoyed Elevação de Petróleo | Production / [FPSO](../../docs/glossary.md) |
| Are good at mechanical/structural thinking | Subsea |
| Want the highest long-term salary ceiling | Reservoir → Operator |
| Want work anywhere in the world (most portable skill) | Drilling or Subsea |
| Want to stay in Angola and be locally irreplaceable | HSE (most local content demand) |
