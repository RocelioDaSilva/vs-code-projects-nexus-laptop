# The Application Playbook — CV, LinkedIn, Interviews, Salary

> Your technical skills get you considered. Your application gets you in the room. This file covers exactly how to build a CV that Angola's oil companies respond to, how to set up your LinkedIn correctly, where to find job openings, what the interview looks like, and how to negotiate your first salary.

---

## Part 1: Your CV

### The One-Page Rule

Angola's oil industry HR teams receive hundreds of applications. Your CV is read in 20–30 seconds on first pass. If it's more than one page, the second page is not read. If the first page is cluttered, it goes in the reject pile.

**One page. Period.**

### CV Structure (Angola Oil Industry Standard)

```
[Your Full Name]
Luanda, Angola | +244 9XX XXX XXX | youremail@gmail.com | linkedin.com/in/yourprofile
──────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────
SUMMARY (2 sentences maximum)
Petroleum Engineering graduate from ISPTEC with BOSIET/HUET certification and [X] 
months offshore experience. Specializing in [your track]. Available for offshore rotation.

CERTIFICATIONS
• BOSIET / HUET — OPITO certified, CFA Luanda, [month/year], valid until [date]
• Offshore Medical — SOS International Luanda, valid until [date]
• IWCF Well Control Level 1 — [if completed]
• SPE Member — #XXXXXXX

EDUCATION
Instituto Superior Politécnico e Universitário (ISPTEC) — Luanda, Angola
Licenciatura em Engenharia do Petróleo | Graduated [Month Year] | GPA: [X.X]/[scale]
Relevant coursework: Engenharia de Poços, Completação de Poços, Simulação de Reservatórios

EXPERIENCE
[Company Name], Luanda, Angola [Month Year – Present or Month Year]
[Your Title]
• [Most impactful bullet point — quantify if possible: "Supported drilling operations on 4 wells in Block 32"]
• [Second bullet: specific task or technology]
• [Third bullet: safety, teamwork, or procedural compliance — shows HSE mindset]

ISPTEC Final Year Project [Month Year – Month Year]
[Project Title]
• [1–2 bullets on what you did and what you found — shows technical capability]

TECHNICAL SKILLS
Software: Petrel (Fundamentals), Microsoft Office (Excel advanced), AutoCAD basics
Languages: Portuguese (native), English (professional — B2/C1), French (basic)
Technical: Well control fundamentals, IADC reporting, formation evaluation basics

──────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────
```

### CV Rules That Get Applications Rejected

**Avoid these:**
- Photo on the CV (standard in Angola but increasingly removed for international companies — omit unless specifically requested)
- Listing irrelevant experience ("sold shoes 2019") — only include if there's a genuine transferable skill
- Vague bullets: "Responsible for assisting with operations" — meaningless. Instead: "Assisted senior drilling engineer in monitoring BHP and ECD for 3 deepwater wells"
- Objectives statement (replace with Summary — one sentence on who you are, one on what you're seeking)
- References listed (write "References available on request" at the bottom — saves space)
- Typos. One typo on a technical CV = immediate disqualification at many companies

**Include these:**
- [BOSIET](../../docs/glossary.md) certification (certificate number + expiry date) — goes at the top, under your name
- Languages with honest level assessments
- Specific Angola block/field references if you have them (shows geographic knowledge)
- Your LinkedIn URL (and make sure the profile matches the CV)

## Part 2: LinkedIn Profile Setup

LinkedIn is how [SLB](../../data/company-directory/slb.md), [Halliburton](../../data/company-directory/halliburton.md), [Baker Hughes](../../data/company-directory/bakerhughes.md), and Azule Energy actively recruit in Angola. If you are not on LinkedIn, you are invisible to 70% of hiring managers.

### The Profile That Gets You Found

**Profile Photo:**  
Professional. Suit or clean button-down. Plain background. No sunglasses. No group photos cropped. This photo represents you to the [SLB](../../data/company-directory/slb.md) recruiter in Houston who is filtering candidates for Angola.

**Headline (the text under your name):**  
This is what shows in search results. Make it work:
```
GOOD: "Petroleum Engineer | BOSIET Certified | Angola Offshore | ISPTEC"
BAD: "Recent graduate looking for opportunities"
BAD: "Student at ISPTEC"
```

**About Section (3–5 sentences):**
- Who you are (ISPTEC PE graduate, year)
- What you specialize in or are building toward
- What certifications you have
- What you're looking for
- Your contact preference

Example:
> "Petroleum Engineering graduate from ISPTEC, Luanda, with a specialization focus in drilling engineering. [BOSIET](../../docs/glossary.md)/HUET certified (OPITO, CFA Luanda) and currently building [IWCF](../../docs/glossary.md) Level 1 knowledge. Seeking a Field Engineer Trainee or equivalent role with a service company operating in Angola's offshore blocks. Active SPE member. Open to connecting with professionals in Angola's oil and gas sector."

**Experience Section:**
- Add every real experience: ISPTEC project, any internship, any industry exposure
- Use the same bullet structure as your CV (action verb + specific thing you did + result/context)

**Education:**
- List ISPTEC correctly: "Instituto Superior Politécnico e Universitário (ISPTEC)" — the full name makes it searchable
- Add relevant coursework if you have no work experience

**Certifications Section:**
- Add [BOSIET](../../docs/glossary.md) with issuing authority (OPITO/CFA Luanda) and expiry date
- Add [IWCF](../../docs/glossary.md) when you complete it
- Add SPE membership

**Connections:**
- Your first 500 connections matter more than anything else in your LinkedIn profile
- Connect with: every classmate, every professor, every person you've met at any industry event
- Then: search "petroleum engineer Angola", "SLB Angola", "drilling engineer Luanda" and connect with professionals (not HR bots — real engineers)
- Send a brief connection note: "Hi [Name], I'm a petroleum engineering graduate from ISPTEC interested in [their company's] operations in Angola. Would value connecting with you."

## Part 4: The Application Process

### How to Apply (Step by Step)

1. **Find the posting** on the company career portal or LinkedIn
2. **Tailor your CV** — adjust your summary to reference their company specifically (takes 5 minutes per application, increases response rate significantly)
3. **Write a cover letter** — 3 paragraphs:
   - Para 1: "I am applying for [role] at [Company]. I am a [track] petroleum engineering graduate from ISPTEC with [BOSIET](../../docs/glossary.md)/HUET, [other certs]."
   - Para 2: "In my [experience/education], I [specific relevant thing]. I am familiar with [Block X / FPSO operations / drilling in Angola deepwater]."
   - Para 3: "I am available to start within [X] weeks and to mobilize offshore immediately. Please find my CV attached."
4. **Apply online** (mandatory — email-only applications are often not logged in the ATS system)
5. **Follow up** 10–14 days later by email to the HR contact listed, or via LinkedIn message to a recruiter

### Response Rate Expectations

Be realistic:
- 10% response rate is normal for cold applications to Tier 1 companies
- 25–40% response rate for Tier 2 programs you are well-matched to (certs + English + Angola-specific)
- 60–80% response rate when you have a warm referral from someone inside the company

This means you should apply to **15–20 positions simultaneously**. Track every application in a spreadsheet.

## Part 5: The Technical Interview

Most companies use a 2-stage interview process:
1. **Phone/video screening** — HR assesses language, communication, motivation
2. **Technical interview** — engineer-to-engineer, assesses competence in your track

### Common Technical Interview Questions by Track

**All tracks (expect these at every interview):**
- "Walk me through a wellbore pressure balance and explain what a kick is."
- "What is ECD and why does it matter in deepwater drilling?"
- "What does the Permit to Work system involve?"
- "What would you do if you noticed the pit volume increasing while drilling?"

**Drilling track:**
- "What is the difference between the driller's method and the wait-and-weight method?"
- "Walk me through the steps of a casing string design."
- "What factors determine mud weight selection?"

**Production/[FPSO](../../docs/glossary.md) track:**
- "Explain nodal analysis and what it tells you."
- "What is watercut and how does it affect production management?"
- "What is gas lift and when would you use it?"

**Reservoir track:**
- "What is a type curve and how would you use one?"
- "Explain the difference between P10, P50, and P90 reserves estimates."
- "What is skin and what causes it?"

**HSE track:**
- "Walk me through the hierarchy of controls."
- "What is ALARP?"
- "What is a HAZOP and who participates in one?"

**Subsea track:**
- "What is a subsea tree and what are its main components?"
- "What is a flexible riser?"
- "What does [SURF](../../docs/glossary.md) stand for?"

### Interview Behavior

- **Speak in structured answers.** Not: "I think maybe the mud weight should be higher." Instead: "Given the ECD constraints and the formation pressure profile, I would increase mud weight by [X ppg] to maintain overbalance while staying below the fracture gradient."
- If you don't know the answer, say: "I'm not certain of that specific detail, but based on [related knowledge], my reasoning would be..."
- Ask smart questions at the end: "What does the typical first 6 months of onboarding look like for this role in Angola?" or "What are the biggest technical challenges on the [Block X] operations right now?"

## Part 6: Salary Negotiation

### The Angola Oil Market Reality

Angola oil salaries are USD-denominated for Tier 2 and Tier 1 companies. Kwanza salaries apply mainly at Tier 3 and for support roles.

**For a fresh graduate with [BOSIET](../../docs/glossary.md) + IWCF Level 1:**

| Company Type | Starting Range | What to Ask For |
|---|---|---|
| Tier 3 | AOA equivalent of $800–1,500 | Ask for job security + offshore schedule + [IWCF](../../docs/glossary.md) sponsorship |
| Tier 2 trainee | $2,000–3,500/month base | Ask for: $3,000 base + $100/day offshore allowance |
| Tier 2 graduate program | $2,500–4,000/month | Negotiate on the allowance — base is usually fixed |
| Tier 1 internship | $1,500–3,500/month | Negotiate: performance bonus after 6 months |

### How to Negotiate

1. **Do not state your expected salary first.** When asked, say: "I'd love to understand the role and the responsibilities fully before we discuss compensation. What is the standard range for this position?"
2. **If forced to answer:** "Based on my research of the Angola market for an engineer with [BOSIET + IWCF + X months experience], I believe $X–Y is appropriate. I'm flexible depending on the full package."
3. **The package includes:** base salary + offshore allowance (per diem) + medical insurance + transport allowance + [BOSIET](../../docs/glossary.md) renewal sponsorship + training. These matter.
4. **Never negotiate your first offer without a competing offer.** If you have no competing offer, accept and negotiate at your 6-month review instead. Being employed is worth more than the first month's salary difference.

## Mobilization Readiness Checklist

Before your first offshore mobilization, have all of the following ready and accessible:

- [ ] [BOSIET](../../docs/glossary.md) certificate (original + digital copy)
- [ ] Offshore medical certificate (original + digital copy)
- [ ] [IWCF](../../docs/glossary.md) Level 1 certificate (if required by role)
- [ ] Passport (valid 6+ months beyond expected return date)
- [ ] BI (Bilhete de Identidade) — Angolan national ID
- [ ] Emergency contact details submitted to employer HR
- [ ] Survival suit fitting (most companies do this on mobilization day)
- [ ] PPE: steel-toe boots, hard hat, coveralls (some companies provide; confirm in advance)
- [ ] Prescription medications (30-day supply; carry doctor's letter for controlled substances)
- [ ] Camera policy checked (some installations prohibit personal cameras/smartphones on the drill floor)
- [ ] Communication plan with family (your daily call schedule, emergency contact at the company shore base)
