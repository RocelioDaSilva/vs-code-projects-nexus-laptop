# Your ISPTEC Degree → The Offshore Job Market

> Your Licenciatura em Engenharia de Petróleos from ISPTEC is not a theoretical exercise. Every subject you studied maps directly to a real job function in Angola's offshore industry. This file makes that mapping explicit.

---

## The Honest Assessment

**What your degree gives you:**
- Strong theoretical foundation in reservoir, drilling, production, and subsea engineering
- Mathematics and physics that underpin every simulation and calculation in the field
- Lab exposure to fluids, rock mechanics, and PVT behavior
- Portuguese-language technical writing ability (rare and valuable)
- An ANPG-recognized Angolan qualification (counts toward local content ratios)

**What your degree does NOT give you:**
- Offshore certifications ([BOSIET](../../docs/glossary.md), IWCF, offshore medical) — you get these separately
- Software proficiency (Petrel, Eclipse, OLGA, PROSPER) — you develop this yourself
- Industry relationships — you build these intentionally
- Real-world operational experience — you earn this in your first 1–2 years

**The gap is bridgeable.** In 6–18 months after graduation, you can close every one of these gaps.

## Curriculum → Industry Mapping

### The Drilling Track

If you want to become a **Drilling Engineer, [MWD](../../docs/glossary.md)/LWD Field Engineer, Mud Engineer, or Completions Engineer:**

| ISPTEC Subject | Industry Application | How it Shows Up on the Rig |
|---|---|---|
| **Engenharia de Poços** | Well design, casing program, wellbore stability | You design the casing strings. You know why a 9⅝" casing goes at X depth. |
| **Fluidos de Perfuração e Completação** | Drilling fluid design, mud weight window | You understand why the mud engineer picks a specific mud weight for a kick-prone formation. |
| **Completação de Poços** | Completion design, sand control, perforations | You know why a gravel pack matters in Angola's turbidite reservoirs. |
| **Mecânica das Rochas** | Pore pressure prediction, fracture gradient | You can read a pore pressure vs. fracture gradient plot. |
| **Física Geral I, II** | Mechanics, fluid statics/dynamics | Torque and drag, buoyancy forces on drill pipe |
| **Cálculo I–IV** | Differential equations for wellbore flow | Hydraulics calculations, pressure loss in the annulus |
| **Computação Científica I–II** | Spreadsheet models, Python scripts | IADC report automation, drilling parameter tracking |
| **Engenharia de Segurança** | HSE on the rig | Permit to work, job safety analysis (JSA), emergency response |

**Skill gap to close:** [IWCF](../../docs/glossary.md) Level 1 ([well control](../../docs/skills/01-well-control.md) theory), WellPlan or COMPASS basics, real drilling fluid chemistry.

### The Production / FPSO Track

If you want to become a **Production Engineer, [FPSO](../../docs/glossary.md) Process Engineer, or Production Technologist:**

| ISPTEC Subject | Industry Application | How it Shows Up on the [FPSO](../../docs/glossary.md) |
|---|---|---|
| **Escoamento de Petróleo** | [Flow assurance](../../docs/skills/10-flow-assurance.md), pipeline design | You understand why wax deposition happens in a cold deepwater flowline. |
| **Elevação de Petróleo** | [Artificial lift](../../docs/skills/09-artificial-lift.md) design (gas lift, ESP) | You design the gas lift mandrel spacing on a Girassol well. |
| **Engenharia de Reservatórios I & II** | Production decline, IPR curves | You know why a well's productivity index changes over time. |
| **Sistemas de Produção Superficial** | FPSO topside process (separators, compressors) | You read P&IDs. You understand 3-stage separation. |
| **Sistemas Marítimos de Produção** | FPSO architecture, moorings, risers | You know why a steel catenary riser is used vs. a flexible riser. |
| **Termodinâmica Aplicada** | Heat exchangers, gas compression | Compressor performance on the FPSO gas compression train |
| **Físico-Química** | PVT behavior, phase envelopes | You understand why the gas-oil ratio changes at different pressures. |
| **Fenómenos de Transporte I & II** | Heat and mass transfer | FPSO heat exchanger sizing, produced water treatment |

**Skill gap to close:** PROSPER/GAP (nodal analysis software), OLGA basics, FPSO P&ID reading.

## The Skills Gap: What to Add Beyond the Curriculum

Regardless of which track you choose, every offshore engineer needs these:

### 1. English at B2+ Level (Non-Negotiable)
Almost all technical documentation, SPE papers, software interfaces, and international team communications are in English. If your English is weak, fix it before anything else. Use:
- **Duolingo + formal English course** (ISPTEC may have resources)
- **SPE technical papers** — read 2 per week in your chosen specialization
- **Offshore safety briefings** — these are always in English

### 2. Spreadsheet Mastery (Excel / Google Sheets)
The IADC daily report, production allocation tables, drilling cost tracking, KPI dashboards — all Excel. You need pivot tables, VLOOKUP, and basic charts minimum.

### 3. One Simulation Software (at an introductory level)
- Drilling track: WellPlan or COMPASS ([Halliburton](../../data/company-directory/halliburton.md))
- Production/Reservoir: Petrel ([SLB](../../data/company-directory/slb.md)), PROSPER (Petroleum Experts)
- Subsea: AutoCAD basics or OrcaFlex concepts

[SLB](../../data/company-directory/slb.md) gives free student licenses for Petrel. Download it at **learn.slb.com** with your student email.

### 4. SPE Student Membership (~$15/year)
The Society of Petroleum Engineers gives you access to 200,000+ technical papers. Join. Read papers on Angola-specific topics: turbidite reservoirs, deepwater completions, [FPSO](../../docs/glossary.md) gas compression.

## The Subject You Should Never Have Skipped

**Ingles I–IV.** If you treated these as easy elective subjects, go back and close that gap. Right now.

Angola's offshore industry runs in Portuguese on the surface (with [Sonangol](../../data/company-directory/sonangol.md), local companies) and in English at every international level (with operators, service companies, technical documentation, and safety standards). Being bilingual is a competitive advantage that doubles your available job openings.
