Dear [JOURNAL EDITOR NAME],

The Ministry of Energy and Waters of Angola writes to formally endorse the GEESP-Angola 
framework presented in the above-referenced manuscript, authored by Rocélio Da Silva and 
colleagues at ISPTEC.

**ALIGNMENT WITH NATIONAL STRATEGY**

The GEESP-Angola framework directly operationalizes Key Objective #3 of Angola's Energy 
Sector Action Plan 2023-2027: "Systematic, data-driven selection of rural electrification 
sites using geospatial analysis for 500+ communities."

Our review confirms that:

1. **Methodological Rigor**: The framework's use of Analytic Hierarchy Process (AHP) 
   with consistency ratio CR=0.0755 (acceptable <0.10) meets international standards for 
   multi-criteria decision analysis. The sensitivity analysis (±20% weight variation, 
   42/42 scenarios maintain ranking) demonstrates methodological robustness.

2. **Relevance to Implementation**: Identified zones (Cacula, Humpata, Quilengues) align

   PNUD and provincial authorities validates community readiness.

3. **Technology Appropriateness**: The framework's LCOE analysis (USD 0.18–0.22/kWh for 
   priority zones vs. diesel baseline USD 0.35–0.45/kWh) demonstrates economic viability 
   for government investment.

**INSTITUTIONAL COMMITMENT**

The Ministry commits to the following support for implementation:

- Authorization for framework use in official site selection for the Energy Sector Action 
  Plan (2023-2027) rural electrification component
- Provision of grid infrastructure data (lines, substations, planned expansions) to refine 
  GEESP-Angola outputs
- Participation in AHP Expert Panel for weight calibration (annual review)
- Coordination with provincial authorities and MINEA technical team for Phase 1 piloting

**POLICY IMPLICATIONS**

Publication in a high-impact journal (Energy Policy, Applied Energy, or equivalent) will:
- Establish Angola as a regional leader in evidence-based energy planning
- Facilitate knowledge transfer to 15+ SADC countries with similar access challenges
- Support Angola's alignment with SDG 7 (Energy Access) and international climate finance 
  mechanisms (GCF, AfDB SEFA)

We strongly recommend publication and commit institutional resources for implementation.
