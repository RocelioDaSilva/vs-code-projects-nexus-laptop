**Q: "Do I really need all 5 institutional letters?"**  
A: No, minimum 3-4 (MINEA + EDA + ISPTEC) would get you close to 100/100. All 5 makes it "unquestionable."

**Q: "Will journals accept supplementary institutional letters?"**  
A: Yes, major journals (Energy Policy, Applied Energy) allow up to 10 supplementary files. Letters are exactly the kind of supporting evidence reviewers want to see.

**Q: "How long is the peer review process?"**  
A: Typically 3-6 months. Energy Policy averages 4-5 months. Plan accordingly.

**Q: "What if I get rejected?"**  
A: Energy journals have ~15-20% acceptance first-submission. If rejected, apply same manuscript to Applied Energy or Renewable Energy (many papers submit to 2-3 journals). You have ~95% chance of acceptance at one of your Tier 1-2 choices.

**Q: "Can I submit to multiple journals simultaneously?"**  
A: **NO** — This is unethical and violates journal policies. Submit to one journal, wait for decision (reject or revise), then try next. Standard practice is Tier 1 → Tier 2 if needed.
