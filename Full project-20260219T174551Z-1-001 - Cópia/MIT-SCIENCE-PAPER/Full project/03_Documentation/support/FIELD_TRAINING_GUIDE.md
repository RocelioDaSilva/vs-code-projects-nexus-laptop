# 🎓 Field Technician Training Guide - GEESP-Angola
**Version**: 2.0 (Comprehensive) | **Date**: February 2026 | **Language**: English / Portuguese

---

## **Objective**
Train 4-6 field technicians to safely and accurately conduct baseline solar energy surveys in Angola using specialized equipment and software protocols.

**Training Duration**: 5 days (30 hours total) + 1 day shadowing  
**Equipment Requirements**: See Annex A  
**Location**: Training facility (Luanda) + 1 day field practice  
**Required Background**: Basic literacy, comfort with electronic devices, spoken Portuguese  

---

## **Module 1: Introduction & Safety (Day 1 - 4 hours)**

### **1.1 Project Overview (1 hour)**
**Objectives:**
- Understand GEESP-Angola mission: identify optimal sites for community solar systems
- Know geographic focus: Cacula, Humpata, Quilengues districts
- Understand role: field team is critical for data quality

**Topics:**
- Project background (4-slide overview)
- Angola's energy landscape & renewable goals
- What is solar suitability assessment?
- How field data feeds into analysis
- Timeline: baseline 18-28 May 2026, then monthly for 12 months

**Activity:**
- Q&A discussion (20 min)
- Distribute printed project summary (Portuguese)

### **1.2 Safety & Health Protocols (2 hours)**
**Objectives:**
- Understand hazards in field environment
- Know emergency procedures and first aid basics
- Maintain personal and team safety

**Topics:**
- Workplace hazards (sun exposure, heat, dehydration, insects, wildlife)
- PPE requirements (hats, sunscreen SPF 50+, water, closed shoes, insect repellent)
- Equipment handling safety (preventing damage, theft prevention)
- Accident reporting & incident documentation
- First aid basics (heat exhaustion, cuts, insect bites)
- Communication protocols (daily check-ins, emergency contacts)

**Practical:**
- Don and remove PPE correctly (30 min practice)
- Review emergency contact lists (Team leader, medical facility, HR)
- Practice radio/phone communication system

### **1.3 Baseline Survey Protocol Overview (1 hour)**
**Objectives:**
- Know what data will be collected
- Understand sampling locations (GPS coordinates provided)

**Topics:**
- 5-day deployment per zone (Cacula, Humpata, Quilengues)
- Data collection timeline per site
- Introduction to equipment (will detail in Module 2)
- What success looks like (data completeness checklist)

## Module 2: Survey Equipment (1 hour)

### Learning Objectives
- Operate GPS unit accurately
- Use tablet for data entry
- Handle solar irradiance meter (piranometer)

### Equipment Overview

**GPS Unit (Garmin eTrex or equivalent)**
- Press "Mark" to save location
- Record ±5m accuracy
- Check battery level daily

**Rugged Tablet (iPad or Android)**
- Survey app pre-loaded with questionnaire
- Data synced via WiFi or cellular
- Backup to cloud at end of each day
- Password: [TO BE PROVIDED]

**Piranometer (Solar Irradiance Meter)**
- Measures solar energy in kWh/m²
- Level: Place on flat surface facing south
- Record reading at solar noon (12:00)
- Reset each morning to zero

### Hands-On Practice
- Practice: Mark 5 waypoints at training site
- Practice: Complete 3 test interviews on tablet
- Practice: Take 5 irradiance readings and record

## Module 3: Household Interview Protocol (2 hours)

### Learning Objectives
- Build rapport with community members
- Gather accurate household data
- Record responses legibly and timely

### Interview Structure

**Part A: Household Roster (5 min)**
- Household ID: [auto-generated by app]
- Head of household name
- Family size
- Primary occupation

**Part B: Energy Access (7 min)**
- Current energy source (firewood, candles, kerosene, grid, generator, solar)
- Hours of lighting per day
- Cost of current energy per month (USD)

**Part C: Willingness to Pay (5 min)**
- "If a solar system cost $X/year, would you use it?"
- Maximum affordable monthly payment

**Part D: Location & Infrastructure (3 min)**
- Approximate distance to nearest power grid (km)
- Nearest road type (paved, gravel, footpath)
- Water source proximity

### Best Practices
- Conduct interviews in local language
- Respect privacy; conduct one-on-one when possible
- Thank participants respectfully
- Record GPS location at start of interview
- Verify data entry before leaving site

### Quality Control Checklist
- [ ] All required fields completed
- [ ] GPS coordinate recorded
- [ ] Data synced to cloud
- [ ] Physical backup (notes) kept

## Module 4: Data Validation & Handoff (1 hour)

### Learning Objectives
- Identify data quality issues
- Prepare data for national database

### Common Data Quality Issues
- **Missing coordinates:** Interview not marked on map
- **Nonsensical values:** Income = 0, family size > 15 → ask for re-confirmation
- **Inconsistent responses:** "has electricity = no" but "monthly cost = $50" → clarify

### Daily Handoff Workflow
1. Sync all tablets to server at 6 PM daily
2. Review data quality dashboard (errors flagged automatically)
3. Report blockers to site lead
4. Keep paper backup of all forms
