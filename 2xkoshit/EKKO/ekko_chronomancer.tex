\section{Who is Ekko?}

Ekko is a prodigy from the rough streets of Zaun who manipulates time using his own invention, the \textbf{Z-Drive}. In 2XKO he occupies the \textbf{Mixup archetype}, thriving on pressure and burst mobility. Unlike characters who rely on raw power, Ekko rewrites the rules of each engagement through temporal tricks---afterimages, looping projectiles, and position-swapping teleports.

His kit rewards creative thinking, adaptation on the fly, and deep knowledge of the opponent's defensive habits. He can play the role of an aggressive rushdown fighter \textit{or} a patient zone-controller depending on his Timewinder and afterimage state.

\section{Strengths and Weaknesses}

\begin{multicols}{2}

\subsection*{\textcolor{ekkoGreen}{Strengths}}
\begin{itemize}[leftmargin=*]
  \item Huge arsenal of mixup tools --- opponents rarely know which direction to block
  \item Extremely versatile: rushdown \textit{and} zoning are both viable
  \item Fast, far-reaching Forward Assist hard to react to
  \item Outstanding okizeme with Timewinder oki setups
  \item Great team support --- both assists are highly effective in neutral and combos
  \item Good meter gain, especially from Chronobreak loops
  \item High creativity and room for player expression
  \item Punishing counterplay against parry with S2 Hold bait
\end{itemize}

\columnbreak

\subsection*{\textcolor{ekkoRed}{Weaknesses}}
\begin{itemize}[leftmargin=*]
  \item Loses poke battle against true zoners
  \item High skill floor AND ceiling
  \item Short normal range --- must stay extremely close
  \item Anti-air is genuinely poor; \notation{2H} is unreliable
  \item Temporal Slip (\notation{6S1}) has a gap and is punishable on block
  \item Cannot Replay out of failed throws (Season 1 nerf)
  \item Backdash slowed in Season 1 --- retreat is less evasive
\end{itemize}

\end{multicols}

\section{Reading the Frame Data}

\begin{tipbox}
\textbf{Startup:} Frames before the attack becomes active. Lower = faster.\\[0.3em]
\textbf{On Hit:} \advantage{Positive values} = your turn to continue pressure. \disadvantage{Negative} = opponent acts first.\\[0.3em]
\textbf{On Block:} \disadvantage{$-6$ or less} = punishable by light attacks. \disadvantage{$-3$ to $-5$} = punishable by fast moves. \disadvantage{$-1$ to $-2$} = mostly safe.\\[0.3em]
\textbf{Launch} = sends opponent airborne; enables air combo.
\end{tipbox}

\section{Practical Frame Data Notes}

\begin{itemize}
  \item \movename{5L} at \disadvantage{$-$1} on block is safe --- always safe to throw out.
  \item \movename{5M} at \disadvantage{$-$5} on block is punishable --- never end block strings with this.
  \item \movename{66H (Step Up)} at \advantage{+1} on block is Ekko's only plus on block normal --- frame trap after this.
  \item \movename{3M (Flip It)} at \advantage{+5} on hit: massive advantage --- confirm into combo when Volatile Afterimage is active.
  \item \movename{Temporal Slip (6S1)}: gap between hits 2 and 3 can be parried, and it is punishable by most medium attacks on block. Never throw out raw.
  \item \movename{2H} has a lingering hurtbox during recovery frames --- whiffing this in neutral is dangerous.
\end{itemize}

\section{Afterimage System}

\subsection{Normal Afterimage (Uncharged S2)}

Using \notation{5S2}, \notation{3S2}, or \notation{j.6S2} creates a \textbf{normal Afterimage} at Ekko's position, then teleports him back.
Follow-up options:
\begin{itemize}
  \item \movename{r.S2 (Replay)} --- teleports back to the afterimage with an attack
  \item \movename{r.2S2 (Time's Up!)} --- returns with downward launcher; hold back to retreat instead
\end{itemize}

\subsection{Volatile Afterimage (Hold S2 until frame 18+)}

Holding \notation{S2} to at least frame 18 charges a \textbf{Volatile Afterimage}, unlocking:
\begin{itemize}
  \item \movename{Chronoswitch} --- teleport without attacking, maintaining neutral position
  \item \movename{Chronobreak (2S2)} --- OTG pickup with wall bounce for corner carry
  \item \movename{Flip It (3M)} becomes combo-able into full routes
  \item Essential for all of Ekko's highest-damage sequences
\end{itemize}

\begin{tipbox}
\textbf{Down S2 (2S2/3S2)} is the fastest and safest way to set up a Volatile Afterimage. It is also \textbf{projectile-invulnerable} --- it passes through Jinx rockets and other projectiles.
\end{tipbox}

\subsection{Afterimage Cancel}

Once any S2 Special or S2 Super has been used, virtually every move can be cancelled into an Afterimage follow-up. This makes opponents extremely hesitant to use Parry or Pushblock --- they cannot be certain whether Ekko will attack or rewind.

\section{Timewinder Mechanics}

\subsection{Distance-Dependent Properties}

\begin{itemize}
  \item \textbf{Early/close hit:} Standard juggle for basic conversions
  \item \textbf{Max-distance (slowed):} Applies a \textbf{Time-slow reaction} --- gives a generous window to follow up for a big combo (Ekko or Assist)
\end{itemize}

\subsection{Batting Timewinder}

Heavy buttons can redirect the Timewinder mid-flight:
\begin{itemize}
  \item \notation{H} --- launches it forward
  \item \notation{2H} --- sends it upward in an arc
  \item \notation{3H} --- arcing downward trajectory
\end{itemize}

\begin{tipbox}
\textbf{Mirror Matchup:} You can hit an opponent's Timewinder back at them with your own heavy attacks to redirect it. This does \textbf{NOT} work with the Mega Timewinder Super.
\end{tipbox}

\section{Air Hop}

Ekko's Air Hop (\notation{L+M} or \notation{66} in the air) is a unique \textbf{forward-only short hop}. It cannot be performed backwards. Use it to:
\begin{itemize}
  \item Bait out anti-airs in neutral before confirming on the opponent
  \item Cross up opponents and sandwich with Assist
  \item Mix empty jump into throw, jump H, or Air Hop cross-up to confuse defensive options
\end{itemize}

  \item \textbf{Timewinder control} --- force opponents to respect the screen at all ranges
  \item \textbf{Clone threat} --- a Volatile Afterimage's mere presence changes opponent defensive habits
  \item \textbf{Mobility and positioning} --- Air Hop and dash to maintain optimal spacing
\end{enumerate}

\section{Key Neutral Buttons}

\begin{itemize}
  \item \movename{5S1 (Timewinder)} --- primary space control; throw at max range for Time-slow reaction
  \item \movename{5L} --- fastest check; interrupt opponent movement
  \item \movename{3L (Heel Tap)} --- longer-range low; superior poke versus \notation{2L}
  \item \movename{5H} --- solid advancing poke; \advantage{+1} on hit
  \item \movename{66H (Step Up)} --- \advantage{+10} on hit, \advantage{+1} on block; best pressure button
  \item \movename{j.H} --- jump-in; mix with empty jumps to condition anti-airs
\end{itemize}

\section{Timewinder Okizeme}

After hard knockdowns and Limit Strikes, use \notation{2S1} to lob a Timewinder that floats down onto the waking opponent.

\subsection{Corner Throw Oki Trap}
\begin{enumerate}
  \item Land \movename{Forward Throw} in the corner
  \item Immediately input \notation{2S1}
  \item Timewinder traps opponent --- they cannot Getup Attack; it hits and slows them even if Ekko is hit
  \item Free mixup: choose tick-throw, left/right with \notation{2S2}, block string into overhead, or Assist sandwich
\end{enumerate}

\begin{tipbox}
Cycling through different oki patterns conditions opponents to freeze on wakeup. Then punish with the overhead.
\end{tipbox}

\section{Approach Tactics}

\begin{itemize}
  \item \textbf{Roll:} Invincibility frames allow passing through horizontal projectiles to close distance
  \item \textbf{Air Timewinder:} Jump and throw \notation{j.S1} to beat grounded anti-airs
  \item \textbf{Down S2 crossup:} Projectile-invulnerable; crosses up at close range before Assist arrives
  \item \textbf{Timewinder cover:} Fire \notation{2S1} then dash forward --- opponent blocked the projectile while you close in
\end{itemize}

\begin{enumerate}
  \item Begin charging \notation{2S2}
  \item Call Tag Assist
  \item Ekko crosses the opponent \textit{before} the Assist arrives
  \item Opponent must block in the opposite direction --- nearly unreactable left-right
\end{enumerate}

\subsection{Flip It Overhead (3M)}

Flip It is reactable on its own (22f startup). In blockstrings and with Assist pressure, it becomes deadly. Conditioning sequence:
\begin{enumerate}
  \item Multiple block strings with \notation{2L}, \notation{3L} (condition to crouch-block)
  \item Land \movename{Flip It} when they expect low
  \item With Volatile Afterimage active: confirm into full combo
\end{enumerate}

\subsection{Temporal Slip 50/50}

The gap between Temporal Slip hits 2 and 3 enables:
\begin{itemize}
  \item Normal Afterimage active: use \movename{Time's Up!} to catch Parry attempts
  \item Hold Down-Back during gap: disengage, making hit 3 a frame trap
  \item This 50/50: \textit{Time's Up vs. Temporal hit 3}
\end{itemize}

\begin{warnbox}
Temporal Slip on block is punishable by most mediums. Never use raw in block strings without afterimage backup.
\end{warnbox}

\section{vs. Zoners}

Zoners control fullscreen and beat Ekko in the poke war. He must get in to win.

\begin{matchupbox}[]{Strategy vs. Zoners}
\begin{itemize}
  \item \textbf{Examples:} Jinx, Teemo
  \item \textbf{Down S2 projectile invulnerability:} \notation{2S2} passes through most projectiles to close distance.
  \item \textbf{Roll approach:} Invincibility frames let you pass through horizontal projectiles.
  \item \textbf{Don't fight at fullscreen:} You lose the poke war. Get a knockdown and lock them into oki.
  \item \textbf{Forward Assist:} \notation{T} travels far quickly, covering your approach from behind.
  \item \textbf{Punish whiffed projectiles hard:} Many zoner projectiles have high end lag.
\end{itemize}
\end{matchupbox}

\section{vs. Rushdowns}

Rushdown players want to stay in your face constantly.

\begin{matchupbox}[]{Strategy vs. Rushdowns}
\begin{itemize}
  \item \textbf{Examples:} Vi, Darius
  \item \textbf{Anti-air consistently:} \notation{2H} into Limit Strike oki; reward yourself for anti-airs.
  \item \textbf{Parry bait:} S2 Hold/Release mixup counters aggressive players who Parry constantly.
  \item \textbf{Timewinder to decelerate pace:} A well-placed Timewinder forces them to respect the screen.
  \item \textbf{Back Throw:} Put them at fullscreen; combo into oki setup.
  \item \textbf{Backdash:} Create space and re-establish Timewinder control.
\end{itemize}
\end{matchupbox}

\section{vs. Grapplers}

Grapplers are deadly at close range; Ekko's mobility is his escape.

\begin{matchupbox}[]{Strategy vs. Grapplers}
\begin{itemize}
  \item \textbf{Examples:} Darius, Blitzcrank
  \item \textbf{Never stay at throw range:} Maintain poke distance at all times.
  \item \textbf{Heel Tap pressure:} \notation{3L} reaches far enough to poke without entering grab range.
  \item \textbf{Punish whiff grabs hard:} High end lag on grab whiffs means full BnB punish.
  \item \textbf{Air Hop to bait:} Hop over grab attempts and punish on landing.
  \item \textbf{Chronostrike crossup:} Push them to the corner where Ekko controls the game.
\end{itemize}
\end{matchupbox}

\section{vs. Defensive / Turtle}

\begin{matchupbox}[]{Strategy vs. Defensive Playstyles}
\begin{itemize}
  \item \textbf{Timewinder:} Force reactions; punish defensive responses.
  \item \textbf{Overhead conditioning:} Repeated low pressure, then Flip It when they crouch-block.
  \item \textbf{Tick throws:} Against opponents who respect blockstrings, mix in tick throws constantly.
  \item \textbf{Corner oki loop:} Extremely oppressive against turtles who won't risk reversals.
\end{itemize}
\end{matchupbox}

  \item Ekko's oki traps are devastating once you land a hit; focus on getting that first opening
  \item During Blinding Dart, Volatile Afterimage follow-ups are unaffected --- continue the combo
\end{itemize}
\end{matchupbox}

\section{vs. \champ{Vi}}

\begin{matchupbox}[]{Ekko vs. Vi --- Roughly Even}
\textbf{Key Threats:}
\begin{itemize}
  \item Vault Breaker armoured gap-closer punishes cautious play
  \item Excessive Force (explosion) big damage on full confirm
  \item Strong anti-air capability
\end{itemize}
\textbf{Counter-strategy:}
\begin{itemize}
  \item Bait Vault Breaker and punish on whiff; significant end lag
  \item Avoid predictable jumps; use empty hop to bait her anti-air
  \item Roll to pass through charged Vault Breakers
  \item Timewinder oki interrupts Vi's offensive patterns on wakeup
  \item \notation{2S2} passes through Vault Breaker armour
\end{itemize}
\end{matchupbox}

\section{vs. \champ{Warwick}}

\begin{matchupbox}[]{Ekko vs. Warwick --- Slightly Disadvantageous}
\textbf{Key Threats:}
\begin{itemize}
  \item Infinite Duress grab Ultimate ignores block; must tech or jump
  \item Primal Howl fear interrupts Ekko's aggressive pressure strings
  \item S2 Hold only \textit{trades} with Warwick \notation{5L} on crossup
  \item Fury mechanic blocks all chip damage
  \item Blood Hunt speed buff at opponent low HP is terrifying
\end{itemize}
\textbf{Counter-strategy:}
\begin{itemize}
  \item Use Timewinder to force reactions; deny his close-range game
  \item Avoid raw Chronostrike at close range; he can Primal Howl interrupt
  \item Do not attempt chip-kill strategies; Fury blocks chip
  \item At crossup: take the trade, then continue with \notation{5L > BnB}
  \item Corner Timewinder oki: his large body makes Timewinder easy to hit
\end{itemize}
\end{matchupbox}

\section{vs. \champ{Yasuo}}

\begin{matchupbox}[]{Ekko vs. Yasuo --- Even to Slightly Disadvantageous}
\textbf{Key Threats:}
\begin{itemize}
  \item Wind Wall (B.S2) blocks ALL of Ekko's projectiles including Timewinder and Mega Timewinder
  \item Last Breath combo off knockups is enormous damage
  \item Strong crossup game of his own
\end{itemize}
\textbf{Counter-strategy:}
\begin{itemize}
  \item Track Wind Wall cooldown; when it expires go aggressive with Timewinder immediately
  \item Wind Wall now has reduced duration and increased cooldown (Season 1 nerf)
  \item Do not jump into Last Breath range when airborne from launcher
  \item \notation{2S2} to go under or through Steel Tempest
  \item Afterimage setups confuse Yasuo's crossup reads; S2 Hold/Release baits his parry
\end{itemize}
\end{matchupbox}

\section{Using Ekko as Assist}

\subsection{Forward Assist (My Time! --- Forward T)}

Primary and most used assist. Chronostrike travels a long distance across the screen:
\begin{itemize}
  \item Covers point champion's approach and block strings
  \item On hit: \textbf{Handshake Tag} to Ekko and confirm into full combo
  \item Hard to react to due to travel distance and speed
\end{itemize}

\subsection{Back Assist (Timewinder --- Back T)}

Use at \textit{maximum range only}. When the slow hits, Time-slow reaction enables a huge combo. When it misses, the lingering projectile still controls the screen:
\begin{itemize}
  \item Forces opponent to play around the slow-field
  \item Enables long-range tag combo from the Time-slow reaction
  \item Blocks Getup Attacks during point champion oki
\end{itemize}

\section{Afterimage Cancel Deep Dive}

With an Afterimage active, virtually every move can be cancelled into follow-ups. This fundamentally changes how opponents approach defense:

\begin{itemize}
  \item Normal move $\to$ Replay (attack from the afterimage side)
  \item Normal move $\to$ Time's Up! (rewind with launcher, adjustable direction)
  \item Block string pause $\to$ teleport to bait Parry $\to$ punish
  \item Bait the Pushblock, then teleport past it for a punish
\end{itemize}

\section{Training Mode Optimization}

\begin{tipbox}
Enable frame data visualization in 2XKO's training mode to see startup, active, and recovery frames in real-time. Visualize hitboxes (red) and hurtboxes (green), and check frame advantage after each interaction. This is the fastest route to locking in combo links and finding unsafe moves.
\end{tipbox}

\section{Common Mistakes to Avoid}

\begin{itemize}
  \item \textbf{Over-relying on anti-air:} \notation{2H} is poor --- position to avoid needing it.
  \item \textbf{Forgetting Forward input for new clone:} \notation{6S2} creates a new clone; \notation{S2} / back \notation{S2} returns to an existing one.
  \item \textbf{Ignoring range limitations:} Ekko's buttons are short. Never out-poke range-dominant characters.
  \item \textbf{Wasting Volatile Afterimage charges:} Tangled Timeline gives 5 moves; spend them on overheads, OTG pickups, and high-damage enders.
  \item \textbf{Temporal Slip in raw pressure:} Always have an Afterimage out before using \notation{6S1} in blockstrings.
  \item \textbf{Trying to Replay out of failed throws:} This no longer works (Season 1 nerf). Adjust throw-pressure patterns.
\end{itemize}

\section{Season 1 Patch Notes Summary (Version 1.1.3)}

\begin{itemize}
  \item \movename{Backdash} slowed for Ekko (and Ahri, Warwick, Vi, Yasuo) --- retreat is more honest
  \item \movename{Heel Tap (3L)} hitstun $+$2 frames (was 13, now 15); less pushback on hit --- better low confirm
  \item \movename{Step Up (66H)} scaling: $+$1 per use instead of $+$3 --- better combo filler
  \item \movename{Replay out of failed throw} removed --- no longer a low-commitment strike-throw
  \item \movename{Chronostrike Assist} startup 17f $\to$ 19f; no longer gapless when doubled; range, hitbox, and distance all reduced
  \item \movename{Timewinder Assist} startup 16f $\to$ 22f; less dominant at space control
  \item \movename{Attempt \#37 (Ultimate)} blockstun reduced by 6f --- less safe on block
  \item \movename{Chronobreak} meter gain reduced from 10 to 8 per use
  \item \movename{Mega Timewinder} first hit now causes damage scaling --- less damage off stray hits
  \item \movename{Flip It (3M)} hitstun decreased on interrupt --- now only combos into \notation{5L} on interrupt
  \item \movename{M range} slightly decreased
\end{itemize}

\section{Tiered Gameplan Checklist}

\subsection*{Beginner Goals}
\begin{itemize}
  \item Learn Pulse Fuse combos (no strict timing required)
  \item Practice Timewinder at max distance for Time-slow confirms
  \item Get comfortable with basic \notation{S2} $\to$ \notation{r.S2} Replay sequence

\begin{itemize}
  \item Land Heel Tap (\notation{3L}) into combo consistently

  \item Incorporate \notation{2S2} crossup into Assist sandwich
  \item Start conditioning opponents for Flip It overhead
\end{itemize}

\subsection*{Advanced Goals}
\begin{itemize}
  \item Convert off Forward Throw solo via Chronobreak OTG
  \item Perform full Tangled Timeline install combo with all 5 charges expended
  \item Execute Down S2 + Assist sandwich for the true left-right
  \item Optimise corner carry with charged air S2 wall bounce sequences
  \item React-confirm to Timewinder distance hits for extended conversions
  \item Exploit Temporal Slip 50/50 with Time's Up! under advanced pressure
