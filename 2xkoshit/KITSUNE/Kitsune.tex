\chapter{How to Use This Master Guide}
% ═════════════════════════════════════════════════════════════════════════════

This guide contains \textbf{two complete interpretations of Ahri} in 2XKO:

\vspace{4pt}

\begin{zonerinfobox}
\textbf{The Zoner Variant} emphasizes screen control through projectile management. Orb placement, wall bounces, and spacing discipline define this playstyle. Best for players who enjoy positioning, reads, and setting traps.
\end{zonerinfobox}

\vspace{6pt}

\begin{rushdowninfobox}
\textbf{The Rushdown Variant} emphasizes aggressive pressure through air mobility and rekka chains. Air dash mixups, oki setups, and corner carries define this playstyle. Best for players who enjoy momentum, mixups, and execution.
\end{rushdowninfobox}

\section{Reading This Guide}

Core content (Move Lists, Frame Data, Mechanics, Neutral Game, Mixups) is shared between both variants. When playstyle-specific information appears, it's marked with:

\begin{itemize}
  \item \zoner Details specific to the zoning playstyle
  \item \rushdown Details specific to the rushdown playstyle
\end{itemize}

Choose the variant that resonates with your playstyle, or learn both for maximum flexibility.

\section{Guide Structure}

This guide is organized into the following chapters, each building on the previous:

\section{Who is Ahri?}

Ahri is the Spirit Fox of Ionia---a celestial enchantress who uses mystical orbs and supernatural charm to manipulate the battlefield. In 2XKO, Ahri occupies a unique position: she can be played as either a \textbf{Zoner} or \textbf{Rushdown} character depending on gameplan and team composition.

Her kit rewards creative, strategic thinking with omnidirectional mobility (Spirit Rush), safe pressure strings (Foxfire Blast rekka), and setup-heavy oki tools (Stalking Dart). With only \textbf{900 HP}---tied for the second lowest in the game---she demands execution and precision, but one good confirm leads to devastating, corner-carrying combinations.

\section{Air Dash System}

Ahri is the \textbf{only character in 2XKO with a traditional air dash}. Activating it with \notation{L+M} or \notation{66} while airborne, she can dash both forward and backward from any air normal or air special (once per airborne sequence).

\begin{tipbox}
Most of Ahri's air normals and air specials can be cancelled into an Air Dash. This makes approach angles nearly unpredictable and is used in the majority of longer combo routes.
\end{tipbox}

\subsection{Baiting Anti-Airs}

Air Dash early upon jumping:
\begin{itemize}
  \item Fly over committed anti-air and land behind opponent
  \item Creates ambiguous left/right that must be read and re-blocked
  \item Enables safejump setups and unblockable pressure after knockdown
\end{itemize}

\subsection{Air Dash in Combos}

The second hit of Foxfire rekka (Foxfire Leap) can be Air Dash cancelled mid-air:
\begin{itemize}
  \item \zoner \notation{S1 > S1 > S1} chain is safe; use 3rd hit for corner positioning
  \item \rushdown \notation{S1 > S1 > AD > j.M > j.H} extends into full mid-air combo
\end{itemize}

\section{Foxfire Rekka System}

\notation{5S1} begins the three-hit rekka sequence:

\begin{enumerate}
  \item \movename{Foxfire Blast (1st):} Projectile traveling 3/4-screen. Safe to throw at mid range
  \item \movename{Foxfire Leap (2nd):} Advancing follow-up; air-dash cancelable for advanced routing
  \item \movename{Foxfire Dive (3rd):} Ground bounce on hit; conversion into launcher
\end{enumerate}

\begin{tipbox}
The entire \notation{S1 > S1 > S1} string is \textbf{safe on block}. On hit, convert to full combo. On block, maintain pressure or tag.
\end{tipbox}

\section{Orb Arc (2S1) and Wall Bounces}

\begin{itemize}
  \item \zoner Wall bounces are your primary zoning advantage; position arcs 1-2 character lengths from corner for double-threat coverage
  \item \rushdown Wall bounce oki is devastating; forces opponent navigation or chip accumulation before you apply pressure
\end{itemize}

\section{Bounce! Overhead and Air Dash Cancels}

\movename{Bounce!} (\notation{3S2}) overhead can be cancelled into Air Dash on hit or block:

\begin{itemize}
  \item After blocked Bounce! + Air Dash: choose double overhead, crossup, single overhead into low, or empty dash throw
  \item Creates nearly unreactable mixup tree from one blocked situation
  \item Conditioning is key: use low pressure to condition crouch-blocking, then \notation{3S2} lands as overhead
\end{itemize}

\section{Wakeup Oki with Stalking Dart}

The primary oki sequence involves Stalking Dart (\notation{2S1}):

\begin{enumerate}
  \item End combo with ground bounce into knockdown
  \item Input \notation{2S1} on opponent wakeup
  \item Stalking Dart explodes and creates free mixup or pins opponent

\section{Strike-Throw Mix}

Ahri's ground mixup uses \notation{5L} and \notation{2L} with dashes:

\begin{enumerate}
  \item Condition with low pressure (\notation{2L}, \notation{2M})
  \item Dash in and either \notation{2L} (they blocked low) or \notation{6T} (they expected low)
  \item Tight timing makes reactions nearly impossible
\end{enumerate}

\begin{tipbox}
Land this 2-3 times to condition, then break with the opposite option. Ahri's fastest low (2L at 6f) wins most strike-throw windows.
\end{tipbox}

\section{High/Low with Bounce! Overhead}

\begin{enumerate}
  \item Establish low pressure with \notation{2L}, \notation{2M}, Foxfire rekka chains
  \item Condition opponent to crouch-block
  \item Input \notation{3S2 (Bounce!)} overhead when they expect another low
  \item Air Dash cancel on hit for extended mixup
\end{enumerate}

\begin{warnbox}
Bounce! startup (slow) makes it reactable raw. Only use in conditioned strings or with Assist covering approach.
\end{warnbox}

\section{Air Dash and Spirit Rush Mixups}

From blocked Heavy attack:

\begin{enumerate}
  \item Cancel \notation{5H} on block into \notation{8S2 (Spirit Rush Up)}
  \item Call Tag Assist simultaneously
  \item Air Dash over opponent from above
  \item Choose same side or crossup side for left/right confusion
\end{enumerate}

\section{Falling Spark Ambiguous Crossups}

After any knockdown (\notation{3M} or ground bounce):

\begin{itemize}
  \item Jump and use \notation{j.2L (Falling Spark)} --- hits both sides
  \item Left/right connection timing depends on spacing --- difficult to react to
  \item Cancelable into air Foxfire on hit for full combo follow-up
\end{itemize}
