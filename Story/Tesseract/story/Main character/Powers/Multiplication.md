In your *Tesseract* universe, **Multiplication** is not simple duplication. It is the power of **constrained multiversal summoning**—the ability to temporarily call forth aspects or full avatars of your own self from adjacent probability spaces and alternate timelines to aid you.

Here is its breakdown within your story's rules of information and identity.

### Core Definition: The Summoning of Adjacent Selves
Multiplication allows you to access the vast data-cloud of your own potential existences in the omniverse. You are not creating something from nothing; you are **rendering a temporary instance of a "what-if" version of you**.

*   **It's a Data Call:** Think of your core **Anchor** as the primary server. Multiplication sends a request—paid for with **Coins**—to nearby dimensional branches or unused probability states, and downloads a limited-use instance of a variant "you" based on search parameters.
*   **Two Modes of Operation:** As you described:
    1.  **Full Summoning:** Calling a complete, autonomous alternate self to fight alongside you. This is costly but powerful.
    2.  **Mimicry (The Strategic Mode):** The more refined and common use. You summon *specific, disembodied attributes* of other selves as needed—a hand, an eye, a skill—which manifest as temporary extensions of your own body.

### Key Mechanics & Rules

1.  **The Cost of Otherness:**
    *   Each summon costs **Coins**. The more complete, powerful, or divergent the summoned self, the higher the cost.
    *   **Anchor Strain:** Maintaining a connection to another self's data pattern puts strain on your own Anchor. Your reality's **checking system** is now validating two very similar data sets, which can cause system lag or glitches around you.

2.  **Limitations of the Summoned:**
    *   **Temporary & Ephemeral:** Summons are not permanent. They last for seconds or minutes before their data destabilizes and they fade, or are "dismissed."
    *   **Limited Agency:** Fully summoned selves often act on a simple, pre-programmed instinct (e.g., "Fight that enemy," "Guard this spot"). They are echoes, not full personalities with free will, though they may possess unique skills your core self lacks.
    *   **Range and Control:** There is a limited range from your body where they can manifest or operate. Losing focus can cause them to flicker or act erratically.

### Tactical Uses: The Power of Synergy
This is where Multiplication shines as a strategic, creative tool.

*   **Mimicry for Defense/Offense (Your Example):** A blade is at your neck. Instantly, a hand—summoned from a version of you trained in disarming—grows from your shoulder to block it, while your main body counter-attacks. You could manifest an extra set of eyes for 360-degree vision, or legs from a sprinter-self for a burst of speed.
*   **Skill Stacking:** Need to hack a terminal? Summon the fingers of a programmer-self. Need to decipher an alien language? Momentarily overlay the mind of a linguist-self.
*   **Overwhelm and Distract:** Summon 3-4 combatant selves to engage multiple enemies, using them as expendable tactical assets.

### The Hidden Risk: Identity Corruption
This is the profound danger that makes Multiplication a double-edged sword.
*   **Data Feedback:** When a summoned self is injured or "killed," or when you use Mimicry extensively, their experiential data—their memories of pain, fear, or expertise—can flood back into your primary Anchor.
*   **The "Who Am I?" Crisis:** Constantly experiencing fragments of other lives can dilute your core identity. If you rely too heavily on a sniper-self's instincts, do you become more them? This power directly threatens the very thing C-43 is searching for: a stable, understood self. It risks turning him from an individual into a **crowded committee of echoes**.

### Why It Fits C-43 and Your Story

1.  **The Metaphor of the Shattered Self:** C-43 is an experiment with no past. Multiplication externalizes his internal crisis. He is literally surrounded by versions of who he *could* be, none of which are *him*. His quest for identity is fought using the power of alternate identities.
2.  **The Tesseract Connection:** A tesseract shows all 3D states of an object at once. **Multiplication allows C-43 to be a living tesseract of himself.** He can manifest multiple states (the fighter, the thinker, the survivor) simultaneously in one moment.
3.  **Contrast to Impossible Paradox:** Both powers explore possibility, but in opposite ways. The **Paradox** is vertical—drilling down through infinite timelines of a single self to find one path. **Multiplication** is horizontal—pulling across timelines to bring multiple selves into one path. One is solitary recursion, the other is collaborative breadth.
4.  **The Anchor's True Test:** This power most directly plays with the concept of the **Anchor as unique self**. Using it is an active gamble with the integrity of that self. It asks: How much otherness can you incorporate before you cease to be *C-43* and become just *C-*?

**In summary, in your story, Multiplication is the controlled, costly summoning of auxiliary data-sets from your own adjacent possibilities in the omniverse. It is the power of collaborative potential, turning the user into a localized hive-mind of their own variants. Its greatest strength—versatility—is also its existential peril: the erosion of a single, coherent identity.** It is the perfect power for a protagonist trying to assemble a self from the fragments of what he might have been.
