In your constructed universe of *Tesseract*, **Teleportation** is not simple magical displacement. It is a controlled, self-contained data transfer—a precise and personal overwrite of spatial coordinates within the reality's information matrix.

Here is a breakdown of its mechanics, strategic use, and inherent limitations based on your rules.

### Core Definition: The Self-Overwrite
Teleportation is the ability for a dimensional traveler (like C-43) to instantly edit their own location data within a single universe's coordinate system.

*   **It's a Local Edit:** Unlike interdimensional travel (which requires condensing your entire Anchor and navigating the 4D omniverse), teleportation happens *within* one reality. You are not leaving the "server"; you are moving your "file" to a new address on the same server.
*   **The Process:** The user spends **Coins** (anchor fragments) to temporarily bypass the local reality's "read-only" lock on their position. They compile a "transfer packet" of their current data, delete their data from Point A, and immediately rewrite it at Point B. To the universe's checking system, this is a valid, instantaneous state change—if done correctly.

### Key Parameters: The Strategic Cost
The power's flexibility and danger come from the variables the user must set, each with a different **Coin cost** and risk profile.

1.  **Momentum Handling:**
    *   **Momentum Preserved:** Lower cost. The vector data of your movement (velocity, direction) is retained. Teleporting while running means you arrive running. Useful for fluid combat, but disastrous if you appear facing a wall.
    *   **Momentum Reset:** Higher cost. Requires editing more of your physical state data. You appear at the new location with zero inertial velocity, perfectly stationary. Safer, but loses tactical advantage.

2.  **Displacement Method:**
    *   **Clearing Space:** Highest cost and risk. The power actively edits the data of the destination volume (e.g., air molecules, dust) to make perfect, empty space for you. This is a major local edit that can trigger more aggressive reality checks. Could cause a sudden vacuum implosion or data corruption in the displaced material.
    *   **Phasing/Overlap:** Extreme risk. You attempt to write your data into a space already occupied by other data (like inside a wall). This almost guarantees a **data collision** and corruption, likely damaging your Anchor as the two datasets conflict and fail to reconcile.

### The Critical Limitation: Why You Can't Teleport Others
This is a cornerstone of your lore. **Teleportation is strictly personal.**

*   **The Anchor is the Key:** You can only compile the "transfer packet" for your own complete data set because you *are* your Anchor. You have root access to your own code.
*   **The Reality Check Problem:** To teleport another conscious being, you would need to:
    1.  Access and copy their entire Anchor/data-self (a profound violation).
    2.  Delete them from one location.
    3.  Rewrite them in another.
*   This process would force the universe's checking system to validate the existence of the same entity in two places at once during the split-second transfer, then validate its sudden disappearance from one point. This **double-check** is highly likely to flag the event as an error, causing **catastrophic data corruption** for the subject. Their information could become mismatched, leading to identity fragmentation or being dumped into the **Forgotten Shores**.

### Offensive & Strategic Uses
Given the parameters, teleportation becomes a potent, high-skill tool:
*   **Combat:** Appearing behind an opponent, inside a guard's blind spot, or above a target. With momentum preserved, you could teleport a falling object (that you're touching) to gain lethal impact force.
*   **Evasion:** Instantaneously moving out of the path of an attack or a collapsing structure.
*   **Precision:** Teleporting just a few millimeters to the left to slip out of bonds, or to realign a broken bone perfectly (though this would be agonizing and risky).

### Why It Fits C-43
1.  **A Solitary Power:** It reinforces his isolation. He can only move himself. He cannot easily save others with it, which can create moral and tactical dilemmas.
2.  **Control and Cost:** It mirrors his entire existence: every significant action requires spending pieces of his self (Coins) and navigating strict parameters to avoid self-destruction. A poorly calculated teleport could be as damaging as a paradox.
3.  **Thematic Parallel:** Teleportation is the 3D version of his 4D travel. It’s a smaller-scale rehearsal of the core mechanic of his life: packaging his data, surviving a transition, and rewriting himself in a new context.

**In summary, in your story, Teleportation is the controlled, localized, and self-only editing of one's spatial coordinates within a reality's information framework. It is a power of precise selfishness, demanding payment in Anchor fragments and carrying the ever-present risk of a fatal system error if its parameters are miscalculated.**
