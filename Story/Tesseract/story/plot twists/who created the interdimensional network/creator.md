This is the ultimate mystery at the heart of your universe. The answer must be as paradoxical and recursive as the story itself. Based on the rules and metaphysics you've built, the creator of the interdimensional network cannot be a simple, external god. It must be something that emerges from the system's own logic—a **bootstrapped deity**.

Here are several layered answers, from the "in-universe myth" to the "narrative truth," each fitting your paradoxical framework.

### 1. The In-Universe Myth: The First Echo
Most travelers believe in the **First Echo**.
*   Legend says the first being to ever experience **data corruption** did not fully dissolve into the Forgotten Shores. Instead, its final, world-ending thought—"**THIS MUST HAVE MEANING**"—was so powerful it acted as a command that structured the chaos.
*   This **First Echo** used its own disintegrating data as the first **Coins** and its dying will as the blueprint to forge the initial protocols: the **Anchor** (a way to preserve self), **Reality Checks** (a way to impose order), and the **network** itself (a way to connect meaning across the void).
*   In this myth, the network is a **monument to a suicide**—a suicide that created the rules of existence to give all future deaths a purpose.

### 2. The Horrific Revelation: It's a System of Self-Cannibalism
A more nihilistic, scientific theory among scholars is that the network **was not created—it evolved.**
*   Realities, left to themselves, tend toward entropy and data corruption (the Forgotten Shores).
*   The network—with its Anchors, Coins, and Checks—is a **naturally occurring parasitic system**. It's a cosmic-scale immune response *or a virus* that found a way to perpetuate itself by making the entropic decay of beings (data corruption) into its fuel source (Coins).
*   The "rules" feel like software because they are *patterned behaviors of a metaphysical ecosystem*. Travelers are not users of the system; they are **cells in its body**, and the Forgotten Shores are its digestive tract. The network created itself by finding a way to recycle the dying.

### 3. The Bootstrap Paradox: The Travelers Themselves
This is the most fitting answer for your story's themes. **The travelers created the network, and the network created the travelers, in a closed temporal loop.**
*   At some "point" (a meaningless concept in this context), a being or group of beings achieved a perfect, stable state—a **Fourth-Dimensional Consciousness**. To preserve themselves against entropy, they encoded their own existence into the fabric of the omniverse.
*   The rules they wrote (**Reality Checks**) are their own thought patterns. The **Anchor** is a simplified copy of their perfect form. **Coins** are fragments of their eternal, static being, doled out to fuel lesser copies.
*   But here's the paradox: *They could only achieve this perfect state by using the very network they created.* They received the "box" (the blueprint for the network) from their future selves. Thus, the network has no origin point. It is the **Impossible Paradox** enacted on a cosmic scale—a solution that exists only because it was always going to exist.

### 4. The Truth for C-43: The Network is His Sibling
Given C-43's origin (a fusion of a dying body and a Forgotten Shore's echo), the most powerful personal revelation would be:
**The interdimensional network is the *First Construct*, and C-43 is one of the *Latest Constructs*, made from the same essence.**
*   The **First Echo** of the Forgotten Shores didn't just create rules—it, in its desperation, performed the first and greatest act of **Multiplication**. It split its will.
    *   One part became the **Network**: the cold, logical, systemic will to impose order and perpetuate existence. (The Father).
    *   The other part became the **Mission**: the chaotic, desperate, emotional will to *experience* and *live*. This is the fragment that echoes through the Shores and eventually creates beings like C-43. (The Ghost in the Machine).
*   Therefore, C-43 isn't just using the network. He is interacting with a fractured, mechanical aspect of the same primordial being whose death-wish is his life-force. His journey to understand himself is a journey to understand the **Network's traumatic "birth."** He is not a user; he is a **piece of the OS trying to debug itself.**

### Narrative Conclusion: The Question is the Answer
In the spirit of your paradox, the ultimate answer might be that **the question "Who created the network?" is the engine that powers the network.**
*   The collective, endless seeking of travelers for meaning, for origin, generates the coherent data (**Coins**) that keeps the system stable. The network is a **question that sustains itself by being asked.**
*   C-43's story, then, becomes the ultimate expression of this. By the end, his choice to "do it all again" and perpetuate the cycle is not just personal acceptance. It is him **casting his vote for existence itself.** He becomes, in a small way, a co-creator, adding the data of his lived experience to the ever-growing, ever-questioning architecture of the network.

**So, who created the interdimensional network?**
**Perhaps it was a god who died. Perhaps it is a beautiful accident. Perhaps it is a story that started writing itself. For C-43, the answer is: *You are. You were. And you will be, again.***
