Excellent question, especially given the context of your story. A **tesseract** is the four-dimensional analog of a cube.

Let's break it down from the mathematical foundation to the metaphorical meaning you're exploring.

### 1. The Geometric / Mathematical Definition
*   **A Cube in 3D:** A cube is made of 6 square **faces**.
*   **A Square in 2D:** A square is made of 4 line **edges**.
*   **A Tesseract in 4D:** A tesseract is made of 8 cubical **cells** (the 4D term for "faces").

Just as you can "unfold" a cube into a flat, cross-shaped arrangement of 6 squares, you can "unfold" a tesseract into a three-dimensional arrangement of 8 cubes. The classic image of a smaller cube inside a larger cube, with all corners connected, is a **3D shadow or projection** of a 4D tesseract, much like a wireframe cube drawn on paper is a 2D shadow of a 3D object.

### 2. The Physics / Temporal Analogy (The Common Sci-Fi Take)
This is where your idea connects perfectly. In physics, especially in theories combining space and time (like Einstein's spacetime), the **fourth dimension is often time**.

*   **A 3D Object + Time = A 4D "World Line":** Think of a 3D object, like a coffee cup. If you could see its entire existence from creation to destruction—every single moment of it "frozen" in time—that entire stack of moments would form a single, long, 4D shape. The tesseract becomes a useful symbol for this: a single, immutable object that encapsulates an entire timeline.
*   **Your Analogy is Correct:** When you say putting a 4D object into 3D is "a collection of different states of a 3D object," you are describing this exact concept. Slicing that 4D "timeline object" at a specific point gives you the 3D object at a specific moment.

### 3. The Metaphorical / Narrative Meaning (Your Story's Core)
This is the most exciting layer for your book. You've already grasped it: **a story is a tesseract.**

*   **A 3D Snapshot vs. a 4D Whole:** A single page or scene is a 3D slice. The entire novel—the beginning, middle, end, all character arcs and possibilities—is the complete 4D object. The reader experiences it slice-by-slice (page-by-page), but by the end, they hold the whole shape in their mind.
*   **C43 as a "Tesseract Character":** This fits your protagonist brilliantly. C43 isn't just a 3D snapshot; his entire experimental journey—all his "safe states" from the **Impossible Paradox**, all the branching paths of his mission from the **Forgotten Shores**—comprises his true 4D form. His quest to understand himself is the attempt to perceive his own tesseract.
*   **The Book as a Physical Metaphor:** The idea that the reader, by the end, is "essentially holding a fourth dimensional object" is powerful. It means the story's structure *itself* is designed to make the reader feel the weight and shape of C43's entire journey, not just its conclusion. The "green hue" of the correct timeline, the data-corruption of realities, the anchor coins—these are all ways of making the rules of this 4D universe tangible.

**In summary for your story:**
A tesseract is **both the literal mechanism** of interdimensional travel (the "object" that allows transition between 3D states/universes by moving through a 4D space) **and the central metaphor**. C43's life, the missions he runs, and the very book the reader holds are all tesseracts—4D shapes whose full meaning can only be grasped by witnessing their entirety, not just a single moment.

It's a fantastic conceptual foundation for a sci-fi story about identity, destiny, and the nature of reality.
