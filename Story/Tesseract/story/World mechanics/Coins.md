In your *Tesseract* universe, **Coins** are the fundamental currency of existence for dimensional travelers. They are not minted metal, but **crystallized, standardized fragments of a stable Anchor**—quite literally, *packets of soul-stuff* used as both fuel and diplomatic tender.

Here is a complete breakdown of their nature, economy, and devastating significance.

### Core Definition: The Solidified Self
A **Coin** is a unit of coherent, self-consistent reality data, carved from a dimensional traveler's **Anchor** and rendered into a stable, transferable form.

*   **It is a Piece of You:** When you "mint" a Coin, you are not creating something new. You are **fragmenting your own identity**, taking a portion of the data that defines you—your memories, your potential, your metaphysical mass—and hardening it into currency.
*   **It is Stable Data:** Unlike a living Anchor, which is complex and dynamic, a Coin is a "frozen" piece of data. It is inert, consistent, and universally recognizable by any reality's checking system as a valid, non-corrupt packet of information.

### The Functions & Uses of Coins
Coins are the oil that lets the machinery of interdimensional travel and power use function.

| Primary Use | How It Works | The Cost |
| :--- | :--- | :--- |
| **1. Paying the Reality Toll** | To enter a new universe, you must alter your Anchor to fit. Coins are paid to the reality's **checking system** as a "bribe" or "processing fee" to accept your altered data without flagging it as corrupt. | Each jump permanently spends a part of your past self. |
| **2. Fueling Powers** | Activating **Teleportation**, opening a **Portal**, or initiating the **Impossible Paradox** requires injecting coherent data into the local reality to overwrite its rules. Coins provide this data-fuel. | Using powers is **active self-cannibalism**. The stronger the power, the more of yourself you burn. |
| **3. Bypassing Local Checks** | When using a power that risks triggering a deep scan, you can spend Coins in real-time to "explain" the anomaly to the local system, smoothing over glitches and avoiding quarantine. | Constant, drip-feed erosion of your identity during conflict. |
| **4. Trading & Barter** | Coins are the universal currency among travelers. They can be traded for information, safe passage, or services. They can even be used in rituals to make requests of the **Forgotten Shores**. | You can trade pieces of your soul for the pieces of another's. |

### The Terrible Economy: A Closed Loop of Entropy
This creates a horrifying existential economy:

1.  **To Live, You Must Spend Yourself.** You need Coins to travel, to use abilities, to survive in hostile realities. Earning more requires taking tremendous risks.
2.  **The Primary Source is the Self.** The only guaranteed way to get Coins is to cut them from your own Anchor, making you weaker and less stable over time—a vicious cycle.
3.  **The Secondary Source is the Dead.** The only other way to obtain Coins without self-harm is to complete high-risk contracts from the **Forgotten Shores**. These missions pay out in Coins *minted from the disassembled data of the dead*. Thus, the entire economy is fueled by entropy and forgotten lives.
4.  **Running Out is Existential Bankruptcy.** If you deplete your Coins, you cannot pay reality's tolls. You become trapped in a dimension, unable to power your abilities, and highly vulnerable to the next reality check that could corrupt your now-unfunded Anchor.

### Why Coins Are Central to C-43's Struggle

1.  **The Experiment's Allowance:** As a created experiment, C-43 likely started with a **finite, pre-minted supply** of Coins baked into his Anchor. His journey is, on a practical level, a resource-management nightmare. Every decision is a calculation: *Is solving this problem worth a piece of my soul?*
2.  **The Pressure of Scarcity:** His quest for identity is directly hampered by the need to spend that identity as cash. To find out who he is, he must destroy who he is.
3.  **Moral Weight:** The Coin system forces grim choices. Does he spend his own soul, or does he venture into the Forgotten Shores to profit from the dead, commodifying their sorrows? Both paths are corrosive.
4.  **The Ultimate Measure of Will:** Choosing to *not* use a power, to accept failure or find a harder path, is the ultimate act of self-preservation. Coins make restraint a tangible, painful sacrifice.

### The Metaphor: Soul as Currency
Coins are the ultimate literalization of the phrase "spending your life energy." They make the metaphysical tragically physical. They represent:
*   **The Cost of Power:** Nothing is free. Omnipotence has a price tag paid in selfhood.
*   **The Fragmentation of Identity:** In a universe of infinite possibilities, maintaining a single, coherent self requires constantly sacrificing your potential.
*   **The Interconnectedness of All Travelers:** Everyone is trading in the same currency—fragments of existence. You might hold a Coin that contains the last memory of a being you'll never know.

**In summary, Coins are the standardized, spendable fragments of a dimensional being's Anchor. They are the blood in the veins of the omniverse's economy, the fuel for reality-breaking powers, and the slow, ticking clock counting down a traveler's existence. For C-43, they are his limited life savings, and every mission is a decision about what piece of himself he is willing to sell to buy one more day.**
