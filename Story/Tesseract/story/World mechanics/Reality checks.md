In your *Tesseract* universe, **Reality Checks** are the fundamental, automated security and maintenance protocols of a dimension. They are not conscious entities, but more like the immune system, error-correction routines, and firewall of a reality—all operating simultaneously to preserve the integrity of its data.

Here is a breakdown of their function, triggers, and consequences.

### Core Definition: The Universe's Immune System
Every dimension or universe is a vast, coherent **information system**. Reality Checks are the processes that run constantly to ensure all data within the system is **self-consistent** and follows the local **physical laws** (the local codebase).

*   **Purpose:** To maintain a stable, causally logical, and continuous experience of reality for all native entities within it.
*   **Analogy:** Think of a reality as a giant, self-verifying database. Reality Checks are the automated scripts that run to find and quarantine corrupted entries, flag foreign data, and prevent paradoxes from crashing the system.

### How They Work: The Validation Process

1.  **Constant Background Scans:**
    *   The system perpetually validates that objects are where their data says they are, that events follow from prior causes, and that the properties of matter are consistent.
    *   **Example:** It "checks" that a ball thrown in an arc follows a parabolic path defined by local gravity. If the ball suddenly teleports (a data inconsistency), the check flags it.

2.  **Triggered Deep Scans:**
    *   Certain events trigger more intensive, localized checks. These are the dangerous ones for travelers.
    *   **Primary Triggers:**
        *   **Introduction of Foreign Data:** When a dimensional traveler (like C-43) arrives, their entire **Anchor** is a massive packet of non-native data. A deep scan activates to validate if this new data can be integrated without breaking local rules.
        *   **Major Local Data Manipulation:** Large-scale or blatant edits to reality—like opening a huge **Portal**, sustaining an **Impossible Paradox**, or using **Telekinesis** to suspend a building—act as flares that draw intensive scrutiny.
        *   **Attempted Data Duplication/Deletion:** Trying to **Teleport** another person or create a permanent clone with **Multiplication** would trigger a critical scan, as it threatens the unique identity registry of the reality.

### Consequences of Failing a Reality Check

The system's response escalates based on the severity of the inconsistency.

| Severity | System Response | Result for the Entity |
| :--- | :--- | :--- |
| **Minor Glitch** | **Autocorrection:** The system uses minimal energy to nudge things back to consistency. The ball's teleport is reverted; momentum is slightly altered. | A sense of "déjà vu" or a visual flicker for natives. For a traveler, a small, involuntary **Coin** tax to pay for the correction. |
| **Major Anomaly** | **Quarantine & Interrogation:** The localized area is locked down. The system aggressively probes the anomalous data (the traveler and their effects) to understand it. | The traveler experiences "lag"—their powers stutter, their senses blur. They must spend **Coins** rapidly to "explain" their presence and actions to the system, bribing it with coherent data to avoid deletion. |
| **Critical Corruption** | **Deletion & Scramble:** The system identifies the data as irreconcilable and a threat to overall stability. It does not delete the data (as information is conserved) but **disassembles** its coherent structure. | **Anchor Corruption.** The traveler's data is shattered and scattered to the **Forgotten Shores**. They cease to exist as a conscious being. |

### Strategic Importance for Travelers
Understanding and manipulating Reality Checks is the core skill of survival.
*   **Coins as Bribes/Fees:** Travelers use **Coins** (stable, neutral data fragments) to pay the "toll" during a check. The Coins provide the system with acceptable data to offset the anomaly of their presence or power use.
*   **Stealth vs. Force:** A skilled traveler minimizes their "data footprint," using subtle powers to avoid triggering checks. A desperate one, using the **Impossible Paradox**, must be prepared to spend a fortune in Coins to withstand the catastrophic checks such a massive paradox triggers.
*   **The Reason for Limitations:** The rule that you **cannot teleport others** exists because the resulting double-check would be almost impossible to pass without causing total corruption for the subject.

### Narrative & Thematic Role for C-43

1.  **The Constant Antagonist:** Reality Checks are the impersonal, omnipresent opposition. Every move C-43 makes is against this clockwork system. It raises the stakes of even small power use.
2.  **The Mystery of the System:** The fact that realities operate this way—like software—is the central mystery of your story. *Who or what wrote the code?* The Checks are the most tangible evidence of a designed universe.
3.  **The Pressure on Identity:** To pass a check, C-43's data (his Anchor) must be *consistent*. But he is a being searching for his past, which means his self-concept is *inconsistent*. His internal journey directly conflicts with the universe's demand for coherent data, forcing him to literally pay to maintain a false consistency.
4.  **The Tesseract Connection:** A tesseract is a perfectly consistent 4D shape. Reality Checks are the process that ensures every 3D "slice" of a moment remains a valid part of that larger 4D whole. They enforce the geometry of reality.

**In summary, Reality Checks are the automated, non-conscious protocols of a dimension that enforce data consistency and causal law. They are the border control, police, and immune system of a reality. For C-43, they are the ever-present barrier that taxes his very soul (Coins) for every extraordinary action he takes, making his struggle not just against enemies, but against the foundational rules of existence itself.**
