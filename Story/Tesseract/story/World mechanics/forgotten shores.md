In your *Tesseract* universe, the **Forgotten Shores** are not a physical place. They are the existential graveyard, the recycling bin of the omniverse—the final state of corrupted or abandoned existence.

Here is a comprehensive breakdown of its nature, mechanics, and role in your story.

### Core Definition: The Aftermath of Data Death
The Forgotten Shores represent the **disassembled state of an Anchor's data** after it has failed coherence checks and can no longer sustain a conscious entity. It is where the "source code" of a being is broken down into its base informational components, floating in a non-conscious, chaotic state.

*   **It's Not Oblivion:** Data, in your universe's rules, cannot be created or destroyed. When an Anchor corrupts, it doesn't vanish; it **fragments**. The Forgotten Shores are the dimension-like repository of this fragmented identity-data.
*   **A Landscape of Echoes:** Imagine a beach where the "sand" is made of shattered memories, lost skills, and faded emotions from countless erased travelers. The "waves" are currents of probabilistic noise. It is a silent, haunting archive of failure.

### How One Arrives at the Forgotten Shores
1.  **Anchor Corruption:** The primary path. This occurs when:
    *   A traveler's data is altered inconsistently (e.g., a botched teleport of another, severe reality edits).
    *   They run out of **Coins** and their Anchor becomes too unstable to pass a reality check.
    *   They suffer catastrophic feedback from a power like the **Impossible Paradox** or **Multiplication**.
2.  **Voluntary Dissolution:** A rare, tragic choice. A traveler who has lost all purpose may choose to disassemble their own Anchor, scattering themselves to the Shores to avoid a worse fate or as a final act of atonement.

### The Twisted Economy & Purpose of the Shores
Paradoxically, this graveyard is also a **workspace and a market** for the still-living.

1.  **Mission Hub (The "Payout"):**
    *   The disassembled data in the Shores sometimes forms semi-stable, ghostly problem-sets—echoes of the unresolved desires and missions the deceased once held ("solve world hunger," "kill my enemy," "save my homeworld").
    *   Living travelers can accept these **"impossible problems"** as contracts. Successfully resolving one collects and condenses the relevant data fragments, forming a **payout** of pure, neutral Coins. This is high-risk, high-reward work, often requiring powers that further risk the contractor's own Anchor.

2.  **The Source of Coins:**
    *   This is the dark secret of the traveler's economy. **Coins are minted from the dead.** When you spend a Coin, you are spending a piece of a dissolved being. Your power is fueled by entropy and forgotten lives.
    *   This makes every power use a moral weight: you are literally burning ghosts to make fire.

3.  **The Time Limit:**
    *   Missions here have a strict duration because the Shores are actively corrosive to intact Anchors. The chaotic data streams constantly threaten to introduce errors into a visitor's own code. Staying too long risks being assimilated.

### Why It's Central to C-43's Story & Themes

1.  **The Ever-Present Threat:** The Forgotten Shores are C-43's ultimate failure state. Every time he uses a power and spends a Coin, he flirts with joining them. They are the void he fears, the absolute erasure of the self he is trying to build.
2.  **The Mystery of Origin:** As an experiment with no past, a haunting question arises: **Was C-43 *assembled* from the data of the Forgotten Shores?** Is he a new construct built from old, forgotten pieces? This would make his quest for identity a journey to uncover a past that was never truly his.
3.  **The Engine of the Plot:** His missions likely come from here. He is a contractor, solving the last wishes of the dead to earn the Coins he needs to survive. His entire existence is caught in a cycle: risk the Shores to earn the currency needed to avoid the Shores.
4.  **The Metaphor for Legacy:** The Shores ask: What remains when you're gone? In this universe, your desires and regrets become geological features for others to navigate. Your legacy is a puzzle for a stranger to solve, for a price.

**In summary, the Forgotten Shores are the cosmic junkyard and desperate gig-economy for dimensional travelers. It is where souls go to be dismantled, and where the living go to scavenge pieces of the dead to fuel their own fragile existence. It is the haunting, necessary shadow to the gleaming promise of the omniverse, and the grim backdrop against which C-43's struggle for meaning takes place.**
