## IDENTIFICATION OF OPTIMAL SITES FOR COMMUNITY SOLAR SYSTEMS IN ANGOLA: A GEOSPATIAL MCDA-GIS FRAMEWORK WITH TECHNOLOGY MATCHING

### PROBLEM STATEMENT (2 lines)
Fifty percent of Angola's 34 million people lack electricity access, particularly in rural areas. Government aims to electrify 500 villages via solar by 2027 but lacks a systematic, data-driven method to prioritize sites, resulting in inefficient resource allocation and suboptimal technology choices.

### SOLUTION (3 lines)
We present GEESP-Angola, a geospatial multi-criteria decision analysis (MCDA) framework that integrates satellite data (solar irradiance, population density, terrain), infrastructure mapping (grid distance, roads, schools/clinics), and technology matching logic to identify priority sites and recommend system types. The framework combines Analytic Hierarchy Process (AHP) weighting with weighted overlay analysis to produce transparent, reproducible aptitude maps robust to parameter uncertainty (±20% sensitivity analysis confirms ranking stability in 42/42 scenarios).

### METHODOLOGY (3 lines)
Applied to Huíla Province as case study, we mapped 45 communities using 6 normalized raster layers processed via Google Earth Engine and QGIS. AHP panel (n=5 experts: Ministry, INE, NGOs) achieved consistency ratio 0.0755 (<0.10 acceptable threshold). Validation protocol specified: baseline measurements (piranometer, weather stations), 6-month operational monitoring, and post-implementation impact assessment using control-treatment design.

### KEY RESULTS (4 lines)
Framework identified 3 priority zones benefiting 191,000 people: Zone A (Cacula) aptitude 0.83 USD 0.18–0.22/kWh LCOE, Zone B (Humpata) aptitude 0.79 USD 0.20–0.24/kWh, Zone C (Quilengues) aptitude 0.76 USD 0.22–0.28/kWh. Financial returns: USD 50.5M investment yields USD 58.3M NPV, 14% IRR, 6.7-year payback. Framework matches sites to 4 technology profiles (PV-fixed, PV-tracker, hybrid solar-diesel, solar water-pumping), maximizing fit to local conditions. Estimated impact: 95,000 households gain access, school study time +150%, clinic vaccine refrigeration coverage +45%, agricultural income +83% (gender-focused).

### NOVELTY (2 lines)
Differentiates from precedent work (Nassar 2025 Iraq, Li 2025 Africa) by: (1) community-level granularity with equity criteria (not utility-scale only), (2) technology diversification linked to site aptitude (not one-size-fits-all), (3) transparent AHP-MCDA process with documented field validation protocol (not black-box optimization).

### IMPLICATIONS (2 lines)
Framework is immediately operational for Angola's Energy Sector Action Plan (2023–2027) and replicable to 15+ SADC contexts with similar rural access challenges. Code is open-source (GitHub, <30-minute replication time), enabling university teaching and policy integration. Supports World Bank energy access programs and climate finance alignment (SDG 7 + SDG 13).
