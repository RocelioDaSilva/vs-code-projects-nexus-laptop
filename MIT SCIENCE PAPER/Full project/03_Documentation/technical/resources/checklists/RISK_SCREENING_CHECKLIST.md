GEESP-Angola — Risk Screening Checklist

Purpose: Rapid pre-implementation screening to flag sites requiring mitigation or exclusion prior to pilot selection.

Checklist (Yes/No + Notes)

- **Population & Social Criteria**
  - Population estimate >= 100 households? (Yes/No) — Minimum viable user base
  - Presence of vulnerable groups (IDPs, conflict-affected, Indigenous)? (Yes/No) — If Yes: require enhanced safeguards
  - Land tenure clarity documented? (Yes/No)

- **Technical & Infrastructure**
  - Road access for installation/maintenance within 10 km? (Yes/No)
  - Grid expansion planned within 5 years (EDA)? (Yes/No)
  - Terrain slope < 15% on proposed site footprint? (Yes/No)
  - Water access for maintenance and dust suppression? (Yes/No)

- **Environmental & Biodiversity**
  - Not inside legally protected area or critical habitat? (Yes/No)
  - NDVI/landcover indicates low biodiversity sensitivity? (Yes/No)
  - Flood risk (1-in-25 year) acceptable? (Yes/No)

- **Governance & Institutional Readiness**
  - Local authority consent (Soba/Local Admin) documented? (Yes/No)
  - Community governance entity (cooperative or committee) exists? (Yes/No)
  - Local operator/technician availability (within 50 km)? (Yes/No)

- **Security & Conflict**
  - Recent conflict incidents in last 24 months? (Yes/No)
  - Crime risk to assets (theft/vandalism) high? (Yes/No)

- **Financial & Economic**
  - Community willing/able to contribute O&M fees? (Yes/No)
  - Local revenue use and tariff acceptability documented? (Yes/No)

Screening outcomes (tick one):
- Green: All critical criteria met — site eligible for pilot.
- Amber: 1–2 minor criteria failed — requires mitigation plan before selection.
- Red: Any critical failure (land tenure unclear; active conflict; environmental prohibition) — exclude site.

Required documents to advance to Pilot:
- Community consent letter
- Land access agreement signed
- Local governance readiness assessment
- Preliminary environmental screening form
- Implementation budget and procurement plan

Notes: This checklist is intentionally conservative; any amber result requires documented mitigation and sign-off by MINEA + PNUD before deployment.
