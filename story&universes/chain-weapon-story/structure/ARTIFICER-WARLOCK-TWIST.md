# Artificer ↔ Warlock Twist Outline (Option C: same person reveal)

Purpose: Provide a beat-by-beat foreshadowing and reveal plan if you choose to make the artificer and the warlock the same individual. This allows an early ensemble spread of skills and keeps allies independent until the twist sharpens stakes.

Principles to follow
- Keep early scenes showing distinct competencies (craft vs. paracausal portals) held by separate visible personas (workshop vs. chapel). The reader should see skills belonging to different contexts.
- Scatter ambiguous evidence: star-carvings, missing time, a ledger with an odd delivery linked to the artificer, and a witness claiming to have seen a figure whisper to the sky.
- Ensure allies each learn something unconnected to the secret (mutual-learning beats) so the ensemble retains agency even after the reveal.

Foreshadowing beats (Act I)
1. Workshop detail (Ch. 6): The Artificer carves a star-motif into a gate frame without thinking; the mark matches a symbol later seen in a portal flare.
2. Small memory gaps (Ch. 9): The artificer misplaces a simple bread recipe, blaming sleep. Make the loss small enough not to alarm but noticeable on re-read.
3. Market rumor (Ch. 12): A vendor mentions seeing a "light-singer" near the forge; a line in a ledger shows an odd delivery the night the artifact was tested.

Investigation beats (Act II)
4. Pattern linking (Ch. 17): Amara finds matching stampings—one in a ledger, one in the Warlock's relics. She notes the same artisan marks as those in Marrek's workshop.
5. Physical evidence (Ch. 19): A living anchor with a star notch is found at a minor portal site; Marrek recognizes the notch as his handwork.
6. Memory sliver (Ch. 21): Varro's regression shows a brief future where the artificer stands in starlight. The regressor is unreliable, but the group treats the sliver as a lead.

Reveal beats (Late Act II → Act III)
7. Confrontation (Ch. 28): A portal misfires and the artificer collapses with a cosmically burned palm; the Warlock's ritual page is discovered in the same workshop drawer as Marrek's tools.
8. Confession + evidence (Ch. 30): Under pressure, the artificer recounts a night near a node, a vision of stars, and hands that began to move on their own. The goddess's name is whispered in an old prayer carved into a gate.
9. Moral fallout (Ch. 32): Allies feel betrayed but also see how the artificer unintentionally kept the group safe; they must decide whether to expose the wartime advantage or to help stabilize the gift.

Payoff beats & consequences (Act III)
- The revelation reframes earlier scenes—Aisen's spool, Kaelen's living anchors, and the ledger entries all link to a single human who could not control all of it.
- The team uses the artificer/warlock's combined skills: Marrek's hands craft safer frames; the Warlock's pact provides strategic portal access—but at cost (memory splinters, entropy scars).
- The final choice: help the artificer bind the gift (ritual with great cost) or weaponize it and attract the Hidden Cabal.

Writing notes
- Keep the reveal earned: show small props (matching carvings, ledger proof) so the moment feels inevitable.
- Keep ensemble agency: after reveal, ensure allies retain unique knowledge—Kaelen's living anchors, Rin's stealth, Amara's routing—so the story doesn't collapse onto a single savior.
